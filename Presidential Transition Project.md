# Presidential Transition Project

The **Presidential Transition Project** is the formal name for the umbrella initiative organized by the [[Heritage Foundation]] beginning in 2022 to prepare a comprehensive playbook for a second [[Donald Trump]] administration. Its **flagship public product is [[Project 2025]]**, whose written manifestation is the 9th edition of Heritage's **[[Mandate for Leadership]]** (published April 2023, ~900 pages). PTP's less-public components include personnel databases, training programs ("Presidential Administration Academy"), and a 180-day playbook of executive actions for the first six months of a Trump second term.

## Structural relationship to Project 2025

The two names are sometimes used interchangeably, but they are not identical:

- **Presidential Transition Project (PTP)** — the umbrella initiative; the four work pillars
- **Project 2025** — the public-facing brand and coalition (~110 partner organizations)
- **Mandate for Leadership** — the written policy document; one of four PTP pillars

The four PTP work pillars:

1. **Policy** — the *Mandate for Leadership* document
2. **Personnel** — a database of vetted conservative candidates for ~20,000 political appointee positions (the "LinkedIn for MAGA" personnel database, hosted at presidentialtransition.org and accessible to vetted partners)
3. **Training** — the "Presidential Administration Academy" — online courses and in-person training for political appointees on regulatory law, communications, and policy execution
4. **180-Day Playbook** — operational sequencing of executive orders and regulatory actions for the first six months of a new administration. This pillar is largely non-public.

## The 2024 campaign episode

In summer 2024, [[Donald Trump]] publicly distanced himself from Project 2025, calling it "ridiculous" and "abysmal" and claiming "I know nothing about Project 2025." This distancing was widely understood as a **campaign-tactical retreat** — public polling showed P2025's specific policies (especially on abortion, Schedule F, and federal-workforce reductions) were liabilities — rather than substantive disengagement. [[Paul Dans]] resigned from the Project 2025 director role in late July 2024 as part of the public distancing.

Post-election (Nov 2024), it became immediately apparent that the distancing was rhetorical: **>200 of Trump's senior appointees in 2025** are drawn from PTP's personnel database and ~14 of 15 cabinet nominees are PTP-affiliated. ProPublica's "Trump shadow president" investigation centered on [[Russell Vought]]'s execution of the 180-Day Playbook through OMB.

## Why this matters

The **structural distinctness** between PTP (the operation) and "Project 2025" (the public brand) matters because:

1. Public discourse about "Project 2025" focuses on the *Mandate for Leadership* document — a long policy wishlist whose impact is debatable
2. The **operationally consequential** parts of PTP are the **personnel database** and the **180-Day Playbook** — both of which have been substantially executed since January 2025
3. Defensive strategies that target only the *Mandate* (e.g., "expose the policies") miss the operational machinery that's actually being deployed

## Connection to PTPs of prior administrations

Heritage's *Mandate for Leadership* has existed since 1981 (Reagan's first term). Past editions:
- 1981 — Reagan ed. (Reagan staff reportedly implemented ~60%+ of recommendations)
- 1984, 1989, 1996, 2000, 2008, 2012, 2016 — successive editions
- 2023 — 9th edition; first to be paired with the full PTP infrastructure (personnel db, training, playbook)

What's unprecedented about the 2023 edition isn't the policy document — Heritage has produced these for 40 years — it's the **operational scaffolding** built around it: the systematic pre-staffing of an administration via the personnel database, the active training academy, and the sequenced 180-day rollout plan.

## Open questions

- **What is the actual content of the 180-Day Playbook?** As of mid-2026, partial reverse-engineering from EOs issued vs PTP-affiliated personnel placements suggests substantial execution, but the full playbook document has never been publicly released.
- **What is the personnel-database verification process?** Vetting standards, sources of candidate information, and adverse-information handling are not public.
- **What happens to PTP after the second Trump term ends?** Heritage produces a new *Mandate* every cycle, but the personnel database is a uniquely valuable asset that could be repurposed for 2028 or beyond.
- **Connection to state-level PTPs:** Are similar transition-project structures being built for governorships (especially in [[Florida]], [[Texas]], and other P2025-aligned states)?
