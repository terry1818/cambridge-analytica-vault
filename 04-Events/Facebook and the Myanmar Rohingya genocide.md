## Summary

[[Facebook]] became the dominant public communications infrastructure in Myanmar in the early-to-mid 2010s, at a time when the country was transitioning from military junta to partial civilian government. For many Myanmar users, Facebook *was* the internet -- it was pre-installed on cheap smartphones and served as the primary news and information environment. Into this context, actors linked to the Myanmar military (Tatmadaw) and radical Buddhist nationalist networks systematically weaponized the platform to spread anti-Rohingya hate speech, dehumanization propaganda, and fabricated atrocity stories. In August 2017, the Tatmadaw launched a military campaign in Rakhine State that the UN subsequently characterized as genocide, displacing over 700,000 Rohingya to Bangladesh. The UN's fact-finding mission concluded in 2018 that Facebook had played a "determining role" in spreading hate speech and that the platform had been used as a "useful instrument" for genocidal incitement. Facebook's own independent human rights assessment (2018) conceded it had not done enough to prevent the platform's use for violence incitement. Meta is now subject to twin lawsuits in the US and UK seeking $150 billion in reparations.

## When & Where

- **Platform accumulation phase**: 2013-2016, as Facebook's market penetration in Myanmar grew rapidly
- **Peak hate-speech amplification**: 2016-2017, coinciding with increasing military pressure on Rohingya in Rakhine State
- **Military campaign**: August-September 2017, Operation of the Tatmadaw in northern Rakhine State
- **UN condemnation**: 2018
- **Amnesty International report**: September 2022
- **Lawsuits filed**: 2021 (UK and US jurisdictions)
- **SEC complaint by Rohingya survivor**: January 2025

## Actors

- **[[Facebook]] / Meta Platforms** -- Platform infrastructure; algorithmic recommendation systems amplified anti-Rohingya content; content moderation team in 2017 had no Myanmar-language moderators at scale
- **Tatmadaw (Myanmar Armed Forces)** -- Created fake accounts and entertainment pages to disseminate military propaganda and anti-Rohingya content; documented by Amnesty and researchers
- **Ma Ba Tha** -- Radical Buddhist nationalist organization; major source of hate-speech content on the platform
- **UN Fact-Finding Mission on Myanmar** -- 2018 report identified Facebook as determinative communications vector for incitement
- **Amnesty International** -- September 2022 report concluded Meta's algorithms and business model "substantially contributed" to atrocities; called for reparations
- **Rohingya plaintiffs** -- Class-action lawsuits in UK and US; SEC whistleblower complaint filed 2025

## Sequence of Events

1. **2013-2016**: Facebook becomes dominant information platform in Myanmar; pre-installed on smartphones; little local-language content moderation capacity
2. **2016**: Escalation of anti-Rohingya content; military and nationalist actors flood the platform; Facebook has no Myanmar-language moderation team proportionate to the threat
3. **Late 2016-mid 2017**: Hate speech and dehumanizing content against Rohingya intensifies; fabricated stories of Rohingya attacks on Buddhist communities circulate widely
4. **August 25, 2017**: Tatmadaw launches large-scale military operation in Rakhine State; mass killing, rape, and arson documented; over 700,000 Rohingya flee to Bangladesh within weeks
5. **2018**: UN Fact-Finding Mission report names Facebook as having played a "determining role" in spreading hatred; calls it a "useful instrument" in the context of genocide
6. **2018**: Facebook commissions independent human rights assessment; report concedes platform not doing enough to prevent violence incitement
7. **2021**: Twin lawsuits filed against Meta in US and UK seeking $150 billion for Rohingya refugees
8. **Sep 2022**: Amnesty International report concludes Meta's "dangerous algorithms" and profit-driven content amplification "substantially contributed" to 2017 atrocities; calls for reparations
9. **Jan 2025**: Rohingya survivor and human rights activist files whistleblower complaint with US SEC, alleging Meta misled shareholders about its Myanmar liability

## Outcome / Consequences

The Myanmar case became the starkest documented example globally of a social media platform's algorithmic and governance failures contributing to mass atrocity. It prefigured subsequent regulatory frameworks (EU Digital Services Act) that imposed algorithmic-risk assessments and crisis response obligations on major platforms. For [[Cambridge Analytica]] research vault purposes, it illustrates the convergence of platform architecture, ethnic-nationalist propaganda, and military influence operations -- with the amplification done not by a third-party data firm but by the platform's own recommendation engine.

## Disputed Facts

- The precise causal weight of Facebook in the genocide (versus pre-existing societal conditions, military impunity, and international failure) is contested in academic literature
- Meta disputes that its platform was a primary causal driver; its legal position holds that Section 230 immunizes it from liability in the US context
- The degree to which Facebook leadership was specifically warned about Myanmar risks before August 2017 is documented but the timeline of internal knowledge is still being litigated

## Open Questions
