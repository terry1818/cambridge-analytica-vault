# Project Texas

## Summary

Project Texas was [[TikTok]]'s roughly $1.5 billion corporate-restructuring program, launched in 2022, designed to answer U.S. national-security objections to the platform's Chinese ownership without forcing a sale. Its premise: if TikTok could physically localize all U.S. user data on U.S. soil, route it through a trusted American partner ([[Oracle Corporation]]), and place the most sensitive functions — data access and content-moderation decisions — under an independent, CFIUS-supervised governance structure, then the security risk of foreign control could be contained. The cornerstone was a new TikTok subsidiary, **TikTok U.S. Data Security Inc. (USDS)**, established in July 2022. For this vault, Project Texas is important as a landmark experiment in "data sovereignty as remedy" — an attempt to solve a problem of platform power and foreign data access through architecture and governance rather than ownership change. It ultimately did not satisfy Congress, which passed a divestment law in 2024; Project Texas was superseded by the 2026 Oracle-led joint venture, though that deal built directly on the Texas template.

## When & where

- **Date(s):** USDS established July 2022; build-out and partial implementation through 2024; superseded by the joint-venture close on 2026-01-22
- **Location:** United States — TikTok U.S. data centers in Virginia; data hosted in the [[Oracle Corporation]] cloud environment
- **Duration:** roughly 2022–2026 as a distinct program

## Actors

- [[TikTok]] — proposed and funded the program; created USDS
- [[ByteDance Ltd.]] — TikTok's Chinese parent; Project Texas was designed to wall its U.S. operations off from ByteDance control
- [[Oracle Corporation]] — chosen technology partner; won the role over Microsoft; hosts U.S. user data and monitors data flows
- **CFIUS** (Committee on Foreign Investment in the United States) — the U.S. interagency body negotiating with TikTok; USDS's independent board reports to CFIUS
- [[U.S. Congress]] — ultimately judged Project Texas insufficient and legislated divestment

## Sequence of events

- `2020` — First Trump administration pushes a forced TikTok sale; litigation stalls it, but talks with Oracle/Walmart begin the "trusted partner" idea
- `2022-07` — TikTok establishes **TikTok U.S. Data Security Inc. (USDS)** as the cornerstone subsidiary
- `2022` — TikTok confirms it is using Oracle infrastructure for U.S. user data
- `2022-2023` — TikTok re-architects data centers; states every U.S. user now communicates with a version of TikTok run in the Oracle environment
- `2023-03` — CEO Shou Zi Chew testifies to Congress; lawmakers express skepticism that Project Texas resolves the risk
- `2024-04-24` — Congress passes, and President Biden signs, the Protecting Americans from Foreign Adversary Controlled Applications Act — effectively rejecting Project Texas as a sufficient remedy
- `2025-2026` — Negotiations produce the Oracle-led joint venture; Project Texas's USDS/Oracle architecture becomes the foundation of the new TikTok USDS entity
- `2026-01-22` — Oracle-led joint-venture deal closes; the de jure ban ends

## Outcome / consequences

- **Direct outcome:** TikTok achieved partial data localization — U.S. user data hosted in Oracle's U.S. cloud, with USDS housing the functions most likely to raise security concerns (U.S.-citizen data access and content-moderation decisions), governed by an independent board reporting to CFIUS rather than to ByteDance.
- **Downstream effects:** Project Texas did not satisfy Congress, which legislated divestment in 2024. But its architecture was not discarded — the 2026 joint-venture deal built directly on the Oracle-partner, USDS-subsidiary model.
- **Precedent:** Project Texas became a reference model for "data localization as a national-security remedy," studied by Lawfare and others as a template for handling foreign-owned platforms.

## Disputed facts

- **Implementation completeness:** Lawfare and other analysts questioned whether Project Texas was ever fully implemented, or whether ByteDance personnel retained backdoor access to U.S. data. `#claim/disputed`
- **Algorithm control:** Project Texas localized *data* but did not clearly sever the *recommendation algorithm* from ByteDance — the influence-operation concern was arguably never resolved by it. `#claim/disputed`
- **Cost:** The ~$1.5 billion figure is TikTok's own; independent verification is limited.
