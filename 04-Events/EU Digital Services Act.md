## Summary

The EU Digital Services Act (DSA) is a landmark regulation that entered into force on 16 November 2022 and became fully applicable to all in-scope services by 17 February 2024. It replaces the 2000 e-Commerce Directive and imposes a tiered set of obligations on online platforms based on scale. Very Large Online Platforms (VLOPs) and Very Large Online Search Engines (VLOSEs) — those with more than 45 million monthly users in the EU — face the most stringent requirements: mandatory risk assessments, algorithmic transparency, independent audits, prohibition on targeting minors with profiling-based advertising, and access for researchers to platform data. [[Marietje Schaake]] was a key intellectual and political architect of the policy framework during her time as MEP and has continued to advocate for rigorous enforcement.

## When & where

- **Proposed**: December 2020 (European Commission)
- **Enacted**: 19 October 2022 (signed by Council and Parliament presidents)
- **Published in Official Journal**: 27 October 2022
- **Entry into force**: 16 November 2022
- **VLOP/VLOSE obligations begin**: August 2023
- **Full application**: 17 February 2024
- **Jurisdiction**: All 27 EU member states; applies to any platform serving EU users regardless of HQ location.

## Actors

- **European Commission**: Sole enforcement authority for Very Large Online Platforms; chairs overall regulatory body.
- **Digital Services Coordinators (DSCs)**: National-level authorities; enforce DSA for smaller platforms within member states.
- **Marietje Schaake**: Key MEP advocate for the underlying framework; later influential commentator on implementation.
- **Regulated entities**: Meta (Facebook, Instagram), Alphabet (Google Search, YouTube), Apple App Store, Amazon Marketplace, Microsoft (Bing), TikTok, X (Twitter), Snapchat, and others designated as VLOPs.

## Sequence of events

1. **December 2020**: European Commission proposes the DSA as part of the Digital Services package alongside the Digital Markets Act (DMA).
2. **April 2022**: European Parliament adopts the DSA with overwhelming majority.
3. **October 2022**: DSA enacted and published.
4. **April 2023**: European Commission designates the first 17 VLOPs and 2 VLOSEs.
5. **August 2023**: VLOP obligations begin applying.
6. **February 2024**: Full DSA application; all in-scope platforms must comply.
7. **2024–2026**: Commission opens investigations against TikTok (addictive design, child protection), X (content moderation), Meta (algorithmic amplification risks); enforcement actions ongoing.

## Outcome / consequences

- First major regulatory framework globally to require platforms to conduct and publish risk assessments for systemic risks (election integrity, gender-based violence, mental health).
- Algorithmic recommendation systems must offer users a non-profiling alternative.
- Targeting of minors using profiling for advertising prohibited.
- Researchers gain DSA-backed rights to access platform data.
- Maximum fine: 6% of global annual turnover.
- The US has no equivalent federal legislation; the DSA is the primary reference point in global platform-governance debates.

## Disputed facts

- Effectiveness of enforcement remains contested; critics (including Schaake) argue the Commission has been slow to impose fines.
- The Trump 2.0 administration has framed DSA enforcement against US-based platforms as a form of anti-American economic discrimination.

## Open questions

- Will the Commission impose first-round fines against VLOPs, and at what level?
- Does DSA reach extend to AI-generated content and synthetic media at scale?
- How does the DSA interact with the EU AI Act for algorithmic systems embedded in platforms?
