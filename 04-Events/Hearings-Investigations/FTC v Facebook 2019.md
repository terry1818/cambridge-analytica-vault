# FTC v Facebook 2019

## Summary

On **July 24, 2019**, the US Federal Trade Commission voted 3-2 to approve a record **$5 billion penalty against [[Facebook]]** -- the largest privacy-related fine in US history -- for violating its 2012 consent decree on user privacy. The FTC complaint alleged:

- Facebook privacy settings had falsely claimed to let users restrict sharing to "Only Friends" while still exposing data to third-party apps installed by friends
- Failed to screen developers or apps before granting access to user data
- Turned on facial recognition by default while claiming user control
- Collected phone numbers for two-factor authentication but used them for advertising without disclosure

Settlement created a 20-year consent decree, independent Privacy Committee on Facebook's board, and affirmative-consent requirement for facial recognition. Commissioner Rohit Chopra (dissenting) called the deal toothless; Commissioner Rebecca Slaughter argued the FTC should have sued [[Mark Zuckerberg]] personally. Also in July 2019, Facebook agreed a $100M SEC settlement for misleading investors about the same data exposure. ([[Source Wikipedia - Facebook-CA scandal]])
