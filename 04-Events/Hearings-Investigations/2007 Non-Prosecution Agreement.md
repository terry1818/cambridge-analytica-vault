# 2007 Non-Prosecution Agreement (Epstein NPA)

Federal non-prosecution agreement between the US Attorney's Office for the Southern District of Florida (led by [[Alexander Acosta]]) and [[Jeffrey Epstein]], finalized September 24, 2007. Among the most documented criminal-justice failures of the modern federal system.

## Terms (Tier 1)

- Federal prosecution shelved in exchange for Epstein pleading guilty to two Florida state prostitution charges
- Epstein sentenced to 18 months in Palm Beach County jail (served 13 months); permitted "work release" 12 hours per day, 6 days per week
- The NPA granted immunity to "any potential co-conspirators" - an unusual provision the [[Julie K Brown]] reporting and 11th Circuit identified as exceptionally favorable
- Victims were not notified of the deal in violation of the Crime Victims' Rights Act (CVRA)

## The crime not charged (Tier 1)

- The original [[Palm Beach Police investigation]] (2005-2006) had identified ~30 minor victims; FBI investigation expanded the count to over 60
- Federal indictment draft had been prepared by line prosecutors with multiple federal sex-trafficking counts
- Acosta's office overruled the line prosecutors

## CVRA litigation

- 2008: Victims filed suit under the Crime Victims' Rights Act
- February 2019: Judge Kenneth Marra (SD Fla) ruled victims' rights had been violated
- 2019: 11th Circuit ruled the manner of negotiation violated CVRA but the agreement itself could not be retroactively invalidated

## Consequences

- November 2018: [[Julie K Brown]]'s Miami Herald "Perversion of Justice" series republicized the case
- July 2019: SDNY indicted Epstein on new federal sex-trafficking charges
- July 2019: Acosta resigned as Labor Secretary
