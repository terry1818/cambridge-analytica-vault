# Trump Foundation NY AG Settlement 2019

The New York Attorney General's office civil action against the Donald J. Trump Foundation, Donald Trump personally, and his adult children Donald Trump Jr, Eric Trump, and Ivanka Trump (the Foundation's directors), alleging "a shocking pattern of illegality" including misuse of charitable funds, self-dealing, and illegal coordination with the 2016 Trump campaign. Filed June 2018 by AG [[Barbara Underwood]]. Trump Foundation agreed to dissolve December 2018. Final settlement November 7, 2019: $2,000,000 in damages, plus admission of misconduct, plus mandatory training and dissolution.

## Allegations

The complaint alleged the Foundation's directors:
- Permitted Trump to use Foundation assets for personal and business benefit
- Allowed the Foundation to be operated as little more than a checkbook for Trump's personal, political, and business interests
- Coordinated illegally with the 2016 Trump campaign
- Falsified records and tax filings

## Specific documented misuses

- **$10,000 portrait of Trump** purchased at an auction with Foundation funds; displayed at Trump's Doral resort
- **$25,000 contribution to Florida AG Pam Bondi's re-election PAC** (And Justice for All) in September 2013 while Bondi's office was considering whether to investigate Trump University; followed by Bondi's office declining to investigate
- **$100,000 settlement of Mar-a-Lago legal dispute** with Palm Beach paid from Foundation funds
- **$158,000 settlement of legal dispute** at Trump National Golf Club paid from Foundation funds
- **$5M payment for use of Trump's name** at Trump Organization-controlled events
- **2016 Iowa veterans' fundraiser**: $2.8M raised was effectively controlled by the Trump campaign in coordination with Foundation, including timing of disbursements to maximize campaign-event benefit

## Court rulings

- **November 2018**: Judge Saliann Scarpulla ruled NY AG had standing to proceed; rejected Trump's arguments for dismissal
- **December 18, 2018**: Trump Foundation agreed to dissolve under judicial supervision; remaining funds ($1.7M+) to be distributed to court-approved charities
- **November 7, 2019**: Final settlement

## Settlement terms (November 7, 2019)

Judge Scarpulla approved final settlement:
- **$2,000,000 in damages** paid by Donald Trump personally
- **Admissions** that Trump personally misused Foundation assets to settle legal claims of his for-profit businesses, support his presidential campaign, and pay for his self-promotional portrait
- **Mandatory restrictions**: Trump barred from serving as director or officer of any New York charity for 10 years; Donald Trump Jr, Ivanka Trump, and Eric Trump required to undergo mandatory training on fiduciary duties of charity directors
- **Distribution**: $2M in damages plus remaining Foundation assets distributed to court-approved charities including Army Emergency Relief, Children's Aid Society, City Meals on Wheels, Give an Hour, Martha's Table, United Negro College Fund, United Way of National Capital Area, and US Holocaust Memorial Museum

## What the settlement established (Tier 1)

- Trump personally misused charitable foundation assets
- Foundation directors breached fiduciary duties
- Coordinated illegally with the 2016 Trump campaign
- These admissions are part of the settlement record and are NOT subject to subsequent appellate reversal

## Significance

The Trump Foundation case is notable for being:
- A pre-presidency civil action that produced documented admissions
- One of the few legal proceedings in which Trump's adult children faced personal restrictions
- A precursor pattern to the later [[NY AG v Trump Organization Civil Fraud 2024]] case
