# NY AG v Trump Organization (Civil Fraud 2024)

New York Attorney General [[Letitia James]] civil action against [[Donald Trump]], his adult sons [[Donald Trump Jr]] and [[Eric Trump]], former Trump Organization CFO [[Allen Weisselberg]], former Trump Organization Controller Jeffrey McConney, the Trump Organization, and related entities. Filed September 21, 2022 in New York Supreme Court. **Trial verdict February 16, 2024 by Judge [[Arthur Engoron]]**: defendants found liable on all causes; ordered to pay $354,868,768 in disgorgement, plus pre-judgment interest bringing total to approximately $454M as of judgment; corporate dissolution and independent monitor imposition stayed pending appeal.

## Charges / claims

Filed under NY Executive Law § 63(12), which gives the AG civil authority to obtain injunctions and disgorgement for "repeated fraudulent or illegal acts" or "persistent fraud or illegality" in the conduct of business in New York. Seven causes of action:

1. Persistent and repeated fraud in violation of NY Exec. Law § 63(12)
2. Falsifying business records (Penal Law § 175.05)
3. Conspiracy to falsify business records (§ 105.05)
4. Issuing false financial statements (§ 175.45)
5. Conspiracy to issue false financial statements (§ 105.05)
6. Insurance fraud (§ 176.05)
7. Conspiracy to commit insurance fraud (§ 105.05)

## Court and parties

- **Court**: New York Supreme Court, New York County
- **Judge**: [[Arthur Engoron]] (bench trial; no jury -- equity action)
- **Plaintiff**: NY State (Letitia James, AG)
- **Defendants**: Donald Trump; Donald Trump Jr; Eric Trump; Trump Organization; Allen Weisselberg; Jeffrey McConney; various Trump entities

(Ivanka Trump was originally a defendant; dismissed by appellate court as outside statute of limitations.)

## Allegations (Tier 1 per filings)

The AG alleged the defendants engaged in a decade-long scheme to fraudulently inflate the value of Trump Organization properties in Statements of Financial Condition (SFCs) submitted to:
- Banks (to obtain loans on favorable terms)
- Insurers (to obtain coverage and favorable premiums)
- Tax authorities (in some instances)

Key valuation distortions documented in the trial record:

- **Mar-a-Lago**: valued at $612M-$739M (treating as residential / development potential) vs ~$18-28M tax-assessed value (treating as a club with deed restrictions). Trial finding: substantial overvaluation.
- **Triplex apartment at Trump Tower**: valued at $327M based on stated square footage of 30,000 sq ft when actual square footage was approximately 10,996 sq ft (a triple overstatement)
- **40 Wall Street**: inflated based on undisclosed lender-imposed restrictions
- **Trump golf clubs**: valued based on hypothetical development potential ignoring legal restrictions
- **Seven Springs estate**: valued at $291M based on assumed development of mansions that lacked permits

## Trial proceedings

- **Pre-trial summary judgment ruling (September 26, 2023)**: Engoron granted partial summary judgment on Cause of Action 1, finding the financial statements were fraudulent as a matter of law; trial proceeded on remaining causes and on damages.
- **Trial**: October 2, 2023 - January 11, 2024. 11 weeks. Bench trial.
- **Verdict**: February 16, 2024.

## Verdict and remedies (Tier 1)

Engoron's 92-page decision found:
- All defendants liable on all causes
- Disgorgement of $364,868,768 in ill-gotten gains
- (Reduced from prosecution-requested amount)
- Trump personal liability: $354,868,768
- Donald Trump Jr and Eric Trump: $4,013,024 each
- Allen Weisselberg: $1,000,000
- Pre-judgment interest at 9% per annum

**Additional remedies** (subsequently stayed pending appeal):
- Trump barred from serving as officer or director of any NY corporation for 3 years
- Donald Trump Jr and Eric Trump barred from same for 2 years
- Trump barred from applying for loans from NY-chartered financial institutions for 3 years
- Independent monitor (Barbara Jones) continued; independent compliance director added
- Trump Organization Inc and affiliated entities ordered dissolved (stayed by appellate court)

## Appeal

- Trump filed appeal; required to post bond to stay enforcement
- NY appellate court reduced required bond from $464M to $175M (Trump posted via Knight Specialty Insurance)
- Oral argument before NY Appellate Division First Department September 26, 2024
- Decision pending as of 2026-05

## What the verdict established (Tier 1)

- Court finding of persistent fraud by Trump and Trump Organization
- Quantified $354M+ in ill-gotten gains attributable to Trump personally
- Bench trial findings on inflated valuations across multiple properties
- Pattern across 10+ years

## What is contested

- Trump's appeals raise procedural and substantive challenges including the "no victim" theory (banks and insurers fully repaid, didn't allege harm) and the magnitude of remedy
- The full corporate dissolution remedy remains stayed
- Final appellate disposition pending
