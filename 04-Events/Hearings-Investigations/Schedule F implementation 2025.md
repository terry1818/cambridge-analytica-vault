# Schedule F implementation (2025-)

Second-term Trump-administration execution of [[Schedule F]] policy reclassifying policy-influencing federal employees from career civil service to at-will political appointment.

## Day 1 executive order

- January 20, 2025: Trump signed "Restoring Accountability To Policy-Influencing Positions Within the Federal Workforce"
- Renamed Schedule F to "Schedule Policy/Career" (Schedule P-C)
- Required agencies to identify positions for reclassification

## Implementation status (latest reporting)

- OPM under Charles Ezell issued interim rule April 2025
- Multiple agencies submitted lists - estimates range from 50,000 to 200,000 positions potentially reclassified
- The exact count is disputed; the larger figure includes positions that exercise any "discretionary authority"

## Litigation

- National Treasury Employees Union v. Trump (DC Circuit) pending
- AFGE v. OPM pending
- Lawsuits argue Schedule P-C violates the Civil Service Reform Act and Administrative Procedure Act
- Administration's response: civil service protections are policy not statutory and may be modified by executive order

## Documented terminations to date

- Approximately 250,000 federal employees separated from service since Jan 20, 2025 across all mechanisms (probationary terminations, deferred resignation offers, RIFs, Schedule P-C reclassifications)
- Schedule P-C-specific firings are still in the early phase as of latest reporting; mass dismissals expected once reclassification lists are finalized

## Significance

Schedule F implementation is the central instrument of the Project 2025 administrative-state restructuring agenda. The substantive question is whether the federal civil service as a professional non-political institution survives the term.
