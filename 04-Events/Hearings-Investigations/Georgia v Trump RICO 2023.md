# Georgia v Trump (RICO 2023)

Fulton County, Georgia District Attorney [[Fani Willis]] prosecution of [[Donald Trump]] and 18 co-defendants on Georgia RICO and 12 additional state-law counts, alleging a multi-state conspiracy to overturn the 2020 presidential election in Georgia. Indicted by Fulton County grand jury August 14, 2023. **Willis disqualified December 19, 2024** by Georgia Court of Appeals due to romantic relationship with appointed special prosecutor Nathan Wade. Case reassignment to a different prosecuting authority pending; trial timing remains uncertain.

## Charges

41 counts across the indictment. Trump faced 13 counts:
- 1 count of violation of the Georgia RICO Act (OCGA Â§ 16-14-4(b))
- 3 counts of solicitation of violation of oath by public officer
- 2 counts of conspiracy to commit impersonating a public officer
- 1 count of conspiracy to commit forgery in the first degree
- 1 count of conspiracy to commit false statements
- 2 counts of conspiracy to commit filing false documents
- 1 count of filing false documents
- 2 counts of false statements

The RICO charge encompassed an "enterprise" theory under Georgia state law, with 161 predicate acts spanning multiple states.

## Allegations (Tier 1 per indictment)

The indictment alleged the enterprise pursued five categories of conduct:

### 1. False statements to Georgia state legislators
- Giuliani December 2020 testimony to Georgia legislative committees containing false election claims
- Coordinated effort to pressure legislators to refuse to certify electoral results

### 2. Pressure on Georgia state officials
- Trump's January 2, 2021 call to Secretary of State Brad Raffensperger asking him to "find 11,780 votes"
- Multiple calls and meetings with Governor Brian Kemp's office, Raffensperger, and other state officials

### 3. False Georgia elector scheme
- 16 "alternate" Trump electors met December 14, 2020 at the Georgia State Capitol and signed false certificates claiming to be Georgia's electors

### 4. Coffee County voting system breach
- Coordinated access to Coffee County election equipment January 7, 2021
- Forensic data and software copied without authorization
- Involved Misty Hampton (Coffee County elections supervisor), Cathy Latham, and others

### 5. Harassment of election workers
- Direct targeting of Fulton County election workers Ruby Freeman and Shaye Moss
- Trevian Kutti (associate of Kanye West) sent to pressure Freeman in her home January 4, 2021

## Co-defendant pleas (Tier 1)

Four co-defendants pled guilty in October-November 2023, agreeing to testify against remaining defendants:

- **Kenneth Chesebro**: pled guilty October 20, 2023 to one felony count of conspiracy to commit filing false documents. Five years probation.
- **Sidney Powell**: pled guilty October 19, 2023 to six misdemeanor counts. Six years probation. Agreed to testify and provide documents.
- **Jenna Ellis**: pled guilty October 24, 2023 to one felony count of aiding and abetting false statements. Five years probation.
- **Scott Hall**: pled guilty September 29, 2023 to five misdemeanor counts. Five years probation.

## Willis disqualification (Tier 1)

In January 2024, defendant Mike Roman filed a motion alleging Willis had been in a romantic relationship with Nathan Wade, whom she had appointed as Special Prosecutor in November 2021. Wade had been paid over $650,000 in legal fees. The motion alleged the relationship created a conflict of interest.

- Evidentiary hearings February-March 2024
- Judge Scott McAfee ruled March 15, 2024 that Willis could continue if Wade withdrew. Wade resigned same day.
- Defendants appealed to Georgia Court of Appeals
- **December 19, 2024**: Georgia Court of Appeals reversed McAfee, holding that the "appearance of impropriety" required disqualification of Willis and her office.
- Case reassignment to a different Georgia prosecuting authority (Prosecuting Attorneys' Council of Georgia, PACGA) pending as of 2025-2026.

## Trump-specific evidence (Tier 1)

The Raffensperger call transcript is direct evidence of Trump's personal involvement in the pressure campaign. The call lasted over an hour and includes Trump's request to "find" a specific number of votes. Full text available at [[Source: FullText Trump Raffensperger call 2021-01-02]].

Additional Trump evidence in the indictment includes:
- Calls and meetings with Georgia officials
- Trump-directed dispatch of Giuliani and others to Georgia
- Trump's coordination with the alternate electors scheme

## Status as of 2026

- Trump-specific charges suspended pending PACGA reassignment
- Co-defendant pleas remain in effect
- Federal supremacy issues (Trump as sitting president) likely to affect timing and substance of any reactivation

## What the case has established to date

- 4 co-defendant pleas including admissions of conduct in furtherance of the enterprise
- Substantial documentary record assembled
- Grand jury finding of probable cause on 41 counts across 19 defendants

## What is unresolved

- Whether the Trump-specific charges will be pursued by the reassigned prosecuting authority
- Whether Trump can be tried while a sitting president
- Disposition of remaining 15 defendants
