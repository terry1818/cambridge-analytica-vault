# Baker v. Carr (1962)

Foundational US Supreme Court decision holding that federal courts could hear challenges to legislative-redistricting questions ("one person, one vote" doctrine). Decided 1962.

## Vault relevance

Baker is the canonical case establishing federal-court oversight of redistricting fairness. It is the doctrinal foundation that contemporary gerrymandering litigation (e.g. Rucho v. Common Cause) builds on - and that the [[Federalist Society]]-influenced majority has substantially narrowed.

The decision's framing ("political-question doctrine") is one of the theoretical battlegrounds in the structural-capture analysis: when federal courts treat redistricting as nonjusticiable, they effectively delegate structural power to state legislatures that the contemporary minoritarian coalition disproportionately controls.
