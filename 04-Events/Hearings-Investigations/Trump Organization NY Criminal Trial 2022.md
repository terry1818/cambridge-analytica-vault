# Trump Organization NY Criminal Trial 2022

Manhattan District Attorney's Office criminal prosecution of two Trump Organization corporate entities (Trump Corporation and Trump Payroll Corp) for a 17-year tax fraud and falsifying business records scheme. Indicted July 1, 2021. **Corporate entities convicted on all 17 counts December 6, 2022** by New York Supreme Court jury. Trump Organization CFO [[Allen Weisselberg]] pled guilty separately August 18, 2022 and served 100 days at Rikers Island.

## Charges

- 17 felony counts against Trump Corporation and Trump Payroll Corp jointly:
  - 9 counts grand larceny (NY Penal Law § 155)
  - 5 counts scheme to defraud (§ 190.65)
  - 1 count falsifying business records (§ 175.10)
  - 2 counts criminal tax fraud (NY Tax Law)
  - 1 conspiracy

Individual charges:
- **Allen Weisselberg**: 15 felony counts (pled guilty August 18, 2022)
- Original individual co-defendants: tax-related counts

## Court and parties

- **Court**: New York Supreme Court, New York County
- **Judge**: [[Juan Merchan]] (same judge who later presided over People v Trump hush-money case)
- **Prosecution**: Manhattan DA's Office under [[Cyrus Vance Jr]] (during indictment) and [[Alvin Bragg]] (during trial)
- **Defense**: Trump Organization counsel Susan Necheles; Weisselberg individual counsel

## Allegations and scheme

Per the indictment and trial evidence, from 2005 through 2018 the Trump Organization paid Weisselberg and other senior executives substantial undisclosed compensation off the books:

- Free apartment on Riverside Boulevard ($100K+/yr value)
- Free parking spaces
- Private school tuition for grandchildren ($350K)
- Mercedes leases for Weisselberg and his wife
- Cash payments treated as Christmas bonuses
- Other in-kind benefits

These items were not reported on W-2 forms or paystubs, allowing Weisselberg and others to avoid income tax on the compensation. The scheme also reduced the Trump Organization's payroll tax obligations.

The total tax loss to NY state and city authorities was approximately $1.7M to Weisselberg alone over the period; broader scheme losses substantially larger.

## Weisselberg guilty plea (August 18, 2022)

Weisselberg pled guilty to all 15 charges against him personally, including:
- Grand larceny in the second degree
- Tax fraud
- Falsifying business records
- Conspiracy

Sentence: 5 months at Rikers Island; agreed to testify against the Trump Organization entities at trial. Served approximately 100 days.

Weisselberg subsequently testified at the Trump Organization trial as a prosecution witness; later was charged separately with perjury related to the NY civil fraud case testimony.

## Trial proceedings (Trump Organization entities)

- Trial: October 24 - December 6, 2022
- 13 days of testimony
- Weisselberg testified as the prosecution's main witness -- including testimony that Trump personally knew about and approved the practice
- Defense argument: Weisselberg acted for personal benefit, not corporate benefit; corporate entities not vicariously liable

## Verdict (December 6, 2022)

- Trump Corporation: guilty on 8 of 9 counts charged
- Trump Payroll Corp: guilty on 8 of 9 counts charged
- Sentence (January 13, 2023): $1.61M fine -- the maximum allowed under NY criminal law (which was substantially less than the disgorgement available in the parallel civil fraud action)

## What the verdict established (Tier 1)

- Two Trump-owned corporate entities convicted of multiple felonies including grand larceny and tax fraud
- Weisselberg's plea and trial testimony established the operational facts of the scheme
- Trial evidence established Trump personal knowledge of compensation practices, though Trump was not personally charged in this case

## What this case did NOT establish

- Personal criminal liability of Donald Trump (deliberately not charged in this case)
- The full scope of the off-books compensation scheme (limited to specific executives and time period)
