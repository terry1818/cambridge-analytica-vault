# Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE)

Executive-branch initiative announced November 12, 2024 to "modernize federal technology and software to maximize governmental efficiency and productivity." Originally co-led by [[Elon Musk]] and [[Vivek Ramaswamy]]; Ramaswamy departed January 20, 2025 to run for Ohio Governor.

## Structural status

- Not a statutory department - "DOGE" is the executive-order rebrand of the US Digital Service combined with US DOGE Service Temporary Organization
- Lives under the executive office of the President
- Personnel obtain detailee status from agencies

## Tier 1 - documented activity (through latest reporting)

- Mass-firings via "Fork in the Road" deferred resignation offer (Jan 28, 2025)
- Probationary employee terminations across multiple agencies (Feb 2025)
- USAID effective shutdown (Feb-March 2025)
- CFPB substantially shuttered
- Education Department reduction-in-force
- DOGE personnel obtained access to Treasury Bureau of Fiscal Service payment systems (subject to multiple lawsuits)

## Tier 1 - litigation outcomes

- Multiple district court injunctions partially restored fired probationary employees
- Supreme Court rulings (multiple emergency stays) allowed administration to proceed with workforce reductions
- USAID employee class actions ongoing
- Multiple cases pending on the legality of DOGE access to Treasury, OPM, SSA, and CMS systems

## Tier 2 - claimed savings vs documented

- DOGE's own "wall of receipts" website has been repeatedly fact-checked finding double-counting, inflated valuations, and falsely claimed savings
- Public reporting (multiple outlets) has documented systematic overstatement of dollar amounts
- Actual federal-spending impact remains contested; the reduction in headcount is unambiguous

## Musk departure

- May 2025: Musk announced departure from DOGE day-to-day role
- Successor: Steve Davis (Musk's longtime operational lieutenant)
