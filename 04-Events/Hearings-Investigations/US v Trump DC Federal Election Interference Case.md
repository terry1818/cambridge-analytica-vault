# US v Trump (DC Federal Election Interference Case)

US Department of Justice Special Counsel [[Jack Smith]] prosecution of [[Donald Trump]] in the US District Court for the District of Columbia, Case No. 1:23-cr-00257-TSC. Original indictment August 1, 2023. **Superseding indictment August 27, 2024** narrowed allegations following the Supreme Court's *Trump v. United States* ruling on presidential immunity. **Dismissed without prejudice November 25, 2024** by DOJ motion under the policy of not prosecuting a sitting president, following Trump's November election.

## Charges (original indictment August 2023)

Four counts:
1. **Conspiracy to defraud the United States** (18 USC § 371) -- conspiracy with co-conspirators to use dishonesty, fraud, and deceit to impair the function of the federal government in collecting, counting, and certifying the results of the 2020 presidential election
2. **Conspiracy to obstruct an official proceeding** (§ 1512(k)) -- the January 6 joint session of Congress
3. **Obstruction of, and attempt to obstruct, an official proceeding** (§ 1512(c)(2))
4. **Conspiracy against rights** (§ 241) -- conspiracy to interfere with the right of millions of Americans to have their votes counted

Six unindicted co-conspirators (identified by description):
- CC1 = [[Rudy Giuliani]]
- CC2 = [[John Eastman]]
- CC3 = [[Sidney Powell]]
- CC4 = [[Jeffrey Clark]]
- CC5 = [[Kenneth Chesebro]]
- CC6 = [[Boris Epshteyn]] (publicly identified by later reporting)

## Pre-trial procedural history (Tier 1)

- **August 1, 2023**: Indictment unsealed
- **December 13, 2023**: Trial date initially set for March 4, 2024 by Judge [[Tanya Chutkan]]
- **December-January**: Trump moved to dismiss on immunity grounds; denied by Chutkan; Trump appealed
- **February-April 2024**: DC Circuit and then SCOTUS heard immunity question; trial date vacated
- **July 1, 2024**: Supreme Court issued *Trump v. United States* (6-3) holding that former presidents have absolute immunity for "core" presidential functions and presumptive immunity for other "official acts," with no immunity for unofficial acts
- **August 27, 2024**: Smith filed superseding indictment narrowing factual allegations to align with the immunity ruling -- removed allegations involving Trump's interactions with DOJ officials (deemed "core" official function)
- **September-October 2024**: pre-trial litigation continued
- **November 5, 2024**: Trump won presidential election
- **November 25, 2024**: DOJ moved to dismiss without prejudice
- **November 25, 2024**: Chutkan granted dismissal

## Key allegations (per superseding indictment August 2024)

The indictment alleged Trump and his co-conspirators pursued a multi-pronged scheme to overturn the legitimate election results:

### 1. False elector schemes in seven states
Coordination of "alternate" or "contingent" elector slates in Arizona, Georgia, Michigan, Nevada, New Mexico, Pennsylvania, Wisconsin, designed to create a pretext for Vice President Pence to reject or delay certification on January 6.

### 2. Pressure on state officials
- January 2, 2021 Trump-[[Brad Raffensperger]] call: "I just want to find 11,780 votes" (full text exists; see [[Source: FullText Trump Raffensperger call 2021-01-02]])
- Pressure on Arizona governor Doug Ducey
- Pressure on Michigan officials
- Pressure on Pennsylvania officials

### 3. Pressure on Vice President Pence
- Multiple meetings and calls in late December 2020 and January 2021 to pressure VP Pence to reject electoral certifications on January 6
- Eastman's January 6 memo proposing Pence reject or delay

### 4. January 6 conduct
- The Ellipse rally speech directing supporters to march to the Capitol
- Knowing the crowd's potential for violence
- Failure to call off the attack for 187 minutes

## Key evidence (in indictment and Smith's public filings)

### Tier 1 -- primary documents
- The Eastman January 6 memos (privileged-then-disclosed under crime-fraud exception)
- The Trump-Raffensperger call transcript and audio
- The Trump-Pence calls timeline
- The Ellipse speech transcript
- Trump's Twitter and Truth Social posts during the January 6 attack
- White House Visitor logs
- White House call records

### Tier 1 -- witness testimony obtained pre-trial
- VP Pence's chief of staff Marc Short
- VP Pence's counsel Greg Jacob (extensive grand jury testimony)
- Trump White House counsel Pat Cipollone (grand jury after privilege challenges)
- Multiple Trump campaign and White House aides

### Tier 1 -- Smith's August 2024 evidentiary appendix
Smith filed a 165-page evidentiary appendix October 2, 2024 (over Trump objection) detailing the evidence the prosecution would have presented at trial including witness summaries.

## What the indictment established but never went to trial

- Probable cause finding by grand jury supporting four felony counts
- Substantial documentary evidence
- 100+ pre-trial witness interviews/grand jury testimony
- No fact-finding trial; no jury verdict on the merits

## What is unresolved by the dismissal

- Whether a jury would have convicted
- The full liability of the named co-conspirators (Georgia case parallel)
- Whether the immunity ruling would have substantially constrained the case at trial
