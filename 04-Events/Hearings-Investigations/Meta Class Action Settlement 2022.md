# Meta Class Action Settlement 2022

## Summary

On **December 22, 2022**, [[Meta Platforms]] (formerly Facebook) agreed to pay **$725 million** to settle a US class-action lawsuit alleging the company had improperly shared user information with [[Cambridge Analytica]]. Filed in US District Court for the Northern District of California. Single largest data-privacy class action settlement at the time.

## Settlement terms

- **$725 million** total settlement amount
- Resolved consolidated claims from US Facebook users whose data was shared with CA
- **No admission of wrongdoing** by Meta
- Per-claimant payouts: relatively small individual amounts (varies by court-approved distribution); much of the fund went to attorneys' fees and administration
- Approved by court May 2024 after extended approval process

## Significance

The $725M settlement is the **largest single CA-related corporate financial consequence** and the most comprehensive financial accounting of the harm to US users. Combined with other CA-related Facebook/Meta liabilities:
