# People v Trump (NY Hush-Money Trial, 2024)

New York State criminal trial. People of the State of New York v. Donald J. Trump.

## Indictment (Tier 1)

- March 30, 2023: [[Alvin Bragg]] indicted Trump on 34 felony counts of falsifying business records in the first degree
- Each count related to entries concealing the [[Stormy Daniels]] hush-money payment as legal expenses
- The felony-elevation depended on proving the records were falsified to commit/conceal another crime (federal campaign finance violations)

## Trial

- April 15 - May 30, 2024 before Judge [[Juan Merchan]]
- Star witnesses: [[Michael Cohen]] (executed the payment), [[David Pecker]] (catch-and-kill arrangement), [[Stormy Daniels]] (recipient)
- Trump did not testify

## Verdict and sentencing

- May 30, 2024: Jury found Trump GUILTY on all 34 counts - first former US president convicted of a felony
- January 10, 2025: Merchan sentenced Trump to unconditional discharge (no jail, no fine, no probation) due to imminent return to presidency

## Significance

The only Trump criminal case to reach a jury verdict. Established that state-criminal accountability against a sitting/imminent president has functional limits even where the criminal case is fully prosecuted.
