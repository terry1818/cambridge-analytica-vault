# "Fork in the Road" - Deferred Resignation Offer (January 2025)

Government-wide email sent January 28, 2025 by the Office of Personnel Management offering federal employees an 8-month paid leave in exchange for resignation effective September 30, 2025.

## Mechanics

- Subject line "Fork in the Road" - same as the email Musk sent Twitter employees in November 2022 when he took over
- Offered approximately 2 million federal employees the option to resign with continued pay through September 30
- Original deadline February 6, 2025; extended after court intervention to February 12

## Legal questions

- The deferred-resignation mechanism has no clear statutory basis - federal employees lack the kind of severance authority OPM purported to offer
- AFGE and NTEU lawsuits challenged the legitimacy of the offer
- District court Judge George O'Toole (D-Mass) imposed a stay on enforcement; ultimately allowed offer to proceed after finding plaintiffs lacked standing

## Uptake

- ~75,000 federal employees accepted (administration figures)
- Concentrated in particular agencies including USAID, CFPB, Department of Education

## Subsequent waves

- "Fork in the Road 2" issued April 2025 to targeted agencies
- DRP-style offers extended through summer 2025

## Significance

Fork in the Road was the first large-scale instrument of the [[DOGE]] / [[Schedule F]] workforce-reduction agenda. It served the structural goal of separating large numbers of career employees before the slower Schedule F litigation could be resolved.
