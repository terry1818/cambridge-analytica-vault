# Enabling Act of 1933 (Germany)

German statute passed March 23, 1933 giving Adolf Hitler's Cabinet authority to enact laws without Reichstag participation. The legal mechanism by which Weimar democracy was formally ended.

## Vault relevance

The Enabling Act is the canonical historical reference for legal-structural authoritarian consolidation. The contemporary debate about whether [[Schedule F]] / [[Project 2025]] / executive-orders-replacing-legislation in the 2025+ Trump administration constitute an analogous structural shift draws repeatedly on Enabling Act framing.

The vault treats these comparisons carefully - the Enabling Act precedent is historically informative but the institutional contexts differ substantially.
