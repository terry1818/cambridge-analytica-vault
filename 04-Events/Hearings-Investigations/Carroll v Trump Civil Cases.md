# Carroll v Trump (Civil Cases)

Two federal civil suits by writer [[E. Jean Carroll]] against [[Donald Trump]] in the Southern District of New York, both tried before Judge [[Lewis Kaplan]]. **Carroll II** (filed November 2022 under the NY Adult Survivors Act) tried May 2023: jury found Trump liable for sexual abuse of Carroll in approximately 1996 and for defamation in his October 2022 statements denying the assault and disparaging her; awarded Carroll $5,000,000 in compensatory and punitive damages. **Carroll I** (filed November 2019 originally, defamation only) tried January 2024 on damages alone after liability had been established: jury awarded Carroll $83,300,000 ($18.3M compensatory + $65M punitive).

These are the **only judicial findings of sexual-misconduct liability against a former US President** in American history.

## Carroll II (filed 2022; tried May 2023)

### Filing and statute

E. Jean Carroll filed November 24, 2022 under the New York Adult Survivors Act (CPLR § 214-j), which opened a one-year lookback window allowing civil suits for sexual abuse committed beyond the normal statute of limitations.

### Claims

1. **Battery** (sexual assault, including rape under NY Penal Law as alleged)
2. **Defamation** (Trump's October 12, 2022 statement on Truth Social denying the assault and characterizing Carroll's allegations as a "Hoax")

### Trial proceedings

- Trial: April 25 - May 9, 2023
- Trump did not appear or testify in person
- Trump's pre-trial videotaped deposition was played for the jury, including portions in which Trump was shown a photograph and misidentified Carroll as Marla Maples (his second wife)
- Carroll testified, as did two friends she told contemporaneously (in 1995-1996) and other corroborating witnesses
- Two other women testified about prior assaults by Trump as Rule 415 evidence: [[Jessica Leeds]] (1979 first-class flight assault) and [[Natasha Stoynoff]] (2005 Mar-a-Lago assault)
- Audio of the Access Hollywood tape played

### Verdict (May 9, 2023)

The jury:
- Found Trump liable for sexual abuse (battery) -- finding rape under the technical NY Penal Law definition NOT proven by preponderance, but sexual abuse (digital penetration without consent) WAS proven
- Found Trump liable for defamation of Carroll
- Awarded Carroll $2,020,000 in compensatory damages and $20,000 in punitive damages for the battery
- Awarded Carroll $2,700,000 in compensatory damages and $280,000 in punitive damages for the defamation
- **Total: $5,000,000**

### Judge Kaplan's clarification

Following the verdict, Judge Kaplan issued an opinion clarifying that the jury's finding meant Trump "in fact did rape Ms. Carroll" as that word is commonly understood, even though the specific NY Penal Law definition of "rape" (requiring penile penetration) was not met. This clarification was issued July 2023 in response to Trump statements about the verdict.

## Carroll I (filed 2019; tried January 2024)

### Filing

E. Jean Carroll filed November 4, 2019 in New York Supreme Court (later removed to SDNY) for defamation arising from Trump's June 2019 statements as President denying the assault and stating he had never met Carroll.

### Procedural history

- Federal government (DOJ) substituted itself as defendant in 2020 under the Westfall Act (claim that Trump's statements were within scope of employment as President); this defense was eventually rejected on appeal
- The case was effectively suspended for the duration; Westfall Act issue resolved 2023
- Following Carroll II verdict (May 2023), liability was effectively established by issue preclusion
- Trial January 16-26, 2024 on damages only

### Trial proceedings

- Judge Kaplan instructed jury that liability (defamation and underlying sexual abuse) was already established by the Carroll II verdict
- Trial focused on damages from Trump's 2019 statements
- Trump did appear at trial; testified briefly (heavily restricted by Kaplan due to prior rulings)

### Verdict (January 26, 2024)

Jury awarded Carroll:
- $7,300,000 in compensatory damages for damage to reputation
- $11,000,000 in compensatory damages for reputation repair
- $65,000,000 in punitive damages
- **Total: $83,300,000**

## Appeals

- Trump appealed both verdicts
- December 30, 2024: Second Circuit affirmed Carroll II verdict
- Carroll I appeal pending as of 2026-05

## What the verdicts established (Tier 1)

- Two separate juries found Trump liable for sexual misconduct against Carroll
- Two separate juries found Trump's denials defamatory
- Carroll II jury finding includes a court-clarified finding equivalent to "raped Ms. Carroll" in common usage
- $88.3M total in compensatory and punitive damages

## What the verdicts do NOT establish

- Criminal liability (these are civil findings, preponderance of evidence)
- Other sexual misconduct allegations by other accusers (not adjudicated in these cases)
