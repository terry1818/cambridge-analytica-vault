# Trump v. United States (2024)

US Supreme Court decision, July 1, 2024. 6-3 ruling on the scope of presidential immunity from criminal prosecution.

## Holding

- Presidents have absolute immunity for "core" constitutional duties
- Presumptive immunity for other official acts
- No immunity for unofficial acts
- The classification of conduct as official vs. unofficial is itself a question with significant doctrinal uncertainty

## Vault relevance

The ruling substantially limited the scope of conduct chargeable in [[Jack Smith]]'s [[Trump January 6 Federal Case]]. Combined with Trump's November 2024 election win, it produced the effective termination of all federal Trump criminal cases.

Chief Justice Roberts wrote the majority. Sotomayor's dissent argued the ruling "makes a mockery of the principle, foundational to our Constitution and system of Government, that no man is above the law."
