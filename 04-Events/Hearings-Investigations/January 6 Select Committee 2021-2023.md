# January 6 Select Committee (2021-2023)

The US House Select Committee to Investigate the January 6th Attack on the United States Capitol. Established by H. Res. 503 (June 30, 2021) following Senate Republican filibuster of a proposed bipartisan independent commission. Operated July 2021 through January 2023 (118th Congress). Conducted approximately 1,000 witness interviews, 10 nationally televised public hearings, and released an 845-page final report on December 22, 2022. Made formal criminal referrals to the DOJ against [[Donald Trump]] and others. The factual record assembled by the Committee became foundational evidence for subsequent prosecutions.

## Mandate and procedural history

H. Res. 503 authorized investigation of "the facts, circumstances, and causes" of the January 6, 2021 attack. The committee was empowered to issue subpoenas, take testimony under oath, and recommend legislation and other action.

McCarthy withdrew his five Republican picks (including Jim Jordan and Jim Banks) after Pelosi rejected two of them; Republican leadership announced boycott. Cheney and Kinzinger served as Republican members despite leadership opposition.

## Investigation scope and methods

- **1,000+ witness interviews/depositions**, including senior Trump administration officials
- Document subpoenas to the National Archives (Trump records); Trump's effort to block their release rejected by SCOTUS January 19, 2022 (Trump v Thompson)
- White House Visitor logs, call records, phone records
- Surveillance video, body-cam footage, communications records
- Social media records (including from Twitter, Facebook, Parler)
- Financial records related to rally funding

## Public hearings (June-October 2022)

10 public hearings presented organized evidence:

1. **June 9** (prime time): overview; Capitol Officer Caroline Edwards testimony; Bill Stepien testimony (taped)
2. **June 13**: false-election-fraud claims; "Big Lie"
3. **June 16**: pressure on VP Pence; Greg Jacob and Michael Luttig testimony
4. **June 21**: pressure on state officials; Brad Raffensperger, Rusty Bowers (AZ), Shaye Moss testimony
5. **June 23**: pressure on DOJ; Jeff Rosen, Richard Donoghue, Steven Engel testimony
6. **June 28** (surprise hearing): [[Cassidy Hutchinson]] testimony -- Meadows aide; testified to Trump's knowledge of armed crowd, struggle in the presidential SUV, and other accounts
7. **July 12**: connections to extremist groups (Proud Boys, Oath Keepers)
8. **July 21** (prime time): Trump's 187 minutes of inaction
9. **October 13**: synthesis; vote to subpoena Trump
10. (Plus several earlier hearings in 2021)

## Key documented findings (Tier 1)

### Trump knew claims were false
- Multiple senior administration officials testified Trump was told repeatedly by his own DOJ, campaign team, and White House counsel that election fraud claims lacked merit
- Bill Barr told Trump the DOJ found no fraud; Trump fired Barr days later
- Trump campaign data analysis showed no path to victory; Trump nevertheless persisted

### Multi-pronged scheme
- False elector scheme in 7 states
- Pressure on VP Pence to reject certifications
- Pressure on state officials (Raffensperger, etc.)
- Pressure on DOJ to issue letter falsely declaring election compromised (Jeffrey Clark draft)
- Pressure on Congress
- Calling supporters to DC; promoting "wild" protest

### Trump's January 6 conduct
- Tweets attacking VP Pence at 2:24 PM during attack
- 187 minutes of inaction despite repeated pleas from staff, family, and allies
- Reported physical struggle in presidential SUV when Secret Service refused to drive to Capitol (Hutchinson testimony, contested by some Secret Service personnel)

### Cassidy Hutchinson testimony (June 28, 2022)
Mark Meadows's senior aide Hutchinson testified:
- Trump knew before the Ellipse speech that the crowd was armed; demanded magnetometers be removed so "his people" could enter
- Trump physically struggled with security to get to the Capitol
- Pat Cipollone (White House Counsel) had said "We're going to get charged with every crime imaginable" if Trump went to Capitol
- Meadows had said in private "He thinks Mike deserves it" regarding chants to hang Pence

## Final report (December 22, 2022)

845-page final report ([[Source: J6 Committee Final Report 2022]]) presenting:
- Eight chapters of findings
- Detailed evidence appendix
- Witness transcripts (1,000+ partial or full)
- Recommendation: legislation to clarify Electoral Count Act
- **Criminal referrals to DOJ** against:
  - Donald Trump (obstruction of official proceeding, conspiracy to defraud US, conspiracy to make false statement, insurrection)
  - [[John Eastman]] (obstruction, conspiracy)
  - And others by implication

## Subsequent legislative outcome

- **Electoral Count Reform Act of 2022**: bipartisan legislation passed December 2022 clarifying VP's role as ministerial; raising threshold for objections to electoral votes

## Subsequent litigation flowing from committee record

- The Committee's documentary and testimonial record became evidence in [[US v Trump DC Federal Election Interference Case]]
- Specific witness testimony became key to Smith's case
- Trump-aligned figures' contempt of Congress for refusing to comply led to separate prosecutions:
  - [[Steve Bannon]]: convicted contempt of Congress; served 4 months
  - [[Peter Navarro]]: convicted contempt of Congress; served 4 months

## What the Committee established

- A factual record of Trump's central, knowing role in the multi-pronged effort to overturn the 2020 election
- Trump knew his fraud claims were false yet continued to make them
- Trump approved or directed each prong of the scheme
- Trump's January 6 conduct was deliberate and prolonged

## What the Committee did NOT do

- Did not have prosecutorial power; could only refer
- Did not address every aspect of the broader scheme (some elements developed in DOJ investigation)
