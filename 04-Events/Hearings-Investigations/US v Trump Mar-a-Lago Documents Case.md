# US v Trump (Mar-a-Lago Documents Case)

US Department of Justice Special Counsel [[Jack Smith]] prosecution of [[Donald Trump]] in the Southern District of Florida for retention of national-defense information after leaving the presidency, and for obstruction of the FBI investigation into that retention. Indicted June 8, 2023. Superseding indictment July 27, 2023, added co-defendant [[Carlos De Oliveira]] and additional obstruction counts. **Dismissed by US District Judge [[Aileen Cannon]] on July 15, 2024**, on a finding that Smith's appointment as Special Counsel was unconstitutional under the Appointments Clause. On appeal at the 11th Circuit when the case became moot upon Trump's election to a second term and DOJ withdrew the appeal per DOJ policy on indictment of a sitting president.

## Documents at issue (Tier 1)

The retained documents included materials marked at the highest classification levels â€” TOP SECRET / Sensitive Compartmented Information (TS/SCI), including Special Access Program (SAP) materials. Per the indictment, the documents covered subjects including:
- US military capabilities and weaknesses
- US nuclear programs
- Potential foreign attack vulnerabilities of the US
- Plans for possible retaliation against foreign attack

Documents recovered:
- January 2022 NARA initial retrieval: 15 boxes containing classified documents intermixed with unclassified materials
- May-June 2022 grand jury subpoena and Trump attorney Evan Corcoran's search: limited additional documents produced
- **August 8, 2022 FBI search of Mar-a-Lago**: recovered 102 additional classified documents that had not been turned over despite subpoena and certification of full production

## Key evidence (Tier 1 â€” included in indictment)

### Documentary
- Photographs of classified-marked documents stored in cardboard boxes in Mar-a-Lago storage room, bathroom, and ballroom
- The "Bedminster recording" (July 21, 2021): audio of Trump showing visitors what he described as a classified Iran attack plan, stating "as president I could have declassified it. Now I can't, you know, but this is still a secret"
- Trump Organization storage room logs

- Multiple Trump employees and Trump Organization staff including the laundry-room staff member who observed document boxes being moved

### Co-conspirator conduct
- Walt Nauta moved boxes of classified documents within Mar-a-Lago after subpoena
- Carlos De Oliveira (Mar-a-Lago property manager) discussed deletion of security camera footage with the head of Mar-a-Lago IT

## Cannon dismissal â€” July 15, 2024 (Tier 1)

Judge [[Aileen Cannon]] granted Trump's motion to dismiss on the ground that Special Counsel Jack Smith's appointment by Attorney General Garland was unconstitutional under the Appointments Clause. Specifically:

- Cannon held that the Special Counsel's office is not authorized by a specific statute creating it as an inferior office
- Therefore the appointment of a Special Counsel by the AG without Senate confirmation is unconstitutional
- Cannon's ruling departed from decades of prior court rulings upholding Special Counsel appointments (notably in *In re Sealed Case* (DC Cir. 2019) and others)

The ruling was widely viewed in legal commentary as a stretch â€” Justice Clarence Thomas had floated a similar argument in a concurrence in *Trump v United States* (the immunity case) in July 2024, but no other federal court had endorsed it.

## Appeal and post-election disposition (Tier 1)

- DOJ filed notice of appeal to the 11th Circuit August 2024
- November 2024 election: Trump elected to a second presidential term
- November-December 2024: DOJ moves to dismiss the case (and the parallel DC case) without prejudice under DOJ policy of not prosecuting a sitting president
- Cases dismissed without prejudice; not on the merits

## What the indictment established but never went to trial

- A 37-count indictment supported by grand jury finding of probable cause
- Documentary and testimonial evidence consistent with willful retention and obstruction
- Substantial photographic and audio evidence
- No fact-finding trial occurred; no jury verdict on the merits

## What is unresolved by the dismissal

- Whether a jury would have convicted on the merits
- The full extent of national-security harm from the retention
- Whether documents were accessed, shared, or transmitted to unauthorized parties
- Whether the prosecution theory of the case would have survived appellate review on the merits
