# LIV Golf and the PGA Tour merger announcement (2023-)

LIV Golf is a Saudi Public Investment Fund (PIF) - backed golf league launched 2022, structurally aimed at displacing the PGA Tour as the top global professional golf circuit.

## Saudi PIF backing (Tier 1)

- PIF chairman is [[Mohammed bin Salman]] (MBS), Crown Prince of Saudi Arabia
- PIF committed approximately  to LIV Golf launch
- LIV signed major PGA stars at multi-hundred-million-dollar contracts (Phil Mickelson, Dustin Johnson, Brooks Koepka, Bryson DeChambeau)

## Trump connection

- LIV held tournaments at multiple Trump-owned properties 2022-2024 (Bedminster NJ, Doral FL, Trump National DC)
- These tournaments generated direct revenue for Trump Organization during the post-presidency / pre-2024-campaign period
- 9/11 families and victim advocacy groups protested the Bedminster events

## June 6, 2023 announcement

- PGA Tour Commissioner Jay Monahan and PIF Governor Yasir Al-Rumayyan announced a framework merger agreement
- Senate Permanent Subcommittee on Investigations opened inquiry July 11, 2023
- Final deal status remains in flux as of latest filings - the framework agreement has been extended but not consummated as originally announced

## Significance for vault

The LIV-PGA case is the cleanest single example of Saudi PIF using sports infrastructure to:
1. Project soft power in the US
2. Generate direct revenue for politically aligned US assets (Trump properties)
3. Acquire institutional capture of a US sports body

It connects directly to the broader [[Saudi-Trump financial relationship]] and is documented in the SPSI inquiry record.
