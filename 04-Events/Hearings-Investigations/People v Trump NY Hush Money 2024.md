# People v Trump (NY Hush Money 2024)

The Manhattan District Attorney's Office prosecution of [[Donald Trump]] in New York Supreme Court, Indictment No. 71543-2023, on 34 counts of falsifying business records in the first degree. **The first criminal prosecution of a former US President in American history, and the first conviction.** Verdict returned May 30, 2024; Trump sentenced January 10, 2025 to unconditional discharge.

## Charges

34 counts of falsifying business records in the first degree, under NY Penal Law § 175.10. Each count corresponded to a specific document (invoice, voucher, or check entry) in the Trump Organization's records related to the 2017 reimbursement of [[Michael Cohen]] for $130,000 paid to [[Stormy Daniels]] (Stephanie Clifford) days before the 2016 election. The records categorized the reimbursements as "legal services" pursuant to a retainer agreement.

The first-degree felony enhancement required proof that the falsifications were committed with intent to commit or conceal another crime. The prosecution argued the predicate offense was violation of NY Election Law § 17-152 -- conspiracy to promote a candidacy by unlawful means -- including violations of federal campaign finance law (excessive in-kind contribution to the campaign) and state tax law.

## Evidence at trial (Tier 1)

### Documentary evidence

- 11 reimbursement checks signed by Trump
- Invoices submitted by Cohen describing services as "legal services pursuant to retainer agreement"
- General ledger entries in Trump Organization books
- Bank records showing Essential Consultants LLC formation and wire transfers
- Trump's October 2016 personal involvement documented in audio recordings (the "Karen McDougal recording" between Trump and Cohen)
- Pecker/AMI "catch and kill" agreement evidence: AMI's $150,000 payment to Karen McDougal in August 2016

### Witness testimony

- **[[Michael Cohen]]** (key witness): testified to Trump's direct involvement in directing the Daniels payment, the reimbursement structure, and the categorization of payments as legal services. Six days of testimony including cross-examination.
- **[[Stormy Daniels]]**: testified to the 2006 encounter and the 2016 negotiation with Cohen.
- **[[David Pecker]]** (AMI/National Enquirer CEO): testified to August 2015 Trump Tower meeting where he agreed to be the "eyes and ears" of the campaign -- to surface negative stories so Cohen and Trump could intervene. Documented the AMI catch-and-kill payments to Karen McDougal and Dino Sajudin. **Granted immunity for testimony.**
- **[[Hope Hicks]]**: former Trump White House communications director; testified to 2016 campaign awareness of the Access Hollywood tape and the McDougal/Daniels stories.
- **[[Madeleine Westerhout]]**: former Trump assistant; testified to Trump Organization workflow and Trump's personal involvement in correspondence.
- **Trump Organization accountants and bookkeepers**: testified to the ledger entries.

### Prosecutorial theory

The hush-money payment was a federal campaign finance violation (excessive in-kind contribution to the Trump campaign) committed with the purpose of suppressing information from voters. The subsequent falsified records concealed both the original violation and the related tax-law violation.

## Verdict

**May 30, 2024**: jury (12 jurors) returned verdict of guilty on all 34 counts after approximately 9.5 hours of deliberation across 2 days.

## Sentencing

**January 10, 2025**: Judge Merchan imposed unconditional discharge -- no incarceration, no probation, no fine. The unconditional discharge nonetheless preserved the conviction itself; Trump remains a convicted felon as a matter of law.

## Appeals status

Trump filed notice of appeal. Constitutional challenges to the prosecution have proceeded through New York appellate courts. Status: pending as of 2026-05.

## What the verdict established (Tier 1)

- Trump personally and knowingly directed the categorization of reimbursement payments as "legal services" in Trump Organization records
- The categorization was false in fact
- The falsification was committed with intent to conceal another crime (campaign finance violation)

## What the verdict did NOT address

- The underlying conduct with Daniels (not an element of the charged offenses)
- Trump's intent regarding the 2016 election outcome
- Any conduct beyond the specific 34 records covered by the indictment
