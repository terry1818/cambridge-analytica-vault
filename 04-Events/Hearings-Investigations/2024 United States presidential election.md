# 2024 United States presidential election

Held November 5, 2024. [[Donald Trump]] (R) defeated [[Kamala Harris]] (D), winning both the Electoral College and the popular vote (the latter only the second time a Republican has done so since 1988; the prior was 2004).

## Outcome (Tier 1)

- Trump 312 EVs, Harris 226
- Popular vote: Trump 49.8%, Harris 48.3% (margin ~1.5%)
- Republican Senate gain; House GOP retained control narrowly

## Significance

The 2024 election restored Trump to the presidency despite:
- 34 felony convictions ([[People v Trump 2024]])
- Multiple ongoing federal indictments (subsequently dismissed)
-  civil-fraud judgment ([[Engoron verdict]])
-  E. Jean Carroll civil judgments

The election outcome confirmed that the coalition behind Trump tolerates documented criminal conduct as a feature of governance choice, not a disqualifying factor. This is one of the structural data points for the [[Demographic Defensiveness Thesis]] analysis - the coalition has internalized that the conventional electoral path may require accepting candidates with documented illegality.
