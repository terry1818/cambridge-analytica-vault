# Tenet Media DOJ Indictment (2024-09-04)

## Summary

On **September 4, 2024**, the US Department of Justice indicted **two Russian nationals** - employees of Russian state-controlled media company **RT** (Russia Today) - for **allegedly funneling approximately $10 million into Tenet Media**, a **Tennessee-based content firm** that paid major US right-wing influencers including **Tim Pool, Benny Johnson, Dave Rubin, Lauren Southern, and Matt Christiansen**.

The indictment charged the Russians with **failing to register as foreign agents** under FARA + money laundering. Per DOJ allegations, **the scheme was to create and distribute content to US audiences with hidden Russian government messaging** - functionally **using US right-wing influencers as unwitting (or witting) Russian propaganda assets** at scale. Tenet Media shuttered. The named influencers claimed **"victim" status** ("we were deceived"), though questions remain about how much editorial-direction overlap they had with the Russian funders.

**The most operationally significant documented case of Russian-funded US right-wing influence-operations in the post-CA era.**

## Actors

- **DOJ** - indicting agency
- **Kostiantyn Kalashnikov + Elena Afanasyeva** - Russian nationals indicted; RT employees
- **Tenet Media** - the cutout; Tennessee LLC; shuttered post-indictment
- **Lauren Chen + Liam Donovan** (Tenet Media co-founders) - **not** indicted; cooperation status unclear
- **Influencers paid:** Tim Pool ($100K/wk reported), Benny Johnson, Dave Rubin, Lauren Southern, Matt Christiansen, Tayler Hansen
- **RT (Russia Today)** - the state-media parent of the operation

## Documented payments (per indictment)

- ~$10M total funneled from RT-controlled accounts to Tenet
- Tim Pool: ~$400K/month for 4 months of content (~$1.6M)
- Benny Johnson: ~$400K/month
- Dave Rubin: ~$400K/month
- Lauren Southern: smaller package
- Tenet co-founders kept some portion as management/operations fee

## Editorial direction

Per indictment, the Russian principals provided **content direction and topic-steering** - specifically:
- Pro-Russia framing on Ukraine
- Anti-NATO framing
- Domestic-American culture-war amplification (immigration, race)
- 2024-election-cycle Trump-favorable framing

## Influencer response

- **Tim Pool, Benny Johnson, Dave Rubin** all claimed "victim" status - that they were deceived about the funding source
- Multiple legal-analysts noted that **even if the influencers were not knowingly Russian-influenced, they were taking Russian state money** - a structural problem regardless of intent
- **Lauren Chen** (Tenet co-founder) attempted post-indictment public-facing repositioning
- **No US influencers have been criminally charged** as of 2026
