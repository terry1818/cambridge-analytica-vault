# Trinidad 2010 "Do So!" Campaign

## Summary

Early documented **SCL/CA voter suppression operation** in the 2010 Trinidad and Tobago general election. SCL designed a campaign called **"Do So!"** aimed at **reducing voter turnout among young Afro-Trinidadians**, who were less likely to support SCL's client, the **United National Congress (UNC)** -- the party representing the South-Asian-descended population. The campaign was disguised as a spontaneous youth social-media protest movement encouraging non-voting as a form of political protest. Used graffiti, billboards, music videos, and social media. **Funded by [[Jack Warner]]**, then FIFA Vice President (later indicted on corruption charges). Audio recording of [[Alexander Nix]] confirms the **ethnically targeted voter suppression strategy**. CA later boasted on its website of helping elect [[Kamla Persad-Bissessar]] as PM. ([[Source Wikipedia - Cambridge Analytica]], [[Source Wikipedia - Facebook-CA scandal]])

## Why this matters

This is the **methodological template** for what CA later marketed in the West:
- Racial/ethnic targeting
- Suppression of an opponent's likely-voter base (vs. mobilization of one's own)
- Faux-grassroots aesthetic to disguise commercial-political origin
- Funded through opaque international money
- 6 years BEFORE the 2016 US election
