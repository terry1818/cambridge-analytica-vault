# Post-October 7 US Political Realignment

The October 7, 2023 Hamas attacks on Israel and the subsequent Israeli military operation in Gaza produced substantial reshaping of US partisan and intra-partisan politics across the 2023-2026 period. The realignment is operationally significant for the vault network because of its cross-cluster consequences.

## Principal realignment dynamics

**Within the Democratic Party:**

- Substantial intra-party tension between traditional pro-Israel positioning and progressive-aligned critical positioning
- [[United Democracy Project]] (UDP) deployed approximately $100M defeating progressive Democratic incumbents in 2024 primaries
- Multiple Democratic incumbents lost primaries principally on Israel-policy positioning (Jamaal Bowman, Cori Bush)
- The "Uncommitted Movement" in the 2024 Democratic primary mobilized substantial anti-Biden positioning principally on Israel-policy questions
- The November 2024 Harris loss was substantially attributed (in some post-election analyses) to Israel-policy-related Arab-American and progressive demobilisation in key battleground states

**Within the Republican Party:**

- Substantial consolidation of pro-Israel positioning across Republican factions
- Trump 2.0 administration substantial alignment with Netanyahu government
- [[Mike Huckabee]] appointment as Ambassador to Israel reflecting religious-right-anchored Israel positioning
- Substantial increase in Republican-aligned campus-confrontation operations targeting pro-Palestinian student protests
- ICC arrest-warrant-pushback as a Trump 2.0 policy thread

**Across the broader political environment:**

- Substantial increase in campus-political activity around Israel-Gaza questions
- Substantial deployment of [[NSO Group]] / [[Paragon Solutions]]-style spyware against journalists covering Israel-Gaza
- Substantial international diplomatic realignment with US substantially isolated from European and global-South positioning on ICC/ICJ proceedings
- Substantial growth in [[Christians United for Israel]] political activity and [[Republican Jewish Coalition]] operational scope
