# ICC Netanyahu Arrest Warrant Pushback

In November 2024 the International Criminal Court (ICC) issued arrest warrants for Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu and former Defense Minister Yoav Gallant on charges related to alleged crimes against humanity in Gaza. The Trump 2.0 administration's coordinated response to those warrants is one of the principal post-October-7 Israel-policy operational threads the vault tracks.

## Principal operational responses

The Trump 2.0 administration's response has included:

- February 2025 executive order imposing sanctions on ICC personnel involved in investigations of US or Israeli citizens
- Substantial diplomatic pressure on ICC member states regarding cooperation with the warrants
- Congressional legislation (the Illegitimate Court Counteraction Act) imposing additional sanctions
- Substantial coordination with Netanyahu government on international-pushback messaging
- Substantial coordination with [[AIPAC]], [[Republican Jewish Coalition]], and the broader pro-Israel infrastructure on US-domestic political messaging

## Operational consequences

The pushback has produced operational consequences including:

- Substantial ICC institutional response including personnel safety concerns and operational constraints
- Substantial pressure on European member-state cooperation with the warrants
- The broader pattern of Trump 2.0 administration explicit rejection of international-institutional jurisdiction over Israeli or US conduct
- Substantial alignment with broader Trump 2.0 administration anti-international-institutional positioning
