# ReAwaken America

## Summary

ReAwaken America (commonly the "ReAwaken America Tour") is a recurring touring rally series founded in 2021 by Oklahoma entrepreneur and former wedding DJ [[Clay Clark]] and retired Lieutenant General [[Michael Flynn]], President [[Donald Trump]]'s first national security adviser. It grew directly out of Clark's pandemic-era "Health and Freedom" anti-lockdown events: a few months after the [[January 6 2021]] Capitol attack failed to overturn the 2020 election, Clark and Flynn rebranded the series and launched the first ReAwaken event in Tulsa in April 2021. NPR, PBS *Frontline*, and *Rolling Stone* have all described the tour as a hybrid form — "part conservative Christian revival, part QAnon expo, part political rally," or, in one frequently quoted phrase, "a political rally in the form of an apostolic revival."

The tour matters to this vault because it is one of the clearest physical infrastructures linking three of the threads the vault tracks: Christian nationalism, post-2020 election denial, and organized disinformation. Its program braids COVID-19 misinformation, QAnon framings, "stolen election" claims, anti-vaccine messaging, and dominionist political theology, and stages mass baptisms alongside speeches by MAGA operatives. By holding most events in churches, it converts congregations into political mobilization nodes — recruiting what Flynn and other speakers have repeatedly called an "army of God."

## When & where

- **First event:** April 2021, Tulsa, Oklahoma (as the rebranded successor to the "Health and Freedom" conferences)
- **Format:** Multi-day weekend events, typically Friday–Saturday, ticketed
- **Venues:** Predominantly evangelical and charismatic churches; some arenas and a Trump-owned resort in Miami; as of January 2023 the majority of events had been held in churches
- **Scale:** Carried to tens of thousands of attendees across more than 30 cities and towns through 2024; ongoing into 2025–2026
- **Sponsor:** The charismatic Christian outlet Charisma News has been a named sponsor; the tour is produced through Clark's ThriveTime Show business

## Sequence of events

- `2020` — Clay Clark begins holding anti-lockdown protests in Oklahoma during the COVID-19 pandemic
- `2021-early` — Clark organizes "Health and Freedom" conferences; meets Michael Flynn, who he says agreed when Clark told him "God wants us to do a tour"
- `2021-04` — First rebranded ReAwaken America event held in Tulsa, Oklahoma, months after January 6
- `2021-summer` — "Health and Freedom" events formally rebranded as the ReAwaken America Tour, sponsored by Charisma News
- `2022` — Tour expands nationally; New York Attorney General's office sends a letter regarding a planned event; events draw mass attendance and national press scrutiny
- `2022-10` — Event at Spooky Nook Sports complex, Manheim, Pennsylvania, featuring Flynn, Clark, and Doug Mastriano
- `2022-11` — NPR coverage describes the tour uniting "conservative Christians and conspiracy theorists"
- `2023` — Event scheduled at a Trump-owned resort in Miami; majority of events to date held in churches
- `2024–2026` — Tour continues; Christian leaders issue repeated public warnings; events continue to feature election-denial and Christian-nationalist content

## Outcome / consequences

- **Direct outcome:** A durable, repeatable mobilization platform that has reached tens of thousands of attendees in person and far larger audiences through livestreams and clips.
- **Downstream effects:** Normalization of QAnon-adjacent and election-denial conspiracism within charismatic church settings; recruitment language ("army of God") that fuses spiritual-warfare theology with political militancy; a venue for MAGA figures to maintain a grassroots presence between elections.
- **Institutional pushback:** Major Christian leaders and outlets (including via *Baptist News Global*) have publicly condemned the tour as among the most egregious post–January 6 examples of [[Christian nationalism]] — described variously as "QAnon 2.0" and a "Who's Who of the new Christian right."

## Disputed facts

- **Effect on elections:** The tour markets itself as movement-defining; its actual measurable effect on voter turnout or election outcomes is not established. Treat mobilization claims as the organizers' framing, not documented impact. `#claim/disputed`
- **Relationship to violence:** Critics link the "army of God" rhetoric to insurrectionary risk; organizers say the events are peaceful and religious. The rhetoric is documented; a causal link to specific violence is interpretive. `#claim/disputed`
