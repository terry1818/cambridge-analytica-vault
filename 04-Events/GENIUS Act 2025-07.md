# GENIUS Act 2025-07

## Summary

The GENIUS Act — the Guiding and Establishing National Innovation for U.S. Stablecoins Act of 2025 — is the first U.S. federal law to create a comprehensive regulatory framework for payment stablecoins (cryptocurrency tokens pegged to a stable reference asset, typically the U.S. dollar). It was signed into law by President [[Donald Trump]] on July 18, 2025, one day after the House of Representatives passed it. The law requires stablecoin issuers to hold 100% reserves in liquid assets such as U.S. dollars or short-term Treasury securities, to publish monthly disclosures of reserve composition, and to comply with the Bank Secrecy Act's anti-money-laundering and sanctions obligations. For this vault, the GENIUS Act is significant less as financial regulation than as a **legislative marker of the tech-right donor wing's policy influence** in the second Trump administration: the crypto industry spent heavily in the 2024 cycle, and the law was its first major federal payoff.

## When & where

- **Introduced (Senate):** February 4, 2025.
- **Senate passage:** June 17, 2025, by a vote of 68–30 (bipartisan).
- **House passage:** July 17, 2025, by a vote of 308–122.
- **Signed into law:** July 18, 2025, at the White House, Washington, D.C.

## Actors

- [[Donald Trump]] — President; signed the bill and championed crypto-friendly policy.
- [[United States Congress]] — passed the bill with bipartisan majorities in both chambers.
- Crypto industry — the principal lobbying and donor force behind the legislation; a key constituency of the [[tech right]].
- United States Department of the Treasury — designated to implement key provisions, including reserve and disclosure rules.

## Sequence of events

- `2024` — the crypto industry becomes a major political-spending force in the U.S. election cycle, backing crypto-friendly candidates.
- `2025-02-04` — the GENIUS Act is introduced in the Senate.
- `2025-06-17` — the Senate passes the bill 68–30.
- `2025-07-17` — the House passes the bill 308–122.
- `2025-07-18` — President Trump signs the GENIUS Act into law; the White House issues a fact sheet framing it as positioning the U.S. to "lead the global digital currency revolution."

## Outcome / consequences

- **Direct outcome:** the first federal regulatory regime for payment stablecoins — reserve requirements, monthly disclosure, Bank Secrecy Act coverage, and a federal/state issuer-licensing structure.
- **Downstream effects:** regulators including the SEC, FDIC, and OCC moved to issue implementing rules; the law accelerated bank and fintech entry into the stablecoin market.
- **Vault relevance:** marks the [[tech right]]'s transition from disruptive outsider to legislative insider — a policy win delivered to a donor coalition this vault tracks alongside the religious right (see [[Lens - Religious Right vs Tech Right]]).

## Disputed facts

- **Consumer-protection adequacy:** the White House framed the law as prioritizing consumer protection and national security; critics argued it left gaps — limited treatment of issuer insolvency, conflicts of interest involving the President's own family crypto ventures, and uneven federal/state oversight. `#claim/disputed`
- **Bipartisanship vs. industry capture:** supporters cite the bipartisan votes as evidence of broad consensus; critics characterize the margins as the product of an intense, well-funded industry lobbying campaign. `#claim/disputed`
