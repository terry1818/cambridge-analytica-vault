# Stop the Steal

## Summary

Stop the Steal was the movement, slogan, and coordinated disinformation campaign that promoted the false claim that the 2020 U.S. presidential election was stolen from [[Donald Trump]] through widespread fraud. The slogan itself was not new — Republican operative [[Roger Stone]] had used a "Stop the Steal" PAC and website in 2016 to pre-emptively delegitimize a possible loss, and it briefly resurfaced in 2018. In 2020 the right-wing activist [[Ali Alexander]] (formerly Ali Akbar), who had joined Stone's 2018 iteration, revived it: his company registered StopTheSteal.us on November 4, 2020 — the day after the election, before counting had finished. Over the following nine weeks the campaign grew explosively across social media, fed by Trump's own statements, amplified by figures including [[Steve Bannon]], and channeled into rallies at swing-state capitals and ultimately the January 6, 2021 march on the U.S. Capitol. No court, recount, or audit substantiated the fraud claim; courts rejected dozens of suits. The movement's lasting product was not a reversed election but the institutionalization of election denial — the [[Election Denial Infrastructure]] built afterward.

## When & where

- **Dates:** November 4, 2020 (domain registered) through January 6, 2021 (Capitol attack); the broader claim persists indefinitely.
- **Location:** primarily online; physical rallies in Georgia, Arizona, Pennsylvania, Michigan, Nevada, Wisconsin capitals and Washington, D.C.
- **Duration:** roughly nine intense weeks, then transition into permanent infrastructure.

## Actors

- [[Donald Trump]] — incumbent president; principal source of the stolen-election claim and the campaign's mobilizing figure.
- [[Ali Alexander]] — founder and national organizer of the 2020 Stop the Steal campaign; registered the website and organized rallies.
- [[Roger Stone]] — originator of the "Stop the Steal" branding (2016, 2018 iterations); links to the 2020 effort are partly disputed by Stone himself.
- [[Steve Bannon]] — amplified the campaign through his *War Room* podcast; tied to pre-January 6 planning.
- [[Cleta Mitchell]] — Trump-aligned election lawyer; present on the January 2, 2021 call pressuring Georgia's Secretary of State; later built the [[Election Integrity Network]].
- [[Republican National Committee]] — channeled fundraising and legal activity around the fraud claims.

## Sequence of events

- `2016` — Roger Stone launches a "Stop the Steal" PAC/website to pre-delegitimize a possible Trump loss ([[Source - SPLC Hatewatch 2020]]).
- `2018` — slogan briefly revived around the midterms; Ali Alexander participates.
- `2020-09` — Alexander says in a circulated video he is "thinking about bringing Stop the Steal out of retirement."
- `2020-11-04` — Alexander's company registers StopTheSteal.us, one day after Election Day.
- `2020-11` — a Facebook "Stop the Steal" group reaches hundreds of thousands of members within roughly a day before Facebook removes it; the hashtag spreads across platforms.
- `2020-11 to 2020-12` — rallies in swing-state capitals; ~60 election lawsuits filed and almost uniformly rejected.
- `2020-12-14` — Electoral College electors vote; the same day, fake "alternate" elector slates are signed in seven states.
- `2021-01-02` — Trump's recorded call asks Georgia Secretary of State to "find 11,780 votes"; Cleta Mitchell is on the call.
- `2021-01-06` — "Stop the Steal"–branded rally precedes the attack on the U.S. Capitol; Congress nonetheless certifies the result that night.

## Outcome / consequences

- **Direct outcome:** the 2020 result was certified; the fraud claim was never substantiated.
- **Downstream effects:** the [[Election Denial Infrastructure]] — standing organizations, the [[Election Integrity Network]], voter-challenge programs — was constructed to make the claim permanent; the [[Election Integrity]] framing became the movement's banner.
- **Investigations / prosecutions:** the House January 6 Select Committee; federal and Georgia (Fulton County) prosecutions; criminal charges and convictions of numerous participants.

## Disputed facts

- **Stone's role in the 2020 iteration:** Stone has both denied affiliation with the 2020 group and claimed credit for "reviving" the movement. The legal/financial link between the 2016 PAC and the 2020 LLC is unclear. `#claim/disputed`
- **Funding:** the money behind the 2020 rallies is poorly documented; reporting describes it as "shrouded in mystery." `#claim/disputed`
- **The core claim itself:** that the election was stolen is false — rejected by courts, audits, and recounts. This is treated as established, not disputed.
