## The August 10 anomalies (all tier 1 documented)

- **Cellmate transferred** out the night before death
- **Two cameras** positioned to surveil the cell **malfunctioned** that night; recordings unavailable
- **Both guards** assigned to check on him every 30 minutes **fell asleep** and **falsified records** to indicate they had performed checks (subsequently charged and pled)
- **Suicide watch was removed** July 29 after the July 23 incident, against typical post-attempt protocol
- **Hyoid bone broken**: Dr. Michael Baden, NYC's longtime former chief medical examiner retained by Epstein's brother to observe the autopsy, publicly stated the broken hyoid was more consistent with strangulation than self-hanging. NYC OCME maintained suicide determination.

These anomalies are individually possible and collectively a profound operational failure at minimum. The cause-of-death dispute remains unresolved in public record.

## 2023 DOJ OIG report (tier 1)

The June 2023 DOJ Office of Inspector General report concluded:
- "Combination of negligence, misconduct, and outright job performance failures"
- Maintained the suicide conclusion
- Did not find evidence of foul play
- Did not address whether external parties had operational access or motivation
