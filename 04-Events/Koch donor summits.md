## Summary

The Koch donor summits are twice-yearly private seminars convened by [[Charles Koch]] (and until his 2019 death, [[David Koch]]) to coordinate fundraising strategy and political priorities among a network of wealthy conservative donors. Launched around 2003 with approximately 17 participants, the seminars grew into the central operational mechanism of what became the most powerful independent political infrastructure in the United States. By 2016, the network raised an estimated $700–900 million in a single election cycle — a figure rivaling the official Republican Party apparatus. Participants are referred to internally as "investors." Christopher Leonard's *Kochland* documents the seminars' structure and escalating financial scale.

## When & Where

- Launched approximately 2003; held twice annually, roughly in January (Palm Springs area, California) and June (undisclosed locations).
- Locations include luxury resorts; participants have included Rancho Mirage, California events.
- Attendance is by personal invitation from Charles Koch; journalists and non-donors are excluded.
- Participants were routinely instructed to destroy all paperwork at the conclusion of each seminar.

## Actors

- **[[Charles Koch]]** — convener and primary organizer.
- **[[Americans for Prosperity]]** — the main political 501(c)(4) of the network; its presidents and strategists are regular presenters.
- **Wealthy donor-investors** — in 2015, at least 18 billionaires were documented as attending.
- **[[DonorsTrust]] / Donors Capital Fund** — the anonymizing pass-through vehicles that channel seminar pledges to recipient organizations without donor attribution.
- **[[Heritage Foundation]], [[Cato Institute]], and allied think tanks** — institutional beneficiaries regularly represented by speakers.

## Sequence of Events

1. **2003:** Inaugural seminar with ~17 participants; fundraising total under $100 million that cycle.
2. **2008:** Network raises under $100 million for election cycle; still relatively small.
3. **2010:** Rapid expansion following Citizens United (January 2010); Tea Party mobilization channel opens; [[Americans for Prosperity]] deploys seminar funds to oppose [[Waxman-Markey climate bill 2009]] and health care reform.
4. **2012:** Network raises more than $400 million; use of shell corporations and pass-through groups revealed in September 2013.
5. **2014:** Network raises under $300 million.
6. **2016:** Network raises an estimated $700–900 million, rivaling the Republican National Committee.
7. **2019:** David Koch dies; Charles continues seminars under "Stand Together" rebranding of some network elements.
8. **Ongoing:** Seminars continue; network restructured under Stand Together umbrella but core coordination mechanism persists.

## Outcome / Consequences

- Created a coordinated multi-hundred-million-dollar political infrastructure operating entirely outside public disclosure requirements applicable to party committees.
- Established the template for large-scale anonymous donor coordination that was later emulated across the ideological spectrum (e.g., [[Democracy Alliance]] on the left).
- Funded the [[No Climate Tax Pledge]], the defeat of [[Waxman-Markey climate bill 2009]], and anti-ACA campaigns.
- The secrecy norm — destroying all paperwork — created significant barriers to investigative accountability and legal scrutiny.
- Became a parallel party structure: by 2016, the Koch network had a larger field operation than the RNC.

## Disputed Facts

- **Total amounts raised** are estimated from partial disclosures and investigative reporting; exact figures are not publicly filed. The $700–900M range for 2016 is Mayer/journalist estimates (Tier-2), not audited totals.
- Whether individual pledge commitments constitute legally reportable coordination with candidates has been contested; no successful prosecution has resulted.

## Open Questions

- The full donor list for any individual seminar has never been published.
- The extent to which seminar strategy documents (instructed to be destroyed) survive in any form is unknown.
- Whether foreign-national donors or foreign capital has been laundered through [[DonorsTrust]] into seminar-pledged funds is unverified (Tier-3).
