# HindsightFiles Release (2020+)

## Summary

On **January 4, 2020**, [[Brittany Kaiser]] began publishing material from her personal archive of [[Cambridge Analytica]] documents via the **@HindsightFiles** Twitter account. The release ultimately encompassed **100,000+ documents** covering CAs work in **68 countries**.

## Release pattern

The release was **phased and country-themed**:

- 2020-01-01 to 2020-01-03 -- preliminary teasers
- 2020-01-04 -- first major batch (Malaysia, Kenya, Brazil)
- 2020-01-06+ -- continuing releases across multiple weeks
- 2020-02 to mid-2020 -- additional country-focused drops
- 2020-2021 -- sporadic continuing material via Kaiser and journalists

## Significance

Largest single document release on CA outside of the original [[2018 Wylie Disclosures]]. Establishes the scope of CAs **global operations far exceeding what was publicly reported during the 2018 scandal**. Confirms multiple specific country operations including:

- Malaysia (Najib Razak)
- Kenya (Kenyatta campaigns)
- Brazil (pre-2018 election)
- Trinidad ("Do So!" details)
- US internal correspondence
- Argentina, Romania, Czech Republic, Italy, Nigeria, Mexico, and others

## Limitations

- **Curated by Kaiser** (selection bias)
- **Most material unanalyzed publicly** (see [[HindsightFiles Analytical Index]] for what has and hasnt been examined)
- Chain of custody from CA servers → Kaiser → public release not third-party-verified

## Continuing significance

The HindsightFiles archive represents **the single largest unanalyzed primary-source repository** on CA. Any future investigation of CA's actual scope of operations should engage with this archive directly.
