# 2018 Wylie Disclosures

## Summary

On **Saturday, March 17, 2018**, [[The Guardian]] / [[The Observer]] and [[The New York Times]] published **simultaneous reports** based on disclosures by [[Christopher Wylie]], former Director of Research at [[Cambridge Analytica]]. The simultaneous publication broke the [[Facebook-CA Data Scandal]] to the global public.

## The journalistic backstory

- [[Carole Cadwalladr]] had coaxed Wylie over **approximately one year** (early 2017 through March 2018) to go on the record
- Wylie had served as anonymous source for Cadwalladrs February and May 2017 Observer pieces
- CAs legal threats against The Guardian/Observer made independent publication risky
- Cadwalladr brought in **Channel 4 News and The New York Times** to ensure the story could not be killed
- Saturday-evening publication chosen for maximum impact (weekend news cycle, Sunday papers follow-up)

## Why this moment was different

The scandal had been building since [[Source Davies 2015-12-11 Guardian]] (~28 months earlier). What made the March 2018 break different:
- **Named whistleblower** with documents and detailed testimony
- **Multi-newsroom coordination** that defeated legal-threat suppression
- **Visual media (Channel 4 sting)** that amplified the documentary case
- **Post-2016 political environment** receptive to influence-operations concerns
- **Concurrent Sanni disclosure** (Brexit angle)

## Consequences

- CA dissolved within six weeks
- $5B FTC Facebook fine (July 2019)
- Multiple government investigations launched (US, UK, EU)
- Triggered global data-privacy regulatory wave
- Founded the modern public understanding of "data scandal" / "psychographic targeting"
