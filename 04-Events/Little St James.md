# Little St James

Privately-owned 70-acre island in the US Virgin Islands, located east of St. Thomas. Purchased by [[Jeffrey Epstein]] in 1998 for approximately $7.95M through his holding company L.S.J. LLC. **Primary documented site of trafficking-related activity** per multiple sworn accounts in [[Maxwell 2021 SDNY Trial]] and other proceedings, and named in the [[Epstein 2019 SDNY Case and Death]] indictment. Subject of FBI search August 2019. Widely referred to in tabloid press as "Epstein Island" or "Pedophile Island."

## Documented features (Tier 1)

- Main residence
- Several guest cottages
- Helipad
- Private dock
- Distinctive temple-like structure with blue-and-white striped dome (function disputed; described variously)
- Underground or basement facilities (extent disputed)

## Documented operations (Tier 1)

The 2019 SDNY indictment of Epstein included Little St James as a charged trafficking site:
- Minor victims trafficked to the island via Epstein aircraft
- Multiple sworn accounts described systematic abuse
- Named in the 2007 Florida NPA's co-conspirator immunity scope

The 2021 Maxwell trial included testimony from "Jane" and others describing trafficking to the island.

## Named visitors to the island (per sworn testimony and other documented sources)

This is the area where evidence-tier discipline is critical. People documented at or near the island include various tier-distinct categories:

- **Sworn-testimony accused of sexual contact on the island** (Tier 1 sworn allegation; underlying truth claims separately tiered):
  - [[Prince Andrew]] -- Giuffre testimony places one alleged encounter at Little St James
  - [[Marvin Minsky]] -- Giuffre testimony places an alleged encounter at the island (Minsky deceased before allegations public)
- **Documented to have visited the island but not subject to sworn allegations of misconduct** (Tier 1 visit; no allegation):
  - Various scientific salon attendees per investigative reporting
- **Documented overflights / unverified visits** (Tier 2/3):
  - Numerous figures named in tabloid coverage have not been documented visiting; this category is the most prone to false amplification

## 2019 FBI search

August 12, 2019 -- two days after Epstein's death -- FBI executed search warrant on Little St James. Materials seized included:
- Photographic and video media (volume and contents not fully disclosed publicly)
- Documents
- Sexual paraphernalia
- Other items

The full inventory and chain of custody of seized materials remain a subject of ongoing public interest. See [[Epstein 2019 SDNY Case and Death]].

## Post-Epstein status

- Sold by Epstein estate in May 2023 to billionaire Stephen Deckoff for approximately $60M
- Deckoff publicly stated intention to develop as luxury resort

## USVI government lawsuit (2020)

The Government of the USVI sued Epstein's estate January 2020 alleging the island operation constituted a continuing pattern of trafficking. Settled November 2022 for approximately $105M.
