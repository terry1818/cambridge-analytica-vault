# Koch to SPN to ALEC to state legislation pipeline

A connection note documenting the systematic state-level policy coordination pipeline from [[Koch Network]] funding through [[State Policy Network]] (SPN) affiliates and [[ALEC]] (American Legislative Exchange Council) model-legislation drafting into actual state-legislative outputs.

## The integrated pipeline

**Funding source:** Koch Network (Charles Koch network of donors plus the broader Koch-aligned donor architecture) provides substantial multi-year funding to both SPN and ALEC infrastructure.

**Research production layer (SPN):** SPN's 65+ state-level affiliated think tanks produce policy research aligned with the broader free-market and conservative-movement policy programme. The research production is decentralized (each state affiliate operates with substantial autonomy) but coordinated at the central SPN level for shared methodology and shared policy priorities.

**Model-legislation drafting layer (ALEC):** ALEC operates as a 501(c)(3) that brings together state legislators and corporate members to draft model legislation. The model-legislation output flows back to state legislators who then introduce the model bills in their respective state legislatures.

**Implementation layer (state legislatures):** Republican-majority state legislatures across the past 15 years have substantially adopted SPN/ALEC model legislation. Specific policy areas with substantial SPN/ALEC influence include:

- Voter-ID and election-administration restrictions
- Right-to-work labour-policy legislation
- Anti-public-employee-union legislation
- Charter-school and school-voucher policy
- Tax-cut and regulatory-rollback legislation
- Stand Your Ground and adjacent firearm-policy legislation
- Anti-abortion regulatory legislation (post-Dobbs particularly)

## Operational scale

The aggregate scale of state-legislative output from this pipeline is substantial. ALEC has reportedly produced 800+ model bills per year that introduce in state legislatures; SPN affiliates produce thousands of policy research outputs annually that feed into state-legislative debates. The cumulative effect across 50 state legislatures over 15-30 years is operationally substantial reshaping of US subnational policy environment.

## Why this connection is load-bearing

The Koch-SPN-ALEC pipeline is the principal mechanism through which conservative-movement policy programme has reshaped US subnational policy environment. Removing the pipeline would force state-level policy coordination back into bilateral-state-legislator-and-policy-organisation relationships with substantial loss of coordination efficiency.

The state-level focus is operationally distinctive: most conservative-movement infrastructure focuses on federal-level positioning, while the Koch-SPN-ALEC pipeline focuses on subnational policy where the implementation pathway is substantially shorter and the conservative-movement policy programme can be implemented even when federal-level positioning is in opposition control.
