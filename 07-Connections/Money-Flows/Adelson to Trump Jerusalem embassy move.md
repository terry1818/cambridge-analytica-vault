# Connection - Adelson to Trump Jerusalem embassy move

A connection note documenting the operational pathway from [[Sheldon Adelson]]'s substantial 2016 Trump-campaign giving to the December 2017 Trump decision to move the US embassy from Tel Aviv to Jerusalem.

## The substantive timeline

**2016 cycle giving:** Adelson contributed approximately $35M to Trump-supporting super PACs and adjacent vehicles in the 2016 cycle, after initially supporting Marco Rubio in the Republican primary. The Trump-aligned giving represented one of the largest single-donor commitments to the 2016 Trump campaign infrastructure.

**Inauguration funding:** Adelson contributed $5M to the Trump Inauguration Committee, the largest individual contribution to the Inauguration Committee.

**Jerusalem embassy lobbying 2017:** Through 2017, Adelson reportedly conducted sustained personal lobbying of Trump on the Jerusalem embassy move. The lobbying was operationally distinct from typical donor-policy-influence patterns because of:

- The personal direct-relationship channel with Trump
- Adelson's longstanding public advocacy for the embassy move pre-2016 (the embassy move was an explicit Adelson personal priority for decades)
- The convergence with Trump's own positioning that he had committed to the move in 2016-cycle campaign positioning

**December 6, 2017 decision:** Trump announced the decision to recognise Jerusalem as Israel's capital and move the US embassy from Tel Aviv. The decision reversed approximately 70 years of US-Israel policy and was opposed by virtually all US allies.

**May 14, 2018 embassy opening:** The new US embassy in Jerusalem opened on the 70th anniversary of Israeli independence. Adelson attended the embassy opening ceremony as a featured guest.

## The quid-pro-quo question

The vault treats the Adelson-embassy-move connection as documented donor-policy-influence-adjacent activity without asserting a specific quid. Multiple analytical frames are available:

- Adelson made substantial donor commitments to Trump campaign infrastructure
- Adelson lobbied Trump personally on the embassy move
- Trump made the embassy-move decision
- The decision aligned with explicit 2016-cycle Trump campaign commitments
- The decision aligned with Adelson's longstanding personal priority

Whether the donor-policy-influence pathway is operationally distinct from typical donor-administration interaction or whether it crosses into transactional-quid-pro-quo territory is a contested analytical question. The vault treats it as a paradigmatic case of donor-policy-influence intersection.
