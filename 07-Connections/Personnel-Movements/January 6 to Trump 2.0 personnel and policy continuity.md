# January 6 to Trump 2.0 personnel and policy continuity

A connection note documenting the operational consequences of the January 6, 2021 Capitol attack across pardons, personnel re-integration, and policy positioning that flowed into the Trump 2.0 administration through 2025-2026.

## The operational consequences

**Pardons (January 2025):** The Trump 2.0 administration issued mass pardons of January 6 defendants on January 20, 2025. The pardons released approximately 1,500 convicted defendants including the principal seditious-conspiracy convicts [[Stewart Rhodes]] (Oath Keepers), [[Enrique Tarrio]] (Proud Boys), and adjacent organisational leadership. The pardons substantially reduced the legal-accountability framework that the post-January-6 prosecution programme had constructed.

**Personnel re-integration:** Multiple January-6-adjacent figures who had been peripheral or formally separated from the Republican-Party institutional infrastructure in the 2021-2024 period have been substantially re-integrated through the Trump 2.0 administration. Specific examples include figures with documented Stop the Steal organisational positioning who returned to administration-adjacent roles, and the broader pattern of post-2021 Republican-Party re-absorption of January-6-adjacent operational figures.

**Policy positioning continuity:** The Trump 2.0 administration's election-administration policy positioning substantially aligns with the post-2020 election-skepticism framework that produced January 6. The [[Election Integrity Network]] coordination through [[Cleta Mitchell]] / [[Conservative Partnership Institute]] / [[True the Vote]] / [[EagleAI]] integration has substantially expanded under Trump 2.0.

**Paramilitary infrastructure operational continuity:** Post-pardon [[Oath Keepers]] and [[Proud Boys]] organisational rebuilding has been continuing at smaller scale than the pre-2021 operational footprint. The pardons substantially eliminated the legal-accountability framework that had been constraining post-2021 organisational rebuilding.

## The accountability-reversal pattern

The cumulative effect of the pardons, personnel re-integration, and policy-positioning continuity is substantial reversal of the post-January-6 accountability framework. The pattern: the period from January 2021 through January 2025 saw substantial legal-and-political accountability for January-6 conduct; the period from January 2025 onward has seen substantial reversal of that accountability framework.
