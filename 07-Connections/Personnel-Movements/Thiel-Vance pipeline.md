# Thiel-Vance pipeline

A pairwise connection note documenting the specific personnel, mentorship, financing, and ideological pipeline that connects [[Peter Thiel]] to [[JD Vance]] across 15 years, culminating in Vance's January 2025 inauguration as Vice President.

## Documented relationship timeline

**2011:** Vance and Thiel reportedly first met at a Yale Law School event where Thiel spoke. The encounter is referenced in multiple journalistic reconstructions of Vance's biographical arc; the specific event has been variously described.

**2016-2017:** Vance's "Hillbilly Elegy" memoir (2016) brings him to national attention. Post-memoir Vance moves into venture-capital work at [[Mithril Capital]], Thiel's later-stage VC vehicle, as a principal. The professional relationship deepens.

**2019-2020:** Vance co-founds [[Narya Capital]] in Cincinnati with Colin Greenspon. Narya is anchored by Thiel as principal investor. Vance is operationally the public face; the firm's investment thesis aligns substantially with the broader Thiel-portfolio direction.

**2021-2022:** Vance announces 2022 Ohio Senate campaign. Thiel personally underwrites the Protect Ohio Values PAC with $15M, the largest single-candidate PAC commitment outside the top-tier 2022 cycle. The commitment is operationally distinct from typical donor giving: made early in the cycle, in primary-not-general phase, sustained through multiple disclosure cycles.

**2022:** Vance wins the GOP primary (defeating Josh Mandel, Matt Dolan), then wins the general election against Tim Ryan by ~7 percentage points. The Thiel-financed primary win is the operational test case for the Thiel political-financing model.

**2023-2024:** Vance establishes himself in the Senate as a leading Tech-Right-aligned Republican voice. Continued operational coordination with Thiel-network political-strategy figures. Public engagement with [[Catholic Integralism]]-adjacent intellectuals ([[Patrick Deneen]], [[Adrian Vermeule]]) deepens.

**July 2024:** Trump selects Vance as 2024 VP nominee at RNC. Thiel's role in the selection has been variously characterised: not direct lobbying, but the Thiel-Vance relationship is widely understood as having been a factor in Trump's decision.

**January 2025-present:** Vance as VP. The Thiel-Vance-Palantir spine becomes the operational channel between the Trump administration and the Tech-Right infrastructure cluster.

## Specific operational consequences

The pipeline produces operational consequences beyond a typical donor-candidate relationship:

- The vice-presidential position gives Vance direct executive-branch policy authority on issues affecting Thiel-portfolio companies (specifically [[Palantir Technologies]], [[Anduril]], and the broader Founders Fund portfolio).
- The Thiel-Vance-Palantir spine is operationally distinct from conventional lobbying: when Vance speaks to a federal CIO about data-integration strategy, the implications for Palantir's positioning are institutionally understood.
- The Vance-Thiel intellectual alignment (Catholic Integralism-adjacent, Yarvin-influenced, post-liberal) substantially shapes the broader Trump 2.0 policy intellectual frame.

## Why this connection is load-bearing for the vault

This connection is the principal operational channel through which Tech-Right capital and intellectual positioning has translated into executive-branch power. Removing the Thiel-Vance pipeline would substantially decouple the Tech-Right cluster from the Trump 2.0 administration operational core.
