# Bannon-Mercer break

A pairwise connection note documenting the January 2018 break between [[Steve Bannon]] and the [[Mercer]] family, and the operational consequences of the break for the post-2018 conservative-movement funding map.

## The triggering event

The break was triggered by Bannon's cooperation with Michael Wolff on the book "Fire and Fury" (published January 2018) and specifically by Bannon's quoted characterisations of Trump family members, including Donald Trump Jr. and Ivanka Trump. Within days of the book's release, the Mercer family issued a public statement distancing from Bannon.

The break was operationally distinct from typical donor-recipient disputes. The Mercers had been Bannon's principal capital source for five years; the family had been the principal funder of the Bannon-led conservative-movement infrastructure across multiple organisations ([[Cambridge Analytica]], [[Breitbart News]], [[Government Accountability Institute]], and others); the break was therefore a substantial restructuring of the conservative-movement funding map, not just a personal disagreement.

## Operational consequences

Specific operational consequences in the immediate post-break period:

- [[Rebekah Mercer]] resigned from the [[Breitbart News]] board.
- The Mercer family's previously-public stake in Breitbart was the basis for Bannon's continued operational authority over Breitbart editorial direction; the post-break Breitbart leadership reverted to the Larry Solov-Andrew Marcus management line.
- Mercer family donor flows away from Bannon-affiliated entities. The post-break Mercer funding map shifted toward [[Parler]] (where Rebekah Mercer invested and held a board seat), the [[Government Accountability Institute]] (which continued under Peter Schweizer without Bannon), and conservative-media infrastructure without direct Bannon adjacency.
- Bannon's loss of Mercer-anchored funding capacity forced operational restructuring. The post-2018 Bannon operations rebuilt with substantially different capital sources: international Movement work, the War Room podcast revenue, the "We Build the Wall" private fundraising operation, and smaller-dollar audience funding via War Room.

## Longer-run consequences

The break ended the Mercer-Bannon coordination that had been the engine of 2014-2017 conservative-movement strategy. The post-2018 right is more decentralised and less coherent partly because the Mercer-Bannon spine that had organised it dissolved.

Whether the broader Mercer family has substantially re-engaged or remains largely demobilised in the 2024-2026 period is a vault research thread. The post-Bannon-break Mercer political-influence footprint is partially reconstructed but is less aggressive than the pre-break footprint at the directly-political level.

## Why this connection is load-bearing

The Bannon-Mercer break is the principal documented pivot point in the post-CA-era conservative-movement funding map. Understanding the conservative movement's 2018-2024 evolution requires understanding what changed when this funder-recipient relationship collapsed; the Tech-Right ascendancy of 2024-2026 partly fills the operational vacuum that the Mercer-Bannon break left behind.

## Evidence sources

- Multiple contemporaneous press accounts of the January 2018 break

- Breitbart News corporate-governance filings (showing Rebekah Mercer board exit)
- Bannon's subsequent on-record commentary about the break (e.g., in War Room podcast contexts)
