# Fellowship to US foreign policy pipeline

The [[The Fellowship]] (founded 1935) and [[Capitol Ministries]] (the contemporary Christian-Right Capitol-Hill Bible-study operation) have produced a substantial pipeline of US foreign-policy personnel across multiple administrations. The pipeline is operationally distinctive because of the Fellowship's longstanding international-engagement orientation and the substantial foreign-government-leadership relationship-network the Fellowship has maintained for nearly a century.

## Documented Fellowship-affiliated foreign-policy figures

- **Mike Pence** (VP, first administration) - longtime Fellowship-adjacent positioning
- **Mike Pompeo** (CIA Director then Secretary of State, first administration) - substantial Christian-Zionist positioning, Fellowship-adjacent
- **Sam Brownback** (Ambassador-at-Large for International Religious Freedom, first administration) - longtime Fellowship-aligned figure
- **Mark Esper** (Defense Secretary, first administration) - Fellowship-adjacent positioning
- **Various sub-cabinet foreign-policy appointees** with Fellowship adjacency

## Trump 2.0 continuing pipeline

- **Mike Huckabee** (Israel Ambassador, Trump 2.0) - CUFI-anchored Christian-Zionist positioning with Fellowship adjacency
- **Pete Hegseth** (Defense Secretary, Trump 2.0) - religious-right intellectual positioning with Fellowship-adjacent networks
- **Various sub-cabinet foreign-policy appointees** with documented Fellowship engagement

## Operational mechanism

The Fellowship's foreign-policy pipeline operates through several mechanisms:

- **National Prayer Breakfast as venue** - the annual Breakfast week is the principal coordination event connecting US legislators, foreign-government leadership, and US administration figures
- **C Street House residential coordination** - the [[C Street]] house provides residential coordination infrastructure for US legislators with foreign-policy portfolios
- **International prayer-cell network** - the Fellowship's international prayer-cell network connects US foreign-policy figures to foreign-government leadership across multiple decades
- **Capitol Ministries policy-adjacent Bible-study programmes** - the Capitol Ministries operation produces explicit policy-positioning material for cabinet-level participants

## Foreign-government relationship-network

The Fellowship's foreign-government relationship-network has included documented relationships with:

- Multiple African heads of state across multiple decades (Uganda thread documented in [[Source NPR Fresh Air Sharlet Uganda]])
- Latin American political leadership across multiple decades
- Eastern European post-Soviet leadership
- South Korean, Indonesian, Filipino political-religious leadership
- Various Israeli political leadership including Netanyahu-aligned figures

## Why this connection is load-bearing

The Fellowship pipeline is the principal mechanism through which US religious-right institutional infrastructure has projected into US foreign-policy personnel positioning. Removing the Fellowship coordination would substantially weaken the religious-right's foreign-policy operational capacity beyond what direct denominational-political coordination could replace.
