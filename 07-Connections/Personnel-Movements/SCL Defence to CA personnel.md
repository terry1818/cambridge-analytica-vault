# Connection: SCL Defence → Cambridge Analytica (personnel and methodology)

## What the link is

[[Cambridge Analytica]] was a **direct organizational and methodological outgrowth of [[SCL Group]]'s defense-contracting work**. Personnel, methods, IP, and infrastructure transferred from SCL Defence into the civilian-political CA subsidiary at formation in 2013.

## Documented evidence

- **[[Nigel Oakes]]** — SCL founder; held both defense and election work under one roof; co-founded CA
- **[[Alexander Nix]]** — moved from SCL elections work to CA CEO at formation
- **[[Christopher Wylie]]** — had prior work on UK military research at SCL Defence before becoming Director of Research at CA
- **Behavioral Conflict methodology** — developed for US DoD and UK MoD; directly applied to CA's political work
- **Shared London building** — SCL and CA offices co-located through to collapse
- **[[Emic]]** — separate post-collapse firm of ex-SCL Defence staff still working for governments per Briant 2020 — demonstrates the defense-side continued operating in parallel

## Confidence

`#confidence/high` — multiple corroborated primary and secondary sources.

## Implications

CA's methods cannot be understood as commercial-political innovations — they are commercialized military information operations doctrine. The CA scandal therefore exposed not a commercial-firm misbehavior but the **boundary between state IO contracting and civilian elections**.
