# CNP to Trump cabinet pipeline

The [[Council for National Policy]] (CNP) membership has produced a substantial fraction of both Trump first-term and Trump 2.0 cabinet/appointee populations. The pipeline is operationally consequential because CNP is the principal coordination forum for movement-conservative leadership across organisational lines, and CNP-member appointment positioning has substantially shaped both administrations' personnel direction.

## First administration appointees with documented CNP membership or close adjacency

- Mike Pence (VP, longtime CNP-adjacent through Indiana religious-right networks)
- Ben Carson (HUD Secretary, CNP member)
- Betsy DeVos (Education Secretary, CNP member, DeVos family CNP-anchored)
- Mick Mulvaney (OMB then chief of staff, CNP-adjacent)
- Tony Perkins (commission appointee, FRC president and longtime CNP officer)
- Various sub-cabinet and judicial appointees with documented CNP membership

## Trump 2.0 appointees with documented CNP membership or adjacency

- Russell Vought (OMB, longtime CNP-adjacent through Heritage and CRA work)
- Mike Johnson (House Speaker, CNP-adjacent through religious-right networks)
- Mike Huckabee (Israel Ambassador, CNP-adjacent through CUFI and adjacent infrastructure)
- Pete Hegseth (Defense, CNP-adjacent through religious-right intellectual networks)
- Kristi Noem (DHS, religious-right-aligned positioning)
- Various sub-cabinet appointees with documented CNP membership

## Operational mechanism

The CNP-to-cabinet pipeline operates through several mechanisms:

- **CNP membership as credentialing infrastructure** - CNP membership signals movement-conservative credibility to Republican-administration personnel-selection processes
- **CNP coordination on personnel recommendations** - quarterly CNP meetings produce coordinated personnel-recommendation positioning that flows into Republican-administration transition teams
- **CNP-affiliated organisational infrastructure** - CNP-member-led organisations ([[Heritage Foundation]], [[Family Research Council]], [[Conservative Partnership Institute]]) provide direct personnel-pipeline infrastructure
- **CNP-anchored donor coordination** - CNP-affiliated mega-donors ([[Mercer]] family, [[DeVos Family Network]], [[Adelson]]) provide political-finance backing for CNP-aligned appointee selection

## Why this connection is load-bearing

The CNP pipeline is the principal mechanism through which the broader religious-right institutional infrastructure converts movement-coordination into executive-branch appointee placement. Removing CNP coordination would force the personnel-pipeline work into bilateral organisational relationships across multiple movement-conservative organisations with substantial loss of coordination efficiency.
