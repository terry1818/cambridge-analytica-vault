# Trump 1.0 to Trump 2.0 personnel continuity

A connection note documenting the specific named individuals who moved from Trump first-administration appointments through the 2021-2024 wilderness years back into Trump 2.0 administration appointments. The continuity is operationally significant because it indicates the degree to which Trump 2.0 represents personnel-pipeline continuation rather than fresh administration.

## Principal documented continuity figures

- **Russell Vought** - first administration: OMB Deputy then Acting Director; wilderness years: founded Center for Renewing America, led Project 2025 chapter authorship; Trump 2.0: OMB Director (returned to same position with substantially expanded scope)
- **Stephen Miller** - first administration: senior advisor to President; wilderness years: founded America First Legal; Trump 2.0: Deputy Chief of Staff for Policy (consolidated and expanded scope)
- **John Ratcliffe** - first administration: Director of National Intelligence; wilderness years: various Republican-aligned advisory roles; Trump 2.0: CIA Director
- **Kash Patel** - first administration: NSC counterterrorism then DoD; wilderness years: various Trump-aligned media and advisory; Trump 2.0: FBI Director
- **Pam Bondi** - not first-administration formal role but substantial first-administration adjacency; Trump 2.0: Attorney General
- **Multiple sub-cabinet figures** with documented continuity across the same agency or adjacent agency

## Operational consequence: institutional memory

The continuity figures bring substantial institutional memory about first-administration policy-and-personnel patterns. The Trump 2.0 administration is operationally distinct from the first administration partly because of this continuity: the first administration faced substantial pushback from career federal staff and from political appointees with mixed Trump-loyalty positioning, while the Trump 2.0 administration has substantially reduced both categories of operational friction through deliberate personnel selection.

## The wilderness-years institutional infrastructure

The 2021-2024 wilderness period produced substantial Trump-aligned institutional infrastructure that processed the continuity figures and added substantial fresh personnel to the 2.0 pipeline:

- [[America First Policy Institute]] - the principal first-administration alumni think tank
- [[Center for Renewing America]] - Vought's policy vehicle
- [[America First Legal]] - Miller's litigation vehicle
- [[Conservative Partnership Institute]] - Meadows-anchored Hill-staff training and policy infrastructure
- [[Heritage Foundation]] and the Project 2025 coordination infrastructure

## Why this connection is load-bearing

The continuity demonstrates that Trump 2.0 is operationally an extension of Trump first-administration personnel positioning rather than a fresh start. The implications: the policy direction is substantially predictable from first-administration patterns plus the four-year wilderness-period intellectual evolution; the personnel pipeline is operationally durable across administrations rather than dependent on Trump himself.
