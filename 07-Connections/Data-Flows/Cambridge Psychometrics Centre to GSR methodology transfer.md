# Cambridge Psychometrics Centre to GSR / Kogan methodology transfer

A connection note documenting the academic-to-commercial methodology transfer pathway from the [[Cambridge Psychometrics Centre]] (CPC) research to [[Aleksandr Kogan]] / [[GSR]] commercial deployment that produced the [[Facebook-Cambridge Analytica Scandal]]. The connection is load-bearing for the CA story because it documents the foundational technical pathway that made the CA operation mechanically possible.

## The academic foundation

The Cambridge Psychometrics Centre, led by David Stillwell with [[Michal Kosinski]] as a founding research figure, pioneered the methodology of predicting personality traits from Facebook Like data. The principal academic outputs:

- **myPersonality application** (2007-2012) - the Facebook application that collected the principal dataset; approximately 6 million users completed psychological-test questions linked to Facebook profile data
- **2013 PNAS paper** (Kosinski, Stillwell, Graepel) - "Private traits and attributes are predictable from digital records of human behaviour" - the foundational publication demonstrating Facebook Like data could predict OCEAN Big-Five personality traits with meaningful accuracy
- **Subsequent academic literature** - the broader academic literature that built on the 2013 PNAS paper across approximately 2013-2020

The academic methodology was operationally distinct from commercial deployment in several respects: the academic research operated under university IRB ethical review, with explicit research-purpose data-collection consent; the academic publication was peer-reviewed and replicable; the academic findings were available to the broader research community.

## The commercial transfer pathway

Kogan was a University of Cambridge social-psychology faculty member at the Department of Psychology during the 2013-2016 period when his GSR / thisisyourdigitallife operation produced the Facebook-CA data exposure. Kogan was operationally adjacent to (but not a member of) the Cambridge Psychometrics Centre.

The commercial-transfer pathway:

1. **Kogan applied the CPC-developed methodology** in his commercial GSR work, deploying the OCEAN-prediction-from-Facebook-Likes framework that CPC had developed academically
2. **Kogan obtained Facebook Graph API v1 access** through the thisisyourdigitallife application
3. **Kogan collected data on approximately 270,000 quiz-takers** plus friend-network data on an estimated 87 million Facebook users via the Graph API v1 friend-data exposure
4. **Kogan licensed the dataset to SCL / Cambridge Analytica** for political-targeting commercial use
5. **CA operationalized the methodology** at scale across 2016-cycle political-campaign deployments

## The Kosinski-Kogan distinction

The vault carefully distinguishes between:

- **Kosinski and Stillwell** (the foundational academic research; explicitly declined commercial deployment of the methodology for CA-style use)
- **Kogan** (commercial operationalization of the methodology; produced the CA data exposure)

Conflating Kosinski-Stillwell with Kogan has been a common error in CA-era reporting. The methodology pathway runs through Kogan, not through Kosinski.

## Why this connection is load-bearing

The CPC-to-GSR pathway documents the academic-research-to-commercial-deployment transfer that made the CA operation mechanically possible. The connection demonstrates that the CA technical infrastructure was not novel innovation; it was operational deployment of academic methodology that had been developed and published in standard academic venues.

The connection also documents the broader pattern of academic-to-commercial transfer in the broader vault network: substantial commercial information-operations methodology is operationalized academic research that has been productized by commercial actors with weaker ethical-review constraints than academic settings.
