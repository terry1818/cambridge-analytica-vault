# School-board takeover programme

A connection note documenting the post-2021 coordinated school-board-takeover programme. The programme integrates multiple distinct organisational vehicles into a single operational coordination structure.

## Coordinated organisational components

- **[[Moms for Liberty]]** — chapter-network candidate recruitment, training, and electoral coordination
- **[[Christopher Rufo]] / [[Manhattan Institute]]** — issue-framing development and tactical-messaging support
- **[[Hillsdale College]]** — curriculum-development and charter-school institutional infrastructure
- **[[PragerU]]** — children's-content curriculum-substitute material
- **[[Heritage Foundation]] / [[Project 2025]]** — policy framework and state-policy advocacy
- **[[Alliance Defending Freedom]] / [[Liberty Counsel]]** — religious-liberty-litigation support for parents' rights cases
- **[[1792 Exchange]]** and adjacent organisations — ESG-and-business-pressure work on adjacent corporate-policy questions
- **[[Council for National Policy]] / [[Conservative Partnership Institute]]** — broader coordination infrastructure

## Operational pattern

The standard operational pattern for a school-board capture:

1. Issue-framing development (Rufo, Heritage, adjacent infrastructure) produces politically-actionable issue framings (CRT, DEI, gender identity, library book content, parental rights)
2. Local organising (Moms for Liberty, local chapters) develops a chapter base in the target school district
3. Candidate recruitment identifies and trains conservative school-board candidates
4. Funding coordination (state-level and national PACs) supplies campaign resources
5. Election execution wins school-board majorities
6. Post-election governance executes the policy programme (curriculum changes, library policies, personnel decisions)
7. Subsequent litigation defense (ADF, Liberty Counsel) provides legal cover for contested policy decisions

## Why this connection is load-bearing

The school-board capture programme is the principal operational vehicle for the broader cultural-right programme at the local-government level. Where state-and-federal-level operations require substantial institutional infrastructure, the school-board level operates at a scale where committed local activism can produce outsized operational consequences.
