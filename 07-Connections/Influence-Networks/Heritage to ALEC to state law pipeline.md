# Heritage to ALEC to state law pipeline

A connection note documenting the federal-policy-to-state-law pipeline from [[Heritage Foundation]] through [[ALEC]] (American Legislative Exchange Council) model-legislation drafting to actual state-legislative outputs. The pipeline is operationally distinct from the [[Koch to SPN to ALEC to state legislation pipeline]] connection note because it focuses specifically on Heritage Foundation's policy-substrate contribution rather than on the broader Koch Network funding architecture.

## Operational pathway

**Heritage Foundation policy production:** Heritage produces substantial federal-level policy research across multiple policy categories including economic policy, social policy, education policy, election-administration policy, and adjacent subject areas. The Heritage policy production is principally federal-policy-oriented but produces substantial output that translates to state-policy applications.

**ALEC model-legislation drafting:** ALEC operates working groups across multiple policy categories that translate Heritage and adjacent federal-policy research into state-legislative model bills. The model bills are then distributed to state legislators who introduce them in state legislatures.

**State-legislative implementation:** Republican-majority state legislatures across the past 15 years have substantially implemented ALEC model legislation across multiple policy categories. The cumulative effect across 50 state legislatures over 15-30 years is operationally substantial reshaping of US subnational policy environment.

## Specific Heritage-ALEC coordination examples

**Voter-ID and election-administration restrictions:** Heritage's Election Integrity research programme has substantially shaped ALEC model legislation on voter-ID requirements, voter-roll-purge procedures, election-board personnel requirements, and adjacent election-administration topics.

**Right-to-work and anti-public-employee-union legislation:** Heritage's labour-policy research programme has substantially shaped ALEC model legislation on right-to-work requirements and adjacent labour-policy categories.

**Charter-school and school-voucher policy:** Heritage's education-policy research programme has substantially shaped ALEC model legislation on school-choice mechanisms.

**Tax-cut and regulatory-rollback legislation:** Heritage's tax-policy research programme has substantially shaped ALEC model legislation on tax-reduction mechanisms and regulatory-rollback frameworks.

**Anti-abortion regulatory legislation (post-Dobbs particularly):** Heritage's social-policy research programme has substantially shaped ALEC model legislation on post-Dobbs state-level abortion regulation.

## The Project 2025 integration

The post-2023 Project 2025 programme has substantially integrated the Heritage-ALEC pipeline with the broader executive-branch policy programme. The pattern: Project 2025 articulates executive-branch policy positions; Heritage-ALEC coordinates state-level implementation infrastructure; the integrated programme operates at both federal and subnational levels simultaneously.

## Why this connection is load-bearing

The Heritage-ALEC-state-law pipeline is the principal mechanism through which Heritage's federal-policy research has produced operational subnational policy consequences. Removing the ALEC translation layer would force Heritage's state-level policy influence into bilateral-state-legislator relationships with substantial loss of coordination efficiency.

The connection also documents the broader pattern of policy-production-and-implementation integration that distinguishes the contemporary conservative-movement infrastructure from earlier periods.
