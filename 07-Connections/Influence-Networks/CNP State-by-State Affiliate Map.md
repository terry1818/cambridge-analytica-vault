# CNP State-by-State Affiliate Map

The [[Council for National Policy]] does NOT operate as a single national lobbying organization. It operates as the **coordinating hub** for a multi-armed state-level operational infrastructure. This note maps that infrastructure.

## The three principal CNP-affiliated operational arms

Per Bill Moyers' *Shadow Network* coverage and Anne Nelson's 2019 book:

### Arm 1: [[State Policy Network]] (SPN) - state-policy think tanks
- 68 affiliates + 99 associates in all 50 states
- State-level policy authoring + model legislation
- Founded 1992; HQ Arlington VA

### Arm 2: [[Leadership Institute]] (LI) - personnel pipeline
- 250,000+ alumni trained since 1979
- Candidate + activist + media training
- Campus Reform + Campus Leadership Program
- Founded 1979; HQ Arlington VA
- Founder Morton Blackwell is a long-time CNP member

### Arm 3: [[Family Research Council]] (FRC) - DC lobbying + Christian-right mobilization
- Founded 1981 by James Dobson
- Currently led by Tony Perkins (also CNP president 2018-2021)
- Pray Vote Stand Summit annual conference
- SPLC-designated hate group

## Methodology for reconstructing the state-by-state map

The CNP State-by-State Affiliate Map reconstructs CNP membership and influence at the state-by-state level using multiple convergent sources: published CNP-leaked membership rosters (principally the [[Source Anne Nelson Shadow Network 2019]] consolidation), state-level political-finance disclosures, [[State Policy Network]] affiliate organisation membership patterns, [[Federalist Society]] state-chapter leadership, and [[Convention of States Action]] / [[Citizens for Self-Governance]] state-coordinator records.

The convergence methodology produces state-level pictures of varying completeness. States with strong CNP presence (Texas, Oklahoma, Tennessee, North Carolina, Virginia, Arizona, Florida) have substantially complete pictures. States with weaker CNP presence (most Northeast, the Pacific Northwest) have substantially less complete pictures because the underlying institutional infrastructure is thinner.

## Specific state-level patterns

**Texas:** Substantially complete CNP / Federalist Society / Heritage / TPPF (Texas Public Policy Foundation) / COSA-Texas institutional convergence. Texas is the most operationally integrated single state for the broader CNP-aligned network.

**Tennessee:** Substantial CNP membership concentration, particularly through Nashville-area religious-right institutional infrastructure. Increasing operational integration through 2024-2026 around state-level Article V organising.

**Virginia:** Operational significance disproportionate to membership density, because of proximity to DC-area institutional infrastructure. Many CNP members domiciled in Virginia operate primarily at the federal-level.

**Arizona:** Substantial growth in CNP-aligned institutional infrastructure since 2020, partly through [[Goldwater Institute]] and adjacent SPN affiliates, partly through Turning Point USA expansion under [[Charlie Kirk]].

**Florida:** Substantial CNP membership concentration and substantial growth in CNP-aligned political infrastructure post-2020 (the post-pandemic Florida political-realignment and the broader DeSantis-era institutional build-out).
