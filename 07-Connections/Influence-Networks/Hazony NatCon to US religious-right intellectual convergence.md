# Connection - Hazony NatCon to US religious-right intellectual convergence

A connection note documenting the intellectual-convergence pathway from [[Yoram Hazony]]'s Israeli religious-Zionist intellectual programme to the US National Conservatism intellectual movement and the broader US religious-right intellectual ecosystem.

## The intellectual pathway

Hazony's intellectual project across his Shalem Center / Edmund Burke Foundation work has principally articulated:

- The case for nationalism as a positive political-philosophical category against post-WWII liberal-cosmopolitan consensus
- The legitimacy of explicitly religious-aligned national identity
- The compatibility of religious-national positioning with constitutional democracy
- The substantive policy positions of post-liberal conservative-intellectual movement

The 2018 book "The Virtue of Nationalism" was the principal published statement; the [[National Conservatism Conference]] (NatCon) series launched 2019 has been the principal operational vehicle.

## US adoption pattern

The Hazony-NatCon intellectual programme has been substantially adopted by US conservative-intellectual figures:

- [[JD Vance]] - frequent NatCon participant; substantial intellectual alignment
- [[Kevin Roberts]] (Heritage Foundation president) - NatCon participant; substantial alignment in Heritage post-2021 pivot
- [[Russell Vought]] - NatCon participant; substantial alignment in Project 2025 framing
- [[Patrick Deneen]] - NatCon participant; substantial intellectual convergence with Catholic Integralism position
- [[Adrian Vermeule]] - NatCon participant; substantial intellectual convergence
- [[Sohrab Ahmari]] - frequent NatCon participant; First Things magazine venue overlap

The adoption pattern is operationally distinctive because it represents direct Israeli-intellectual influence on US religious-right and Tech-Right intellectual movements. The pathway operates principally through formal intellectual venues (NatCon conferences, First Things magazine, adjacent publications) rather than through informal personal networks.

## Theological-political compatibility

The pathway is operationally enabled by substantial theological-political compatibility:

- Religious Zionism's explicit religious-national positioning maps onto US Christian-Nationalist framings
- The post-liberal critique of post-WWII liberal-cosmopolitan consensus is shared across Israeli religious-Zionist and US Catholic-Integralism / Christian-Nationalism intellectual traditions
- The substantive policy positions (family-policy, immigration-policy, scepticism of international institutions) substantially align

## Operational consequences

The intellectual convergence produces operational consequences:

- Substantial Trump 2.0 personnel pool with explicit NatCon-alignment positioning
- Substantial coordination between US National Conservatism intellectual movement and Israeli religious-Zionist intellectual right
- Substantial alignment with broader [[International Right MOC]] network including Hungarian, Italian, Argentine right-wing intellectual movements
