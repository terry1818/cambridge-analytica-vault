# Connection: Mercers ↔ Bannon (political alliance)

## What the link is

The **Mercer-Bannon political alliance** was the strategic engine of the CA-era right-wing infrastructure. Bannon brought political vision and media operations; the Mercers brought capital. Together they built a coordinated stack: targeting tool ([[Cambridge Analytica]]), opposition research ([[Government Accountability Institute]]), narrative engine ([[Breitbart News]]), donor vehicles ([[Make America Number 1 Super PAC]]).

## Evidence

- Mercers acquired Breitbart News stake; Bannon became executive chair after Andrew Breitbart's 2012 death
- Mercer Family Foundation co-founded GAI with Bannon and [[Peter Schweizer]] in 2012
- Bannon held $1-5M CA stake; Mercers held the controlling position
- Mercers' Make America Number 1 PAC supported candidates Bannon advised
- Bannon became Trump campaign CEO in August 2016 with Mercer backing
- Bannon-Mercer alliance fractured after 2018 (Mercers distanced from Bannon following his criticism of Trump family in Wolff book)

## Confidence

`#confidence/high` — extensively documented across multiple primary sources.

## Implications

CA is best understood as one component of the Mercer-Bannon political infrastructure, not as a standalone firm. Tracking the alliance's continued/discontinued operations post-2018 is essential.
