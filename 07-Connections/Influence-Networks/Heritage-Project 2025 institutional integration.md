# Heritage-Project 2025 institutional integration

A pairwise connection note documenting the institutional integration between [[Heritage Foundation]] operations and [[Project 2025]]. The vault treats this integration as operationally significant because it means Project 2025 is not a discrete project with a defined start and end; it is the current operational mode of Heritage itself.

## The institutional embedding

Project 2025 is institutionally housed at Heritage. Specific institutional-embedding elements:

- The project management is Heritage staff (Paul Dans was Project Director through 2024; the post-Dans operational leadership has continued as Heritage staff functions).
- The personnel database is hosted on Heritage infrastructure; applicant intake and vetting flows through Heritage staff.
- The training programme (Presidential Administration Academy) is delivered by Heritage.
- The publishing-and-distribution capacity for the 922-page mandate document and adjacent materials is Heritage's.
- The institutional sponsor and lead public face is Heritage Foundation president [[Kevin Roberts]].

## The Roberts-era pivot context

The Heritage-Project 2025 institutional integration occurs against the backdrop of the broader Roberts-era pivot at Heritage. Under Kevin Roberts (president since October 2021), Heritage repositioned from a traditional movement-conservative think tank toward an explicitly New Right organisation aligned with [[Catholic Integralism]]-adjacent and post-liberal intellectual positions.

The pivot involved substantial staff turnover, ideological realignment, public confrontation with previously aligned organisations (notably [[Cato Institute]] over libertarian-vs-national-conservative tensions), and the operational leadership of Project 2025. The [[Source National Review 2025-12 Heritage Implodes]] reporting documents the internal controversies that followed.

## The continuing operational model

Because Project 2025 is housed at Heritage rather than as a separate organisation with a defined end-date, the Project 2025 operational mode will continue post-2024 in whatever institutional shape Heritage adopts for the 2028 cycle and beyond. The staffing, the policy production, the personnel pipeline, and the training programme are all Heritage's operational mode now.

This is operationally distinct from the 1980 [[Mandate for Leadership]] precedent. The 1980 document was a discrete project; Heritage published it, the Reagan administration drew from it, and Heritage moved on to other operational priorities. The 2023-onwards Project 2025 is a sustained operational mode rather than a discrete project.

## Why this connection is load-bearing

The vault's analytical position is that understanding Project 2025 as a continuing operational mode of Heritage rather than as a discrete project is necessary to understand its operational implications. A discrete project ends; an institutional mode continues. The Trump 2.0 implementation of Project 2025 policy programme draws on continuing Heritage operational capacity that will be available regardless of which specific administration is in office at any given point.

## Trump 2.0 cabinet and appointee implications

The Heritage-Project 2025 institutional integration substantially shapes the Trump 2.0 cabinet and appointee population. The "approximately 70 percent" Project 2025 / Trump 2.0 overlap figure documented in [[Source DeSmog 2025-06-02 70 Percent Cabinet]] reflects the personnel-pipeline output of the integration: candidates vetted through Heritage's Project 2025 personnel database become the appointee pool for Trump 2.0 positions.

## Cross-cluster implications

The Heritage-Project 2025 integration also intersects with the [[Council for National Policy]] coordination layer (Heritage and CNP have substantial overlapping board membership), with the [[Federalist Society]] judicial-pipeline ecosystem (Heritage's policy positions complement Federalist Society judicial-nomination work), and with the broader [[Religious Right MOC]] cluster (Heritage's religious-right policy positioning is operationally aligned with the broader religious-right institutional infrastructure).
