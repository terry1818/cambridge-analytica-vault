# Counter-COS Strategy for the Left

This is the operational counter-strategy companion to [[Lens - COS as Constitutional Capture Vector]] and [[Hypothesis - COS is the permanent capture arm of Project 2025]]. Built for Terry's stated goal: "build something that counters it for the Left."

## The core problem

The Left has been **playing reactive defense on COS for 13 years** while the Right has been on offense. State-by-state, COS has flipped 20 of 34 needed states. The Left's counter-infrastructure does not exist at the scale the threat warrants.

**The Left needs to move from defense to offense.** Specifically:

1. **Defensive infrastructure** (block COS in remaining target states + rescind in already-passed states)
2. **Offensive infrastructure** (use the Article V mechanism itself against COS)
3. **Long-game capital** (sustained funding apparatus matching Mercer/Koch scale)
4. **Personnel pipeline** (state legislators trained to fight; legal infrastructure ready)
5. **Public awareness** (the average voter has never heard of Article V; this is the biggest leverage point)

## The seven tactical priorities (ranked by impact x feasibility)

### Priority 1: Stop the next 4 states (highest leverage, lowest cost)

The pace is currently ~3 states per year passing COS. **Block 4 states in 2026-2027 and you push the timeline beyond the current Trump political window.**

**Tactic:** Identify the 3-5 Democratic state senators or state reps in each chamber who could lead an anti-COS coalition. Fund them with research, talking points, model amendments to weaken/derail the COS resolution.

**Cost estimate:** $1-2M per state for sustained 2-year campaigns. **Total: $5-10M for 5 states.**

### Priority 2: Rescission campaigns in already-passed states with Dem trifectas

States that have passed COS but now have Democratic trifectas should formally rescind. Rescissions are legally contested but politically powerful Ã¢â‚¬â€ they:

- Reduce the apparent count toward 34
- Create legal ambiguity that helps litigation
- Shift the narrative

**Target states:** Virginia (if Dem trifecta returns), North Carolina (split), others.

**Cost estimate:** $500K-$1M per state.

### Priority 3: Delegate-selection statutes in blue states

**This is the most under-discussed leverage point.** Even if a convention is called, **the delegates are selected by state legislatures**. Blue states should enact statutes NOW that:

- Require delegate selection by gubernatorial appointment (where Dem governor exists)
- Bind delegates to specific instructions
- Allow recall of delegates
- Specify proportional or population-weighted state representation if convention rules allow

**Cost estimate:** $200K per state x 20 blue/purple states = $4M.

### Priority 4: Standing litigation packet

Build a standing legal-action package ready to deploy the moment a 34th state is on the verge of passing. Includes:

- Suit to challenge application aggregation (different applications on different subjects shouldn't aggregate)
- Suit to enforce state rescissions
- Suit to invalidate the convention rules adoption
- Delegate-selection constitutional challenges

**Lead orgs:** ACLU, Brennan Center, Public Citizen. **Cost estimate:** $5M to build the package; $20M+ to litigate.

### Priority 5: A counter-application strategy (the offensive move)

**The most aggressive option:** the Left files its own Article V applications, on different subjects (campaign finance reform, voting rights amendment, abortion rights amendment).

**Why this is risky-but-potentially-powerful:**

- Creates legal ambiguity about whether applications "on different subjects" aggregate to the 34 threshold
- If they DO aggregate, the convention's scope is constitutionally unlimitable Ã¢â‚¬â€ which **destroys COS's "limited convention" claim**
- If they DON'T aggregate, the Left has the same playbook tool the Right has
- Forces a Supreme Court ruling on aggregation that constrains the Right

**Risk:** the same convention that emerges might still be GOP-dominated. This is a doomsday option.

**Cost estimate:** $10M to mount the campaign.

### Priority 6: Public awareness campaign (mass media)

Most voters have never heard of Article V. Conservative voters HAVE heard of it via WarRoom, COS Action, conservative talk radio. Liberal/independent voters have not. **Massive asymmetry.**

**Tactic:** national-scale ad campaign + creator/influencer pipeline + late-night-comedy coverage + The Daily Show / John Oliver-style explainer.

**Cost estimate:** $20-50M sustained over 2-3 years.

### Priority 7: Build the Left's "Conservative Partnership Institute" analog

The Right has [[Jim DeMint]]'s CPI as the personnel/training/coordination hub. The Left has nothing equivalent specifically for democracy infrastructure. **Build one.**

Functions:

- Train state legislators on the COS threat (model bill defense, rescission tactics)
- Train litigators on Article V case law
- Coordinate across orgs (Common Cause, Brennan, ACLU, etc.)
- Operate as the personnel pipeline for democracy-defense work

**Cost estimate:** $5M/year ongoing.
