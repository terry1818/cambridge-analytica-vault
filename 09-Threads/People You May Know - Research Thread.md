## Key claims documented in PYMK

### 1. The COFI-Gloo-CA collaboration
- [[Koch network]] commissioned [[COFI]] (religious charity) and [[Gloo]] (software company) to work with [[Cambridge Analytica]] to build a church-targeting data platform called "Insights"
- The platform applies CA-style psychographic methodology to **church congregants and seekers**

### 2. The vulnerable-population targeting
- The platform specifically targets people in **vulnerable life states**: mental illness, grief, addiction, financial distress, recent divorce, recent bereavement, recent job loss
- Targeting these populations for **evangelical-church recruitment**, then mobilizing the recruits for far-right political action via the church-pastor network

### 3. The 20,000-pastor disclosure
- Canadian Pastor **Craig Buroker** revealed on camera (March 2017) that he learned Trump won because **20,000 pastors in 11 American swing states** had given their **congregation databases of 15 million individuals** to a "big data firm" for a political campaign
- This is the most-cited specific datapoint from the documentary

### 4. The Steve Bannon / evangelical conclave bridge
- The June 2016 Times Square hotel meeting organized by [[Bill Dallas]] (UIP) was a turning point in evangelical-Trump alignment
- Bannon attended; this meeting is partial origin of the formal CA-evangelical pipeline
- The post-meeting infrastructure became the operational fusion documented in PYMK

### 5. The COFI / Gloo / UIP architecture
- COFI = religious charity (501(c)(3))
- Gloo = software platform; "Cambridge Analytica on steroids"
- UIP = the broader political-mobilization umbrella
- Per documentary: tied to the heart of the White House (Trump 1.0)

## Operational hypothesis behind this thread

The "People You May Know" research thread starts from a specific observation: the People You May Know (PYMK) feature on Facebook, LinkedIn, and similar social platforms produces friend-and-connection suggestions that are operationally consequential for the broader network-effect dynamics the vault tracks. The PYMK algorithm is not neutral: it amplifies certain network-formation patterns over others, with documented consequences for political-organising network structure, for movement-cluster formation, and for the broader information-environment topology.

The research thread investigates a specific subhypothesis: the PYMK-style network-amplification mechanisms have substantially shaped the operational formation of the Tech-Right, Religious-Right, and broader vault-tracked network clusters in ways that distinguish their network topology from prior political-movement network structures.

## Specific dynamics the thread tracks

**The clustering effect.** PYMK algorithms preferentially recommend connections within existing tight clusters (where many of your existing connections already know each other) over bridge connections (where the suggested person is in a different cluster from your existing connections). The dynamic produces network-topology consequences: existing tight clusters tighten further; bridges across clusters become rarer; the resulting network is more cluster-segregated than a random-network baseline.

**The amplification effect.** Within a tight cluster, PYMK preferentially recommends the highest-degree-centrality individuals (those already maximally connected within the cluster). The dynamic produces hub-formation consequences: the most-connected individuals become even more connected, with disproportionate effect on cluster-level information-flow and coordination capacity.

**The endogenous formation effect.** PYMK algorithms learn from the connection-formation behaviour they themselves enable. The dynamic produces lock-in consequences: once a cluster has formed, the algorithm becomes a positive-feedback mechanism that maintains and amplifies the cluster structure even if external conditions would otherwise favour cluster dissolution.

## Implications for the vault network analysis

The PYMK-amplification thesis has specific implications for how the vault interprets the network topology it documents:

- The tight integration of the [[Council for National Policy]] / [[The Fellowship]] / [[Federalist Society]] / [[Heritage Foundation]] cluster is partially algorithmically reinforced by social-platform network-formation dynamics, beyond the operational-coordination factors the vault primarily tracks.
- The tight integration of the [[Tech Right]] cluster around [[Peter Thiel]] / [[Elon Musk]] / [[Marc Andreessen]] / [[David Sacks]] / All-In Podcast / a16z ecosystem is partially algorithmically reinforced.
- The relative absence of bridge connections between vault-tracked clusters (the Religious Right and the Tech Right, while operationally converging in the 2024-2026 period, do not have the dense social-platform network-formation cross-cluster that within-cluster integration shows) is partially the inverse-amplification consequence of the PYMK-style algorithm.
