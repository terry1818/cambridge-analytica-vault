# Full Arc — Evidence Strength Audit

## Purpose

This note audits the vault's central thesis — **the "full arc"** — for external scrutiny. It exists because the thesis cannot be "proven as fact" in one move: it is a chain of links, and the links are not all equal. Some are documented record; some are well-supported inference; some are genuinely contested. A reviewer (funder analyst, journalist, hostile critic) will attack the weakest link and use it to dismiss the rest.

The audit's job: separate the **load-bearing facts** from the **interpretation that connects them**, so the thesis can be stated at the strength each part actually supports. The audit does **not** contradict the vault's hypothesis confidence ratings — it does a finer-grained split (documented fact vs. analytic claim) that a `confidence/high` tag alone does not make.

Grading vocabulary maps to [[Evidence Tiers]] and [[Methodology - Verifying a CA claim]]:

- **LOCKED** — Tier-1 primary record (filings, registries, regulatory findings, the EO text itself). State flatly, no hedge.
- **STRONG INFERENCE** — multiple independent Tier-1/Tier-2 sources, consistent, but the *claim itself is interpretive* (a structural argument, not a recorded fact). State with the reasoning visible.
- **CONTESTED / OPEN** — sources disagree, causation is unprovable, or the vault's own falsification conditions are live. Never assert as fact; pre-empt instead.

## The thesis under audit

> Cambridge Analytica was never a self-contained 2013–2018 scandal. It was a *prototype*. The harvesting-and-targeting capability did not die with CA's 2018 bankruptcy — it dispersed into successor firms, into state-actor operations, and into AI. By 2024–2026 the equivalent capability has been absorbed *inside the US federal government*, and it operates as one coordinated project — backed by a ~90-year coalition infrastructure — aimed at defeating democratic majorities.

That paragraph contains ~13 distinct claims. They are audited below.

## Column 1 — LOCKED (Tier-1 record; state as fact)

These are documented. State them without hedging.

**L1. Cambridge Analytica existed and ran data-driven political targeting, 2013–2018.**
Basis: UK Companies House filings; [[Source FTC Complaint Cambridge Analytica 2019]]; [[Source DCMS Final Report 2019]]; [[Source ICO 2020-11 Cambridge Analytica investigation]]. Tier-1.

**L2. CA obtained the personal data of tens of millions of Facebook users via the Kogan/GSR app, in violation of platform terms.**
Basis: FTC complaint; ICO investigation; Facebook's own admission; [[Source Rosenberg et al 2018-03-17 NYT]] (reporters viewed extant data). Tier-1/Tier-2.
⚠️ **State the scale carefully.** The vault's own [[Methodology - Verifying a CA claim]] fixes the documented range at **50M–87M profiles**. Use "up to 87 million." Do not use CA's marketing figures ("5,000 data points on 230M Americans") — the vault flags those as fabricated for client acquisition.

**L3. CA / SCL were paid contractors on the 2015–16 Cruz campaign, the 2016 Trump campaign, and CA-linked AggregateIQ worked Vote Leave.**
Basis: FEC records; [[Source UK Electoral Commission Vote Leave 2018]] (regulatory enforcement); ICO. Tier-1.

**L4. CA and SCL entered insolvency in May 2018; Nix was later disqualified as a UK director.**
Basis: Companies House; [[Source gov.uk 2020-09-24 Nix disqualification]] (UK Insolvency Service). Tier-1.

**L5. Emerdata Limited was incorporated in August 2017 — before the public scandal — and named CA/SCL personnel moved into it and other successor entities.**
Basis: [[Source Companies House 2018-03-16 Emerdata appointments]]; Companies House incorporation record. Tier-1. *(This date is the single strongest fact in the "continuity" half of the arc — the successor vehicle pre-dates the reckoning.)*

**L6. Under Trump 2.0, Palantir holds $1B+ in federal contracts and the EO "Eliminating Information Silos" (2025-03-20) exists as signed executive action.**
Basis: USAspending.gov; the Federal Register text of [[Trump EO Eliminating Information Silos 2025-03-20]]. Tier-1.

**L7. The institutional overlap between Project 2025, Heritage, ALEC, and Convention of States is documented:** Heritage publishes P2025 and formally endorses an Article V convention; ALEC publishes the Article V model resolution; Mercer Family Foundation gave Citizens for Self-Governance a founding grant; named individuals (DeMint, Farris, Meckler) hold roles across both.
Basis: organizational records, 990s, [[Source EXPOSEDbyCMD 2024-10 Article V Supercharge P2025]]. Tier-1/Tier-2.

**L8. State-actor and commercial influence-for-hire operations using persona/psychographic methods exist and are active:** [[Team Jorge]] (exposed 2023, hidden-camera primary footage), [[Doppelganger]], [[Spamouflage Dragonbridge]].
Basis: [[Source Forbidden Stories 2023 Team Jorge]]; Meta/Graphika threat reports. Tier-1/Tier-2.

## Column 2 — STRONG INFERENCE (well-evidenced, but interpretive; show the reasoning)

These rest on real evidence the vault rates `confidence/high`, but each is a **structural argument**, not a recorded fact. State them as "the evidence strongly indicates," never as "it is a fact that."

**S1. CA's methodology, personnel, and IP survived the bankruptcy substantially intact.**
Vault: [[Hypothesis - CA methodology survived 2018 bankruptcy]], [[Hypothesis - Successor entities preserve operational capability]] — both `confidence/high`. The *facts* underneath (L5; personnel moves; Emerdata "fully acquired" SCL/CA assets per Fast Company 2019) are locked. The *inference* — "capability preserved **intact**" — carries a real ambiguity the vault names itself: no successor reached CA's peak scale, and "CA-specific continuity" is hard to distinguish from "the technique became industry-baseline." Claim the dispersal; don't overclaim the magnitude.

**S2. There is a continuous defense → commercial-elections pipeline.**
Vault: [[Hypothesis - Defense-to-elections pipeline]], `confidence/high`. Multiple documented cases across jurisdictions (SCL's MoD/DoD contracts; HBGary; Psy-Group; Team Jorge). Solid as a *pattern*; it is not a claim about one chain of custody.

**S3. Multiple right-wing donor networks converged in Trump 2.0; the federal-database model now supersedes the CA external-vendor model.**
Vault: [[Hypothesis - Multiple right networks converged in Trump 2.0]], [[Hypothesis - Federal database supersedes CA model]], [[Hypothesis - CA infrastructure migrated to Big Tech government convergence]] — all `confidence/high`. Component facts are locked (L6; cabinet appointments; contract values). The claim that this **is the CA capability migrating** is a structural analogy — a strong one, but an analytic claim. The vault's own disconfirming evidence (Koch never fully aligned; Musk–Trump fracture mid-2025) belongs in the statement.

**S4. COS and Project 2025 are coordinated as a deliberate short-game / long-game strategy.**
Vault: [[Hypothesis - COS is the permanent capture arm of Project 2025]], `confidence/high`. The overlap (L7) is locked. "Coordinated **by design** as one strategy" is the inference — well-supported by personnel, funding, and the Aug 2023 Williamsburg mock-convention agenda match, but still inference.

## Column 3 — CONTESTED / OPEN (never assert as fact; pre-empt)

These are the links a hostile reviewer will go for. Naming them first is what protects the rest of the thesis.

**C1. That CA's psychographics *worked* or were *decisive* in 2016 / Brexit.**
This is the single most attackable point. The vault's own [[Methodology - Verifying a CA claim]] worked example rates the strong causal version `claim/disputed` + `confidence/low`: NYT (Confessore–Hakim, 2017) reported CA executives conceded psychographics were not actually used in the Trump campaign; academic work (Hersh, Nyhan) is skeptical; election causation is "unprovable." **Defensible version:** "CA was a paid contractor whose specific electoral contribution cannot be empirically isolated." The arc does **not** need CA to have "won" 2016 — it only needs CA to have been a prototype. Frame it that way.

**C2. That CA's actual harvested *dataset* is still in operational use today.**
Vault: [[Hypothesis - CA data survives]] — `confidence/medium`, not high. Persistence *in 2018* is documented (NYT viewed samples; Wylie). Operational use *now* is "plausible but not publicly documented." Do not state current-use as fact.

**C3. That Russian/Chinese state operations descend causally from CA specifically.**
L8 (these ops exist, use similar methods) is locked. "The CA playbook spread to them" is partly inference, partly contested — parallel independent evolution is a live alternative the vault acknowledges. Say "convergent methods," not "CA's playbook."

**C4. That COS is close to triggering a convention.**
The vault's own falsification test #1 says the state count has moved slowly across 15+ years and reaching 34 "may take additional decade-plus timeframes"; Article V's subject-limited theory is constitutionally untested. The threat is real and worth tracking; "imminent" is not supported.

**C5. KEYSTONE — that all of this is *one coordinated project* with continuous intent across ~90 years, trending to "fascism."**
This is the thesis's roof, and the vault is deliberately careful here. [[Hypothesis - Three-wing coalition]] states it is "a **coalition** rather than a unified ideology," with measurable internal tensions, and that "whether it consolidates into textbook fascism is the open empirical question." Scholarship is split (Paxton vs. Levitsky/Ziblatt vs. Snyder/Stanley). The 1935→2026 theocratic infrastructure is real and individually documented — but "90-year arc" is an **organizing lens**, not a proven chain of causation. Present the keystone as the vault's **leading interpretation with stated falsification conditions** — which is exactly how the vault itself frames it. Asserting it as settled fact is the fastest way to lose a serious audience.

## How to pitch the arc (the payoff)

1. **Open on the LOCKED spine (L1–L8).** Eight documented facts, Tier-1 sourced. Nothing here is arguable. This is the floor a reviewer cannot knock out.
2. **Present STRONG INFERENCE (S1–S4) as inference, out loud.** "The evidence strongly indicates X" beats "X is fact" — because the honest framing is the one that survives the follow-up question.
3. **Raise CONTESTED (C1–C5) yourself, before the reviewer does.** Pre-empting C1 ("no, the arc doesn't require CA to have won 2016") and C5 ("the vault holds this as a leading interpretation with falsification tests, not a proven fact") turns your biggest vulnerabilities into evidence of rigor.

A thesis presented this way is **harder to dismiss** than one asserted as proven fact — because the grading *is* the credibility. The vault's tier discipline and falsification tests are not hedging to apologize for; they are the strongest asset in the pitch.
