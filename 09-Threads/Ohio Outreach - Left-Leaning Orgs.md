## Coalition-Hub Strategy

Pitching one-off education sessions makes you the visiting expert. Pitching a coalition with you as the hub makes you the connector - substantially more leverage.

### Coalition name candidates

- Ohio Democracy Defense Coalition (clearest, broadest tent)
- Counter-Capture Ohio
- Defend Ohio's Constitution
- Ohio Allies for Democracy

Pick one and own it. Brand consistency matters.

### Infrastructure to stand up (week 0, before any outreach)

1. **Landing page** - Carrd.co or Squarespace; mission, member-org list (starts blank/aspirational), join button, education-session calendar, resource library link. Budget: ~$10/mo.
2. **Email list** - Mailchimp/Beehiiv/Substack free tier; monthly newsletter to member orgs. Budget: free at small scale.
3. **Shared resource library** - Google Drive or Notion workspace with slide deck, prototype app, briefing memos, opposition research, ready-to-share content. Budget: free.
4. **Monthly convening** - recurring Zoom call (first Wednesday?); member-org reps share what they're seeing; Terry facilitates. Budget: free.
5. **Signal/Slack channel** - fast-channel for inter-convening coordination. Budget: free.
6. **Brand basics** - logo (Canva), email signature, one-pager PDF, ~15-slide pitch deck. Budget: free or ~$10/mo Canva.

Total infrastructure cost: under $50/month.

### Reframed pitch template

**Subject:** Joining [Coalition Name] - Ohio orgs defending against constitutional capture

> Hi [name],
>
> I'm Terry Rhea, Columbus-based. I'm building a coalition of Ohio left-leaning organizations to share intelligence, coordinate education, and defend against the constitutional-capture programme that COS, Project 2025, and the broader right-wing infrastructure are running in Ohio and nationally.
>
> The coalition is starting small and growing deliberately. We want orgs doing real work on adjacent threats (voting rights, abortion rights, public-ed defense, labor, civil liberties, climate, faith-based progressive).
>
> The value to your org:
>
> 1. Free monthly intelligence briefings on what the right-wing infrastructure is doing in Ohio and where it is heading next
> 2. Shared resource library - slide decks, talking points, briefing memos you can use with your members
> 3. Cross-org coordination - when something hits, we move together instead of separately
> 4. A custom counter-COS web app (prototype) you can share with your members for education and engagement
> 5. No dues, no membership obligations - just a shared signal channel and a monthly call you can opt into
>
> First step is a 20-minute conversation about whether the fit is right for your org. I am based in Columbus and happy to do in-person, otherwise Zoom.
>
> What does your scheduling look like in the next two weeks?
>
> Terry Rhea
> [email] / [phone]
> [coalition landing page URL]

### Execution sequence

**Week 0 (before any outreach):** Stand up infrastructure (landing page, email list, Drive, brand). Pick the coalition name and stick with it.

**Week 1:** Soft-pilot with 3-5 Columbus orgs you have any warm thread into. The first 3 yeses become founding members - list them on the landing page as social proof for the next round.

**Week 2-3:** Tier 1 Columbus + statewide cold outreach (24 orgs). Include landing page link with founding-member logos.

**Week 4:** First monthly Zoom convening. Even if only 4-5 orgs show, that is the proof of concept.

**Week 5+:** Tier 1 Labor + Tier 2 metros. Each new member becomes another logo on the landing page.

### Why coalition-hub beats one-off speaker

- Orgs do not have time for one-off speakers; they DO have time for a no-cost intelligence-sharing network
- Being the hub means controlling the calendar, the messaging, and the resource library
- Scales: 30 orgs in the coalition is the same admin work as 5 orgs once infrastructure exists
- The app pitch lands more naturally as "the coalition shared tool" than as "Terry personal project"
- Gives a credible answer to "who else is in this?" - the founding-member list

## Outreach tracking (kanban)

Use whatever tool works (Notion, Trello, Todoist, plain text):

- **To contact:** cold outreach not sent
- **Contacted:** email or call sent, awaiting response
- **Booked:** intro conversation scheduled
- **Founding member:** signed on to the coalition
- **Active member:** attending monthly convenings
- **Cold / declined:** maintain relationship for future re-approach

## Pre-outreach asset checklist

Before sending Week 1 cold outreach, have ready:

- [ ] Coalition name picked
- [ ] Landing page live (even minimal v1)
- [ ] Email signup list active
- [ ] Resource Drive populated with at least: 1-pager PDF, 15-slide pitch deck, app prototype walkthrough video
- [ ] Speaker bio paragraph (Terry, what you bring)
- [ ] Founding-member commitment from at least 1 Columbus org (warm intro)
- [ ] Monthly Zoom calendar invite drafted (first convening date set)
