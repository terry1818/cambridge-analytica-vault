What the dated record locks:

- **The successor vehicle pre-dates the scandal by 7 months.** SCL chairman Wheatland incorporated Emerdata 11 Aug 2017; the Wylie disclosures broke 17 Mar 2018.
- **23 Jan 2018:** a £1,912,502 capital injection (1,596,874 ordinary + 315,628 preference shares) coincides with Nix, Ko, Peng, and Al Khatib joining as directors; Wheatland and Tayler cease as significant owners (Siegelman, Companies House filing review).
- **16 Mar 2018** — one day before the Wylie disclosures — Rebekah and Jennifer Mercer appointed directors.
- **28 Mar 2018:** Nix resigns; Tayler appointed director the *same day*.
- **Emerdata is still an active company in 2026** — the Mercers remain directors; a new director (Golub) was appointed Dec 2024. The holding entity did not dissolve with CA.

### Corroboration & the wider web (Tier-2)

- Investigative journalist **Wendy Siegelman** broke the Emerdata story (Mar 2018) from Companies House filings and wrote it up for **The Guardian** ("Cambridge Analytica is dead — but its obscure network is alive and well"). Tier-2 (named researcher, primary-document access — the vault's exact Tier-2 definition).
- **NYT, May 2018** confirmed Emerdata was created to become the new CA; SCL executive **Nigel Oakes** described SCL Group and CA "rolling up into Emerdata." Tier-2.
- Related successor vehicles, all Companies House–documented: **Firecrest Technologies Ltd** (inc. 7 Mar 2018, Nix director, Emerdata listed as person with significant control); **Auspex International** (London, Jul 2018 — Mark Turnbull, former head of CA's international political division, director until Sept 2021; Al Khatib sole investor; Craig Tiedman COO); **Questionardo Ltd / Qudo** (Al Khatib + Ko; Mihajlo Popesku, ex-SCL Elections Head of Research); **Dynamo Recoveries Ltd** (litigation special-purpose vehicle, per the SCL Elections progress report filed 15 Jun 2022).
- **Data Propria** (US, 2018) — President Matt Oczkowski (ex-CA head of product), David Wilkinson (ex-CA lead data scientist), ≥4 ex-CA staff (AP, NBC News). Tier-2.

### Verdict

The audit's **S1** ("methodology, personnel and IP survived the bankruptcy") splits cleanly:

- The **corporate / directorship / personnel-flow facts → LOCKED.** Tier-1 Companies House, with Tier-2 corroboration. State flatly.
- The **operational-capability claim stays STRONG INFERENCE.** Directorships are corporate control, not proof the targeting capability moved. Siegelman's own 2022 review states the question of whether "the data, tools and intellectual property… transferred to Emerdata or other companies" is **"murky"** and unresolved. Do not overclaim the magnitude.

## Target 2 — Post-2018 CA-lineage campaign → **PARTIAL (does NOT upgrade C2)**

Two documented post-2018 engagements by CA-lineage firms:

- **Data Propria → Trump 2020 re-election effort + RNC 2018 work.** Reported by AP (overheard Oczkowski–Parscale conversation: "doing the president's work for 2020") and NBC News. Tier-2.
- **Auspex International → €200,000 consulting contract for ALDE (Alliance of Liberals and Democrats) in Romania, 2019.** Byline Times investigation (2022, Matt Bernardini) found Auspex's projects "similar to those performed by Cambridge Analytica," tracing the work to a leaked 2016 SCL Group proposal to ALDE. Tier-2/Tier-3.

**Verdict — be precise about which claim this touches.** The audit's **C2 is specifically "CA's harvested *dataset / models* are still in operational use."** These findings document CA-lineage **personnel and firms** running post-2018 campaigns — they do **not** document CA's *data* surviving. The most authoritative voice on exactly that question, Siegelman (2022), calls the data/IP-transfer question unresolved. **C2 stays CONTESTED at `confidence/medium`.** What *does* upgrade: the personnel-continuity component of S1 (above). Claim the personnel; do not claim the data.

## Target 4 — COS state metrics → **RECONCILED (revised per clarification)**

The metric that matters here is **how many states Republicans control**, not how many have already filed COS applications — because the thesis is that GOP-controlled states convert into COS applications over time. Four distinct current numbers:

| Number | What it is | Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| **28** | States where Republicans control **both legislative chambers** (2026) | **The operative ceiling.** An Article V application is a legislative concurrent resolution — no governor needed — so ~28 states are the realistic pool the COS drive can convert. |
| **23** | Republican **trifectas** (governor + both chambers), April 2026 | Full agenda control, but the governor is not required to pass a COS application. |
| **20** | States that have **filed** a COSA Article V application (Kansas #20, Jan 2026; zero rescissions) | Progress to date — a subset of the 28. |
| **34 / 38** | Applications needed to **trigger** a convention / states needed to **ratify** an amendment | The thresholds. |

**The honest structural point.** The operative number is 28 (GOP legislative control). But **28 is still 6 short of the 34-state trigger.** At maximum *current* GOP legislative control the COS drive cannot reach the threshold on GOP-controlled states alone — it would need either (a) GOP chamber control to expand past 34 states, or (b) applications to pass in states the GOP does not fully control (some COS and legacy applications have passed with cross-party votes). That gap is the thesis's strongest falsification surface, and a reviewer will raise it — so the vault should state it first.

Sources: Ballotpedia / MultiState (state partisan control); COSAction register + Congressional Record applications list (filed applications).

**Fix applied:** `Hypothesis - COS is the permanent capture arm of Project 2025` — the earlier "28 = application count" error is corrected, and the appended correction note now lays out all four metrics with GOP legislative control (28) as the operative one. `Lens - COS as Constitutional Capture Vector` already frames "GOP controls ~28 of 50 state delegations" — correct, and now the load-bearing figure.

## What the Medium / X / forum sweep added

- **Medium — real value (Tier-2).** Wendy Siegelman's Companies House forensic work is the strongest single thread for Target 1, and her explicit "murky / unresolved" verdict on data-transfer is the strongest support for keeping C2 contested. Medium here behaved as Tier-2 because the author is a named investigative researcher working from primary documents — not because it is Medium.
- **X — narrative circulation only (Tier-4).** Confirms the federal-database thesis is widely discussed; moves no grade.
- **Forums — nothing primary.** Reddit/forum searches surfaced no primary material; they re-pointed to COSAction and Wikipedia, which re-confirmed the filed-application count at 20.

The discipline that matters: extending the sweep to low-tier sources was worth doing, and exactly one of them (Siegelman's Medium) carried Tier-2 weight. Letting the others "bulk up" the thesis would have weakened it.
