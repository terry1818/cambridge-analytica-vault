# Book Review — Vault Gap Report

## What this is

32 books on the vault's subject areas were reviewed against the full vault inventory (2,018 files). Each produced a source note in `06-Sources/Books/`; this note aggregates the **gaps** — specific entities, events, and concepts the books document that the vault did not appear to cover. It is a research backlog, not a set of finished notes. Items are marked **[absent]** (no matching file found in the inventory) or **[candidate]** (uncertain — may exist under another title, verify before creating).

Coverage check method: filename scan of the vault inventory plus targeted content greps. This is a strong first pass, not a guarantee — confirm against the live vault before creating each node.

## Results

- **32 books reviewed.** 25 new source notes written to `06-Sources/Books/`; 7 already in the vault (Wylie *Mindf*ck*, Kaiser *Targeted*, Hersh *Hacking the Electorate*, Mayer *Dark Money*, Sharlet *The Family* and *C Street*, Nelson *Shadow Network*).
- **~130 gap items surfaced.** The single clearest finding: the vault documents the *operations* of the patron/constitutional-capture project thoroughly but is **thin on its intellectual lineage** — the ideas, theorists, and analytical frameworks behind it.

## P1 — highest priority (structural or cross-book)

These either underpin a load-bearing vault hypothesis or were flagged by multiple books.

- **James M. Buchanan / the Virginia school / public-choice theory / Mont Pelerin Society** — the intellectual foundation of the constitutional-capture project. The vault documents COS, ALEC, and the Koch network operationally but has no note for the *ideas* driving them. Source: MacLean. This is the #1 structural gap.
- **Coleman v. Miller (1939)** — the Supreme Court case holding that most Article V questions are non-justiciable political questions, i.e. courts cannot stop a runaway convention. The legal cornerstone of why the COS campaign is dangerous; absent despite heavy COS coverage. Source: Feingold & Prindiville.
- **Commercial data-broker vendors** — Venntel, X-Mode/Outlogic, Seisint/MATRIX, Premise Data, Accurint, UberMedia, Neustar — the named firms that instantiate `Hypothesis - Federal database supersedes CA model`. Source: Tau.
- **Vladislav Surkov + "managed democracy" / Surkovism** — the doctrinal architect of modern Russian reality-construction; flagged by two books (Pomerantsev, Rid). Also **GRU / Fancy Bear (APT28)** — the vault covers the IRA troll track but not the GRU hack-and-leak track.
- **NAR January 6 operatives** — Dutch Sheets, Lou Engle / "The Call", the Appeal to Heaven flag, the Cyrus Anointing — cross-book (Sharlet *Undertow* + Taylor), operationally central to Jan 6 and the NAR layer the vault recently added.
- **Catholic hard-right node** — Tom Monaghan, Opus Dei, Ave Maria University — cross-book (Stewart x2). The vault's theocratic model is dominionist/Protestant-heavy and under-covers the Catholic-integralist money-and-institutions wing.
- **CNP co-founders** — Richard Viguerie and Morton Blackwell — named co-founders of the Council for National Policy missing from the vault's CNP coverage. Source: Nelson.
- **Analytical concept-nodes for the "is this fascism?" question** — Paxton's five-stage model and conservative-complicity thesis; Snyder's politics of eternity and schizofascism; Stanley's normalization. The vault cites these scholars but has no navigable concept notes to test its evidence against. Sources: Paxton, Snyder, Stanley.
