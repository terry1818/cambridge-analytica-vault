# Micro-Timeline: [[Robert Mercer]]

The full chronology of Robert Mercer's career arc from Renaissance Technologies through CA-era political infrastructure investment to post-2017 reduced public profile.

- `1946-07-11` — Born (US)
- `~1972` — Joins IBM Research as computer scientist
- `1993` — Joins **Renaissance Technologies** (hedge fund) — Mercer's wealth-generation engine
- `2008-01` — Becomes co-CEO of Renaissance Technologies (with Peter Brown)
- `~2010-2012` — Begins major political donations to conservative causes
- `2012` — Co-founds **[[Government Accountability Institute]]** with [[Steve Bannon]] and [[Peter Schweizer]]
- `2012` — Mercer family acquires **[[Breitbart News]]** stake
- `2013-2014` — Begins investing in [[Cambridge Analytica]] (ultimately $15M+ per NYT estimate)
- `2014` — Founds **[[Mercer Family Foundation]]** as major political-giving vehicle (operated by [[Rebekah Mercer]])
- `2014-2016` — Funds CA US operations buildout
- `2015-2016` — Backs [[Ted Cruz]] primary campaign (substantial PAC funding)
- `2016-05` — Cruz drops out of Republican primary
- `2016-05/06` — **Mercer family switches support to [[Donald Trump]]** within weeks
- `2016` — Funds **[[Make America Number 1 Super PAC]]** which works with CA on pro-Trump operations
- `2016-08` — [[Steve Bannon]] becomes Trump campaign CEO
- `2017-01` — Trump inaugurated; multiple Mercer-aligned figures in administration
- `2017-02-26` — **[[Source Cadwalladr 2017-02-26 Observer Robert Mercer]]** profile published — first major public mapping of the Mercer-Bannon-Trump-Brexit infrastructure
- `2017-08` — Bannon departs Trump White House
- `2017-11-02` — **Mercer steps down as co-CEO of Renaissance Technologies** following media pressure related to political activities
- `2017+` — Substantially reduced public political profile
- `2018-03` — CA scandal breaks; daughters Jennifer and Rebekah become directors of [[Emerdata Limited]]
- `2018+` — Substantially withdraws from public political activity
- `2020+` — Family continues conservative political investments via [[Rebekah Mercer]] as more visible operator
- `2024+` — Reduced direct political role; broader Mercer infrastructure (Heritage, Project 2025, etc.) continues operating
