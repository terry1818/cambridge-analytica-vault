## Phase-anchored chronology

### Phase 1: Defense origins (1989-2005)
- **1989-1993**: Behavioural Dynamics Institute (BDi) work and SCL methodological prehistory; founding of [[SCL Group]] by Nigel Oakes in 1993. Origins in Saatchi & Saatchi-style consumer persuasion crossed with British military psychological-operations doctrine.
- **2000-2004**: SCL expands into government work. UK MoD and US DoD contracts for "behavioural change communications" and counter-radicalisation messaging. Early field deployments in Indonesia, the Philippines, and parts of Africa.
- **2005**: Academic groundwork. [[Michal Kosinski]] and David Stillwell begin Facebook personality research at Cambridge Psychometrics Centre.

### Phase 2: Commercial weaponisation (2010-2014)
- **2010-2012**: [[SCL Group]] runs PsyOps-derived campaigns in [[Trinidad Tobago elections]] and [[India SCL elections 2003-2012]]. Operational template for later CA work is established.
- **2013**: [[Cambridge Analytica]] incorporated as SCL US entity, branded for North American political market. [[Robert Mercer]] becomes principal funder. [[Steve Bannon]] becomes vice president.
- **2014**: [[Aleksandr Kogan]] builds thisisyourdigitallife app via GSR (Global Science Research). Data of up to 87 million Facebook users harvested via [[Facebook Graph API v1]].

### Phase 3: Election deployment (2014-2016)
- **2014-2015**: CA works for [[Ted Cruz Campaign]] in the GOP primary. Mercer money flows in via [[Make America Number 1 Super PAC]].
- **2016**: CA pivots to Trump general election (post-Cruz withdrawal). [[Brad Parscale]] runs digital. [[Brexit Referendum 2016]] sees CA-linked [[AggregateIQ]] work for [[Vote Leave]] and [[BeLeave]]. Both campaigns win.

### Phase 4: Exposure (2017-2019)
- **2017-03**: [[Carole Cadwalladr]] publishes "The Great British Brexit Robbery" in The Observer.
- **2018-03**: [[Christopher Wylie]] whistleblows via The Observer, NYT, and Channel 4. [[ICO Investigation]] escalates. [[Alexander Nix]] is suspended after Channel 4 sting. CA files bankruptcy by May 2018.
- **2019**: [[FTC v Facebook 2019]] settles for $5 billion. [[Mueller Investigation]] closes without indicting CA principals on collusion but documents Russian operations.

### Phase 5: Successor continuation (2018-2024)
- **2018-2020**: [[Emerdata]], [[Auspex International]], and other CA successor vehicles inherit personnel and methods. [[Frontier Services Group]] continues the [[Erik Prince]] private-mil thread.
- **2020-2023**: [[Doppelganger]] (Russia) and [[Spamouflage Dragonbridge]] (China) operationalise the CA playbook at state scale. [[Team Jorge]] (Israel) commercialises it for hire.

### Phase 6: AI-era convergence (2024-2026)
- **2024-2025**: Tech Right consolidates. [[JD Vance]] (Thiel-funded) wins VP. [[DOGE]] formed under [[Elon Musk]]. [[Project 2025]] becomes operational policy. [[Palantir Technologies]] expands federal data integration.
- **2025-2026**: [[Stargate Project 2025]], [[Big Tech Trump 2.0 Alignment 2024-2025]], state-level [[Article V Convention]] push via [[Citizens for Self-Governance]]. The CA playbook is now infrastructure, not insurgency.

- The 1989-2005 phase is thinly sourced compared to later phases. SCL pre-2010 work needs primary documents from UK Companies House and MoD FOIA.
- The transition from CA bankruptcy (2018) to fully operational successor orgs is documented inconsistently. The [[Emerdata]] formation chain, in particular, would benefit from a single connected timeline note.
- The 2025-2026 Tech Right phase is reported in near-real-time and will need retrospective revision once court records and congressional investigations catch up.

## Timeline maintenance and uncertainty handling

The Master Timeline is maintained as a curated chronology rather than as an exhaustive event log. The curation criteria: an event qualifies for the Master Timeline if it has operational consequences across multiple vault entities, if it has substantial primary-source documentation, or if it is referenced by hypothesis-tier analytical claims elsewhere in the vault.

Uncertainty handling: where an event's date is contested or imprecise, the timeline entry reflects the most-specific date the public record supports, with the precision-level explicit. Where an event has multiple competing-narrative interpretations, the timeline entry uses a neutral characterisation with cross-reference to the relevant entity / event / hypothesis notes where the competing interpretations are analysed.

## Specific timeline anchors that load-bear vault analysis

**1981:** Founding of [[Council for National Policy]] by [[Tim LaHaye]] and co-founders. The 45-year operational continuity of CNP is the longest-running single institutional thread in the vault network.

**1993:** Founding of [[SCL Group]] by Nigel Oakes. The 30-plus-year operational continuity of the SCL methodology (through SCL Group, [[Cambridge Analytica]], [[AggregateIQ]], [[Emerdata]], and successor structures) is the second-longest single institutional thread.

**2010:** Citizens United v FEC Supreme Court decision. The post-Citizens-United campaign-finance regime is what made the [[Mercer]] family and other patron-class donors operationally able to fund the scale of political-influence apparatus the vault tracks.

**2014:** [[Cambridge Analytica]] founding and Mercer capitalisation. The operational anchor for the entire CA-era story.

**2016:** [[Brexit Referendum 2016]] and [[2016 US Presidential Election]]. The twin events that made the CA methodology globally visible.

**2018:** CA collapse and the regulatory reckoning. The point at which the CA-era story moves from operator-incumbent to regulatory-post-mortem.

**2022:** Mar-a-Lago search, mid-term elections, [[Twitter Files Release 2022-2023]] start. The operational reset that produces the Tech-Right ascendancy.

**2024-2025:** Trump 2.0 election and inauguration, [[DOGE]] launch, [[Project 2025]] implementation. The present-day vault operational window.
