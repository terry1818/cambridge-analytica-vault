# Brown, Julie K. Perversion of Justice: The Jeffrey Epstein Story (2021)

## Publication

- Dey Street Books / HarperCollins, July 20, 2021
- 432 pages
- Builds on the Miami Herald 2018 series "Perversion of Justice" by the same author that re-opened federal interest in the Epstein case

## Author standing

- [[Julie K Brown]] is a Miami Herald investigative reporter
- The 2018 series was the proximate cause of the 2019 federal indictment of [[Jeffrey Epstein]] in SDNY
- Won 2019 George Polk Award and 2019 Robert F Kennedy Journalism Award

## Key documented claims (Tier 1, deeply sourced reporting)

**The 2007-2008 Non-Prosecution Agreement:**
- Documents the secret negotiations between Epstein's lawyers ([[Alan Dershowitz]], [[Ken Starr]], [[Jay Lefkowitz]]) and US Attorney [[Alexander Acosta]]
- Establishes the agreement was structured to grant immunity to "any potential co-conspirators" - a provision Brown's reporting and later court rulings identified as exceptionally unusual
- The 11th Circuit ruled in 2019 (US v. Wright / In re Wild) that the manner of negotiation violated the Crime Victims' Rights Act

**Victim identification:**
- Brown's series identified over 60 victims by name (with consent) - far more than the federal case originally documented
- Established that the Palm Beach Police Department had built a detailed case the FBI joined and then was undercut by the federal NPA

**Maxwell role:**
- Reporting that fed into the SDNY [[Ghislaine Maxwell]] indictment July 2020

## Significance for vault

The most thorough single-volume primary-source-based account of the documented institutional failures around the 2007-2008 federal Epstein case. Distinguished from later commentators because the book is built on FOIA documents, court records, and direct victim interviews.
