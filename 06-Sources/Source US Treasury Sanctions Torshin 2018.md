# Source US Treasury Sanctions Torshin 2018

## Citation

> US Department of the Treasury, Office of Foreign Assets Control. (2018-04-06). *Treasury Designates Russian Oligarchs, Officials, and Entities in Response to Worldwide Malign Activity*. US Department of the Treasury. https://ofac.treasury.gov/recent-actions/20180406

## What it is

A US Treasury Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) sanctions action and accompanying press release issued 6 April 2018. It added named Russian individuals and companies to the Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons (SDN) List. The action followed enactment of the Countering America's Adversaries Through Sanctions Act (CAATSA) and a 29 January 2018 Treasury report to Congress on Russian oligarchs and parastatal entities. [[Alexander Torshin]] appears on the SDN list as one of the designated officials.

## Key claims / findings

- The action designated 7 oligarchs and 12 companies they own or control, 17 senior Russian government officials, a state-owned arms-trading company, and a bank — commonly summarized as 24 individuals plus entities.
- [[Alexander Torshin]] was designated as State Secretary and Deputy Governor of the Central Bank of the Russian Federation, "for being an official of the Government of the Russian Federation," under Executive Order 13661.
- Designation freezes any US-jurisdiction assets and generally bars US persons from dealings with the designee.
- Treasury framed the action as a response to Russia's "worldwide malign activity," including subverting Western democracies and supplying weapons to the Assad regime.
- The action did not itself cite the NRA; Torshin's NRA cultivation was documented separately in press and congressional reporting.

## Notable quotes

> "The Russian government engages in a range of malign activity around the globe... Russian oligarchs and elites who profit from this corrupt system will no longer be insulated from the consequences." — Treasury Secretary Steven Mnuchin, 2018-04-06
