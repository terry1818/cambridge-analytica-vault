# Source Politico AIPAC Spending 2024 Primary

## Citation

> Politico. (2024-06). *Reporting on AIPAC's spending in competitive 2024 Democratic primaries*. Politico. https://www.politico.com/

## What it is

Politico campaign-finance reporting from mid-2024 documenting the scale and strategy of spending by the American Israel Public Affairs Committee ([[AIPAC]]) and its affiliated super PAC, the United Democracy Project (UDP), in that year's Democratic congressional primaries. The reporting frames AIPAC as an unusually large outside spender targeting progressive incumbents critical of Israel's conduct in Gaza.

## Key claims / findings

- Politico reported that AIPAC was "the biggest source of Republican money flowing into competitive Democratic primaries" in 2024.
- AIPAC and the UDP super PAC together spent record sums; by cycle's end totals exceeded $100 million across the 2024 elections.
- AIPAC/UDP money helped defeat Reps. [[Jamaal Bowman]] (D-N.Y.) and [[Cori Bush]] (D-Mo.), two of Congress's most vocal critics of the Gaza war.
- The UDP spent roughly $9.9 million opposing Bowman and backed his opponent George Latimer, setting a record for outside spending in a House primary.
- AIPAC backed Wesley Bell against Bush and Latimer against Bowman, unseating both incumbents.
- The reporting framed the spending as a deliberate strategy to reshape the Democratic caucus on Israel policy.
