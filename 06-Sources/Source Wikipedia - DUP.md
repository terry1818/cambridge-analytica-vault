## What it is

A community-maintained encyclopedia article on the [[Democratic Unionist Party]] (DUP), a unionist and socially conservative political party in Northern Ireland founded in 1971 by Ian Paisley. The article aggregates party history, ideology, electoral record, and controversies into one narrative. It serves as a navigational secondary source for the dark-money / opaque-donor thread linked to the Brexit campaign.

## Key claims / findings

- The DUP was founded in 1971 by [[Ian Paisley]] and grew into the largest unionist party in Northern Ireland.
- It is described as right-wing and socially conservative, opposing same-sex marriage and abortion, with roots in Protestant evangelicalism.
- The party campaigned for Leave in the 2016 EU referendum.
- During the referendum it received a £435,000 donation channeled through the Constitutional Research Council (CRC), fronted by Richard Cook; the ultimate source was never disclosed.
- Part of the money funded a pro-Brexit advertising wrap in a newspaper not circulated in Northern Ireland, and £32,750 went to the data-analytics firm AggregateIQ.
- From 2017 to 2019 the DUP held a confidence-and-supply arrangement with the UK Conservative government.
