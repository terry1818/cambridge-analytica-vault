## What it is

A community-maintained encyclopedia article on the [[Bharatiya Janata Party]] ("Indian People's Party"), one of India's two major political parties. The article aggregates party history, ideology, and electoral record into one narrative. It serves as a navigational secondary source for the comparative-authoritarianism thread — religious-nationalist parties governing nominal democracies.

## Key claims / findings

- The BJP is described as a right-wing to far-right party whose policies adhere to Hindutva, a Hindu-nationalist ideology.
- Its origins lie in the Bharatiya Jana Sangh, founded in 1951 by Shyama Prasad Mukherjee as the political wing of the [[Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh]] (RSS).
- The BJP itself was formally established in 1980 following a split within the Janata coalition.
- It maintains close ideological and organizational ties to the RSS, a right-wing paramilitary volunteer organization.
- Since 2014 it has been India's ruling party under Prime Minister Narendra Modi; as of 2026 it is the largest party in parliament and state legislatures.
- The party advocates social conservatism and a nationalist foreign policy.
