## What it is

The English Wikipedia biographical article on **David Ferris Ellison** (born January 9, 1983), American film producer, media proprietor, and former actor — founder of [[Skydance Media]] and, since August 2025, chairman and CEO of [[Paramount Skydance]].

## Key claims / findings

- Ellison is the son of [[Oracle Corporation]] co-founder [[Larry Ellison]] and Barbara Boothe Ellison; his sister is producer [[Megan Ellison]].
- A licensed pilot, he founded [[Skydance Media]] in **2006**; the name derives from aerobatic "skydancing." His first film, *Flyboys* (2006), starred him and was co-financed with MGM.
- He produced major franchise films including *Mission: Impossible – Rogue Nation*, *Terminator Genisys* (both 2015), and *Top Gun: Maverick* (2022), the last nominated for the Best Picture Oscar.
- In **2025** Ellison led Skydance's merger with Paramount Global, becoming chairman and CEO of Paramount Skydance.
- The article notes the merger's completion under FCC approval during the second Trump administration, and Ellison's October 2025 acquisition of [[The Free Press]].
