## What it is

Internal investigation commissioned by Apollo Global Management's independent directors after public reporting in fall 2020 raised questions about [[Leon Black]]'s payments to [[Jeffrey Epstein]] over 2012-2017. Apollo retained Dechert LLP (and within Dechert specifically a team led by former federal prosecutor Roger Burlingame) to conduct an independent review.

## Key findings

### The payments

Per the report's documentation:
- Black paid Epstein approximately **$158 million** between 2012 and 2017
- Plus $10 million loan from Black to Epstein
- Combined transactions exceeded $170M
- Payments were personal -- from Black to Epstein -- not from Apollo corporate

### Stated purpose of payments

Per Black's account documented in the report:
- "Tax and estate planning" services
- Specifically, advisory work related to Black's trust structures, art donations, and wealth-management entities
- Black stated Epstein provided "value" through aggressive tax-minimization structures

### Dechert's assessment

The Dechert report concluded:
- The payments were documented and the work was performed
- Apollo itself had no Epstein-related liabilities (Black's payments were personal)
- The fee level was extraordinary but did not constitute Apollo corporate exposure
- Black was not aware of Epstein's trafficking conduct **specifically** at the time of payments (Dechert's finding; subsequently disputed)

### What Dechert did NOT establish (per the report's own framing)

- Did NOT establish that the services rendered were proportionate to the fees
- Did NOT establish the absence of any other purpose for the payments
- Did NOT examine pre-2012 or post-2017 Black-Epstein relationship
- Did NOT examine Black's possible knowledge of or involvement in Epstein's other operations

## Black's resignation

Following the report's release:
- January 25, 2021: Black announced he would step down as Apollo CEO
- March 22, 2021: Marc Rowan succeeded Black as CEO
- Black retained substantial Apollo equity stake post-departure

## Subsequent developments

### Giuffre civil suit (2023)

[[Virginia Giuffre]] filed a civil suit against [[Leon Black]] personally in 2023 alleging she had been trafficked to Black. The suit settled.

### Additional disclosures

- 2022-2023 reporting suggested Epstein attempted to leverage knowledge of Black's affairs / other compromising material to extract payments
- These accounts add tier-2 dimensional context to the "tax advice" framing

## Key quoted findings from the Dechert report

> "Mr. Black retained Mr. Epstein to provide professional advice regarding trust and estate planning, tax issues, philanthropic endeavors, and the operation of his Family Office."

> "The Committee found no evidence that Mr. Black engaged in any wrongdoing in his dealings with Mr. Epstein... The Committee did not find that Mr. Black was complicit in any way in Mr. Epstein's criminal activity."

> "The Committee was not asked to and did not investigate any allegation that Mr. Epstein engaged in any criminal activity in connection with Mr. Black."

The careful framing of the Dechert findings -- particularly the explicit limitation of scope -- is notable. The report establishes that Apollo (the corporate entity) was not exposed; it does NOT extensively probe Black's personal knowledge or motives.
