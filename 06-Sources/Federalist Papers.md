# The Federalist Papers

Series of 85 essays published 1787-1788 in New York newspapers by [[James Madison]], [[Alexander Hamilton]], and [[John Jay]] (using the pseudonym "Publius") to argue for ratification of the proposed US Constitution.

## Vault relevance

The Federalist Papers are referenced in the vault as primary documentary basis for [[Originalism]] and as the source for Madison's specific arguments against repeatedly opening the constitutional structure to revision (Federalist No. 49) - the foundation of [[Runaway convention]] critiques.
