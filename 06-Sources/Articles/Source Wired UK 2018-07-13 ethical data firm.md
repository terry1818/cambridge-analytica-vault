# Source: Wired UK 2018-07-13 ethical data firm

## Citation

Wired UK editorial staff. (2018-07-13). "Friday briefing: Former Cambridge Analytica chiefs set up 'ethical' data analysis firm." *Wired UK.*

## What it is

**Wired UK's contemporaneous reporting** on the **July 2018 launch of [[Auspex International]]** by **[[Mark Turnbull]] and other ex-CA staff** — published within days of the Auspex public emergence. The piece is significant primarily as **journalistic skepticism** of Auspex's **"ethical data analysis"** self-presentation: Wired and other outlets noted the explicit contradiction between Auspex's framing and the **operational and personnel continuity** with [[Cambridge Analytica]]. Together with [[Source BBC 2018-07-12 staff set up new firm]], [[Source Ram FT 2018-07-11 Ex-CA launch venture]], and [[Source Jardin Boing Boing 2018-07-11 Auspex]], it forms the **immediate-aftermath journalistic cluster** documenting the CA-to-successor personnel transition.

## Key claims / findings

- **Auspex launch timing:** **July 2018** — approximately **two months after CA's May insolvency**
- **Founders:** **[[Mark Turnbull]]** — CA's former Managing Director, Political Global; **Ahmad Al-Khatib** — Egyptian-British political operative
- **Self-presentation:** Auspex marketed itself as a **clean break from CA** — focused on "ethical" data analysis, "purpose-driven" campaigns, and a focus on Africa and Middle East political consulting
- **Journalistic skepticism:** Wired UK and contemporaneous coverage explicitly noted:
  - Same personnel, same methodology, new corporate brand
  - "Ethical" framing as PR positioning rather than substantive change
  - Continuity of operating geography (Africa / Middle East) with CA's prior international operations
- **Channel 4 sting context:** the Wired piece notes that **Turnbull was featured in the [[Channel 4 Sting 2018]]** discussing CA operations in multiple jurisdictions — the same person now leading the "ethical" successor
- **The pattern:** part of the broader **CA-successor cluster** ([[Emerdata Limited]], [[Data Propria]], [[Firecrest Technologies]], [[Emic]]) emerging in the post-collapse period
