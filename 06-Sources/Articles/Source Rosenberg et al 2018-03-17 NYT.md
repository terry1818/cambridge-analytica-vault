# Source: Rosenberg et al 2018-03-17 NYT

## Citation

Rosenberg, Matthew; Confessore, Nicholas; Cadwalladr, Carole. (2018-03-17). "How Trump Consultants Exploited the Facebook Data of Millions." *The New York Times.* <https://www.nytimes.com/2018/03/17/us/politics/cambridge-analytica-trump-campaign.html>.

## What it is

The **NYT half of the coordinated 2018-03-17 break** of the Cambridge Analytica scandal — published simultaneously with [[Source Cadwalladr Graham-Harrison 2018-03-17 Guardian|Cadwalladr and Graham-Harrison's Observer/Guardian piece]]. Rosenberg, Confessore, and Cadwalladr (jointly bylined) reported the [[Christopher Wylie]] whistleblower disclosures, the [[Global Science Research (GSR)|Kogan/GSR]] data-harvest mechanism, and — most consequentially — that **NYT reporters had viewed extant samples of the harvested dataset still in CA's possession**, proving that 2016 deletion certifications signed by [[Alexander Nix]] and [[Aleksandr Kogan]] were false.

## Key claims / findings

- **The harvest:** [[Aleksandr Kogan]] / [[Global Science Research (GSR)|GSR]] built the "thisisyourdigitallife" personality-quiz app that exploited the [[Facebook Graph API v1|Facebook Graph API v1.0]] friends-data permission to harvest profiles of ~50M users (later revised by Facebook to **87M**) without those users' consent
- **The sale:** GSR sold the dataset to [[Cambridge Analytica]] in 2014 in breach of Facebook's Terms of Service
- **The deletion-certification falsehood:** Facebook had asked CA in 2015-2016 to certify deletion of the dataset; Nix and Kogan signed such certifications; **NYT reporters viewed samples of the dataset in March 2018, proving the certifications were materially false**
- **Whistleblower:** [[Christopher Wylie]] was the named on-the-record source — first major appearance
- **Mercer / Bannon funding:** [[Robert Mercer]] funded CA (~$15M reported); [[Steve Bannon]] was VP at the time of the data acquisition
- **2016 Trump campaign use:** CA used the harvested data in support of the 2016 Trump presidential campaign per Wylie

## Notable quotes

> "The breach allowed the company to exploit the private social media activity of a huge swath of the American electorate, developing techniques that underpinned its work on President Trump's campaign in 2016." — Rosenberg et al.

> Wylie: "We exploited Facebook to harvest millions of people's profiles. And built models to exploit what we knew about them and target their inner demons. That was the basis the entire company was built on."

## Reliability assessment

- **Type:** investigative journalism, primary source for the deletion-certification claim
- **Independent corroboration:** [[Source Cadwalladr Graham-Harrison 2018-03-17 Guardian]] published simultaneously with shared reporting threads; [[Source Channel 4 2018-03-19 Exposed]] two days later corroborated CA's operational methods on undercover video
- **Authors' track record:** Rosenberg (national security reporter), Confessore (investigations / campaign finance), Cadwalladr (multi-year CA reporting under her byline at the Observer)
- **Disputed by:** CA disputed *characterizations* but not the core mechanical facts; Facebook initially objected to "breach" framing (calling it Terms-of-Service violation rather than a hack)
