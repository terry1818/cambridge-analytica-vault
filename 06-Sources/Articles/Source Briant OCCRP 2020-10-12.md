# Source: Briant OCCRP 2020-10-12

## Citation

Briant, Emma. (2020-10-12). "OPINION: Governments Have Failed to Learn from the Cambridge Analytica Scandal." *OCCRP* (Organized Crime and Corruption Reporting Project). <https://www.occrp.org/en/perspectives/2020/governments-have-failed-to-learn-from-the-cambridge-analytica-scandal>.

## What it is

A 2020 opinion-and-analysis piece by **Dr. Emma Briant** — academic researcher on propaganda and influence operations, longstanding investigator of SCL Group / Cambridge Analytica, and author of [[Source Briant 2015 Propaganda and Counter-Terrorism]]. Briant has unique access to internal SCL documents (her academic research preceded the 2018 scandal). The piece argues that governments, regulators, and platforms have **failed to learn from the CA scandal** because they have treated CA as an anomalous bad actor rather than as a symptom of structural defence-to-elections industry pipelines that continue operating under new corporate brands.

## Key claims / findings

- **Emic continuity:** Briant identifies **[[Emic]]** as a UK firm **set up by ex-SCL Defence personnel** that **continued working for governments after 2018** — the cleanest single primary source for [[Emic]]'s existence and lineage
- **Structural failure thesis:** The 2018 regulatory response (FTC consent orders, UK ICO findings, Facebook policy changes) was **narrowly targeted at CA-specific data abuse** and did not address:
  - The defence-to-elections personnel pipeline ([[Hypothesis - Defense-to-elections pipeline]])
  - Successor entity continuity ([[Hypothesis - Successor entities preserve operational capability]])
  - Israeli private-intel parallel ecosystem ([[Hypothesis - Israeli intel-for-hire is parallel to Russian state operations]])
- **Government complicity:** UK government in particular has continued contracting with SCL-lineage firms despite the 2018 scandal — a pattern that undermines public accountability claims
- **Academic-investigative bridge:** Briant's position is unusual — academic researcher who is also a primary-source investigator with document access — and her analysis carries weight across both communities

## Notable claims (precise enough to cite)

- "Many of the same people, the same techniques, and even the same firms remain active in government and political work."
- "[Emic] was set up by former SCL Defence staff and continues to work for government clients."
