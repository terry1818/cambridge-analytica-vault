## Why the Forbidden Stories / Guardian 2023 disclosure is operationally significant

The February 2023 Forbidden Stories / Guardian "Team Jorge" disclosure documented a specific operational firm (Demoman International, doing business as "Team Jorge") that had been operating for over a decade as a for-hire election-manipulation and disinformation operator. The disclosure was operationally significant for the vault because it documented:

**A documented operational successor to CA-style methodology.** Team Jorge's operational toolkit (AIMS persona-management platform supporting tens of thousands of fake social-media accounts; documented election interventions in 33+ countries; explicit commercial-pitch material to undercover journalists posing as prospective clients) was operationally indistinguishable from what CA's methodology had been before 2018, with the additional capability that the 2023 platform was more sophisticated than what CA had deployed.

**Continuity of personnel between Israeli-intelligence and commercial information-operations firms.** Team Jorge's named principal [[Tal Hanan]] had a documented Israeli-intelligence background. The pattern (former Israeli-intelligence operators founding commercial information-operations firms that serve foreign-government and political-campaign clients) is the same pattern documented at [[Black Cube]] (the Israeli private-intelligence firm that worked for Harvey Weinstein and others), at multiple other Israeli-intelligence-adjacent firms, and at the broader commercial information-operations ecosystem the vault tracks.

**Specific documented deployments.** The Team Jorge disclosure documented specific deployments including a Nigerian-election engagement, a Latin American electoral campaign deployment, and undercover-recorded pitches to prospective Sri Lankan and Cambodian clients. The pitch material was operationally similar to the Channel 4 sting footage of [[Alexander Nix]] from 2018: same self-presentation style, same service menu, same client-recruitment posture.

## Why this is the strongest single piece of evidence that the CA methodology persists

The vault's broader analytical claim is that CA-style methodology did not disappear with the 2018 CA collapse; it continued at successor firms and at parallel operations across multiple jurisdictions. The Team Jorge disclosure is the strongest single piece of evidence for this claim because it documents a specific firm, with specific named personnel, operating with specific commercial-pitch material that matches the CA methodology, with documented client engagements across multiple jurisdictions, with operational continuity from at least the early 2010s through 2023.

The Team Jorge case is also distinctive because the disclosure followed substantially the same methodology Channel 4 had used against Nix in 2018: undercover journalists posing as prospective clients, multiple meetings, recorded pitch material that the firm's principals did not realise was being recorded. The methodological symmetry (an investigative-journalism methodology that has worked twice to surface for-hire information-operations firms) is itself part of the operational record.
