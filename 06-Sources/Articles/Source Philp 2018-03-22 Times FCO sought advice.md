# Source: Philp 2018-03-22 Times FCO sought advice

## Citation

Philp, Catherine. (2018-03-22). "Foreign Office sought advice from Cambridge Analytica chiefs." *The Times*.

## What this article established

Five days after the Wylie disclosures broke, Times reporter Catherine Philp documented that **the UK Foreign and Commonwealth Office (FCO) had formally sought advice from [[Cambridge Analytica]] during [[Boris Johnson]]s tenure as Foreign Secretary** (July 2016 - July 2018). Specifically:

- FCO civil servants engaged CA in 2016-2017
- The engagement was not publicly disclosed at the time
- Johnsons own meeting with [[Alexander Nix]] in 2016 was confirmed (Nelson Spectator follow-up)
- The substance of the FCO-CA exchanges was not detailed publicly

## Significance

This is the **most direct documented contact between CA leadership and senior UK government officials at ministerial level**. Demonstrates that CA was not merely a fringe political-consulting firm but had **access to and engagement with the British state** at high level.
