# Source: Cadwalladr Graham-Harrison 2018-03-17 Guardian

## Citation

Cadwalladr, Carole; Graham-Harrison, Emma. (2018-03-17). "Revealed: 50 million Facebook profiles harvested for Cambridge Analytica in major data breach." *The Observer / The Guardian*.

## What this established

The Guardian/Observer side of the **joint Saturday-evening publication** with NYT on March 17, 2018, that broke the Cambridge Analytica scandal to the global public. The Guardian/Observer article emphasized:

- [[Christopher Wylie]] on the record as primary whistleblower
- The 50 million figure (later revised upward by Facebook to up to 87M)
- The role of [[Aleksandr Kogan]] and [[Global Science Research (GSR)]]
- The breach of Facebook's Terms of Service in selling the data to CA
- The failure of Facebook to enforce its "deletion" certifications

## Coordination with NYT

The simultaneous publication was deliberate — Cadwalladr brought in NYT (and Channel 4 News) after CA legal threats against The Guardian/Observer made independent publication risky. The dual byline + dual-newsroom strategy ensured the story could not be killed by suing any single outlet.

## Significance

This article + the NYT companion piece + the Channel 4 sting two days later = the **break moment** of the entire CA scandal. Facebook stock dropped ~$134B in market cap in the following week. Zuckerberg testified before Congress within a month. The scandal had been brewing since 2015 but March 17, 2018 is when it became globally inescapable.
