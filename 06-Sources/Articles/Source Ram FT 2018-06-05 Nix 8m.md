# Source: Ram FT 2018-06-05 Nix 8m

## Citation

Ram, Aliya. (2018-06-05). "Cambridge Analytica chief accused of taking $8m before collapse." *Financial Times.* <https://www.ft.com/content/c0a48e8e-68f7-11e8-b6eb-4acfcfb08c11>.

## What it is

**Aliya Ram's Financial Times reporting**, published approximately **one month after [[Cambridge Analytica]]'s May 2018 insolvency**, documenting allegations that [[Alexander Nix]] **withdrew approximately $8 million from CA in the period preceding the collapse**. The allegations originated from CA's insolvency administrators, who were obligated to investigate transactions in the period leading up to insolvency for any evidence of asset stripping or director misconduct. The FT story is the **primary public source** for the specific $8M figure that recurs in subsequent reporting on the financial dimensions of CA's collapse.

## Key claims / findings

- **The $8M figure:** Approximately **$8 million** withdrawn by Nix from CA "**in the months leading up to**" the May 2018 insolvency
- **Mechanism:** the precise transactional pathway — withdrawals as compensation, transfers between SCL-group entities, or other mechanisms — is reported as under investigation by administrators
- **Insolvency-administrator perspective:** the allegations come from the **statutory administrators' obligation** to investigate director-related transactions in the period preceding insolvency under UK insolvency law
- **Context — Mercer funding:** the **~$15M Mercer funding** of CA over multiple years places the $8M Nix withdrawal in proportion — a substantial fraction of CA's working capital
- **Implications for successor structure:** the withdrawal allegations are **directly relevant** to the [[Emerdata Limited]] succession picture — whether assets were stripped from CA into Nix's personal control or into Emerdata's corporate structure is a key question

## Reliability assessment

- **Type:** investigative financial journalism
- **Author's track record:** Aliya Ram was an FT technology/companies reporter; FT financial reporting has high editorial standards
- **Sourcing:** the FT cites insolvency-administrator findings — i.e., a regulated process with statutory obligations
- **Caveats:**
  - "Accused of" framing in the headline is precise: this is an allegation under investigation, not a confirmed finding
  - The specific transactional pathway is not fully detailed in the FT piece
  - Nix did not face criminal charges over the alleged withdrawals
- **Disputed by:** Nix and CA's professional advisers contested aspects of the reporting; no public refutation of the $8M figure itself
