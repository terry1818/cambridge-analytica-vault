# Source: Cadwalladr 2018-03-24 Observer Sanni

## Citation

Cadwalladr, Carole. (2018-03-24). Sanni whistleblower disclosure on Vote Leave / BeLeave / AIQ funds transfer. *The Observer*.

## What this article established

One week after the Wylie disclosures and Channel 4 sting, Cadwalladr published a parallel whistleblower story: **[[Shahmir Sanni]]** (BeLeave volunteer) had documented evidence that [[Vote Leave]] illegally coordinated spending with [[BeLeave]] by directing £625K to AggregateIQ via the BeLeave entity. Documented:

- Vote Leaves directives to Sanni and BeLeave organizer Darren Grimes
- The mechanics of the £625K transfer
- Sannis decision to come forward despite personal cost
- The retaliatory outing of Sanni (he was outed as gay by people aligned with Vote Leave the day his story published; later subject of Equality Act tribunal)

## Significance

The Sanni disclosure was **the Brexit-specific equivalent of Wylies CA disclosure**. Together they made the global story bilateral (US + UK) rather than just CA-focused. The subsequent [[Source UK Electoral Commission Vote Leave 2018]] finding vindicated Sannis evidence.

The retaliatory outing of Sanni made him personally a victim of the campaign machinery he had exposed — a chilling effect on subsequent UK political whistleblowers.
