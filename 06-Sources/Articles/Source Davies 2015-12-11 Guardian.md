# Source: Davies 2015-12-11 Guardian

## Citation

Davies, Harry. (2015-12-11). "Ted Cruz campaign using firm that harvested data on millions of unwitting Facebook users." *The Guardian*.

## Significance

**The first major public exposure** of Cambridge Analytica's harvest of Facebook data. Published ~28 months before the global break of the scandal in March 2018. Davies established the essential facts:

- CA was working for the Ted Cruz presidential campaign
- CA had built psychographic profiles using Facebook data
- The data was collected without consent of most affected users
- The harvest had been conducted by a UK academic ([[Aleksandr Kogan]])

## Why the story didn't break in 2015

Several reasons the 2015 Davies story didn't trigger the eventual scandal:

1. Davies relied on documents and on-record sources but not a public whistleblower
2. The Cruz campaign was a primary candidate — early in the cycle
3. Facebook claimed it was "investigating" but no enforcement followed
4. Mainstream media largely treated it as a campaign-data curiosity rather than a structural problem
5. CA's actual scale and methods only became clear with the Wylie disclosures

## What changed by 2018

Wylie went on record + Channel 4 sting + NYT's parallel investigation + Trump win + post-election attention to information operations = global story.

## Davies the journalist

Davies continued reporting at The Guardian on data and surveillance issues. His 2015 piece is the **journalistic ground zero** of the CA scandal — frequently undercited in retrospectives that focus on the 2018 break.
