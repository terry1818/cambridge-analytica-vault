# Source: Knight New Yorker - Life Inside SCL

## Citation

Knight, Sam. "Life Inside S.C.L., Cambridge Analytica's Parent Company." *The New Yorker*.

## What this article established

Long-form New Yorker piece on the **internal culture** of SCL Group / Cambridge Analytica during the operational period. Knight had access to multiple former employees who described:

- The mixture of Etonian / Oxbridge British upper-class culture with mercenary commercialism
- The Nigel Oakes founding philosophy and BDI academic-research framing
- The disjunction between SCL's claimed academic legitimacy and actual operational practice
- The personal dynamics among Nix, the Oakes brothers, Mercer-side US staff, and the international rotating cast

## Significance

One of the best sources on the **culture and personalities** that produced CA, rather than just the corporate or financial structure. Useful as cultural context for understanding the firm's operational decisions.
