# Source: FTC Complaint Cambridge Analytica 2019

## Citation

US Federal Trade Commission. (2019-07-23). *In the Matter of Cambridge Analytica, LLC*, FTC File No. 182-3107. Administrative complaint.

## What FTC found

- CA engaged in **deceptive practices** by representing it did not collect personally identifiable information when it did
- CA collected personal data from up to 87 million Facebook users
- CA's claims about EU-US Privacy Shield compliance were false
- CA's claim that data was deleted (Jan 2016 certification to Facebook) was false

## Resolution

- **Cambridge Analytica LLC**: case proceeded administratively; CA had already declared bankruptcy
- **Alexander Nix**: separate consent order, **20-year restrictions** on:
  - Misrepresenting practices for collecting/using/disclosing data
  - Required deletion of all data collected through deceptive means
  - Required deletion of all "work product" (models, derived analyses)
- **Aleksandr Kogan**: separate consent order, same 20-year restrictions

## Significance

FTC consent orders are the **primary US enforcement actions** against the individuals. The 20-year scope is unusually long. The required deletion of "work product" (derived models, not just raw data) was a novel approach to data-rights enforcement.
