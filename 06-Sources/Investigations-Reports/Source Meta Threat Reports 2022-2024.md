# Source: Meta Threat Reports 2022-2024

## Citation

Meta Platforms Inc. (2022-present). *Adversarial Threat Reports* (quarterly series). <https://about.fb.com/news/tag/coordinated-inauthentic-behavior/>.

## What it is

A **quarterly disclosure series** from [[Meta]] (Facebook / Instagram parent) reporting **coordinated inauthentic behavior (CIB) takedowns** across Meta's platforms. Began in 2018-2019 as occasional posts; formalized into a structured **quarterly report series in late 2021 / early 2022**. The reports are the **single most consistent public-facing index of state-linked and commercial influence operations on Meta surfaces**, with campaign-by-campaign metrics: number of accounts removed, pages, follower counts, ad spend, target languages, geographic origin attribution, and operational signatures.

## Key claims / findings (recurring report topics)

- **[[Doppelganger]] (Russia):** repeated quarter-on-quarter takedowns documenting the Russian network that clones Western mainstream-media outlets (BBC, Le Monde, Bild, Washington Post, Fox News, etc.) — see [[Source EU DisinfoLab 2022 Doppelganger]]
- **[[Spamouflage Dragonbridge]] (China):** consistent takedown reporting on the China-linked persistent network — see [[Source Graphika 2019 Spamouflage Dragon]]
- **Iran-linked operations:** multiple campaigns disclosed across 2022-2024
- **Cuba, Mexico, Venezuela, Iran, Israel:** smaller but recurring CIB clusters
- **Generative AI in influence operations:** late-2023 onward reports explicitly track AI-generated text, images, and video in CIB campaigns
- **Commercial operators:** distinct from state-linked, Meta has disclosed CIB activity tied to private PR/political-marketing firms (this category overlaps with the [[Hypothesis - Israeli intel-for-hire is parallel to Russian state operations|Israeli intel-for-hire ecosystem]])

## Methodology Meta discloses

- Takedown criteria: **coordinated inauthentic behavior** as defined by Meta's policies
- Attribution methodology: technical fingerprints (account creation patterns, behavioral signatures, infrastructure), open-source corroboration
- **Limitations Meta acknowledges:** attribution to specific state actors is often suggested rather than definitively asserted; takedown reports cover what Meta detected, not the full operational universe

## Reliability assessment

- **Type:** corporate disclosure (Meta is the platform being targeted; it's both reporter and affected party)
- **Independent corroboration:** [[Source Graphika 2019 Spamouflage Dragon]], [[Source EU DisinfoLab 2022 Doppelganger]], Stanford Internet Observatory, NewsGuard, and academic researchers regularly cross-reference Meta's disclosures
- **Strengths:** scale (Meta sees billions of pieces of content); technical fingerprint access unavailable to outside researchers; quarterly cadence enables longitudinal trend analysis
- **Caveats / biases:**
  - **Selection effect:** Meta reports what it removed; campaigns that escaped detection or were tolerated for policy reasons are not in the dataset
  - **Self-interest:** Meta has commercial reasons to demonstrate adversarial takedown competence; some critics argue under-reporting in politically sensitive contexts
  - **Attribution conservatism:** Meta tends toward "linked to" language rather than "operated by" — defensible for legal exposure reasons but limits analytical certainty
- **Disputed by:** academic researchers (Briant, Stanford Internet Observatory) have at points argued specific takedowns under-disclosed scale or actor identity
