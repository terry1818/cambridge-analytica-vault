# Source: FCC 2024-05 Kramer fine

## Citation

US Federal Communications Commission. (2024-05-23). Forfeiture Order proposing $6 million fine against Steven Kramer for violations of the Telephone Consumer Protection Act and the Truth in Caller ID Act.

## What this established

**$6 million proposed FCC forfeiture** against [[Steve Kramer]] for his role in the [[NH Biden AI Robocall 2024]]. The FCC found Kramer:

- Used spoofed caller ID (the personal number of a prominent NH Democratic operative)
- Sent prerecorded calls without consent (TCPA violation)
- Used AI voice cloning of a sitting US president to suppress voter turnout

## Significance

- **First major FCC enforcement** against an AI-generated political deepfake
- Combined with the [[Source NHAG 2024 Kramer indictment]], established a multi-jurisdictional enforcement framework
- Triggered the **FCC formal rule** (2024-02) banning AI-voice robocalls
- Parallel $1M settlement with **Lingo Telecom** (the carrier that transmitted the calls) established carrier liability for AI-generated content

## Significance for AI-era influence operations

The Kramer case demonstrated that **existing US regulatory infrastructure** (TCPA, Truth in Caller ID Act, voter suppression statutes) **can be applied to AI-generated political content** without waiting for new AI-specific legislation.
