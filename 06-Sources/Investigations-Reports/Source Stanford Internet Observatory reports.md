# Source: Stanford Internet Observatory reports

## Citation

Stanford Internet Observatory. (2019-2024). Body of research on coordinated inauthentic behavior, election integrity, and influence operations.

## What this body of work documents

The Stanford Internet Observatory (SIO), founded 2019 at Stanford University, produced one of the most comprehensive academic bodies of work on:

- **Coordinated Inauthentic Behavior** across platforms
- Specific state-linked operations including [[Spamouflage Dragonbridge]], Iranian operations, Russian IRA continuation
- Election integrity work for US 2020 and 2024
- Platform-policy analysis
- Generative AI threat assessment

Notable reports include the Election Integrity Partnership work covering 2020 and analysis of post-2020 election misinformation.

## 2024 dissolution

In June 2024, Stanford announced significant restructuring of SIO amid political pressure (House Judiciary Committee Republican investigations, lawsuits). Multiple senior researchers departed. The body of pre-2024 work remains available but ongoing operational research was substantially reduced.

The political pressure on SIO is itself part of the **regulatory-response-to-research backlash** that emerged in the US around 2023-2024 â€” government-aligned research on coordinated inauthentic behavior became politically contested.

## Significance

SIO was one of the **leading independent voices** in the influence-operations research field 2019-2024. The reduction of its operational capacity is significant for the field.
