# Source: ICO 2020-11 Cambridge Analytica investigation

## Citation

UK Information Commissioner's Office (Elizabeth Denham). (2020-10-02). Letter to Julian Knight MP, Chair of the Digital, Culture, Media and Sport Committee, closing the ICO's investigation into use of personal information and political influence.

## What it concluded

**Key conclusion:** "SCL/CA were not involved in the EU referendum campaign in the UK." Additionally: "I found no further evidence to change my earlier view that SCL/CA were not involved in the EU referendum campaign in the UK." Found no evidence of Russian involvement in the referendum.

## What the ICO did find

- Confirmed Facebook had inadequate safeguards (resulting in the £500,000 fine, October 2019)
- Found data protection failures by [[Leave.EU]] and [[Eldon Insurance]] — fined separately
- Found that SCL/CA's actual UK operations were less extensive than the Wylie/Kaiser characterization

## Disputed conclusions

Cadwalladr, Briant, Wylie, and Kaiser have all publicly disputed the ICO's "not involved in Brexit" conclusion. The dispute hinges on:

- Definition of "involvement" (paid contract vs. preparatory work vs. informal advice)
- Whether the ICO had access to all relevant documents
- Whether HindsightFiles material was reviewed

## Significance

The ICO conclusion is the **single most important counter-evidence** to the popular "CA decided Brexit" narrative. The vault treats this conclusion with `#claim/disputed` because primary sources differ.
