# Source: NHAG 2024 Kramer indictment

## Citation

New Hampshire Attorney General. (2024-05-23). Indictment of Steven Kramer on multiple counts including voter suppression and candidate impersonation.

## What this established

**First major US criminal indictment over an AI-generated political deepfake.** [[Steve Kramer]] was indicted by the New Hampshire Attorney General on:

- **11 felony counts of voter suppression** under New Hampshire RSA 659:40
- **11 misdemeanor counts of candidate impersonation**
- Charges related to the **[[NH Biden AI Robocall 2024]]** that went to ~25,000 NH voters two days before the 2024-01-23 NH Democratic primary
- Calls used an AI-cloned Biden voice telling Democrats not to vote in the primary

## Significance

- **First criminal prosecution** in the US of an AI-generated political deepfake under existing election law
- Established that voice-cloning a candidate is enforceable under existing state law independent of any future AI-specific statute
- Set precedent for using **voter suppression statutes** (originally written for traditional voter intimidation) against AI-generated content
- Combined with the parallel [[Source FCC 2024-05 Kramer fine]] action, established federal + state enforcement approaches

## Resolution status

- Kramer pleaded not guilty initially
- Case proceeding through NH state courts as of 2026
- Conviction would carry significant prison time
- Kramer also separately fined $6M by FCC

## Companion actions

- Federal Communications Commission $6M fine on Kramer (May 2024)
- Lingo Telecom $1M FCC settlement (August 2024)
- FCC rule banning AI-voice robocalls (2024-02)
