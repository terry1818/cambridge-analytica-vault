## Key claims / findings (sections summary)

- **Founding and ownership:** Established 2013; subsidiary of [[SCL Group]]; primary financial backer [[Robert Mercer]] (~$15M); [[Steve Bannon]] as VP and board member through 2016
- **Methodology:** Psychographic targeting drawing on [[OCEAN Model]] / Five-Factor research; "behavioral microtargeting"; data acquired primarily via the [[Global Science Research (GSR)|GSR]] / Kogan "thisisyourdigitallife" Facebook app — see [[Facebook Graph API v1]]
- **Scale of harvest:** Up to 87 million Facebook profiles (initial figure was 50M, revised upward by Facebook April 2018)
- **Client list (publicly documented):** 2014 US midterms (Cruz, Bolton, others); 2015 Cruz primary; 2016 Trump general; UK Brexit ([[Leave.EU]] orbit, with [[AggregateIQ]] handling [[Vote Leave]] / [[BeLeave]]); Kenya 2013 & 2017 (Kenyatta); Nigeria 2007 & 2015; Trinidad 2010 (UNC); Malaysia 2013 (Najib Razak); Malta 2013 (Joseph Muscat); plus other ops across India, Argentina, Mexico, Czech Republic, Romania, Italy
- **Channel 4 sting March 2018:** Undercover footage of [[Alexander Nix]] and [[Mark Turnbull]] discussing honey-trap operations, Ukrainian "girls" for entrapment, and the use of Israeli intelligence firms ([[Psy-Group]] confirmed via WSJ)
- **Whistleblower disclosures March 2018:** [[Christopher Wylie]] via Cadwalladr/NYT; [[Brittany Kaiser]] following
- **Investigations:** UK ICO; UK Parliament DCMS Committee; US FTC; SEC; Mueller (limited scope); UK Insolvency Service (resulted in 2020 Nix 7-year director ban)
- **May 2018 collapse:** CA and SCL Elections file for insolvency; assets transferred to [[Emerdata Limited]] which "fully acquired" the CA/SCL asset book
- **Successor structure:** Documented via Companies House — [[Emerdata Limited]], [[Auspex International]], [[Data Propria]], [[Firecrest Technologies]], [[Emic]]
- **Disputed effectiveness:** Article notes academic critique (Hersh, Nyhan) that CA's actual electoral impact may have been overstated relative to its marketing
