## What it is

Tertiary synthesis on [[SCL Group]] — Strategic Communication Laboratories — the **parent company** that birthed [[Cambridge Analytica]] as a US-focused election-services spinout. SCL was a UK defence-contractor operating in **PSYOPS, behavioural change, and influence operations** for **UK Ministry of Defence, US Department of Defense, NATO StratCom**, and a long roster of foreign government and political clients **before pivoting to commercial election services**. The article is the cleanest free synthesis of SCL's two-track structure: **SCL Defence** (military / government contracting) and **SCL Elections** (which became CA).

## Key claims / findings

- **Founding:** Behavioural Dynamics Institute (BDI) founded **1990**; SCL incorporated **1993**; [[Nigel Oakes]] is the central founder figure
- **Subsidiaries / corporate structure:** SCL Group as holding entity; [[Cambridge Analytica]], SCL Elections, SCL Defence, and **Crow Business Solutions MENA** as known subsidiaries
- **Defence contracting:** Held contracts with **UK MoD, US DoD, NATO**, and multiple foreign militaries for behavioural / influence operations training and field work over multiple decades pre-2014
- **Operational footprint:** Worked in 60+ countries before the CA-era election services emerged
- **Personnel pipeline:** SCL Defence operatives moved into SCL Elections and onward into CA — see [[Hypothesis - Defense-to-elections pipeline]]
- **Methodology:** SCL pioneered what it called **"Behavioural Dynamics"** — applied behavioural-science methods for **target-audience analysis and message engineering** in conflict and political environments
- **CA spinout:** SCL Elections established CA in 2013 specifically to handle US political work (US law required US-based entity for election-services contracting)
- **Emerdata succession:** [[Emerdata Limited]] established **August 2017** (months before CA's public collapse); raised **$19M January 2018**; designated as effective successor entity
- **Nix disqualification:** [[Alexander Nix]] **disqualified as a UK director for 7 years effective 2020-10-05** by the UK Insolvency Service
- **2018 collapse:** SCL Elections and CA enter insolvency May 2018; assets transferred to [[Emerdata Limited]]
- **Erik Prince linkage:** [[Johnson Chun Shun Ko]] (Emerdata director) ties [[Frontier Services Group]] / [[Erik Prince]] into the successor structure
- **SCL Defence continuity:** Less affected by the public scandal; personnel and contracts continued via successor vehicles including [[Emic]] per [[Source Briant OCCRP 2020-10-12]]
- **Backer roster:** UK aristocratic / Conservative-business funding circle ([[Jonathan Marland]], [[Roger Gabb]], [[Lord Ivar Mountbatten]], [[Vincent Tchenguiz]] reported among others) preceded the Mercer capital injection
