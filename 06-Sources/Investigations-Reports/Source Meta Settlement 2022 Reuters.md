# Source: Meta Settlement 2022 Reuters

## Citation

Raymond, Nate. (2022-12-23). "Facebook parent Meta to settle Cambridge Analytica case for $725 million." *Reuters*.

## What it documents

Meta agreed to pay **$725 million** to settle a US class-action lawsuit alleging the company improperly shared user information with Cambridge Analytica. The settlement was filed in the US District Court for the Northern District of California on December 22, 2022.

- Single largest class-action settlement in any data-privacy case at that time
- Resolved consolidated claims from US Facebook users whose data was shared with CA
- No admission of wrongdoing by Meta
- Per-claimant payouts: relatively small (varies by case approval)

## Significance

The $725M settlement is the **largest single CA-related corporate financial consequence** and the most comprehensive financial accounting of the harm to US users. Combined with:

- $5B FTC fine on Facebook (July 2019)

- $100M SEC settlement (July 2019)

Brings Facebook/Meta's CA-related liabilities to ~**$6.35 billion** across all jurisdictions and forums.
