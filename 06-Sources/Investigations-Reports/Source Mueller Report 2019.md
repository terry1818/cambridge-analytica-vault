# Source: Mueller Report 2019

## Citation

Mueller, Robert S. III. (2019). *Report On The Investigation Into Russian Interference In The 2016 Presidential Election*. Two volumes; 448 pages.

## Structure

**Volume 1** — Russian interference operations:
- Internet Research Agency (IRA) social media operations
- GRU hacking operations (DNC, Podesta emails)
- Russian government contacts with Trump campaign
- WikiLeaks coordination
- The Manafort-Kilimnik relationship
- The Trump Tower June 2016 meeting

**Volume 2** — Obstruction of justice:
- 10 separate episodes investigated for obstruction
- Mueller declined to make a prosecutorial decision (DOJ OLC opinion: sitting president cannot be indicted)
- Explicit statement: "While this report does not conclude that the President committed a crime, it also does not exonerate him."

## CA-relevant findings

The Mueller Report touches CA at multiple points:
- Manafort-Kilimnik polling data sharing (August 2016 Grand Havana Room meeting)
- Sam Patten FARA violations
- Michael Flynn CA advisory role
- The August 2016 Trump Tower meeting with Zamel/Prince/Nader
- The January 2017 Seychelles meeting (Prince + Dmitriev)

## Significance

The most authoritative US federal document on Russian 2016 interference until [[Source Senate Intel Vol 5 2020]] superseded it for breadth. Mueller's volume is **narrower in scope** (focused on prosecution potential) where Senate Intel Vol 5 is **broader** (counterintelligence threat assessment). Both are essential.

## Cross-references within the vault

The Mueller Report is the canonical primary source for the [[Mueller Investigation]] event node and for the Russian-state-actor side of the [[2016 US Presidential Election]]. It documents [[Russian interference]] (Volume I), obstruction-of-justice analysis (Volume II), and the 34 individual and 3 corporate indictments produced by the investigation.

Vault uses: Russian operational picture (IRA social media, GRU hack-and-leak via Wikileaks coordination); Trump-campaign contact catalogue (without rising to the level of criminal conspiracy in the Special Counsel's judgement); the formal record on key indicted figures including [[Paul Manafort]], [[Roger Stone]], [[Michael Flynn]], and George Papadopoulos.

For the [[Cambridge Analytica]]-specific thread, Mueller is relatively thin; the CA story is more thoroughly reconstructed by the [[ICO Investigation]] and the [[DCMS Disinformation Inquiry]].
