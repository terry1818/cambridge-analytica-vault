# Source: HindsightFiles 2020

## Citation

Kaiser, Brittany [@HindsightFiles]. (2020-01-04 onwards). *HindsightFiles release* — phased publication of internal Cambridge Analytica documents from Kaiser's personal archive. Twitter/X. <https://twitter.com/HindsightFiles>.

## What it is

A **phased document release** beginning 2020-01-04 from [[Brittany Kaiser]]'s personal archive of [[Cambridge Analytica]] materials accumulated during her tenure as CA's Director of Business Development (2014-2018). The archive comprises **100,000+ documents** spanning internal emails, contracts, project decks, country-specific operational files, and personnel records. Released country-by-country through 2020-2021 via the @HindsightFiles Twitter account in coordination with multiple journalists. **The single largest CA-related primary-document corpus in public circulation** after the 2018 Wylie disclosures.

See [[HindsightFiles 2020]] (event note) for the release timeline and [[HindsightFiles Analytical Index]] for the vault's decomposition of analyzed vs. unanalyzed material.

## Key claims / findings

- **Scope:** CA operated in **68 countries** — far broader than the ~30-country footprint visible in 2018 public reporting
- **Country-themed phasing:** initial releases (Jan 2020) covered Malaysia, Kenya, Brazil; subsequent drops added Trinidad, Mexico, Nigeria, US internal documents, Argentina, Czech Republic, Romania, Italy, and others
- **Operational methodology:** documents corroborate the [[Channel 4 Sting 2018]] claims about CA's overseas methods — honey-trap planning, racial/ethnic targeting frameworks, voter-suppression operations
- **Personnel chains:** internal correspondence revealed CA staffing pipelines and country-team rotation patterns
- **Client communications:** internal records of client-engagement scope, fees, and deliverables across multiple country operations
- **Curated, not dumped:** Kaiser selected and contextualized; this is not an indiscriminate dump but a phased disclosure with editorial choices

## Reliability assessment

- **Type:** primary documents, curated release
- **Chain of custody:** Kaiser's personal archive (her statement); not independently verified end-to-end from CA servers to public release
- **Selection bias:** Kaiser chose which countries to release and in what order; non-released material remains in her archive — see [[HindsightFiles Analytical Index]] open questions
- **Authenticity:** journalists at multiple outlets (Guardian, NYT, Daily Nation, others) have verified specific documents against external records; no major authenticity dispute publicly recorded
- **Disputed by:** none publicly contesting the documents themselves; CA's successor entities have generally declined to comment

## Notable per-country findings

- **Malaysia 2013:** documents corroborate CA-precursor SCL work for the Najib Razak campaign — see [[Malaysia 2013 Najib Razak]]
- **Kenya 2013 & 2017:** corroborates the [[360 Media]] partnership and ethnic-targeting against Raila Odinga — see [[Kenya 2013 2017 Elections]]
- **Trinidad 2010:** operational detail on the [[Trinidad 2010 Do So Campaign]] — voter suppression against Afro-Trinidadians funded by Jack Warner
- **Brazil:** pre-2018-election CA pitches and engagements — see [[HindsightFiles - Brazil material]]
