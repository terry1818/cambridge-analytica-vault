## What it is

A series of trade-press articles in Variety (industry's leading entertainment outlet) covering [[Paramount Skydance]]'s October 2025 acquisition of [[The Free Press]] and the appointment of its co-founder [[Bari Weiss]] as editor-in-chief of [[CBS News]]. The reporting spans the announcement, staff reaction, and Weiss's editorial intentions.

## Key claims / findings

- On October 6, 2025, Paramount Skydance CEO [[David Ellison]] announced the company had acquired The Free Press for **$150 million** and named Weiss editor-in-chief of CBS News.
- Weiss has **no broadcast-news experience**; she publicly quit The New York Times in 2020 and co-founded The Free Press as an alternative to "legacy media."
- The move is read by Variety as Paramount seeking to "expand its appeal among right-leaning viewers."
- Variety reports CBS News staff are "terrified" and the division is "veering toward dysfunction," with low trust between Weiss's managers and rank-and-file journalists. Former ABC/CBS executive Paul Friedman is quoted on the concern.

## Notable quotes

> "She's been extremely outspoken about her views. She said some things that I can't conceive of somebody running a news division saying out loud." — Paul Friedman, quoted in Variety
