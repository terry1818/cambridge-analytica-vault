# Source Project 2025 Mandate for Leadership

## Citation

> Dans, P., & Groves, S. (eds.). (2023-04-21). *Mandate for Leadership: The Conservative Promise* (Project 2025: Presidential Transition Project). The Heritage Foundation. https://static.project2025.org/2025_MandateForLeadership_FULL.pdf

## What it is

A primary policy document — the central published text of **Project 2025** (formally the "2025 Presidential Transition Project"), an initiative led by the **Heritage Foundation** and an advisory coalition of conservative organizations. Published April 2023, "Mandate for Leadership: The Conservative Promise" is the ninth in Heritage's "Mandate for Leadership" series, which dates back to 1981 and the Reagan transition.

## Key claims / findings

- The document is **roughly 900 pages**, organized into **30 chapters** covering nearly every federal department and agency.
- It was assembled by editors **Paul Dans** and **Steven Groves** with **more than 35 primary authors** and hundreds of contributors — many of them former Trump-administration officials.
- It articulates **four stated aims**: (1) restore the family as the centerpiece of American life; (2) dismantle the "administrative state"; (3) defend national sovereignty and borders; (4) secure "God-given individual rights to live freely."
- Core proposals include **expanding presidential control over the executive branch** (reviving "Schedule F" to reclassify and replace civil servants), **abolishing or restructuring agencies** (e.g. dismantling the Department of Education), and reorienting the Department of Justice and intelligence agencies toward presidential direction.
- It advocates policies on **abortion, immigration, climate (rolling back climate regulation), and education** consistent with the religious-right and small-government agendas.
- Project 2025 has additional components beyond this document: a personnel database, a transition "playbook," and training ("Presidential Administration Academy").
- Critics — including the ACLU and Center for American Progress — characterize it as a blueprint for **authoritarian consolidation of executive power**; Heritage frames it as standard transition planning.

## Notable quotes

> "It is not enough for conservatives to win elections... we need a coalition of conservatives committed to reorienting the federal government." — paraphrasing the document's framing introduction by Kevin Roberts

## Reliability assessment

- **Primary document.** Tier-1 for what Project 2025 *proposes* — it is the actual published text, not a description of it.
- Important distinction: the document is authoritative evidence of **intent and policy design**. Whether it functions as the operative agenda of a given administration is a separate, contested question (Trump publicly distanced himself from it during the 2024 campaign while many of its authors entered or were tied to his administration).
- Known biases: an explicitly ideological advocacy document produced by a partisan think tank — by design, not neutral.
