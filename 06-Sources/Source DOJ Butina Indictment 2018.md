# Source DOJ Butina Indictment 2018

## Citation

> US Department of Justice. (2018-07-17). *United States v. Maria Butina — Criminal Complaint (filed 2018-07-15) and Indictment*. US District Court for the District of Columbia. https://www.justice.gov/archives/opa/pr/russian-national-charged-conspiracy-act-agent-russian-federation-within-united-states

## What it is

A US federal criminal case. The criminal complaint against [[Maria Butina]] was filed and her arrest made on 15 July 2018; the District of Columbia grand jury returned an indictment on 17 July 2018. The charging documents were produced by the DOJ National Security Division and the US Attorney for D.C. They allege a clandestine Russian influence operation conducted on US soil from roughly 2015 through at least early 2017.

## Key claims / findings

- Butina conspired to act, and acted, as an agent of the Russian Federation inside the United States without notifying the Attorney General, in violation of 18 U.S.C. §§ 951 and 371.
- She worked at the direction of a "high-level official" in the Russian government — identified in reporting as [[Alexander Torshin]], a former Federation Council member and deputy governor of the Russian Central Bank.
- Her tasked objective was to develop relationships with US persons and infiltrate organizations with influence in American politics — centrally the [[National Rifle Association]] — to advance Russian interests.
- The filings describe a written "influence operation" proposal and characterize her gun-rights advocacy group as a vehicle for cultivating the NRA and Republican contacts.
- A later affidavit allegation that Butina traded sex for access was retracted by prosecutors as a misreading of texts.

## Notable quotes

> "The Russian official and Butina took steps to develop relationships with American politicians in order to establish private, or as Butina called them, 'back channel' lines of communication." — DOJ press release, 2018-07-16

## Reliability assessment

- Primary: a Tier-1 government court filing; sworn allegations, not yet proven at filing.
- Independent corroboration: Butina pleaded guilty 13 December 2018 to conspiracy under § 371, converting the core allegation into an admitted fact; Senate Intelligence Committee findings corroborate the back-channel effort.
- Known biases / agenda: prosecutorial document; states the government's case. The sex-for-access claim was withdrawn — a known overreach.
- Disputed by: Butina's defense argued she was a student and networker, not a spy; sentencing court accepted a narrower conspiracy than espionage.
