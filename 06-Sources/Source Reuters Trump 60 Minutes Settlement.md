# Source Reuters Trump 60 Minutes Settlement

## Citation

> Reuters staff. (2025-07-02). *Paramount settles with Trump over '60 Minutes' interview lawsuit*. Reuters. https://www.reuters.com/legal/ — corroborated by NPR, NBC News, and Time.

## What it is

Reuters wire reporting on [[Paramount Global]]'s July 2, 2025 settlement of a lawsuit brought by President [[Donald Trump]]. Trump alleged that a *60 Minutes* interview with Vice President Kamala Harris, aired in October 2024 on [[CBS News]], was deceptively edited. The settlement was reached while the [[Skydance Media]]–Paramount merger awaited FCC approval.

## Key claims / findings

- Paramount agreed to pay **$16 million** to settle; the original suit, filed in the Northern District of Texas, sought **$20 billion** in damages.
- The settlement covers plaintiffs' fees and costs; the remainder is allocated to **Trump's future presidential library** — not paid to Trump personally.
- Paramount did **not** issue an apology and admitted no wrongdoing.
- As part of the settlement, *60 Minutes* agreed to **release transcripts** of future interviews with presidential candidates after they air.
- The settlement was widely linked to Paramount's need for FCC clearance of the Skydance merger; Senator Elizabeth Warren and others called it possible "bribery."
