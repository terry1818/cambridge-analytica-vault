## What it is

The English Wikipedia article on **CBS News**, the news division of the CBS broadcast network — now a unit of [[Paramount Skydance]]. It summarizes the division's history, programming, and recent leadership upheaval.

## Key claims / findings

- CBS News' first television broadcasts began in **1941** with WCBW in New York City; its radio news operation predates that.
- The division built its reputation in the **Edward R. Murrow / Walter Cronkite** era and produced *60 Minutes*, created by Don Hewitt and premiering **September 24, 1968**.
- Current programming includes *CBS Evening News*, *CBS Mornings*, *CBS News Sunday Morning*, *60 Minutes*, *48 Hours*, and *Face the Nation*.
- *60 Minutes* is described as the most successful broadcast in US television history, holding a long run as the country's top-rated news program.
- The article documents the **2024–2025 turmoil**: President Trump's lawsuit over a *60 Minutes* Harris interview, Paramount's $16M settlement, the resignations of CBS News executives, the Skydance merger, and the October 2025 appointment of [[Bari Weiss]] as editor-in-chief.
