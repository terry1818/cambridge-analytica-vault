# Source NYT Skydance Paramount Merger 2024

## Citation

> Mullin, B., & Hirsch, L. (2024-07-07). *Skydance Reaches Deal to Merge With Paramount, Ending Redstone Era*. The New York Times. https://www.nytimes.com/2024/07/07/business/media/paramount-skydance-merger.html

## What it is

New York Times media-and-deals reporting on the merger agreement between [[Skydance Media]] and [[Paramount Global]], announced July 7, 2024 after months of on-and-off negotiation. The NYT business desk (Mullin and Hirsch are its lead media-deal reporters) tracked the deal across 2024, including a collapsed earlier round of talks and a special-committee review.

## Key claims / findings

- Skydance and partners reached an agreement for a **three-way merger** of Skydance, National Amusements (the Redstone family holding company), and Paramount Global, creating "New Paramount."
- The [[Ellison family]] and **RedBird Capital Partners** committed to invest **over $8 billion**, including a roughly **$2.4 billion** purchase of National Amusements.
- Paramount Class A shareholders were offered **$23/share** and Class B shareholders **$15/share** in a cash/stock election.
- The deal valued the combined enterprise at roughly **$28 billion** and ended the **Redstone family's** decades-long control of Paramount/CBS via [[Shari Redstone]].
- [[David Ellison]] would become chairman and CEO; former NBCUniversal CEO Jeff Shell would become president.
