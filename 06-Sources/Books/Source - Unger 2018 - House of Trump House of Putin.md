# Source — House of Trump, House of Putin: The Untold Story of Donald Trump and the Russian Mafia (Craig Unger, 2018)

## Bibliographic
Craig Unger; *House of Trump, House of Putin: The Untold Story of Donald Trump and the Russian Mafia*; Dutton; 2018.

## Thesis & scope
Unger argues that over a period of more than four decades, Russian organised crime — operating with the knowledge and protection of Russian intelligence and ultimately of Vladimir Putin — systematically used Trump-branded real estate as a money-laundering vehicle, and that this financial entanglement created leverage over Trump that amounts to a long-running "asset" relationship. The book traces the connection from the 1984 Trump Tower condo sales to Russian mobsters, through the Atlantic City casino bailout period, to the Felix Sater/Bayrock era, and into the 2016 campaign. Unger is a credentialed investigative journalist (former editor, Boston Magazine; long-time contributor to The New Republic and Vanity Fair), and many of the specific facts he cites are documented; the *causal* claim — that the cumulative relationship constitutes an intentional asset-cultivation operation — is inferential and contested.

**Contested-claim guidance:** The book became a NYT bestseller but received mixed critical reviews. The Washington Post's reviewer noted that Unger documents real connections but that the "asset" framing involves inferential leaps not supported by documentary evidence. The Mueller Report neither confirmed nor denied the broader conspiracy thesis; it found insufficient evidence of a criminal conspiracy between the Trump campaign and the Russian government. Specific real-estate transactions, individual biographies, and documented meetings can be used at Tier-2; the intentionality/asset framing should be treated as Tier-3/speculative.

## Key content relevant to the vault

- **David Bogatin** — Russian mobster who in 1984 purchased five condos at Trump Tower for $6 million in cash, meeting personally with Trump; US government subsequently seized the condos, ruling they were purchased to launder money; Bogatin's brother ran a $150 million stock fraud with Semion Mogilevich [documented in court records].
- **Semion Mogilevich** — described by the FBI as the "boss of bosses" of the Russian mafia; Unger traces his alleged connections to multiple Trump-adjacent figures; Mogilevich's organisation has been linked by US authorities to weapons trafficking and large-scale financial fraud [FBI designation is documented; specific Trump connections are more inferential in Unger's treatment].
- **Felix Sater** — twice-convicted felon; ran Bayrock Group's daily operations; collaborated with Trump Organisation on the Trump SoHo and other projects; Sater's father was convicted for Mogilevich-linked extortion; Sater subsequently became an FBI informant — this dual role (mob-connected / FBI asset) is documented and not disputed. Unger argues Sater served as a Kremlin-connected intermediary [Sater's mob connections documented; deliberate Kremlin-direction is contested].
- **Bayrock Group** — real estate development firm that partnered with Trump on multiple projects 2003–2008; Unger presents it as a vehicle for Russian-origin money; Bayrock was the subject of a separate civil lawsuit alleging money laundering (settled).
- **Atlantic City casino period** — Unger argues that Russian-linked investors provided crucial capital to bail out Trump's failing casinos in the early 1990s, creating the first financial leverage; this is inferential — Trump's casino failures are documented, the Russian-capital-as-bailout claim is not established by primary sources.
- **The "59 people" claim** — Unger claims to have traced 59 individuals with alleged ties to Russian organised crime or intelligence who had business relationships with Trump or his associates; this is a book-level claim that should be disaggregated: individual figures vary in how well-documented the connections are.
- **Pattern: condominium purchases as a laundering vehicle** — Unger draws on a well-documented pattern (also covered in a 2017 New Republic piece by the same author and in the FinCEN Files investigations) showing that all-cash, LLC-structured luxury real estate purchases in Trump properties in Miami and New York had unusually high concentrations of Russian-origin purchasers; this pattern is documented by journalists and FinCEN separately from Unger's book.
- **Sater / Moscow Trump Tower 2015–2016** — Unger covers the Felix Sater–Michael Cohen correspondence about a proposed Trump Tower Moscow during the 2016 campaign; this is established by the Mueller investigation and Cohen's guilty plea [Tier-2 on these specific facts].

## Evidence tier
Tier-3 for the overall "asset" thesis. Tier-2 for specific documented facts: David Bogatin's 1984 Trump Tower purchases and government seizure; Felix Sater's criminal history and FBI informant status; Bayrock's partnership with Trump Organisation; Sater-Cohen Moscow Trump Tower correspondence (confirmed by Mueller). Unger is a credentialed journalist but the causal inference connecting these facts into a deliberate asset-cultivation operation is not established by primary documents available publicly. The Mueller Report's findings should be weighted over Unger's interpretations where they conflict.
