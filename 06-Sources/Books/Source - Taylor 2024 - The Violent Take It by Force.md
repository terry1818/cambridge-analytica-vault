# Source — The Violent Take It by Force: The Christian Movement That Is Threatening Our Democracy (Matthew D. Taylor, 2024)

## Bibliographic
Matthew D. Taylor; *The Violent Take It by Force: The Christian Movement That Is Threatening Our Democracy*; Broadleaf Books; 2024. Taylor is a scholar at the Institute for Islamic, Christian, and Jewish Studies and a researcher at Political Research Associates.

## Thesis & scope
Taylor argues that the New Apostolic Reformation (NAR) — the network of independent charismatic churches and apostolic/prophetic leaders founded by C. Peter Wagner in the 1990s–2000s — has become the single most politically consequential religious movement in American democracy, playing a direct operational role in the January 6 Capitol attack and in the broader Trump-era political mobilization. The book traces the NAR's theological foundations (apostles, prophets, spiritual warfare, Seven Mountains), its organizational structure, and the documented chain of events connecting specific NAR leaders to January 6. This is the most scholar-sourced, footnote-dense account of the NAR's political role available.

## Key content relevant to the vault
- Establishes **[[C. Peter Wagner]]** (1930–2016) as the organizational architect of the NAR — a seminary professor at Fuller Theological Seminary who systematically constructed the apostolic/prophetic leadership network from the 1990s onward. Vault has a Wagner person note; this is the deepest single-source on his institutional role.

- **[[Che Ahn]]** (also Ché Ahn) — Korean American NAR apostle based in Pasadena; documented as a principal figure in the NAR governance network; present in DC around January 6 events.
- **[[Paula White]]** — Trump's official White House spiritual advisor; her call for "angelic reinforcements" on January 6 is documented as spiritually-authorized mobilization language within the NAR frame.
- **[[Sean Feucht]]** and **Bethel Music** — documents how Bethel Church (Redding, CA) and its worship music have become a mass cultural delivery system for NAR ideology, making NAR aesthetics mainstream across independent charismatic churches.
- **[[Bill Johnson]]** (Bethel Church, Redding CA) — the apostolic leader who made Bethel the flagship institution of the commercial/worship side of the NAR, giving it cultural reach far beyond the explicit apostolic networks.
- **Chuck Pierce** — apostle in the NAR network; Globe of Prayer / Glory of Zion International Ministries; documented as a key figure in NAR prophetic strategy.
- **[[Lance Wallnau]]** — documents his "Cyrus Anointing" prophecy for Trump (the idea that God anoints a flawed leader like the Persian King Cyrus to restore Israel) as the single most influential theological frame enabling evangelical support for Trump despite his personal conduct. This is the specific theological mechanism the vault's NAR notes should carry.
- Documents **over 50 charismatic Christian leaders** who were in DC on January 6, all with documented NAR ties — establishing that Jan. 6 was not incidental to NAR theology but a direct expression of spiritual warfare practice.
- Theological genealogy: **Latter Rain movement** (1940s–50s) → **Kansas City Prophets** (1980s, Mike Bickle / IHOPKC) → **C. Peter Wagner's apostolic networks** (1990s–2000s) → present NAR. Taylor documents this lineage in detail.
- **Shofar-blowing, worship music, and kneeling prayer at the Capitol** on January 6 as documented spiritual warfare ritual, not decoration — Taylor argues these were intentional acts within the NAR's theological framework.
- The **Appeal to Heaven / Pine Tree flag** as a documented symbol binding NAR spiritual warfare theology to insurrectionist action; traced from its pre-Revolutionary War origin through Dutch Sheets' deliberate redeployment.
