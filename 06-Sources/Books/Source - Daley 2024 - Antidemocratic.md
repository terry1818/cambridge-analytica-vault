# Source — Antidemocratic: Inside the Far Right's 50-Year Plot to Control American Elections (David Daley, 2024)

## Bibliographic
David Daley, *Antidemocratic: Inside the Far Right's 50-Year Plot to Control American Elections* (HarperCollins / Mariner Books, 2024). Daley is senior fellow at FairVote and author of *Ratf**ked* (2016). This is the sequel and companion volume; the two books together cover redistricting (2016) and judicial/VRA destruction (2024).

## Thesis & scope
A coordinated, well-funded, 50-year conservative legal project has systematically targeted the Voting Rights Act of 1965 with the goal of eliminating its enforcement mechanisms and ratifying minority rule in American elections. The through-line is **John Roberts**: as a 26-year-old Reagan DOJ lawyer in 1981 he drafted memos opposing the VRA's Section 2 extension; as Chief Justice in 2013 he wrote *Shelby County v. Holder* gutting Section 5; in 2021 his majority in *Brnovich v. DNC* severely weakened Section 2. The [[Federalist Society]] and [[Heritage Foundation]] provided the ideological infrastructure and judicial pipeline. REDMAP and partisan gerrymandering (covered in Daley 2016) are one tool; judicial dismantlement of the VRA is the other.

## Key content relevant to the vault

**John Roberts's 50-year arc.** In 1981, Roberts wrote internal Reagan DOJ memos arguing against strengthening Section 2 of the VRA, which covers discriminatory voting practices not requiring proof of discriminatory intent. He consistently opposed the 1982 VRA reauthorization. Daley documents this paper trail with specificity. Three decades later, Roberts used *Shelby County* to strike down the Section 5 preclearance formula (the mechanism requiring states with a history of discrimination to get DOJ approval before changing election laws), and *Brnovich* to limit Section 2 challenges to discriminatory voting rules.

**Shelby County v. Holder (2013).** Roberts's majority opinion declared the preclearance coverage formula outdated, effectively suspending Section 5. Within hours of the ruling, Texas announced a strict photo ID law and a redistricting plan previously blocked by DOJ. North Carolina enacted a comprehensive voting restriction package within weeks. The ruling is the pivot point in the modern voter-suppression wave.

**Brnovich v. DNC (2021).** The Supreme Court upheld Arizona's out-of-precinct ballot discard rule and its ban on ballot-harvesting (third-party collection of mail-in ballots). The majority created a new multi-factor test for Section 2 challenges that substantially raises the evidentiary bar. Daley presents this as *Shelby County*'s logical successor — Section 5 was killed in 2013, Section 2 was crippled in 2021.

**Federalist Society and Heritage Foundation pipeline.** Daley documents the institutional investment in placing judges who would uphold voting restrictions: from Reagan-era DOJ hiring through the Federalist Society's judge-vetting process to the Senate confirmation pipeline that the vault covers elsewhere. The [[Judicial Crisis Network]] (Concord Fund) — already in vault — funded the campaigns to confirm Gorsuch, Kavanaugh, and Barrett.

**Section 2 VRA under further attack.** Post-*Brnovich*, conservative litigants brought cases in Alabama, Louisiana, and Georgia challenging Black-majority congressional districts as racial gerrymanders — using Section 2 itself as a weapon against the districts it was meant to protect. *Allen v. Milligan* (2023) partially pushed back on this in Alabama, but the overall trajectory is continued erosion.

**State-level coordination.** The book connects the judicial project to the same patron-class funding networks (Heritage, Federalist Society, [[Judicial Crisis Network]]) and the same state-level legislative coordination (Heritage Action's voter suppression model legislation) documented in Berman 2024.

**Election subversion infrastructure.** Daley extends his analysis to post-2020 election denial legislation — bills in multiple states shifting certification authority from election administrators to partisan legislatures, creating a mechanism for legislative override of election results. This connects to the vault's [[Election Denial Infrastructure]] lens.
