# Source — This Is Not Propaganda: Adventures in the War Against Reality (Peter Pomerantsev, 2019)

## Bibliographic
Peter Pomerantsev; *This Is Not Propaganda: Adventures in the War Against Reality*; PublicAffairs; 2019.

## Thesis & scope
Modern authoritarian information warfare aims not to persuade people of a specific lie but to destroy the very concept of objective truth — making citizens so overwhelmed, confused, and cynical that they abandon the idea that facts can adjudicate political disputes. Pomerantsev argues this "war against reality" began as a distinctive feature of Putinist Russia (which he documented in his earlier *Nothing Is True and Everything Is Possible*, 2014) but has migrated to democracies globally. The book blends personal memoir — his family's Soviet-era dissident experience, his father's work as a Russian dissident broadcaster — with investigative reporting across the Philippines, Ukraine, Georgia, Brexit Britain, and the 2016 US election.

## Key content relevant to the vault
- **"Firehose of falsehood"** doctrine: the Russian state does not need citizens to believe its lies, only to be unable to distinguish truth from fabrication — documented through the Kremlin's RT, troll farms, and the [[Internet Research Agency (IRA)]].
- **[[Rodrigo Duterte]] campaign in the Philippines**: the book's most detailed non-Russian case study — Duterte's team used Facebook as a low-cost mass-persuasion platform, weaponizing patriotic trolls and influencer networks. Documented as a model later replicated globally.
- **Ukraine information environment**: Pomerantsev profiles Ukrainian activists and civil society organizations attempting to counter Russian narratives — presenting the Ukrainian information war as the laboratory for Western democratic resilience strategies.
- **Brexit information environment**: observes that the propaganda techniques refined in Russia appeared in the Leave campaign's targeting, emotional manipulation, and contempt for fact-checking. Connects to the vault's Brexit cluster without naming CA specifically.
- **2016 US election**: Russian interference analyzed through the lens of the "reality-destruction" thesis — the IRA's goal was not to elect Trump per se but to corrode the epistemic commons.
- **Family memoir thread**: Pomerantsev's father Igor Pomerantsev was a BBC Russian Service journalist — the Soviet Union's targeting of émigré radio broadcasters is presented as the ancestral form of current information warfare.
- **"Political technologists"** (Kremlin term): specialists in narrative manipulation and reality construction; presented as the professional class who designed post-Soviet information strategy. Gleb Pavlovsky is named as a key figure.
- **Surkovism / "managed democracy"**: Vladislav Surkov's doctrine that reality is a permanent political construction project — no fixed truth, only competing narratives managed by the state.
- **Maidan/EuroMaidan**: the Ukrainian revolution as a case study in how social media-enabled civic mobilization can both succeed and be subsequently contaminated by disinformation.
- **Digital counter-measures**: the book's final section examines grassroots and institutional responses — fact-checking organizations, media literacy programs, platforms accountability efforts — framed as early and inadequate.
