# Source — On Tyranny: Twenty Lessons from the Twentieth Century (Timothy Snyder, 2017)

## Bibliographic
Timothy Snyder. *On Tyranny: Twenty Lessons from the Twentieth Century*. Tim Duggan Books (Crown), 2017. 128 pp. ISBN 978-0804190114. (Illustrated edition 2021, art by Nora Kuperberg.)

Note: Snyder's longer theoretical works are *Bloodlands* (2010) and *The Road to Unfreedom* (2018) — the latter is separately noted in the vault ([[Source - Snyder 2018 - Road to Unfreedom]]).

## Thesis & scope
Democracies die in part because citizens comply in advance with what they anticipate authoritarian governments will want. Drawing on the history of Nazi Germany, Stalinist USSR, interwar Eastern Europe, and the post-Soviet authoritarian turn, Snyder offers twenty practical lessons for citizens who want to resist democratic backsliding. The book is prescriptive and explicitly addressed to Americans watching the early Trump presidency (published February 2017).

The book is short, polemical, and deliberately accessible — it is not a scholarly monograph but a civic intervention. Its value to the vault is as a synthesis of Snyder's scholarly positions in compressed form, and as a document of what a leading historian of fascism considered the most important warnings in early 2017.

## Key content relevant to the vault

**Selected lessons with vault relevance:**

1. *Do not obey in advance* — anticipatory compliance is how authoritarianism obtains most of its power without coercion. Relevant to the vault's [[Conservative complicity thesis]] and the behavior of Republican legislators.
2. *Defend institutions* — institutions do not defend themselves; they depend on people choosing to honor them.
3. *Beware the one-party state* — support for multi-party competition even when your party loses.
4. *Take responsibility for the face of the world* — the visual and symbolic environment matters; resist the normalization of authoritarian symbols.
5. *Remember professional ethics* — lawyers, journalists, doctors, and civil servants who subordinate professional judgment to political loyalty enable tyranny.
6. *Be wary of paramilitaries* — when the government arms a group of people outside normal state structures, it is preparing for violence against the citizenry.
7. *Be reflective if you must be armed* — those who bear arms should be loyal to the law, not the leader.
8. *Stand out* — someone has to be first to resist; collective action starts with individual visible dissent.
9. *Be kind to our language* — avoid clichés and bureaucratic abstraction; Snyder draws on Orwell and Arendt (banality of evil) on language as a vehicle for moral evasion.
10. *Believe in truth* — post-truth politics is a tool of authoritarian control; surrendering the idea of truth surrenders the basis for accountability.
11. *Investigate* — do not rely on passive media consumption; pursue primary sources.
12. *Make eye contact and small talk* — maintain social solidarity as a counter to atomization.
13. *Contribute to good causes* — support civil-society organizations that defend rights.
14. *Learn from peers in other countries* — the international democratic community is a resource; isolationism benefits authoritarians.
15. *Listen for dangerous words* — "extremism," "terrorism," "emergency" are used to justify emergency powers.
16. *Be calm when the unthinkable arrives* — do not let crisis be used to permanently expand executive power.
17. *Be a patriot* — distinguish between nationalism (we are the best, follow me) and patriotism (we must live up to our ideals).
18. *Be as courageous as you can.*

**Historical cases cited:** Nazi Germany (Enabling Act, Reichstag fire), Stalinist purges, Czech 1948 communist takeover, Polish Solidarity movement, interwar fascist movements in Austria and Hungary.

**Key conceptual contributions to the vault:**
- **"Anticipatory obedience"** — the mechanism by which authoritarian power expands without direct commands.
- **Politics of eternity** (developed more fully in *Road to Unfreedom*, referenced here): the use of mythologized past to justify authoritarian present. The vault has [[Politics of eternity (Snyder)]] — this book is the accessible entry point.

## Evidence tier
Tier-2: Snyder is a leading academic historian of Eastern Europe and fascism (Yale; author of *Bloodlands*, *Black Earth*). *On Tyranny* is explicitly polemical and prescriptive rather than scholarly — it should be used as a synthesis of his analytical positions and a document of the 2017 resistance moment, not as primary historical evidence. Historical examples cited are well-documented; analogical applications to contemporary US politics are Snyder's argument.
