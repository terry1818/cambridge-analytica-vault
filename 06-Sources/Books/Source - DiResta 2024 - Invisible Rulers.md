# Source — Invisible Rulers: The People Who Turn Lies into Reality (Renée DiResta, 2024)

## Bibliographic
Renée DiResta; *Invisible Rulers: The People Who Turn Lies into Reality*; PublicAffairs; 2024. 436 pp.

## Thesis & scope
DiResta argues that a new architecture of persuasion — composed of influencers, platform algorithms, and activated crowds — has replaced the 20th-century model of top-down mass media propaganda. The key operational maxim of the new system: "if you make it trend, you make it true." Bespoke realities are constructed not by centralized state broadcasters but by a distributed network of influencer-propagandists whose content is amplified by engagement-optimizing algorithms and spread by ideologically primed crowd participants. The book draws on DiResta's years of research at the Stanford Internet Observatory and her personal experience being targeted by the coordinated political attack on disinformation researchers in 2023–24.

## Key content relevant to the vault
- **Influencers, algorithms, crowds triad**: the three mutually reinforcing elements. Influencers manipulate algorithmic amplification; algorithms reward engagement regardless of truth; crowds spread content and provide social proof.
- **Anti-vax networked activism (2015)**: DiResta's entry point — she traced how a pre-COVID anti-vax movement used Facebook Groups, Twitter bots, and influencer coordination to overwhelm pro-vaccine messaging. She identifies this as the prototype for all subsequent disinformation campaigns.
- **[[QAnon]]**: analyzed as a distributed conspiracy network that merged anti-vax, Stop the Steal, and Great Replacement narratives; Facebook Groups as the organizational infrastructure.
- **Stop the Steal / Big Lie infrastructure**: DiResta documents the influencer network that propagated 2020 election fraud claims — Trump, Musk, and lower-tier influencer-propagandists as nodes.
- **[[Jack Posobiec]]**: named as a Pizzagate and MAGA influencer-propagandist.
- **"Amazing Polly"**: QAnon thought leader who helped instigate the Wayfair child-trafficking conspiracy.
- **[[Matt Taibbi]]**: profiled as a formerly left-leaning journalist who became a vehicle for the congressional attack on disinformation research (alongside [[Jim Jordan]]).
- **[[Kate Starbird]]** (University of Washington Center for an Informed Public): colleague targeted alongside DiResta.
- **[[Stanford Internet Observatory]]**: shuttered effectively in 2024 after legal/political pressure; DiResta's termination recounted.
- **"Bespoke realities"**: each ideological community constructs its own information environment; consensus on basic facts becomes impossible.
- **Great Replacement Theory**: cited as an example of a "bespoke reality" spread via YouTube, TikTok, Telegram.
- **Facebook Groups**: identified as the primary organizational infrastructure for MAGA/QAnon mobilization; role in January 6 documented.
- **[[January 6 Capitol Attack]]**: DiResta's argument that online information ecosystems produced real-world political violence — "Here were crowds of people screaming the online slogan 'Stop the Steal' and smashing Capitol windows."
- **Congressional harassment of researchers**: Jim Jordan's "weaponization" subcommittee subpoenas and lawsuits against DiResta and colleagues; framed as a deliberate defunding of the disinformation research infrastructure ahead of 2024.
- **[[Russian interference]] 2016**: path prepared by domestic anti-vax and conspiracy ecosystems that degraded trust in institutions before Russian operations began.
- **Elon Musk's X (Twitter)**: identified as an amplifier of election-fraud claims and influencer-propagandists in 2024 cycle.
