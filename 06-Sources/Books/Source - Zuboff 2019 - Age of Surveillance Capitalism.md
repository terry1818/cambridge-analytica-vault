# Source — The Age of Surveillance Capitalism: The Fight for a Human Future at the New Frontier of Power (Shoshana Zuboff, 2019)

## Bibliographic
Shoshana Zuboff; *The Age of Surveillance Capitalism: The Fight for a Human Future at the New Frontier of Power*; PublicAffairs; 2019. 704 pp.

## Thesis & scope
Zuboff argues that a novel economic logic — "surveillance capitalism" — emerged at Google around 2001 and was subsequently adopted by Facebook, Amazon, and others. The model claims human experience as raw material, extracts behavioral data at scale, refines it into "behavioral surplus" (data beyond what is needed to improve products), and sells predictions about future behavior in "behavioral futures markets." The ultimate ambition of the most advanced surveillance capitalists is not merely to predict behavior but to *modify* it — creating what Zuboff calls a "means of behavioral modification." The book situates this as a civilizational threat comparable to what industrial capitalism did to the natural world.

## Key content relevant to the vault
- **"Behavioral surplus"**: the excess behavioral data harvested beyond product improvement purposes; the core economic feedstock of the model.
- **"Behavioral futures markets"**: markets where predictions about human actions are bought and sold; advertisers as the initial clients, political campaigns as the logical extension.
- **Google** as the originating company: Zuboff traces the model to Google's 2001 pivot when it began monetizing search query data beyond its stated service purpose.
- **Facebook** as the second-order adopter: extended the model to social graph, emotional state, and interpersonal relationship data.
- **"Extraction imperative"**: the compulsion to expand data collection to every domain of human life — hence smart devices, always-on microphones, location tracking, health monitors.
- **"Instrumentarian power"**: Zuboff's term for the new form of power that doesn't require coercion because it shapes the "behavioral herd" through nudges, subliminal cues, and environmental manipulation.
- **"The uncontract"**: the fiction of user consent via Terms of Service that no user reads and that do not represent genuine informed agreement.
- **Pokemon Go** and **Facebook emotional contagion experiment (2014)**: cited as examples of real-world behavioral modification experiments conducted without meaningful consent.
- **Cambridge Analytica** appears in Zuboff's analysis as a downstream expression of the surveillance capitalist logic: [[Cambridge Analytica]] purchased behavioral surplus (via [[Aleksandr Kogan]] / [[Global Science Research]]) and used it for political behavioral modification — the exact "behavioral futures market" in political form.
- **OCEAN model** / psychographic targeting: Zuboff situates [[Psychographic Targeting]] within the broader logic of behavioral prediction and modification.
- **Eric Schmidt** (Google CEO) and **Sheryl Sandberg** (Facebook COO) are profiled as architects of the model.
- **HAL 9400** metaphor (from Kubrick): used to describe the ambition of total behavioral prediction.
