# Source — The Good News Club: The Christian Right's Stealth Assault on America's Children (Katherine Stewart, 2012)

## Bibliographic
- Author: Katherine Stewart (journalist and author; also authored *The Power Worshippers: Inside the Dangerous Rise of Religious Nationalism*, 2020 — already in vault as [[Source - Stewart 2020 - The Power Worshippers]])
- Publisher: PublicAffairs, 2012
- ISBN: 978-1586488437
- Note: Stewart has a dedicated vault entry as an investigator/journalist (line 179)

## Thesis & scope
Stewart investigates the Good News Club (GNC), an after-school program operated by Child Evangelism Fellowship (CEF), which gained the right to meet in public school buildings after the Supreme Court's 2001 ruling in *Good News Club v. Milford Central School* (held that excluding religious clubs from school buildings while permitting secular clubs was viewpoint discrimination under the Free Speech Clause). Stewart argues that CEF's program is not merely about after-school religious expression but is part of a deliberate, strategically coordinated effort by the Christian Right to: (1) evangelize public school children during school hours in the school environment; (2) use the access as a lever to blur and ultimately dismantle the Establishment Clause; and (3) pursue a long-term strategy to defund and eliminate public education itself.

## Key content relevant to the vault
**Central organization:**
- **Child Evangelism Fellowship (CEF)** — the primary organization; a parachurch group aiming to place a GNC chapter in every public school in the US; Stewart provides detailed organizational history, funding sources, and political strategy; CEF has vault entry at line 889 ([[Child Evangelism Fellowship]])
- The GNC curriculum: Stewart examines CEF's content, which includes specific evangelical theological content (sin, Hell, salvation through Jesus alone) taught in public school buildings immediately after school hours in ways that blur the distinction between school and church

**Legal and political strategy documented:**
- *Good News Club v. Milford Central School* (2001) — the Supreme Court ruling CEF used as a wedge; Stewart explains how CEF prepared this litigation over years with the [[Alliance Defending Freedom]] (then called Alliance Defense Fund) and the ACLJ as co-strategists
- Alliance Defense Fund (now ADF) / [[Alliance Defending Freedom]] — documented as the legal infrastructure behind CEF's expansion; the litigation strategy as a deliberate constitutional-capture project
- Mathew Staver / Liberty Counsel — Liberty Counsel is the other major litigation arm Stewart documents as supporting GNC expansion; [[Liberty Counsel]] has a vault entry (line 481)
- The strategy of using incremental free-speech rulings to establish precedents that erode Establishment Clause protections

**Broader education-capture strategy:**
- Final chapter: Stewart documents an overarching Christian Right imperative to "defund and ultimately eliminate" public schools; GNC is one vector in a multi-pronged strategy that includes voucher programs, charter school networks, and homeschool legislation
- The school as contested terrain: Stewart maps how the Christian Right treats the public school system as both a target for infiltration (GNC, See You at the Pole, released-time programs) and a system to be eventually replaced with private Christian alternatives
- **Released-time religious education programs** — another legal vehicle (upheld by *Zorach v. Clauson*, 1952) that allows students to leave school for religious instruction; Stewart documents how these are being revived and expanded as parallel to GNC
- **See You at the Pole** — annual student-led prayer event at school flagpoles; Stewart covers it as part of the broader strategy of normalizing Christian presence in public schools

**Named figures:**
- Reece Kauffman — CEF president (at time of writing); documented as the public face of the national expansion strategy
- The legal architects at ADF — Stewart names specific attorneys involved in the GNC litigation strategy
