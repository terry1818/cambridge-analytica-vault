# Source — Ratf**ked: The True Story Behind the Secret Plan to Steal America's Democracy (David Daley, 2016)

## Bibliographic
David Daley, *Ratf**ked: The True Story Behind the Secret Plan to Steal America's Democracy* (Liveright / W. W. Norton, 2016; paperback retitled *Ratf**ked: Why Your Vote Doesn't Count*, 2017). Daley is a journalist and senior fellow at FairVote. This is the first of two Daley books assigned; the second (2024) is a separate note.

## Thesis & scope
In 2010, Republican operatives coordinated by the **Republican State Leadership Committee (RSLC)** executed a premeditated plan — REDMAP (Redistricting Majority Project) — to target and flip state legislative chambers in key swing states immediately before the decennial redistricting cycle. By controlling legislatures in Pennsylvania, Ohio, Michigan, North Carolina, Florida, and Wisconsin, Republicans drew congressional and state maps so favorable that they could expect durable House majorities regardless of statewide vote swings. The book is the definitive account of how partisan gerrymandering became a precision instrument of structural minority rule.

## Key content relevant to the vault

**REDMAP architecture.** Karl Rove publicly outlined the strategy in a March 2010 Wall Street Journal op-ed, alerting no one because the Democrats were not reading it. **Ed Gillespie** (later Virginia governor, RNC chair) and **Chris Jankowski** (RSLC president) executed it: they identified approximately $30 million in targeted RSLC spending in roughly 100 key state legislative races in swing states. Winning those races — by margins sometimes as small as a few hundred votes — gave Republicans redistricting control worth orders of magnitude more than the investment.

**State-level case studies.** Daley profiles six states in depth:
- *Pennsylvania*: Democrats won 51% of 2012 statehouse votes but received 28% of seats.
- *North Carolina*: Congressional map drawn by Republican-connected law firm Hofeller & Associates (Thomas Hofeller) to maximize Republican seats.
- *Michigan, Ohio, Florida, Wisconsin*: Similar structural lock-ins documented with vote/seat disparity data.

**Thomas Hofeller.** The Republican redistricting specialist — described as the "Michelangelo of the modern gerrymander" — drew or consulted on maps across multiple states. (His files, released after his death in 2018, later confirmed citizenship question motive in the census fight — a connection the vault may want to make explicit.)

**Technology.** REDMAP used new mapping software (Maptitude for Redistricting) combined with micro-targeting voter data to draw lines with precision previously impossible. This is a direct methodological parallel to the CA/OCEAN psychographic targeting the vault documents — both use individual-level data to achieve structural political outcomes.

**Structural consequence.** After 2012, Democrats could win the national House popular vote by millions of votes and still lose the chamber. Daley frames this as the moment American democracy became structurally decoupled from majority will — a precondition for every subsequent development the vault documents.

**Democratic failure of attention.** State legislative races in 2010 were effectively uncontested by national Democrats, who were focused on federal races. The DLCC (Democratic Legislative Campaign Committee) was massively outspent. This asymmetry of attention and investment is a recurring pattern in the patron-class strategy.
