# Source — The Project: How Project 2025 Is Reshaping America (David A. Graham, 2025)

## Bibliographic
David A. Graham; *The Project: How Project 2025 Is Reshaping America*; Crown (Random House); April 2025. ISBN 9798217153725.

Graham is a staff writer at *The Atlantic* covering US politics and national affairs.

## Thesis & scope
*Mandate for Leadership*, the Heritage Foundation's ~900-page policy blueprint published in 2023, was not merely aspirational: within weeks of Trump's January 2025 return to the White House it became the operational template for the administration. Graham's book is a topic-by-topic demystification of what Project 2025 proposes and documents its early-stage implementation — covering Schedule F, the civil service, immigration enforcement, gender policy, deregulation, and the reorganization of executive-branch agencies. The argument is that Project 2025 is the intellectual architecture of the second Trump administration, not a discarded document.

## Key content relevant to the vault

- **[[Heritage Foundation]]** as the institutional author of *Mandate for Leadership* (2023, the Project 2025 volume) and as the multi-decade organizational predecessor: the book contextualizes Heritage's role from its 1973 founding through the Reagan-era *Mandate for Leadership* volumes to the 2023 document.

- **[[Russell Vought]]** — Office of Management and Budget director (Trump 1.0 and 2.0), Center for Renewing America founder — identified as the ideological architect who gave Project 2025 its operational direction. Graham documents Vought's Christian nationalist framing of executive power.

- **Schedule F**: The executive order reviving Schedule F (reclassifying policy-adjacent federal employees as at-will) is treated as the central mechanism for dismantling the professional civil service and replacing it with ideologically vetted loyalists. The book explains the original 2020 EO, its Biden-era revocation, and Trump's 2025 reinstatement.

- **[[Paul Dans]]** — Heritage Foundation, Director of Project 2025's presidential personnel database — named as the operational manager of the personnel screening and placement system.

- **The Presidential Administration Academy**: Graham covers Heritage's training program for prospective political appointees, designed to prepare vetted personnel to slot into agencies from day one.

- **Mass deportation and [[ICE]]**: The immigration enforcement chapters document how Project 2025 proposals — including expanded use of expedited removal, reinstatement of "Remain in Mexico," and interior enforcement escalation — were operationalized rapidly.

- **Gender policy enforcement**: The rollback of Title IX transgender protections, executive orders on gender in federal programs, and the "traditional gender norms" agenda are documented as among the most rapidly implemented Project 2025 planks.

- **Agency restructuring and the unitary executive theory**: Graham explains the constitutional theory underpinning Project 2025 — that all executive power is vested solely in the President, making independent agency structures and career-service protections constitutionally suspect.

- **[[Kevin Roberts]]** (Heritage Foundation president) is documented as the public face of Project 2025 and its most vocal defender when the document drew scrutiny during the 2024 campaign.

- **The 2024 "disavowal" and continuation**: The book documents Trump's public distancing from Project 2025 during the campaign and the subsequent rapid implementation after inauguration — treating the disavowal as a tactical public-relations maneuver.

- **[[Center for Renewing America]]** (Vought's organization) is identified as one of the co-sponsoring organizations alongside Heritage for Project 2025's "coalition" structure.

- **DOGE** is covered as complementary to Project 2025's civil service dismantlement strategy, though Graham notes DOGE was not in the original 2023 document.

- **[[Leonard Leo]]** and the judicial capture apparatus are contextualized as the earlier phase of the same strategy (courts first, then executive branch).
