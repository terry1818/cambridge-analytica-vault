# Source: Targeted (Kaiser 2019)

## Citation

Kaiser, Brittany. (2019). *Targeted: The Cambridge Analytica Whistleblower's Inside Story of How Big Data, Trump, and Facebook Broke Democracy and How It Can Happen Again*. New York: HarperCollins.

## Structure / coverage

Kaiser's account differs from Wylie's by **focusing on the business-development side**: client pitches, contract negotiations, country-specific operations, the international scope of CA's work.

## Key disclosures unique to this account

- Leave.EU work specifics (which Kaiser confirmed despite CA's denials)
- Bongbong Marcos pitch
- Multiple international pitch meetings
- Internal disputes about taking on questionable clients
- Her transition from operator to whistleblower
- HindsightFiles archive provenance

## Complementarity with Wylie

- Wylie's account is **methodology + Bannon + strategic vision**
- Kaiser's account is **client list + international scope + Leave.EU detail**
- Both should be cross-referenced for any specific factual claim

## Significance

Second major insider account; gives a different angle from Wylie's. Combined the two memoirs provide the **richest public window** into CA from inside the firm.
