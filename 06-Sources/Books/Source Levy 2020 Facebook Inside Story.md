# Source: Levy 2020 Facebook Inside Story

## Citation

Levy, Steven. (2020). *Facebook: The Inside Story*. Blue Rider Press. ISBN 9780735213159. 592 pp.

## What this book documents

Steven Levys comprehensive 592-page history of Facebook from 2004 founding through ~2019. Based on hundreds of interviews including extensive access to [[Mark Zuckerberg]], Sheryl Sandberg, and other senior Facebook figures. Covers:

- Founding and early growth
- The decision architecture around platform openness and friends-data access
- The Cambridge Analytica scandal from Facebooks internal perspective
- Internal debates about response to 2016 election interference
- The platforms evolution from social network to ad-targeting platform

## CA-specific coverage

Levys book includes substantial coverage of the Cambridge Analytica scandal from inside Facebook. Documents:

- Internal awareness of the CA harvest before public exposure
- The debates about how to respond
- The role of Joseph Chancellor (GSR co-founder who joined Facebook in 2015)
- Zuckerbergs personal response to the scandal
- Internal politics around content moderation and ad targeting

## Significance

Levy is **the leading journalistic historian of Silicon Valley** — author of earlier definitive books on Google, Apple, Iverson. His access to Facebook leadership makes this the **most authoritative inside-Facebook account** of the CA scandal era. Essential for understanding Facebooks own perspective on what happened.

## Note on access vs critical distance

Levys access depended on Facebooks cooperation — a tension that affects all such books. The volume documents Facebooks internal debates with notable detail but is less critical than independent reporting like Cadwalladrs.
