# Source — Active Measures: The Secret History of Disinformation and Political Warfare (Thomas Rid, 2020)

## Bibliographic
Thomas Rid; *Active Measures: The Secret History of Disinformation and Political Warfare*; Farrar, Straus and Giroux; 2020.

## Thesis & scope
The history of organized political disinformation stretches back over a century, and the most successful operations share a consistent structural feature: they are built on a foundation of real truth with only small amounts of fabrication layered on top — fabrications that cannot be challenged without seeming to confirm the underlying narrative. Rid traces this tradecraft from 1920s Bolshevik operations through Cold War KGB "active measures" to Russian operations targeting the 2016 US election, demonstrating that what appeared novel in 2016 was the latest iteration of documented, institutionalized political warfare doctrine. He draws on materials in more than ten languages and interviews with current and former operatives from multiple intelligence services.

## Key content relevant to the vault
- **"Active measures" (активные мероприятия)**: the Soviet/Russian intelligence term for a category of operations combining disinformation, forgeries, influence agents, and political warfare — Rid recovers the specific institutional machinery (Department A of the KGB's First Chief Directorate).
- **Operation NEPTUNE (1964)**: Czech intelligence operation in which crates of "Nazi documents" were staged at the bottom of a lake to smear West German politicians; used to taint Theodor Oberländer.
- **Robert Lee Johnson documents operation**: KGB leaked documents stolen by US Army courier Robert Lee Johnson (arrested 1964), adding one forged paragraph "authorizing" local commanders to use small nuclear weapons in neutral countries — the classic truth-plus-small-fabrication technique.
- **AIDS / Operation INFEKTION**: the KGB's 1983–1987 campaign planting the claim that AIDS was a US bioweapon developed at Fort Detrick, Maryland. The claim was seeded via an anonymous letter in an Indian newspaper, subsequently amplified through left-wing and anti-imperialist media globally. Demonstrates how fabrications that resonate with existing grievances (history of US medical experiments on Black Americans) achieve organic spread.
- **Anti-Semitic forgery operations / "hate creep"**: Rid documents KGB operations that deliberately seeded anti-Semitic content in Western Europe, which then "crept back" across the Iron Curtain — an early example of blowback from disinformation.
- **CIA's Congress for Cultural Freedom / Encounter magazine**: the US counter-operation; CIA backed a network of intellectual publications and cultural organizations as a counter to Soviet active measures. Included financing of what became Germany's best jazz magazine, run by a former Wehrmacht U-boat commander.
- **"White Russian ploy" / New York police commissioner**: an early 1920s disinformation operation that backfired.
- **Mitrokhin Archive**: Vasili Mitrokhin's defection brought KGB operational files that Rid uses to document multiple operations.
- **Vladislav Surkov** and post-Soviet continuity: Rid bridges Cold War active measures to the post-Soviet period, situating [[Internet Research Agency (IRA)]] operations as institutional descendants.
- **2016 US election / Fancy Bear (GRU) and Cozy Bear (FSB) operations**: Rid's coverage of the IRA and hacking-and-leaking operations (DNC hack, Podesta emails routed through WikiLeaks) presents them as the contemporary expression of a century-old tradecraft.
- **Reflexive control theory** (Vladimir Lefebvre): the Soviet/Russian strategic doctrine of inducing an adversary to make decisions favorable to you by shaping their information environment — presented as the theoretical backbone of active measures.
