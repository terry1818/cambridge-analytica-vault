# Source — Unholy: Why White Evangelicals Worship at the Altar of Donald Trump (Sarah Posner, 2020)

## Bibliographic
- Author: Sarah Posner (investigative journalist; bylines in The Nation, Rolling Stone, The Guardian, The American Prospect; specialist in the religious right for 15+ years)
- Publisher: Random House, 2020 (paperback 2021 updated: *Unholy: How White Christian Nationalists Powered the Trump Presidency, and the Devastating Legacy They Left Behind*)
- ISBN: 978-1984820426 (hc); 978-1984820440 (pb/updated)

## Thesis & scope
Posner's core argument is that the evangelical-Trump alliance was not a transactional anomaly but the logical outcome of a decades-long movement in which racial anxiety and white Christian identity were always central, even when masked by moral-values language. The religious right's infrastructure — built from the 1954 *Brown v. Board* shock through the IRS/Bob Jones controversy, the abortion mobilization, and the Obama-era backlash — created a base that Trump could capture precisely because he spoke the racial and civilizational anxiety more directly than his predecessors. Religion provided the moral cover; race was the engine. Posner traces this from the ground up, with extensive reporting on figures and organizations across the evangelical, Catholic, and alt-right ecosystems.

- Catholic conservatives (Neuhaus, Weigel, Novak) and evangelical conservatives forged an alliance that gave intellectual respectability to cultural-racial anxieties
