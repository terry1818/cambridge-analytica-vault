# Source — An Ugly Truth: Inside Facebook's Battle for Domination (Sheera Frenkel & Cecilia Kang, 2021)

## Bibliographic
Sheera Frenkel and Cecilia Kang; *An Ugly Truth: Inside Facebook's Battle for Domination*; Harper (HarperCollins); July 2021.

## Thesis & scope
The central argument is that Facebook's repeated failures — Cambridge Analytica, Russian election interference, Myanmar genocide, COVID misinformation — were not anomalies or products of negligence but structural inevitabilities produced by the company's founding design: an irreconcilable contradiction between connecting the world and maximizing engagement-driven profit. Frenkel and Kang further argue that Mark Zuckerberg and Sheryl Sandberg actively managed the company to protect growth at the expense of safety, and that internal dissenters who surfaced problems were systematically marginalized. Based on over 1,000 hours of interviews with current and former executives, employees, and associates.

## Key content relevant to the vault

- **Mark Zuckerberg** — portrayed as knowing about Russian IRA activity on Facebook by December 2016 (security team briefing) but initially calling it "crazy" to suggest social media influenced the election; his resistance to regulation and fixation on growth are documented through named internal decision points.
- **Sheryl Sandberg** — documented role in commissioning and overseeing **Definers Public Affairs**, a Republican-operative PR firm Facebook hired in 2017 to run opposition research against critics and deflect Cambridge Analytica coverage. Sandberg reportedly saw Definers materials and was personally more involved than she later acknowledged publicly.
- **Definers Public Affairs** — Washington, DC Republican-aligned PR firm hired by Facebook c. 2017; the book documents Definers publishing articles attacking Soros-linked groups that criticized Facebook, and pitching stories to reporters to divert from the Cambridge Analytica scandal. Sandberg severed the relationship after the NYT exposed it in November 2018.
- **Joel Kaplan** — Facebook VP of Global Public Policy, George W. Bush White House veteran, close friend of Brett Kavanaugh (attended Kavanaugh's Senate confirmation hearing in support while on Facebook's clock); documented in the book as the internal voice consistently arguing for political accommodation of conservatives, downplaying Russian interference findings, and blocking content policy changes that might antagonize Republican officials. His 2015 advice to Zuckerberg on Trump's Muslim-ban post — "don't poke the bear" — led to the creation of a "newsworthiness" exemption for political hate speech that persisted for years.
- **Alex Stamos** — Facebook Chief Security Officer who initiated "Project P" after the 2016 election, an internal probe that traced coordinated inauthentic behavior to the **Internet Research Agency (IRA)**. Stamos briefed Zuckerberg and Sandberg on December 9, 2016, nine months after his team first detected IRA activity. He was gradually pushed out after raising inconvenient findings; left in 2018.
- **Cambridge Analytica scandal internal handling** — the book documents that Facebook's leadership knew the CA data-harvesting had occurred well before the March 2018 public revelations; Sandberg and Zuckerberg's responses are shown to prioritize legal exposure management and reputational damage control over transparent disclosure.
- **Myanmar / Rohingya genocide** — detailed account of how Facebook's failure to invest in local-language content moderation or hate-speech detection in Burmese allowed incitement to ethnic cleansing; more than 24,000 Rohingya killed, hundreds of thousands displaced. The book documents specific internal decisions to delay or defund moderation investment.
- **COVID-19 misinformation** — Facebook's algorithmic amplification of health misinformation and the company's slow, politically-shaped response; Kaplan again is documented as resisting aggressive enforcement against right-wing COVID misinformation to avoid accusations of liberal bias.
- **Nick Clegg** — former UK Liberal Democrat leader and Deputy Prime Minister; hired as VP for Global Affairs and Communications in 2018; deployed by Zuckerberg as a political interface with European regulators and Washington; the book documents his role in managing the company's public image around platform responsibility debates.
- **The "integrity team"** and **Guy Rosen** (VP Integrity) — internal teams charged with election security and content integrity; the book documents their findings being filtered, minimized, or set aside before reaching Zuckerberg.
- **Facebook's algorithmic engagement model** — engineers instructed to maximize time-on-platform; the book documents internal research showing that inflammatory and divisive content outperforms in engagement metrics, and that leadership declined to act on it.
- **FTC $5 billion settlement (2019)** — context: the book covers the regulatory aftermath of Cambridge Analytica, the FTC investigation, and the settlement; frames it as inadequate because it imposed financial penalty without structural remedy.
- **The "Supreme Court" (Oversight Board)** — documented as Zuckerberg's preferred deflection mechanism to avoid directly making controversial content decisions.
