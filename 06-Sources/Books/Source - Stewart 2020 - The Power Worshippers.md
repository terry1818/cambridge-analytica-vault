# Source — The Power Worshippers: Inside the Dangerous Rise of Religious Nationalism (Katherine Stewart, 2020)

## Bibliographic
Katherine Stewart; *The Power Worshippers: Inside the Dangerous Rise of Religious Nationalism*; Bloomsbury Publishing; 2020.

## Thesis & scope
Stewart's central argument is that the "Religious Right" has been systematically mislabeled as a values-based social movement concerned with abortion and gay marriage; it is in fact a disciplined political infrastructure project aiming at seizure of state power and imposition of authoritarian theocratic governance. The book covers the full sweep of the movement's organizational infrastructure — think tanks, legal arms, pastoral mobilization networks, international alliances, and school privatization — with particular attention to funding flows and the anti-democratic historical roots of the movement in opposition to abolitionism, the New Deal, and the civil rights movement.

## Key content relevant to the vault
- Provides systematic anatomy of the **dense network of think tanks, advocacy organizations, and pastoral groups** that constitute the real power of Christian nationalism: [[Alliance Defending Freedom]], [[Family Research Council]], [[Focus on the Family]], Capitol Ministries, **Church United**, [[United in Purpose]], and the **Child Evangelism Fellowship**.
- Documents [[Edgar Prince]] and the [[DeVos Family Network]]'s role in funding the privatization of public education as a multi-decade strategy to redirect state resources to religious schools and advance right-wing ideological reproduction.
- Identifies the **World Congress of Families** as an international organizing vehicle linking US Christian nationalist organizations to anti-democratic religious nationalist movements in Europe and Russia — a connection the vault's international-right cluster needs.
- Traces movement funding to **super-wealthy ultraconservative donors and family foundations** operating through dark money vehicles, with explicit analysis of how this money bypasses disclosure requirements.
- Documents Christian nationalists' explicit rejection of church-state separation and the "biblical founding" mythology as a deliberate political strategy — not sincere history — citing key movement intellectuals.
- Shows how **charter school and voucher programs** function simultaneously as ideological reproduction infrastructure and as a mechanism to defund public civic institutions — links education capture to the broader theocratic project.
- Reports on international alliance-building with Russian Orthodox-aligned organizations, Hungarian Fidesz-linked networks, and other anti-democratic religious movements — giving the "comparative analysis" lens of the vault a theocratic-wing counterpart.
- Documents **[[HSLDA]] (Home School Legal Defense Association)** and [[Michael Farris]] as pivotal infrastructure linking homeschool movement to theocratic political organizing.
- Examines how **pastoral mobilization networks** (pastors briefed on political talking points, organized through groups like [[Capitol Ministries]] and Church United) bypass traditional political disclosure channels.
