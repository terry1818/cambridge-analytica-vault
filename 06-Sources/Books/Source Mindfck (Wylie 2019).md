# Source: Mindf*ck (Wylie 2019)

## Citation

Wylie, Christopher. (2019). *Mindf*ck: Cambridge Analytica and the Plot to Break America*. New York: Random House. 354 pp. ISBN 9781984854636.

## Structure

- **Part I — *The Underwater Light*** — Wylies biography, early political work, joining SCL
- **Part II — *Cambridge Analytica*** — Building the firm, Bannon, Mercer dynamics, the psychographic methodology
- **Part III — *Whistleblower*** — Departure, Cadwalladr handler relationship, going public

## Key disclosures (cited throughout this vault)

- The "I made Steve Bannon's psychological warfare mindfuck tool" framing
- Specific decisions about who to harvest, who to target
- Internal CA culture and methodology decisions
- The Liberal Party of Canada prior work history
- SCL Defence prior work history (UK military research)
- Bannons strategic vision for CA described in detail
- The departure timeline and what triggered Wylies reservations
- The AggregateIQ "SCL Canada" framing
- The Lukoil meetings (Russian state interest in CA data)
- The Israeli intelligence firm connections

## Limitations

- Memoir; first-person, subjective
- Wylie has been disputed on specific operational details by other parties (Nix, Tayler, others)
- Best read against [[Source Targeted (Kaiser 2019)]] for cross-corroboration where they overlap

## Significance

The **primary insider account** of CAs strategic direction and methodology. Essential reading. Cited heavily by journalists, academics, regulators, and the [[Source DCMS Final Report 2019]]. Translated into ~20 languages globally. One of the foundational texts of contemporary literature on data-driven political manipulation.
