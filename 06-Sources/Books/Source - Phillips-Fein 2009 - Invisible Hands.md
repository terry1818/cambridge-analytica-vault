# Source — Invisible Hands: The Businessmen's Crusade Against the New Deal (Kim Phillips-Fein, 2009)

## Bibliographic
Phillips-Fein, Kim. *Invisible Hands: The Businessmen's Crusade Against the New Deal*. W. W. Norton, 2009. 354 pp. ISBN 978-0-393-33766-2.

## Thesis & scope
The conservative movement's rise was not primarily a product of 1960s cultural backlash or Southern realignment but of a decades-long, deliberately organized campaign by a narrow layer of corporate executives and their ideological allies to dismantle New Deal political economy. Beginning in the 1930s and accelerating through the postwar period, this patron class built the think tanks, funded the economists, and trained the cadres that made Reagan's 1980 program possible. Business ideology — not cultural grievance — was the original engine.

## Key content relevant to the vault

**Core organizations and figures (1934–1980)**
- **National Association of Manufacturers (NAM)**: the primary institutional vehicle for anti-New Deal business coordination from the 1930s. NAM funded propaganda, sponsored radio programs, and built employer associations at state level.
- **Foundation for Economic Education (FEE)**, founded 1946 by **Leonard Read**: the first dedicated free-market think tank, designed to cultivate ideological conviction among business elites and recruit European economists. Read is treated as the organizational prototype for every subsequent infrastructure node.
- **Jasper Crane** (DuPont executive) and **W. C. Mullendore** (Southern California Edison): exemplary patron-class figures who provided early financing and social networks connecting NAM, FEE, and the Mont Pelerin Society.
- **[[Mont Pelerin Society]]** (1947, founded by Friedrich Hayek): the transatlantic intellectual clearinghouse that formalized neoliberal doctrine and supplied the movement with academic legitimacy. Phillips-Fein traces the American business community's deliberate recruitment and financing of Hayek and Ludwig von Mises.
- **Lemuel Boulware** (GE vice president): architect of "Boulwarism" — the strategy of bypassing union bargaining through direct ideological communication with workers. Ronald Reagan's years as GE spokesman are treated as the proving ground for supply-side populism.
- **[[Fred Koch]]** and the early Koch network: Fred Koch's anti-communist and anti-New Deal activism is a bridge figure connecting the 1950s business right to the later Koch political infrastructure.
- **Barry Goldwater campaign (1964)**: the first attempt to nationalize the business right's agenda electorally, and the organizational apprenticeship for a generation of conservative operatives.
- **Powell Memo (1971)**: Lewis Powell's confidential memorandum to the U.S. Chamber of Commerce calling for systematic corporate investment in politics, law, academia, and media — the proximate blueprint for the post-1971 infrastructure build.
- **Business Roundtable (1972)**: the large-corporation coordinating body that operationalized the Powell agenda.
- **Heritage Foundation (1973)** and **Cato Institute (1977)**: products of the patron-class infrastructure campaign described throughout the book.

**Mechanisms**
The book's central mechanistic contribution is documenting how ideological infrastructure — think tanks, academic chairs, leadership programs, and media — was financed before electoral politics, creating a supply of cadres and doctrine that politicians could later adopt. This precedes and explains the [[DonorsTrust]] / [[Donors Capital Fund]] model by thirty years.

## How it maps to the vault
The vault covers the post-2000 end-state of this infrastructure ([[Koch Network]], [[Heritage Foundation]], [[DonorsTrust]], [[Federalist Society]], [[Atlas Network]], [[State Policy Network]], [[Americans for Prosperity]]) but has thin coverage of the *founding generation* — NAM, FEE, the Mont Pelerin recruitment campaign, Boulwarism, the Powell Memo, and the Business Roundtable. Phillips-Fein is the primary scholarly source for the 1934–1980 arc that explains *why* these institutions exist. It directly supports the vault's [[Patron Wing MOC]], [[Lens - Funders]], and [[Donors Trust to conservative movement map]] connection note.
