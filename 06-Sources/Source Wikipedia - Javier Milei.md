## What it is

A community-maintained encyclopedia biography of [[Javier Milei]] (Javier Gerardo Milei, born 22 October 1970), an Argentine economist who became the 52nd president of Argentina in December 2023. The article aggregates biography, ideology, electoral rise, and presidency into one narrative. It serves as a navigational secondary source for the comparative-authoritarianism and patron-wing threads.

## Key claims / findings

- Milei is described as a right-wing populist and right-wing libertarian who supports laissez-faire economics and self-identifies with minarchist and anarcho-capitalist principles.
- He rose to prominence in the 2010s as a television pundit and vocal critic of Argentina's political establishment.
- He ran for president as part of La Libertad Avanza, a right-wing coalition, and was inaugurated on 10 December 2023 after defeating economy minister Sergio Massa.
- His program centers on a sweeping fiscal and structural overhaul, deep cuts to state spending, and reduced government intervention; he blames Keynesian policy for Argentina's crises.
- In foreign policy, Argentina shifted away from BRICS and China, aligning more closely with the United States and coordinating with [[Donald Trump]].
- He has been a prominent speaker at international conservative and libertarian gatherings, including CPAC.
