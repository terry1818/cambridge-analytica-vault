## Network context: New Apostolic Reformation and the religious-right pipeline

Lance Wallnau and C. Peter Wagner are foundational figures in the New Apostolic Reformation (NAR) movement, which provided the theological scaffolding for the Seven Mountains Mandate, a doctrine that calls on Christians to take dominion over seven cultural spheres (government, media, business, education, family, religion, and arts/entertainment). The NAR network connects to the rest of the vault through multiple threads.

First, the NAR shares funder and personnel overlap with the [[Council for National Policy]], the [[Faith and Freedom Coalition]], and the [[Family Research Council]]. Wallnau is a frequent speaker at right-wing political events and has explicitly endorsed Donald Trump as a divinely appointed figure, paralleling the way [[Ralph Drollinger]] of Capitol Ministries built theological cover for Trump-era policy inside the executive branch.

Second, the Seven Mountains theology is operationally compatible with both [[Christian Reconstructionism]] (R.J. Rushdoony) and [[Catholic Integralism]] (Patrick Deneen, Adrian Vermeule), even though the three movements come from different theological starting points. All three converge on the same political demand: replacing pluralist democracy with explicitly Christian rule. This convergence is one of the reasons [[Project 2025]] reads as coherent across denominational lines that historically did not cooperate.

Third, the NAR pipeline supplies large-scale, decentralized mobilization capacity (churches, prayer networks, prophetic conferences) that the CA-style microtargeting playbook can layer on top of. Where CA needed to build a persuasion infrastructure from scratch, the NAR provided a pre-existing one already aligned on issue positions.
