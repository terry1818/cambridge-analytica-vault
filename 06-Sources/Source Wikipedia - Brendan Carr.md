## What it is

The English Wikipedia biographical article on **Brendan Thomas Carr** (born January 5, 1979), American lawyer and chair of the Federal Communications Commission since 2025. It summarizes his career, appointments, and his tenure as FCC chairman under the second Trump administration.

## Key claims / findings

- Carr studied government at Georgetown University and earned a law degree from the **Columbus School of Law** (Catholic University) in 2005.
- He joined the FCC in **2012**, became an adviser to Commissioner Ajit Pai in 2014, and was nominated by President [[Donald Trump]] as an FCC **commissioner in 2017**.
- In **November 2024**, president-elect Trump named Carr his FCC chair; he took office after the January 2025 inauguration.
- Carr authored the FCC chapter of the Heritage Foundation's **Project 2025** policy blueprint.
- As chairman he opened **investigations of ABC, CBS, and NBC**, pursued companies over DEI hiring policies, and approved the [[Skydance Media]]–[[Paramount Global]] merger in July 2025 with conditions on programming "viewpoint diversity" and an ombudsman.
- The article documents criticism that Carr used FCC authority to pressure broadcasters' editorial decisions.
