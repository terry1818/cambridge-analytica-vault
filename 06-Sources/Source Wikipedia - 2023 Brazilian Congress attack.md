# Source Wikipedia - 2023 Brazilian Congress attack

## Citation

> Wikipedia contributors. *8 January Brasília attacks*. Wikipedia, The Free Encyclopedia. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Brazilian_Congress_attack (accessed 2026-05-23).

## What it is

A community-maintained encyclopedia article on the [[8 January Brasília attacks]], the storming of Brazil's three federal government buildings by supporters of former president [[Jair Bolsonaro]]. The article aggregates news reporting, official statements, and prosecution outcomes into one narrative. It serves as a navigational secondary source for the comparative election-denial thread.

## Key claims / findings

- On 8 January 2023, following Bolsonaro's defeat in the 2022 election and the inauguration of [[Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva]], a mob of Bolsonaro supporters attacked the presidential palace (Planalto), the Supreme Federal Court, and the National Congress in Brasília.
- The attack came one week after Lula's inauguration and followed weeks of election-denial unrest, including encampments outside army barracks.
- Rioters smashed windows and furniture; the Congress floor flooded when sprinklers activated after an attempted arson.
- Security forces took more than five hours to clear all three buildings, ending around 21:00 local time.
- More than a thousand people were arrested; officials cited roughly 1,500 detentions in the aftermath.
- The event drew swift international condemnation and is widely compared to the 6 January 2021 US Capitol attack.

## Reliability assessment

- Secondary: tertiary aggregator; Tier-3 alone, with underlying citations to NPR, CNN, Al Jazeera, and Brazilian outlets.
- Independent corroboration: events are extensively documented; Brazil's Supreme Court later ruled the riots the climax of a coup attempt and convicted Bolsonaro in 2025.
- Known biases / agenda: crowd-edited; the "coup" characterization, while affirmed by Brazilian courts, remains politically contested by Bolsonaro allies.
- Disputed by: Bolsonaro supporters dispute his responsibility for the attack.
