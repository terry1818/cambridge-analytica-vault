## What it is

A community-maintained encyclopedia article on the [[National Rifle Association]] of America, a US nonprofit founded in 1871. The article aggregates organizational history, lobbying activity, finances, and litigation into one narrative. It serves as a navigational secondary source for the NRA's role in the Russia-cultivation thread and the broader patron/donor right.

## Key claims / findings

- The NRA was founded in 1871 to advance rifle marksmanship; Civil War general Ambrose Burnside was its first president.
- It did not pursue a gun-rights political agenda until the 1930s; in 1934 it created a Legislative Affairs Division and testified for the National Firearms Act.
- The Institute for Legislative Action (NRA-ILA), its lobbying arm, was established in 1975; the Political Victory Fund followed in 1976.
- The 1977 "Revolt at Cincinnati" reoriented the organization from hunting, conservation, and marksmanship toward defense of the right to bear arms.
- The NRA became one of the most influential advocacy groups in US politics and a major spender in congressional elections.
- Later sections cover the New York attorney general's lawsuit and the organization's financial troubles in the 2010s–2020s.
