# Bolton, John. The Room Where It Happened: A White House Memoir (2020)

## Publication

- Simon and Schuster, June 23, 2020
- 592 pages
- DOJ unsuccessfully sued in federal court to block publication (Judge Royce Lamberth ruled Bolton had likely violated NDA but the manuscript was already too widely distributed to enjoin)

## Author standing

- [[John Bolton]] served as Trump's National Security Advisor April 2018 - September 2019
- In the Oval Office and Situation Room for daily briefings during the period covered

## Key documented claims (Tier 1, on-record, Bolton speaking under his name)

**Ukraine quid pro quo (impeachment-relevant):**
- Bolton states Trump directly told him security aid to Ukraine was conditioned on Ukraine announcing investigations into the Bidens
- Trump told Bolton on Aug 20, 2019 he preferred sending no Ukraine aid until Zelensky helped with investigations
- Bolton declined to testify at the first impeachment trial - book was published after the trial

**Other claims:**
- Trump asked Xi Jinping to help him win re-election by purchasing US agricultural goods (Osaka G20, June 2019)
- Trump expressed willingness to halt criminal prosecutions of major foreign donors as personal favors
- Trump did not know UK was a nuclear power; asked if Finland is part of Russia

## Significance for vault

The book is the highest-credibility account from a senior Trump official directly contradicting the Trump defense in the first impeachment. Bolton's reputation as a Republican hawk made the testimony more politically damaging than a Democrat defector's would have been.

## What the source does NOT establish

- Bolton did not testify under oath to the contents - they are unsworn memoir claims
- His refusal to testify at the impeachment trial weakens but does not eliminate evidentiary weight
- The 2024 [[Trump Documents Case]] / Smith special counsel investigation cited some Bolton claims but did not call him
