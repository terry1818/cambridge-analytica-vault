# Wikipedia - Nick Land

The Wikipedia entry on [[Nick Land]] documents his trajectory from University of Warwick philosophy faculty (1987-1998) through the Cybernetic Culture Research Unit (CCRU) at Warwick, through his post-1998 independent work principally in Shanghai, through the 2012 "Dark Enlightenment" essay that became the foundational text for what is variously called neoreactionary, NRx, or Dark Enlightenment intellectual movement.

## Why this source matters

Land's intellectual influence on the contemporary US Tech Right (principally via [[Curtis Yarvin]]) is substantial. His accelerationist intellectual frame substantially influences [[Effective Accelerationism]] (e/acc) as articulated by [[Marc Andreessen]] and adjacent figures. The vault treats Land as one of the intellectual sources for the broader Tech-Right intellectual ecosystem even though he has not operationally engaged with US politics directly.
