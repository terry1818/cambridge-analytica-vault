## What it is

A community-maintained encyclopedia biography of [[Alexander Torshin]] (Aleksandr Porfiryevich Torshin), a Russian politician and central banker. It aggregates Russian and Western reporting, congressional material, and OFAC records into a single biographical narrative. It functions as a navigational secondary source for the Russia/NRA thread.

## Key claims / findings

- Torshin served in the Federation Council of Russia from 2001 to 2015, acting as its chairman for four months in 2011, and later became deputy governor of the Central Bank of Russia.
- Between roughly 2011 and 2018, Torshin and his assistant [[Maria Butina]] built a cooperative relationship between the [[National Rifle Association]] and the Russian group "Right to Bear Arms."
- He attended every NRA annual meeting from 2012 to 2016 and met every NRA president from 2012 onward.
- In January 2018, McClatchy reported that the Mueller investigation examined allegations Torshin had links to Russian organized crime and routed money to the NRA to benefit the Trump campaign.
- In April 2018, OFAC sanctioned Torshin as part of a designation of Russian officials and oligarchs.
- Allegations tied him to the Taganskaya organized-crime gang, which he denied.
