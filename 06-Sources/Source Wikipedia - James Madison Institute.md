## What it is

A community-maintained encyclopedia article on the [[James Madison Institute]] (JMI), a conservative think tank headquartered in Tallahassee, Florida. The article aggregates founding history, mission, network membership, and policy focus into one narrative. It serves as a navigational secondary source for the state-level think-tank-network and constitutional-capture threads.

## Key claims / findings

- JMI is a conservative American think tank headquartered in Tallahassee, Florida, and a member of the [[State Policy Network]].
- It was founded in 1987 by J. Stanley Marshall, a former president of Florida State University.
- It is named after James Madison, the fourth US president and a principal author of the US Constitution.
- Its self-described mission is to advance free-market capitalism, limited government, the rule of law, and economic liberty in Florida.
- It is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit conducting policy research across education, energy and natural resources, governance, health, justice, and economics.
- Its work has appeared across Florida media markets and national outlets including USA Today, The Wall Street Journal, and Politico.
