# Source: 2007 Non-Prosecution Agreement (Epstein)

**Full title**: Non-Prosecution Agreement between the United States Attorney's Office for the Southern District of Florida and Jeffrey Epstein

**Signed**: September 24, 2007 (by [[Alex Acosta]] as US Attorney SDFL)
**Filed under seal**; sealed status maintained through 2019
**Pages**: 7
**Parties**: US (Acosta) + Epstein (via attorneys including [[Alan Dershowitz]], Jay Lefkowitz, Roy Black)

## Key terms

### What Epstein agreed to (Section 2)

Epstein agreed to plead guilty to two state charges in Florida state court:
1. Solicitation of prostitution (Florida Statutes Â§ 796.07)

### What the federal government agreed to (Section 3)

> "...the United States also agrees that it will not institute any criminal charges against any potential co-conspirators of Epstein, including but not limited to [[Sarah Kellen]], [[Adriana Ross]], [[Lesley Groff]], or [[Nadia Marcinkova]]."

**This is the extraordinarily unusual prospective co-conspirator immunity clause.** It granted federal immunity to four named individuals AND to "any" other potential co-conspirators â€” extending into territory not normally covered by US Attorney's office authority.

### Settlement of civil claims (Section 9)

Epstein agreed to settle civil claims of ~40 identified victims as part of the agreement.

### Sealing (Section 10)

> "...the parties agree that this agreement... will remain confidential."

This sealing provision was the basis of the CVRA violation finding.

## Key quoted passages

### Co-conspirator immunity (Section 3, paragraph 5):

> "If Epstein successfully fulfills all of the terms and conditions of this Agreement, the United States also agrees that it will not institute any criminal charges against any potential co-conspirators of Epstein, including but not limited to Sarah Kellen, Adriana Ross, Lesley Groff, or Nadia Marcinkova."

### Victims' rights waiver (excerpted):

The NPA included provisions requiring Epstein to settle civil claims without admission of liability in cases initiated by the victims. The NPA did NOT include a victim-notification provision per CVRA â€” the basis of the later Marra ruling.

## Marra CVRA ruling (February 21, 2019)

US District Judge Kenneth Marra ruled the 2008 NPA violated the Crime Victims Rights Act (18 USC Â§ 3771) because federal prosecutors failed to notify victims of the agreement before signing.

Key Marra finding:

> "Particularly problematic was the Government's decision to conceal the existence of the NPA and mislead the victims to believe that federal prosecution was still a possibility... When the Government gives information to victims, it cannot be misleading. While the Government spent untold hours negotiating the terms and language of the NPA with Epstein's attorneys, scant information was shared with victims."

## Acosta's later statements

In subsequent years, Acosta gave varying explanations for the NPA's terms. Per Daily Beast reporting (July 2019), during 2017 vetting for the Labor Secretary position, Acosta allegedly told Trump transition team that he had been told Epstein "belonged to intelligence" and to leave him alone. Acosta has denied making this statement.
