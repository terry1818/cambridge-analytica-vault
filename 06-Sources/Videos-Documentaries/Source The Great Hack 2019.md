# Source: The Great Hack (2019)

## Citation

Amer, Karim; Noujaim, Jehane (Dirs.). (2019). *The Great Hack*. Netflix / The Othrs. 114 minutes. Premiered Sundance Film Festival 2019-01-26.

## Featured subjects

- [[Brittany Kaiser]] — central narrative figure
- [[Christopher Wylie]]
- [[Carole Cadwalladr]]
- [[David Carroll]] — Parsons professor whose UK Data Protection Act case is the narrative spine

## What it documents

- The unfolding of the Wylie / Kaiser whistleblower disclosures
- Carroll's UK Data Protection Act lawsuit against CA
- Cadwalladr's TED talk and ongoing reporting
- Kaiser's transition from CA insider to public advocate
- Visual presentation of CA's data flows and methodology

## Influence

Major impact on public awareness. Brought CA into mainstream conversation outside policy/journalism circles. Often cited as catalyst for #DeleteFacebook discussions and post-2018 data-rights advocacy.

## Limitations

- Documentary form; advocacy-shaped narrative
- Kaiser-centered POV; some viewers/critics argued for over-redemption of Kaiser's role
- Cross-reference specific claims with primary documents
