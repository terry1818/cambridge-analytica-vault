# Cohen, Michael. Disloyal: A Memoir (2020)

## Publication

- Skyhorse Publishing, September 8, 2020
- 432 pages

## Author standing

- [[Michael Cohen]] was Trump's personal attorney and "fixer" 2007-2018
- Pleaded guilty in 2018 to 8 federal charges including campaign finance violations relating to [[Stormy Daniels]] hush money
- Served as star witness in [[Michael Cohen testimony 2019]] and [[People v Trump 2024]] (New York hush-money trial)

## Reliability framework

This is Tier 2 (memoir by a convicted felon with a known motive). The relevant question for any specific Cohen claim is whether it is corroborated by other evidence:

**Corroborated (Tier 1 once cross-referenced):**
- Hush money payments to [[Stormy Daniels]] and [[Karen McDougal]] - confirmed by financial records, [[David Pecker]] testimony, [[Allen Weisselberg]] documents
- Trump Tower Moscow project negotiations during 2016 campaign - confirmed by Mueller documents
- Trump's tax return and asset-valuation manipulation - confirmed by Engoron case findings

**Uncorroborated / contested:**
- Specific quoted private statements by Trump
- Cohen's recollections of mental state ascribed to Trump

## Key claims relevant to vault

- Cohen describes the Trump Tower Moscow effort continuing well into June 2016 (corroborated by Mueller)
- Cohen describes the hush-money structure as directed by Trump personally (corroborated at 2024 trial)
- Cohen describes Trump's repeated explicit racism in private (uncorroborated specifics; consistent in pattern with multiple other employee accounts)

## Significance

The book is more useful as evidence of HOW the Trump Organization operated procedurally than as documentation of any single statement. The financial and structural claims are where it has held up best under cross-examination.
