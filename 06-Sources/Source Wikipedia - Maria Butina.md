## What it is

A community-maintained encyclopedia biography of [[Maria Butina]] (Maria Valerievna Butina, born 10 November 1988), a Russian political activist, journalist, and former entrepreneur. The article aggregates court filings, congressional findings, and news reporting into a single biographical narrative, serving as a navigational secondary source rather than original evidence.

## Key claims / findings

- Butina was convicted in December 2018 of conspiracy to act as an unregistered foreign agent of Russia within the United States.
- She worked as an assistant to [[Alexander Torshin]], former Federation Council member and deputy governor of the Central Bank of Russia.
- She founded a Russian gun-rights group, "Right to Bear Arms," described by analysts as a front organization for infiltrating US conservative groups and forging cooperation with the [[National Rifle Association]].
- She attempted to infiltrate the US conservative movement to promote Russian interests around the 2016 US presidential election.
- The Senate Intelligence Committee concluded she sought to persuade the Trump campaign to establish a secret communications back channel with Russia.
- She was sentenced to 18 months in April 2019, deported to Russia in October 2019, and elected to the State Duma in 2021.
