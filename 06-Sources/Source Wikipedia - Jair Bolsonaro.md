## What it is

A community-maintained encyclopedia biography of [[Jair Bolsonaro]] (Jair Messias Bolsonaro, born 21 March 1955), the 38th president of Brazil (2019–2023) and a former army captain and longtime congressman. The article aggregates biography, ideology, presidency, and post-presidency legal proceedings into one narrative. It serves as a navigational secondary source for the comparative-authoritarianism thread.

## Key claims / findings

- Bolsonaro served in the Chamber of Deputies from 1991 to 2019 before winning the presidency in 2018.
- His politics are described as far-right and populist; he opposed same-sex marriage, abortion, affirmative action, and state secularism, and made admiring comments about Brazil's 1964–1985 military dictatorship.
- He lost the 2022 runoff to [[Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva]] (~49% to ~51%), becoming the first Brazilian incumbent in over 30 years not re-elected.
- In September 2025, Brazil's Supreme Court convicted him on coup-related charges and sentenced him to 27 years and 3 months in prison for seeking to retain power after his 2022 loss.
- The Supreme Court ruled the 8 January 2023 riots the climax of a coup attempt he masterminded.
