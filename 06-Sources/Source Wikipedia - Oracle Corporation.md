## What it is

The English Wikipedia article on **Oracle Corporation**, an American multinational computer-technology company. It summarizes Oracle's founding, its database and enterprise-software business, its pivot to cloud computing, and its leadership under co-founder [[Larry Ellison]].

## Key claims / findings

- Oracle was founded in **1977** by [[Larry Ellison]], Bob Miner, and Ed Oates as Software Development Laboratories; it later took the name Oracle.
- Its first commercial product, **Oracle V2 (1979)**, was a relational database management system built around SQL — making Oracle a pioneer of commercial RDBMS software.
- By the mid-1980s Oracle was a dominant database vendor, competing with IBM.
- In the 2010s–2020s Oracle pivoted aggressively into **cloud computing** (Oracle Cloud Infrastructure, OCI), positioning itself as a provider of AI-data-center capacity.
- Oracle is a partner in the [[Stargate Project]] AI joint venture (announced January 2025) and in the Oracle-led consortium taking over [[TikTok]]'s US operations.
- Ellison is the company's largest shareholder, chairman, and chief technology officer; he is among the wealthiest people in the world.
