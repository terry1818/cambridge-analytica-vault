## What it is

The English Wikipedia article on **Meta Platforms, Inc.**, the American multinational technology conglomerate known before October 2021 as Facebook, Inc. It summarizes the company's founding, products, leadership, and major controversies.

## Key claims / findings

- Facebook was founded in **2004** by [[Mark Zuckerberg]] with Harvard classmates Eduardo Saverin, Andrew McCollum, Dustin Moskovitz, and Chris Hughes.
- The company went public on **February 1, 2012** and is headquartered in Menlo Park, California.
- Meta owns and operates **Facebook, Instagram, Threads, Messenger, and WhatsApp**.
- In **October 2021** the parent company rebranded from Facebook, Inc. to **Meta Platforms**, signaling a focus on the "metaverse."
- Zuckerberg serves as founder, chairman, and CEO and retains voting control via a dual-class share structure.
- The article documents the company's central role in the **[[Cambridge Analytica]]** data-harvesting scandal and the resulting $5 billion FTC fine, as well as Meta's later realignment toward the second Trump administration (ending fact-checking, settling Trump litigation).
