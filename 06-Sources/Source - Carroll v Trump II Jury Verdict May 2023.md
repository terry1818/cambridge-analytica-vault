# Source: Carroll v Trump II Jury Verdict + Kaplan Opinion

## The jury verdict (May 9, 2023)

### Liability findings

The jury found Trump liable on both claims. On battery, the jury specifically found:
- **Did Trump rape Carroll** (NY Penal Law definition requiring penile penetration)? **NO**
- **Did Trump sexually abuse Carroll** (battery, including digital penetration without consent)? **YES**
- Did Trump injure Carroll? **YES**

On defamation: jury found Trump's October 2022 statements were defamatory.

### Damages

- Battery: $2,020,000 compensatory + $20,000 punitive = $2,040,000
- Defamation: $2,700,000 compensatory + $280,000 punitive = $2,980,000
- **Total: $5,000,000**

## Kaplan's Rape Clarification Opinion (July 19, 2023)

Following the verdict, Trump and his supporters publicly claimed the jury had "exonerated" him of rape because the specific NY Penal Law "rape" definition was not met. Judge Kaplan issued a separate Memorandum Opinion July 19, 2023 directly addressing this.

Key Kaplan finding:

> "The jury... in finding that Trump committed sexual abuse, in finding that he forcibly penetrated her finger or fingers into her vagina without her consent and against her will, found that Trump 'raped' her in the broader, common modern parlance of that word."

Kaplan's reasoning:

> "The point is that the definition of rape in the New York Penal Law was substantially narrower than the meaning of 'rape' in the common modern parlance, in the dictionary, in the federal criminal code, and elsewhere. The finding that Ms. Carroll failed to prove that Mr. Trump 'raped' her within the narrow, technical meaning of the New York Penal Law... is not, as Mr. Trump asserts, a 'verdict' that he 'did not rape' her in any sense of the word."

> "In short, Mr. Trump's argument that the jury's verdict establishes that he did not 'rape' Ms. Carroll is unpersuasive. The jury found that Mr. Trump in fact did rape Ms. Carroll, as the term commonly is used."

## Rule 415 evidence at trial

Federal Rule of Evidence 415 (sexual assault evidence in civil cases) admits prior similar-conduct evidence in civil sexual-assault cases. The trial admitted:
- **[[Jessica Leeds]]** testimony: 1979 first-class flight groping
- **[[Natasha Stoynoff]]** testimony: 2005 Mar-a-Lago forcible kiss
- **[[Access Hollywood tape]]**: "grab them by the p---y" recording

## Trump's pre-trial deposition

Trump did not testify at trial in person. His videotaped pre-trial deposition was played including:
- Trump shown photograph of him with Carroll; misidentified Carroll as his second wife Marla Maples
- Trump's Access Hollywood "stars can do anything" statement, when asked about it, characterized as celebrities being able to engage in conduct "unfortunately or fortunately, for the last million years, that's been largely true"

## Second Circuit affirmance (December 30, 2024)

US Court of Appeals for the Second Circuit affirmed the verdict in December 2024. The unanimous opinion rejected Trump's arguments and upheld:
- The Rule 415 admission of Leeds, Stoynoff, and Access Hollywood evidence
- Judge Kaplan's evidentiary and procedural rulings
- The verdict itself
