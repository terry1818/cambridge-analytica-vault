## What it is

The English Wikipedia article on **Skydance Media** (originally Skydance Productions), an American media-production company founded by [[David Ellison]]. It summarizes the company's founding, financing history, film and television output, and its 2025 absorption of [[Paramount Global]].

## Key claims / findings

- Skydance Productions was **formed in 2006** by David Ellison, son of [[Larry Ellison]]; the name draws on aerobatic "skydancing."
- Its first film was *Flyboys* (2006), co-financed with Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer.
- In fall **2009**, Skydance signed a five-year co-financing/production/distribution deal with **Paramount Pictures**; in August 2010, with an equity investment by Larry Ellison, it raised **$350 million** in equity and credit.
- Skydance co-financed the *Mission: Impossible*, *Terminator*, and *Star Trek* franchises, *True Grit*, and *Top Gun: Maverick*.
- The company expanded into Skydance Animation, Skydance Television, Skydance Interactive (games), and Skydance Sports.
- In **2025**, Skydance merged with National Amusements and Paramount Global to form [[Paramount Skydance]] (completed August 7, 2025).
