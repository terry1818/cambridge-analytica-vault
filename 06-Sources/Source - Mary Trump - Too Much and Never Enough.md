# Trump, Mary L. Too Much and Never Enough: How My Family Created the World's Most Dangerous Man (2020)

## Publication

- Simon and Schuster, July 14, 2020
- 240 pages
- Donald Trump unsuccessfully sued (via his brother Robert) to block publication on alleged NDA grounds

## Author standing

- [[Mary L Trump]] is Donald Trump's niece (daughter of Fred Trump Jr.)
- Clinical psychologist
- Inherited family financial documents through her grandfather Fred Trump Sr.'s estate
- Provided New York Times reporters with the documents that became the 2018 NYT tax investigation series (won 2019 Pulitzer)

## Key documented claims (Tier 1 where document-backed)

**Trump family wealth origin:**
- The 2018 NYT investigation built on Mary Trump's documents established Donald Trump received the equivalent of over  (2018 dollars) from Fred Trump Sr.'s estate through complex tax-fraud structures
- The structures included sham vendor relationships (All County Building Supply and Maintenance) and undervaluation schemes
- New York State Department of Taxation and Finance has commented on the report but has not (as of latest filings) brought additional charges - the relevant statute-of-limitations periods had expired

**Family psychological dynamics:**
- Mary Trump's professional framing of Donald's personality is Tier 2 - psychological profiling at distance is methodologically contested

## Significance

The book is the source-of-record for understanding the family financial structure that produced Donald Trump's early-career capital base. The factual financial claims have substantially held up in independent New York Times reporting; the psychological framing is more an interpretive layer.
