# Source White House Stargate Announcement 2025-01-21

## Citation

> OpenAI. (2025-01-21). *Announcing The Stargate Project*. OpenAI. https://openai.com/index/announcing-the-stargate-project/ — corroborated by CNBC, "Trump announces AI infrastructure investment backed by Oracle, OpenAI and Softbank" (2025-01-21) and CNN Business.

## What it is

A corporate press release plus a televised White House announcement. On January 21, 2025 — the day after his second inauguration — [[Donald Trump]] appeared in the Roosevelt Room with [[Sam Altman]] ([[OpenAI]]), [[Masayoshi Son]] ([[SoftBank]]), and [[Larry Ellison]] ([[Oracle Corporation]]) to unveil the [[Stargate Project]], a new private-sector joint venture to build AI data-center infrastructure. The OpenAI release is the primary corporate text; broadcast and wire coverage (CNBC, CNN, CBS News) documents the White House staging.

## Key claims / findings

- Stargate intends to invest **$500 billion over four years** building AI infrastructure for OpenAI in the United States, deploying **$100 billion immediately**.
- Initial equity funders: **SoftBank, OpenAI, Oracle, and MGX** (an Abu Dhabi state-backed fund). SoftBank holds financial responsibility; OpenAI holds operational responsibility; Son is chairman.
- First data centers were already under construction in **Abilene, Texas**.
- The release frames the project as securing "American leadership in AI" and a "strategic capability to protect the national security of America and its allies."

## Notable quotes

## Reliability assessment

- Primary (corporate announcement) plus secondary corroboration from multiple wire/broadcast outlets — Tier-1 for the fact of the announcement; Tier-3 for the dollar figures, which are pledges, not committed capital.
- Independent corroboration: CNBC, CNN, CBS News all reported the same event.
- Known biases / agenda: The release is promotional. The $500B figure was widely questioned; Elon Musk publicly claimed the money was not secured.
- Disputed by: Skeptical reporting on funding sufficiency; the figure is a projection.
