## Key findings (eight central conclusions per Executive Summary)

1. Trump purposely disseminated false allegations of fraud knowing they were untrue
2. Trump's fake-elector scheme was the central illegal plan
3. Trump corruptly pressured Pence to refuse certification or delay
4. Trump corruptly pressured DOJ
5. Trump corruptly pressured state officials and state legislators
6. Trump summoned a violent mob and directed them to march to the Capitol
7. Trump knew the crowd was armed and angry but directed them to the Capitol anyway
8. Trump failed to act for 187 minutes despite repeated pleas

## Cassidy Hutchinson testimony (Tier 1)

The Committee's June 28, 2022 surprise hearing featured [[Cassidy Hutchinson]]'s testimony, which became one of the most-cited witness accounts. Key Hutchinson testimony:

> "I was in the dining room a foot or two from where the President is throwing his lunch... I just remember hearing noise coming from the dining room, like an alarm or, like, something had fallen... I went into the dining room and noticed the President was extremely angry at the Attorney General's [Barr's] AP interview, and had thrown his lunch against the wall, which was causing the ketchup to drip down the wall and there was a shattered porcelain plate on the floor."

On Trump knowing the crowd was armed (Hutchinson re-creating Trump's words):

> "I don't fucking care that they have weapons. They're not here to hurt me. Take the fucking mags away. Let my people in. They can march to the Capitol from here. Let the people in. Take the fucking mags away."

On Pat Cipollone's "we are going to get charged with every crime imaginable":

> "And Mr. Cipollone said something to the effect of, 'Please make sure we don't go up to the Capitol, Cassidy. Keep in touch with me. We're going to get charged with every crime imaginable if we make that movement happen.'"
