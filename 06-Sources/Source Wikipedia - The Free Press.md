## What it is

The English Wikipedia article on **The Free Press**, an American media company founded by opinion writer [[Bari Weiss]] and her spouse Nellie Bowles. It summarizes the outlet's origins, editorial orientation, and 2025 acquisition by [[Paramount Skydance]].

## Key claims / findings

- Weiss and Bowles launched the publication as a **Substack newsletter named "Common Sense" in January 2021**, after Weiss publicly resigned from The New York Times in 2020.
- It grew into an associated media company in **2022**, rebranded as **The Free Press**, based in New York City.
- The outlet's editorial stance is broadly described by news media as **conservative, "anti-woke," and pro-Israel**.
- In **2025**, Paramount Skydance acquired The Free Press for **$150 million**; Weiss was named editor-in-chief of [[CBS News]].
- Since October 2025 it operates as an **independent entity within CBS News**.
