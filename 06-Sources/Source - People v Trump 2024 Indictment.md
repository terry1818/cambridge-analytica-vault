# Source: People v Trump 2024 Indictment + Verdict

**Court**: New York Supreme Court, New York County (Criminal Term), Indictment No. 71543-2023
**Judge**: [[Juan Merchan]]
**Indictment unsealed**: April 4, 2023 (returned March 30, 2023)
**Trial**: April 15 - May 30, 2024
**Verdict**: May 30, 2024 -- **guilty on all 34 counts**
**Sentencing**: January 10, 2025 -- unconditional discharge

## The 34 counts

All 34 counts charged: **falsifying business records in the first degree** (NY Penal Law § 175.10).

Each count corresponded to a specific business record:
- 9 counts: invoices (Cohen's invoices to Trump Org for "legal services")
- 11 counts: checks (the actual reimbursement checks signed by Trump)
- 12 counts: ledger entries (Trump Organization general ledger)
- 2 counts: additional vouchers

First-degree (felony) enhancement required proof of intent to commit or conceal **another crime** -- argued as violation of NY Election Law § 17-152 (conspiracy to promote a candidacy by unlawful means) including federal campaign finance violations and state tax violations.

## Statement of Facts -- key passages

The Statement of Facts filed with the indictment laid out the scheme:

> "The defendant DONALD J. TRUMP repeatedly and fraudulently falsified New York business records to conceal criminal conduct that hid damaging information from the voting public during the 2016 presidential election."

> "From August 2015 to December 2017, the Defendant orchestrated a 'catch and kill' scheme through a series of payments that he then concealed through months of false business entries."

Specific scheme elements:
1. August 2015 meeting between Trump, Cohen, and Pecker forming the catch-and-kill agreement
2. October 2016 Daniels payment by Cohen
3. February-December 2017 reimbursement structure categorized as "legal services"

## The jury verdict (May 30, 2024)

Jury (12 jurors) returned guilty on all 34 counts after approximately 9.5 hours of deliberation across 2 days (May 29-30, 2024).

Trump became the **first former US President criminally convicted in American history**.

Trump's response statement, immediately after verdict:

> "This was a disgrace. This was a rigged trial by a conflicted judge who was corrupt. This is a rigged disgraceful trial. The real verdict is going to be November 5 by the people."

## Sentencing (January 10, 2025)

Judge Merchan imposed **unconditional discharge** -- no incarceration, no probation, no fine. Sentence rationale included consideration of Trump's election to a second presidential term and constitutional concerns about restricting a President-elect.

Important: unconditional discharge **preserves the conviction itself**. Trump remains a convicted felon as a matter of law.

## Appeals status

Trump filed notice of appeal. Multiple appellate filings have proceeded; status as of 2026-05: pending in NY Appellate Division First Department, with constitutional challenges to the prosecution proceeding through state appellate courts.

## What the verdict established (Tier 1)

- Trump personally and knowingly directed the categorization of reimbursement payments as "legal services" in Trump Organization records
- The categorization was false
- The falsification was committed with intent to conceal another crime (campaign finance violation)
- The catch-and-kill scheme operated to suppress information from voters before the 2016 election

## What the verdict did NOT address

- The underlying 2006 alleged Trump-Daniels encounter (not an element of charged offenses)
- Other Trump campaign conduct beyond the records covered by the indictment
- Trump's intent regarding the 2016 election outcome (only intent to conceal another crime was required)
