# Source NYT October 7 Anniversary US Politics

## Citation

> The New York Times. (2024-10-07). *Coverage of the first anniversary of the October 7 attack and its place in the 2024 presidential campaign*. The New York Times. https://www.nytimes.com/section/politics

## What it is

New York Times reporting published on and around 7 October 2024, the first anniversary of the Hamas-led attack on Israel. The coverage situated the anniversary within the closing month of the 2024 US presidential campaign between [[Kamala Harris]] and [[Donald Trump]], examining how both candidates marked the date and how the Israel–Gaza war shaped the electorate. (Companion live coverage appeared at NBC News, CNN, and The Hill on the same day.)

## Key claims / findings

- Both Vice President Harris and former President Trump publicly commemorated the anniversary, with the war a salient issue less than a month before Election Day.
- Harris and second gentleman Doug Emhoff planted a memorial pomegranate tree at the vice president's residence; Harris pledged to ensure Hamas could not repeat the attack.
- Trump visited the Ohel Chabad-Lubavitch in Queens and held a remembrance event at his Doral, Florida golf club.
- Coverage highlighted the war's political cost to Democrats, including discontent among Arab American and progressive voters in swing states such as Michigan.
- The reporting tied foreign-policy anxiety to a close, polarized race.

## Notable quotes
