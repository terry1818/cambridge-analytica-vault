# Connections MOC

A **connection note** documents a specific relationship between two or more entities — a money flow, a personnel migration, a data flow, an influence channel — that deserves its own analytical surface rather than being captured only as wikilinks on the related entity notes.

For routing rules on when a relationship deserves its own connection note vs. when wikilinks on the related notes suffice, see [[Categorization Architecture]].

## How connections work in the graph

A connection note functions as a **named edge in the entity-relationship graph**. When the relationship is the analytical subject — not just a fact about an entity — it gets its own surface:

- A connection note can be wikilinked from each entity's note (entity → connection → entity)
- A connection note is itself a citable analytical object
- A connection note can have its own confidence label and falsification criteria when the relationship is contested

When the relationship is **not contested or analytically central**, capture it as wikilinks on the entity notes instead — don't manufacture a connection note for every documented relationship in the vault.
