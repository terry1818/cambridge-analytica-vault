## Methodology for tracking decentralized conspiracy infrastructure

The conspiracy cluster the vault tracks is operationally distinctive because of its decentralized structure. Unlike the religious-right and Tech-Right clusters which have substantial institutional infrastructure that can be inventoried as discrete entities, the conspiracy cluster operates principally through:

- Decentralized online communities (Telegram channels, X/Twitter accounts, alternative-social-media platforms, encrypted-messaging networks)
- Movement-coordination through irregular events (conferences, conventions, rallies) rather than continuous institutional infrastructure
- Cross-pollination between conspiracy-aligned operational figures (RFK Jr, Tim Ballard, Bigtree, Mercola) without formal coordinating organisational structure
- Continuing influence on Trump-administration personnel and policy direction without the formal advisory or contracting relationships that more institutionally-structured clusters maintain

## Why this matters for vault analysis

The decentralized structure makes the cluster harder to track and harder to characterize operationally. The vault treats individual operational figures and specific high-visibility vehicles (Sound of Freedom, MAHA report, World Liberty Financial) as the principal trackable units, with the broader decentralized infrastructure documented through movement-pattern analysis rather than discrete-entity catalogue.
