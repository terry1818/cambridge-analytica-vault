# Regulatory Response MOC

The post-CA regulatory landscape -- the legal and political response that emerged from 2018 onward. Tracks how data privacy, election integrity, AI safety, and information operations have been addressed (or not addressed) by states.

## Data privacy

- [[GDPR]] (EU, 2018) -- the foundational regulation
- **CCPA** (California Consumer Privacy Act, 2020)
- **LGPD** (Brazil, 2020)
- **DPDP Act** (India, 2023)
- **PIPL** (China, 2021)
- Multiple US state laws: Virginia VCDPA, Colorado CPA, Connecticut DPA

## EU platform regulation

- **Digital Services Act (DSA)** (2022/2065) -- adopted October 2022; in force February 2024
- **Digital Markets Act (DMA)** (2022/1925) -- adopted September 2022
- **AI Act** (2024/1689) -- adopted July 2024; phased enforcement

## US platform regulation

- Section 230 reform debates (no major legislation as of 2026)
- AI executive orders (Biden, then Trump)
- State-level AI laws (California SB 1047 vetoed; multiple smaller laws)
- FCC AI robocall ban (2024-02)

## Election integrity

- [[FARA]] expanded enforcement
- 2024 state-level AI-in-elections laws
- Multiple state laws on deepfake-in-campaign-ads

## What hasn't happened

- **No US federal data-privacy law** as of 2026
- **No US federal AI-in-elections statute** (despite high-profile incidents)
- **No effective regulation of private-intelligence-for-hire** in any jurisdiction
- **No consistent international framework** for cognitive warfare doctrine
