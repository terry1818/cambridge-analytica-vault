# Compare: Platform Responses to CA-Era Operations

How the major social platforms responded to the 2016-2018 revelations and what they're doing now (2024-2026).

## Facebook / Meta

- **Pre-scandal (2015):** received complaints about CA harvest; minimal action
- **2018-2019:** banned CA, then $5B FTC fine, £500K ICO fine, $100M SEC settlement
- **2018+:** Applied GDPR provisions globally; deprecated friends-data permanently; introduced ad transparency tools
- **2022:** $725M class-action settlement
- **2022-present:** Quarterly Adversarial Threat Reports identifying CIB takedowns
- **Current state:** most active platform-level threat reporting; takedowns include [[Doppelganger]], [[Spamouflage Dragonbridge]] networks
- **Limitations:** still hosts substantial influence operations; AI-content detection lagging

## X / Twitter

- **Pre-2022 (Twitter):** quarterly transparency reports; account takedowns; ad transparency tool
- **2022 acquisition by Musk:** mass layoffs at trust-and-safety teams; dissolution of policy enforcement infrastructure
- **2023-present (X):** largely dropped CIB transparency reporting; restored many previously-banned accounts; criticized for hosting unaddressed influence operations
- **Notable:** EU has opened formal DSA proceedings against X over disinformation; ongoing as of 2026

## YouTube / Google

- **2017-present:** policy enforcement against state-actor influence operations
- **Notable:** delays vs. Facebook in enforcement; deepfake-related policy 2024+
- **Current state:** active CIB enforcement; transparency limited

## TikTok

- **2018-present:** emerging influence-operations target
- **2022-present:** US national security concerns lead to state and federal restrictions; ByteDance ownership central issue
- **Marcos victory (Philippines 2022)** documented to involve significant TikTok influence operations
- **AI-content:** policy on AI-generated content evolving; enforcement uneven

## Reddit

- **2016:** subject of IRA influence operations (suspended accounts)
- **2018-2020:** active CIB enforcement; transparency reports
- **2023+:** API changes and platform changes have reshaped influence-ops landscape

## Telegram

- **Limited platform-level action against influence ops**
- **CEO Pavel Durov arrested 2024-08 in France** -- increased pressure on platform compliance
- **Critical infrastructure for many state operations (Russian, others)**

## What's the same

- All major platforms now have **some level of CIB policy enforcement**
- All publish **some level of transparency reporting** (highly variable)
- All face **regulatory pressure** (EU DSA most aggressive)
- All struggle with **AI-generated content detection**

## What's different

- **Meta** has the most mature threat-reporting infrastructure
- **X** has actively dismantled its policy enforcement
- **TikTok** is geopolitically singular (Chinese ownership)
- **Telegram** has historically been a haven; pressure now increasing
- **Encrypted messaging platforms** (WhatsApp, Signal) face different challenges
