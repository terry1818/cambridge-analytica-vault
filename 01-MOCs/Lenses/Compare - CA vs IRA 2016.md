# Compare: Cambridge Analytica vs. Internet Research Agency (2016)

Two influence operations targeting the **same election** with **fundamentally different methodologies**. Comparing them clarifies what each operation could and couldn't do.

## Side-by-side

| Dimension | **Cambridge Analytica** | **Internet Research Agency** |
|---|---|---|
| **Country of operation** | UK (with US arm) | Russia (St. Petersburg) |
| **Funding** | Mercer family ($15M+) + Trump campaign + MA1 PAC | Russian state via Yevgeny Prigozhin |
| **Personnel** | ~100 staff | ~80+ "Translator" department US-focused |
| **Approach** | **Microtargeting** real users with tailored content | **Persona networks** -- fake accounts impersonating Americans |
| **Audience access** | 87M Facebook profiles harvested + targeted political ads | 126M Facebook users reached via fake-persona content |
| **Content creation** | Mostly human-produced; targeted via psychographic clusters | Mostly human-produced by IRA staff impersonating Americans |
| **Ad spending** | Tens of millions (CA + Trump campaign + MA1 PAC combined) | $100K+ on Facebook ads (publicly known); plus organic reach |
| **Real-world impact** | Trump-favorable advertising; disputed effectiveness | Organized real-world rallies via fake personas; documented to attendance |
| **Discovery** | 2018 Wylie/Kaiser whistleblowers + journalism | 2017-2018 Mueller indictment + Facebook disclosure |
| **Consequences** | CA dissolved 2018; Nix banned 2020 | IRA still operating 2026 (Prigozhin killed 2023) |

## What's the same

- Both targeted the 2016 US election
- Both aligned with Trump-favorable outcomes (per documentation)
- Both relied on Facebook's data and ad infrastructure
- Both attracted post-election regulatory and journalistic attention
- Neither has been definitively shown to have changed the outcome

## What's different (the key contrast)

**CA targeted real users with tailored content; IRA created fake personas pretending to be real users.** These are **opposite methodological approaches** to influence operations:

| | CA | IRA |
|---|---|---|
| Speaker (who appears to be talking) | Real political campaigns | Fake "American activists" |
| Audience | Real users targeted by ML model | Real users reached by fake-account organic content |
| Detection method | Audit CA's targeting and data | Audit fake accounts and coordinate behavior |
| Defense | Privacy law (data harvest restrictions) | Platform-policy enforcement (account takedowns) |

## What modern operations have done

Post-2016 operations ([[Doppelganger]], [[Spamouflage Dragonbridge]], [[Team Jorge]]) have **fused the two approaches**: AI-managed persona networks (IRA-style) that also do automated audience targeting (CA-style). Modern threat assessment requires understanding both.

## Coordination question

Mueller and Senate Intel Vol 5 found:
- **No documented direct coordination** between CA and IRA
- They were running in parallel, both Trump-aligned in net effect
- Some Russian-IP infrastructure overlap (Kogan / Cambridge Psychometrics Centre)
- The two operations existed in the same information environment without clear handoffs
