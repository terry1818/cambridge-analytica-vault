# Compare: Cambridge Analytica vs. HBGary Federal

[[HBGary Federal]] was the **conceptual ancestor of Cambridge Analytica** â€” the same toolkit (persona management, behavioral profiling, targeted influence operations) marketed to the same kinds of clients (corporate, military, intelligence-adjacent) **3-5 years earlier**.

## Side-by-side

| Dimension | HBGary Federal (2009-2012) | Cambridge Analytica (2013-2018) |
|---|---|---|
| **Country** | United States | United Kingdom (with US arm) |
| **Founder/CEO** | Aaron Barr (HBGary Federal); Greg Hoglund (HBGary parent) | [[Alexander Nix]] (CA); [[Nigel Oakes]] (SCL parent) |
| **Parent firm** | HBGary, Inc. | [[SCL Group]] |
| **Government clients** | US Centcom ("Operation Earnest Voice"), defense contractors | US DoD, UK MoD (via SCL Defence) |
| **Commercial pitches** | Bank of America, US Chamber of Commerce | Mercer family, Ted Cruz campaign, Trump campaign, dozens of countries' governments |
| **Methodology** | Persona management software (manage many fake online identities) | Psychographic targeting (profile and target real users) |
| **Data source** | Open-source intelligence on individuals | Facebook Graph API harvest (87M profiles) |
| **Exposed by** | Anonymous hack (February 2011) | Whistleblower (March 2018) |
| **Outcome** | Aaron Barr resigned; HBGary Federal effectively closed | CA dissolved; Nix banned 7 years; FTC fines |
| **Successor entities** | Various; most personnel returned to other defense contractors | [[Emerdata Limited]], [[Auspex International]], [[Data Propria]], [[Emic]] |

## What HBGary Federal pitched (revealed in 2011 leaks)

- **Bank of America** â€” campaign to discredit [[WikiLeaks]] supporters, use of social-media personas to inject disinformation
- **US Chamber of Commerce** â€” campaign against labor unions and progressive groups

## What CA pitched (revealed 2015-2018)

- Ted Cruz, Trump, Carson campaigns (US)
- Leave.EU (UK)
- Dozens of governments globally
- Mercer-funded Super PACs

## What's different

- **Scale** â€” CA orders of magnitude larger
- **Data architecture** â€” HBGary Federal worked from open-source intel; CA built proprietary psychographic models
- **Public impact** â€” HBGary Federal scandal was tech-press; CA scandal was global political
- **Successor continuity** â€” HBGary Federal's afterlife is harder to trace; CA's is well-documented

## What's the same

- **Same toolkit philosophy** â€” model audiences, deliver targeted influence content
- **Same client mix** â€” government, corporate, political
- **Same plausible-deniability structure** â€” multiple entities, government work alongside commercial
- **Same exposure mechanism** â€” leak (HBGary) and whistleblower (CA)
- **Lessons not learned** â€” the 2011 HBGary scandal did not prevent CA

## Implication

If the HBGary Federal scandal of 2011 had been better understood and acted upon, **CA's 2018 scandal might have been preventable**. The fact that the same toolkit re-emerged at larger scale 3-5 years later, with similar exposure, suggests the structural conditions enabling these firms (defense-contractor lineage, lax regulatory oversight, demand from political/corporate clients) have not been addressed.
