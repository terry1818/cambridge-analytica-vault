# Compare: Brexit vs. 2016 US Presidential Election

Both 2016 votes were major populist-right victories, both featured CA / SCL ecosystem involvement, both became foundational case studies for influence-operations concerns. But the mechanics differ in important ways.

## What's the same

- Same firm ecosystem (CA/SCL/AIQ)
- Same Mercer political infrastructure influence
- Same Cadwalladr investigative journalism arc
- Same broad temporal window
- Both featured "shock" populist-right victories

## What's different

- US case has more **direct documentation** of CA involvement (paid contracts, FEC records)
- US case has **confirmed Russian state role**; Brexit has disputed/peripheral Russia connection
- US case had **stronger institutional response** ($5B FTC, 947-page Senate Intel Vol 5, Mueller probe)
- Brexit's regulatory response was more **diffuse and contested** (ICO's "no significant breach" remains disputed)
- US 2016 had **more campaign-finance flexibility** than UK so coordination questions are less central
- UK had **clearer spending-cap violations** (Vote Leave / BeLeave / AIQ funnel)

## What this reveals

The comparison illuminates how **legal frameworks shape both the misconduct and the response**. UK's stricter spending caps created the BeLeave/AIQ workaround that became prosecutable. US's looser campaign finance left coordination questions less clear-cut but enabled broader Russia investigations.
