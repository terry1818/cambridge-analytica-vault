# Compare: Religious Right vs. Tech Right

The contemporary US right-wing coalition is dominated by **two distinct intellectual / movement currents** with substantial overlap and significant tensions: the **Religious Right** (Christian conservative, with substantial Catholic integralist and evangelical components) and the **Tech Right** (Silicon Valley conservative, with Thiel network at center).

## The two movements

### Religious Right
- **Decades-old**: emerged in 1970s-1980s
- **Donor networks**: DeVos family, traditional evangelical donor networks, Catholic conservative networks
- **Institutional infrastructure**: [[Heritage Foundation]] (partial), [[Federalist Society]] (legal), churches/denominations, Christian universities
- **Intellectual currents**: Catholic integralism ([[Patrick Deneen]], [[Adrian Vermeule]]), evangelical political theology
- **Policy priorities**: abortion restriction, school choice, religious-school funding, anti-LGBTQ+ legislation
- **Key figures**: Mike Pence (less so in Trump 2.0), Mike Johnson (House Speaker), Betsy DeVos
- **Council for National Policy alignment**

### Tech Right
- **Recent emergence**: ~2016-2024 consolidation
- **Donor networks**: [[Peter Thiel]] network, [[Elon Musk]], [[Marc Andreessen]] / a16z, [[David Sacks]]
- **Institutional infrastructure**: [[Founders Fund]], [[Anduril]], [[Palantir]], DOGE
- **Intellectual currents**: [[Curtis Yarvin]] neoreaction, [[Effective Accelerationism]], [[Network States]] ([[Balaji Srinivasan]])
- **Policy priorities**: anti-regulation, anti-DEI, anti-AI-safety, federal restructuring, crypto deregulation
- **Key figures**: [[JD Vance]] (bridges both), [[David Sacks]], [[Elon Musk]] (until 2025 departure)

## Tensions and ruptures

### 2024 H-1B visa controversy (December 2024)

A defining intra-coalition rupture:
- [[Vivek Ramaswamy]] tweeted defending H-1B visas as enabling American technological excellence
- Triggered massive backlash from Religious Right / MAGA base
- Musk defended Ramaswamy
- Religious Right base argued H-1B undermines American workers / Christian families
- Ramaswamy departed DOGE within weeks
- **Demonstrated the underlying Tech Right â†” Religious Right tension**

### Trans issues
- Tech Right is **mostly mixed or libertarian** on trans issues
- Religious Right is **strongly restrictive**
- This is one of the most consistent tension points

### Foreign policy
- Religious Right has historically been **pro-Israel / pro-Ukraine / interventionist** on broader foreign policy
- Tech Right (especially Vance, Trump 2.0) is **substantially more isolationist** especially on Ukraine
- 2025 Vance Munich speech attacking European allies was Tech Right position

## Why this matters for this vault

The Religious Right â†” Tech Right coalition is **structurally novel** in US political history. Previous Republican coalitions (Reagan, Bush) had Religious Right + business + foreign-policy hawks. The Trump 2.0 coalition has Religious Right + Tech Right + populist nationalists with **substantially reduced foreign-policy hawkishness and substantially increased Tech Right institutional power**.

The coalition's **internal tensions** (especially H-1B, trans rights, foreign policy) are likely to be **ongoing flashpoints** as the Trump 2.0 administration progresses. Future political analysis should track these tensions as more determinative than traditional left-right framing.
