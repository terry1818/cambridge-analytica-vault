# Compare: Defense IO Doctrines

The state-side doctrinal frameworks that shape (and were shaped by) the kind of work [[SCL Group]] commercialized. Understanding these makes clear that the CA scandal is the **public face of a much older state practice**.

## NATO Cognitive Warfare (Western)

- Formally articulated in [[Cognitive Warfare]] concept paper by NATO Allied Command Transformation (2020-2021)
- Treats the human mind as the strategic battlefield
- Multi-domain integration
- Acknowledges PSYOPS, information warfare, cyber as sub-domains
- Aligned with US Joint Concept for Operating in the Information Environment (JCOIE)

## Russian Reflexive Control (Soviet/Russian)

- Soviet doctrine dating to 1960s; modernized by Russian intelligence services
- Goal: shape adversary's information environment so adversary "freely" chooses desired path
- Operates through controlled information injection
- Foundation of modern Russian state operations: [[Internet Research Agency]], [[Doppelganger]], etc.

## PLA "Three Warfares" (Chinese)

- Public Opinion Warfare (yulun zhan)
- Psychological Warfare (xinli zhan)
- Legal Warfare (falu zhan)
- Operationalized through PLA Strategic Support Force
- Spans state-media operations, [[Spamouflage Dragonbridge]] persona networks, lawfare

## Gerasimov Doctrine (Russian, contested label)

- Named after General Valery Gerasimov's 2013 article
- Often (somewhat misleadingly) described as "hybrid warfare" â€” blending kinetic and informational
- Western analysts dispute whether it's an actual doctrine or descriptive framework
- Influential in Western thinking about Russian operations regardless

## JTRIG / Five Eyes (UK / US / Allies)

- [[JTRIG]] (UK GCHQ unit) revealed via Snowden documents
- US Centcom "Operation Earnest Voice"
- DARPA SMISC program
- Officially defensive in framing; operationally indistinguishable from offensive

## What's the same across all

1. **Population-level audience targeting** rather than just elite manipulation
2. **Cognitive / psychological as the strategic terrain** (not just physical)
3. **Long-time-horizon** operations (not just crisis-driven)
4. **Information environment shaping** as the goal

## What's different

| Doctrine | Primary actor | Authorization | Target | Methodology |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| NATO Cognitive Warfare | Western state militaries | Alliance treaty | Adversary populations + own |
| Reflexive Control | Russian intelligence | State authorization | Adversary decision-makers + populations |
| Three Warfares | PLA | CCP state directive | All audiences |
| JTRIG / Five Eyes | UK / US intel | Statute + executive order | Terrorism-adjacent + adversary |

Its toolkit was developed for Western state clients (US DoD, UK MoD) using methods drawn from the broader Western IO doctrine.
