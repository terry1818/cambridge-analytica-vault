## Source tier definitions and how the vault grades evidence

The Source Bibliography uses the [[Evidence Tiers]] framework to grade source weight. The principal tiers:

**Tier 1: Primary documents and adjudicated regulatory findings.** Corporate filings, court records, regulator reports, sworn testimony, contemporaneous internal documents (where authentic), and the underlying records of legal and regulatory proceedings. The CA story has substantial Tier-1 evidence: ICO findings (UK), FTC settlement order (US), DCMS sworn testimony (UK), Mueller Report (US), Senate Intel Vol 5 (US), Companies House filings (UK), FEC filings (US), and the various international regulatory records.

**Tier 2: Insider testimony and high-quality investigative journalism with named sources.** Whistleblower testimony (Wylie, Kaiser, Sanni), insider memoirs (Wylie 2019 Mindf*ck, Kaiser 2019 Targeted), long-form investigative journalism with named on-record sources (Cadwalladr Guardian/Observer reporting, NYT Cambridge Analytica coverage, Channel 4 News undercover footage, BBC investigations), and book-length investigative reconstructions (Sharlet on the Fellowship, Anne Nelson on CNP, Levy on Facebook, Briant on SCL).

**Tier 3: Working journalism, secondary commentary, aggregator reporting.** Daily-news coverage of CA-era events, commentary and opinion pieces, third-party aggregator summaries (Wikipedia, SourceWatch, Influence Watch, Ballotpedia), and content from less-rigorously-sourced publications. Useful for orientation and lead generation; not load-bearing for hypothesis-tier claims.

**Tier 4: Anonymous claims, partisan-aligned advocacy material, and material with disputed sourcing.** Used in the vault only when no higher-tier source exists for a specific claim and the claim is independently corroborated; explicitly flagged when used.

## Citation patterns in the vault

The vault uses several distinct citation patterns:

**Wikilink citation:** [[Source Cadwalladr 2017-02-26 Observer Robert Mercer]] â€” used in the body of entity, event, hypothesis, and connection notes to reference a specific source-tier note that contains the source's bibliographic details and the vault's annotation of the source.

**Paragraph-level citation:** for analytical claims that depend on a specific passage, the citation includes a paragraph-or-page-level reference (e.g., [[Source Mueller Report 2019]] Vol I p. 4-5) so a reader can locate the supporting passage.

**Convergence citation:** when multiple independent sources converge on the same claim, the vault prefers to cite the convergence rather than any single source. The Hypothesis notes typically use this pattern; the entity-and-event notes use it less consistently.

## Maintenance of the source bibliography

The bibliography is incrementally maintained as new sources are added to the vault. Specific maintenance tasks: ensuring every Source: note has the standard frontmatter (date, publication, URL, retrieval date, tier classification); ensuring every Source: note is wikilinked from at least one substantive entity / event / hypothesis / connection note; ensuring no duplicate Source: notes exist (the vault has had periodic dedup-pass requirements when OneDrive sync produced shadow copies).

The bibliography is intentionally not exhaustive: not every published item about CA appears here. The principle is "include sources that are referenced by vault analytical claims" rather than "include all available sources."
