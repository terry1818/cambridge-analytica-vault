# Platforms MOC

Tech platforms that have served as **vectors and venues** for political influence operations. Each has its own policy history, exploitation patterns, enforcement track record, and current state.

## Major platforms

- [[Facebook]] / [[Meta]] -- CA exploited Graph API v1; $5B FTC fine; quarterly threat reports
- **Instagram** -- Meta-owned; Russian IRA used heavily 2016+
- **WhatsApp** -- Meta-owned; key Brazilian 2018 disinfo vector
- **X / Twitter** -- IRA + many operations; trust-and-safety dismantled post-Musk 2022
- **YouTube** / Google -- recommendation algorithm exploited; deepfake policy 2024+
- **TikTok** -- Chinese ownership controversies; Marcos 2022 use case
- **Telegram** -- Doppelganger / Russian ops heavy; Durov arrest 2024
- **Reddit** -- IRA target 2016; active CIB enforcement
- **Snapchat** -- limited CIB enforcement
- **LinkedIn** -- state-actor recruitment surface

## Policy / regulatory history (per platform)

### Facebook / Meta
- 2010-2015 -- Graph API v1 friends-data permission (the CA harvest mechanism)
- 2018+ -- Adversarial Threat Reports; quarterly takedowns
- 2019 -- $5B FTC fine; 20-year consent decree
- 2022 -- $725M class settlement
- 2025+ -- Reduced content moderation programs (Zuckerberg announcement)

### X / Twitter
- Pre-2022 -- quarterly transparency reports
- 2022 -- Musk acquisition; mass T&S layoffs
- 2023+ -- EU DSA formal proceedings opened
- 2024 -- significant influence-ops backsliding

### TikTok
- 2018+ -- emerging influence-ops target
- 2022+ -- US national security restrictions
- 2024-2025 -- divestment law battles in US
- 2026 -- TikTok continues operating in US under various arrangements

## Cross-cutting themes

- Recurring **API access** issues (third-party developer permissions)
- Recurring **political ad policies** (transparency, targeting restrictions)
- Recurring **Coordinated Inauthentic Behavior** enforcement
- Recurring **synthetic media** policies (deepfakes, AI-generated content)
- Recurring **public transparency reporting** (highly variable across platforms)
