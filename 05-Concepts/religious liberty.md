# Religious liberty

First Amendment legal-doctrinal framework. In contemporary US politics, has functioned operationally as the legal mechanism for [[Religious right]] policy advancement.

## Strategic deployment

- 1990: Employment Division v. Smith narrowed Free Exercise Clause
- 1993: Religious Freedom Restoration Act (RFRA) restored higher protection
- 2014: Hobby Lobby case extended RFRA to closely-held corporations
- 2020+: Multiple religious-liberty exemption rulings from contraception mandates, anti-discrimination protections, LGBTQ+ protections

## Significance for vault

Religious liberty is the central legal-doctrinal vehicle of contemporary Religious right legal advancement. Operationally, the Federalist Society judicial pipeline has produced the jurisprudence permitting religious-liberty exemptions to substantively constrain liberal anti-discrimination and equal-protection policy.
