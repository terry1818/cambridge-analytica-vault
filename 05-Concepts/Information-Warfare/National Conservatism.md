# National Conservatism (NatCon)

## Definition

**National Conservatism** is the intellectual-philosophical wing of the contemporary international populist-right. Anchored by **Yoram Hazony** (Israeli-American political theorist; *The Virtue of Nationalism* 2018) and the **Edmund Burke Foundation** he leads. Hosts **annual National Conservatism Conferences** in the US, UK, and continental Europe since 2019.

**The philosophical legitimation layer** for the political program implemented by Trump 2.0, Orbán's Fidesz, Meloni's FdI, Milei's LLA, and other parallel movements. Frames itself as "post-liberal conservatism" - explicitly rejecting classical liberalism's individual-rights primacy in favor of nation, family, religion, and rootedness.

## Core tenets (per NatCon statement of principles)

1. **National independence** - rejection of supranational governance (EU, UN)
2. **Limited international "rules-based order"** - skepticism of post-WW2 liberal-international order
3. **God and public religion** - rejection of state-religion separation in practice
4. **The rule of law** - against "rule by judges"; pro-legislative supremacy
5. **Free enterprise** - market economics but rejecting libertarianism
6. **Public research** - state-led R&D
7. **Family and children** - pronatalist; anti-LGBTQ in practice
8. **Immigration** - restriction
9. **Race** - colorblind framing (rejecting "anti-racism" as itself racial framing)
10. **Anti-woke** - culture-war framing

## Catholic Integralism subset

Within NatCon, **Catholic Integralism** is a distinct sub-movement:
- **Adrian Vermeule** (Harvard Law) - most explicit; advocates "common-good constitutionalism"
- **Patrick Deneen** - *Regime Change* (2023)
- **Sohrab Ahmari**
- **R.R. Reno** (*First Things*)
- **Pope Leo XIV's predecessors' Vatican positions sometimes overlap**
- More extreme than mainstream NatCon: explicit advocacy for confessional state
