# QAnon

QAnon is a decentralized conspiracy-theory movement that originated in October 2017 with anonymous "Q" posts on 4chan (subsequently 8chan, then 8kun). The core intellectual claim: a deep-state cabal of pedophile elites operates a global child-trafficking-and-blood-harvesting network, with Trump as the secret operative working to defeat the cabal. The Q posts ceased in late 2020; the broader movement has continued in decentralized form.

## Post-Q operational continuation

The post-Q period (late 2020-present) has seen the decentralized continuation of QAnon-adjacent material across multiple operational vehicles:

- The "Save the Children" hashtag campaign that operationalised the child-trafficking framing for broader audience reach
- [[Operation Underground Railroad]] / [[Tim Ballard]] / [[Sound of Freedom film]] mainstreaming of the child-trafficking framing
- Substantial Republican-congressional-member alignment with QAnon-adjacent positioning (Marjorie Taylor Greene, Lauren Boebert, others)
- Continuing decentralized online infrastructure across Telegram, alternative-social-media platforms, and broader right-aligned media ecosystem

## Operational consequences

QAnon-adjacent intellectual material has substantially influenced:

- Trump-administration personnel positioning (Mike Flynn's post-administration QAnon-adjacent positioning; substantial cross-pollination with the broader Trump-orbit ecosystem)
- The January 6 Capitol attack participant base (substantial documented QAnon-adjacent affiliation across multiple participants)
- The broader "child trafficking" framing that has substantially mainstreamed
- Public-health-and-vaccine policy positioning (substantial overlap with the [[MAHA]] intellectual ecosystem)
