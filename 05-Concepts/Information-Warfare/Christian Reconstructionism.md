# Christian Reconstructionism

## Definition

**Christian Reconstructionism** is the theological system developed by **[[R.J. Rushdoony]]** at the **[[Chalcedon Foundation]]** (founded 1965). It holds that **all of society should be reconstructed according to the laws that governed the Israelites in the Old Testament** (theonomy). The central tenet is that **God has tasked Christians with taking dominion over society**, beginning with Genesis 1:28, and that this creation mandate is in fact a dominion mandate commanding humans to bring every sphere of society into subjugation under Christ.

## Core tenets

1. **Theonomy** -" Old Testament biblical law (the Mosaic Law) should govern modern civil society
2. **Postmillennialism** -" Christ will return AFTER Christians gradually establish dominion (gives the project an unhurried multi-generational time horizon)
3. **Presuppositional apologetics** -" all knowledge presupposes God; secular epistemology is incoherent
4. **Federal vision** -" civil law should reflect biblical-covenant structure
5. **Education capture** -" Christian education and homeschooling are critical (escape secular state control)
6. **Sphere sovereignty** -" different spheres of society (family, church, state) each have their own divinely-ordained authority

## Historical development

- **1965** -" Rushdoony founds Chalcedon Foundation
- **1973** -" *Institutes of Biblical Law* published; foundational text
- **1970s-1980s** -" Rushdoony's homeschooling testimony helps legalize homeschooling in many US states; HSLDA founded by [[Michael Farris]] 1983 within Rushdoony lineage
- **1980s-2000s** -" Influences broader evangelical political project (Moral Majority, Christian Coalition)
- **2010s-2020s** -" Re-emerges in popular form via [[Seven Mountains Mandate]] and Christian Nationalism

## Why it matters in the vault

Christian Reconstructionism is **the theological taproot** of everything in the [[Theocratic Wing Infrastructure 1935-2026|theocratic-wing infrastructure note]] from the 1970s onward. The dominionist political program ([[Dominionism]]), the homeschooling movement (HSLDA -- Patrick Henry College -- ADF), and the broader Christian-nationalist policy program of Project 2025 all trace back here.
