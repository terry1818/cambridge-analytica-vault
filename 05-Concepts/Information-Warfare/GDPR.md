# GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)

## Definition

EU Regulation 2016/679 governing **data protection and privacy for individuals within the European Union and European Economic Area**. Adopted April 2016 by the European Parliament; enforcement began **May 25, 2018** -- six weeks after the [[Facebook-CA Data Scandal]] broke globally on March 17, 2018. Replaced the 1995 Data Protection Directive.

## Key provisions relevant to CA

- **Consent** must be explicit, informed, freely given, and revocable
- **Right to access** -- individuals can request all data held about them and how it's processed
- **Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")** -- individuals can require deletion under specific circumstances
- **Data portability** -- individuals can export their data in machine-readable format
- **Breach notification** -- within 72 hours of awareness
- **Privacy by design and by default** -- built into systems from the start
- **Data Protection Officers** required for many organizations
- **Penalties** -- up to 4% of global annual revenue or €20M, whichever higher
- **Extraterritorial scope** -- applies to any firm processing EU residents data regardless of where the firm is based

## CA-specific relevance

- GDPR enforcement began **six weeks after the CA scandal broke** -- the scandal massively amplified GDPRs symbolic weight and public salience
- [[Facebook]] **applied GDPR provisions globally** (not just in EU) in April 2018 in direct response to the scandal -- a notable example of "Brussels Effect" extraterritoriality
- GDPR has not been used to prosecute CA-related entities directly (CA was UK-registered, with US operations, and dissolved before sustained GDPR enforcement)
- **Ireland** (Facebooks EU HQ) is the primary GDPR-enforcement jurisdiction for Facebook/Meta -- multiple fines including **€1.2B in 2023** for data transfers
- [[Source ICO 2020-11 Cambridge Analytica investigation]] used GDPR successor framework (UK GDPR after Brexit) in its CA enforcement work

## Influence on other jurisdictions

GDPR is the **principal regulatory legacy** of the CA-era. Other jurisdictions modeled regulation on GDPR:

- **California** -- CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act, 2020)
- **Brazil** -- LGPD (2020)
- **India** -- Digital Personal Data Protection Act (2023)
- **South Africa** -- POPIA (Protection of Personal Information Act, 2020)
- **China** -- PIPL (Personal Information Protection Law, 2021)

## Effectiveness debate

- **Pro:** Enabled the multi-billion-euro fines against tech platforms; established baseline data rights for EU residents; influenced global regulation
- **Con:** Critics note that consent fatigue (cookie banners), checkbox compliance, and limited enforcement against US tech giants in early years undermined the regulations intent
- **Mixed:** GDPR has not prevented the post-CA influence-operations ecosystem from continuing -- addressed the data-harvest mechanism without addressing the targeting / persona-network methodology
