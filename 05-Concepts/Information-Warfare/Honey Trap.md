# Honey Trap

## Definition

Intelligence tradecraft technique using **sexual or romantic seduction to extract information from or compromise a target**. Historically associated with Cold War-era state intelligence operations (KGB, CIA, MI6, Mossad, etc.) but has continued into the modern era as both state and private-intelligence tactic.

The term covers a spectrum:
- **Pure extraction:** seduction to obtain documents, passwords, or admissions
- **Compromise:** creation of photographic/video evidence for later blackmail
- **Honey-trap-as-disinformation:** fabrication of fake compromising materials for character assassination

## CA-relevance

[[Alexander Nix]] was caught on hidden camera during the [[Channel 4 Sting 2018]] **offering honey-trap services as part of CAs commercial menu**:

- Specifically mentioned using "beautiful Ukrainian girls" to discredit politicians
- Said CA would deploy operations whether or not the targets accepted bribes (in which case the *offer* itself could be made compromising)
- Referenced using "Israeli companies" (later confirmed as [[Psy-Group]]) for entrapment operations
- Made clear honey-trap operations were a regular part of CAs international political work, not an exception

## Documented modern honey-trap operations

- 2014-2018 -- [[Psy-Group]] offered honey-trap operations to political clients
- 2016-2017 -- [[Black Cube]] used false-identity operations against Weinstein accusers (a variant of honey-trap methodology)
- 2018+ -- Various continuing operations by Israeli private-intelligence firms
- [[JTRIG]] (GCHQ) documents leaked by Snowden indicate UK state-side honey-trap use

## Significance

The honey-trap as a tool of the commercialized influence-operations industry illustrates how state-intelligence tradecraft has **flowed downstream to commercial firms** serving paying clients. The same techniques developed for Cold War espionage are now sold to corporate and political clients globally.
