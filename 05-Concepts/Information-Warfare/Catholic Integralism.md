# Catholic Integralism

## Definition

**Catholic Integralism** is the political theology holding that **political authority should be subordinated to Church (Catholic) authority** and that the state should explicitly advance Catholic teaching, including in spheres (education, family law, sexuality, religious tolerance) that liberalism considers private.

Distinct from **Christian Nationalism** (which is Protestant-dominated and US-centric) and from **[[Dominionism]]** (which has both Protestant and broader Christian-nationalist forms). **Catholic Integralism is specifically Catholic and pre-modern in lineage** - reviving pre-Vatican II Catholic political-theological positions.
