# TESCREAL

## Definition

**Critical academic framework** for analyzing what its authors describe as a "bundle" of interconnected ideologies prevalent in Silicon Valley and especially around AI labs. Acronym stands for:

- **T**ranshumanism
- **E**xtropianism
- **S**ingularitarianism
- **C**osmism
- **R**ationalism
- **E**ffective **A**ltruism
- **L**ongtermism

Coined by philosopher **Émile P. Torres** and computer scientist **Timnit Gebru** (former Google AI ethics researcher) in a 2023 paper.

## Argument

Torres and Gebru argue these ideologies share:
- Roots in 20th-century eugenics
- Future-focused moral frameworks that discount present suffering
- Disproportionate concern for hypothetical future entities (digital minds, etc.)
- Convergent positions on AI development and existential risk
- Influence on key AI lab leadership and funding decisions

## Why it matters for this vault

TESCREAL provides **academic critical framework** for analyzing the intellectual ecosystem of AI labs including:
- [[OpenAI]] (Altman pre-2023 explicitly aligned with parts of TESCREAL)
- [[Anthropic]] (EA-aligned founding)
- DeepMind
- Various AI safety / alignment research organizations

The framework illuminates the **EA → AI safety → Anthropic** pipeline that the vault has documented, contextualizing it in a broader ideological history.

## TESCREAL vs. Tech Right e/acc

Importantly, **TESCREAL ideologies and e/acc are in some tension**:
- TESCREAL includes EA and longtermism (AI safety advocacy)
- e/acc explicitly opposes EA and AI safety

However, both share **utopian-future orientation** and roots in similar Silicon Valley intellectual milieus. The conflict between TESCREAL-aligned and e/acc-aligned figures is partly an **intra-Tech ideological dispute** rather than fundamental disagreement.

## Critique of TESCREAL framework

The TESCREAL framework has critics who argue:
- It conflates diverse ideologies into a single label
- Eugenics-roots argument is contested
- Framework is more polemical than analytic

Despite controversy, the framework has become widely used in critical AI studies and adjacent academic work.
