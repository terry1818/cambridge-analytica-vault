# Dominionism / Dominion Theology

## Definition

**Dominionism** (also called dominion theology) is the theocratic theory that **God has called conservative Christians to exercise dominion over society** by taking control of political and cultural institutions. Derived from a particular reading of Genesis 1:28 ("Be fruitful and multiply... and have dominion over the fish of the sea and over the birds of the heavens and over every living thing"), dominionists hold that the "creation mandate" is in fact a **"dominion mandate"** â€" commanding humans (specifically Christians) to bring every sphere of society into subjugation under Christ.

Operationally, dominionism is the **political program** underlying contemporary Christian nationalism. It is the theological framework that justifies:

- [[Russ Vought]]'s Christian-nationalist self-identification and Schedule F program
- The [[Seven Mountains Mandate]] strategy
- [[Project 2025]]'s policy frame
- [[Convention of States Action]]'s constitutional capture goals
- [[Alliance Defending Freedom]]'s litigation strategy (e.g. Dobbs)
- The federal-lands-transfer agenda (matches "have dominion over the earth")

## Origin

The contemporary movement traces to **[[R.J. Rushdoony]]'s 1973 *Institutes of Biblical Law*** and the broader [[Christian Reconstructionism]] he developed at [[Chalcedon Foundation]] (founded 1965).

**Note on terminology:** "Dominion theology" and "dominionism" are sometimes used interchangeably; sometimes "Dominion Theology" refers specifically to [[Christian Reconstructionism]] while "dominionism" is the broader political application. [[TheocracyWatch]] uses "dominionism" as the umbrella term.

## Variants

### Christian Reconstructionism (Rushdoony tradition)
- Postmillennial: Christ returns AFTER Christians gradually establish dominion
- Theonomic: Old Testament biblical law as governing template
- Slow-and-steady infiltration strategy

### Seven Mountains Mandate (1975 Bright/Cunningham; 2000s charismatic)
- Identifies 7 cultural "mountains" Christians must control: government, education, media, arts/entertainment, religion, family, business
- Charismatic-evangelical orientation
- More immediate-action strategy
- See [[Seven Mountains Mandate]]

### Christian Nationalism (current popular term)
- Less theological specificity than Reconstructionism
- More broad-base political mobilization
- Self-identified by [[Russ Vought]] and others
- Often combined with white-nationalist or ethno-nationalist content
