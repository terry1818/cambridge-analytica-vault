# Sovereign Citizens

The Sovereign Citizens movement is a decentralized US movement claiming individual legal sovereignty independent of federal-state-local government authority. The movement's intellectual origins are in the 1970s-1980s [[Posse Comitatus]] movement (William Potter Gale, Christian Identity-adjacent intellectual base); the contemporary movement has substantially expanded beyond the original Christian-Identity-affiliated base.

## Operational claims

Sovereign Citizens claim:

- US federal government legal authority is constitutionally illegitimate
- Individual sovereignty can be claimed through specific legal procedures (filing UCC documents, declaring "natural person" status, refusing legal identification)
- Tax obligations, vehicle-registration requirements, court jurisdiction, and adjacent government claims can be legally rejected
- Coordinated paper-terrorism tactics (filing liens, fraudulent court documents) can be used to harass perceived adversaries

## Operational consequences

The FBI has classified Sovereign Citizens activity as a substantial domestic terrorism threat. Specific operational consequences have included multiple Sovereign-Citizen-affiliated police shootings, substantial paper-terrorism operations targeting judges, prosecutors, and IRS personnel, and the broader operational base for adjacent paramilitary infrastructure.

- [[Oath Keepers]] and adjacent militia-network operational base
- [[Christian Identity]] racially-aligned extremist subgroups
