# Seasteading

## Definition

Movement and political-theoretical framework to **build floating cities in international waters** as a means of escaping existing national governance and enabling political-economic experimentation. Co-founded as the **Seasteading Institute** in **2008** by [[Patri Friedman]] (grandson of Milton Friedman) with [[Peter Thiel]] as anchor funder.

## Original concept

- Floating habitats in international waters (outside national jurisdictions)
- Allow experimentation in libertarian / alternative political models
- Compete for residents who choose preferred governance
- "Exit" rather than "voice" framework for political reform

## Why it (mostly) failed

By mid-2010s, seasteading had **mostly failed in its original maritime form**:
- Engineering challenges of ocean habitats
- Unclear international legal status of floating cities
- Cost vs. benefit problems
- Limited investor interest beyond Thiel patronage

## Why this matters for this vault

Seasteading represents the **longest-running Thiel-funded political-experimentation framework** and the **conceptual ancestor** of:
- Charter cities movement
- Network States framework
- Various crypto-state convergence proposals

The 2008-onwards seasteading project illustrates the **deep historical roots** of contemporary Tech Right exit-over-voice politics in libertarian-utopian thought.
