# Dominion Theology

Dominion Theology is the cluster of Christian theological doctrines that hold that believers are commanded by God to **take "dominion" over the institutions of earthly society** — government, education, media, business, family, religion, arts — and reorder them according to biblical law. It is the ideological substrate of the contemporary American **[[Christian Nationalism]]** movement and the operational doctrine behind the **[[Council for National Policy]]**, the **[[New Apostolic Reformation]]** (NAR), and the **Seven Mountains Mandate** (7MM). Understanding Dominion Theology is prerequisite for understanding why the theocratic wing of the 2024–2026 power coalition is not metaphorically religious — it operates on an explicit theological mandate.

## Theological origin: Rushdoony and Christian Reconstructionism

The 20th-century origin point is **[[Rousas John Rushdoony]]** (1916–2001), an Armenian-American Calvinist theologian who developed **Christian Reconstructionism** in the 1960s–1970s. Rushdoony's core argument:

1. **Genesis 1:28** ("be fruitful and multiply, and replenish the earth, and subdue it: and have dominion") is not a one-time creation mandate but an ongoing command to Christians.
2. **Christ's atonement** restores believers to the role originally given to Adam — i.e., dominion-bearer.
3. Therefore Christians are commanded to **reconstruct human society according to Mosaic law** — including the civil and judicial laws of the Old Testament (theonomy).
4. Public policy should derive from biblical law, not Enlightenment liberalism.

Rushdoony founded the **[[Chalcedon Foundation]]** in 1965 to develop and propagate these ideas. His son-in-law **[[Gary North]]** (1942–2022) became the most prolific Reconstructionist writer, applying the framework to economics, education, and politics.

Reconstructionism in its pure form was always a fringe minority within American evangelicalism — but its **mode of thinking** (Christians have a positive mandate to govern, not merely to evangelize) infiltrated mainstream evangelical and charismatic networks across the 1980s–2000s and is now the operational doctrine of the most politically active segment.

## The New Apostolic Reformation (NAR) and 7MM

By the 2000s, dominion theology had been substantially reformulated within the **[[New Apostolic Reformation]]** (NAR) — a network of charismatic and Pentecostal churches led initially by **[[C. Peter Wagner]]** (1930–2016). NAR added:

- **Apostolic and prophetic offices** — modern "apostles" and "prophets" are believed to be in active operation, receiving direct revelation from God
- **Spiritual warfare** — political and cultural battles are interpreted as literal combat with demonic principalities
- **Strategic-level prayer mapping** — geographic targeting of "spiritual strongholds"
- **The Seven Mountains Mandate (7MM)** — popularized by Lance Wallnau and Bill Bright, identifying seven cultural "mountains" Christians are commanded to take dominion of:
  1. Religion
  2. Family
  3. Education
  4. Government
  5. Media
  6. Arts and Entertainment
  7. Business

The 7MM is the **most influential current formulation** of dominion theology — it is taught in megachurches, charter-school networks ([[Hillsdale K-12 Charter School Network]], [[Classical Christian Education]] schools), and is the operational frame behind orgs like [[Capitol Ministries]], [[Family Research Council]], [[Alliance Defending Freedom]], and the [[Turning Point Faith]] arm of [[Turning Point USA]].

The crucial difference from Rushdoony's Reconstructionism: where Rushdoony imagined gradual cultural transformation over generations, the NAR / 7MM formulation imagines **active conquest** — Christians are commanded to *take* these mountains, not just to occupy them passively.
