# Data broker

Company that aggregates personal data on individuals from public records, commercial transactions, social media, and tracking pixels, then sells access to that data to other entities (advertisers, political campaigns, law enforcement, financial firms, employers).

## Top players

- Acxiom - foundational consumer data graph; ~10,000 data points per US adult per public filings
- Epsilon - merged into Publicis; ad-tech / direct-mail
- Experian / Equifax / TransUnion - credit bureaus that also sell broader data
- LexisNexis Risk Solutions - law enforcement and ICE contracts
- CoreLogic - real-estate-anchored consumer profiles
- Palantir is NOT a broker in the same sense - it processes its clients' data and contracted third-party data

## How [[Cambridge Analytica]] fits

CA was a layer on top of conventional data brokers. The 2014 Kogan / Aleksandr Kogan harvest from Facebook supplied a psychographic overlay to an otherwise broker-style data set. The novel CA element was not data collection - it was the OCEAN model derived from Facebook personality quizzes layered onto otherwise-purchased voter file data.

## Regulatory landscape (Tier 1)

- US: No federal data broker statute. Vermont (2018) and California (2024) have state registries
- EU: GDPR Article 17 (right to erasure) bites here; brokers must respond to deletion requests
- The 2024 California Delete Act creates a one-stop deletion mechanism but has not yet been tested at scale

## Surveillance state interface

- ICE has been documented to purchase LexisNexis and Thomson Reuters CLEAR data to bypass jurisdictional restrictions on direct database queries
- DOJ Office of the Inspector General 2024 report flagged the broker workaround for warrantless data acquisition
