# Fake electors scheme

Coordinated effort by the Trump campaign in December 2020 to submit slates of electors from Arizona, Georgia, Michigan, Nevada, New Mexico, Pennsylvania, and Wisconsin falsely certifying that Trump won those states.

## Mechanics (Tier 1)

- December 14, 2020: Trump electors met in seven state capitols and signed certificates stating they were the "duly elected" electors
- Certificates transmitted to the National Archives and Senate
- Coordinated through [[Kenneth Chesebro]] (memo Dec 6, 2020) and [[Rudy Giuliani]] team
- [[Mark Meadows]] involvement documented in his Jan 4-5, 2021 communications

## Criminal cases stemming from the scheme

- Georgia (Fulton County) RICO indictment August 14, 2023: included multiple fake-elector defendants (Cathy Latham, David Shafer, Shawn Still). [[Donald Trump]] also charged.
- Michigan state charges July 18, 2023 against 16 fake electors
- Arizona indictment April 24, 2024: 18 defendants including [[Rudy Giuliani]], [[Mark Meadows]], [[John Eastman]], [[Boris Epshteyn]]
- Nevada indictment December 2023: 6 fake electors (dismissed June 2024 on venue grounds, refiled)
- Federal: charged as Counts 2-4 in [[Trump January 6 Federal Case]] (Smith); operative until July 1, 2024 Supreme Court immunity ruling

## Significance

The fake electors are the operational link that connects "Stop the Steal" speech to actual interference with the Electoral Count Act process. Pence rejecting the slates required there be slates to reject - the scheme created that requirement deliberately.
