# Christian Nationalism

Political ideology asserting that the United States is or should be defined as a Christian nation, with Christianity (typically a specific theological tradition) given preferred legal and cultural status. Merges religious identity with national identity. Central organizing framework for the **theocratic right wing of the contemporary Republican coalition** as mapped in the vault's [[Deep Dive - Three Wing Coalition Anatomy]].

## Academic definition (Whitehead & Perry, 2020)

In *Taking America Back for God: Christian Nationalism in the United States*, [[Andrew Whitehead]] and [[Samuel Perry]] (2020) operationalize Christian Nationalism as a six-factor scale measuring beliefs about whether:

1. The US government should declare the US a Christian nation
2. The US government should advocate Christian values
3. The US government should enforce strict separation of church and state (reverse-coded)
4. The US government should allow display of religious symbols in public spaces
5. The success of the US is part of God's plan
6. The US government should allow prayer in public schools

The scale identifies an "Ambassadors" category (highest commitment) constituting approximately 20% of Americans per their data.
