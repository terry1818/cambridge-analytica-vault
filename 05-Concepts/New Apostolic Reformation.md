# New Apostolic Reformation

Charismatic Christian movement that elevates contemporary "apostles" and "prophets" to authority over local congregations and over the broader Christian witness in society.

## Origin (Tier 1)

- Term coined by [[C. Peter Wagner]] (Fuller Theological Seminary, then independent) 1990s-2000s
- Theological framing: the offices of apostle and prophet are restored for the contemporary church
- Operationally: networks of apostles and prophets supplant traditional denominational structure

## Key contemporary figures

- [[Lance Wallnau]] - public-facing media voice; [[Seven Mountain Mandate]] originator
- [[Cindy Jacobs]] - prophetic ministry; predicted Trump victory 2016, 2020
- [[Dutch Sheets]] - prophetic / appeal to heaven movement
- [[Paula White]] - Trump's spiritual advisor 2016-2020; NAR-adjacent
- [[Sean Feucht]] - worship leader; political organizer

## Political alignment 2016-2024

NAR figures coordinated heavily around Trump's 2016, 2020, and 2024 campaigns. The "appeal to heaven" flag carried at January 6 is associated with this movement (Dutch Sheets's book by the same name).

## Significance for COS analysis

NAR is one of the religious tributaries feeding [[Christian Nationalism]] organizing. It is not the only one - traditional evangelical, traditionalist Catholic, and reformed-Calvinist streams also feed CN - but NAR provides the prophetic-confidence vocabulary that has been operationally important to the [[Stop the Steal]] period and to ongoing COS organizing.
