# Seven Mountain Mandate

Theological / political framework popular in [[New Apostolic Reformation]] circles. Calls for Christians to take control of seven "mountains" (spheres) of cultural influence: government, education, media, arts, business, family, religion.

## Origin (Tier 1)

- 1975: independently formulated by [[Bill Bright]] (Campus Crusade for Christ) and [[Loren Cunningham]] (YWAM) in similar form
- 2008: [[Lance Wallnau]] codifies the explicit "Seven Mountains" framing in his book
- Wallnau is also the originator of the influential 2016 "Cyrus the Great" framing of Trump

## Relationship to Christian nationalism

The 7M Mandate provides the theological warrant for Christian engagement in secular governance as a top-down rather than bottom-up project. It does not require persuasion of a majority - it requires capture of decision-making nodes.

This makes it ideologically downstream of the [[Council for National Policy]]-style elite-coordination strategy and upstream of the [[Convention of States]] structural-amendment strategy.

## Significance for COS analysis

COS organizing materials and donor pitches frequently invoke 7M language. Not all COS supporters are 7M adherents, but the structural overlap is substantial - both frameworks treat conventional democratic deliberation as insufficient to reach the desired end state.
