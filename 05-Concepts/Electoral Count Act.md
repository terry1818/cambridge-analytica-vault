# Electoral Count Act

Federal statute (1887, substantially reformed 2022) governing how Congress counts presidential electoral votes.

## 1887 ECA - key provisions challenged in 2020

- Vice President's role purely ministerial (per overwhelming legal consensus, though the [[Eastman Memo]] disputed this)
- Each state's electors counted unless objections from at least one Senator AND one Representative
- "Safe harbor" deadline 6 days before electors meet - after which Congress generally accepts the state's certified result

## The 2020 attack vector

The [[Eastman Memo]] argued ambiguities in the 1887 ECA gave the Vice President discretion to reject or delay electors. Every major constitutional scholar - left and right - and Pence's own counsel [[Greg Jacob]] rejected this reading.

## Electoral Count Reform Act of 2022 (Tier 1)

Passed as part of the Consolidated Appropriations Act, signed December 29, 2022:

- Makes the VP role explicitly ministerial
- Raises objection threshold to 1/5 of each chamber
- Tightens "safe harbor" rules
- Clarifies governors' role in identifying valid electors

Sponsored by [[Susan Collins]] (R) and [[Joe Manchin]] (D). Notable bipartisan response to the documented 2020 vulnerabilities.
