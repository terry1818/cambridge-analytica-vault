# Religious right

Political coalition of conservative US Protestant evangelicals, traditionalist Catholics, and Mormons, organized 1970s-present to influence elections and judicial appointments.

## Founding moment (Tier 1)

- 1979: [[Jerry Falwell]] founds the Moral Majority with strategist Paul Weyrich
- Common framing: organized around abortion (Roe v. Wade, 1973). Randall Balmer and others have documented the original catalyst was federal pressure on segregated Christian schools (Green v. Connally, 1971, IRS revocation of Bob Jones University's tax exemption); abortion became the public-facing organizing issue after that

## Coalition over time

- Moral Majority (1979-1989)
- Christian Coalition under [[Ralph Reed]] (1989-)
- [[Focus on the Family]], [[Liberty Counsel]], Family Research Council (1990s-)
- [[Federalist Society]] judicial pipeline (1982-, technically secular but heavily aligned)
- [[Council for National Policy]] (1981-) - umbrella coordination body
- Most recently: [[Christian nationalism]] explicitly named as the post-Roe organizing frame

## Strategic shift 2016-2024

- Pre-2016: Coalition required pious public conduct from candidates
- 2016+: Coalition adopts a "Cyrus the Great" framing for Trump - a flawed instrument used by God to advance Christian objectives
- This was a strategic decision led by leaders including Robert Jeffress, [[James Dobson]], and Ralph Reed
