# Schedule F

Proposed category of federal employment that would reclassify policy-influencing career civil servants as at-will political appointees, removing standard civil-service protections (merit hiring, due process for termination).

## 2020 origin (Tier 1)

- October 21, 2020: Trump signed Executive Order 13957 creating Schedule F
- Estimated 50,000 federal employees would have been reclassified
- January 22, 2021: Biden rescinded via Executive Order 14003 before significant implementation

## 2025 revival (Tier 1)

- January 20, 2025: Trump signed Executive Order on Day 1 of second term restoring Schedule F (renamed "Schedule Policy/Career")
- Implementation underway under [[Russ Vought]] at OMB
- Lawsuits filed by NTEU and AFGE federal employee unions challenging the rulemaking; cases pending in DC Circuit as of latest filings

## Why it matters

Schedule F is the institutional spine of Project 2025's executive-branch agenda. The argument: without removing civil-service protections from policy-influencing positions, no presidential agenda can be implemented against career-staff resistance.

The counter-argument: removing those protections eliminates the post-Pendleton-Act professional civil service that the US has had since 1883, returning the federal workforce to a spoils system.
