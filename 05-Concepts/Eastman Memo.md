# Eastman Memo

Two related memoranda authored by [[John Eastman]] in late December 2020 / early January 2021 laying out a plan for [[Mike Pence]] to reject electoral votes from contested states on January 6, 2021.

## The two memos (Tier 1)

- Two-page version: Pence declares the seven states with "competing slates" (the [[fake electors]]) unable to be counted, then declares Trump the winner with the remaining electoral count
- Six-page version: A more elaborate sequence ending in the House of Representatives deciding the election under the 12th Amendment (one vote per state, which Republicans controlled)

## What was established at J6 Committee (Tier 1)

- Eastman conceded to [[Greg Jacob]] on January 6 itself that the theory would lose 9-0 at the Supreme Court
- Eastman still pressed Pence to delay certification after the riot was underway
- Eastman acknowledged in real time that the plan violated the Electoral Count Act

## Criminal consequences for Eastman

- Indicted in Georgia (Fulton County) August 14, 2023
- Indicted in Arizona April 24, 2024
- California Bar disbarred Eastman March 27, 2024 (recommendation; final disbarment in process)

## Why the memo matters

It is the documentary spine of the J6 plot. "Stop the Steal" speeches by themselves were not actionable; the Eastman memo described an actual sequence of acts by named officials that, if executed, would have changed the certified outcome. Pence refusing to play his role is the reason the plot failed at that point.
