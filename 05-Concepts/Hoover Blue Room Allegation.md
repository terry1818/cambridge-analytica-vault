# Hoover Blue Room Allegation

Allegation, primarily sourced from [[Anthony Summers]]'s 1993 biography "Official and Confidential: The Secret Life of J. Edgar Hoover," that mafia figures (specifically Meyer Lansky's organization) possessed compromising photographs of FBI Director [[J Edgar Hoover]] and that this material may have constrained FBI action against the Mafia during Hoover's tenure.

## Status

- **Tier 2 / contested.** Summers's primary source was Susan Rosenstiel, ex-wife of Lewis Rosenstiel (Schenley Industries CEO with documented organized-crime connections). Susan claimed to have seen Hoover in compromising circumstances at a 1958 Plaza Hotel party
- Independent corroboration has not surfaced in the 30+ years since publication
- Many Hoover biographers (Curt Gentry "J Edgar Hoover: The Man and the Secrets" 1991, Tim Weiner "Enemies" 2012) treat the specific Blue Suite claim as poorly sourced

## What is established (Tier 1)

- Hoover did possess massive personal-secrets files on senior US officials (the "Official and Confidential" files were partially destroyed at his death)
- FBI under Hoover was strikingly inactive against organized crime through the 1950s, only pivoting after the 1957 Apalachin Meeting forced public acknowledgment of the Mafia's existence
- The pattern of FBI inaction against organized crime is consistent with possible compromise but does not prove the specific Susan Rosenstiel allegation
