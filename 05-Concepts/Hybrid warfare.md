# Hybrid warfare

Coordinated use of conventional military, irregular forces, cyber operations, economic pressure, information operations, and political subversion below the threshold that would trigger conventional Article 5 / NATO response.

## Origin of the term in current usage

- Frank Hoffman (US Marine Corps, 2007) - "Conflict in the 21st Century: The Rise of Hybrid Wars" - coined modern usage
- 2013 Valery Gerasimov essay "The Value of Science in Prediction" - Russian framing, often called the "Gerasimov Doctrine" though Gerasimov denies authoring a doctrine

## Russian application (Tier 1)

- 2014 Crimea seizure: "little green men" - unmarked Russian special forces operating with local proxies, paired with information operations and economic pressure on Ukraine
- 2014-2022 Donbas: deniable Wagner forces, separatist proxies, cyber attacks on Ukrainian power grid (BlackEnergy, NotPetya)
- 2016+ US: information operations ([[Internet Research Agency]]) paired with hack-and-leak (GRU)
- 2022-: Conventional invasion of Ukraine has shown the model has limits when the target state and its allies push back

## Why it matters in this vault

The structure of the [[Cambridge Analytica]] operation, [[Project Birmingham]], the [[Project Lakhta]] IRA work, and the [[Mueller Report]] documented Russian contacts all sit inside the hybrid-warfare frame - they were ALL below the conventional-conflict threshold.
