# Election Integrity

## Definition

"Election integrity" is, on its face, a neutral and longstanding term referring to the accuracy, security, and trustworthiness of elections — correct vote counts, secure equipment, accurate voter rolls. This note treats it as a **contested framing**: a phrase whose surface neutrality has been deliberately adopted by the post-2020 election-denial movement to relabel a program that, in practice, restricts ballot access, generates mass voter challenges, and casts doubt on legitimate results. The analytical point is that the same two words name two very different things — a genuine administrative goal shared across the political spectrum, and a movement whose effects run opposite to that goal. This vault flags the term itself as a site of contestation.

## Origin / history

Election administration as a professional concern is old; nonpartisan bodies and academics have used "integrity" language for decades. The contested, movement sense crystallized in two stages. First, the [[Presidential Advisory Commission on Election Integrity]] (2017) — the Kobach–Pence "voter fraud" commission — placed the phrase in the title of a body premised on Donald Trump's false claim that millions of illegal votes were cast in 2016; it disbanded in 2018 having found no evidence. Second, after the 2020 election, the phrase became the movement's permanent banner: the [[Election Integrity Network]] founded by [[Cleta Mitchell]] under the Conservative Partnership Institute, "election integrity units" created in several state governments, and a wave of state laws all marketed under the "integrity" label.

## How it works

The framing works as a **euphemism and a Trojan horse**. Because "integrity" is unimpeachable as a goal, opposing a bill or program labeled "election integrity" can be cast as opposing honest elections — a rhetorical trap. Mechanically, the movement operationalizes the frame through: mass challenges to voter registrations (often generated by error-prone software and list-matching); recruitment and training of partisan poll watchers and "vote challengers"; pressure on election officials to slow or refuse certification; and model legislation tightening ID rules, shortening early voting, and restricting mail ballots and drop boxes. Because the documented rate of in-person voter fraud is vanishingly small, the practical effect of these measures is to raise barriers to legitimate voting rather than to catch fraud.

## Application in this domain

In this vault's arc, "election integrity" is the rebranding layer of the [[Election Denial Infrastructure]] — the public-facing, sanitized vocabulary that lets an apparatus born from a false stolen-election claim present itself as good governance. It connects directly to [[Stop the Steal]] (the movement's mobilizing event), to [[Cleta Mitchell]] and the Election Integrity Network (its organized form), and to the [[Republican National Committee]]'s "election integrity" legal and poll-watching programs. The frame is also a disinformation mechanism: by maintaining permanent "integrity" structures, the movement keeps fraud salient regardless of evidence.

## Effectiveness / critique

- **Claimed effectiveness:** proponents claim the program restores public confidence in elections.
- **Empirical evidence:** confidence among Republican voters has fallen, not risen, since 2020 — the "integrity" program itself sustains the doubt it purports to cure; meanwhile audits, recounts, and courts found no fraud capable of changing the 2020 result. A widely cited finding from the right's own EagleAI/voter-roll software efforts is that they generated large numbers of false positives.
- **Academic critique:** voting-rights scholars and bodies such as the Brennan Center argue the framing is a textbook euphemism — "integrity" measures correlate empirically with reduced turnout among targeted groups, not with detected fraud. Defenders dispute that characterization; this vault treats the disjunction between the label and the documented effect as the core contested claim. `#claim/disputed`
