# Voter suppression

Practices that make voting more difficult, particularly for specific demographic groups. Includes:

- Voter ID requirements
- Polling-location closures
- Registration purges
- Restricted absentee/early voting windows
- Felon disenfranchisement
- Gerrymandering (structural variant)

## Vault relevance

The contemporary debate frames voter-suppression measures as either:
- Legitimate election-integrity reforms (the conservative framing)
- Targeted suppression of disfavored voter demographics (the liberal framing)

Both framings cite real evidence. The vault treats specific instances on their merits and tracks how the asymmetric demographic impact (which voter groups are affected) correlates with the [[Demographic Defensiveness Thesis]] - the coalition that has lost the popular vote in 8 of 9 recent presidential elections has substantial incentive to constrain participation by demographics likely to vote against it.
