# Astroturfing

Coordinated effort to manufacture the appearance of authentic grassroots support for a position, candidate, or product. Named for the difference between real grass and AstroTurf.

## Mechanisms

- Sock-puppet social media accounts amplifying coordinated talking points
- Paid letter-writing operations to legislators
- Funded "grassroots" organizations with corporate or political-party patronage hidden
- Bot networks pushing trending hashtags
- Coordinated review-bombing campaigns

## Documented cases (Tier 1)

- [[Tea Party]] origins - significant Koch network funding documented through Americans for Prosperity (Mayer, "Dark Money", 2016)
- 2010-2014 climate denial campaigns funded by ExxonMobil and Koch sources (UCS analysis)
- 2016 [[Internet Research Agency]] "BlackTivist", "Texas Heart" sock-puppet pages
- 2018-2024 [[Team Jorge]] (Israeli firm) operated 30,000+ personas across platforms per Forbidden Stories investigation

## Significance for this vault

The line between astroturfing and ordinary political organizing matters because the [[COS movement]] is sometimes characterized as a manufactured top-down operation rather than a grassroots one. Public records (state legislative call applications, Convention of States Action 990s, donor disclosures) bear on which framing is closer to true.
