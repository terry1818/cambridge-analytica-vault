## Definition

**Bespoke realities** are fragmented, group-specific consensus realities — tailored information environments constructed by propagandists for distinct audience communities. In [[Renée DiResta]]'s framework (*Invisible Rulers*, 2024), modern information operations do not seek to persuade the entire public of a single false narrative. Instead, they manufacture separate, internally consistent belief packages for different tribes: each group inhabits a custom-built version of reality that reinforces its existing identity and alienates it from the factual frameworks shared by other groups. The result is not a battle over one contested truth, but the erosion of any shared epistemic commons.

## Origin / history

- DiResta coined or systematically developed the term in *Invisible Rulers: The People Who Turn Lies Into Reality* (PublicAffairs, 2024).
- The phenomenon it describes is rooted in the structural logic DiResta traces back to [[Edward Bernays]]'s "engineering of consent" — mass persuasion — but represents its digital-era transformation: instead of broadcasting one message to the masses, propagandists can micro-target fragmented audiences at scale.
- Antecedent concepts include:
  - **Filter bubbles** (Eli Pariser, 2011): algorithmic curation producing personalized information environments.
  - **Epistemic bubbles / echo chambers** (academic literature, 2010s): communities insulated from corrective information.
  - **Post-truth** (widely used post-2016): public discourse in which objective facts are less influential than appeals to emotion.
  - **Active measures** (Soviet / Russian doctrine, [[Thomas Rid]]): destabilizing target societies by amplifying existing divisions rather than introducing new ideas.
- Bespoke realities is more specific than these predecessors: it foregrounds the *intentional construction* of tailored belief environments by actors with operational goals, not merely the passive filtering produced by algorithms.

## How it works

1. **Audience segmentation:** Propagandists (state actors, political operatives, or organic influencer networks) identify discrete audience communities with distinct information diets and identity-formation logics.
2. **Content tailoring:** Narratives are crafted or adapted to resonate with each segment's existing beliefs, fears, and in-group/out-group structures. The same underlying operation produces different content for different communities.
3. **Platform amplification:** Social media algorithms reward engagement over accuracy, amplifying emotionally resonant content within communities, deepening the segmentation.
4. **Cross-community incompatibility:** Because each community's bespoke reality is internally consistent but contradicts others', corrective information from outside the community reads as propaganda or attack — immunizing the audience against correction.
5. **Cumulative fragmentation:** Over time, the sum of bespoke realities is a public that cannot agree on basic facts, making democratic deliberation dysfunctional.

## Application in this domain

Bespoke realities is directly applicable to the CA/SCL operational model:
- [[Cambridge Analytica]]'s [[psychographic microtargeting]] was an early operationalized version of the same segmentation logic: different messages for OCEAN-scored audience clusters.
- The [[Internet Research Agency (IRA)]] 2016 operation constructed separate content streams for Black Lives Matter supporters, gun rights advocates, evangelical Christians, and other communities — each a bespoke reality reinforcing tribal identity and political disengagement.
- In the AI era, the capacity to construct bespoke realities at scale has expanded dramatically: LLMs can generate audience-specific content at near-zero marginal cost.
- The concept explains why fact-checking has limited effectiveness: corrections delivered in one reality-frame are invisible to or dismissed by audiences in another.

## Examples

- **IRA 2016 operation:** Separate Facebook groups, Twitter accounts, and Instagram channels built for mutually incompatible audiences (Black Americans, white conservatives, evangelical Christians, Second Amendment advocates) — each served a distinct bespoke reality.
- **COVID-19 infodemic:** Vaccine hesitancy narratives customized for libertarian, religious, and health-freedom communities simultaneously.
- **Election denial (2020–present):** Distinct variants of "stolen election" narratives calibrated for different conservative sub-communities (evangelical, MAGA nationalist, constitutional originalist).

## Effectiveness / critique

- The concept is analytically powerful for explaining why the modern information environment is resistant to correction at the individual level.
- Critics note that "bespoke realities" may overweight intentional construction relative to emergent fragmentation produced by algorithmic curation without coordinated operators.
- The concept implies that counter-strategies must address the segmentation infrastructure (platform architecture, recommendation algorithms) rather than individual false claims — a systemic rather than content-moderation solution.
