# Russian interference in the 2016 US election

A coordinated Russian state operation aimed at the 2016 US presidential election. Established by the US Intelligence Community Assessment (January 6, 2017), the [[Mueller Report]] (March 2019), and the bipartisan [[Senate Intelligence Committee 2016 Russia Report]] (5 volumes, 2017-2020).

## Two prongs

**Prong 1 - Information operations ([[Internet Research Agency]])**

- St. Petersburg-based troll farm funded by [[Yevgeny Prigozhin]]
- Created hundreds of fake US-persona social media accounts
- Indictment Feb 16, 2018 (Mueller): 13 Russians charged
- Goal per Mueller: sow discord, denigrate Clinton, support Trump

**Prong 2 - Hack and leak ([[GRU Unit 26165]] and [[GRU Unit 74455]])**

- Hacked DNC, DCCC, John Podesta emails spring/summer 2016
- Released through DCLeaks, Guccifer 2.0, and [[WikiLeaks]]
- Indictment July 13, 2018 (Mueller): 12 GRU officers charged

## Assessed motivation

ICA January 2017: "Putin and the Russian Government aspired to help President-elect Trump's election chances when possible by discrediting Secretary Clinton."

Senate Intelligence Committee Vol. 5 (August 2020) confirmed the ICA's conclusions with bipartisan agreement.

## What was NOT established

- Mueller did not establish a criminal conspiracy between the Trump campaign and the Russian government
- He DID document multiple contacts (Trump Tower meeting June 2016; [[Paul Manafort]] / [[Konstantin Kilimnik]] data transfers; [[George Papadopoulos]] - [[Joseph Mifsud]])
- "No collusion" is a political framing; "insufficient evidence to charge conspiracy" is the legal finding
