# Article V Convention (constitutional)

Procedure in Article V of the US Constitution permitting amendments to be proposed by either (a) two-thirds of both houses of Congress OR (b) a convention called by Congress on application of two-thirds of state legislatures.

## Text of Article V (operative phrase)

"The Congress, whenever two thirds of both Houses shall deem it necessary, shall propose Amendments to this Constitution, or, on the Application of the Legislatures of two thirds of the several States, shall call a Convention for proposing Amendments..."

## Has never been used

Every constitutional amendment to date (1-27) has been proposed by Congress under the first procedure. The convention-of-states method has been theoretically available since 1789 but never triggered.

## Ratification requirement

Once proposed (by either method), amendments must be ratified by 3/4 of states (38 of 50) to take effect.

## Currently active applications

The [[Convention of States Action]] - led campaign has produced [[19 state applications]] under a specific subject-limited form. Additional applications under different subject framings exist (balanced budget amendment applications, etc.) but the count of unrestricted Article V applications is contested.

## The threshold gap

19 applications (COS form) plus potentially overlapping balanced-budget-amendment applications totaling ~28-30 by some counts. The 34-state threshold has not been crossed under any defensible counting method.

## Why this matters for the vault

Article V is the structural mechanism the [[COS movement]] is using to seek constitutional amendments that have not achievable under the Congress-proposes path. It is the only legal route to amendments without congressional approval. The vault treats the COS effort as the most significant non-electoral structural-power project in contemporary US politics.
