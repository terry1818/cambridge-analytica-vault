# Education-capture

## Definition

**Education-capture** is this vault's term for the strategy of removing public education from democratic and professional control so that schooling produces favored ideological and market outcomes. It works through several converging instruments: diverting public funds to private and religious schools via vouchers and education savings accounts; legislating curriculum content directly; degrading professional autonomy (teacher credentialing, academic freedom, tenure); and deliberately defunding public schools and the federal Department of Education. It is the application of the broader [[constitutional capture]] logic to a single, high-leverage institution — the one that shapes the next generation of voters.

## Origin / history

**Intellectual roots (1950s–1960s):** Economist Milton Friedman proposed school vouchers in 1955 as a way to introduce market competition into education. The public-choice tradition of [[James M. Buchanan]] and [[Gordon Tullock]] reframed public schooling as a site of [[Government failure]] and [[Rent-seeking]] — captured, on this account, by teachers' unions and bureaucracies rather than serving children.

**The privatization campaign (1990s–2020s):** Investigative reporting (Save Our Schools Arizona, Jacobin, education researchers) documents a decades-long, donor-funded "privatization playbook" associated with the Walton, DeVos, and [[Koch Network]] funders and circulated through [[American Legislative Exchange Council]] model legislation. Betsy DeVos's tenure as U.S. Secretary of Education (2017–2021) elevated choice and privatization to federal policy.

**The Rufo turn (2020s):** Conservative activist [[Christopher Rufo]], a Manhattan Institute senior fellow, articulated the strategy with unusual candor. He stated that "to get to universal school choice, you really need to operate from a premise of universal public school distrust" — explicitly recommending that activists manufacture anger at public schools (via campaigns against "critical race theory" and other targets) to drive a privatization movement. By 2025, Rufo's social-media posts were reportedly driving cuts at the U.S. Department of Education.

## How it works

Education-capture combines four mechanisms:

1. **Fiscal diversion** — vouchers and education savings accounts move per-pupil public money to private and religious schools, structurally shrinking the public system's funding base.
2. **Curriculum legislation** — state laws mandating or banning specific content (book restrictions, CRT bans, mandated curricula) transfer pedagogical authority from educators to legislatures.
3. **De-professionalization** — removing credentialing requirements, weakening tenure, and attacking academic freedom reduce the capacity of educators to resist political direction.
4. **Manufactured distrust** — coordinated campaigns (the CRT panic; school-board takeovers) generate the public anger that makes the other three mechanisms politically feasible. Rufo's "universal public school distrust" formula names this step directly.

Once a state has converted to a voucher-dominated system and stripped professional protections, reversal is structurally difficult — a captured outcome, not a reversible policy.

## Application in this domain

Education-capture sits at the intersection of the vault's patron wing and theocratic wing. For the patron wing it is privatization and market expansion; for the [[Christian nationalism]] / theocratic wing it is the chance to channel public money into religious schooling and to mandate favored curricula. The CRT panic — which [[Christopher Rufo]] explicitly described as a deliberately constructed political weapon — is a documented case of manufactured cultural grievance being engineered to serve a structural privatization goal, paralleling the vault's broader interest in psychographic and disinformation-driven manipulation.

## Examples

- [[Christopher Rufo]] — articulated and executed the "manufactured distrust → privatization" strategy.
- [[American Legislative Exchange Council]] — circulates model voucher and education-savings-account bills.
- Betsy DeVos / U.S. Department of Education (2017–2021) — federal elevation of choice and privatization.
- 2023–2025 wave of state "universal" voucher programs (Arizona, Florida, and others).

## Effectiveness / critique

- **Claimed effectiveness:** Proponents argue choice improves outcomes and parental control.
- **Empirical evidence:** Mixed-to-negative — multiple studies of large voucher programs found flat or declining academic results; the strategy is also broadly unpopular in polling (Jacobin/education reporting).
- **Critique:** Critics argue it drains and destabilizes public schools, subsidizes religious instruction with public funds, and replaces democratic and professional governance with ideological and market control. The "manufactured distrust" step is criticized as deliberate disinformation.
