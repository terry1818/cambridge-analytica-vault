# Content moderation

Practice of platform companies removing, demoting, or labeling user-generated content for violations of platform policies. Includes hate speech, harassment, misinformation, terrorism, CSAM, copyright violations.

## Vault relevance

Content moderation became a structural battleground in the 2020-2024 period:
- 2020-2021: Major platforms removed COVID-19 disinformation and Stop-the-Steal content
- 2021-2024: Twitter Files framing and Murthy v. Missouri litigation challenged the legality of government-platform coordination
- 2024+: [[Election Integrity Partnership]] (EIP) and [[Stanford Internet Observatory]] effectively curtailed under political pressure
- The structural effect: 2024 election cycle had substantially less institutional capacity for tracking and responding to disinformation than 2020
