## Definition

The Ashli Babbitt martyrdom narrative is the post-January 6, 2021 political-religious construction that frames Ashli Babbitt — shot and killed by a Capitol Police officer while attempting to breach a barricaded door in the US Capitol during the January 6 insurrection — as a martyr, innocent victim, and sacred symbol of MAGA resistance. Jeff Sharlet's *The Undertow: Scenes from a Slow Civil War* (2023) documents the rapid emergence and active cultivation of this narrative as the third theological stage of what Sharlet calls the "Church of Trump," following the stages of prophet and king: the age of martyrs.

Sharlet emphasizes that this is not a "death cult" but an "innocence cult" — the central psychological and political work of the Babbitt narrative is not the glorification of death but the aggressive erasure of history: the rioter's documented actions, criminal past, and explicit intent to breach the Capitol are systematically effaced in favor of an image of innocent victimhood.

## Origin / History

Babbitt, a 35-year-old Air Force veteran with a history of debts and a restraining order, was shot in the neck while attempting to climb through a broken window into a barricaded hallway near the House chamber. She died at the scene.

The martyrdom narrative emerged within hours. By January 7, Trump and allies were already framing her death as murder. The narrative accelerated through specific institutional channels:

- **Trump's amplification**: Trump repeatedly mentioned Babbitt in public statements and on social media as an example of government brutality
- **Sidney Powell, Mike Flynn, and allied figures** promoted the narrative in far-right media
- **Right-wing media ecosystem**: OAN, Newsmax, and aligned social media accounts constructed and disseminated the martyr imagery
- **Merchandise and iconography**: Babbitt's image appeared on T-shirts, flags, and murals in the spaces Sharlet documents — roadside shrines, rally gatherings, and private homes of Trump supporters

The Capitol Police officer who shot Babbitt (Lt. Michael Byrd, later publicly identified) was subjected to a sustained harassment and death-threat campaign. His identity was leaked and amplified by right-wing media. Two separate Justice Department investigations found the shooting legally justified under the circumstances. Trump subsequently pardoned multiple January 6 convicted rioters in January 2025 and spoke of Babbitt in terms that reinforced her martyr status.

## How It Works

The narrative operates through several mechanisms:

1. **Selective presentation of facts**: Babbitt's documented criminal history (stalking-related restraining order), her active participation in the breach, and the documented chaos of the Capitol attack are omitted; she is presented as a patriotic bystander
2. **Attribution of innocence through identity markers**: Her Air Force service, her whiteness, her status as a Trump supporter — these identity markers are used to construct innocence independent of specific actions
3. **Enemy construction**: The narrative requires a villain; the Capitol Police officer, the Biden administration, and "deep state" actors provide one
4. **Sacred iconography**: Religious imagery is explicitly applied to Babbitt — shrines, prayer cards, references to her as "fallen" in ways that invoke soldier/saint language
5. **Reinforce the larger lost-cause narrative**: The martyr becomes proof that January 6 was a righteous cause and that the state response was illegitimate persecution

Sharlet's framing — "innocence cult" — captures the psychological function: the narrative protects participants from confronting what actually happened on January 6 by displacing accountability onto the state.

## Effectiveness / Critique

The martyr narrative has proven durable within MAGA circles. Polling consistently shows high proportions of Republican voters view the January 6 participants sympathetically; the Babbitt narrative is one mechanism for that sympathy. External to the MAGA ecosystem, the narrative has essentially no purchase: law enforcement, legal adjudication, and mainstream journalism have uniformly found the shooting legally justified and the martyrdom construction factually false.

The narrative is most accurately assessed as a **disinformation product** that functions as **political theology** — it simultaneously provides a religious-emotional anchor for MAGA identity and a justification for continued political conflict framed as righteous resistance.
