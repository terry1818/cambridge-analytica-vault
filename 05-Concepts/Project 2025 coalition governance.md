# Project 2025 Coalition Governance

## Definition

The structural model through which the Heritage Foundation coordinated more than 100 conservative partner organizations to develop a unified governance agenda for a future Republican administration. Rather than a single think tank producing a policy blueprint, Project 2025 operated as a **distributed coalition infrastructure**: partner organizations contributed policy sections, personnel recommendations, and implementation expertise across their respective domains, all organized under four structural pillars and a shared timeline. David Graham's *The Project* (2025) documents how this coordination model represented an unprecedented degree of pre-transition governance planning in American conservative movement history.

## Origin / History

Project 2025 was formally launched by the Heritage Foundation in 2022, building on Heritage's prior "Mandate for Leadership" tradition (first produced for the Reagan transition in 1981). The scale of coalition-building was new: Heritage announced reaching 75 coalition partners, then 100, by 2023. The coalition included organizations spanning the religious right, the economic libertarian wing, the nationalist/populist wing, and more conventional establishment conservative institutions — reflecting and formalized the three-wing coalition structure the vault documents.

David A. Graham, a staff writer at *The Atlantic*, documented the project's structure and implementation in *The Project: How Project 2025 Is Reshaping America* (2025), written after the Trump administration took office in January 2025 and began implementing the agenda.

## How It Works

**The four pillars**:

1. **Policy agenda** (*Mandate for Leadership: The Conservative Promise*, 900 pages): Policy prescriptions for every federal agency and department, contributed by subject-matter experts from partner organizations. Contributors represented many organizations, not just Heritage.

2. **Personnel recruitment** (Presidential Personnel Database): A pre-built database of vetted, loyalty-screened candidates for executive branch positions. This addressed the first-term failure mode where Trump appointees did not share his agenda. Estimated 20,000+ screened candidates.

3. **Training** (Presidential Administration Academy): Online and in-person training programs preparing vetted conservative candidates to immediately function in executive branch roles. Removed the learning curve that slowed first-term implementation.

4. **180-Day Playbook**: A detailed operational guide for the first six months of a new administration — sequencing executive orders, agency actions, and personnel moves for maximum speed before political resistance could organize.

**Coalition coordination mechanism**:

Partner organizations formally affiliated, contributing their domain expertise and personnel lists. The Advisory Board included leaders from across the coalition spectrum. The governance model allowed each organization to maintain independent identity and donor relations while contributing to the shared infrastructure — creating network effects without requiring formal merger.

**Post-election implementation**: After Trump's 2024 victory, many Project 2025 architects and alumni moved directly into administration roles, including Russ Vought (OMB), Paul Dans (who ran Project 2025's personnel database), and others. The 180-Day Playbook began being executed from January 20, 2025.

## Application in This Domain

This concept is central to the vault's analysis of how the three-wing coalition operationalizes governance. The coalition governance model resolves the coordination problem: disparate wings (religious right, tech libertarians, economic conservatives, nationalists) with different ultimate agendas can work together on a shared platform without resolving their ideological differences, because the platform is primarily procedural (who gets hired, which agencies get restructured) rather than doctrinal. The vault should test: once implementation begins, which partners' priorities are actually implemented, and do the coalition fissures (theocratic wing vs. tech libertarian wing, for example) emerge during implementation?

## Examples

- Heritage Foundation's public announcement of reaching 100 coalition partners (2023): https://www.heritage.org/press/project-2025-reaches-100-coalition-partners-continues-grow-preparation-next-president
- Policy contributors included: Alliance Defending Freedom, Claremont Institute, American Principles Project, FRC Action, Center for Immigration Studies, and dozens of others representing each wing.
- Paul Dans oversaw the personnel database; he described it as the most systematic conservative personnel operation in US history.
- Schedule F implementation (Jan 2025), mass agency restructuring, and DOJ/FBI personnel purges followed the 180-Day Playbook sequencing.

## Effectiveness / Critique

- **Unprecedented in scope**: Even critics acknowledge the coalition governance model was more operationally sophisticated than prior conservative transition efforts.
- **Distance claimed during campaign**: Trump and his campaign publicly distanced themselves from Project 2025 during the 2024 general election when it drew negative media attention. Post-election, the distance evaporated — most analysts consider the campaign disavowal a tactical move, not a substantive one.
- **Internal tensions**: The coalition's breadth meant significant internal disagreement on issues like immigration enforcement targets, social policy priorities, and economic nationalism vs. free trade. Whether the coalition maintains cohesion through implementation is an open empirical question.
- **Democratic response**: The ACLU, state attorneys general, and various civil society organizations have challenged specific Project 2025 implementations in court; the legal durability of the coalition governance model depends partly on how much the Supreme Court constrains or enables it.
