# Astroturfing

## Definition

**Disguising a paid, orchestrated, or commercially-motivated political message as a spontaneous grassroots movement**. The term is a pun on "AstroTurf" (synthetic grass) coined by Senator Lloyd Bentsen in 1985 when criticizing apparent grassroots opposition to insurance regulation that was actually insurance-industry-funded.

## How it works

Classic astroturfing involves:

1. **Identify desired political outcome** for paying client
2. **Create apparent grassroots organization** to advocate for that outcome
3. **Conceal funding sources** and orchestration
4. **Generate content** appearing to come from authentic concerned citizens
5. **Amplify** through media coverage of the "movement"

## Modern industrial-scale evolution

Contemporary [[Astroturf at Scale]] uses AI-generated personas, content, and amplification:

- AI-generated faces (defeat reverse-image-search)
- LLM-generated text (defeat perplexity detection)
- Cross-platform persona management ([[Persona Management]])
- Real-time engagement at scale

This is **structurally the same** as classic astroturfing but at orders of magnitude larger scale and much harder to detect.

## Why this matters for the vault

Astroturfing is the **methodological core** of CA-style operations. Recognizing astroturfing in the wild requires:

1. Following the money (who paid for this?)
2. Identifying organizational connections (who actually runs this?)
3. Comparing content patterns (is it really organic?)
4. Cross-referencing with known operators (CA, Team Jorge, etc.)
