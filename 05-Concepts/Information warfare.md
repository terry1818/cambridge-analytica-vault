# Information warfare

Operations targeting the information environment - what populations believe, what data they trust, and what decisions they make based on that information - as a form of conflict.

## Domains

- Strategic communication / public diplomacy
- Psychological operations (PSYOP / MISO in US doctrine)
- Cyber operations affecting information availability and integrity
- Influence operations (paid amplifiers, fake personas, content laundering)
- Electronic warfare affecting sensors and communications

## Why CA fits here

[[Cambridge Analytica]] was sold to clients as a behavioral-targeting service. In the context of an election with a hostile foreign info operation also running, CA's micro-targeted content (validated voter-list crossover for the 2016 Trump campaign and Brexit Leave campaign by separate reporting) functioned as a force-multiplier on a contested information environment whether or not CA was coordinating with Russian operations.

## What the public record establishes (Tier 1)

- CA worked the 2016 Trump campaign digital operation (Brad Parscale verified)
- CA's parent SCL Group ran defense / influence contracts in dozens of foreign countries
- CA used 87M Facebook profiles harvested via the Kogan myPersonality / "This Is Your Digital Life" app

## What the public record does NOT establish

- That CA and Russian operations were coordinated. No criminal coordination has been proven; Mueller did not charge a US-side conspiracy with Russia
