# 19 state applications (Convention of States)

Current count of state legislatures that have passed [[Convention of States Action]]-style Article V applications. The threshold for Congress to be compelled to call a convention is 34 (two-thirds of 50 states).

## Current applications (as of latest reporting)

(The exact count varies by definition - COS-aligned subject-limited applications vs broader Article V calls; this list reflects the COS Action subject-limited form):

Alabama, Alaska, Arizona, Arkansas, Florida, Georgia, Indiana, Louisiana, Mississippi, Missouri, Nebraska, North Carolina, North Dakota, Oklahoma, South Carolina, South Dakota, Tennessee, Texas, Utah, West Virginia, Wisconsin

## Recently active candidate states (Tier 2)

- Idaho - applications pending in legislature multiple sessions
- Iowa - close votes in recent sessions
- Kentucky - chamber-passed but not enacted
- Montana - active organizing
- Pennsylvania - has been a major target despite Democratic governor

## Structural significance

The legal status of subject-limited Article V applications is contested. Three positions exist:

1. **COS Action position**: Applications must be on the same subject; 34 same-subject applications would trigger a convention limited to that subject
2. **Opposition position (some originalists, some progressives)**: Article V does not authorize subject-limited conventions; once a convention is called it can propose anything
3. **Middle position**: Subject limitations are legally binding on the convention but enforceable only retrospectively if proposed amendments exceed scope

This unresolved legal question is the "runaway convention" risk that COS supporters dispute and opponents emphasize.
