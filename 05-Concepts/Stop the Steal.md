# Stop the Steal

Movement and slogan asserting that the 2020 US presidential election was stolen from Donald Trump. Used as the organizing frame for the November 2020 - January 2021 effort that culminated in the [[January 6 Capitol Attack]].

## Origins

- The hashtag predates 2020 - Roger Stone used "Stop the Steal" during the 2016 Republican primary
- November 4, 2020+ revival driven by [[Ali Alexander]], [[Roger Stone]], and Trump campaign affiliates
- Rallies coordinated through StopTheSteal.us and a Facebook group that grew to 320K members before Facebook removed it Nov 5, 2020

## What every court found (Tier 1)

Trump campaign and allied lawsuits in 2020-2021: 60+ filed, 0 succeeded on the merits in establishing election fraud sufficient to overturn results. Includes the Supreme Court declining Texas v. Pennsylvania (December 11, 2020) on standing grounds.

Independent audits (Maricopa County "Cyber Ninjas" audit, Georgia ballot review) confirmed the certified results.

## J6 Committee finding (Tier 1)

The Committee documented that Trump was repeatedly informed by his own DOJ, White House Counsel, campaign data team, and senior advisers that the fraud claims were false - and continued to make the claims publicly. See [[J6 Committee Final Report]].
