# NAR international networks

## Definition

"NAR international networks" refers to the distinctive organizational form of the [[New Apostolic Reformation]]: instead of denominations with formal membership, hierarchies, and bylaws, the NAR is constituted by *loosely structured, relational networks of "apostles"* — leaders who exercise translocal spiritual authority — and the churches, ministries, and lesser leaders aligned under them. These networks are transnational by design: an apostle in one country oversees churches and prophets across many. They are the institutional skeleton of the movement and a principal reason NAR theology spreads so rapidly across the global South and North.

## Origin / history

The missiologist [[C. Peter Wagner]] coined the term "New Apostolic Reformation" in 1996, presenting it not as a new movement he founded but as a name for an existing phenomenon he had observed. Wagner argued that a "Second Apostolic Age" had begun and that contemporary churches were taking the form of "loosely structured apostolic networks" rather than denominations. He saw these new apostolic churches as "the fastest-growing segment of Christianity" in "virtually every region of the world."

Wagner then built network infrastructure to match the theory. He founded the **International Coalition of Apostles** (ICA, 1999; later the International Coalition of Apostolic Leaders), a membership body for self-identified apostles; the **Eagles' Vision Apostolic Team**; and other coordinating bodies, and served as a vice president of **Global Spheres, Inc.**, a successor coordinating organization. Through these structures the NAR's ideas — dominionism, the [[Seven Mountains Mandate]], strategic-level [[spiritual warfare]] — propagated through the networks, and feedback from the networks shaped the ideas in turn.

## How it works

The mechanics of NAR networks differ sharply from denominational Christianity:

1. **Apostolic authority is relational, not bureaucratic.** An apostle's reach depends on voluntary alignment — churches and prophets "submit" relationally rather than through legal membership. This makes the networks fluid, hard to map, and easy to expand or reconfigure.
2. **Translocal and transnational by structure.** Apostolic oversight crosses congregational, regional, and national lines. A single apostolic network can span continents.
3. **Prophets and apostles paired.** Networks pair "apostles" (governing authority) with "prophets" (revelatory authority), giving the movement both an organizational and a charismatic-legitimacy engine.
4. **Decentralized and deniable.** Because there is no central registry, no creed, and no membership roll, the NAR can be simultaneously coordinated and diffuse — and its leaders can plausibly deny that any single "NAR organization" exists. Scholars (e.g., Matthew Taylor) treat this network form itself as a key feature, not a bug.

## Application in this domain

For this vault, the network form explains how NAR political theology achieves *organized scale without a visible organization*. The same apostolic networks that coordinate revivals and "spiritual mapping" can be activated for political mobilization: prayer campaigns in swing states, the post-2020 election-denial effort, mass worship events, and tours such as [[ReAwaken America]] all run partly along network lines rather than through a formal institution. Internationally, the networks give the movement a footprint across Latin America, Africa, and Asia — exporting dominionist political theology and importing leaders and energy. The decentralized structure also frustrates accountability: there is no headquarters to investigate, no membership list to subpoena. Understanding the NAR's political reach therefore requires understanding the network as the unit of analysis.

## Examples

- **International Coalition of Apostles / International Coalition of Apostolic Leaders** — Wagner's flagship apostolic membership body (founded 1999).
- **Global Spheres, Inc.** — successor coordinating organization Wagner was associated with.
- **Eagles' Vision Apostolic Team** — one of Wagner's network bodies.
- Individual apostolic networks led by figures such as [[Dutch Sheets]], [[Lance Wallnau]], Cindy Jacobs, and Che Ahn — each a node spanning many churches and nations.
- [[ReAwaken America]] — a touring platform whose speaker rosters and church venues overlap with NAR networks.

## Effectiveness / critique

- **Claimed effectiveness:** NAR proponents claim the network model is the God-ordained, fastest-growing form of the global church.
- **Empirical evidence:** Charismatic Christianity is indeed growing rapidly worldwide, and apostolic-network forms are real and widespread; precise NAR membership numbers cannot be counted, by design.
- **Academic critique:** Religion scholars note the difficulty of studying a movement that denies it is a movement; some NAR-associated leaders reject the "NAR" label entirely. Mainstream evangelical and Pentecostal theologians criticize the restoration of governing "apostles" as unscriptural. Researchers of democracy warn that the network form lets dominionist political theology mobilize at scale while remaining diffuse and unaccountable. Whether "NAR" is a coherent single movement or a journalistic/scholarly construct is itself contested. `#claim/disputed`
