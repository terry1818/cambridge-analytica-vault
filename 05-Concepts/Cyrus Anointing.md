# Cyrus Anointing

## Definition

The Cyrus Anointing is a prophetic theological framing — originating in the [[New Apostolic Reformation]] but spreading broadly across charismatic evangelical networks — that identifies Donald Trump with the ancient Persian king Cyrus the Great, who is described in Isaiah 45 as God's "anointed" despite being a pagan ruler. The framework resolves a central tension for religious-right Trump supporters: how can a thrice-married, profane, non-churchgoing real-estate mogul be God's choice for the presidency? The Cyrus frame answers that God regularly uses "flawed vessels" for divine purposes — in this case, delivering conservative Christians from the captivity of secular-progressive governance, just as Cyrus delivered the Israelites from Babylonian captivity. The anointing implies divine appointment, protection, and a mandate that must not be obstructed by earthly or institutional means.

## Origin / History

**Biblical basis:** King Cyrus of Persia (c.600–530 BCE) is the only non-Israelite ruler referred to in Hebrew scripture as God's "mashiach" (anointed/messiah). Isaiah 44:28–45:1 describes Cyrus as the one who will rebuild Jerusalem and release the exiles from Babylon. The theological hook for modern application is that God chose a pagan — someone outside the covenant people — to accomplish a redemptive historical purpose. This made the Cyrus analogy available for exactly the moral-character problem Trump posed to evangelical voters.

**2015–2016 popularization:** The contemporary Trump-Cyrus framing is primarily attributed to [[Lance Wallnau]], a NAR figure and [[Seven Mountains Mandate]] theorist. Wallnau reports that early in 2016, upon meeting Trump, he felt an "anointing." After seeing a meme connecting Trump to the 45th presidency, Wallnau says God directed him to Isaiah 45 (the 45th chapter), which opens with the Cyrus anointing. In October 2016, one month before the election, Wallnau published "Why I Believe Trump Is the Prophesied President," explicitly framing Trump as "the 45th president...meant to be an Isaiah 45 Cyrus."

**Spread and institutionalization:** [[Paula White]] (Trump's personal pastor and liaison to the evangelical community), Franklin Graham, and dozens of NAR apostles and prophets rapidly adopted the framework. It became the dominant theological legitimation device in charismatic/NAR circles for the 2016 and 2020 Trump campaigns. By 2020, more than fifty NAR-aligned prophets had publicly predicted Trump's re-election, most framing it through the Cyrus lens.

## How It Works

The Cyrus Anointing operates at several political-theological levels simultaneously:

1. **Moral bypass:** It explicitly exempts Trump from the character tests normally applied to evangelical candidates. Since Cyrus was a pagan, Trump's sexual scandals, business ethics, and lack of Christian practice are theologically irrelevant. God "anoints" whom he chooses.

2. **Divine mandate:** Anointing in the biblical tradition implies a specific divine task. For NAR apostles and prophets, this task is clearing the way for the [[Seven Mountains Mandate]] — installing Christian governance across the seven spheres of cultural influence. Trump is not the end goal but the bulldozer.

3. **Prophetic stakes:** When NAR prophets publicly declare an anointing, their credibility (and in NAR theology, their legitimacy as prophets) is staked on the outcome. A prophesied Cyrus who loses means a failed prophet. This stakes dynamic is what made the 2020 election result so catastrophic for NAR institutional credibility and directly drove the election-denial movement.

4. **Election-denial engine:** Post-November 2020, the logic became: genuine prophecy cannot fail (God's word does not return void); therefore Trump did not actually lose; therefore the reported result must be fraud perpetrated by demonic forces. This theological reasoning — not merely political motivated reasoning — drove NAR apostles and prophets to demand "biblical" (non-legal, non-institutional) resolution and, according to Taylor's research, to mobilize their networks for January 6.

## Application in This Domain

The Cyrus Anointing was the theological infrastructure underpinning the entire 2020 election-overturn effort within NAR networks:

- [[Dutch Sheets]] framed the post-election period as a spiritual battle against forces attempting to reverse God's anointed choice, using "Operation Valkyrie" as a prayer-warfare construct against the stolen-election "demon."
- NAR apostles and prophets mobilized to contested swing states for prayer-and-prophecy meetings explicitly framed around restoring the Cyrus-anointed president.
- The White House reportedly reached out to NAR apostles after Biden was declared the winner, with officials encouraging Sheets and others to lead the spiritual-warfare campaign in swing states — connecting the theological frame to a political operation.
- On January 6, the presence of the [[Appeal to Heaven flag]] — flown by the same networks that promoted the Cyrus Anointing — visually linked the two frameworks: God's anointed was being enthroned, and the flag signaled divine license for the action.

**Post-2020 credibility crisis:** The mass prophetic failure created what Taylor and academic researchers describe as an institutional crisis. Rather than abandoning the anointing framework, leading NAR voices doubled down: the prophecy was not wrong; the fraud was real; the result would eventually be reversed. Some prophets offered partial apologies; others redoubled their claims. Per academic research in the *Journal of Pentecostal Studies* (2024), over fifty NAR-linked prophets had predicted Trump's 2020 victory, and the movement's response to the failure was to collapse prophecy into conspiracy theory rather than revise the theological framework.

**2024 and beyond:** The Cyrus frame persisted into Trump's 2024 campaign and re-election. Some NAR voices pivoted from Cyrus (a liberator) to more militaristic biblical figures (avengers, warriors), reflecting escalating dominion expectations.

## Examples

- Lance Wallnau, October 2016: published op-ed framing Trump as "Isaiah 45 Cyrus"
- Paula White: regular invocation of divine appointment in White House contexts; led NAR-style prayer at Trump's 2017 inauguration
- Franklin Graham: cited divine choice as basis for Trump support despite moral objections
- Dutch Sheets GiveHim15, Nov 2020–Jan 2021: Cyrus framing used to delegitimize Biden's election and frame election officials as agents of demonic obstruction
- Coin (produced 2019–2020): Trump-Cyrus commemorative coin widely circulated in evangelical communities, featuring both figures

## Effectiveness / Critique

**Effectiveness:** The Cyrus Anointing was highly effective as a mobilization technology. It resolved cognitive dissonance for millions of evangelical voters who found Trump personally repugnant. It created deep psychological investment in Trump's political survival (a prophetic community's credibility depended on his winning). It provided a theological framework that elevated Trump's re-election to a matter of divine obedience, making non-support or electoral-outcome acceptance feel like apostasy.

**Critique:** Academic and theological critics note that the analogy fundamentally misreads Isaiah 45 (Cyrus was anointed for a specific, bounded historical task, not a recurring divine mandate); that it evacuates the prophetic tradition of accountability; and that it collapses the distinction between divine sovereignty and prophetic prediction. From a democratic-governance standpoint, Taylor documents that the framework's logical structure — genuine prophecy cannot fail, therefore the result must be fraud, therefore extraordinary measures are sanctioned — is a theological driver of insurrectionary violence, not merely rhetorical excess.
