# Project 2025

A presidential transition project organized by the [[Heritage Foundation]] (2022-2024) to staff and direct a potential second Trump administration. Centerpiece: "Mandate for Leadership: The Conservative Promise" - 922-page policy blueprint.

## Components

- Mandate for Leadership policy book
- Presidential Personnel Database - resume bank screening for ideological alignment
- "Presidential Administration Academy" - online training for incoming political appointees
- 180-day playbook for each federal department

## Authors

- Lead editor: Paul Dans (former Trump OPM chief of staff)
- Over 350 contributing authors
- Coalition of ~110 conservative organizations
- Notable contributors: [[Russ Vought]], [[Kevin Roberts]], [[Stephen Miller]] (informally), [[John Eastman]] (election law section), and dozens of Trump-era officials

## Key policy threads

- Schedule F revival: reclassify tens of thousands of federal career staff as political appointees, allowing dismissal
- DOJ restructuring to reduce prosecutorial independence
- Department of Education abolition
- Aggressive immigration enforcement, expanded ICE authorities
- FCC content rules
- HHS rules limiting access to mifepristone

## Political distancing 2024 / actual implementation 2025

Trump publicly distanced himself from Project 2025 during the 2024 campaign. Post-inauguration, multiple Mandate authors took senior administration roles ([[Russ Vought]] returned as OMB Director). The personnel database and Schedule F directions have been substantially implemented.
