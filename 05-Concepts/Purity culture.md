## Definition

"Purity culture" is the retrospective term applied to a set of evangelical sexual ethics practices, institutions, and cultural products that dominated white evangelical youth ministry from approximately the early 1990s to the mid-2010s. Its core prescriptions were: pre-marital sexual abstinence (particularly virginity preservation); avoidance of romantic or physical contact outside courtship frameworks designed to lead to marriage; and the symbolic affirmation of these commitments through purity pledges, purity rings, and "True Love Waits" declarations.

The movement is analyzed by historian [[Kristin Du Mez]] in *Jesus and John Wayne: How White Evangelicals Corrupted a Faith and Fractured a Nation* (2020), which situates purity culture within a broader evangelical masculine and feminine identity project — one in which women's purity functioned as a site of male authority and community boundary maintenance, and male sexual control was framed as a form of Christian heroism.

## Origin / history

**True Love Waits (1992):** The Southern Baptist Convention's LifeWay Christian Resources launched True Love Waits, a campaign encouraging Christian teenagers to sign abstinence pledges. It rapidly spread across denominations. At its peak, hundreds of thousands of pledges were collected; in 1994, pledge cards were displayed on the National Mall in Washington, D.C.

**Joshua Harris — *I Kissed Dating Goodbye* (1997):** The most influential single text of purity culture. Harris was 21 when he wrote it; the book sold approximately 1.2 million copies. It argued against casual dating, advocating "courtship" (intentional, family-supervised, marriage-directed relationship formation) as the Christian alternative. Harris's book gave purity culture an intellectual framework beyond the pledge card.

**Institutional infrastructure:** Purity culture was not merely a marketing campaign — it was embedded in:
- Youth group curricula (WWJD bracelets, Silver Ring Thing programs, Acquire the Fire events)
- Christian contemporary music (the genre's audience was largely formed in the purity culture era)
- Summer camp and retreat programming
- Home school curricula (connections to the [[Segregationist origins of Christian schools|homeschool movement]] are documented — the same families using [[HSLDA]]-connected home education were often deep in purity culture content)
- Father-daughter "purity balls" (formal events at which daughters pledged their virginity to their fathers as symbolic protectors of their chastity)

**Decline:** By the mid-2010s, purity culture faced sustained criticism from within evangelical communities, particularly from women who described psychological harm from the shame-based messaging. Harris publicly retracted and apologized for *I Kissed Dating Goodbye* in 2018–2019 and later announced he was no longer a Christian. The documentary *I Survived I Kissed Dating Goodbye* (2018) documented former adherents' experiences of harm.

## How it works

Purity culture operated through several interlocking mechanisms:

1. **Identity formation:** Young people were invited to define themselves through a public commitment to sexual purity — making purity central to Christian identity, especially for girls.
2. **Community boundary maintenance:** The purity/impurity distinction became a marker of in-group vs. out-group status; adherents distinguished themselves from "the world."
3. **Shame as enforcement:** The movement attached significant social shame to sexual activity before marriage, particularly for girls; research has documented lasting psychological effects.
4. **Institutional reproduction:** Church youth groups, Christian schools, and home school communities reinforced the message in sustained, community-embedded ways — not merely through passive media consumption.
5. **Gender hierarchy:** The framework explicitly assigned men the role of spiritual protectors of women's purity and women the role of maintaining their own chastity partly for their future husband's benefit — embedding patriarchal gender roles in the language of spiritual virtue.

Purity culture is relevant to the vault's theocratic infrastructure mapping in several ways:

## Examples

- True Love Waits campaigns in Southern Baptist churches (1992–2000s)
- Joshua Harris, *I Kissed Dating Goodbye* (1997) — ~1.2 million copies sold
- Silver Ring Thing — secular/Christian hybrid abstinence program that received federal funding under Bush-era abstinence-only education policy
- Father-daughter purity balls — documented in conservative evangelical communities across the South and Midwest
- Christian modesty culture (modest dress codes framed as girls' responsibility to avoid causing men to sin) — pervasive in homeschool communities

## Effectiveness / critique

**As sexual education policy:** Extensive social science research found that abstinence pledge programs did not reliably delay sexual initiation and were associated with lower contraceptive use at sexual debut, leading to higher STI and unintended pregnancy rates among pledge-takers who broke their vows.

**As cultural formation:** The movement effectively built a generationally cohesive evangelical identity around sexual ethics — even as its specific prescriptions failed — and transmitted patriarchal gender norms in a theologically legitimized form.

**Internal critique (2015–present):** The #ChurchToo movement (2017, parallel to #MeToo) documented sexual abuse within evangelical communities that had promoted purity culture, arguing that the same power structures that enforced female purity also enabled male abuse. This critique has been a major driver of evangelical institutional crisis.

**Du Mez's critique:** *Jesus and John Wayne* argues that purity culture and the militant evangelical masculinity it was paired with (Promise Keepers, Mark Driscoll's Mars Hill, etc.) were co-constitutive: female sexual submission and male sexual authority were two sides of the same patriarchal coin.
