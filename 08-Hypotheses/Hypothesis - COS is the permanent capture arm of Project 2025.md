# Hypothesis: COS is the permanent-capture arm of Project 2025

## Claim

[[Convention of States Action]] and [[Project 2025]] are **not independent conservative initiatives**. They are **coordinated short-game / long-game arms of the same patron-class strategy**, designed to:

- **Short game (Project 2025):** rapid executive-branch capture, reversible by future presidents and courts; immediate policy gains
- **Long game (COS):** permanent constitutional capture, irreversible by any future executive or court; locks in the patron-class agenda for generations

The coordination is structural and intentional, not coincidental.

## Why it matters

If true, then:

- Defeating Project 2025 in 2028 (electoral reversal + executive-order rescission + court rulings) does **not** defeat the architecture
- The COS amendment vector continues regardless of Project 2025 outcomes
- A Democratic president in 2028 would have **zero authority** over a ratified Article V amendment
- The Left's strategy must address both vectors simultaneously, or it wins one war and loses the more important one

## Falsification tests for this hypothesis

The vault treats this hypothesis as confidence/high but explicitly falsifiable. Specific evidence that would refute or substantially weaken the hypothesis:

**Falsification test 1: COS movement remains stalled.** If the Convention of States Action state-application count remains well short of the 34-state trigger (it stands at 20 states as of January 2026) through 2027-2028, the hypothesis that COS is operationally close to triggering loses substantial weight. The hypothesis depends on the state-application momentum continuing; sustained stagnation falsifies the timeline component.

**Falsification test 2: Project 2025 implementation reverses.** If the post-2024 Trump 2.0 administration's Project 2025 implementation rate declines substantially or reverses (e.g., through judicial nullification of major EOs, congressional pushback that succeeds, or executive-branch personnel turnover that displaces the Project 2025 personnel pipeline), the COS-as-permanent-capture-arm framing weakens because the underlying programme it would entrench loses operational reality.

**Falsification test 3: Personnel overlap dissipates.** The [[P2025 COS Personnel Overlap]] connection note documents the substantial overlap between Project 2025 contributing-organisation personnel and Convention of States Action / Citizens for Self-Governance leadership. If that personnel overlap meaningfully dissipates over 2025-2027 (different personnel cohorts running the two operations), the operational-integration claim weakens.

**Falsification test 4: Constitutional drafting becomes pluralistic.** If the COS-affiliated constitutional drafting work (the Simulated Convention of States and adjacent drafting infrastructure) produces output that is meaningfully pluralistic across the conservative-libertarian-religious-right ideological space â€” rather than the more uniform integralist-leaning output the current drafting infrastructure has produced â€” the "permanent capture arm" framing loses force because COS would be a forum for genuine constitutional deliberation rather than a vehicle for specific policy locked-in.

## Cross-referencing evidence

Supporting evidence (which makes the hypothesis credible):

- Substantial documented personnel overlap between [[Convention of States Action]] / [[Citizens for Self-Governance]] leadership and [[Project 2025]] / [[Heritage Foundation]] leadership.
- Coordinated 501(c)(3) and 501(c)(4) funding flows across the two operations from a substantially overlapping donor base.
- Constitutional drafting work that has produced specific text (the Simulated Convention output) closely aligned with Project 2025 policy positions.
- The strategic alignment between the COS legal theory (Article V state-application requires 34 states, then convention proposes amendments, then 38 states must ratify) and the Project 2025 policy ambition (executive-branch policy changes that would benefit from durable constitutional entrenchment).

Counter-evidence (which weakens the hypothesis):

- The COS movement has been slow-moving across multiple decades. The 20-state count as of January 2026 reflects 15+ years of organising; reaching 34 may take additional decade-plus timeframes.
- The Article V mechanism itself is constitutionally contested. The "convention of states" interpretation has not been judicially tested at the federal level; if a federal court ultimately holds that Article V conventions must be plenary (not subject-limited), the COS strategy is operationally derailed.
- The Republican Party itself is internally divided on whether constitutional convention is desirable. Some prominent conservatives (the late Antonin Scalia, current ALEC-adjacent thinkers, parts of the Federalist Society leadership) have publicly opposed COS as too risky.

## 2026-05-22 correction: COS state metrics (revised)

An earlier revision of this note's falsification test and counter-evidence cited a "28-state count" as the Convention of States *application* count. That label was wrong. The fix below distinguishes the metrics that actually matter.

Four distinct numbers, all current as of 2026:

- **20** -- states that have passed a COSA Article V *application* (Kansas #20, January 2026; zero rescissions). Source: COSAction register + Congressional Record applications list ([[Source Wikipedia - List of Article V state applications]]).
- **28** -- states where Republicans control *both legislative chambers*. This is the operative ceiling: an Article V application is a legislative concurrent resolution that does not require the governor, so ~28 states are the realistic pool the COS drive can convert. Source: Ballotpedia / MultiState, 2026.
- **23** -- Republican trifectas (governor + both chambers), Ballotpedia April 2026. Full agenda control, but not specifically required to pass a COS application.
- **34 / 38** -- applications needed to trigger a convention / states needed to ratify any amendment produced.

The load-bearing point for this hypothesis: the operative metric is **GOP legislative control (28 states)**, not applications filed (20) -- the thesis is that GOP-controlled states convert to COS applications over time. But note the structural ceiling: **28 is still 6 short of the 34-state trigger.** At maximum *current* GOP legislative control the COS drive cannot reach the threshold without either (a) GOP chamber control expanding past 34 states, or (b) applications passing in states the GOP does not fully control. That gap is the hypothesis's strongest falsification surface and should be stated openly. Reconciliation detail: [[Full Arc - Research Findings]].
