# Hypothesis: The federal master database supersedes (and obsoletes) the CA-era influence-operations model

## Claim

The Cambridge Analytica scandal of 2016-2018 represented a **specific historical model**: external private consultants harvesting platform data (Facebook) under ToS-violating conditions to model voter psychographics for individual political campaigns. That model **cannot repeat in its 2016 form because it has been structurally superseded**. The 2024-2026 architecture moves the equivalent capability **inside the US federal government** via:

- [[Palantir Technologies]] as integration vendor ([[Federated Master Database]])
- [[Trump EO Eliminating Information Silos 2025-03-20]] as legal pretext
- [[Flock Safety]] + ICE / CBP partnerships as the distributed physical-sensor mesh
- [[Project 2025]]-trained executive personnel as the operating staff
- [[The Heritage Foundation]] as the policy authoring body
- [[Affinity Partners]] / Saudi PIF + Mercer-aligned capital as offshore/onshore patron tiers

## Why it matters

If true, threat-modeling and political-app design that still centers on "find the next CA" misses the actual capability stack. The threat surface has moved up one layer:

- **CA-era response:** investigate vendors, regulate platforms, GDPR-type laws
- **2025-era response:** challenge the EO, defend Privacy Act, contest agency-level Palantir contracts, regulate ALPR/Flock at state level, prosecute foreign-influence conflicts

- Palantir held **$1.3B+** in federal contracts since Trump 2.0 inauguration; 2025 federal spend $970M (nearly doubled prior year) ([[Source ACLU Palantir Deportation]])
