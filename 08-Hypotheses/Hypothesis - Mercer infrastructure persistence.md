# Hypothesis: The Mercer-funded political infrastructure operates as a coordinated long-term political-data ecosystem

## Claim

[[Robert Mercer]] and [[Rebekah Mercer]] do not fund a sequence of discrete political projects. They fund a **coordinated multi-layer infrastructure**:
- **Targeting tool:** [[Cambridge Analytica]] → [[Emerdata Limited]]
- **Opposition research engine:** [[Government Accountability Institute]]
- **Narrative distribution:** [[Breitbart News]]
- **Direct political vehicles:** [[Make America Number 1 Super PAC]]
- **Platform ownership:** [[Parler]]
- **Personnel network:** Bannon, Schweizer, Conway, and others rotating across vehicles

## Why it matters

If true, the post-CA period did not destroy this infrastructure — it shifted operations to less-visible parts of the stack. Tracking present-day Mercer-funded entities and personnel movements is essential to understanding ongoing operations.

## Supporting evidence

- Documented funding flows from Mercer Family Foundation to GAI, Breitbart, Heritage, etc.
- Personnel rotation: Bannon (Breitbart → CA VP → Trump campaign → White House → back to right-wing media), Schweizer (GAI → Breitbart podcast → Trump-aligned researcher)
- Rebekah Mercer's Parler involvement post-CA
- Jacquelyn James-Varga personnel pipeline from Mercer Family Foundation to SCL Group

## Current confidence

`#confidence/medium` — pattern is documented but coordinated-system thesis vs. opportunistic-funding thesis remains debated.
