# Hypothesis - Theocratic and Tech-Right convergence is structural

## Hypothesis statement

The post-2020 convergence between the theocratic wing (religious right, [[Catholic Integralism]] adjacent, [[New Apostolic Reformation]], [[Council for National Policy]] coordinated) and the tech-surveillance wing ([[Peter Thiel]] / [[Palantir Technologies]] / [[Elon Musk]] / [[DOGE]] / [[Marc Andreessen]] / [[a16z (Andreessen Horowitz)]]) represents a structurally new political configuration rather than an opportunistic tactical alliance. The convergence is durable because the two wings have substantive ideological overlap rather than merely shared tactical opponents.

## Confidence level: medium

## Evidence in favour

**Shared anti-liberalism.** Both wings have explicit critical positioning toward mainstream-progressive liberalism, with overlapping intellectual frameworks ([[Catholic Integralism]] and [[Curtis Yarvin]]-style neoreactionary thought converge in opposition to "the Cathedral" / "the regime" / "the secular liberal state").

**Personnel convergence.** Specific individuals occupy both wings: [[JD Vance]] (Catholic Integralism intellectual positioning + Thiel-Vance tech-right financial backing); [[Kevin Roberts]] (Catholic Integralism + Heritage operational leadership + Tech-Right intellectual engagement); [[Russell Vought]] (evangelical Protestant positioning + Project 2025 / DOGE operational coordination).

**Operational integration in Trump 2.0.** The [[Project 2025]] policy programme combines religious-right policy priorities (anti-abortion absolutism, traditional-family policy, scepticism of religious neutrality in public institutions) with tech-right operational priorities (federal data-aggregation via DOGE, AI-policy posture, anti-administrative-state programme). The operational integration is not opportunistic; the policy document explicitly treats the two priority categories as complementary.

**Shared institutional venues.** Both wings participate in the same conference circuit (First Things magazine events, Hoover Institution programming, certain Federalist Society events, the broader Catholic-and-conservative intellectual venue ecosystem). The shared participation has produced sustained personal-relationship infrastructure.

## Counter-evidence

**Theological-philosophical incompatibility.** The Catholic Integralism intellectual frame holds that political authority should be explicitly subordinated to religious authority. The Tech-Right intellectual frame ([[Effective Accelerationism]], [[Network States]], the broader Thiel-network reading culture) is largely secular or post-religious. The two intellectual traditions are not naturally compatible.

**Tactical-not-strategic alliance possibility.** The Tech-Right principals (Musk, Andreessen, Sacks) may be operationally aligned with the religious-right wing only because both wings are currently opposed to mainstream-progressive cultural politics. If the cultural-politics environment shifts, the Tech-Right could substantially de-align without operational loss.

**Personal-religious-identity gaps.** Most Tech-Right principals are not personally religious; the religious-right intellectual frame requires religious-identity participation that the Tech-Right does not provide. Vance is exceptional because his Catholic conversion gives him personal-religious-identity legitimacy that other Tech-Right principals lack.

## Falsification conditions

The hypothesis would be substantially refuted if:

- The 2025-2028 period sees substantial public disputes between Tech-Right and Religious-Right principals over specific policy questions where the two wings have to choose (e.g., AI development pace and content vs religious-right opposition to AI displacement of traditional roles).
- The post-2028 political environment shows substantial Tech-Right de-alignment from the broader conservative coalition (e.g., if Vance is not the 2028 Republican nominee, or if Andreessen / Musk publicly distance from the Republican coalition).
- The intellectual-venue overlap dissolves: if Catholic-Integralism intellectual venues stop engaging with Tech-Right principals and Tech-Right intellectual venues stop engaging with Catholic-Integralism principals, the alliance is operationally tactical rather than structural.

## Why this hypothesis is load-bearing

This hypothesis underwrites the vault's broader [[Hypothesis - Three-wing coalition]] analysis. If the convergence is structural, the three-wing coalition is durable across multiple electoral cycles. If the convergence is tactical, the three-wing coalition is fragile and the post-2028 political environment will be substantially different.
