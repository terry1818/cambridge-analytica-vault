# Hypothesis - Developing World Test Bed

## Hypothesis

**[[SCL Group]] + [[Cambridge Analytica]] systematically used developing-world / less-scrutinized political contests as operational test beds** for the psychographic-targeting + influence-operations methodology that was subsequently deployed in high-profile Western political contests (US 2016, Brexit 2016, Mexico 2018, multiple Indian state elections).

**Implication:** The CA-era methodology was not invented in 2014-2015 for US 2016 deployment; rather, it represents the **culmination of 10+ years of operational refinement** in jurisdictions where:
1. Regulatory scrutiny was lower
2. Documentation of campaign tactics was sparse
3. Democratic accountability mechanisms were weaker
4. Local + international media attention was minimal

## Load-bearing evidence

- **SCL Defence existed since ~1993** doing military psyops contracts
- **SCL Elections established ~2003-2005** doing commercial political work
- **Documented SCL engagements** in: Trinidad + Tobago 2010, Kenya 2007 + 2013, Nigeria 2007 + 2015, Argentina 2015, multiple Indian state elections 2003-2012, Pakistan, multiple sub-Saharan African countries, multiple Caribbean states, Romania, Czechia, Latvia
- **Channel 4 footage** of Mark Turnbull + Alexander Nix discussing operational methodology shows extensive prior international experience
- **HindsightFiles 2020 cache** confirms multiple developing-world client engagements pre-CA-era
- **PRI Mexico defensive contract 2017-2018** indicates ongoing developing-world activity contemporary with US/Brexit work

## Counter-evidence

- Some claims about specific elections may be overstated (CA had incentive to inflate claimed track record for sales purposes)
- Some "developing-world" engagements may have been smaller in scope than later claimed
- Direct causation between CA work + election outcomes is often impossible to prove

## Escalation triggers

- Additional whistleblower disclosures detailing pre-2014 developing-world operations would strengthen
- Documentary evidence of methodology-transfer between developing-world + Western contexts would strengthen
- Discovery of analogous "test bed" methodology in successor architectures (Palantir + post-CA vendors) would strengthen
