# Hypothesis: Three-Wing Coalition

## Claim

The 2024-2026 power architecture documented in [[Lens - 2024-2026 Power Map]] is best understood as a **coalition of three distinct ideological wings**, not as a single unified movement. The three wings have **different ideologies, different goals, and different time horizons** but share an **aligned interest in defeating popular democratic majorities** that would constrain any of them.

The three wings:

| Wing | Core actors | What they want | Time horizon |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Patron class** | Mercer, Koch, Thiel, Heritage Foundation donors, Saudi PIF via [[Affinity Partners]] | Permanent regulatory rollback; constitutional capture of property / contract law; tax structure; deregulation | Multi-generational |
| **Theocratic / mass-mobilization** | Christian nationalists; [[Russ Vought]], [[Michael Farris]], [[Alliance Defending Freedom|ADF]], [[The Fellowship]] / Family, [[Capitol Ministries]] / Drollinger, [[United in Purpose]] / Dallas, Heritage social-policy wing | End separation of church and state; restrict reproductive rights, LGBTQ+ rights, civil rights enforcement; education capture | Generational (Dobbs was proof of concept) |
| **Tech / surveillance** | Thiel network, [[Palantir Technologies]], [[Flock Safety]], DOGE personnel, A16Z | State-scale data integration; AI-mediated population control; private contractors as state apparatus | Decadal Ã¢â‚¬â€ betting on permanence |

## Why these are best understood as separate wings

The wings have **measurable internal tensions**:

- The **patron class is largely indifferent to theology.** Thiel is openly atheist. Mercer is more deal-driven than confessional. The Saudi PIF wing is non-Christian.
- The **theocrats are uncomfortable with Thiel's atheism and Kushner's Saudi capital.** Drollinger's Capitol Ministries materials reject "false religions" including Islam and Catholicism.
- The **tech wing finds the religious base distasteful but useful.** Elon Musk-era X has tolerated evangelical content as a coalition concession; Thiel funds religious-liberty litigation primarily for instrumental anti-regulatory reasons, not personal piety.

## What holds them together

None of the three wings can win alone:

- The **patron class lacks democratic mass.** Billionaires are 0.0001% of the electorate.
- The **theocrats lack capital.** They have voters but no Mercer-scale endowments.
- The **tech wing lacks legitimacy.** Direct rule by tech CEOs is unpopular across the political spectrum.

**Together they have:** capital + mass + technical implementation capacity. This is the operational coalition.

## What this means for the "is this fascism?" question

The vault has gathered substantial evidence matching scholarly fascism criteria (Paxton, Stanley, Snyder, Ben-Ghiat):

- Patron-state fusion (Heritage authors policy; admin executes)
- Cult of leader (Trump)
- Scapegoated out-groups (immigrants via [[ImmigrationOS]]; voters of color via DOJ voter-roll lawsuits)
- Suppression-of-dissent infrastructure (Schedule F; DOJ retribution)
- Mass-mobilization religious-nationalist coalition
- Surveillance apparatus ([[Federated Master Database]]; [[Flock Safety]])
- Permanent-capture mechanisms ([[Article V Convention]]; Schedule F; judicial capture)
- Foreign-state capital entanglement (Saudi PIF via Kushner; PRC via FSG)

**Missing from textbook fascism (so far):**

- Mass political violence at scale (Jan 6 was a discrete event, not sustained)
- Fully consolidated single-party state (elections still occur)
- Total suppression of opposition press (though degraded)

Most current scholarship places the US 2025-26 on the **descending slope but pre-consolidation**. Robert Paxton himself flipped to using "fascism" after Jan 6; Levitsky and Ziblatt prefer "competitive authoritarianism"; Snyder, Stanley, and Ben-Ghiat use "fascism" directly.

## Strategic implication for counter-strategy

Each wing has a **different attack surface**. A unified-front counter-strategy underperforms; a **coalition that exploits the seams between the wings** outperforms.

| Attack surface | Wing targeted | Tactic |
|---|---|---|
| Financial exposure | Patron class | DAF reform legislation; tax-status challenges; foreign-influence investigations (Kushner) |
| Establishment Clause | Theocratic | ADF-style litigation in reverse; mainline Protestant counter-coalition; suburban-women coalition |
| Privacy Act + antitrust | Tech / surveillance | 12+ Privacy Act lawsuits already filed; state-level data restrictions; antitrust against Palantir-government fusion |

The patrons would drop the theocrats if financial costs got high enough (recall: corporate ALEC donors withdrew under exposure pressure). The theocrats would split from the tech bros over IVF / abortion-surveillance / data-mining overreach. The tech wing would defect on any specific policy that threatens IPO-class returns.

## Operational dependencies among the three wings

The three-wing-coalition hypothesis (patron / theocratic / tech-surveillance) treats the Trump 2.0 coalition as a structurally distinct configuration from prior Republican coalitions. The operational dependencies among the three wings:

**The patron wing supplies capital and political-influence-purchasing capacity.** The [[Mercer]] family, the [[DeVos Family Network]], the [[Koch Network]] (with the qualification that Koch has been less directly aligned with the post-2018 evolution), the various sovereign-wealth-adjacent flows ([[Affinity Partners]] / Saudi PIF, broader UAE state-aligned giving), and the broader large-donor architecture. The wing's operational function is capitalising the other two wings.

**The theocratic wing supplies the popular base and the long-running movement infrastructure.** The [[New Apostolic Reformation]] / [[Council for National Policy]] / [[The Fellowship]] / [[Faith and Freedom Coalition]] convergence. The wing's operational function is providing electoral turnout, policy advocacy at scale, and the multi-decade institutional continuity that the patron wing's funding alone cannot purchase.

**The tech-surveillance wing supplies the operational infrastructure for governance.** [[Palantir Technologies]], the [[Anduril]] / defence-tech cluster, the broader Tech Right data-integration ecosystem, the [[DOGE]] federal data-aggregation programme. The wing's operational function is providing the technical-infrastructure layer through which the patron and theocratic wings' political objectives are operationally implemented.

## Why this configuration is structurally distinct

Prior Republican coalitions (the Reagan-era convergence of religious right + free-market right + foreign-policy hawks; the Bush-era convergence of business right + religious right + neoconservative foreign policy; the first-term Trump coalition's looser convergence) did not have the tech-surveillance wing as a co-equal partner. The 2024-2026 configuration is the first in which the tech infrastructure is treated as load-bearing rather than instrumental.

The structural significance: prior coalitions could operationally degrade if any one wing weakened (e.g., the post-Iraq decline of the neoconservative foreign-policy wing did not destabilise the broader Bush-era coalition). The three-wing configuration the vault is tracking treats all three wings as operationally required: weakening of any one wing would degrade the others' operational capacity.

## Falsification tests

**Test 1: Tech-surveillance withdrawal.** If the Tech Right principals withdraw or substantially de-align from the coalition (e.g., if the Thiel-Vance-Palantir spine weakens, if Musk departs Trump-orbit entirely and stays departed, if Andreessen and the broader a16z-aligned cohort reverse the 2024 alignment), the three-wing hypothesis loses force because the coalition reduces back to a more familiar two-wing patron + theocratic configuration.

**Test 2: Theocratic-wing dissolution.** If the NAR / CNP / Fellowship cluster fractures internally (e.g., over Catholic-Integralism vs evangelical-Protestant theological tensions, or over Project 2025 vs Convention of States tactical disputes), the hypothesis weakens because the theocratic wing's operational coherence is the assumption the hypothesis depends on.

**Test 3: Patron-wing realignment.** If the major patron-wing donors (Mercer family, DeVos family, the broader large-donor base) reduce their alignment or shift to other configurations (e.g., a Vance-2028-only configuration that excludes the broader theocratic wing), the hypothesis requires modification.

Subsequent research has addressed multiple structural gaps in the original three-wing framing:

Heavy Thiel-orbit overlap with the tech-surveillance wing, but distinct operationally (direct presidential family financial entanglement is novel).

[[Lens - Election Denial Infrastructure]] surfaces the paramilitary layer ([[Oath Keepers]], Proud Boys, [[Constitutional Sheriffs and Peace Officers Association]]). Trump's January 2025 pardon of Jan 6 convicts restored operational capacity. This is not yet documented as a unified wing - operates more as enforcement-capacity hired by the other wings as needed - but warrants tracking as potential fifth wing.

Added: [[Federalist Society]] (~70K members, all 3 Trump SCOTUS appointments), [[Leonard Leo]] ($1.6B Marble Freedom Trust war chest), [[Marble Freedom Trust]] (largest single political donation in US history). This is **the judicial spine** of the broader coalition.
