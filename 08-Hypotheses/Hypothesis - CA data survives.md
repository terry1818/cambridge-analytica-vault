# Hypothesis: Cambridge Analytica's harvested data and derived models survived the "deletion" certifications and remain in working use within successor organizations or unauthorized hands

## Claim

Despite signed certifications by [[Alexander Nix]] and [[Aleksandr Kogan]] in January 2016 that the Facebook-harvested dataset was destroyed, copies persisted on company servers and likely propagated to successor entities and/or third parties. The data and the derived psychographic models continue to be operationally useful as of 2026.

## Why it matters

If true, the post-2018 collapse of CA was substantively cosmetic. The data infrastructure and capabilities transferred to:
- [[Emerdata Limited]] (which "fully acquired" SCL/CA assets per Fast Company 2019)
- [[Auspex International]] (ex-CA staff, Africa/ME focus)
- [[Data Propria]] (ex-CA staff, US political)
- Possibly Russian/Israeli/Chinese state-linked parties given the Russian IP exposure and Emerdata's China-linked director

## Supporting evidence

- NYT viewed extant data samples in March 2018 ([[Source Rosenberg et al 2018-03-17 NYT]]) — proving deletion certifications were false
- [[Christopher Wylie]] confirmed data persisted on company servers
- Multiple successor firms continue with same core personnel
- [[Brittany Kaiser]]'s [[HindsightFiles]] released only a slice of her archive
- [[Source Pasternack Witt Fast Company 2019-07-26]] details ongoing operations

## Disconfirming evidence

- FTC consent orders (2019) require destruction
- No specific 2020+ reporting documenting operational use of original dataset
- Models may have lost predictive validity as base populations and Facebook behavior shifted

## Current confidence

`#confidence/medium` — direct evidence of persistence existed in 2018; subsequent operational use is plausible but not publicly documented.

## What would falsify this

Verified independent audit showing destruction of all data and derived models across all successor entities — has not occurred.
