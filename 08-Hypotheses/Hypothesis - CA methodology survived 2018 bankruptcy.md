# Hypothesis - CA methodology survived 2018 bankruptcy substantially intact

## Hypothesis statement

The 2018 [[Cambridge Analytica]] bankruptcy eliminated the specific corporate brand but did not eliminate the methodology, personnel, or operational capability the firm had built. The post-2018 disposition of CA assets, personnel, and IP is documentable as a pattern of successor-org formation that substantially preserved the operational capability.

## Confidence level: high

## Evidence in favour

**Successor-org formation pattern.** The [[Emerdata]] formation in late 2017 (months before the public CA reckoning, structured to preserve methodology and personnel through the corporate-collapse window), the [[Auspex International]] formation, the [[Data Propria]] formation in the US, and the [[AggregateIQ]] continuing operation post-2018 are all documented vehicles that absorbed pieces of the CA capability.

**Personnel-flow documentation.** Specific named CA staff moved to specific successor organisations along documentable timelines. The [[CA to successor orgs]] connection note documents the personnel-flow record at the level of specific named individuals and specific destination organisations.

**Methodology continuity.** The Channel 4 / [[Source Guardian 2023-02-15 Team Jorge]] disclosure of the Israeli-intelligence-adjacent [[Team Jorge]] operation documents methodology indistinguishable from what CA pitched and deployed. The [[Tal Hanan]] methodology is operationally similar to the [[Alexander Nix]] methodology, with the difference that Team Jorge has been operating for longer and at higher technical sophistication than CA achieved.

**Regulatory limitation.** The [[ICO Investigation]] (UK), the [[FTC v Facebook 2019]] settlement (US), and the various international regulatory actions targeted specific corporate entities (CA, SCL Group, SCL Elections, AIQ). The regulatory actions did not extend to the methodology, did not extend to personnel-level criminal prosecutions, and did not extend to the broader information-operations commercial ecosystem.

## Counter-evidence

**Successor-org reduced scale.** No single CA-successor entity has reached the operational scale or commercial visibility that CA achieved at peak (2016-2017). [[Emerdata]] in particular has been substantially less visible than CA was; specific Emerdata operational outputs have not been publicly documented at the same scale.

**Industry-wide methodology adoption.** What looks like CA methodology continuity in successor organisations may instead be industry-wide methodology adoption: the audience-segmentation-and-ads-testing infrastructure that CA pioneered has become baseline practice across the entire political-consulting industry. Distinguishing between "CA methodology persisting" and "CA methodology becoming industry baseline" is operationally difficult.

## Falsification conditions

The hypothesis would be substantially refuted if:

- A comprehensive forensic accounting of CA personnel post-2018 showed substantial dispersion into unrelated industries (rather than continuing in information-operations or political-consulting roles).
- Documentary evidence emerged that the [[Emerdata]] / [[Auspex International]] / [[Data Propria]] successor entities had substantially different methodology, personnel, or operational capability from CA.
- The Team Jorge methodology was demonstrably independent of the CA-era methodology rather than parallel to it.

The hypothesis would be substantially weakened (without being refuted) if:

- The post-2018 commercial information-operations market was substantially smaller in aggregate than the 2014-2018 market, suggesting the underlying capability shrank.
- The 2024-2026 Trump 2.0 federal-data-aggregation programme operated independently of the post-CA commercial information-operations ecosystem (suggesting two parallel evolutions rather than a continuous methodology stream).

## Why this hypothesis is load-bearing

This hypothesis underwrites a substantial fraction of the vault's broader analytical claim that the CA-era story is not over. If the methodology, personnel, and IP did not survive the bankruptcy substantially intact, the vault's analysis of the 2024-2026 information environment as a successor to the CA-era environment is substantially weakened. If they did survive, the broader vault analysis is substantially strengthened.
