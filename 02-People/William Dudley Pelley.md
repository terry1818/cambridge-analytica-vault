## Summary

William Dudley Pelley was a Massachusetts-born journalist, O. Henry Award-winning short-story writer, Hollywood screenwriter, and spiritualist who transformed himself into the founder and leader of the [[Silver Legion of America]] — the "Silver Shirts" — in 1933. He modelled the organisation explicitly on Hitler's SA, founded it on the same day Hitler was appointed Chancellor, and called for a "Christian Commonwealth" that would exclude Jews and non-whites from American public life. At the Silver Legion's peak Pelley commanded approximately 15,000 members across 46 states. He was convicted of sedition in 1942 and served nine years in federal prison. Rachel Maddow's *Prequel* treats Pelley as the archetypal figure of indigenous American fascism — someone who developed an authoritarian mass movement not imported from abroad but grown from domestic soil.

## Key facts

- Born 12 March 1890, Lynn, Massachusetts; died 30 June 1965, Noblesville, Indiana.
- Won two O. Henry Awards for short fiction; wrote screenplays for Hollywood silent films including *Drag* (1929).
- 1929: published "Seven Minutes in Eternity" in *The American Magazine* — a proto-near-death experience account that launched his career as an occultist; attracted a large following among spiritualists and New Thought readers.
- 1930–33: moved into explicitly political mysticism; antisemitism became central to his worldview.
- 30 January 1933: founded the Silver Legion of America in Asheville, NC — deliberately timed to coincide with Hitler's appointment as Chancellor.
- 1936: ran for president on the Christian Party ticket; received fewer than 2,000 votes; ballot access limited to Washington state.
- 1941: disbanded the Silver Legion after Pearl Harbor, believing this would limit his legal exposure; the strategy failed.
- 1942: arrested and tried in Indiana for making seditious statements. Convicted on 11 of 12 counts; sentenced to 15 years.
- 1950: released from prison on grounds of ill health, age 60.
- Post-release: returned to occultist writing; published *Star Guests* (1950) and other works; the political career was finished.
- 1965: died in Noblesville, Indiana.

## Relationships

- [[Silver Legion of America]] — Pelley's primary creation; the organisation had no existence independent of him.
- [[Father Charles Coughlin]] — ideological fellow-traveller; both operated in the antisemitic fascist milieu of the 1930s.
- [[German-American Bund]] — contemporary parallel organisation; some overlapping milieu but separate structures.
- [[Lawrence Dennis]] — fellow defendant in the [[Great Sedition Trial 1944]].
- [[Great Sedition Trial 1944]] — Pelley was among the defendants; trial ended in mistrial.

## Open questions

- The relationship between Pelley's occultist belief system and his political fascism deserves more scholarly attention — to what extent was the Silver Shirts a religious movement?
- Whether the "Christian Commonwealth" theological framework contributed directly to post-war Christian Identity movements.
