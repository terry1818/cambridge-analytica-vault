## Summary

Guy Rosen is a technology executive who entered Facebook through the 2013 acquisition of Onavo, an Israeli mobile analytics startup he co-founded. After several years working on Facebook's connectivity initiatives and growth products, he was placed in charge of what the company called "integrity, trust and safety" in 2017, formally holding the title VP of Integrity. He succeeded [[Alex Stamos]] as the primary safety and security executive following Stamos's departure in August 2018. Rosen subsequently oversaw Facebook's election-integrity programs, its coordinated inauthentic behavior enforcement, and its external communications on platform safety. He was later elevated to Meta's first Chief Information Security Officer — a title that had not existed at Facebook/Meta before his appointment — consolidating the security, safety, and integrity functions under a single executive.

## Key Facts

- Co-founded Onavo, a mobile data analytics company, in Israel; Facebook acquired Onavo in October 2013 for approximately $150–200 million
- At Facebook, initially worked on Internet.org connectivity initiatives and growth product strategy
- Appointed head of integrity, trust and safety in 2017, fusing security background with product-at-scale experience
- Title "VP of Integrity" was publicly announced in February 2019 — though it briefly disappeared from his LinkedIn profile within a day, prompting press coverage (Gizmodo)
- Gave a major on-record interview to PBS FRONTLINE's "The Facebook Dilemma" (2018) on election security
- Elevated to Meta's first-ever CISO, a role that continued to encompass safety and security risks across Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp
- Oversaw Meta's Threat Intelligence and Election Integrity teams during the 2018, 2020, and 2022 election cycles
