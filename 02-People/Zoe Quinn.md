# Zoë Quinn

## Summary

Zoë Quinn (born 1987) is an American independent game developer best known for the 2013 interactive-fiction game "Depression Quest," a free text-based game about living with depression. In August 2014, Quinn's former boyfriend, Eron Gjoni, published a long, accusatory blog post (the so-called "Zoe Post") that falsely insinuated Quinn had traded a sexual relationship for favorable press coverage. The post was seized on by online communities — particularly on 4chan, Reddit, and Twitter — as the pretext for a sustained harassment campaign that became known as [[Gamergate]].

Quinn matters to this vault as the origin-point target of Gamergate, the 2014 episode that functioned as a prototype for coordinated digital harassment and astroturfed grievance. Gamergate participants framed the campaign as a consumer movement "about ethics in games journalism" — a deniability shell that masked targeted abuse of a small number of women. The campaign's mechanics (manufactured scandal, swarm coordination across decentralized platforms, doxxing, and a respectable-sounding cover narrative) directly anticipated the techniques later industrialized in election-related info-warfare. Quinn was doxxed, received death and rape threats, and was forced to leave home; in response Quinn co-founded the Crash Override Network, a support resource for targets of online abuse, and later wrote "Crash Override: How Gamergate (Nearly) Destroyed My Life" (2017).
