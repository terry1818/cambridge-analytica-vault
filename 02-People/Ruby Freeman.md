# Ruby Freeman

Former Fulton County, Georgia election worker who served as a temporary employee processing absentee ballots at the State Farm Arena tabulation center in November 2020. Mother of [[Shaye Moss]] (also a Fulton County election worker). Both were falsely accused by [[Donald Trump]] and [[Rudy Giuliani]] of pulling fraudulent ballots from suitcases under a table â€” a claim repeatedly disproven by Georgia Bureau of Investigation review of the security footage. The false claims resulted in extensive harassment including death threats and home invasion attempts. Freeman and Moss filed defamation suit against Giuliani; **December 15, 2023 federal jury in DC awarded Freeman and Moss $148 million** ($75M punitive + $73M compensatory).

## The false claims

In December 2020, Giuliani â€” at a December 3, 2020 Georgia State Senate hearing and in subsequent media appearances â€” falsely claimed that security camera footage from State Farm Arena showed Freeman and Moss:
- Pulling "suitcases" of fraudulent ballots from under a table after election observers had been told to leave
- Engaging in "drug dealer"-like passes of USB drives between mother and daughter

Trump amplified the claims, naming Freeman in his January 2, 2021 call to [[Brad Raffensperger]] (referenced as "Ruby Freeman" 18 times) and characterizing her as "professional vote scammer" and "hustler."

## What the footage actually showed (Tier 1)

The Georgia Bureau of Investigation and Fulton County election officials reviewed the full footage and unequivocally established:
- The "suitcases" were standard ballot containers used at every election
- The containers had been placed under tables earlier in the day as normal procedure
- Observers had not been told to leave; counting continued normally
- The "passes" between Moss and Freeman were a piece of candy (a ginger mint) from mother to daughter
- No fraud occurred

The State Farm Arena footage and the GBI investigation findings have been repeatedly available in the public record since December 2020.

## Harassment campaign (Tier 1 documented in litigation)

After Giuliani named her publicly:
- Hundreds of threats including death threats received
- Trevian Kutti (an associate of Kanye West) sent to her home January 4, 2021 attempting to pressure her to confess to fraud
- Forced to flee her home of 21 years
- Lost her business
- Subjected to racist abuse
- FBI advised her to leave her home for safety reasons before January 6, 2021

## J6 Committee testimony (June 21, 2022)

Both Freeman and Moss testified publicly at the J6 Select Committee hearing on pressure on state and local officials. Moss provided the more detailed account (she had been more involved in the operations footage); Freeman's testimony established the human cost of the false claims and the harassment.

Moss's testimony quoting her grandmother â€” "Grandmother just kept saying, 'They're going to be at my door'" â€” became one of the most-cited human testimonies of the J6 Committee process.

## Freeman v Giuliani (D.D.C.)

Filed December 2021 alleging defamation.
- Liability established by Giuliani's failures to comply with discovery (default judgment August 2023 on liability)
- December 11-15, 2023: jury trial on damages
- **December 15, 2023 verdict**: $148 million total
  - $40M compensatory each for Freeman and Moss ($80M)
  - $20M emotional distress each ($40M)
  - $75M punitive damages total
- Giuliani filed bankruptcy December 21, 2023 in part to delay collection
- Bankruptcy dismissed July 2024 without discharge; Freeman and Moss pursuing collection in state court
- Federal judge ordered Giuliani to surrender significant assets including his NYC apartment

## Freeman in Georgia RICO case

Freeman is a witness in [[Georgia v Trump RICO 2023]]. The Trump-Raffensperger call (which names her) and the harassment campaign are predicate acts in the indictment.
