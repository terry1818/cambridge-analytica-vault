## Summary

Milo Yiannopoulos is a British far-right media personality who in 2014–2017 was one of the most visible amplifiers of online far-right culture in the English-speaking world. His career illustrates the mechanism by which anonymous chan-board grievances were translated into mainstream-adjacent political narrative: he adopted the GamerGate controversy as a vehicle at [[Breitbart]], legitimizing its misogynist framing for a broader audience and funneling gaming-culture resentment into the alt-right pipeline. His trajectory — rise, deplatforming, attempted rehabilitation — is itself a case study in the lifecycle of far-right media figures. Documented in Donovan, Dreyfuss, and Friedberg's *Meme Wars* as a key node linking [[Gamergate]] to the alt-right's mainstreaming.

## Key facts

- Worked at Breitbart 2014–2017; headed its "Breitbart Tech" section from October 2015.
- Became a "lead actor" in the [[Gamergate]] harassment campaign through sustained Breitbart coverage.
- Leaked emails (obtained by BuzzFeed News, 2017) showed he solicited story ideas and editorial suggestions from white nationalists including editors of *American Renaissance* and *The Daily Stormer*.
- February 2017: Video clips circulated showing him stating sexual relationships between 13-year-old boys and adult men could be "perfectly consensual." He subsequently resigned from Breitbart, lost his CPAC invitation, and had his Simon & Schuster book contract cancelled.
- 2022: Worked as an intern for Representative Marjorie Taylor Greene.
- 2022–2023: Affiliated with white nationalist Nick Fuentes; worked on Kanye West's short-lived 2024 presidential campaign.
- Has publicly identified as "ex-gay" and Catholic since approximately 2019.
- Ali Alexander allegations involve Yiannopoulos's name as a lure in alleged grooming of minors (unproven as of this writing).
