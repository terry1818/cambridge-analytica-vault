## Summary

Edgar Prince was a Holland, Michigan industrialist whose automobile parts company — Prince Corporation — grew from a 1965 startup he founded with two co-workers into a highly profitable manufacturer best known for inventing the lighted sun visor and the universal garage door opener. Prince sold the company in 1995 for $1.35 billion, weeks before his death from a heart attack.

His religious philanthropy made him one of the principal patrons of the post-1970s religious right's organizational infrastructure. With his wife Elsa, Edgar founded the Edgar and Elsa Prince Foundation in 1979, which directed substantial funding toward organizations working to eliminate the legal separation between church and state. Most significantly, Edgar was a co-founder of the [[Family Research Council]], the Washington lobbying organization that became one of the most influential evangelical advocacy groups in the country.

Katherine Stewart's [[Source - Stewart 2020 - The Power Worshippers]] places Edgar Prince in the network of industrial and financial patrons who built the religious right's institutional capacity from the 1970s through the 1990s. The Prince family — his children [[Erik Prince]] and [[Betsy DeVos]] (née Prince) — continued and extended his funding patterns into the next generation, with Erik founding Blackwater (later Academi) and Betsy becoming a major school-choice advocate and Trump's Secretary of Education.

The family were members of the Reformed Church in America, a theologically conservative Calvinist denomination that influenced Edgar's conviction that Christian principles should directly govern public life.

## Key Facts

- Born June 9, 1930; died March 2, 1995, of a heart attack (Holland, Michigan)
- US Air Force veteran; worked as die caster and engineer before entrepreneurship
- Founded Prince Manufacturing (later Prince Corporation) in 1965 with two co-workers
- Company inventions included the lighted sun visor and universal garage door opener
- Sold Prince Corporation to Johnson Controls for $1.35 billion in 1995 shortly before his death
- Founded Edgar and Elsa Prince Foundation, 1979
- Co-founder, [[Family Research Council]] (which opposes LGBTQ rights and advocates for conservative Christian policy positions)
- Member, Reformed Church in America; deeply Calvinist in theological orientation
- Father of [[Betsy DeVos]] (née Prince), [[Erik Prince]], Emilie Prince, and Eileen Prince
- His children Dick DeVos (Betsy's husband) and Erik Prince continued major conservative and religious-right philanthropy
