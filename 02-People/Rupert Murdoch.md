# Rupert Murdoch

## Summary

Keith Rupert Murdoch (born 1931) is an Australian-born, naturalized-American media proprietor who assembled one of the largest media empires in history. Inheriting a single Adelaide newspaper from his father in 1952, Murdoch expanded across Australia, then the United Kingdom (acquiring *The Sun*, *News of the World*, and *The Times*), and then the United States, where he built the Fox film and television businesses and acquired *The Wall Street Journal* (2007) and the *New York Post*. His most politically consequential act was founding [[Fox News]] in 1996 — hiring [[Roger Ailes]] to run it — which became the dominant cable news network and the central organ of American conservative media. Murdoch's outlets have functioned as both commercial enterprises and instruments of political influence, repeatedly shaping elections in Australia, Britain, and the United States. He is relevant to this vault as the proprietor whose business decisions made the [[MAGA media ecosystem]] possible: Fox News supplied the mass-audience infrastructure that newer outlets like [[Newsmax]] and [[One America News]] later competed to out-flank. The 2023 [[Dominion Voting Systems]] defamation settlement exposed, through discovery, the gap between what Fox executives privately believed about 2020 election-fraud claims and what the network broadcast.

## Timeline

- `1931-03-11` — Born in Melbourne, Australia
- `1952` — Inherits *The News* (Adelaide) on the death of his father, Sir Keith Murdoch
- `1969` — Acquires *News of the World* and *The Sun* in the UK
- `1981` — Acquires *The Times* and *The Sunday Times* (London)
- `1985` — Becomes a naturalized US citizen (required to own US television stations); founds Fox Broadcasting
- `1996-10-07` — Launches [[Fox News]] with [[Roger Ailes]] as founding CEO
- `2007` — Acquires Dow Jones & Company, owner of *The Wall Street Journal*
- `2011` — UK phone-hacking scandal forces closure of *News of the World*; Murdoch testifies to Parliament
- `2013` — Splits the empire into News Corp (publishing) and 21st Century Fox (entertainment)
- `2016-07` — Resumes direct management of Fox News after [[Roger Ailes]]'s ouster
- `2019` — Sells most 21st Century Fox assets to Disney; retains Fox News under the new Fox Corporation
- `2023-04` — Fox News settles the [[Dominion Voting Systems]] defamation suit for $787.5 million
- `2023-11` — Steps down as chairman of News Corp and Fox Corporation; son Lachlan Murdoch becomes sole chair; Rupert becomes Chairman Emeritus
- `2024–2025` — Family trust succession dispute; arrangements reported to leave voting control of News Corp and Fox with Lachlan Murdoch

## Relationships

- **Reports to / worked under:** None (proprietor)
- **Subordinates / hired:** [[Roger Ailes]] (founding Fox News CEO); a series of editors and executives across the empire
- **Co-conspirators / collaborators:** Lachlan Murdoch (son, successor as chair); the family trust structure governing control
- **Opponents / accusers:** [[Dominion Voting Systems]] and [[Smartmatic]] (defamation litigation against Fox News); James Murdoch (son, reportedly estranged over the company's political direction)

## Public statements

- In the [[Dominion Voting Systems]] litigation, Murdoch acknowledged in a deposition that some Fox hosts had "endorsed" false 2020 election-fraud narratives — a documented admission obtained through discovery.
- Murdoch has long publicly framed his outlets as giving voice to audiences underserved by what he characterizes as a liberal-leaning establishment press.
