# Stacey Abrams

## Summary

Stacey Yvonne Abrams (born 1973) is an American voting-rights organizer, lawyer, author, and politician who served in the Georgia House of Representatives from 2007 to 2017, including six years as Democratic Minority Leader. She was the Democratic nominee for Governor of Georgia in 2018 and 2022. Abrams is best known not for elected office — she lost both gubernatorial races — but for building durable voter-registration and anti-suppression infrastructure that is widely credited with helping shift Georgia's electorate.

Abrams matters to this vault as the leading figure of democratic counter-mobilization against the voter-suppression and election-administration tactics it tracks. In 2013 she founded the New Georgia Project to register voters of color, who were systematically under-registered. After her narrow 2018 loss to Brian Kemp — who, as Georgia Secretary of State, simultaneously oversaw the election in which he was a candidate, and whose office had purged voter rolls and held registrations under an "exact match" rule — Abrams declined to formally concede and founded [[Fair Fight Action]], which litigated against Georgia's election practices and built a national voter-protection operation. Her organizing is frequently cited as a factor in Georgia's 2020 results (Biden's win and the two Democratic Senate victories in the January 2021 runoffs). The vault treats Abrams's organizational record as documented, while noting that the precise causal weight of any single organizer in an electoral outcome is inherently contested. She is, in the vault's framework, the affirmative-democracy counterpoint to the [[voter suppression]] and [[democratic backsliding]] threads.

## Public statements

- "I acknowledge that former Secretary of State Brian Kemp will be certified as the victor in the 2018 gubernatorial election. But to watch an elected official ... baldly pin his hopes for election on the suppression of the people's democratic right to vote has been truly appalling. So let's be clear: This is not a speech of concession." — Abrams, November 2018.
- Abrams has consistently framed registration and turnout work as a long-horizon project, not a single-cycle campaign.
