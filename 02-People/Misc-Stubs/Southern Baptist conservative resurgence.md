# Southern Baptist conservative resurgence (1979-1990)

Coordinated takeover of the Southern Baptist Convention by theological conservatives. Architects [[Paul Pressler]] and Paige Patterson explicitly planned the multi-year sequence of presidential elections that produced trustee appointments shifting all major SBC institutions rightward.

## Significance

The SBC resurgence is one of the cleanest documented cases of structural-organizational capture within a religious denomination. The methodology - patient multi-year movement-organizing producing institutional capture via control of presidential appointments - parallels the contemporary [[Structural Capture Thesis]] applied to federal institutions.
