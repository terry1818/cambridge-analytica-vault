# Investigative methodology

Range of techniques used by investigative journalists, researchers, and analysts to reconstruct facts about contested or covert events.

## Key contemporary methodologies (vault-cited)

- [[OSINT]] - open-source intelligence ([[Bellingcat]], [[Forensic Architecture]])
- Document forensics (Wikileaks, ProPublica, NYT investigative)
- Financial-network analysis (ICIJ Panama/Paradise Papers, OpenSecrets)
- Communication-network analysis (Mueller report, J6 Committee)

## Vault relevance

The vault's approach to source evaluation depends heavily on which methodology produced a given claim. Tier-1 evidence typically rests on documentary or sworn-testimony bases; Tier-2 evidence relies on journalistic synthesis; Tier-3 evidence is contested or single-sourced.
