# US v. Trump (DC) - January 6 Federal Case

Federal prosecution of Donald Trump by Special Counsel [[Jack Smith]] for conduct related to the [[January 6 Capitol Attack]].

## Indictment (Tier 1)

- August 1, 2023: 4-count indictment in DC District Court
- Charges: conspiracy to defraud the United States; conspiracy to obstruct an official proceeding; obstruction; conspiracy against rights
- Judge Tanya Chutkan presiding

## Supreme Court intervention

- July 1, 2024: Trump v. United States ruled presidents have absolute immunity for "core" official acts and presumptive immunity for other official acts
- The ruling substantially limited the scope of conduct chargeable in the DC case
- Smith filed a superseding indictment August 27, 2024 conforming to the immunity ruling

## Dismissal

- November 25, 2024: Smith moved to dismiss the case in light of Trump's election win and DOJ policy against prosecuting sitting presidents
- November 26, 2024: Case dismissed without prejudice
- January 14, 2025: Smith's final Special Counsel report released
