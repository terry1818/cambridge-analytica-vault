# Equal Rights Amendment (ERA)

US constitutional amendment proposed by Congress 1972 to prohibit denial or abridgement of equality of rights based on sex. Ratified by 38 states (the constitutional threshold), but ratification beyond the 1982 deadline remains disputed.

## Schlafly defeat (Tier 1)

[[Phyllis Schlafly]]'s STOP ERA campaign (organized through [[Eagle Forum]]) successfully blocked ratification before the 1982 deadline. The campaign became the prototype for conservative movement organizing at the state-legislature level.

## Significance for vault

The ERA campaign is the foundational precedent for state-legislature-by-state-legislature conservative organizing. The same methodology Schlafly perfected in defeating ERA has been deployed by [[Convention of States Action]] in pursuing [[19 state applications]] for an Article V Convention.
