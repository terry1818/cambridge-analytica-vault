# Friedrich Hayek (1899-1992)

Austrian-British economist. 1974 Nobel laureate. Major figure of the Austrian school of economics. Author of "The Road to Serfdom" (1944), "The Constitution of Liberty" (1960), and "Law, Legislation and Liberty" (1973-79).

## Influence

- Foundational intellectual figure for post-WWII libertarian conservatism
- Heavy influence on the [[Mont Pelerin Society]] / [[Heritage Foundation]] / [[Cato Institute]] intellectual current
- Cited by [[James M Buchanan]] as a direct influence
- Read enthusiastically by [[Margaret Thatcher]] and influenced UK conservative-economic policy 1979-1990

## Vault relevance

Hayek is one of the foundational intellectual figures of the contemporary structural-conservative project. The Austrian-school skepticism of democratic majorities + market faith + suspicion of administrative expertise threads through to:
