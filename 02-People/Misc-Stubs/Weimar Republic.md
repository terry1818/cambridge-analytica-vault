# Weimar Republic

German government 1919-1933. Ended with Hitler's appointment as Chancellor (January 30, 1933) and the subsequent Enabling Act (March 23, 1933).

## Vault relevance

Weimar is the canonical historical case study for democratic-backsliding analysis. Levitsky/Ziblatt "How Democracies Die" (2018) and Ben-Ghiat "Strongmen" (2020) both draw on Weimar's institutional collapse for comparative US analysis. The [[Enabling Act of 1933]] is one specific reference point for [[Schedule F]]-style structural-power consolidation.
