# OLC opinion

Office of Legal Counsel - DOJ unit providing legal opinions binding within the executive branch. Most-cited OLC opinions in vault context are the 1973 and 2000 OLC memos holding that a sitting US president cannot be indicted.

## Vault relevance

The OLC memos on sitting-president-indictment were the formal legal basis for [[Robert Mueller]]'s decision not to charge [[Donald Trump]] during the Russian interference investigation. The memos remain binding within DOJ. The 2024 [[Trump v United States]] ruling extended an analogous principle to immunity beyond indictment.
