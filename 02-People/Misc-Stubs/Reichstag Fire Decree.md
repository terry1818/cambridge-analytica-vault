# Reichstag Fire Decree (February 28, 1933)

German presidential decree issued by Paul von Hindenburg the day after the Reichstag fire. Suspended habeas corpus, freedom of expression, freedom of assembly, and the secrecy of postal communications.

## Vault relevance

The Reichstag Fire Decree + the subsequent [[Enabling Act of 1933]] are the canonical historical case study for emergency-power-based authoritarian consolidation. Contemporary analyses of US executive-order authority and emergency-declaration power frequently reference this precedent.
