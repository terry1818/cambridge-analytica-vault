# James M. Buchanan (1919-2013)

US economist. 1986 Nobel Prize in Economics. Founder of [[Public choice theory]]. Founder of the Center for Study of Public Choice at Virginia Tech, later at George Mason University.

## Key works

- Co-authored "The Calculus of Consent" (1962) with [[Gordon Tullock]] - founding text of public choice
- "The Limits of Liberty" (1975)
- "Democracy in Deficit" (1977, with Wagner)

## Significance for vault

Buchanan is the intellectual architect of the structural-constraint framework that has shaped:
- The [[Convention of States]] movement's three subjects (balanced budget, term limits, federal limits) directly track public choice priorities
- The [[Federalist Society]] judicial-selection philosophy (originalism + anti-administrative-state)
- The [[Koch network]] policy agenda

Nancy MacLean's "Democracy in Chains" (2017) framed Buchanan as the explicit intellectual progenitor of the donor-class project for constraining democratic majorities. The MacLean book has been academically controversial but the documented Koch-Buchanan funding relationship is not seriously disputed.
