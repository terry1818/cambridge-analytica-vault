# Clarence Thomas

## Summary

Clarence Thomas (born 1948) is an Associate Justice of the U.S. Supreme Court, appointed by President George H. W. Bush in 1991 after a bruising confirmation marked by Anita Hill's sexual-harassment testimony. He is now the longest-serving sitting Justice and the Court's most consistent originalist-libertarian voice. Thomas's jurisprudence — willing to revisit and overturn long-standing precedent — has been central to the conservative legal movement's most consequential recent victories, including the 2022 *Dobbs v. Jackson Women's Health Organization* decision overturning *Roe v. Wade*, in which his concurrence urged the Court to reconsider other substantive-due-process precedents.

Thomas matters to this vault for two intertwined reasons. First, his jurisprudence is a load-bearing element of the "constitutional capture" project — the decades-long, donor-funded effort to remake American constitutional law along libertarian and religious-conservative lines, advanced through the [[Federalist Society]] judicial pipeline. Second, ProPublica's 2023 investigative series documented that Thomas accepted years of luxury travel, gifts, and other benefits from conservative billionaire benefactors — most prominently real-estate magnate and Republican megadonor Harlan Crow — without disclosing them. Reporting also documented a 2014 real-estate transaction in which Crow purchased property from the Thomas family, and tuition payments for a relative Thomas was raising. These revelations connect a sitting Justice directly to the [[patron wing]] of the conservative network this vault tracks, raising documented questions about judicial ethics and the influence of private wealth on the Court.
