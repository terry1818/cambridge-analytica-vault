## Summary

Greg Locke is the founder and pastor of Global Vision Bible Church, a non-denominational charismatic congregation in Lebanon, Tennessee (near Nashville). He rose to national attention through viral social-media videos combining Christian nationalist rhetoric, election denial, COVID conspiracy, and eventually theatrical mass exorcisms. Timothy Alberta's *The Kingdom, the Power, and the Glory* (2023) profiles Locke as representative of a radicalized fringe of American evangelicalism that abandoned mainstream denominational accountability structures. Unlike [[Robert Jeffress]], who operates within the Southern Baptist institutional framework, Locke is explicitly non-denominational — a feature that removes normal accountability mechanisms. He has been reported to the IRS for alleged Johnson Amendment violations.

## Key facts

- Founded Global Vision Bible Church in Lebanon, Tennessee (services held in a large tent after noise complaints).
- Became a major social-media figure around 2020–21, with Facebook videos on COVID ("a hoax"), mask mandates, and election fraud accumulating millions of views.
- Was present in Washington, DC, on January 6, 2021; encouraged congregants to attend.
- In January 2021 urged clergy to join the "Black Robe Regiment" — a Christian nationalist mobilization concept invoking Revolutionary-era clergy who supported armed resistance.
- Told congregants who vote Democrat that he "doesn't want them around the church" — reported to the IRS as a potential Johnson Amendment violation.
- Increasingly shifted toward charismatic exorcism ministry from 2022 onward; produced and released the film *Come Out in Jesus Name* (2023).
- His church has been forced to address noise complaints from neighbors due to outdoor tent-based services.
- No major denominational affiliation; operates as an independent congregation.
