## Summary

Renée DiResta is one of the foremost American researchers on disinformation, computational propaganda, and online information manipulation. As Research Manager at the [[Stanford Internet Observatory]] (2019–2024), she led investigations into state-sponsored influence operations and contributed authoritatively to US Senate Intelligence Committee reporting. Her 2024 book *Invisible Rulers: The People Who Turn Lies Into Reality* synthesizes her research into a theory of how small communities of propagandists manufacture group-specific consensus realities at scale. After the SIO was wound down under congressional pressure in 2024, she joined Georgetown University's McCourt School of Public Policy.

## Key facts

- Led investigations at the SIO into the Russian [[Internet Research Agency (IRA)]] and GRU efforts to manipulate the 2016 US Presidential Election; contributed to official Senate Intelligence Committee reports.
- Co-authored major platform transparency reports including the IRA Twitter dataset analysis (2018–2019).
- Coined the term "[[bespoke realities]]" to describe fragmented, group-specific consensus realities engineered by propagandists — a central concept in *Invisible Rulers*.
- Her contract at SIO was not renewed in June 2024 amid the [[Congressional campaign against disinformation research]] led by the House Judiciary Committee under [[Jim Jordan]].
- Previously worked in venture capital and technology before pivoting to disinformation research.
- Has written extensively for outlets including The Atlantic, Wired, and MIT Technology Review.
- Named to the McCourt School faculty (Associate Research Professor) in October 2024.
