# Trae Stephens

## Summary

Trae Stephens is an American venture capitalist and defense-technology entrepreneur who sits at the center of the [[tech right]] — the cluster of Silicon Valley figures, anchored by [[Peter Thiel]], who have moved capital, talent, and political energy toward national-security technology and toward the political right. Stephens began his career as a computational linguist inside the U.S. Intelligence Community, joined [[Palantir Technologies]] as an early employee in 2008, became a partner at [[Founders Fund]] (Thiel's venture firm) in 2014, and in 2017 co-founded [[Anduril Industries]] — the defense-tech company built explicitly to challenge legacy primes like [[Lockheed Martin]] and [[Raytheon]] — alongside [[Palmer Luckey]] and fellow Palantir alumni. For this vault, Stephens matters as a connective figure: he links the Thiel/Founders Fund donor-and-investor network, the Palantir surveillance-and-data lineage, the new defense-tech industry, and the broader political project of aligning the tech industry with the security state and the Trump-era right.

## Key facts

- **Role(s):** Partner at [[Founders Fund]] (since 2014); co-founder and Executive Chairman of [[Anduril Industries]] (since 2017)
- **Affiliations:** [[Founders Fund]], [[Anduril Industries]], [[Palantir Technologies]], the U.S. Intelligence Community, the Federalist Society (listed bio)
- **Active period:** c.2006–present
- **Known for:** Co-founding Anduril; directing venture capital into government and defense startups; articulating a "tech right" case for disrupting defense procurement

## Timeline

- `c.2006` — After graduating from Georgetown University, Stephens begins his career as a computational linguist working within the U.S. Intelligence Community, building Arabic/Persian name-matching and data-enrichment systems
- `2008` — Joins [[Palantir Technologies]] as one of its early employees; leads teams expanding Palantir's defense and intelligence business, international growth, and product development
- `2014` — Becomes a partner at [[Founders Fund]], focusing on government and defense-technology investments
- `2014` — Meets [[Palmer Luckey]] (founder of Oculus VR) at a Founders Fund retreat; the two bond over applying Silicon Valley startup methods to the defense sector — the seed of Anduril
- `2017` — Co-founds [[Anduril Industries]] with Palmer Luckey, Matt Grimm, Joe Chen, and Brian Schimpf (the latter three also Palantir alumni)
- `2016` / `2024` — Reportedly considered for / connected to Trump-administration defense-transition roles, reflecting his standing in the tech-right policy network
- `2024-2025` — Anduril closes a $2.5 billion Series G round led by [[Peter Thiel]]'s [[Founders Fund]] — including a reported $1 billion single check, the largest in the firm's history

## Relationships

- **Reports to / worked under:** [[Peter Thiel]] — as a Founders Fund partner, Stephens operates within Thiel's venture firm and ideological orbit
- **Co-founders / collaborators:** [[Palmer Luckey]] (Anduril co-founder); Matt Grimm, Joe Chen, Brian Schimpf (Anduril co-founders, fellow Palantir alumni)
- **Institutional lineage:** [[Palantir Technologies]] — Stephens is part of the "Palantir DNA" diaspora that seeded Anduril's culture and personnel
- **Network peers:** other [[tech right]] figures including [[Elon Musk]], [[J.D. Vance]] (a Thiel protégé), and the wider Founders Fund / Thiel circle

## Public statements

- Stephens has publicly framed Anduril's mission through "just war theory" and the argument that Silicon Valley has a moral obligation to build for U.S. defense — context provided in interviews (Sourcery, World of DaaS).
- He has argued that the legacy defense-procurement system is broken and that venture-backed firms must displace incumbent contractors — a core "tech right" thesis.
