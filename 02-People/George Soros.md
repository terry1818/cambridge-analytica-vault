# George Soros

## Summary

George Soros (born György Schwartz, 1930) is a Hungarian-born American financier, a survivor of the Nazi occupation of Hungary, and one of the world's most prominent philanthropists. Through his [[Open Society Foundations]] — which he has funded with the bulk of a fortune made in finance — he has supported democracy, human-rights, transparency, education, and civil-society work in more than 100 countries. He belongs in this vault for a specific and double-sided reason. He is a major funder of pro-democracy and anti-authoritarian work, which connects him to the institutional ecosystem the vault tracks. But far more importantly for the vault's disinformation thread, Soros has become the single most pervasive personalized target of right-wing conspiracy theory in the world — a "code word," in researchers' phrasing, around which a vast volume of disinformation is organized.

The Soros conspiracy complex is itself a documented case study in how disinformation works. His name is invoked as the hidden hand behind an enormous and mutually contradictory set of events: anti-corruption protests in Eastern Europe, Black Lives Matter, migrant "caravans," campus protests, election outcomes. Researchers at the ADL, SPLC, and elsewhere have established that these narratives frequently recycle classic antisemitic tropes — the wealthy, hidden Jewish manipulator orchestrating world events. The earliest such use researchers identify dates to 1992, when Hungarian far-right figure István Csurka deployed imagery of Soros as part of a "Jewish global conspiracy." The vault treats the Soros conspiracy theories as a primary specimen of how a real person is converted into a universal scapegoat — a mechanism directly relevant to understanding manufactured narratives and information warfare.

## Key facts

- **Role(s):** Financier; founder and chair of the [[Open Society Foundations]]; philanthropist.
- **Affiliations:** Open Society Foundations; Soros Fund Management. Management of the foundations' work has increasingly passed to his son, Alexander Soros.
- **Active period:** 1969 (founds his fund) – present; philanthropy from the late 1970s.
- **Known for:** Large-scale democracy and human-rights philanthropy; "breaking the Bank of England" in 1992 currency speculation; being the recurring villain of global right-wing conspiracy narratives.

## Relationships

- **Subordinates / hired:** Alexander Soros — his son and designated successor at the helm of the Open Society Foundations.
- **Opponents / accusers:** Viktor Orbán's Hungarian government, which built an entire "Stop Soros" political and legislative campaign against him; a broad range of right-wing political figures and media outlets internationally who invoke his name as a conspiracy explanation.
- **Comparative context:** Soros functions in right-wing disinformation much as a single named "globalist" enemy figure recurs across authoritarian propaganda historically — the vault notes the structural parallel without equating the content.

## Public statements

- Soros has described his philanthropic mission through the concept of the "open society" — drawn from the philosopher Karl Popper — meaning a society of pluralism, accountable government, and free debate, as against "closed" or authoritarian society.
- He has publicly acknowledged that his prominence and Jewish identity make him a useful target, while continuing to fund the work that attracts the attacks.

## Open questions

- **Scale of actual political funding vs. attributed influence:** Soros does fund advocacy and civil-society organizations, including some politically active U.S. groups; this is real and documented. The conspiracy theories vastly inflate this into secret control of governments and movements. The vault's standard is to distinguish his documented grant-making from the fabricated "puppet-master" narrative. `#claim/disputed` applies to nearly all specific "Soros funded X plot" claims.
- **The antisemitism question:** Researchers broadly agree the Soros conspiracy complex draws on antisemitic tropes, though defenders of specific claims dispute this characterization. The vault follows the ADL/SPLC documentation here.
