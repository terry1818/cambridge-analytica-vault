# James Dobson

## Summary

James Clayton Dobson Jr. (1936–2025) was an American evangelical psychologist, author, and broadcaster who became one of the most consequential organizers of the late-twentieth-century [[religious right]]. Born in Shreveport, Louisiana, into a family of Nazarene evangelists, he earned a Ph.D. in child development from the University of Southern California and served as an associate clinical professor of pediatrics at the USC School of Medicine. He came to national attention in 1970 with the bestselling parenting book *Dare to Discipline*, which advocated corporal punishment and strict parental authority within an explicitly Christian framework.

In 1977 Dobson left academic life to launch the radio program *Focus on the Family*, which grew into a media empire heard, at its peak, on more than 7,000 stations in over a dozen languages. Dobson's distinctive achievement — and the reason he appears in this vault — was converting an apolitical-seeming parenting ministry into a political mobilization engine. He helped invent the language of "family values" politics, co-founded the [[Family Research Council]] as a Washington lobbying arm in 1983, and built voter-targeting and action infrastructure that delivered conservative Christian voters to the Republican Party. He advised every Republican president from [[Ronald Reagan]] to [[Donald Trump]] and was repeatedly ranked among the most influential conservative figures in American public life.

## Key facts

- **Role(s):** Child psychologist; author; radio host; founder and chairman of [[Focus on the Family]]; co-founder of the [[Family Research Council]]; founder of the Dr. James Dobson Family Institute.
- **Affiliations:** [[Focus on the Family]], [[Family Research Council]], Dr. James Dobson Family Institute, USC School of Medicine.
- **Active period:** 1970–2022 (book publication through retirement from Family Talk leadership).
- **Known for:** *Dare to Discipline* (1970); the *Focus on the Family* broadcast; "family values" political framing; mobilizing the evangelical vote.

## Relationships

- **[[Focus on the Family]]** — organization he founded and led 1977–2010; the institutional base of his influence.
- **[[Family Research Council]]** — Washington lobbying organization he co-founded in 1983; later led by [[Tony Perkins]].
- **[[Ronald Reagan]]** — first president Dobson advised; appointed Dobson to federal commissions, including on pornography.
- **[[Donald Trump]]** — Dobson endorsed Trump in 2016 and publicly vouched for the authenticity of his religious conversion, helping consolidate evangelical support.
- **[[Tony Perkins]]** — succeeded into leadership of the Family Research Council; an institutional heir.
- **Phyllis Schlafly** — contemporary and ally in the broader "family values" coalition of the religious right; see [[Phyllis Schlafly]].

## Public statements

- "The first principle of the Christian school is that God is the author of all knowledge..." — recurring framing of his support for Christian and home education, context for the [[Christian school movement]].
- 2016: Publicly stated he believed Donald Trump had recently come to a "personal relationship with Jesus Christ" — a claim widely amplified to reassure evangelical voters and later questioned.
