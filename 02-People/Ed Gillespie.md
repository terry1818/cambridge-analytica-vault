# Ed Gillespie

## Summary

Ed Gillespie is a veteran Republican strategist who served as RNC chairman (2003–2005), counselor to President George W. Bush (2007–2009), and crucially as national chairman of the [[Republican State Leadership Committee]] beginning January 2010. In that role, he partnered with RSLC executive director [[Chris Jankowski]] to launch and fund [[REDMAP]], the Redistricting Majority Project. Gillespie brought the donor network, national credibility, and strategic framing that allowed the RSLC to raise ~$30 million for the 2010 state-legislature targeting campaign. He subsequently ran unsuccessfully for U.S. Senate in Virginia (2014, lost by 0.8%) and for Virginia governor (2017, lost by approximately 9 points).

## Key Facts

- Born August 1, 1961; grew up in New Jersey.
- Worked as a telephone solicitor for the RNC in 1985 as an early career position.
- Spent a decade as a top aide to House Majority Leader Dick Armey (R-TX).
- Was a principal drafter of the GOP's 1994 "Contract With America."
- In 2003, was selected as chairman of the RNC; oversaw the party through Bush's 2004 re-election.
- In 2005, Bush assigned him to lead the Supreme Court nomination process that resulted in the confirmation of Samuel Alito.
- Co-founded Quinn Gillespie & Associates, a bipartisan lobbying firm with Democrat Jack Quinn.
- In January 2010, announced as RSLC national chairman; immediately pivoted the RSLC toward redistricting strategy.
- Gillespie articulated the REDMAP theory of change: winning state legislatures = controlling Congressional maps = controlling Congress.
- RSLC under Gillespie raised over $30 million in 2010 and helped turn legislatures red in Ohio, Florida, Wisconsin, North Carolina, Michigan, and elsewhere.

## Timeline

- **1985:** Begins at RNC as phone solicitor.
- **~1985–1994:** Senior aide to House Majority Leader Dick Armey; helps draft "Contract With America."
- **1996:** RNC communications director.
- **2000:** Senior communications advisor, Bush presidential campaign; managed Florida recount communications.
- **2003–2005:** Chairman of the Republican National Committee; oversaw 2004 elections.
- **2005:** Leads Supreme Court nomination process resulting in Alito confirmation.
- **2007–2009:** Counselor to President Bush.
- **January 2010:** Named RSLC national chairman; REDMAP strategy launched.
- **2010:** RSLC raises ~$30M; Republicans gain ~680 state legislative seats; REDMAP succeeds.
- **2014:** Virginia U.S. Senate race; loses to Mark Warner by 0.8%.
- **2017:** Virginia governor's race; loses to Ralph Northam 45%–54%.

## Relationships

- **[[Chris Jankowski]]:** RSLC executive director; Jankowski was the operational architect; Gillespie provided strategic vision and donor credibility.
- **[[Thomas Hofeller]]:** Gillespie's RSLC created the legislative majority conditions that Hofeller then exploited as mapmaker.
- **[[Leonard Leo]]:** Both orbit the same national Republican infrastructure; Gillespie's Federalist Society-adjacent donor network overlaps with Leo's.
- **George W. Bush:** Employer in multiple senior roles; Gillespie was a trusted Bush insider.

## Public Statements

Gillespie has publicly defended REDMAP as a legitimate and rational use of resources, pointing out that both parties target state races. He has been interviewed extensively about REDMAP and has not disavowed the strategy.
