## Summary

Todd Rogers is an American behavioral scientist and professor at the Harvard Kennedy School whose academic work on voter mobilization helped bridge the gap between experimental political science (the [[Gerber and Green field experiments]] tradition) and behavioral economics. He co-founded the [[Analyst Institute]] with [[Hal Malchow]] and [[Mike Podhorzer]], bringing a behavioral-science lens to the question of what GOTV messages actually change voting behavior. [[Source - Issenberg 2012 - The Victory Lab]] discusses Rogers's contributions to the experimental-testing culture on the Democratic side, particularly around message framing and social identity cues.

## Key Facts

- **Education**: BA, Williams College (Religion and Psychology); MA Social Psychology, Harvard (2005); PhD Organizational Behavior, Harvard Business School (2008).
- **Behavioral science framing**: Rogers's experimental work examined not just *whether* a contact raised turnout but *why* — introducing behavioral mechanisms such as identity consistency ("you're a voter" framing), social pressure effects, and the role of habit formation in civic participation.
- **Analyst Institute**: Co-founded the Institute as a vehicle to run and disseminate results from randomized GOTV tests for Democratic-aligned organizations, preventing duplication and building cumulative knowledge.
- **EveryDay Labs**: Co-founded a spinoff applying absence-reduction interventions (proven to cut chronic absenteeism by 10–15%) in K–12 settings — demonstrating the transfer of behavioral-science methods from political to social-policy domains.
- **Harvard Kennedy School**: Currently holds the Weatherhead Professorship in Public Policy and directs the Behavioral Insights Group, which advises governments and campaigns on evidence-based policy and communication.
