# Richard Viguerie

## Summary

Richard Art Viguerie (born September 23, 1933) is the American conservative activist, fundraiser, and publisher most responsible for transforming the financial and organizational infrastructure of the US right through the use of computerized political direct mail. Beginning in the early 1960s, Viguerie pioneered the use of mailing lists to raise money for conservative candidates and organizations — a technique that enabled the New Right to build financially independent institutions outside the Republican Party establishment and without reliance on mass media.

Anne Nelson's *Shadow Network* situates Viguerie as a critical node in the founding generation of the modern institutional right. He was a co-founder of the [[Council for National Policy]] in 1981, alongside [[Paul Weyrich]], Tim LaHaye, Howard Phillips, Morton Blackwell, and Phyllis Schlafly — the organization that became the most significant closed-door coordination mechanism for the American hard right. His direct-mail operations funded many of the organizations that now form the CNP's member base.

By his own account, Viguerie mailed more than 4 billion letters over five decades and raised funds for more than 100 public policy groups. He has been called the "godfather of the New Right" and the "funding father of the conservative movement."

## Key Facts

- Born September 23, 1933, in Houston, Texas.
- Graduated from the University of Houston Law School (did not practice law; entered conservative movement work directly).
- In 1961, became executive secretary of Young Americans for Freedom (YAF), gaining access to the donor and activist lists that seeded his direct-mail operation.
- After the 1964 Goldwater campaign, hand-copied approximately 12,500 donor names from Federal Election Commission records at the Capitol — a foundational act that gave him an independent list to build from.
- Founded American Target Advertising (later Richard A. Viguerie Company / RAVCO), which became the dominant direct-mail firm for conservative organizations.
- Client list grew to include: Conservative Caucus, Committee for the Survival of a Free Congress (with [[Paul Weyrich]]), NCPAC (with Terry Dolan), National Right to Work Committee, American Conservative Union, Gun Owners of America, and dozens more.
- Founded *Conservative Digest* in 1975; served as publisher for ten years. The publication served as an intellectual and organizational hub for the New Right.
- Co-founded the **Council for National Policy** in 1981 with Tim LaHaye, [[Paul Weyrich]], Howard Phillips, [[Morton Blackwell]], Phyllis Schlafly, and others. CNP was designed as a closed, secretive coordination body for the right.
- Clients over the decades included George Wallace, Jesse Helms, Ronald Reagan, Pat Buchanan, and hundreds of conservative organizations.
- As of 2022, listed as a senior executive committee member of the CNP.
- Operates ConservativeHQ.com as an ongoing commentary and organizing platform.
- Known for criticizing the Republican Party establishment from the right, particularly on immigration and what he describes as insufficient conservatism.

## Public Statements

- "Conservatives have an obligation to challenge the old order and the old power structure." (widely attributed in movement conservative sources)
- Called himself the "funding father of the conservative movement" (ConservativeHQ.com and media interviews).
- On direct mail: described it as enabling conservatives to "go around the liberal media" and reach donors directly.
- Frequently criticized the Republican establishment for insufficient ideological commitment, including during the George W. Bush and later Trump eras.
