## Summary

Jack Posobiec is an American far-right political activist and media figure who served as a US Navy intelligence officer before becoming a prominent online personality and television correspondent. He worked as a political correspondent and host for One America News Network (OAN) from 2018 to 2021, then joined Human Events as Senior Editor and Turning Point USA as a show host. Researchers and the Southern Poverty Law Center have documented his role in amplifying disinformation narratives; characterizations of his activities and associations are attributed to those sources. Renée DiResta discusses figures like Posobiec in *Invisible Rulers* as examples of influential online amplifiers who shape information ecosystems without holding formal political office.

**Note on sourcing:** Characterizations of Posobiec's activities and associations below are explicitly attributed to their sources (SPLC, Wikipedia, documented reporting). This note documents publicly reported facts; the vault does not independently adjudicate disputed characterizations.

## Key facts

- Former US Navy intelligence officer, which provides his media persona a credibility framing that analysts note is actively deployed.
- Rose to prominence during the 2016 election as an online amplifier of pro-Trump content and, separately, of conspiracy narratives.
- In 2017, promoted the Pizzagate conspiracy theory — later debunked — on Twitter and via live streams, including a disruptive action at a Washington DC theatre. (Documented in contemporaneous news reporting.)
- OAN correspondent and host from 2018 to May 2021.
- In September 2018, presented the pseudonymous far-right online figure "Microchip" on OAN without disclosing that person's documented role in spreading disinformation (per SPLC, 2022).
- The SPLC opened a formal extremist file on Posobiec in June 2022, citing documented links to Proud Boys, Oath Keepers, white nationalist networks, and Polish far-right organizations. The SPLC's characterizations are contested by Posobiec.
- The SPLC and researchers have documented his amplification of Russian military intelligence-aligned narratives (per SPLC extremist file).
- Participated in and helped promote the "Stop the Steal" campaign (2020–2021), which cast doubt on presidential election results.
- Left OAN in May 2021 for Human Events (Senior Editor) and Turning Point USA (host).
- Renée DiResta's *Invisible Rulers* uses influencer networks of this type — high-follower, institutionally amplified, ideologically consistent — to illustrate how modern propaganda operates without central coordination.
