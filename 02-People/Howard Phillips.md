# Howard Phillips

## Summary

Howard Phillips (1941–2013) was an American conservative organizer who was one of the four or five central architects of the 1970s [[New Right]]. A Boston-born Harvard graduate, Phillips first gained national notice in 1973 when President Nixon appointed him to dismantle the Office of Economic Opportunity — the agency administering Lyndon Johnson's War on Poverty. When Nixon's resignation stalled that effort, Phillips left government and in 1974 founded **The Conservative Caucus**, a grassroots organizing vehicle designed to give conservatives a nationwide field operation.

Phillips matters to this vault as a connective node between movement conservatism and the religious right. Working alongside [[Paul Weyrich]], direct-mail pioneer [[Richard Viguerie]], and Terry Dolan of the National Conservative Political Action Committee, Phillips helped construct the institutional New Right and was instrumental in persuading the Rev. Jerry Falwell to found the Moral Majority in 1979 — the moment evangelical Protestantism was organized as a Republican voting bloc. He also helped Judie Brown launch the American Life League. Increasingly convinced that the Republican Party would not deliver on social and constitutional issues, Phillips broke with it: in 1992 he founded the U.S. Taxpayers Party, renamed the [[Constitution Party]] in 1999, running as its presidential candidate in 1992, 1996, and 2000 on a platform of returning American government to a strict, often explicitly Christian, reading of the Constitution. That project — locking governance to a fixed constitutional reading beyond ordinary democratic revision — is an early sibling of the vault's [[constitutional capture]] thread.

## Key facts

- **Role(s):** Conservative organizer; founder of The Conservative Caucus (1974); founder of the U.S. Taxpayers Party / Constitution Party; three-time presidential candidate.
- **Affiliations:** The Conservative Caucus; [[Constitution Party]]; U.S. Office of Economic Opportunity (1973).
- **Active period:** ~1973–2008.
- **Known for:** Co-founding the institutional New Right; brokering the Moral Majority; building a third party around constitutional originalism and Christian governance.

## Relationships

- **[[Paul Weyrich]]** — fellow New Right founder; the movement's institution-builder (Heritage Foundation, Committee for the Survival of a Free Congress).
- **[[Richard Viguerie]]** — direct-mail fundraising pioneer who financed New Right organizations, including Phillips's.
- **Terry Dolan** — head of the National Conservative Political Action Committee (NCPAC); fourth New Right strategist.
- **Jerry Falwell** — evangelical leader Phillips helped recruit into politics via the Moral Majority.
- **[[Constitution Party]]** — the third party Phillips founded and led.
- **[[New Right]]** — the 1970s movement Phillips helped create.

## Public statements

- Phillips framed The Conservative Caucus as giving conservatives "a nationwide grassroots voice" independent of the Republican establishment.
- As a Constitution Party candidate, he campaigned to "return government to the U.S. Constitution's provisions and limitations," explicitly invoking a biblical and originalist constitutional vision.
