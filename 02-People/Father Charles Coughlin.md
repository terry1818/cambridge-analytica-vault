## Summary

Charles Coughlin was a Canadian-American Catholic priest based in Royal Oak, Michigan, whose radio programme made him one of the most listened-to voices in 1930s America. Initially a New Deal supporter, he broke with Roosevelt by 1934 and built a vast audience for increasingly demagogic content. By 1938 he was publishing explicit antisemitic material in his magazine *Social Justice*, reproducing passages from the Nazi propaganda minister Joseph Goebbels and serialising excerpts from the forged *Protocols of the Elders of Zion*. He inspired the formation of the [[Christian Front]], a street-level antisemitic militia. Rachel Maddow's *Prequel* treats Coughlin as the archetype of mass-media radicalisation — the 1930s precedent for information-warfare techniques refined by later movements.

## Key facts

- Born 25 October 1891, Hamilton, Ontario, Canada; died 27 October 1979.
- Ordained 1916; assigned in 1923 to the National Shrine of the Little Flower, Royal Oak, Michigan.
- Began radio broadcasting in 1926 as a local programme; went national in 1930 on CBS, later WOR/WGN network. By the early 1930s, an estimated 30–40 million weekly listeners (US population ~120 million).
- 1932–33: strong public supporter of Franklin D. Roosevelt and the New Deal ("Roosevelt or Ruin").
- 1934: broke with Roosevelt; founded the **National Union for Social Justice**, a mass-membership political organisation.
- 1936: founded *Social Justice* magazine; ran the Union Party presidential campaign of William Lemke (received 2% of the vote).
- 1938: *Social Justice* began explicit antisemitic content following Kristallnacht; Coughlin blamed Jews for their own persecution.
- *Social Justice* serialised the *Protocols of the Elders of Zion* (1938–39) and reproduced passages from Goebbels speeches without attribution.
- 1938: broadcast call for a "crusade against the anti-Christian forces of the Red Revolution" — the broadcast credited with inspiring the formation of the [[Christian Front]].
- 1940: FBI arrested 17 Christian Front members in Brooklyn for conspiring to overthrow the government.
- April 1942: Attorney General Francis Biddle opened grand jury investigation of *Social Justice* under the Espionage Act.
- May 1942: US Post Office revoked *Social Justice*'s second-class mailing privilege.
- Archbishop of Detroit Edward Mooney ordered Coughlin to cease all non-priestly activities; Coughlin complied and returned to parish work at Royal Oak until his retirement in 1966.

## Relationships

- [[Christian Front]] — Coughlin's 1938 broadcast directly inspired the formation of this antisemitic militia; he later disavowed it after FBI arrests.
- [[German-American Bund]] — ideological fellow-travellers; both organisations participated in antisemitic far-right milieu of the late 1930s.
- [[Lawrence Dennis]] — shared the 1930s pro-fascist intellectual space; both appeared as defendants (or witnesses) in the sedition trial milieu.
- [[Great Sedition Trial 1944]] — Coughlin himself was not indicted but several figures in his orbit were; his *Social Justice* content was part of the evidentiary backdrop.

## Open questions

- Coughlin's relationship with the Vatican and whether any formal ecclesiastical disapproval of his antisemitic content was communicated before 1942.
- The extent to which Coughlin's media infrastructure (mailing lists, radio network affiliations) was inherited by successor far-right organisations.
