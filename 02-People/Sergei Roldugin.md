## Summary

Sergei Roldugin is a celebrated Russian cellist and conductor, artistic director of the St. Petersburg House of Music, and one of [[Vladimir Putin]]'s oldest friends — he introduced Putin to his future wife and is godfather to Putin's elder daughter Maria. He is unremarkable by normal political or financial criteria. His significance in this vault is his prominent appearance in the 2016 Panama Papers, where ICIJ and partner organizations found his name listed as the nominal owner of offshore companies that had received payments from other companies worth, in total, at least $2 billion — a sum vastly disproportionate to his earnings as a musician. Catherine Belton's *Putin's People* (2020) presents him as a nominee custodian for Putin-circle assets, using offshore structures linked to [[Bank Rossiya]]'s network.

Roldugin himself has acknowledged holding companies but stated the funds were donations from wealthy Russian businessmen for the purchase of musical instruments for Russia. He has not been criminally charged in any jurisdiction. These remain allegations supported by documentary evidence (the leaked Mossack Fonseca records) but not adjudicated findings.

## Key Facts

- Born 1951; studied cello at the Leningrad Conservatory; internationally recognized performer.
- Artistic director of the St. Petersburg House of Music, a cultural institution he founded with state support.
- Friendship with Putin dates to the 1970s in Leningrad; among the most longstanding personal relationships in Putin's documented life.
- ICIJ (April 2016): Panama Papers records show Roldugin as listed owner of at least three offshore companies incorporated by Mossack Fonseca; these companies had received or paid sums in the hundreds of millions of dollars.
- One company, Sonnette Overseas, held a stake giving it influence over a large share of Russia's TV advertising market. Another, International Media Overseas, received a payment from a state bank structured as a discounted loan that was never repaid — functioning as an outright transfer of funds.
- OCCRP Troika Laundromat investigation (2019): Further Roldugin-linked entities found to have received funds from the broader $4.6 billion Troika Laundromat scheme.
- Swiss authorities investigated Swiss bank employees in connection with transactions flowing through Roldugin-linked accounts; indictments followed (as of ICIJ 2024 reporting).
- Roldugin has not been placed on US, EU, or UK sanctions lists as of mid-2026.

## Relationships

- **[[Vladimir Putin]]**: Lifelong friend; introduced Putin to Lyudmila Shkrebneva; godfather to Maria Putin.
- **[[Bank Rossiya]]**: His offshore companies appear to operate within the Kovalchuk/Rossiya financial network, per ICIJ analysis.
- **[[Yury Kovalchuk]]**: Rossiya network principal; Roldugin's offshore structures interlock with Rossiya-affiliated entities per Panama Papers.

## Public Statements

In a 2016 Russian television interview, Roldugin stated that the money in his companies was "largely made up of donations from rich businessmen" for purchasing musical instruments for Russian conservatories. He did not deny holding the companies. He has not otherwise addressed the investigation in detail.
