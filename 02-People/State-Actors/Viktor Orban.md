# Viktor Orban

Viktor Orban (born 1963) is the Prime Minister of Hungary (1998-2002 and continuously since 2010), leader of the Fidesz party. Orban is the principal European right-wing leader and the explicit operational-and-intellectual model for the contemporary US Tech-Right and religious-right intellectual programme.

## The "illiberal democracy" framing

Orban's 2014 speech in which he explicitly endorsed "illiberal democracy" as a governance model was the operationally-significant intellectual articulation of the Hungarian governance pattern. The framing distinguishes between:

- Liberal democracy (the traditional Western-European governance model with substantial institutional protection for minorities, independent courts, free press, and limited state involvement in cultural-political questions)
- Illiberal democracy (the Orban-articulated alternative with majoritarian decision-making, substantial state involvement in cultural-political questions, and reduced institutional protection for minorities and dissenting press)

The framing has been substantially adopted by contemporary US conservative-intellectual figures including [[Patrick Deneen]], [[Adrian Vermeule]], [[Sohrab Ahmari]], and the broader [[Catholic Integralism]] / [[First Things magazine]] intellectual venue.

## CPAC Hungary

CPAC (Conservative Political Action Conference, the [[American Conservative Union]]-organised annual US conservative-movement conference) launched CPAC Hungary in 2022 as the first European CPAC. The 2022, 2023, 2024, and 2025 CPAC Hungary events have substantially integrated Hungarian-government-and-Fidesz-party leadership with US conservative-movement leadership including Trump (in some cycles), [[Steve Bannon]], [[Kari Lake]], [[Mike Pence]] (early on, less recently), and various Project 2025-aligned figures.

## The Hungarian model in practice

The Orban-era Hungarian operational pattern has included:

- Substantial restructuring of the Hungarian judiciary
- Substantial state involvement in media ownership (multiple major Hungarian media outlets have moved into government-aligned ownership)
- Substantial state involvement in higher-education governance (including the closure of Central European University, which relocated to Vienna)
- Substantial state involvement in cultural-policy questions including the 2021 anti-LGBTQ law
- Sustained tension with EU institutions over rule-of-law and minority-rights questions
- Substantial state-aligned media-and-information-environment management

US conservative-movement leadership has publicly endorsed substantial elements of the Hungarian operational pattern as a model for US policy.
