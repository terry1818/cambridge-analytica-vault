# Kirill Dmitriev

## Summary

Russian businessman; CEO of the **Russian Direct Investment Fund (RDIF)** since its 2011 founding. RDIF is Russia's sovereign wealth fund, established by Putin as a direct investment vehicle. Considered to operate **closely with Putin personally** per [[Source Senate Intel Vol 5 2020]]. Met with [[Erik Prince]] in the **[[Seychelles January 2017 meeting]]** to discuss potential back-channel between incoming Trump administration and Kremlin.

## Background

- Born in Kyiv, Soviet Ukraine
- Education at Stanford and Harvard Business School
- Pre-RDIF career at Goldman Sachs, McKinsey
- Married to Natalia Popova, friend of Putin's daughter Katerina Tikhonova
- Spent significant time in US during education and early career

## RDIF role

- Founded by Putin 2011 as sovereign wealth fund
- $10B+ in assets under management
- Co-investment partnerships with sovereign wealth funds globally
- US Treasury sanctioned RDIF in 2022 after Russian invasion of Ukraine

## Seychelles January 2017 meeting

Per [[Source Mueller Report 2019]] and [[Source Senate Intel Vol 5 2020]]:

- **2017-01-11** -- meeting in Seychelles between Dmitriev, [[Erik Prince]], [[George Nader]] (UAE intermediary)
- Subject of Mueller investigation
- Prince claimed meeting was incidental; Mueller documented evidence contradicting this
- Topic per multiple sources: establishing back-channel between Trump and Russia
- UAE acting as intermediary; Mohammed bin Zayed arranged

## 2024-2026 Trump 2.0 engagement

- Reported as continuing back-channel role in Trump 2.0 Russia-Ukraine negotiations
- 2025+ -- appears in journalism on Trump 2.0 Russia engagement
- Dmitriev remains under US Treasury sanctions but reportedly conducts informal diplomacy
