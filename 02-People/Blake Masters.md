## Summary

Blake Masters is a venture capitalist and political operative best known as a protégé and close associate of [[Peter Thiel]]. He co-authored *Zero to One: Notes on Startups, or How to Build the Future* (2014) with Thiel, based on notes Masters took at Stanford Law School in 2012 when Thiel delivered a startup course. Masters subsequently served as COO of Thiel Capital and president of the [[Thiel Fellowship|Thiel Foundation]]. In 2022, backed by $15 million from Thiel, he won the Republican primary for US Senate in Arizona but lost the general election to incumbent Democrat Mark Kelly. His campaign is the primary case study in Chafkin's *The Contrarian* for Thiel's direct electoral investment in installing ideological allies in government.

## Key facts

- Stanford undergraduate; Stanford Law School (JD, where he took Thiel's startup course that became *Zero to One*).
- Served as COO of Thiel Capital and president of the Thiel Foundation — operational roles inside the Thiel enterprise, not merely advisory.
- *Zero to One* (Portfolio/Penguin, 2014): co-authored with Thiel; bestselling business book arguing that the greatest value is created by building genuinely new things ("going from zero to one") rather than copying existing models. The book encodes Thiel's contrarian, monopoly-seeking investment philosophy.
- In 2022, Thiel donated $15 million to a super PAC supporting Masters, the largest single donor commitment to any Senate primary in that cycle.
- Masters won the Republican primary over Arizona AG Mark Brnovich and businessman Jim Lamon.
- Lost the general election to Mark Kelly; received the fewest votes of any major statewide Republican candidate in the 2022 Arizona races — a significant underperformance suggesting Thiel's money could not overcome Masters's liabilities as a candidate.
- Masters ran on an immigration restrictionist, tech-right platform that mirrored Thiel's ideological priorities: skepticism of globalism, anti-China hawkishness, and anti-establishment rhetoric.
- [[J.D. Vance]] is the parallel case: another Thiel protégé funded to Senate victory in 2022 (Ohio).

## Relationships

- **[[Peter Thiel]]**: mentor, employer, primary financial backer; Masters is Thiel's most prominent direct investment in an electoral candidate (alongside [[J.D. Vance]]).
- **[[J.D. Vance]]**: parallel Thiel protégé; both backed in 2022 Senate races; Vance won, Masters lost.
- **[[Thiel Fellowship]]**: Masters ran the Thiel Foundation which administers the Fellowship — direct operational link.
- **[[Founders Fund]]**: Thiel's primary venture fund; Masters is connected through Thiel Capital.

## Public statements

- Campaigned on immigration restriction, confrontational China policy, skepticism of the administrative state, and tech-right themes.
- Has expressed views aligned with Thiel's documented skepticism of democratic institutions and globalism.
