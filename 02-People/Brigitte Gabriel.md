## Summary

Brigitte Gabriel (born Nour Saman, October 21, 1964) is a Lebanese-American conservative activist who founded [[ACT for America]] and its predecessor organization, American Congress for Truth. A Maronite Christian whose family home was destroyed during the Lebanese Civil War, Gabriel built an influential public persona around her personal experience of Islamist violence. She has authored two major books — *Because They Hate* (2006) and *They Must Be Stopped* (2008) — that became bestsellers in conservative circles. Susan Posner's *Unholy* (2020) places Gabriel within the broader theocratic and Islamophobic wing of the American religious right. Critics, including the SPLC and CAIR, characterize her as a hate group leader; she disputes the designation.

## Key facts

- Born October 21, 1964, in Marjeyoun District, Lebanon, to a Maronite Christian family.
- During the Lebanese Civil War, Muslim militants destroyed her family home; she spent approximately seven years (roughly ages 10–17) living in an 8-by-10-foot bomb shelter, sustained by Israeli military food aid.
- Emigrated to the United States; worked in television broadcasting.
- Founded the American Congress for Truth (2002) as a speakers bureau; expanded into ACT for America (2007) as a formal advocacy organization.
- Regular Fox News commentator; invited speaker at CPAC, Faith and Freedom Coalition events, and [[Council for National Policy]] gatherings.
- Had documented access to Trump administration officials 2017–2021, including meetings at the White House and with Department of Homeland Security.
- The New York Times described her book as presenting "a portrait of Islam so thoroughly bent on destruction and domination that it is unrecognizable to those who study or practice the religion."
- SPLC lists ACT for America as a hate group; Gabriel contests this characterization vigorously.
- Has stated: "Every practicing Muslim is a radical Muslim."
