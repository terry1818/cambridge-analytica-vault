## Summary

Andrew L. Whitehead is a Professor of Sociology at Indiana University Indianapolis (IUI, formerly IUPUI) and one of the leading empirical scholars of [[Christian Nationalism]] in the United States. He co-authored *Taking America Back for God: Christian Nationalism in the United States* (Oxford University Press, 2020) with [[Samuel Perry]], which became the foundational academic text measuring and analyzing the Christian nationalist worldview. The book won the 2021 Distinguished Book Award from the Society for the Scientific Study of Religion. Whitehead's work moves Christian nationalism from the realm of political commentary into quantifiable social science by using survey data to track who holds these beliefs, how intensely, and to what political effect.

## Key facts

- Professor of Sociology at Indiana University Indianapolis; also serves as Project Director at the Center for the Study of Religion and American Culture at IUI.
- Co-Director of the Association of Religion Data Archives (ARDA), described as the world's largest online repository of religion data.
- *Taking America Back for God* (2020) draws on multiple waves of the [[Baylor Religion Survey]] and in-depth interviews to document Christian nationalism's scope and political consequences.
- Developed the [[Christian Nationalism Scale]] — a six-item survey instrument that measures adherence to Christian nationalist ideology — in collaboration with Perry.
- The scale produces the [[Whitehead Perry CN typology|four-category typology]]: Ambassadors, Accommodators, Resisters, and Rejecters.
- Received the Indiana University Indianapolis Research Trailblazer Award and the Outstanding Tenure Track Faculty Award (School of Liberal Arts), both in 2023.
- His institutional context (IUI is a public research university in Indianapolis) is notable: Christian nationalism scholarship here is not produced from within a religious institution with an ideological stake.

## Relationships

- **[[Samuel Perry]]** — co-author and close collaborator; Perry is based at the University of Oklahoma; together they are the architects of the CN Scale and four-category typology.
- **[[Philip Gorski]]** — Gorski (Yale) collaborated with Perry (not Whitehead) on *The Flag and the Cross* (2022); the two projects are complementary and frequently cited together.
- **[[Robert P. Jones]]** — parallel scholar tracking Christian nationalism via [[PRRI]] surveys; Jones's work overlaps in subject matter but uses different instruments.
- Center for the Study of Religion and American Culture at IUI is an important institutional node for this scholarship.

## Public statements

Whitehead has argued that Christian nationalism is not simply intensified religiosity but a distinct ideological system that fuses Christian identity with ethnic and civic belonging. In a 2020 Salon interview he noted: "Christian nationalism is not just people who are very religious and conservative. It's a particular political ideology that can be held by people who rarely, if ever, attend church."
