## Summary

Joseph Coors Sr. was the grandson of Adolph Coors, president of the family's Colorado brewing company, and the pivotal early funder who made the [[Heritage Foundation]] possible. In 1973, Coors provided $250,000 to cover the Foundation's first-year budget and committed $300,000 annually thereafter, working with [[Paul Weyrich]] and Edwin Feulner to build the institution. The Heritage Foundation became the central intellectual infrastructure node of the modern conservative movement, and Coors's early capitalization is documented as foundational.

Anne Nelson's [[Source Anne Nelson Shadow Network 2019]] situates Coors within the broader donor and organizational network — the interconnected think tanks, media organizations, and policy groups — that the [[Council for National Policy]] coordinated. Coors was among the generation of industrial-fortune donors (alongside the Hunt family, [[Edgar Prince]], and later the DeVos family) who provided the capital base for the religious and political right's institutional expansion from the 1970s onward.

The *Wall Street Journal* wrote in his obituary: "The conservative movement simply would not exist in the form it does today without the profound influence of Joe Coors."

## Key Facts

- Born November 12, 1917, in Golden, Colorado; died March 15, 2003
- Grandson of brewer Adolph Coors; B.S. chemical engineering and M.S. Cornell University (1939/1941)
- Served in US Army during World War II
- Became president of Coors Brewing Company
- Early Ronald Reagan supporter; backed Reagan's 1968 presidential bid
- Concluded after 1968 that conservative intellectual infrastructure was as important as good candidates
- Provided $250,000 founding capital for Heritage Foundation (1973); $300,000/year ongoing
- Principal backer of the Free Congress Foundation (Paul Weyrich's social-issues think tank)
- Principal funder of Mountain States Legal Foundation (public-interest law firm, forerunner to Federalist Society-connected litigation shops)
- Supported the Independence Institute (Colorado free-market think tank)
- Member of Reagan's Kitchen Cabinet during the 1980 campaign
- Served on Heritage Foundation board; deeply involved in its programmatic direction
- Died March 15, 2003, at age 85
