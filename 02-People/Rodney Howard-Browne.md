## Summary

Rodney Howard-Browne (born June 12, 1961) is a South African-born American charismatic evangelist and pastor who is credited with introducing or transmitting the "holy laughter" revival that swept through Pentecostal and charismatic churches in the early 1990s. His services — characterized by spontaneous laughter, shaking, speaking in tongues, and "falling under the Spirit" — sparked the Toronto Blessing (1994) and the Brownsville Revival (1995), two of the most significant charismatic renewal events in modern American church history. Now pastor of The River at Tampa Bay Church in Tampa, Florida, Howard-Browne became a political figure in the Trump era through his White House access and his 2020 arrest for violating COVID public health orders. Susan Posner's *Unholy* (2020) situates Howard-Browne within the intersection of Pentecostal charismatic Christianity and the theocratic-wing political project.

## Key facts

- Born June 12, 1961, in Port Elizabeth, South Africa.
- Emigrated to the United States; settled in Tampa, Florida, in the mid-1990s.
- In 1993, conducted a revival series at Carpenter's Home Church in Lakeland, Florida, with Karl Strader, where "holy laughter" manifestations became widely documented.
- In 1994, ministered at the Toronto Airport Vineyard Church (with Randy Clark); this revival became known as the "Toronto Blessing" and spread globally.
- In 1995, associated with the beginning of the Brownsville Revival at Assemblies of God Brownsville Church in Pensacola, Florida.
- Founded The River at Tampa Bay Church in 1996; founded Revival Ministries International in 1997; founded River Bible Institute.
- Participated in a White House prayer ceremony laying hands on Donald Trump in 2017.
- March 29, 2020: held two large in-person church services at The River despite Hillsborough County's safer-at-home order limiting gatherings to 10 people.
- March 30, 2020: arrested on misdemeanor charges of unlawful assembly and violation of public health rules; released on $500 bond.
- May 15, 2020: charges dropped after Howard-Browne took steps toward social distancing compliance.
- Has made public statements promoting conspiracy theories, including claims about population control and global elites.
