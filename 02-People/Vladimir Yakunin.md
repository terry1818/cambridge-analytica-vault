## Summary

Vladimir Yakunin is a former KGB officer and one of the eight founding members of the [[Ozero Cooperative]], the St. Petersburg dacha cooperative that serves as the founding social network of the Putin inner circle. He served as president of Russian Railways (RZhD) from 2005 to 2015, overseeing Russia's largest employer (approximately 800,000 employees) and a critical piece of economic infrastructure. The US Treasury sanctioned him in March 2014 in response to Russia's annexation of Crimea, citing his role as a close Putin associate. He was removed from the Railways post in August 2015; observers linked the dismissal to allegations of corruption and mismanagement, though the official explanation cited restructuring. Since leaving government, Yakunin has become a prominent advocate for Russian Orthodox Christian conservatism and a critic of Western liberalism.

## Key Facts

- Born 1948; military engineer training; joined the KGB in the 1970s.
- Worked as a Soviet trade official in the US in the 1980s, where he is believed to have conducted intelligence activities.
- Met Putin in St. Petersburg in the early 1990s; became a neighbor and co-founder of the Ozero Cooperative in 1996.
- Appointed head of Russian Railways in 2005; the role made him one of the most powerful executives in Russia.
- Russian Railways under his tenure was the subject of multiple whistleblower and journalistic corruption allegations (RFERL reporting, 2016), including claims of inflated procurement and asset appropriation.
- The Australian government described him as "a close personal and financial associate of Vladimir Putin" in its sanctions documentation.
- World Public Forum – Dialogue of Civilizations: a non-governmental organization Yakunin founded (2002) ostensibly promoting civilizational dialogue; criticized by some analysts as a Kremlin soft-power vehicle.
- St. Andrew the First-Called Foundation: another Yakunin-associated body promoting Russian Orthodoxy and cultural conservatism.
- After leaving Russian Railways, Yakunin based himself partly in Germany (until UK/EU sanctions) and gave lectures at European universities promoting a "multipolar" and anti-liberal worldview.

## Relationships

- **[[Ozero Cooperative]]**: Founding member; this relationship predates Putin's national prominence.
- **Viktor Ivanov**: Fellow KGB alumnus and St. Petersburg Putin associate; documented in investigative reporting on Tambov Gang-connected security arrangements.

## Public Statements

Yakunin has been publicly critical of Western liberalism and NATO. He has promoted Russian Orthodox Christian civilization as an alternative to what he describes as the atomized, materialistic West. He has denied corruption allegations.
