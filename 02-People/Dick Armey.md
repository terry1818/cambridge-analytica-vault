# Dick Armey

## Summary

Richard Keith "Dick" Armey is a former Republican congressman from Texas (1985–2003) and House Majority Leader (1995–2003) who, after leaving Congress, became the public face and organizational chairman of [[FreedomWorks]], the Koch-linked nonprofit that served as principal infrastructure for the [[Tea Party movement]] after 2009. Armey's biography bridges two eras of the patron-class right: he was a policy architect of the Gingrich Revolution of 1994 (co-writing the Contract with America) and then a post-congressional organizer who helped convert Tea Party energy into electoral primary pressure against moderate Republicans. His career exemplifies the revolving door between government power and the dark-money organizational ecosystem.

## Key Facts

- Born July 7, 1940, in Cando, North Dakota, a small farming town
- BA from Jamestown College; MA from University of North Dakota; PhD in economics from University of Oklahoma
- Academic career: economics professor at West Texas State (1967–1968), Austin College (1968–1972), North Texas State University (chairman of economics department, 1977–1983)
- Elected to Congress 1984 as part of the "Texas Six-Pack" — six freshman Republicans from Texas
- Served 18 years in the House; rose to Republican Conference Chairman (1993) and Majority Leader (1995)
- With Newt Gingrich, co-drafted the 1994 Contract with America — the Republican legislative manifesto that ended 40 years of Democratic House control
- Known for libertarian leanings within the Republican Party; favored flat tax proposals and limited government
- Name gave FreedomWorks its name: his saying "freedom works" was adopted as the organization's title
- Resigned from FreedomWorks in December 2012 amid an internal power struggle, receiving an $8 million consulting contract

## Relationships

- **Newt Gingrich**: House partner; Armey served as Gingrich's deputy as Majority Leader after the 1994 Revolution; the two had a collegially competitive relationship
- **Matt Kibbe**: FreedomWorks president who was the operational counterpart to Armey's chairman role; their relationship ended acrimoniously in 2012
- **[[Charles Koch]] / Koch Network**: Armey's FreedomWorks operated in close coordination with the Koch network; CSE was a Koch-founded organization before the 2004 split
- **Jack Kemp**: Co-chairman of FreedomWorks alongside Armey; supply-side conservative icon

## Public Statements
