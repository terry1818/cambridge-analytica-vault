## Summary

Hans-Hermann Hoppe is a German-American economist and political philosopher associated with Austrian School economics and anarcho-capitalism. His 2001 book *Democracy: The God That Failed* argues that democracy is a degenerate form of government that systematically raises time preference, inflates the state, and destroys civilization. He prefers a stateless natural order composed of private "covenant communities" whose owners set and enforce their own rules — including exclusion of those deemed incompatible. His work is a key intellectual upstream source for both the libertarian anti-democratic right and, more distantly, for [[Plutocratic secession]] thinking in [[Peter Thiel]]'s orbit and the broader [[Tech Right]].

## Key facts

- Born 1949 in Eschwege, West Germany. PhD from Goethe University Frankfurt; influenced by Frankfurt School critical theory before converting to Rothbardian libertarianism under Murray Rothbard at NYU.
- Professor of economics at UNLV 1986–2008 (emeritus thereafter).
- Senior fellow at the Ludwig von Mises Institute, Auburn, Alabama — the primary US institutional home for Rothbardian anarcho-capitalism.
- Founded the Property and Freedom Society in 2006 in Bodrum, Turkey; annual conferences bring together anarcho-capitalists, paleoconservatives, and figures from the European far-right.
- *Democracy: The God That Failed* (2001): compares monarchy favorably to democracy on the grounds that monarchs have longer time horizons and treat the state as private property; advocates covenant communities with exclusion rights; includes passages favoring removal of "democrats and homosexuals" from private communities that have been widely cited by far-right movements.
- His covenant community concept parallels the "governance as a service" model of [[Próspera]] and other ZEDEs — private jurisdiction, selective entry, contractual coexistence agreements.
- Hoppe explicitly influenced by [[Curtis Yarvin]] (Mencius Moldbug) and Nick Land's Dark Enlightenment circles, which drew heavily on his anti-democratic arguments. The influence ran in both directions.
- Not a direct organizational actor in the charter-cities or Tech Right projects; his role is ideological supply.

## Relationships

- **Murray Rothbard**: mentor; Hoppe developed and radicalized Rothbard's anarcho-capitalism.
- **Ludwig von Mises Institute**: primary institutional affiliation; also home to paleoconservative and neo-Confederate currents.
- **[[Curtis Yarvin]]**: parallel influence on anti-democratic Tech Right; both reject democratic legitimacy and favor private sovereign jurisdictions.
- **[[Peter Thiel]]**: Thiel's 2009 Cato Unbound essay ("I no longer believe that freedom and democracy are compatible") echoes Hoppean premises; no documented direct relationship but clear ideological overlap.
- **[[Dark Enlightenment]]** / neoreaction: Hoppe's work cited extensively; covenant communities parallel neoreactionary "patchwork" sovereignty.
- **[[Próspera]]** / ZEDEs: the covenant community model (private entry agreements, owner-set rules, exclusion rights) maps closely onto ZEDE governance structures.

## Public statements

- "Democracy is a soft variant of communism." (frequently quoted)
- On covenant communities: property owners have the right to establish admission criteria and to physically exclude those incompatible with community norms — including "democrats" and others whose lifestyle undermines the community's character. (*Democracy: The God That Failed*, Chapter 12)
- Characterized democracy as systematically producing decivilization through high time preference and state expansion.
