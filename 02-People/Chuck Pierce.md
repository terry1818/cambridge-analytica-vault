## Summary

Chuck Pierce is one of the foundational institutional figures of the [[New Apostolic Reformation]] (NAR), the loose but consequential network of charismatic Christian leaders who believe that God has restored the offices of apostle and prophet to the contemporary church, and that these restored offices give NAR leaders authority to "take dominion" over the Seven Mountains of culture — including government and media.

Pierce was a co-founder of the NAR movement infrastructure in 1999 alongside [[C. Peter Wagner]], who coined the "New Apostolic Reformation" term. He became a member of Wagner's Apostolic Council of Prophetic Elders — a small network of recognized "prophets" that included [[Cindy Jacobs]], [[Mike Bickle]], Dutch Sheets, and James Goll — which functioned as an informal magisterium for the movement's prophetic declarations.

His ministry base, Glory of Zion International Ministries in Corinth, Texas, is a substantial operation: the site includes a former Boeing aircraft factory as its Sunday worship center (200,000 square feet) and is affiliated with more than 100 nonprofits and 600 house churches. Pierce has issued numerous public prophecies about US elections, national destiny, and geopolitical events. A 2021 doctoral dissertation (Yvie Ruth Baker) found that Pierce's prophecies showed patterns of post-hoc revision and unverifiable claims.

Matthew D. Taylor's [[Source - Taylor 2024 - The Violent Take It by Force]] documents Pierce within the NAR's political mobilization and its role in the post-2020 election period, including the movement's overlap with Stop the Steal activities and the January 6 environment.
