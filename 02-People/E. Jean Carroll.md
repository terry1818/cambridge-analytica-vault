# E. Jean Carroll

Born December 12, 1943, Detroit, MI. American journalist; longtime advice columnist for *Elle Magazine* (1993-2019, the "Ask E. Jean" column); author. Disclosed in her June 2019 memoir *What Do We Need Men For?* and accompanying *New York* magazine cover story that [[Donald Trump]] had sexually assaulted her in a Bergdorf Goodman dressing room in approximately 1996. After Trump publicly denied her allegations and stated he had never met her, Carroll filed two civil suits in federal court (SDNY). **Both Carroll I and Carroll II resulted in jury verdicts in her favor totaling $88.3M** ($5M + $83.3M). She is the only person whose civil suit produced a judicial finding of sexual-misconduct liability against a former US President.

## The alleged assault

Per Carroll's sworn testimony and contemporaneous accounts she told two friends in 1995-1996, the assault occurred in the late spring 1995 or early 1996 at Bergdorf Goodman department store on Fifth Avenue:

- Carroll and Trump met by chance entering the store
- Trump asked Carroll's help shopping for a present
- They went to the lingerie department; Trump suggested she try on lingerie
- In the dressing room, Trump pinned Carroll against the wall and sexually assaulted her including digital penetration without consent
- Carroll escaped after a few minutes
- She told two friends contemporaneously: Lisa Birnbach (journalist) and Carol Martin (former TV journalist) -- both of whom testified at trial

## Decision to come forward (2019)

Carroll included the account in her memoir during the post-#MeToo period. The *New York* magazine cover story was June 21, 2019. Trump immediately denied:
- "I've never met this person in my life"
- "This is a hoax"
- "She's not my type"
Trump also referenced a 1987 photograph of him with Carroll and Carroll's then-husband John Johnson at an NBC event -- when shown the photograph in his 2022 deposition, Trump misidentified Carroll as his second wife Marla Maples.

## Carroll II (2022-2023)

Filed November 24, 2022 under the NY Adult Survivors Act:
- Battery (sexual assault)
- Defamation (Trump's October 2022 statements continuing to deny)

**Verdict May 9, 2023**: jury found Trump liable for sexual abuse (battery) and for defamation; awarded $5,000,000.

Judge Kaplan's subsequent July 2023 opinion clarified that the jury's "sexual abuse" finding meant Trump "in fact did rape Ms. Carroll" as commonly understood, even though the specific NY Penal Law definition of "rape" (requiring penile penetration) was not met.

## Carroll I (2019-2024)

Filed November 4, 2019:
- Defamation arising from Trump's June 2019 statements as President

After the Westfall Act substitution issue resolved in 2023, and liability established by Carroll II issue preclusion, trial January 16-26, 2024 on damages only.

**Verdict January 26, 2024**: jury awarded $83,300,000 ($18.3M compensatory + $65M punitive).

## Second Circuit affirmance (December 2024)

Carroll II conviction affirmed by Second Circuit December 30, 2024. Carroll I appeal pending.

## Other women who testified at Carroll II under Federal Rule of Evidence 415

- **[[Jessica Leeds]]**: testified that Trump groped her on a first-class flight in approximately 1979
- **[[Natasha Stoynoff]]** (former *People Magazine* reporter): testified that Trump forcibly kissed her at Mar-a-Lago in December 2005 during a pregnancy-cover-story interview

The *Access Hollywood* tape was also introduced as Rule 415 evidence -- Trump's recorded boast that "when you're a star they let you do it" including "grab them by the p---y."

## Significance

Carroll's case stands as a uniquely-positioned legal accountability outcome:
- Two separate juries found Trump civilly liable for sexual misconduct
- Judicial findings stand as a matter of law unless reversed on appeal
- Second Circuit has affirmed Carroll II
- Total $88.3M in compensatory and punitive damages
- Only judicial sexual-misconduct findings against a US President

## What the verdicts established (Tier 1)

- Trump sexually abused Carroll in approximately 1996
- Trump's denials in 2019 and 2022 were defamatory
- Pattern of prior sexual misconduct as Rule 415 evidence

## What the verdicts do not establish

- Criminal liability (civil findings only)
- Allegations by other Trump accusers (not adjudicated in these cases)
