# Xi Jinping

## Summary

Xi Jinping (born 1953) has led the Chinese Communist Party since 2012 and the Chinese state since 2013, and is the most powerful Chinese leader since Mao Zedong. He appears in this vault as the central comparative case for *digital authoritarianism* — the use of modern data, networking, and surveillance technology to monitor a population, shape its information environment, and pre-empt dissent. Where the vault's American material concerns commercial data brokers, psychographic targeting, and contested platform power, the Chinese case shows what an unconstrained state surveillance apparatus looks like when fully built out and openly avowed. Xi's China is, in the assessment of scholars at the *Journal of Democracy* and elsewhere, "by far the most sophisticated model of digital authoritarianism" in existence.

Under Xi, the Chinese state has integrated internet service providers, data analytics firms, and social media platforms into a population-wide surveillance system; expanded a vast network of cameras with facial-recognition capability; built toward a nationwide Social Credit System designed to score the conduct of every person and entity; and reinforced the "Great Firewall" of internet censorship with new technology. Xi has described the goal of the social credit system in plain terms: "everything is convenient for the trustworthy, and the untrustworthy are unable to move a single step." The vault treats this not as a model the United States is becoming, but as a *reference architecture* — the clearest available picture of where surveillance and information-control capabilities point when no legal or democratic constraint stops them, and as a system China actively exports to other governments.

## Key facts

- **Role(s):** CCP General Secretary; President of the PRC; Chairman of the Central Military Commission — the three roles that concentrate party, state, and military authority.
- **Affiliations:** [[Chinese Communist Party]].

- **Known for:** Consolidating personal power; the social credit system; mass digital surveillance; internet censorship; global export of surveillance technology.

## Relationships

- **Reports to / worked under:** Nominally accountable to the CCP Politburo Standing Committee; in practice Xi has concentrated authority to a degree unmatched since Mao.
- **Co-conspirators / collaborators:** The Chinese state's surveillance build-out depends on domestic technology firms (telecoms, AI and facial-recognition companies, platform operators) operating under party direction — a state–corporate fusion distinct from the commercial data-broker ecosystem the vault tracks in the U.S.
- **Comparative peers:** Vladimir Putin — the vault's other principal authoritarian comparator; Russia's information-control model is cruder and more focused on disinformation than China's systematic surveillance architecture, but the two are frequently studied together.

## Public statements

- On the social credit system's purpose: "Everything is convenient for the trustworthy, and the untrustworthy are unable to move a single step." — articulating the system as a tool of behavioral control, not merely credit scoring.
- Xi has consistently framed tight information control as a matter of "cyber sovereignty" — the doctrine that each state has the right to govern its own internet, used to legitimize censorship internationally.

## Open questions

- **How integrated is the system, really:** Reporting and scholarship sometimes overstate the coherence of China's surveillance apparatus. The Social Credit System in practice is fragmented across regional pilots and multiple sub-systems rather than a single unified score. The vault notes the *capability and intent* are documented; the degree of seamless integration is contested.
- **Export and convergence:** The most vault-relevant question is how far Chinese surveillance technology and the "cyber sovereignty" model spread to other governments, and whether democratic states adopt comparable tools under commercial or security framing. `#claim/disputed` on the pace and extent.
