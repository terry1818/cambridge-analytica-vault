# Roger Ailes

## Summary

Roger Eugene Ailes (1940–2017) was an American television executive and Republican political consultant who, more than any single individual, fused partisan politics with the production grammar of television. He entered politics from entertainment: a producer on *The Mike Douglas Show*, he was recruited as a media adviser to [[Richard Nixon]]'s 1968 presidential campaign, where he taught the candidate to perform for the camera. In 1970, Ailes and other Nixon aides authored a memo, "A Plan For Putting the GOP on TV News," proposing a television operation that would bypass what they called the "prejudices of network news" and deliver pro-administration content directly to heartland viewers. He spent the 1970s and 1980s as one of the Republican Party's top media consultants — advising the [[Ronald Reagan]] and George H. W. Bush campaigns — before [[Rupert Murdoch]] hired him in 1996 to build [[Fox News]] from scratch. Ailes ran Fox News as founding chairman and CEO for twenty years, making it the most-watched cable news network and a defining instrument of American political polarization. He resigned in July 2016 amid multiple sexual-harassment allegations and died in May 2017. Ailes belongs in this vault as the originator of the "orchestrated television" model — a deliberate, decades-long project to convert broadcast news into a partisan persuasion machine, an intellectual precursor to the [[MAGA media ecosystem]].

## Timeline

- `1940-05-15` — Born in Warren, Ohio
- `~1967–1968` — Producer on *The Mike Douglas Show*; meets and is recruited by Richard Nixon
- `1968` — Serves as media adviser to the Nixon campaign; later subject of Joe McGinniss's *The Selling of the President 1968*
- `1970` — Co-authors the memo "A Plan For Putting the GOP on TV News," proposing a partisan TV news operation (document later found in the Nixon Presidential Library)
- `1980s` — Top Republican media consultant; works on Reagan and George H. W. Bush campaigns
- `1993–1995` — Runs CNBC and the talk channel "America's Talking" for NBC
- `1996-10-07` — [[Fox News]] launches; Ailes is founding chairman and CEO, hired by [[Rupert Murdoch]]
- `2016-07-21` — Resigns from Fox News amid sexual-harassment allegations; reportedly receives ~$40 million exit settlement from 21st Century Fox
- `2016` — Briefly advises [[Donald Trump]]'s 2016 campaign on debate preparation
- `2017-05-18` — Dies in Palm Beach, Florida

## Relationships

- **Reports to / worked under:** [[Rupert Murdoch]] (as Fox News chairman); [[Richard Nixon]] (as 1968 campaign adviser)
- **Subordinates / hired:** Built and staffed Fox News, including hosts whose programs later amplified election-fraud claims; mentored a generation of conservative television talent
- **Co-conspirators / collaborators:** Nixon aides who co-authored the 1970 memo; Republican campaign operatives of the 1980s
- **Opponents / accusers:** Gretchen Carlson and other Fox News employees who alleged sexual harassment; their accounts dramatized in the film *Bombshell* (2019)

## Public statements

- 1970 memo (co-authored): "People are lazy. With television you just sit — watch — listen. The thinking is done for you." — argued for a network delivering "pro-administration" coverage, context for "A Plan For Putting the GOP on TV News"
- Ailes reportedly framed Fox News's mission as countering perceived liberal bias in network journalism — the public articulation of the 1970 blueprint.
