# Shaye Moss

Former Fulton County, Georgia election worker. Daughter of [[Ruby Freeman]]. Worked at Fulton County registration and elections department for approximately 10 years prior to 2020; was a supervisor in the operations group responsible for processing absentee ballots in November 2020. Subject of the same false fraud claims by [[Donald Trump]] and [[Rudy Giuliani]] as her mother. Co-plaintiff in the December 2023 $148M defamation verdict against Giuliani. Her June 21, 2022 J6 Committee testimony provided one of the most widely-cited human-cost accounts of the Trump election challenge effort.

## J6 Committee testimony (June 21, 2022)

Moss's public testimony at the J6 Select Committee hearing on pressure on state and local officials provided detailed personal account of the false-fraud-claim campaign's impact. Key testimony excerpts:

- Described that after Giuliani's claims circulated, she received "a lot of threats, wishing death upon me, telling me that I'll be in jail with my mother, and saying things like, 'Be glad it's 2020 and not 1920'"
- Described receiving racial slurs in her work emails
- Described inability to work or move freely
- Quoted her grandmother: "Grandmother just kept saying, 'They're going to be at my door'"
- Stated her mother had to leave her home: "Everybody just turned on her... she's no longer her happy, bubbly self"

The testimony was widely viewed as humanizing the human costs of the election-challenge disinformation campaign in a way more abstract evidence had not.

## The "passing a USB drive" lie

Giuliani specifically claimed Moss and Freeman were passing USB drives like "drug dealers." The actual footage showed Freeman passing Moss a ginger mint candy. Moss explained this directly in testimony.

## Co-plaintiff in Freeman v Giuliani

The $148M federal jury verdict in December 2023 was for both Freeman and Moss; the damages were divided between them. Each was awarded approximately $36-40M in compensatory damages, $20M each for emotional distress, with $75M total in punitive damages.

## Career impact

Moss left her job at Fulton County and the state election system after the harassment campaign made it impossible for her to continue working in election administration. She has subsequently testified in multiple proceedings and was named TIME 100 in 2022.
