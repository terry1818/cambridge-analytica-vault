## Summary

Basil Manly Sr. (January 28, 1798 – December 21, 1868) was one of the most influential Baptist clergymen in the antebellum American South. Best known today as the author of the "Alabama Resolutions" — the formal Baptist document demanding that slaveholders be eligible for missionary appointment, whose rejection by the Triennial Convention precipitated the 1845 founding of the [[Southern Baptist Convention]] — Manly was also president of the University of Alabama (1837–1855) and a prolific defender of slavery as a divinely ordained institution. Robert P. Jones's *White Too Long* (2020) identifies Manly as a foundational figure in embedding racial hierarchy into Southern Baptist theology and institutional identity.

## Key facts

- Born January 28, 1798, in Pittsboro, North Carolina.
- Succeeded Richard Furman as pastor of First Baptist Church of Charleston, SC, in 1826 — the same congregation Furman had used as his platform for pro-slavery theology.
- Served as president of the University of Alabama from 1837 to 1855, combining educational leadership with pastoral activity.
- Authored the Alabama Resolutions (1844) — the formal Baptist position demanding assurance that slaveholders could be missionaries and mission board officers; the Triennial Convention's refusal to guarantee this directly triggered the 1845 split.
- Was a leading figure at the founding Augusta convention of the Southern Baptist Convention in 1845.
- An ardent and early advocate for secession; used scripture to justify both slavery and Confederate independence.
- On February 18, 1861, in Montgomery, Alabama, opened the inauguration of Jefferson Davis as President of the Confederate States of America with prayer — the most visible public expression of Southern Baptist theological sanction for the Confederacy.
- Died December 21, 1868, in Greenville, South Carolina.
- His son, Basil Manly Jr., co-authored the Baptist Faith and Message — the foundational doctrinal statement that still governs Southern Baptist belief.

## Relationships

- **Richard Furman** (predecessor): Manly succeeded Furman at First Baptist Charleston and continued Furman's theological project of providing biblical justification for slavery.
- **[[SBC founding 1845]]** — Manly's Alabama Resolutions were the proximate theological document triggering the split; he is a co-founder of the denomination.
- **Jefferson Davis** — Manly's role at Davis's inauguration represents the apex of Southern Baptist theological sanction for the Confederate project.
- **Basil Manly Jr.** (son): co-authored the Baptist Faith and Message; founding faculty of The Southern Baptist Theological Seminary, Louisville — meaning Manly's intellectual legacy persisted directly into the flagship SBC seminary.
