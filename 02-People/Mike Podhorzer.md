## Summary

Mike Podhorzer spent more than two decades as the AFL-CIO's political director, transforming labor's electoral operations from traditional mobilization into one of the most rigorously tested GOTV programs in American politics. Reacting to the 2004 Bush victory, he assembled an informal "Analyst Group" of left-of-center data practitioners at AFL-CIO headquarters, which by 2007 became the formalized [[Analyst Institute]]. [[Source - Issenberg 2012 - The Victory Lab]] profiles Podhorzer as a central figure in the Democratic data-and-experiments ecosystem, emphasizing his conviction — shared with academic colleagues like [[Alan Gerber]] and [[Donald Green]] — that randomized testing, not intuition, should determine which GOTV tactics get resources.

## Key Facts

- **AFL-CIO Political Director, 1997–2019**: Oversaw the federation's electoral program across more than two decades and multiple presidential cycles. Made the AFL-CIO a national test-bed for randomized GOTV experiments.
- **Post-2004 reaction**: After Bush's re-election — partly attributed to the GOP's superior ground game via [[Voter Vault and the 2004 72-Hour Task Force]] — Podhorzer convened an informal working group to share what Democrats knew about what worked in voter mobilization.
- **Analyst Institute (2007)**: Co-founded with [[Hal Malchow]] and affiliated academic researchers including [[Todd Rogers]] and contributors from the Gerber-Green research tradition. The Institute became the institutional home for the Democrats' experimental-research clearinghouse, running randomized tests for hundreds of organizations.
- **2020 election**: Podhorzer was a visible coordinator in the cross-sector effort to defend election administration in 2020 — documented in reporting on what he called the "informal alliance" of business, labor, and civic groups. This is a separate, post-Issenberg chapter of his career.
