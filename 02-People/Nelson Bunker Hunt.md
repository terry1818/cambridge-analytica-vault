## Summary

Nelson Bunker Hunt was the son of oil magnate H.L. Hunt and heir to one of the largest private fortunes in mid-20th century America. He channeled significant portions of that wealth into both speculative commodity ventures and the institutional infrastructure of the American religious right. He was a founding member of the [[Council for National Policy]] in 1981 and served on its Executive Committee through the 1980s, making him a foundational patron of the CNP network that Anne Nelson traces in [[Source Anne Nelson Shadow Network 2019]].

Bunker Hunt's religious funding was substantial: he and Wallace Johnson donated $15.5 million to Bill Bright's Campus Crusade for Christ, and he provided a $3.5 million loan guarantee for the 1979 Campus Crusade film *Jesus*, which became one of the most widely distributed evangelical films in history. He was chairman of the Bible Society of Texas and deeply involved in Campus Crusade's "Here's Life" campaign (1976–1980).

His political profile extended beyond the religious right: he was a council member of the John Birch Society and a major sponsor of the Western Goals Foundation (1979), a hardline anti-communist research organization. Separately, his attempt with his brother William Herbert Hunt to corner the global silver market in 1979–1980 resulted in financial ruin and federal commodities fraud charges (settled in 1989).

## Key Facts

- Born February 22, 1926, in El Dorado, Arkansas; died October 21, 2014
- Son of H.L. Hunt; inherited stake in Hunt Oil
- Founding member of the [[Council for National Policy]], 1981
- CNP President Executive Committee (1983), Senior Executive Committee (1983–84), Executive Committee (1988)
- Donated approximately $15.5 million to Campus Crusade for Christ with Wallace Johnson
- Past chairman, Bible Society of Texas
- Provided $3.5 million loan guarantee for the 1979 Campus Crusade *Jesus* film
- Active member of the John Birch Society council
- Major sponsor of Western Goals Foundation, founded by General John Singlaub and Rep. Larry McDonald (1979)
- Attempted to corner global silver market with brother William Herbert Hunt in 1979–1980; silver prices collapsed March 1980 ("Silver Thursday"), resulting in billions in losses
- Settled federal commodities fraud charges in 1989
- Declared personal bankruptcy 1988
