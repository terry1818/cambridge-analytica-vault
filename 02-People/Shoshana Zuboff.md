## Summary

Shoshana Zuboff is an American social psychologist and author whose theoretical framework of "surveillance capitalism" has become the dominant analytical lens for understanding how Big Tech platforms monetize human behavior. Her 2019 book *The Age of Surveillance Capitalism: The Fight for a Human Future at the New Frontier of Power* (PublicAffairs) argues that platform companies like Google and Facebook developed a new economic logic: capturing behavioral surplus data from users, refining it into prediction products, and selling those predictions to advertisers and other buyers seeking to influence human behavior. This framework provides the structural economic context for understanding how commercial data operations — including those underpinning [[Cambridge Analytica]] — are not aberrations but expressions of a systemic logic.

## Key facts

- Born November 18, 1951. Was among the first women to earn tenure at Harvard Business School.
- First introduced "surveillance capitalism" in a 2014 essay, "A Digital Declaration," followed by a 2015 scholarly article in the *Journal of Information Technology* titled "Big Other: Surveillance Capitalism and the Prospects of an Information Civilization" — winner of the 2016 ICIS Best Paper Award.
- *The Age of Surveillance Capitalism* (2019) is available in 26 languages and has been compared to *Silent Spring*, *Das Kapital*, and *The Wealth of Nations* in its cultural impact.
- Earlier book *In the Age of the Smart Machine: The Future of Work and Power* (1988) anticipated digitalization's transformation of labor.
- Awards include: Axel Springer Award (2019), EPIC Lifetime Achievement Award (2021), inaugural Global Privacy Assembly Giovanni Buttarelli Award (2021). Honorary degrees from University of Amsterdam, Copenhagen Business School, McGill University, University of London.
- Her framework specifically names how behavioral data collected by platforms generates "behavioral modification" products — the economic substrate that made [[Cambridge Analytica]]-style psychographic targeting commercially viable.
- Testified before the US House Energy and Commerce Committee on tech regulation (February 2022).

## Relationships

- Her surveillance capitalism framework provides the economic theory underlying commercial data operations that [[Cambridge Analytica]], data brokers (see [[Byron Tau]]), and tech platforms operate within.
- Cited by [[Renée DiResta]] and other disinformation researchers as foundational context.
- Her concept of "behavioral modification at scale" is structurally related to [[psychographic microtargeting]] as practiced by CA/SCL.

## Public statements

- "Surveillance capitalism unilaterally claims human experience as free raw material for translation into behavioral data." (*The Age of Surveillance Capitalism*, 2019)

## Open questions

- Whether the surveillance capitalism framework fully accounts for state actors (vs. private-sector actors) as drivers of the data economy.
- Whether her proposed regulatory responses have been operationalized in any jurisdiction.
