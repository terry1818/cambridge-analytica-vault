## Summary

Becca (Rebecca) Greenwood is a [[New Apostolic Reformation]] leader whose work centers on "strategic-level spiritual warfare" — the NAR practice of identifying and praying against demonic spirits believed to control geographic regions, social institutions, and political systems. Her ministry, Christian Harvest International (CHI), which she co-founded with her husband Greg Greenwood, is affiliated with the Generals International network of [[Cindy Jacobs]] and Mike Jacobs, one of the NAR's most prominent apostolic couples.

Greenwood's distinctive contribution to NAR theology and practice involves "spiritual mapping" — the systematic identification of historical, spiritual, and geographic "strongholds" — combined with prophetic intercession and deliverance ministry. She has led prayer journeys to 34 countries, including Egypt, Nepal, Italy, Russia, Ukraine, Ireland, Spain, and China, applying these practices internationally.

She earned a Doctorate of Practical Ministry from the Wagner Leadership Institute (C. Peter Wagner's credentialing institution), where she also serves as core faculty, giving her a role in transmitting NAR spiritual warfare theology to the next generation of NAR leaders. Matthew D. Taylor's [[Source - Taylor 2024 - The Violent Take It by Force]] documents how figures like Greenwood participate in the NAR's political mobilization through the spiritual warfare framework — reframing political opponents and electoral processes in terms of demonic opposition requiring spiritual combat.

## Core Theological/Methodological Commitments

Greenwood's framework combines three NAR practices:
1. **Strategic-level spiritual warfare**: identifying and binding territorial spirits believed to control nations or regions
2. **Spiritual mapping**: historical and geographic research to locate demonic "strongholds"
3. **Prophetic intercession**: receiving and acting on prophetic direction in prayer

All three practices are central to how the NAR conceptualizes political and cultural change as a spiritual battle requiring "kingdom" warriors.
