## Summary

Semion Mogilevich is a Ukrainian-born Russian organized crime figure whom the FBI has described as the most powerful Russian mobster alive and the "boss of bosses" of Russian Mafia syndicates operating across multiple continents. He was placed on the FBI's Ten Most Wanted list in 2009 — the 494th individual to appear there — but was removed in December 2015 after it was determined he was in Russia and no longer met the criteria for that designation. An outstanding US federal indictment charges him with racketeering, securities fraud (the YBM Magnex stock fraud, which cost investors approximately $150 million), wire fraud, and money laundering. He has never been extradited. Craig Unger's *House of Trump, House of Putin* (2018) places Mogilevich within the broader ecosystem connecting Russian organized crime to the Trump real estate world, arguing that he represents the layer of organized crime beneath and around the more politically prominent oligarchs.

## Key Facts

- Born 30 June 1946 in Kyiv; educated in economics at Lviv University.
- Operates through multiple passports and nationalities; has held Russian, Ukrainian, Hungarian, and Israeli citizenship at various times.
- The YBM Magnex scheme (late 1990s): Mogilevich used a Pennsylvania-based company manufacturing industrial magnets as a vehicle for securities fraud and money laundering; the scheme defrauded investors of approximately $150 million, placing him on FBI radar.
- FBI accusations include weapons trafficking (including anti-aircraft systems), contract murders, extortion, drug trafficking, and prostitution "on an international scale."
- Arrested in Moscow in January 2008 on tax evasion charges; released without prosecution in 2009. Russian authorities declined to pursue further charges, reinforcing Western analysts' assessments that he operates under Kremlin protection.
- Alleged connection to [[Donald Trump]]: investigative accounts, including Unger's book, document that in the early 1990s Mogilevich was among individuals connected to Russian organized crime who purchased apartments at Trump Tower. This is documented as a transactional connection; no evidence of a personal relationship between Trump and Mogilevich has been established.
- Alleged connection to [[Felix Sater]] and [[Bayrock Group]]: Unger and other investigators note overlapping criminal networks but stop short of claiming a direct operational link.
- No Western government has successfully prosecuted Mogilevich; Russia has not extradited him.

## Relationships

- **[[Donald Trump]]**: No documented personal relationship. Mogilevich-connected individuals purchased Trump properties in the 1990s; noted in Unger's book as evidence of the broader overlap between Russian organized crime capital and Trump real estate.
- **[[Felix Sater]]**: Sater's criminal background involved Russian Mafia-connected stock fraud; both operate in overlapping criminal networks. Direct organizational link not established.
- **[[Bayrock Group]]**: Indirect through criminal network overlaps documented by Unger.
- **Russian state**: Alleged to operate under FSB protection; not proven but inferred from Russia's repeated failure to prosecute or extradite.

## Public Statements

Mogilevich has given limited public interviews. He has denied criminal activity and FBI characterizations.
