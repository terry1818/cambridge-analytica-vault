# Philip Gorski

## Summary

Philip S. Gorski (born 1963) is an American sociologist and the Frederick and Laura Goff Professor of Sociology and Religious Studies at Yale University, where he has also chaired the sociology department. A comparative-historical sociologist trained at the University of Wisconsin–Madison, his earlier scholarship addressed state formation, secularization, nationalism, and the long-run interaction of religion and politics in Europe and the United States — including the influential book *The Disciplinary Revolution* (2003).

Gorski appears in this vault as one of the principal scholars who turned "[[Christian nationalism]]" from a loose epithet into a defined, measurable object of social science. His 2022 book *The Flag and the Cross: White Christian Nationalism and the Threat to American Democracy*, co-authored with sociologist [[Samuel Perry]] (and with contributions from historian Jemar Tisby), combines historical analysis with survey data to argue that white Christian nationalism is a coherent ideology — one that reveres a founding myth of a white Christian nation and works "in concert with Christian libertarianism." Crucially for this vault's [[democratic backsliding]] thread, Gorski and Perry use survey instruments to show a statistical association between Christian-nationalist belief and anti-democratic, authoritarian, and conspiratorial attitudes, including support for political violence around [[January 6 2021]].

## Key facts

- **Role(s):** Sociologist of religion and politics; Yale professor; former chair of Yale's sociology department; author.
- **Affiliations:** [[Yale University]]; trained at the University of Wisconsin–Madison.

- **Known for:** *The Flag and the Cross* (2022, with Samuel Perry); *American Covenant* (2017); *The Disciplinary Revolution* (2003); operationalizing white Christian nationalism in survey research.

## Relationships

- **[[Samuel Perry]]** — University of Oklahoma sociologist; co-author of *The Flag and the Cross* and frequent collaborator on Christian-nationalism survey research.
- **Jemar Tisby** — historian who contributed to *The Flag and the Cross*, adding a racial-history dimension.
- **[[Kristin Du Mez]]** — historian whose *Jesus and John Wayne* is often paired with Gorski-Perry's sociological work as complementary accounts of the same phenomenon.
- **[[Andrew Whitehead]]** — sociologist (with Perry, author of *Taking America Back for God*) working in the same survey-research program.
- **[[Yale University]]** — his institutional home; the platform from which much of his public commentary is issued.

## Public statements

- Gorski has argued that white Christian nationalism is not fringe but a "shared political vision" held by a substantial minority of Americans, and that it is statistically linked to support for authoritarian measures.
- *The Flag and the Cross* frames the ideology as the union of ethno-nationalism and "Christian libertarianism" — the combination that, in the authors' analysis, makes it dangerous to pluralist democracy.
