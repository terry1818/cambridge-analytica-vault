# Sviatlana Tsikhanouskaya

## Summary

Sviatlana Tsikhanouskaya (born 1982) is the leader of the Belarusian democratic opposition. A former teacher and translator with no prior political career, she entered politics in 2020 only because her husband, video blogger Syarhei Tsikhanouski, was barred and then imprisoned when he sought to run against long-ruling President Alexander Lukashenko. Unable to register him, she registered herself. Lukashenko's authorities reportedly permitted her candidacy on the assumption that a woman could not mount a serious challenge — a miscalculation. Her campaign drew the largest opposition rallies in Belarusian history.

She belongs in this vault as a compact case study in several of its core themes: the stolen election, the machinery of post-election repression, and the shape of exiled opposition under a consolidated dictatorship backed by a larger authoritarian patron. The official 2020 result credited Lukashenko with about 80% of the vote; Tsikhanouskaya and independent observers rejected this as fraudulent, and she claimed a first-round victory of at least 60%. Western governments described the election as "deeply flawed and fraudulent." Mass protests followed; the regime responded with mass arrests, beatings, and torture documented by human-rights organizations. Tsikhanouskaya was forced into exile in Lithuania within a day of the vote, and Lukashenko's survival was ultimately underwritten by Russian backing — a key reason the Belarusian case is part of the vault's Russia/global-authoritarianism thread rather than a standalone story.

## Key facts

- **Role(s):** 2020 presidential candidate; leader of the Belarusian democratic opposition; head of the United Transitional Cabinet.
- **Affiliations:** Belarusian democratic opposition; the exiled United Transitional Cabinet of Belarus, operating principally from Lithuania.
- **Active period:** 2020–present.
- **Known for:** The 2020 campaign and disputed result; leading the exiled movement; international advocacy for sanctions against the Lukashenko regime.

## Relationships

- **Opponents / accusers:** Alexander Lukashenko — the entrenched Belarusian ruler whose regime declared the contested 2020 victory, drove Tsikhanouskaya into exile, and sentenced her in absentia.
- **Reports to / worked under:** Her entry into politics was on behalf of her husband, Syarhei Tsikhanouski, an imprisoned opposition video blogger.
- **Co-conspirators / collaborators:** Other Belarusian opposition figures, including Maria Kalesnikava and Veranika Tsapkala, with whom she campaigned in 2020; the exiled opposition leadership of the United Transitional Cabinet.
- **Comparative context:** Backed by [[Vladimir Putin]]'s Russia, Lukashenko's survival ties the Belarusian case directly to the vault's Russia/authoritarianism thread; the dynamic parallels [[Mikhail Khodorkovsky]]'s exiled opposition to the Russian state.

## Public statements

- Tsikhanouskaya has consistently framed her role as temporary and representative — not seeking personal power but representing a stolen democratic mandate until free elections can be held.
- At the UN and in Western capitals, she has called for targeted sanctions and for non-recognition of Lukashenko's claimed mandate.

## Open questions

- **The actual vote:** Independent verification of the 2020 result was impossible because the regime controlled the count and barred meaningful observation. Tsikhanouskaya's specific claim of a 60%+ victory cannot be independently confirmed; what *is* well established is that the official 80% figure for Lukashenko was fraudulent. - **Russian leverage:** The extent of direct Russian operational involvement in suppressing the Belarusian protests, versus general political and economic backing of Lukashenko, is debated.
