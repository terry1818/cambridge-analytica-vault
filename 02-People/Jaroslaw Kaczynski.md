## Summary

Jarosław Kaczyński is the central figure in Poland's democratic backsliding of 2015–2023 and the primary subject of Anne Applebaum's analysis in [[Source - Applebaum 2020 - Twilight of Democracy]]. A veteran anti-communist dissident who co-founded [[Law and Justice party]] with his twin brother Lech, Kaczyński pivoted in the mid-2000s from liberal democratic opposition to an ethno-nationalist, Catholic-conservative programme that Applebaum documents as a deliberate authoritarian project. As the party's unchallenged chairman and de facto power behind successive PiS governments, he oversaw the systematic subordination of Poland's constitutional court, the conversion of public broadcaster TVP into a government mouthpiece, the curtailment of abortion rights, and the introduction of anti-LGBTQ policies — all while maintaining formal electoral competition. His career is a core comparative case for the vault's "illiberal democracy" and [[Counter-majoritarian institutions]] clusters.

## Key Facts

- Born June 18, 1949 in Warsaw; twin brother of Lech Kaczyński (President of Poland, died in 2010 Smolensk air disaster).
- Participated in the March 1968 student protests; collaborated with the KOR dissident network; active in the Solidarity movement.
- Co-founded Law and Justice in 2001; served as chairman since 2003.
- Served as Prime Minister only briefly (July 2006–October 2007); exercised more influence as party chairman during the tenures of PM Beata Szydło and PM Mateusz Morawiecki.
- His brother Lech's death in the Smolensk plane crash (2010) — which killed 96 Polish officials — became a central myth in Kaczyński's politics; he promoted, without evidence, a theory that it was a Russian assassination, which Applebaum documents as a case study in elite conspiracy politics.
- Directed PiS to pack Poland's Constitutional Tribunal beginning in 2015, triggering a prolonged rule-of-law crisis with the EU that resulted in Poland's cohesion funding being frozen under the EU's conditionality mechanism.
- Oversaw the conversion of TVP (public broadcaster) into an overt government propaganda channel — a close parallel to Milošević's RTS strategy.
- PiS lost its parliamentary majority in October 2023; Donald Tusk formed a coalition government in December 2023, ending eight years of PiS rule.
- Applebaum uses Kaczyński as a case study of how former dissidents — people who once fought for liberal democracy — can become its most effective opponents.

## Relationships

- Subject of [[Source - Applebaum 2020 - Twilight of Democracy]]; Applebaum knew Kaczyński personally in the 1990s and documents his authoritarian turn as a personal and intellectual betrayal.

- Parallel comparative cases: [[Viktor Orban]] (Hungary), who developed an even more advanced version of the same illiberal governance template.

## Open Questions

- Applebaum's framing (personal resentment, wounded narcissism) is contested by scholars who emphasize structural incentives (electoral competition) over psychological explanation. Which frame better accounts for the evidence?
- Can PiS-era judicial and media changes be reversed under the Tusk government, or has institutional damage proven durable?
