# Tom Monaghan

## Summary

Thomas Stephen Monaghan (born March 25, 1937) is the founder of Domino's Pizza and one of the most consequential individual funders of Catholic conservative institutions in American history. After selling approximately 93% of Domino's to Bain Capital in 1998 for an estimated $1 billion, Monaghan redirected his fortune almost entirely toward building a network of orthodox Catholic educational, legal, and political organizations. His institutional footprint — [[Ave Maria University]], Ave Maria School of Law, [[Thomas More Law Center]], Legatus International — represents one of the most sustained and substantial single-donor buildouts in the American Catholic hard right.

Katherine Stewart's *Money, Lies, and God* situates Monaghan within the broader Catholic integralist and theocratic-wing funding architecture that intersects with, but is organizationally distinct from, the evangelical Protestant wing dominant in the [[Council for National Policy]]. His philanthropy is explicitly aimed at advancing orthodox Catholic values in law, culture, and political life — a project that makes him a foundational figure in any map of the US patron-wing right.

## Key Facts

- Born in Ann Arbor, Michigan. Father died when Monaghan was four years old; he and his brother Jim were placed in St. Joseph Home for Children, a Felician Sisters orphanage in Jackson, Michigan. That experience formed his Catholic faith; he subsequently entered St. Joseph's Seminary in Grand Rapids intending to become a priest but was expelled for disciplinary infractions.
- Founded Domino's Pizza in 1960 with a $900 loan (with brother James) to purchase a pizzeria called DomiNick's in Ypsilanti, Michigan. Built the chain on a 30-minute delivery guarantee model. Eventually bought out his brother's half-share in exchange for a Volkswagen Beetle.
- Sold approximately 93% of Domino's to Bain Capital in 1998 for an estimated $1 billion; withdrew from day-to-day business and dedicated his resources to Catholic causes.
- Founded **Legatus International** in 1987 (while still running Domino's) — an organization of Catholic business executives (CEOs, presidents, and spouses) committed to studying and spreading the Catholic faith in the marketplace. Membership has grown to more than 5,000.
- Co-founded **Thomas More Law Center** with former Oakland County prosecutor Richard Thompson on December 29, 1998. Initial funding came from the Ave Maria Foundation. The TMLC operates as a public interest law firm pursuing anti-abortion litigation, opposition to same-sex marriage, and First Amendment free-exercise cases; it has been involved in major federal cases.
- Established **Ave Maria College** in Ypsilanti, Michigan in 1998 (the institution that became Ave Maria University). After a zoning dispute blocked an expanded campus near Domino's Farms, Monaghan relocated the university to southwest Florida, partnering with the Barron Collier family (who donated the land in rural Collier County). The resulting Ave Maria, Florida is a planned Catholic community built around the university.
- Pledged $250 million at the university's Florida founding; ultimate documented donations exceed $500 million.
- Also established the **Ave Maria List**, an anti-abortion political action committee, and the **Ave Maria Foundation**, his primary philanthropic vehicle.
- Received Knight of Magistral Grace status in the Sovereign Military Order of Malta.
- Survived a National Organization for Women boycott of Domino's Pizza over his funding of anti-abortion causes while still CEO.

## Public Statements

- After selling Domino's (1998): "I'm going to spend the rest of my life trying to get to heaven and trying to take as many people with me as I can." (widely quoted in Ave Maria University founder materials and Catholic press)
- Described his goal for Ave Maria University as creating an institution "completely faithful to the Magisterium of the Catholic Church."
