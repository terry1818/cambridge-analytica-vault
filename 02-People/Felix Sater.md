## Summary

Felix Sater is an American real estate developer and financier born in the Soviet Union who became a central figure in investigations into the intersection of Russian organized crime, real estate development, and the Trump Organization. He pleaded guilty in 1998 to his role in a $40 million stock fraud scheme connected to the Russian Mafia and became a cooperating witness for the FBI and CIA, providing intelligence on organized crime and al-Qaeda for approximately two decades. His cooperation was so significant that his guilty plea was sealed and his sentencing delayed for nearly 20 years. In parallel, he joined [[Bayrock Group]] as managing director (2003–2008), where he partnered with the Trump Organization on Trump SoHo and other projects. In 2015–2016, while Trump was running for president, Sater worked with Trump attorney Michael Cohen to pursue a Trump Tower Moscow deal, sending Cohen an email stating he could "get all of Putin's team to buy in" and help elect Trump. Craig Unger's *House of Trump, House of Putin* (2018) treats Sater as the pivot figure connecting Russian organized crime money flows to Trump's business empire.

## Key Facts

- Born 1966 in the Soviet Union; emigrated to the US as a child; grew up in Brooklyn.
- 1991: Served time for assault with a broken margarita glass.
- 1998: Pleaded guilty to racketeering in connection with a $40 million stock fraud organized by Russian Mafia figures; began cooperating with federal authorities.
- Cooperation credited with: convictions of Bonanno crime family members; intelligence on al-Qaeda financing; counterterrorism contributions (claimed; some disputed by investigators).
- 2003: Joined Bayrock Group as managing director; worked on Trump SoHo, Trump International Fort Lauderdale, and other projects.
- Post-2009: Left Bayrock; received business cards identifying himself as "Senior Adviser to Donald Trump" and an office in Trump Tower. Trump has variously denied knowing Sater well.
- 2015–2016: With Trump running for president, Sater emailed Michael Cohen a letter of intent for a Trump Tower Moscow project and wrote that he could use Russian connections to boost Trump's political prospects. The project did not proceed but continued to be discussed through June 2016.
- Mueller investigation: Sater provided cooperation to the Mueller investigation; the scope and content of his cooperation has not been fully disclosed.
- He has testified before Congress and invoked his cooperation history as context for his activities.

## Relationships

- **[[Bayrock Group]]**: Managing director 2003–2008; drove Trump partnership.
- **[[Donald Trump]]**: Worked on multiple real estate projects; Trump has minimized the relationship ("If he said hello to me at a cocktail party, I might have said hello back"). The extent of direct interaction is disputed and subject to ongoing investigation.
- **Michael Cohen**: Trump attorney; co-recipient of Moscow deal emails; subsequently prosecuted separately.
- **[[Semion Mogilevich]]**: Sater's stock fraud involved individuals in Mogilevich-connected networks; direct relationship unproven.
- **Tevfik Arif**: [[Bayrock Group]] founder and Sater's employer.

## Public Statements

Sater has been publicly forthcoming compared to other figures in this network. He has testified under oath that he was a patriotic asset to the US government and that his Russia contacts were used for intelligence purposes. He has not contradicted the basic facts about his criminal record or Bayrock activities.
