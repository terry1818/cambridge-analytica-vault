# Uihlein family

## Summary

Richard "Dick" Uihlein and his wife Elizabeth "Liz" Uihlein are the billionaire owners of [[Uline]], the privately held Wisconsin-based shipping- and packaging-supplies giant they founded in 1980. Dick Uihlein is a descendant of the family that founded Milwaukee's Schlitz Brewing Company. Over the post-2016 period the couple emerged as among the most prolific Republican mega-donors in the country — by some tallies the single largest GOP donors of the 2021–2022 cycle — channeling a fortune built on cardboard boxes into a dense network of super PACs, 501(c)(4) "dark money" groups, hard-right primary challengers, and organizations promoting false claims about the 2020 election.

The Uihleins belong to the vault's "patron wing": donors who finance the institutional infrastructure of the American right rather than running for office themselves. They are distinguished from the [[Koch Network]] by a sharper appetite for ideological combat — backing insurgent primary challengers against establishment Republicans, and, after 2020, funding election-denial organizations and campaigns against ballot-initiative direct democracy. Their giving sits at the intersection of the vault's "constitutional capture" and "election interference" threads.

## Key facts

- **Role(s):** Co-founders and owners of [[Uline]]; Republican mega-donors and dark-money funders.
- **Affiliations:** [[Uline]], [[Restoration of America]] / Restoration Action, Ed Uihlein Family Foundation, [[Club for Growth]], [[Federalist Society]], Wisconsin and Illinois conservative networks.
- **Active period:** ~1980 (Uline) – present; major political giving from roughly 2010, escalating sharply after 2016.
- **Known for:** Being among the top Republican donors of the post-2016 era; financing election-denial and anti-direct-democracy efforts.
- The Uihleins have collectively given more than $250 million to federal candidates and political groups since the 2016 cycle, the large majority from Richard Uihlein.
- Richard Uihlein was a major early backer of insurgent and Tea Party–aligned candidates, often opposing the preferred candidates of Republican leadership.
- The couple's "Restoration of America" / Restoration Action dark-money operation reported raising roughly $30 million in 2022; as a 501(c)(4) it need not disclose donors, but reporting (ProPublica, OpenSecrets) attributes its funding overwhelmingly to Uihlein.
- The Uihleins have funded groups that promoted false 2020 voter-fraud claims and campaigns to constrain citizen ballot initiatives in multiple states.

## Relationships

- **[[Uline]]** — the family company and the source of the political fortune.
- **[[Restoration of America]] / Restoration Action** — the Uihlein-funded dark-money hub.
- **[[Club for Growth]]** — anti-tax PAC the Uihleins have substantially funded; a key conduit for backing primary challengers.
- **[[Donald Trump]]** — beneficiary of Uihlein-linked spending; the Uihleins funded election-denial efforts after his 2020 loss.
- **[[Koch Network]]** — parallel patron-wing operation; the Uihleins are sometimes contrasted with the Kochs as more combative and Trump-aligned.
- **[[Federalist Society]] / [[Leonard Leo]]** — overlapping judicial-conservative infrastructure the family supports.
- **Schlitz Brewing family** — Richard Uihlein's ancestral fortune lineage.

## Public statements

- Liz Uihlein has written combative columns in the company's Uline catalog and newsletters criticizing regulation, taxation, and progressive politics — an unusual fusion of corporate communication and political messaging.
- The couple have generally avoided press interviews; their political profile is documented chiefly through campaign-finance filings and investigative reporting rather than public statements.

## Open questions

- Because much of the Uihlein giving flows through 501(c)(4) dark-money vehicles, the full scale and all recipients cannot be precisely documented; published figures rely on attribution by investigative reporters.
- How tightly coordinated is the Uihlein network with the [[Club for Growth]], the [[Federalist Society]], and state-level conservative groups versus parallel but independent funding?
- The degree of Richard versus Elizabeth Uihlein's individual involvement in specific funding decisions is not fully documented.
