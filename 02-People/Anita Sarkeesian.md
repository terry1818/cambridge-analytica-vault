# Anita Sarkeesian

## Summary

Anita Sarkeesian (born 1983) is a Canadian-American media critic and the founder of Feminist Frequency, a nonprofit producing video commentary on the representation of women in popular media. In 2012 she launched a Kickstarter campaign to fund a video series, "Tropes vs. Women in Video Games," analyzing recurring sexist patterns in game design. The campaign immediately drew an organized harassment response; the abuse it generated paradoxically pushed the campaign far past its funding goal and made Sarkeesian a public figure in debates over gaming culture.

Sarkeesian matters to this vault as one of the principal targets of [[Gamergate]], the 2014 online harassment campaign that functioned as an early, large-scale demonstration of coordinated digital mobbing. Gamergate's participants used social media, anonymous imageboards, and "review-bombing" to swarm a small set of women; the tactics — manufactured grievance, plausible-deniability framing ("it's about ethics in games journalism"), and decentralized swarm coordination — directly anticipated the harassment and disinformation methods later scaled in election interference and culture-war info-warfare. Several figures who gained prominence through Gamergate, including [[Steve Bannon]]-adjacent media operations, carried its audience and methods into the 2016 political environment.
