# Thomas Hofeller

## Summary

Thomas Hofeller was the Republican Party's preeminent redistricting expert and mapmaker for roughly four decades, from the 1970s through his death in August 2018. As RNC redistricting director and as a consultant to Republican state parties, Hofeller drew or supervised the drawing of congressional and state legislative maps across the United States through multiple redistricting cycles, including the critical post-2010 cycle that followed [[REDMAP]]'s success. After his death, his estranged daughter Stephanie discovered four external hard drives and 18 USB drives containing approximately 75,000 files documenting his work. She made these public in January 2020 after a court battle, revealing the internal mechanics of partisan gerrymanders and — crucially — documenting Hofeller's role in the Trump administration's attempt to add a citizenship question to the 2020 Census.

## Key Facts

- Worked as RNC redistricting director through multiple cycles.
- Often described, including by opponents, as the most sophisticated Republican redistricting practitioner of his generation.
- Was primary consultant for the post-2010 maps in North Carolina and other key states following REDMAP's legislative gains.
- North Carolina maps he drew were later struck down by courts as both racial and partisan gerrymanders; his files provided evidentiary basis for those rulings.
- His files revealed he authored a study concluding that a citizenship question on the Census would allow Republicans to draw maps favorable to "Republicans and Non-Hispanic Whites" — directly contradicting the Trump administration's stated rationale for the question (DOJ Voting Rights Act enforcement needs).
- The Hofeller files were central evidence in litigation over the citizenship question; the Supreme Court ultimately blocked the question in *Department of Commerce v. New York* (2019), with Roberts citing the pretextual justification.
- Died of natural causes in August 2018 without having told his daughter about the files.

## Timeline

- **1970s–2000s:** Serves as RNC redistricting director and consultant to state parties through multiple cycles.
- **2010:** REDMAP gives Republicans legislative control of key states; Hofeller is engaged as primary mapmaker.
- **2011–2012:** Draws congressional maps in North Carolina and other states; maps later become targets of litigation.
- **~2015:** Produces internal study concluding a Census citizenship question would benefit "Republicans and Non-Hispanic Whites."
- **August 2018:** Dies of natural causes.
- **Late 2018:** Daughter Stephanie finds hard drives and USB drives in his room; enters them into evidence in a North Carolina redistricting lawsuit through Common Cause.
- **2019:** Files surface in census citizenship question litigation; Roberts cites pretext issue in blocking the question.
- **November 2019:** Court rules the files can be made public.
- **January 5, 2020:** Stephanie Hofeller publishes the files publicly; NPR and others report extensively.

## Relationships

- **[[Chris Jankowski]] and [[Ed Gillespie]] / [[Republican State Leadership Committee]]:** Hofeller was the mapmaker who translated RSLC's 2010 legislative gains into durable Congressional advantages. RSLC won the legislatures; Hofeller drew the maps.
- **Stephanie Hofeller (daughter):** Estranged from her father for years before his death; discovered and released the files.
- **Common Cause:** Received the hard drives from Stephanie under court order; used them in North Carolina redistricting litigation.
- **Trump administration:** Hofeller's citizenship question study was used — covertly — to argue for adding the question, while the administration's public rationale was VRA enforcement.

## Public Statements

Hofeller gave few public statements about his methods. He was known internally as a meticulous and secretive operator. His internal files contain candid discussion of racial and partisan targeting that he did not make public during his lifetime.
