# Gleb Pavlovsky

## Summary

Gleb Olegovich Pavlovsky (5 March 1951 – 27 February 2023) was one of the two leading "political technologists" of Putin's Kremlin — the other being [[Vladislav Surkov]] — who designed and operated the system of centralized media management that converted Russia's nominally free press into an instrument of managed democracy in the late 1990s and 2000s. A former Soviet-era dissident who was prosecuted under Brezhnev, Pavlovsky became a political consultant during the chaotic 1990s and moved into the Kremlin orbit from 1996. He is described in Peter Pomerantsev's *This Is Not Propaganda* (2019) as an emblematic figure of the post-Soviet political-technology industry: someone who understood the difference between truth and narrative, and chose narrative as the governing logic. He was dismissed from the presidential administration in 2011, reportedly for backing Medvedev over Putin's return to power, and subsequently became a public critic of the regime before his death.

## Key Facts

- **Early life**: Born in Odessa; active in Soviet-era dissident samizdat publishing in the late 1970s; arrested and sentenced under Article 190-1 (anti-Soviet agitation) in 1982; after release, became an underground publisher.
- **Founder, Foundation for Effective Politics (FEP)**: established in 1995; the FEP became the primary vehicle for Kremlin political-technology contracts.
- **Kremlin role, 1996–2011**: served as political adviser to the presidential administration, working across Yeltsin's and then Putin's tenures; his core function was coordinating media coverage — which channels would run which stories, what newspapers would publish, what websites would post — around elections and political crises.
- **Managed democracy**: Pavlovsky was one of the architects of the concept (associated more publicly with [[Vladislav Surkov]]), which held that Russian democracy required management through controlled competition among pre-approved actors.
- **Dismissal, 2011**: removed from the presidential administration, reportedly for public statements favoring Medvedev's continuation over Putin's return.
- **Post-2011**: became a commentator and critic of the Kremlin's direction; gave extensive interviews documenting the media-management system from the inside.
- **Death**: died 27 February 2023, aged 71.

## Relationships

- **[[Vladislav Surkov]]**: co-equal political technologist in the Kremlin; they worked in parallel on different aspects of managed democracy — Surkov on ideology and legislative manipulation, Pavlovsky on media management and electoral technology.
- **Vladimir Putin** and **Dmitry Medvedev**: principals whom Pavlovsky served as adviser; his 2011 dismissal followed perceived disloyalty to Putin during the Medvedev succession question.
- **Peter Pomerantsev**: documented Pavlovsky extensively in *This Is Not Propaganda*; Pavlovsky gave interviews describing the inner logic of the political-technology system.

## Public Statements

In later interviews (documented by Pomerantsev and others), Pavlovsky provided unusually candid accounts of how Kremlin media management worked: daily meetings with broadcast executives, decisions about which stories to lead with, how to frame political opponents. He also described the political-technology industry's epistemological premise — that all political reality is constructed, and that the job was to construct it better than the opposition.
