# Leonard Read

## Summary

Leonard Edward Read (September 26, 1898 – May 14, 1983) was the organizational founder of the modern American free-market intellectual movement. Before founding the [[Foundation for Economic Education]] (FEE) in 1946, he was a Chamber of Commerce executive whose hands-on experience with corporate America gave him the fundraising relationships needed to build the first ideologically dedicated free-market institution in the United States. Read's significance lies less in original intellectual contributions than in institution-building: he created the organizational template — a corporate-funded nonprofit producing ideological education at arm's length from its funders — that became the dominant structural form of patron-class infrastructure for the rest of the twentieth century. Every major think tank that followed, from the [[Heritage Foundation]] to the [[Cato Institute]], can trace its institutional DNA to FEE under Read.

## Key Facts

- Born in Hubbardston, Michigan; raised in the farming Midwest
- Served in the US Army Air Service in World War I
- Ran a failed grocery wholesale business in Ann Arbor before pivoting to chamber of commerce work
- Rose to become General Manager of the Los Angeles Chamber of Commerce — the largest chamber branch in the country — by 1939
- Ideological conversion driven by reading Albert Jay Nock, then Ayn Rand, Ludwig von Mises, and Henry Hazlitt
- Founded FEE on March 7, 1946; served as president until his death 37 years later
- Wrote 29 books and numerous essays
- His 1958 essay "I, Pencil" — narrated from the perspective of a pencil describing the decentralized knowledge needed to produce it — became one of the most widely circulated arguments for spontaneous economic order; Milton Friedman later adapted it for his *Free to Choose* television series

## Relationships

- **Henry Hazlitt**: Co-founder of FEE; journalist-economist alliance gave FEE both organizational and intellectual credibility
- **Ludwig von Mises**: Recruited to FEE staff; Read provided Mises with institutional home and audience in the US
- **Friedrich Hayek**: FEE's founding inspired Hayek to establish the [[Mont Pelerin Society]] (1947); the two were allied but organizationally separate
- **Ayn Rand**: Influence on Read's ideological conversion; Read and Rand later had a falling-out over Rand's insistence on doctrinal purity
- **Harold Luhnow / William Volker Fund**: Key early funder; Luhnow's backing was essential to FEE's launch
- **Charles Koch**: [[Charles Koch]] has cited FEE seminars as formative in his own ideological development

## Public Statements

Read's most quoted line, from "I, Pencil" (1958): "There is a fact still more astounding: the absence of a mastermind, of anyone dictating or forcibly directing these countless actions which bring me into being."

On the purpose of FEE: "The role of FEE is not to change men's minds but to find men who have already changed their minds."

## Open Questions

- How extensively did Read coordinate with the Mont Pelerin Society's activities beyond the inspirational relationship?
