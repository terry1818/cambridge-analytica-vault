## Summary

Peter Pomerantsev is a British author, journalist, and academic of Ukrainian origin who worked as a television producer in Russia during the 2000s and early 2010s. His insider account of the Russian state's propaganda apparatus, *Nothing Is True and Everything Is Possible* (2014), established him as a key interpreter of post-truth political culture for Western audiences. His follow-up, *This Is Not Propaganda: Adventures in the War Against Reality* (2019), extended his analysis globally, examining how disinformation campaigns operate in the Philippines, Ukraine, the US, and beyond. He is a Senior Fellow at the LSE's Institute of Global Affairs and co-directs the Arena programme researching how democracies respond to [[information-warfare]].

## Key facts

- Born in Kyiv (then USSR); his father, Igor Pomerantsev — broadcaster and poet — was arrested by the KGB for distributing anti-Soviet literature, giving Pomerantsev a biographical grounding in state censorship and repression.
- Worked as a television producer in Russia from approximately 2001 to 2010, producing content for Russian state-adjacent channels; this gave him direct observation of how the Kremlin uses media as a political instrument.
- *Nothing Is True and Everything Is Possible* (PublicAffairs, 2014): memoir-cum-analysis of Russia's surrealist media landscape under Putin, introducing Western readers to "managed reality" and the weaponization of confusion.
- *This Is Not Propaganda* (PublicAffairs, 2019): global survey of disinformation showing how Russia's methods have spread or paralleled tactics in multiple democracies; includes the [[Internet Research Agency (IRA)]] context and the Brexit environment.
- His concept of "non-linear warfare" — where the goal is to disorient rather than persuade — complements [[Thomas Rid]]'s "active measures" history and [[Renée DiResta]]'s "bespoke realities" model.
- Co-directs the Arena programme at LSE examining how democratic societies counter disinformation at scale.
- Frequently cited by policymakers and researchers including DiResta as a foundational interpreter of the current information environment.
