## Summary

Thomas Rid is a German-born political scientist and historian of technology and conflict. A professor at Johns Hopkins University's School of Advanced International Studies (SAIS), he is the George H.W. Bush Professor and founding director of the Alperovitch Institute for Cybersecurity Studies. His 2020 book *Active Measures: The Secret History of Disinformation and Political Warfare* (Farrar, Straus and Giroux) is the most comprehensive scholarly history of disinformation as a state instrument, tracing operations from the Soviet 1920s through Russian interference in the 2016 US election. He testified before the US Senate Intelligence Committee on Russian active measures.

## Key facts

- Born 1975 in Aach, Baden-Württemberg, Germany. Studied social and political science at Humboldt University Berlin (PhD) and London School of Economics.
- Was Professor of Security Studies at King's College London (War Studies, 2011–2016) before joining Johns Hopkins SAIS.
- *Active Measures* demonstrates that Soviet and Russian [[disinformation]] operations share structural continuities with contemporary campaigns — meaning the 2016 IRA/GRU operations fit a documented, century-long playbook, not a novel phenomenon.
- Prior book: *Rise of the Machines: A Cybernetic History* (2016); earlier work *Cyber War Will Not Take Place* (2013).
- Testified before the Senate Select Committee on Intelligence in March 2017 on Russian hacking and the 2016 election.
- His framework of "active measures" — a Soviet term for political warfare operations short of armed conflict — provides the conceptual vocabulary used throughout Western government and academic discussions of Russian interference.
- Named founding director of the Alperovitch Institute (named for CrowdStrike co-founder Dmitri Alperovitch) at Johns Hopkins SAIS.
