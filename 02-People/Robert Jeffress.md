## Summary

Robert Jeffress is the senior pastor of [[First Baptist Dallas]], a 16,000-member Southern Baptist megachurch in downtown Dallas — historically among the largest Baptist congregations in the United States. A Fox News contributor and host of the nationally syndicated Pathway to Victory broadcast, Jeffress emerged from regional prominence to national significance in 2015–16 as one of the earliest evangelical leaders to publicly endorse Donald Trump. He became arguably the most media-prominent Southern Baptist pastor aligned with the MAGA movement, combining institutional prestige with regular cable-news access. Timothy Alberta's *The Kingdom, the Power, and the Glory* (2023) uses Jeffress as a central exhibit of what Alberta describes as the evangelical church's accommodation of Trumpism at the expense of the gospel.

## Key facts

- Born November 29, 1955; grew up in Richardson, Texas; attended First Baptist Dallas as a child.
- Completed a Doctor of Ministry at Southwestern Baptist Theological Seminary.
- Senior pastor, First Baptist Church of Wichita Falls, 1992–2007; then elected senior pastor of First Baptist Dallas on August 12, 2007.
- Pathway to Victory airs on more than 1,200 television stations in the US and in 28 countries; heard on approximately 900 radio stations.
- Delivered the sermon at a private prayer service at St. John's Episcopal Church attended by Trump on January 19, 2017 — the evening before the inauguration.
- Member of Trump's informal Evangelical Advisory Board throughout the 2016 campaign and both White House terms.
- Has made public statements characterizing Catholicism, Islam, Judaism, and Mormonism as false religions leading people to hell — statements that generated national controversy but did not sever his Trump relationship.
- In 2020, described any Christian who would not vote for Trump as "motivated by their own self-righteousness," and predicted that Trump's loss would usher in "the apocalypse."
- Remained at Fox News well after the 2020 election; continued as a vocal defender of Trump through the 2024 campaign.
