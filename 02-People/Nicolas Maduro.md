## Summary

Nicolás Maduro became president of Venezuela on the death of Hugo Chávez in March 2013 and won a narrow special election that year. Under his rule Venezuela experienced hyperinflation, economic collapse, and the departure of an estimated 7–8 million refugees — the largest displacement crisis in the Western Hemisphere. Maduro survived through election manipulation, repression of the political opposition, co-optation of the military with economic benefits, and, crucially, material support from allied autocracies. He is a central case in Anne Applebaum's *Autocracy, Inc.* (2024) demonstrating how regimes incapable of delivering prosperity can persist through transnational autocratic infrastructure — Russian loans, Iranian fuel shipments, Chinese surveillance equipment, and Cuban intelligence personnel.

**Vault relevance — comparative case:** Maduro's Venezuela illustrates Applebaum's core argument that the survival of modern autocracies is not primarily a domestic phenomenon. The regime was propped up by: Russian debt refinancing; Iranian gasoline shipments to bypass refinery failures; Chinese-supplied water cannons and surveillance tools used against protesters; and Cuban intelligence advisory personnel. The case also documents how a petro-state kleptocracy ("boligarchy") operates, generating at least $300 billion in estimated corruption from $1.2 trillion in Chávez-era oil revenues.

## Key facts

- Succeeded Chávez as vice president, then won April 2013 special election by approximately 1.5 percentage points — already disputed.
- By 2016 Venezuela's GDP had contracted by roughly 35% in three years; hyperinflation followed.
- 2018 re-election declared a sham by the US, EU, and most of the OAS; Juan Guaidó declared interim president in January 2019 with broad international recognition.
- Russia provided loans; Venezuelan police used Chinese-manufactured water cannons and tear gas against demonstrators; Iranian-origin gasoline restored collapsing refinery output.
- Transparency International documented over 125 corruption cases at PDVSA (state oil company), 17 exceeding $1 billion each.
- July 2024 presidential election: electoral authorities failed to produce supporting vote tallies; opposition claimed to have published 80%+ of voting records showing Edmundo González Urrutia won with ~67%.
- Maduro commenced a third term in January 2025.
- January 3, 2026: US military operation "Absolute Resolve" resulted in his capture and transport to New York; arraigned on narco-terrorism charges. (See Open questions for evidence-tier caveat.)
