## Summary

Alex Stamos is a cybersecurity professional who served as [[Facebook]]'s Chief Security Officer from 2015 to 2018. He previously held the same role at Yahoo!, where he led the company's response to Snowden-era disclosures and major nation-state intrusions. At Facebook, Stamos became the internal champion for more robust and transparent handling of the Russian disinformation and influence operations that had exploited the platform in the 2016 US election. His push for Facebook to publicly disclose more about Russian interference met resistance from [[Sheryl Sandberg]], [[Joel Kaplan]], and other senior executives who were concerned about political blowback and advertiser relations. After being progressively sidelined, he resigned in August 2018 and joined Stanford University, where he co-founded the Stanford Internet Observatory — a major academic center for studying platform manipulation and information operations.

## Key Facts

- UC Berkeley graduate (BS Electrical Engineering and Computer Science)
- Co-founded iSEC Partners in 2004, a security consultancy; served as expert witness in cases including Sony v. George Hotz and US v. Aaron Swartz
- Joined Yahoo! as CISO in 2014; led cryptographic security improvements in response to Snowden disclosures
- Joined Facebook as CSO in 2015
- Led Facebook's internal investigation into manipulation of the 2016 US presidential election — the most significant internal security-integrity effort Facebook had undertaken to that point
- Advocated in 2017 for Facebook to disclose more publicly about Russian election interference; met sustained resistance from colleagues including communications and policy leadership
- Plans to depart were first reported in March 2018; formally left Facebook on August 17, 2018
- Founding Director of the Stanford Internet Observatory (SIO), which became one of the leading academic bodies studying coordinated inauthentic behavior and platform manipulation
- Left SIO directorship in 2024 amid controversy over the Virality Project and political pressure from right-wing actors targeting the lab's work

## Public Statements

Stamos gave an extensive interview to PBS FRONTLINE's "The Facebook Dilemma" (2018), providing detailed public account of tensions inside Facebook over Russian election interference response. He has remained an active public commentator on platform safety and cybersecurity policy.
