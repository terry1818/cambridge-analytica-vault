# Adelson family

## Summary

Sheldon Adelson (1933–2021) and his wife Dr. Miriam Adelson (b. 1945) were, for over a decade, the most powerful single source of Republican Party money in the United States. Sheldon Adelson built his fortune as chairman and CEO of Las Vegas Sands Corporation, which he turned into the world's largest casino operator through the Venetian in Las Vegas and vast gaming properties in Macau and Singapore. Miriam Adelson, Israeli-born, is a physician and addiction-medicine researcher. After their 1991 marriage the couple's political giving grew steadily, becoming a torrent after the 2010 *Citizens United* decision opened super PACs.

The Adelsons sit in the vault's "patron wing," but with a distinctive profile: their giving was organized around two priorities — defeating Democrats and shaping U.S. policy toward Israel along Likud-aligned lines. Sheldon Adelson's wealth made the "Adelson primary" a real stage in Republican candidate selection: contenders traveled to Las Vegas to court him. After Sheldon's death in January 2021, Miriam Adelson continued as a leading GOP donor, and a renewed "Adelson primary" was reported ahead of the 2024 cycle. The family illustrates how concentrated private wealth can directly steer both domestic party politics and foreign policy.

## Key facts

- **Role(s):** Casino magnate / Las Vegas Sands CEO (Sheldon); physician and addiction researcher (Miriam); Republican mega-donors; newspaper owners.
- **Affiliations:** Las Vegas Sands Corporation, [[Republican Jewish Coalition]], Israel Hayom (free Israeli daily they founded), Las Vegas Review-Journal (purchased 2015).
- **Active period:** ~1990s–present in politics; major giving from roughly 2012.
- **Known for:** The "Adelson primary"; record-setting GOP donations; the Jerusalem embassy move.
- The Adelsons reportedly gave over $340 million to GOP causes across the 2018 and 2020 cycles, and around $133 million in 2016 backing Trump and Republican congressional campaigns.
- Sheldon Adelson reportedly offered to help pay for the new U.S. embassy in Jerusalem; State Department lawyers reviewed the legality of accepting private donations toward embassy costs. The Adelsons sat in the front row at the May 2018 embassy unveiling.
- Miriam Adelson received the Presidential Medal of Freedom from Donald Trump in 2018.
- The couple secretly purchased the Las Vegas Review-Journal in 2015, initially concealing their ownership — a controversy over donor influence on local journalism.

## Relationships

- **[[Donald Trump]]** — principal political beneficiary; the Adelsons were among his largest funders, and Sheldon pressed successfully for the Jerusalem embassy move.
- **Newt Gingrich** — 2012 candidate whose campaign Adelson kept alive with large super PAC donations.
- **Benjamin Netanyahu** — Israeli prime minister and political ally; Israel Hayom functioned as a pro-Netanyahu paper.
- **[[Republican Jewish Coalition]]** — organization closely tied to Adelson funding and influence.
- **[[Koch Network]] / [[Uihlein family]] / [[Wilks Family Foundation]]** — parallel patron-wing mega-donors; the Adelsons were often the single largest among them.
- **Miriam Adelson** — after Sheldon's death, the continuing family principal in GOP politics.

## Public statements

- Sheldon Adelson framed his political giving openly around opposition to Democrats and unwavering support for Israel, once reportedly saying he was prepared to spend "whatever it takes" to influence elections.
- Miriam Adelson has emphasized continuity of the family's commitment to Israel and to the Republican Party after Sheldon's death.

## Open questions

- The full scale of Adelson giving through 501(c)(4) and super PAC vehicles is only partly documented; published totals are estimates from campaign-finance reporting.
- How directly did Adelson money translate into specific U.S. foreign-policy decisions beyond the well-documented embassy case? The causal link is argued in reporting but hard to fully verify. `#claim/disputed`
- The extent of Miriam Adelson's independent political strategy versus continuation of Sheldon's playbook remains an open reporting question.
