# William Barr

Born May 23, 1950, New York, NY. **77th United States Attorney General** February 14, 2019 - December 23, 2020 under [[Donald Trump]]. Previously held the same position 1991-1993 under George H.W. Bush. Central figure in the Mueller Report public framing through the so-called "Barr letter" of March 24, 2019. Central figure in DOJ's response to Trump's post-2020 election challenge effort. Resigned December 23, 2020 after refusing to support Trump's stolen-election narrative.

## Pre-Mueller-confirmation memo (June 2018)

While in private practice, Barr submitted an unsolicited 19-page memo to DOJ leadership criticizing the Mueller obstruction-of-justice theory. The memo argued that the obstruction statute should not apply to "facially lawful" presidential actions (firing Comey, etc.). The memo was widely seen as Barr auditioning for a return to the AG position.

## Mueller Report framing (March 2019)

[[Robert Mueller]] submitted his report to Barr March 22, 2019. On March 24, 2019, Barr released a 4-page letter to Congress summarizing what he characterized as the report's "principal conclusions" â€” specifically that:
- Mueller had not established conspiracy/coordination between the Trump campaign and Russia
- On obstruction, Mueller did not reach a conclusion; Barr (and Deputy AG Rosenstein) concluded the evidence was "not sufficient" to establish obstruction crimes

The Barr letter was widely treated by the press as the report's conclusions. Mueller subsequently wrote Barr a March 27, 2019 letter (released later) stating Barr's summary "did not fully capture the context, nature, and substance" of the report and that it "threatened to undermine" the report's public credibility.

The full Mueller Report (redacted) was released April 18, 2019. The 10 documented obstruction episodes (see [[Deep Dive - Mueller Report 10 Obstruction Episodes]]) revealed substantial evidence on obstruction that the Barr letter had elided.

## Post-2020 election response

- December 1, 2020: Barr told the Associated Press that the DOJ had found no evidence of widespread fraud sufficient to affect the election outcome
- Trump reportedly threw food at the wall in response (per [[Cassidy Hutchinson]] testimony)
- December 14, 2020: Barr announced his resignation effective December 23
- During the December meeting in which Barr informed Trump of his resignation, Trump reportedly was furious about the DOJ statement

## January 6 era

Barr was no longer AG by January 6. [[Jeffrey Rosen]] became Acting AG. Barr publicly opposed Trump's election-challenge claims throughout 2021. He testified to the J6 Select Committee in 2022 calling Trump's fraud claims "bullshit."

## Trump 2.0 disposition

Barr publicly supported other Republican candidates in the 2024 primary and was not appointed to a position in the Trump 2.0 administration. He authored a memoir *One Damn Thing After Another* (2022).
