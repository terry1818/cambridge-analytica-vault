# Tim LaHaye

Baptist minister, author, and Christian-right organizer. **Co-founded the [[Council for National Policy]] in 1981** with [[Paul Weyrich]] and others. Active in [[Moral Majority]] and founded American Coalition for Traditional Values. **Bridge figure** between the institutional Christian-right (CNP) and mass-market evangelical culture.

## Founding of the Council for National Policy

Tim LaHaye (1926-2016) founded the [[Council for National Policy]] in 1981 with co-founders including [[Paul Weyrich]] and Texas oilman T. Cullen Davis. The founding context: the 1980 Reagan election had demonstrated the operational possibility of unified religious-right-and-movement-conservative political coordination; existing institutional infrastructure (Heritage Foundation, the various direct-mail-based political organisations) did not provide a forum for off-the-record leadership-level strategic coordination across the movement; the CNP was designed to fill that gap.

LaHaye's distinctive contribution to the founding was the religious-right legitimacy that the early CNP needed to attract evangelical-Christian leadership participation. His prior career as a pastor (Scott Memorial Baptist Church, San Diego, 1956-1981), as founder of Christian Heritage College and the Pre-Trib Research Center (premillennial-dispensationalist theological scholarship), and as a public-facing religious-right figure gave the early CNP the evangelical-Christian institutional legitimacy that a purely movement-conservative founding would have lacked.

## The Left Behind franchise

The Left Behind novel series (12 books co-written with Jerry B. Jenkins, 1995-2003, plus subsequent prequels and adaptations) sold over 65 million copies and is one of the most commercially successful religious-fiction series in publishing history. The series operationally articulated the premillennial-dispensationalist eschatology that LaHaye taught theologically and provided a popular-culture vehicle for the broader religious-right political positioning the CNP-affiliated movement was building.

The vault treats the Left Behind franchise as operationally significant for the same reason as the CNP founding: it provided the cultural-and-narrative substrate that the political-organising infrastructure depended on for popular legitimacy. The eschatological framing (which holds that current geopolitical events fulfil specific Biblical prophecy) is one of the principal popular-religious frameworks within which the contemporary religious-right political programme is understood by its base.
