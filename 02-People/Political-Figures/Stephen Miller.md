## Trajectory through the first administration

Stephen Miller (born 1985) is the longest-serving senior policy operator across both Trump administrations. His first-term roles included senior advisor to the President and the operational lead on immigration policy throughout 2017-2021. His specific authored or co-authored policy outputs included the multiple iterations of the travel ban EOs, the family-separation policy at the southern border, the Public Charge rule expansion, the Title 42 invocation during COVID-19, and the broader restructuring of refugee admissions and asylum processing.

His operational style is distinctive: high working-hour intensity, direct presidential access bypassing standard interagency process, willingness to take public visibility on contested policies, and durable network relationships across the immigration-restrictionist policy infrastructure (Center for Immigration Studies, Federation for American Immigration Reform, [[Center for Renewing America]] adjacencies).

## America First Legal and the second-term position

Between the first administration and the second, Miller founded [[America First Legal]] (AFL) as a 501(c)(3) litigation organisation. AFL functions as the conservative-aligned counter to the ACLU and other public-interest litigation organisations: it files lawsuits challenging Biden-administration policies, files amicus briefs in conservative-aligned cases, and operates as a personnel and infrastructure base for litigators who would staff Trump-administration positions in a subsequent administration.

In Trump 2.0, Miller returned as Deputy Chief of Staff for Policy and Homeland Security Advisor (a consolidated role that effectively unified the policy-and-immigration portfolio under a single White House senior staff position). The operational scope is substantially broader than his first-term role: in addition to immigration, Miller's Trump 2.0 portfolio covers the broader policy-formulation function that the [[Project 2025]] policy programme requires for execution.
