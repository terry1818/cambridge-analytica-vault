# Adrian Vermeule

## Summary

**Harvard Law professor** (Ralph S. Tyler Jr. Chair of Constitutional Law). Converted to Catholicism 2016. **Most explicit current Catholic Integralist legal theorist** - argues for **"Common Good Constitutionalism"** (2022 book) replacing originalism with a substantive Catholic-influenced framework for constitutional interpretation. Argues the state should explicitly advance Catholic conceptions of the good - including in areas (sexuality, family law, religious tolerance) where classical liberalism considers private. **Influence on JD Vance + Federalist Society debates**.

The most radical of the current Catholic Integralism circle - more explicitly anti-liberal than Deneen, Ahmari, or Hazony.

## Key facts

- **Role:** Harvard Law professor
- **Notable book:** *Common Good Constitutionalism* (Polity, 2022)
- **Religion:** Catholic convert (2016); attends Latin Mass
- **Notable for:** Most explicit current Catholic Integralist legal theory

## "Common Good Constitutionalism" central argument

- Originalism is insufficient for conservative constitutional law
- Constitutional interpretation should reflect "common good"
- "Common good" should be understood through natural law (Catholic-influenced)
- Practical implications: stronger state authority on moral, sexual, family questions
- Rejection of liberal individual-rights primacy
