# R.J. Rushdoony

## Summary

Armenian-American Presbyterian minister and theologian. Founder of **[[Chalcedon Foundation]]** (1965). **Architect of Christian Reconstructionism** -" the theological system holding that **Old Testament biblical law should govern all spheres of society** (theonomy), and that Christians are commanded by Genesis 1:28 to take "dominion" over earthly institutions. Author of the **1973 *Institutes of Biblical Law*** -" the foundational text of the modern dominionist movement. Instrumental in the legalization of homeschooling in the US (his testimony was central to multiple state and federal court cases). **The intellectual source for the theological grammar that all subsequent Christian-right political movements adopted.**

## Key facts

- **Born:** 1916-04-25 to Armenian-genocide refugees in New York City
- **Died:** 2001-02-08
- **Ordained:** Presbyterian (Orthodox Presbyterian Church)
- **Founded:** Chalcedon Foundation, 1965
- **Notable book:** Institutes of Biblical Law (1973)

## Theological positions

- **Theonomy:** Old Testament biblical law should govern modern society
- **Postmillennialism:** Christians will incrementally bring society under Christ before the Second Coming
- **Dominion mandate:** Genesis 1:28 read as command for Christians to take dominion over all earthly institutions
- **Reconstructionism:** Society should be reconstructed according to biblical-law principles
- **Homeschooling advocacy:** Christian education must escape secular state control
- **Free-market economics:** theologically defended as biblical
