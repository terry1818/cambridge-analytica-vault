# Cassidy Hutchinson

Born December 12, 1996, Pennington, NJ. Senior aide to [[Mark Meadows]] when Meadows was White House Chief of Staff March 2020 - January 2021. **Key witness before the [[January 6 Select Committee 2021-2023]]**, providing sworn deposition testimony beginning February 2022 and culminating in a televised live "surprise hearing" June 28, 2022 that became one of the most-cited evidence presentations on Trump's January 6 conduct. Subsequently published memoir *Enough* September 2023. Continued public testimony on Trump 2.0 risks.

## J6 Committee testimony

### June 28, 2022 public hearing (key testimony, all under oath)

Hutchinson testified to:

1. **Trump knew the crowd was armed before the Ellipse speech**: she heard Trump demand the magnetometers (metal detectors) be removed because the people "aren't here to hurt me," saying "Let my people in. They can march to the Capitol from here. Let the people in. Take the f-ing mags away"

2. **Physical struggle in the presidential SUV**: she described being told by Tony Ornato (Deputy White House Chief of Staff for Operations) that Trump had physically reached for the steering wheel and lunged at Secret Service agent Bobby Engel when they refused to drive to the Capitol after the rally. (This account was disputed by some Secret Service personnel; Hutchinson testified to her contemporaneous understanding from Ornato.)

3. **Pat Cipollone warnings**: White House Counsel [[Pat Cipollone]] said to Meadows on January 6 morning, "We're going to get charged with every crime imaginable" if Trump went to the Capitol

4. **Meadows on "Hang Mike Pence" chants**: Meadows reportedly said in private "He thinks Mike deserves it" (Trump's view) when discussing the crowd chants to hang VP Pence

5. **Pre-J6 warnings**: Hutchinson testified that Trump White House officials had received specific warnings before January 6 about potential violence and that the warnings did not result in additional protective measures or changes to plans

6. **Trump throwing food at the wall** when AG Barr told the press there was no widespread election fraud (December 1, 2020)

### Additional cooperation

- Multiple closed-door depositions before the J6 Committee
- Subsequently cooperated with DOJ Smith investigation
- Testified to grand juries

## What her testimony established

Hutchinson's testimony provided multiple Tier 1 (sworn-testimony) accounts of:
- Trump knowing the January 6 crowd presented physical danger before directing it to the Capitol
- Trump's determined intent to go to the Capitol
- Trump's reported endorsement of "hang Mike Pence" sentiment
- Specific White House counsel warnings of criminal conduct

The Cipollone "every crime imaginable" account in particular has been widely treated as a smoking-gun-level statement of contemporaneous White House Counsel's view of January 6 conduct.

## Significance

Hutchinson's June 28 testimony shifted public discourse and is widely viewed as having strengthened the case for prosecution that subsequently developed into [[US v Trump DC Federal Election Interference Case]]. She testified despite intense pressure from Trump-aligned lawyers and reported direct overtures from Trump World figures seeking to influence her testimony.
