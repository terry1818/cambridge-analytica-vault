## Career and the Heritage presidency

Kevin Roberts (born 1974) became president of the [[Heritage Foundation]] in October 2021, succeeding Kay Coles James. His career path includes academic roles (history PhD from University of Texas, Catholic University of America faculty), think-tank roles (the Texas Public Policy Foundation, which he led 2016-2021), and a substantial post-2021 transformation of Heritage that the vault tracks as the institutional reorientation behind the Roberts-era [[Project 2025]] launch.

The Texas Public Policy Foundation (TPPF) under Roberts was the operational template for what Roberts then implemented at Heritage. TPPF combined traditional state-level think-tank policy work with an explicit New Right intellectual frame; Roberts brought that combination to Heritage at the national-and-federal scale.

## The Project 2025 leadership

Roberts has been the public face of Project 2025 since the project's late-2022 launch through the 2024 election and into the Trump 2.0 implementation period. His operational role: institutional sponsor (Heritage provides project management, personnel database hosting, training infrastructure), public communicator (the high-profile media appearances, the book "Dawn's Early Light" published in late 2024 articulating the broader New Right policy frame), and personnel coordinator (the Heritage staff and partner-organisation coordination that the project requires).

His public commentary has explicitly aligned Heritage with [[Catholic Integralism]]-adjacent and post-liberal intellectual positions in ways the traditional movement-conservative Heritage had not done. The 2024 Dawn's Early Light book (with foreword by [[JD Vance]]) is the most explicit statement of the institutional-philosophical pivot.

## The Heritage internal disputes

The Roberts-era pivot has produced substantial internal disputes documented in the [[National Review 2025-12 Heritage Implodes]] reporting and elsewhere. Departures from Heritage have included longtime staff who view the New Right alignment as a departure from Heritage's founding-mission; new hires have brought in staff from [[America First Policy Institute]], [[Claremont Institute]], and the broader New Right institutional ecosystem.

The vault treats the disputes as Tier-1 documented evidence of institutional capture: the traditional movement-conservative Heritage has been substantially replaced by Roberts-era New Right Heritage, with operational consequences across the conservative-policy ecosystem.
