# Mark Meadows

Born July 28, 1959, Verdun, France (US citizen, born to American parents). US Representative for North Carolina's 11th district 2013-March 2020. Co-founder and chair of the House Freedom Caucus. White House Chief of Staff to [[Donald Trump]] from March 31, 2020 through January 20, 2021. Co-defendant in [[Georgia v Trump RICO 2023]]; pivotal figure in 2020 election challenge and January 6 events; central documentary record contributor including the texts he produced under congressional subpoena.

## Documented Trump-related activities

### As Chief of Staff (March 2020 - January 2021)
- Central operational figure in the 2020 election challenge effort
- Coordinated communications between Trump and outside lawyers ([[Rudy Giuliani]], [[John Eastman]], [[Sidney Powell]])
- Coordinated the Justice Department pressure campaign culminating in the threatened Jeffrey Clark appointment
- Participated in or arranged Trump calls and meetings with state officials
- Was on the January 2, 2021 Trump-Raffensperger call

### Specific documented conduct
- Coordinated Trump's pressure on Georgia (visited Cobb County to attempt to observe signature verification; rebuffed)
- Coordinated pressure on Michigan officials
- Coordinated planning of January 6 rally
- Failed to take action to stop January 6 attack despite pleas from family members and Republicans including Donald Trump Jr

## Cassidy Hutchinson testimony

Meadows's senior aide [[Cassidy Hutchinson]] testified to the J6 Committee in June 2022 to many details about Meadows's conduct including:
- Meadows received warnings about violence potential before January 6 but did not act
- Meadows did not push Trump to act on January 6 despite multiple staff pleas
- Meadows reportedly stated of crowd chants to hang Pence that Trump "thinks Mike deserves it"

## Investigations and prosecutions

### Georgia RICO indictment (August 2023)
Indicted in [[Georgia v Trump RICO 2023]] on:
- Georgia RICO violation
- Additional counts
Status: active, pending Willis-replacement reassignment.

### Federal DC case
- Not identified as unindicted co-conspirator in Smith's DC indictment
- Reportedly cooperated with Smith's investigation under limited immunity

### J6 Committee
- Subpoenaed by J6 Committee; initially cooperated, then ceased cooperation
- House voted 222-208 to recommend contempt of Congress charges December 14, 2021
- DOJ declined to prosecute Meadows on contempt charges (June 2022)
- His text messages produced to the Committee (2,300+ texts including from Republican members of Congress, Fox News personalities, and others) became key evidence
