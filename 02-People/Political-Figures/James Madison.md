# James Madison

Fourth President of the United States (1809-1817). Primary author of the US Constitution and the Bill of Rights. Co-author of The Federalist Papers (with Hamilton and Jay).

## Vault relevance

Madison is referenced as the founding-era authority on the design of Article V (the constitutional-amendment mechanism) and on the original-intent framework for constitutional interpretation that [[Federalist Society]] originalism claims as its basis.

Madison's Federalist No. 49 specifically addressed the dangers of repeatedly opening the constitutional structure to revision - a passage frequently cited in [[Runaway convention]] critiques of the contemporary [[Convention of States]] movement.
