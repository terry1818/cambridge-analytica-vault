# William Barber II

## Summary

African-American minister, bishop, civil-rights leader, and **co-founder of the Poor People's Campaign: A National Call for a Moral Revival** with Liz Theoharis. Led the **Moral Mondays** movement in North Carolina 2013+. Founded **Repairers of the Breach**. Now also directs **Yale Divinity School Center for Public Theology and Public Policy**. Featured in **[[Source Bad Faith Documentary 2024|Bad Faith]]**. **The leading African-American faith-based voice against Christian nationalism** - critically important because his moral authority cannot be dismissed by claiming faith and democratic values are incompatible.

## Network position

[[William Barber II]] is the North Carolina-based Disciples of Christ minister who co-leads the Poor People's Campaign (the moral-revival movement modelled on the late-period Martin Luther King Jr. Poor People's Campaign of 1968). He is the principal national-level Religious-Left counter-figure to the [[Religious Right]] / [[New Apostolic Reformation]] / [[Council for National Policy]] cluster the vault tracks.

The vault includes Barber not because he is part of the CA-era operator network (he is not) but because the analytical question of "who is the operational counter to the convergence the vault documents" needs at least some representation of the Religious-Left infrastructure that actually exists. Barber's Repairers of the Breach organisation, his joint work with Liz Theoharis, and his historical-fusion organising in North Carolina (Moral Mondays) are the principal documented vehicles.
