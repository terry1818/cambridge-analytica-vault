# Richard Mack

Richard Mack is a former sheriff of Graham County, Arizona (1988-1997) and the founder of the [[Constitutional Sheriffs and Peace Officers Association]] (CSPOA). His career trajectory from elected county sheriff to national constitutional-doctrine activist is the documented foundational arc of contemporary sheriff-sovereignty doctrine.

## The Printz v United States case

Mack was a co-plaintiff in Printz v. United States (1997), the Supreme Court case that struck down the Brady Handgun Violence Prevention Act provisions requiring local sheriffs to conduct federal background checks. The Court's holding (that the federal government cannot commandeer state-and-local officials to enforce federal law) became the constitutional anchor for the broader sheriff-sovereignty doctrine Mack subsequently developed.

The Printz precedent is operationally significant for the contemporary CSPOA doctrine. Where CSPOA claims sheriffs may refuse federal cooperation, the Printz holding provides limited but real constitutional support for that claim.

## Founding of CSPOA and continuing operational role

Mack founded CSPOA in 2011 and has remained the principal public-facing figure. His training programmes (CSPOA conferences and workshops) have trained a substantial fraction of US county sheriffs in the constitutional-sheriff framework. His public-positioning includes substantial overlap with the broader patriot-movement intellectual ecosystem.
