# Alvin Bragg

Manhattan District Attorney (2022-). Brought the New York State hush-money / business-records prosecution against [[Donald Trump]] that resulted in the May 30, 2024 conviction.

## Background

- Harvard Law
- Federal civil rights prosecutor at SDNY
- Chief Deputy NY State Attorney General under Letitia James 2017-2021
- Elected Manhattan DA 2021

## Trump hush-money prosecution

- Indicted Trump March 30, 2023
- 34 counts of falsifying business records (each a felony due to underlying intent to commit / conceal another crime)
- Trial: April-May 2024 before Judge [[Juan Merchan]]
- May 30, 2024: Jury convicted on all 34 counts - Trump became first former US president convicted of a felony
- Sentencing: January 10, 2025 - unconditional discharge (no jail, no fine, no probation) due to imminent return to presidency

## Significance

The Bragg prosecution is the only Trump criminal case that reached a jury verdict. The conviction's practical effect was nullified by the 2024 election timing - establishing in real time the limit of state-criminal accountability against a sitting or imminent president.
