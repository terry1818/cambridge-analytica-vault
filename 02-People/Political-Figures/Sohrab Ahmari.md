# Sohrab Ahmari

Sohrab Ahmari (born 1985) is an Iranian-American journalist whose trajectory from Wall Street Journal editorial-page work (2014-2017) through First Things magazine op-ed editor (2017-2021) through co-founding of Compact magazine (2022) has made him one of the principal contemporary post-liberal intellectual figures.

## The 2019 "Against David French-ism" essay

Ahmari's May 2019 First Things essay "Against David French-ism" was the foundational document of the contemporary post-liberal vs classical-liberal intellectual dispute on the US right. The essay argued that David French-style classical-liberal conservatism was inadequate to the contemporary cultural-political environment, and that conservatives should pursue more aggressive political-cultural confrontation framed in explicitly Christian-moral terms.

The essay produced sustained intellectual dispute on the right, including French's published response and substantial subsequent intellectual development of both the post-liberal and classical-liberal positions.

## Compact magazine

Ahmari co-founded Compact magazine in 2022 with Matthew Schmitz (also formerly of First Things) and Edwin Aponte. Compact is a heterodox political-economic publication that crosses traditional left-right intellectual boundaries; it has published material from substantially-left-aligned writers alongside substantially-right-aligned writers, with shared editorial framing around anti-establishment political-economic positioning.

## Connection to broader vault network

Ahmari's positioning is operationally adjacent to [[Patrick Deneen]], [[Adrian Vermeule]], [[Yoram Hazony]], and the broader [[Catholic Integralism]] / post-liberal / National Conservatism intellectual ecosystem. His operational influence has been principally intellectual rather than directly political; the intellectual influence has subsequently shaped Trump-administration-adjacent personnel and policy positioning.
