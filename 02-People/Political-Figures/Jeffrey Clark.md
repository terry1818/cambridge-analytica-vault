# Jeffrey Clark

Former US Department of Justice official. Served as Assistant Attorney General for the Environment and Natural Resources Division 2018-2020; also held Acting Assistant Attorney General role for Civil Division September 2020 - January 2021. **Central figure in the late-December 2020 / early-January 2021 plan to use DOJ to falsely declare the 2020 election compromised**, including drafting a letter to Georgia state officials for that purpose. Indicted as defendant in [[Georgia v Trump RICO 2023]]; identified as Unindicted Co-Conspirator 4 in [[US v Trump DC Federal Election Interference Case]].

## The "Clark letter" / January 3 Oval Office meeting

In late December 2020, Clark -- through introductions facilitated by Trump-aligned Representative Scott Perry (R-PA) -- began meeting with Trump directly outside normal DOJ chain of command. Clark drafted a December 28, 2020 letter to Georgia state officials (Speaker, Senate President, Governor) stating DOJ had "identified significant concerns" affecting the outcome of the 2020 election and urging the state legislature to convene a special session.

Acting AG [[Jeffrey Rosen]] and Acting Deputy AG [[Richard Donoghue]] refused to sign or send the letter, characterizing it as based on false statements unsupported by any DOJ investigation.

**January 3, 2021 Oval Office meeting**: Trump met with Rosen, Donoghue, Clark, White House Counsel [[Pat Cipollone]], and Steven Engel. Trump considered firing Rosen and installing Clark as Acting AG to send the letter. Rosen, Donoghue, Engel, and Cipollone all stated they (and the DOJ leadership team broadly) would resign if Trump proceeded. Cipollone reportedly characterized the Clark plan as a "murder-suicide pact." Trump did not proceed.
