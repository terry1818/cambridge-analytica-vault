## The Affinity Partners / MBS thread

The single most operationally consequential thread in Kushner's vault role is the [[Affinity Partners]] / Saudi PIF arrangement. Within months of leaving the White House in January 2021, Kushner formed Affinity Partners, a private-equity firm, and secured a $2 billion commitment from the Saudi Public Investment Fund (PIF). The PIF investment committee initially recommended against the commitment, citing the inexperience of Kushner's firm; PIF leadership overrode the committee.

The arrangement is unusual on standard private-equity terms (a first-time fund managed by an executive with no PE track record does not normally receive such a commitment from a sovereign wealth fund). The vault treats the arrangement as documented quid-pro-quo-adjacent activity without asserting a specific quid; the relevant policy decisions Kushner made during his 2017-2021 White House tenure (substantial alignment with Saudi and Emirati preferences on regional issues; Khashoggi-killing response; Abraham Accords structuring) are independently documented.

## First-administration role and the Middle East policy thread

Kushner served as senior advisor to the President during 2017-2021, with operational authority over the Middle East peace process, the Mexico relationship, government reorganisation initiatives, and the broader sub-cabinet appointments-and-clearance question. His specific operational outputs included the Abraham Accords (the 2020 UAE-Israel-Bahrain-Sudan-Morocco normalisation agreements), the "Peace to Prosperity" Israeli-Palestinian framework that the Palestinian side rejected, and the structural alignment of US Middle East policy with Saudi and Emirati positions on Iran, Qatar, and regional governance.

The [[Mueller Investigation]] documented Kushner's attendance at the June 2016 Trump Tower meeting with Russian-linked attorney Natalia Veselnitskaya. The investigation did not produce charges against Kushner.
