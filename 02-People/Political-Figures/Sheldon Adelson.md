# Sheldon Adelson

Sheldon Adelson (1933-2021) was the founder of Las Vegas Sands Corporation (LVS) and the principal historical Republican mega-donor of the post-Citizens-United era. His combined giving across the 2012, 2016, and 2020 election cycles exceeded $500M, making him the single largest Republican donor by significant margin until his January 2021 death.

## Las Vegas Sands wealth source

LVS is the world's largest casino-and-resort operator by market capitalisation, with principal operations in Macau, Singapore, and Las Vegas. The Macau operation specifically produced the substantial 2000s-2010s wealth expansion that funded Adelson's political-giving programme.

## Israel and Jerusalem embassy

Adelson's most operationally consequential single policy intervention was the Trump 2017 decision to move the US embassy from Tel Aviv to Jerusalem. Multiple reports and post-administration disclosures documented Adelson as the principal force behind the decision, with substantial direct lobbying of Trump personally and substantial coordinated lobbying through the broader Republican-aligned Israel-policy infrastructure.

The embassy-move decision was operationally distinct from typical donor-policy-influence patterns because the decision had been opposed by virtually the entire US foreign-policy establishment across multiple prior administrations, was opposed by virtually all major US allies, and produced substantial diplomatic consequences. The decision was substantially attributable to Adelson's personal advocacy.

## Israeli-American Council (IAC)

Adelson founded the Israeli-American Council in 2007 as the principal coordination organisation for Israeli-Americans in US political life. IAC has become a substantial Republican-aligned political-organisation infrastructure.

## Republican donor architecture

Adelson's political-giving operated principally through:

- Direct candidate and PAC giving (FEC-disclosed)
- Adelson-funded super PACs at substantial scale across cycles
- The [[Republican Jewish Coalition]] (RJC) as institutional coordination
- Substantial coordinated giving through adjacent vehicles
- The Adelson Family Foundation as philanthropic infrastructure
