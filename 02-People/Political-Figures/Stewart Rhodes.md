# Stewart Rhodes

Founder and leader of the [[Oath Keepers]]. Yale Law graduate. Convicted of seditious conspiracy for his role in the [[January 6 Capitol Attack]].

## Convictions (Tier 1)

- November 29, 2022: Federal jury, DC: GUILTY of seditious conspiracy, obstruction of an official proceeding, tampering with documents
- May 25, 2023: Sentenced to 18 years in federal prison - longest sentence handed down for J6 conduct at that time
- Trial judge Amit Mehta found at sentencing that Rhodes's conduct met the definition of terrorism under the federal sentencing guidelines

## Conduct established at trial

- Pre-positioned a "quick reaction force" of weapons at a Comfort Inn in Arlington, VA on January 5
- Rhodes was on encrypted Signal channels coordinating Oath Keepers movement at the Capitol on January 6
- Sent messages invoking the Insurrection Act and urging Trump to "do nothing and let me and my boys handle this"

## What the record does NOT establish

- No direct communication between Rhodes and Trump or the White House was proved at trial
- The seditious conspiracy verdict establishes coordinated illegal use of force - not that the conspiracy was directed from above

## Pardon

- January 20, 2025: Trump issued blanket J6 pardons including Rhodes. Rhodes released from federal prison
