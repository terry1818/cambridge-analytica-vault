# Paula White-Cain

## Summary

**Donald Trump's longtime spiritual advisor**, dating back to ~2002 when she began counseling him. **Brought NAR apostles, prophets, televangelists, and messianic rabbis into Trump's earliest 2015 campaign meetings** - functionally **the institutional bridge from NAR to the Trump campaign**. Led the White House Faith and Opportunity Initiative under Trump 1.0. Comes from prosperity-gospel + Black-Pentecostal + televangelist circles. Continues as Trump 2.0-era religious advisor.

## Key facts

- **Born:** 1966-04-20
- **Role:** Trump spiritual advisor (2002-present); WH Faith and Opportunity Initiative (Trump 1.0)
- **Church:** City of Destiny (Apopka FL); previously Without Walls International Church (Tampa FL)
- **Notable for:** Bringing NAR into the Trump campaign and administration

## Key relationships

- **Donald Trump:** Two-decade spiritual-advisor relationship
- **NAR apostles:** Networked dozens of NAR figures into early Trump campaign
- **Trump 1.0 cabinet:** Various interactions; less programmatic than [[Ralph Drollinger]]'s Capitol Ministries but more politically central

## Why she matters

White is **the institutional bridge** between:

- The NAR / charismatic / prosperity-gospel evangelical wing
- The Trump-personal-network operational layer

Without White's institutional networking, **NAR figures would not have had direct access to Trump and his campaign** in the way they did. Her role parallels - and predates - the more bureaucratic theocratic infrastructure ([[Capitol Ministries]], [[Conservative Partnership Institute]]) that emerged later.
