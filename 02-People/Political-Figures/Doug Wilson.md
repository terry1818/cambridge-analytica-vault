# Doug Wilson

Doug Wilson (born 1953) is the pastor of Christ Church in Moscow, Idaho, founder of [[New Saint Andrews College]], co-founder of the [[Association of Classical Christian Schools]] (ACCS) and Logos School, and principal figure of the post-2020 "TheoBros" classical Christian education and Christian nationalist movement.

## Christ Church Moscow Idaho

Christ Church is the operational base of the Wilson institutional complex. The congregation has grown substantially since the 1990s and now operates in coordination with adjacent Wilson-aligned institutions including New Saint Andrews College, Logos School (K-12), ACCS-accredited schools nationally, Canon Press (the Wilson publishing operation), and Greyfriars Hall (the Wilson seminary-training programme).

The Christ Church complex has been the subject of substantial local controversy in Moscow, Idaho over the past two decades on multiple grounds including church-state-relations questions, accusations of mishandling specific abuse cases, and the broader "demographic mission" framing through which Wilson has publicly advocated coordinated congregational growth in specific geographic areas.

## Theological-political framing

Wilson's theological framing is explicitly Christian nationalist, drawing on [[Christian Reconstructionism]] / theonomist tradition (RJ Rushdoony, Gary North) with substantial post-2020 visibility. His 2022 book "Mere Christendom" articulates the position. Wilson has substantially influenced the broader "TheoBros" network of younger Reformed-evangelical pastors who explicitly advocate Christian-nationalist political positions.

## ACCS and the classical-Christian-education infrastructure

The Association of Classical Christian Schools, co-founded by Wilson in 1994, is the principal accrediting and coordinating institution for the classical-Christian-education movement. ACCS-accredited schools number in the hundreds nationally; the movement has substantial overlap with the broader homeschool ecosystem and with state-level school-choice / voucher programmes.

## Connection to the broader vault network

Wilson is the principal operational figure connecting the older Christian Reconstructionism intellectual tradition to contemporary [[Moms for Liberty]] / school-choice / classical-Christian-education infrastructure. The post-2020 visibility of Wilson's framing has been substantial; multiple Trump-2.0-adjacent figures (Pete Hegseth and others) have explicit Wilson-network adjacency.
