# Cruz Campaign (2016)

## Summary

The 2016 presidential campaign of US Senator [[Ted Cruz]] (R-TX). Operationally significant for this vault as the **first major US presidential campaign to engage [[Cambridge Analytica]]** for psychographic targeting and data analytics work. Total CA payments approximately **$5.8 million** per FEC records.

## CA engagement

- **2015-07:** Cruz partnership with CA publicly disclosed ([[Source Vogel 2015-07-07 Politico Cruz psychographic]])
- **2015-10:** Washington Post reports $750K already paid to CA ([[Source Sellers 2015-10-19 WaPo Cruz $750K]])
- **2015-11:** Bloomberg profile of CA's Cruz work ([[Source Issenberg 2015-11-12 Bloomberg Cruz data miner]])
- **2015-12-11:** [[Source Davies 2015-12-11 Guardian]] â€” first major exposÃ© on Cruz/CA Facebook data use
- **2016-02-20:** Cruz finishes 3rd in South Carolina primary; campaign drops CA shortly after, reportedly concluding psychographic models had failed to identify likely Cruz supporters
- **2016-05-03:** Cruz suspends campaign after losing Indiana primary to Trump

## Why this matters for the vault

The Cruz campaign engagement of CA represents the **operational validation moment** for the CA business model:
- Major US presidential campaign explicitly hired CA
- Mercer family funded both Cruz and CA simultaneously
- "Cruz Crew" mobile app tested CA-style data collection
- Cruz's eventual rejection of CA (after South Carolina loss) was an **early warning signal** that CA's effectiveness claims were exaggerated

After Cruz dropped out (May 2016), Mercers transferred CA support to Trump campaign within weeks.
