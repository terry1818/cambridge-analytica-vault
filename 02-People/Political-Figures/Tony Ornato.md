# Tony Ornato

Career Secret Service agent. Served as White House Deputy Chief of Staff for Operations (2019-2022) - a rare reassignment of an active USSS agent into a political role.

## Why he matters

- Was a key node between Trump's political operation and his security detail
- Cassidy Hutchinson testified that Ornato told her Trump lunged at a Secret Service agent ([[Robert Engel]]) in the presidential SUV on January 6 when told he could not go to the Capitol
- Ornato disputes Hutchinson's account; Engel through counsel disputes the lunge but not the underlying anger
- USSS has not produced contemporaneous radio logs or written notes corroborating either version

## Evidence tier

- **Tier 1**: Hutchinson's testimony was given under oath. She has been corroborated on other January 6 details (e.g. White House visitor logs, contemporaneous texts)
- **Tier 2**: The lunge specifically - Ornato told Hutchinson it happened, then later disputed having said it. There is no third-party witness identified to date
- **Tier 3 / NOT established**: That Trump physically attacked any agent. The Committee did not affirm the lunge as proven

## Other January 6 conduct

- Ornato's role in the J6 communications structure is a continuing question - the J6 Committee found Secret Service text messages from January 5-6 had been deleted as part of a routine device migration that USSS conducted AFTER the Committee had requested preservation
