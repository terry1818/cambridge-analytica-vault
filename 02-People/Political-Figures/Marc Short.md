# Marc Short

Chief of Staff to Vice President [[Mike Pence]] (2019-2021). Cooperated with [[January 6 Select Committee]].

## Role around January 6

- Present in the Oval Office and West Wing meetings as the pressure campaign on Pence escalated December 2020 - January 2021
- Testified to the [[January 6 Select Committee]] under subpoena (May 2022, July 2022)
- Notified Secret Service on January 5, 2021 that Pence and Trump were on a collision course over the certification - a warning later cited as evidence the security risk was foreseen (J6 Committee Final Report)

## What he told the Committee (Tier 1, sworn)

- Pence "made very clear to the President" he had no constitutional authority to reject electors
- Short was in the room when [[John Eastman]] presented the two-page memo
- Pence directed Short and [[Greg Jacob]] to research the Electoral Count Act question

## Post-J6

- Joined Advancing American Freedom (Pence's PAC) after January 2021
- Has criticized Trump's role in J6 publicly while remaining a Republican operative

## What the public record does NOT establish

- Short has not alleged that Trump knowingly directed violence on January 6
- He has framed J6 as a failure of Trump's judgment, not as a criminal conspiracy he personally witnessed
