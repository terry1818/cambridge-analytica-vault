# Peter Navarro

Born July 15, 1949, Cambridge, MA. PhD economist (Harvard). Director of the White House Office of Trade and Manufacturing Policy 2017-2021 under Trump 1.0. Architect of the China trade-war policy. **Central figure in the post-2020 "Green Bay Sweep" plan to delay election certification on January 6**. **Convicted of two counts of contempt of Congress September 7, 2023** for defying [[January 6 Select Committee 2021-2023]] subpoena; sentenced to 4 months federal prison and $9,500 fine; served full term March-July 2024. Returned as **Senior Counselor for Trade and Manufacturing in Trump 2.0** January 2025.

## January 6 / Green Bay Sweep plan

Navarro authored memos in late 2020 / early 2021 outlining what he called the "Green Bay Sweep" â€” a multi-phase plan to delay Electoral College certification on January 6 by having Republican members of Congress object to state electoral counts, generating debate, and pressuring VP Pence to refuse certification or delay it for state-legislature reconsideration. The plan was operationally linked to:

- The Chesebro-Eastman alternate-elector theory
- The Trump January 6 rally
- The Republican congressional objection effort

Navarro discussed the plan publicly in his 2021 book *In Trump Time* and in subsequent media appearances.

## J6 Committee subpoena and contempt prosecution

- February 9, 2022: J6 Committee subpoenaed Navarro for testimony and documents
- Navarro refused to comply, citing claimed executive privilege (which Trump had not formally asserted)
- June 2, 2022: indicted on two counts contempt of Congress (18 USC Â§ 192)
- September 7, 2023: convicted at jury trial in DC District Court before Judge Amit Mehta
- January 25, 2024: sentenced to 4 months federal prison + $9,500 fine
- DC Circuit affirmed conviction March 2024
- Served March 19, 2024 - July 17, 2024 at FCI Miami

Navarro is the first sitting or former senior White House official to serve federal prison time for contempt of Congress in over half a century.

## Trump 2.0 role

January 2025: Trump appointed Navarro as Senior Counselor for Trade and Manufacturing in the second-term White House. Position not requiring Senate confirmation. Continues authorship of trade-war policy.
