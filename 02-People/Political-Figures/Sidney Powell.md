# Sidney Powell

Born May 1, 1955, Durham, NC. Former federal prosecutor (Western District of Texas) 1978-1988; defense attorney best known prior to 2020 for representation of Enron defendant Merrill Lynch executives and authorship of *Licensed to Lie*. Joined the Trump 2020 election challenge team in November 2020; promoted the most extravagant of the post-2020 election fraud theories (Dominion Voting Systems and Smartmatic claims; the "Kraken" theory). Indicted as defendant in [[Georgia v Trump RICO 2023]]; **pled guilty October 19, 2023** to six misdemeanor counts of conspiracy to commit intentional interference with performance of election duties. Identified as Unindicted Co-Conspirator 3 in [[US v Trump DC Federal Election Interference Case]].

## Documented Trump-related activities

### November 2020 - January 2021
- Joined Trump campaign legal team November 14, 2020
- Co-presented at November 19, 2020 RNC press conference (the "hair dye" press conference) with Giuliani
- Promoted Dominion Voting Systems and Smartmatic conspiracy theories
- Trump publicly stepped back from her November 22, 2020 ("Sidney Powell is practicing law on her own") -- but she continued working with Trump through January
- **December 18, 2020 White House meeting**: Powell, [[Michael Flynn]], and Patrick Byrne met with Trump in the Oval Office; reportedly proposed seizure of voting machines under emergency authority and appointment of Powell as Special Counsel
- Coordinated [[Coffee County voting system breach]] in Georgia January 7, 2021 (per Georgia indictment)

### Litigation
- Filed multiple losing election challenges including "Kraken" suits in Georgia, Michigan, Arizona, Wisconsin
- All dismissed; sanctions imposed in Michigan (August 2021)

## Defamation cases against Powell

### Dominion Voting Systems
- January 2021: $1.3B defamation suit filed
- August 2021 federal judge ruled the case could proceed
- Powell's defense argument that "no reasonable person" would have believed her election fraud statements was widely covered; the court denied the motion to dismiss
- Still pending as of 2026-05

## Investigations and prosecutions

### Georgia RICO indictment (August 2023)
Indicted in [[Georgia v Trump RICO 2023]] on:
- Georgia RICO violation
- Multiple additional counts including conspiracy to commit election fraud

### Georgia plea (October 19, 2023)
**Pled guilty** to six misdemeanor counts of conspiracy to commit intentional interference with performance of election duties (the Coffee County breach charges).
- 6 years probation
- $6,000 fine
- Letter of apology to Georgia
- Agreed to testify truthfully in remaining proceedings

### DC federal case
- Identified as Unindicted Co-Conspirator 3 in Smith's DC indictment
- Not personally indicted federally
