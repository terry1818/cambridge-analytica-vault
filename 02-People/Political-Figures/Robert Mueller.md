# Robert Mueller

FBI Director (2001-2013, longest-serving in modern history). Special Counsel for the [[Russian interference 2016]] investigation (May 2017 - March 2019).

## Special Counsel investigation

- Appointed May 17, 2017 by Deputy Attorney General [[Rod Rosenstein]] following Trump's firing of FBI Director [[James Comey]]
- Final [[Mueller Report]] delivered March 22, 2019, redacted version released April 18, 2019

## Investigation outputs (Tier 1)

- 37 indictments / guilty pleas
- Including 13 Russians + 3 Russian companies ([[Internet Research Agency]] indictment)
- And 12 GRU officers (hack-and-leak indictment)
- US convictions: [[Paul Manafort]], [[Rick Gates]], [[Michael Flynn]], [[George Papadopoulos]], [[Roger Stone]], Michael Cohen (tax / lying to Congress portion)

## Mueller Report key findings

**On Russian interference:** "The Russian government interfered in the 2016 presidential election in sweeping and systematic fashion."

**On collusion:** "The investigation did not establish that members of the Trump Campaign conspired or coordinated with the Russian government in its election interference activities." Multiple contacts documented but insufficient to charge conspiracy.

**On obstruction:** Identified 10+ episodes of potentially obstructive conduct by Trump. Did not exonerate. Explicitly cited [[OLC memo]] guidance that a sitting President cannot be indicted.

## July 2019 congressional testimony

- Mueller testified before House Judiciary and Intelligence Committees
- Testimony reaffirmed report findings
- Trump characterized Mueller as "lost" in the testimony
