# Catherine Engelbrecht

Catherine Engelbrecht (born 1969) is the founder and principal operational figure of [[True the Vote]]. Her trajectory from Texas small-business owner to Tea Party local-organising to national election-skepticism operator is a documented case study in the operational continuity between 2009-era Tea Party infrastructure and 2020-onward election-denial infrastructure.

## Pre-2020 trajectory

Engelbrecht founded the King Street Patriots Tea Party group in 2009 and spun out TTV the same year. The 2010-2018 TTV operation focused on local Houston-area election monitoring with occasional national visibility. The organisation faced IRS scrutiny in the 2013 IRS Tea Party targeting controversy; Engelbrecht has cited this scrutiny as a formative experience.

## Post-2020 visibility

The 2020 election produced TTV's national-visibility breakthrough. Engelbrecht became a recurring speaker at conservative venues, a frequent guest on the broader MAGA media ecosystem (including [[Steve Bannon]]'s War Room), and the principal source for the [[Dinesh D'Souza]] 2000 Mules film. Engelbrecht and Gregg Phillips (her TTV operational partner) were jailed for contempt of court in 2022 following their refusal to disclose information in a defamation case brought by Konnech (an election-software company TTV had publicly accused of Chinese-state ties).

## Operational role in the broader ecosystem

Engelbrecht is the principal connector between the post-2020 election-denial infrastructure ([[Election Integrity Network]], [[Cleta Mitchell]] coordination) and the populist-right media ecosystem (Bannon, the broader War Room circuit). Her operational reach across both layers is the documented basis for TTV's continuing relevance despite the substantial debunking of the 2000 Mules narrative.
