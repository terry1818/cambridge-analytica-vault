# JD Vance

## Summary

**50th Vice President of the United States**, inaugurated January 20, 2025. Previously US Senator from Ohio (2023-2025). Author of *Hillbilly Elegy* (2016 memoir). **The single most direct documented example of the Tech Right successfully placing one of its own into executive-branch power.** Patron-funded throughout his pre-political career by [[Peter Thiel]] via [[Mithril Capital]] and then [[Narya Capital]] (Vances own Thiel-funded VC).

## Pre-political career

- 2010s -- Marine Corps service (Iraq deployment)
- 2013 -- Ohio State undergraduate
- 2016 -- Yale Law School graduate; published **Hillbilly Elegy** (memoir of Appalachian poverty)
- 2016-2017 -- joined **[[Mithril Capital]]** (Peter Thiel's mid-stage VC) as principal
- 2017-2019 -- Mithril, then **Revolution LLC** (Steve Case's VC)
- 2019 -- founded **[[Narya Capital]]** in Cincinnati with Thiel as anchor investor; raised ~$93M for fund I

## Political career

### 2022 Ohio Senate race

- **Thiel funded the Vance Senate campaign with ~$15 million** through the Protect Ohio Values PAC
- The single largest individual Senate-race donation in US history at that time
- Vance won the Republican primary (May 2022), then defeated Democrat Tim Ryan in November 2022
- Sworn in January 3, 2023

### Political evolution

Vance's political positions shifted dramatically over time:
- **2016:** publicly criticized Trump ("America's Hitler," "cultural heroin," "noxious")
- **2019-2021:** softened positions
- **2021+:** became enthusiastic Trump-aligned populist
- **2022-2024:** US Senator with hardline immigration, anti-China, pro-Trump positions

The shift coincided with Thiel patronage and the founding of Narya Capital -- pattern critics describe as **opportunistic alignment with the funder/political-network that could elevate him**.

- The selection was a clear win for the Thiel-aligned Tech Right faction over more traditional Republican picks

## Career trajectory through the Thiel pipeline

JD Vance (born James Donald Bowman, August 1984) had a public career arc with three distinct phases. Phase one (2003-2017): military service (US Marine Corps, Iraq deployment), undergraduate at Ohio State, JD at Yale Law School, "Hillbilly Elegy" (2016) memoir of his Appalachian-rooted childhood and the cultural commentary on white working-class economic dislocation that became a touchstone of post-2016 political-cultural analysis. Phase two (2017-2021): venture capital, principally through [[Mithril Capital]] (where he worked for Peter Thiel as a principal) and then [[Narya Capital]] (the Cincinnati-based VC fund Vance co-founded with Colin Greenspon, anchored by Thiel as principal investor). Phase three (2021-present): politics, beginning with the 2022 Ohio Senate campaign that Thiel financed at a then-unprecedented scale for a single Senate race.

The Thiel-Vance relationship is the operationally consequential thread. Thiel personally introduced Vance to relevant Republican donor and operator networks during Vance's transition from VC to politics; Thiel financed the Vance Senate PAC (Protect Ohio Values PAC) with $15 million in 2022, the largest single PAC commitment for a Senate race outside the very top tier of contested races. The Thiel commitment was operationally distinct from typical donor giving: it was made early in the cycle, in a primary-not-general phase, and was sustained through multiple campaign-finance disclosure cycles.

## The 2022 Ohio Senate campaign and the post-election period

The 2022 campaign saw Vance win the GOP primary (defeating a substantial field including Josh Mandel and Matt Dolan), then win the general election against Democrat Tim Ryan by approximately 7 percentage points. The win established Vance as a credible Tech-Right-aligned Republican senator and as the operational test case for Thiel's political-financing model.

In the Senate (2023-2024), Vance positioned aggressively on the populist-nationalist policy spectrum: opposition to continued Ukraine aid (a position that aligned with Trump-administration foreign policy preferences); active engagement with the [[Catholic Integralism]]-adjacent intellectual right (Vance is a Catholic convert; his public commentary cites Patrick Deneen and Adrian Vermeule directly); and continuing visibility on technology-policy questions (TikTok, AI regulation, social-media content moderation).
