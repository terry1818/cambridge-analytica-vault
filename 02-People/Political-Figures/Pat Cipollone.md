# Pat Cipollone

Born July 28, 1966, Bronx, NY. American attorney. Catholic conservative active in Federalist Society networks. White House Counsel from December 7, 2018 - January 20, 2021 under [[Donald Trump]]. Key behind-the-scenes figure in resisting some of the most extreme post-2020 election challenge schemes including the [[Jeffrey Clark]] plan to install Clark as Acting AG. **Testified extensively to the [[January 6 Select Committee 2021-2023]]** after privilege challenges; testified to grand jury in [[US v Trump DC Federal Election Interference Case]] investigation.

## January 3, 2021 Oval Office meeting (Tier 1)

Cipollone was present at the January 3, 2021 Oval Office meeting where Trump considered firing Acting AG [[Jeffrey Rosen]] and installing [[Jeffrey Clark]] in his place to send a false letter to Georgia officials. Cipollone, along with Rosen, [[Richard Donoghue]], Steven Engel, and White House counsel office personnel, made clear they would resign en masse if Trump proceeded. Cipollone reportedly characterized the Clark plan as a "murder-suicide pact." Trump did not proceed.

## January 6 conduct (per Cassidy Hutchinson testimony, Tier 1)

Per Hutchinson's J6 Committee testimony (corroborated in part by other witnesses), Cipollone told [[Mark Meadows]] on the morning of January 6 that if Trump went to the Capitol after the rally:

- "We're going to get charged with every crime imaginable"

Cipollone was also reportedly the source of the "I don't want to see any pardons of these people" statement opposing pardons for Trump-aligned figures involved in the post-2020 election challenge effort.

## J6 Committee testimony (July 8, 2022)

Cipollone testified to the J6 Select Committee under subpoena for approximately 8 hours on July 8, 2022. Testimony was videorecorded and excerpts shown at public hearings.

Cipollone confirmed many key Hutchinson points but disputed specifics on which he was not present. Established:
- His own role in opposing the Clark plan
- His own opposition to pardons for election-challenge figures
- His attempts to dissuade Trump from various courses of conduct

## Grand jury testimony

Cipollone testified to the DC federal grand jury investigating January 6 conduct under subpoena, after the DOJ overcame executive-privilege challenges in 2023.

## Trump 2.0

Despite his role in resisting some January 6 conduct, Cipollone has continued in Republican legal-network roles. He has not joined the Trump 2.0 administration.
