# Rick Gates

Born November 21, 1972, Richmond, VA. Longtime business partner of [[Paul Manafort]] in international political consulting. **Deputy chair of the Trump 2016 campaign** following Manafort's elevation to campaign chair March 2016. Remained on the Trump-Pence transition team through January 2017 then on the Trump Inaugural Committee. **Pled guilty February 23, 2018** in the Mueller investigation to two counts: conspiracy against the United States and making false statements to federal investigators. Subsequently cooperated extensively including testifying against Manafort. Sentenced December 17, 2019 to 45 days intermittent confinement + 3 years probation + $20,000 fine + 300 hours community service.

## Mueller cooperation

Gates's plea and cooperation produced material evidence used in:
- Manafort's August 2018 conviction at Alexandria, VA trial
- Manafort's September 2018 plea on remaining DC charges
- Roger Stone's November 2019 conviction
- Mueller's broader investigation findings on Russian government contacts

Gates provided sworn testimony to the grand jury and at trial about:
- His and Manafort's Ukraine-related lobbying work (failure to register under FARA)
- Manafort's August 2, 2016 Havana cigar bar meeting with [[Konstantin Kilimnik]] -- at which Manafort shared Trump campaign polling data with a Russian intelligence-connected operative
- Trump campaign's awareness of and coordination with Manafort
- Inaugural Committee financial practices

## SDNY Inaugural Committee investigation

Gates was reportedly cooperator in the parallel SDNY investigation into the Trump Inaugural Committee's $107M fundraising and spending. He provided documents and testimony. The Inaugural Committee subsequently faced civil action by the DC Attorney General (settled May 2022 for $750K paid by Tom Barrack's company).

## Sentencing (December 17, 2019)

Despite Mueller-team recommendation for substantial confinement, Judge Amy Berman Jackson -- citing Gates's "extraordinary cooperation" -- imposed 45 days of intermittent confinement (weekends), 3 years probation, $20,000 fine, and 300 hours community service.
