# C. Peter Wagner

## Summary

American missionary, theologian, and movement-builder. **Coined the term "New Apostolic Reformation"** in the 1990s to describe the charismatic-dominionist movement he was building. Wrote **80 books** including the 2008 *Dominion!: How Kingdom Action Can Change the World* which **explicitly endorsed dominion theology** as the bedrock of NAR. Founded Global Harvest Ministries (1993-2011) and Wagner Leadership Institute. **Explicitly traced his theology back through [[R.J. Rushdoony]] to Abraham Kuyper to John Calvin** - making him the most explicit institutional bridge between [[Christian Reconstructionism]] and contemporary charismatic Christianity. **Endorsed Donald Trump shortly before his October 2016 death.**

## Key facts

- **Born:** 1930-08-15
- **Died:** 2016-10-21
- **Career:** Missionary to Bolivia (1956-1971); Fuller Theological Seminary professor (1971-2001); independent ministry (2001-2016)
- **Founded:** Global Harvest Ministries (1993-2011), Wagner Leadership Institute
- **Notable books:** Dominion! (2008), The New Apostolic Churches (1998), Apostles Today (2006), 75+ others

## Theological positions

- **Dominion theology** explicit (2007 letter: *"Our theological bedrock is what has been known as Dominion Theology"*)
- **Five-fold ministry** including modern apostles and prophets
- **Strategic-level spiritual warfare**
- **Spiritual mapping** of geographic regions
- **[[Seven Mountains Mandate]]** integration with apostolic governance
- **Postmillennial dominionism** - similar time horizon to Rushdoony but Pentecostal expression

## Quoted self-description of NAR

*"The most radical change in how churches operate since the Protestant Reformation."*

## Why he matters

Wagner is the **central institutional bridge** in the theocratic wing:

- **Backward:** explicitly cited Rushdoony as theological source
- **Sideways:** integrated [[Seven Mountains Mandate]] (Bright/Cunningham/Schaeffer line) into NAR
- **Forward:** trained / mentored / commissioned most of the current generation of NAR apostles (Wallnau, Ahn, Jacobs, Bickle, etc.)
- **Political:** endorsed Trump in 2016, providing theological legitimation to the NAR-Trump alignment

Without Wagner, NAR would not exist as a coherent movement.
