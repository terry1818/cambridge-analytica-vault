# Jack Smith

Special Counsel (November 18, 2022 - January 2025) overseeing the federal prosecutions of [[Donald Trump]] for January 6 conduct and Mar-a-Lago classified documents handling.

## Background

- DOJ career prosecutor
- Chief of the Public Integrity Section, DOJ (2010-2015)
- Prosecutor in The Hague (2018-2022) handling Kosovo war crimes cases
- Appointed Special Counsel by Attorney General [[Merrick Garland]] November 18, 2022

## Cases brought

**Mar-a-Lago classified documents case (SDFL)** - [[US v Trump SDFL]] - indicted June 8, 2023; superseding indictment July 27, 2023 adding Walt Nauta + Carlos De Oliveira charges. Dismissed by Judge [[Aileen Cannon]] July 15, 2024 on appointments-clause grounds. Smith appealed.

**January 6 federal case (DC)** - [[US v Trump DC]] - indicted August 1, 2023. Substantial limitation imposed by Supreme Court immunity ruling July 1, 2024.

## Post-2024 election

- January 13, 2025: Resigned from DOJ before Trump took office
- January 14, 2025: Released final Special Counsel report on the J6 case
- The Trump-Documents case appeal was dismissed by DOJ shortly after Trump's inauguration

## Significance

Smith's federal prosecutions represented the highest-stakes legal accountability vehicle of the post-J6 period. Their dismissal / mooting by the 2024 election outcome and Supreme Court immunity ruling established that federal criminal prosecution of a sitting or former president faces structural obstacles beyond ordinary prosecution.
