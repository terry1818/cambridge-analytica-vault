# Robert F. Kennedy Jr

Robert F. Kennedy Jr (born 1954) is the founder of Children's Health Defense, longtime anti-vaccine advocate, 2024 presidential candidate (initially Democratic primary, then independent), and Trump 2.0 Health and Human Services Secretary (since January 2025). RFK Jr is the principal operational architect of the [[Make America Healthy Again]] (MAHA) movement that has become a substantial sub-cluster of the Trump 2.0 coalition.

## Pre-2024 trajectory

RFK Jr's pre-2024 trajectory included substantial environmental-law work (Hudson Riverkeeper, Pace University Environmental Litigation Clinic), substantial vaccine-skeptic advocacy (founded Children's Health Defense in 2007 as the World Mercury Project, rebranded in 2018), and substantial conspiracy-theory positioning across multiple topic areas (CIA-Kennedy-assassination, AIDS-skepticism, 5G-health-concern, COVID-origin theories).

## The 2024 campaign

RFK Jr launched a 2024 Democratic primary challenge against Biden in April 2023, then withdrew from the Democratic primary in October 2023 to launch an independent campaign. The independent campaign drew substantial support across both Democratic-and-Republican-leaning voters; RFK Jr suspended the independent campaign in August 2024 and endorsed Trump in exchange for the HHS Secretary appointment.

## HHS Secretary tenure

The Trump 2.0 HHS Secretary tenure has produced substantial operational outputs:

- Substantial CDC restructuring including substantial personnel changes affecting Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices (ACIP) composition
- Substantial FDA restructuring including changes to vaccine-approval pathways
- Substantial NIH restructuring including changes to research-funding priorities
- The broader Make America Healthy Again policy programme implementation
- Operational coordination with [[Project 2025]] HHS-restructuring programming

## Why this is load-bearing

RFK Jr represents the principal operational case of an anti-establishment-medical-system intellectual programme being implemented at the cabinet level. The MAHA movement's operational consequences for federal health-policy infrastructure are substantial and ongoing.
