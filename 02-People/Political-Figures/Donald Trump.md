## 2016 cycle: the CA contract and the Russian adjacency

The Trump campaign engaged CA after the Cruz primary withdrawal in May 2016, brought it under [[Brad Parscale]]'s digital operation, and used CA's data and microtargeting infrastructure through the general election. Whether CA's specific contribution was decisive is the contested question; that CA was operationally embedded in the campaign is established. The Russian-side operation (IRA social media, GRU hack-and-leak, Wikileaks coordination) ran in parallel; the degree of coordination between the two is documented in places but not fully adjudicated.

## 2017-2021 first term: cabinet and personnel

The first-term cabinet drew from the Mercer / Bannon network (Bannon as chief strategist, Kellyanne Conway from Make America Number 1 PAC), the religious-right / [[The Fellowship]] / Capitol Ministries network (Pence, Pompeo, Drollinger advisory presence, Russ Vought initial OMB tenure), and the traditional GOP donor class. The vault's reading is that the first term was a coalition government across these factions, with no single one dominant.

## The 2016 campaign data operation in detail

The Trump campaign's digital operation through 2016 was nominally led by [[Brad Parscale]] (then a relatively unknown San Antonio-based web developer who had built Trump Organization sites for years). The actual operation was a stack: Parscale's San Antonio team managed day-to-day digital ad buying through the Facebook ad platform; [[Cambridge Analytica]] supplied data integration, audience modelling, and creative-testing capacity; the Republican National Committee data operation supplied the underlying voter file; and the Bannon-led strategic team set the messaging direction.

The CA integration intensified after Bannon joined the campaign as chief executive in August 2016. The "Project Alamo" digital operation (Parscale's name for the San Antonio data-and-ads team) deployed an estimated 5.9 million different ad variants over the final months, with most testing concentrated on Facebook. CA's specific operational contribution included the persuasion modelling, the voter-suppression message development (particularly for low-propensity Democratic-leaning audiences in Wisconsin, Michigan, and Pennsylvania), and the audience-segmentation infrastructure that Parscale's team used to run the actual ad campaigns.

Whether CA's specific contribution was decisive is contested. The campaign won three Rust Belt states by combined margins of about 80,000 votes. The vault's analytical position is that the digital operation was a load-bearing component of the campaign's narrow path to 270 electoral votes, but it is not possible to attribute the win specifically to CA's psychographic-targeting overlay vs the more conventional audience-segmentation-and-ads-testing infrastructure that any sophisticated campaign would have run.

## First-term cabinet personnel by faction

The first-term cabinet drew personnel from multiple distinct conservative-movement factions, with no single one dominant:

**Mercer-Bannon political-operations faction:** [[Steve Bannon]] (chief strategist), Kellyanne Conway (counselor to the president, formerly chair of Make America Number 1 PAC), [[Stephen Miller]] (senior policy advisor).

**Religious-right / Fellowship faction:** Mike Pence (vice president, longtime [[The Fellowship]] adjacent), Mike Pompeo (CIA director then Secretary of State, Christian-nationalist-adjacent positioning), [[Ralph Drollinger]] (Capitol Ministries Bible studies for cabinet officials), [[Russell Vought]] (OMB deputy then acting director, first-term tenure).

**Traditional GOP donor-class faction:** Wilbur Ross (Commerce), Steve Mnuchin (Treasury), Rex Tillerson (early State), Gary Cohn (NEC).

**Military-and-intelligence faction:** James Mattis (Defense), H.R. McMaster (national security advisor), John Kelly (DHS then chief of staff).

The first term's policy outputs reflected the tension between these factions: substantial tax cuts (donor-class win), aggressive immigration enforcement (Mercer-Bannon win), traditional Republican foreign policy with selective populist deviations (military-faction-plus-Trump), and consistent judicial-pipeline output (Federalist Society / religious-right convergence).

## The 2024 campaign and Trump 2.0 cabinet

The 2024 campaign was operationally tighter than 2016. The [[Susie Wiles]] / Chris LaCivita management structure was professional in a way the 2016 operation was not. The data operation continued to evolve, with the underlying voter-file and persuasion infrastructure now largely commodified across all serious campaigns (the CA distinctive contribution had been absorbed into baseline practice).

The Trump 2.0 cabinet reads as a much higher degree of factional convergence under explicit [[Project 2025]] / Tech Right coordination than the first-term cabinet. Documented Project 2025 / Heritage / Tech Right overlap is substantial:

- [[Russell Vought]] returns to OMB (Project 2025 lead architect)
- [[JD Vance]] is vice president (Thiel-Mithril-Narya pipeline)
- [[Elon Musk]] runs [[DOGE]] (Tech Right operational arm)
- [[Stephen Miller]] runs deputy chief of staff for policy via [[America First Legal]]
- [[Kristi Noem]] at DHS
- [[Howard Lutnick]] at Commerce
- [[Scott Bessent]] at Treasury (hedge-fund-adjacent, Thiel-ecosystem-adjacent)
- [[Pete Hegseth]] at Defense (Tech Right + religious right convergence)
- John Ratcliffe at CIA
- Brendan Carr at FCC

The Religious Right retains presence (Vought, Noem, Mike Johnson in House leadership, the Pence-era Fellowship network's continuing influence on personnel decisions), but the operational centre of gravity has shifted toward the Tech Right relative to the first term.

## The Foreign-money question

The Trump-orbit foreign-money question is the longest-running unresolved thread in the vault. Documented contact points include the [[Mueller Investigation]] documentation of Russia contacts (Trump Tower June 2016 meeting, Manafort-Kilimnik thread, Stone-Wikileaks contacts), the [[Seychelles 2017 Meeting]] with [[Kirill Dmitriev]] brokered by [[George Nader]] (UAE) and attended by [[Erik Prince]], the [[Jared Kushner]] / [[Affinity Partners]] post-presidency Saudi PIF $2 billion arrangement, and the [[Jared Kushner]]-led 2017-2018 Middle East policy thread that aligned closely with UAE and Saudi preferences.

What is established: the contacts happened. What is contested: the operational consequence of each contact, the degree of coordination across them, and whether any specific policy decision can be causally traced to a specific contact.
