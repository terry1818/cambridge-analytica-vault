# Sheriff Mark Lamb

Mark Lamb was the sheriff of Pinal County, Arizona from 2017 to early 2025 (he declined re-election in 2024 to run for US Senate, losing the GOP primary to Kari Lake). His tenure made him the most nationally-visible CSPOA-aligned sheriff of the 2020-2024 period.

## Pinal County operational footprint

Lamb's Pinal County operation included:

- Aggressive 287g federal-cooperation programme with ICE (substantially larger than typical AZ county participation)
- Post-2020 election-administration positioning, including assertions of sheriff authority to inspect voting equipment
- Founding of [[Protect America Now]] (PAN), a national network of CSPOA-adjacent sheriffs
- Substantial media engagement and reality-television visibility ("Live PD" cast member)

## Protect America Now

PAN is a national network Lamb founded as an operational complement to CSPOA. Where CSPOA focuses on constitutional-doctrine training, PAN focuses on coordinated media-and-political-action work across member sheriffs. The two organisations are operationally complementary.

## The 2024 Senate campaign

Lamb's 2024 GOP primary campaign for Arizona Senate positioned him as the establishment-conservative alternative to Kari Lake. Lamb lost the primary; Lake went on to lose the general to Ruben Gallego. The campaign trajectory is documented as a case study in the tension between CSPOA-aligned constitutional-conservative positioning and Lake-style MAGA-aligned election-denial positioning.

## Post-2024 operational positioning

Following the 2024 Senate-primary loss to [[Kari Lake]], Lamb's post-2024 operational positioning has been principally focused on:

- Continuing CSPOA-affiliated speaking engagements
- The [[Protect America Now]] (PAN) network leadership
- Media-and-podcast appearances within the broader MAGA media ecosystem
- The continuing reality-television presence ([[A&E]] Live PD-successor programming)

Lamb's specific influence on Trump 2.0 ICE-and-sheriff coordination policy has been documented as substantial despite his lack of formal administration position.
