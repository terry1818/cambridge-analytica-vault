# Liz Cheney

Born July 28, 1966, Madison, WI. Republican politician. Daughter of former Vice President [[Dick Cheney]]. **US Representative for Wyoming's at-large district 2017-January 3, 2023**. House Republican Conference Chair 2019-2021. **One of 10 House Republicans who voted to impeach [[Donald Trump]]** in [[Trump Second Impeachment 2021]]. **Vice Chair of [[January 6 Select Committee 2021-2023]]**. Removed from House Republican Conference Chair position May 2021. Lost 2022 GOP primary to Trump-endorsed Harriet Hageman by 37 points. Central figure in mainstream-Republican opposition to Trump.

## J6 Select Committee role (Tier 1)

Cheney served as Vice Chair of the J6 Committee alongside Chair [[Bennie Thompson]]. She was one of two Republican members appointed by Speaker Pelosi after McCarthy withdrew his picks. Adam Kinzinger was the other.

### Cheney's framing throughout the Committee

In her opening statement at the first public hearing (June 9, 2022):

> "Tonight, I say this to my Republican colleagues who are defending the indefensible: There will come a day when Donald Trump is gone, but your dishonor will remain."

In her concluding remarks (December 19, 2022):

> "The Committee believes that based on the facts and the law, there is sufficient evidence to support a recommendation that the Department of Justice pursue charges against Donald Trump."

## Anti-Trump positioning
