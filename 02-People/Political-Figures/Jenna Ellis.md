# Jenna Ellis

Trump 2020 election challenge attorney. Senior legal adviser to the Trump campaign post-November 2020. Worked closely with [[Rudy Giuliani]] on state-level election challenges and false-statement testimony to state legislative committees. Indicted as defendant in [[Georgia v Trump RICO 2023]]; **pled guilty October 24, 2023** to one felony count of aiding and abetting false statements. Subject to disciplinary censure by Colorado Supreme Court March 2023 (prior to Georgia indictment).

## Documented Trump-related activities

### Co-architect of "Giuliani team" public events
- Appeared at multiple post-2020 press conferences with Giuliani
- Submitted false claims about the 2020 election to courts in multiple states (some of which were sanctioned)
- Testified to or participated in pressure on state legislators in Pennsylvania, Michigan, Arizona, Georgia

### Colorado bar censure (March 2023)
Colorado Supreme Court censured Ellis for 10 false public statements she made about the 2020 election, including statements that:
- The election was stolen
- The election was illegitimate
- President Biden was illegitimate
- Various election-fraud claims known to be unsupported

Ellis agreed to the censure and admitted the violations.

## Indictments and prosecutions

### Georgia RICO indictment (August 2023)
- Georgia RICO violation + additional counts including aiding and abetting false statements

### Georgia plea (October 24, 2023)
**Pled guilty** to one felony count of aiding and abetting false statements (Georgia OCGA Â§ 16-10-20).

In her plea allocution, Ellis tearfully stated: "If I knew then what I know now, I would have declined to represent Donald Trump in these post-election challenges." Notable for being the first public expression of regret from a Trump 2020 legal team member.

Sentence:
- 5 years probation
- $5,000 fine
- 100 hours community service
- Letter of apology to Georgia
- Agreed to testify truthfully
