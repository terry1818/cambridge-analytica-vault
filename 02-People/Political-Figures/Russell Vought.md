# Russell Vought

## Summary

American conservative political operative; **Director of the US Office of Management and Budget (OMB) in the Trump 2.0 administration**, confirmed February 2025. Previously Director of OMB during Trump's first term (July 2020 - January 2021). **Lead author of the Project 2025 chapter on OMB and Executive Office structure**. Founder of the **Center for Renewing America** (2021), a Trump-aligned think tank.

## Project 2025 role

Vought was a **central author of Project 2025** -- the [[Heritage Foundation]] policy blueprint for the Trump 2.0 administration. Specifically:

- Wrote the Executive Office of the President / OMB chapter
- Helped develop the **personnel database** (pre-vetted candidates for thousands of positions)
- Designed the **Schedule F executive order** framework for reclassifying federal civil service positions to enable political firing

## Schedule F

Vought is the **architect of Schedule F** -- the executive order originally issued by Trump in October 2020 (rescinded by Biden 2021) and **re-implemented by Trump 2.0 in 2025**. Schedule F:

- Reclassifies federal positions in "policy-influencing" roles
- Removes civil-service protections for those positions
- Enables mass political firing without traditional process
- Operationalizes the [[Curtis Yarvin]] "RAGE: Retire All Government Employees" framework

The Schedule F → DOGE mass-firings pipeline is a **defining feature** of Trump 2.0 administration governance.

## Career and the Project 2025 architect role

Russell Vought (born 1976) has held three principal operational roles relevant to the vault: deputy director then acting director of the Office of Management and Budget in the first Trump administration (2018-2021); founder and president of the [[Center for Renewing America]] (2021-2024); and director of OMB in the second Trump administration (January 2025-present).

The Center for Renewing America was the operational vehicle through which Vought authored the principal [[Project 2025]] chapters on executive-power expansion, the impoundment-authority claim (the legal theory that the executive can refuse to spend appropriated funds, which the post-Watergate Impoundment Control Act explicitly rejected), and the broader "Schedule F" personnel-restructuring programme that the Trump 2.0 administration has substantially implemented.

His chapter authorship in the 922-page "Mandate for Leadership: The Conservative Promise" document is the most operationally consequential single contribution to Project 2025 because it articulates the executive-power expansion theory that the rest of the policy programme depends on. Without the impoundment-authority claim, the broader Project 2025 spending-control programme is not legally executable; with it, the administration claims authority to refuse to implement Congress's appropriations decisions when the President disagrees with them.

## The Schedule F restructuring

Schedule F is the personnel-classification mechanism that the first Trump administration created (via October 2020 executive order) to reclassify a portion of the career federal civil service into a new "policy-influencing" category that would be removable at the President's discretion. The Biden administration revoked Schedule F on Day One; the Trump 2.0 administration reissued Schedule F via executive order in January 2025; Vought's OMB has been the principal implementing authority.

The substantive consequence: an estimated 50,000 to several-hundred-thousand federal civil servants could be reclassified into Schedule F, becoming removable for any reason or no reason. The actual implementation rate through 2025-2026 has been partial but progressive; the legal challenges to the reclassification programme have been variously successful and unsuccessful, with appellate-court precedents still developing.

Vought's authorship of the Schedule F intellectual programme (in the Project 2025 mandate document) and his operational implementation of it as OMB director are the same person performing both functions in the same policy area, which is operationally distinctive. Most policy entrepreneurs who author influential documents do not then execute the documents from the executive-branch implementation role; Vought has done both.

## The impoundment authority claim

The impoundment-authority claim is the second load-bearing legal theory Vought has operationalised in OMB. The theory: the executive branch retains constitutional authority to refuse to spend congressionally-appropriated funds when the President judges the spending inconsistent with executive-branch priorities. The Impoundment Control Act of 1974 explicitly rejected this theory, requiring the President to formally request rescissions from Congress and to spend funds Congress has not agreed to rescind. The Vought theory holds that the 1974 Act is itself constitutionally infirm.

Operational consequences in Trump 2.0: substantial impoundment of foreign-aid appropriations (the USAID restructuring), substantial pauses on domestic-programme appropriations across multiple agencies, and an aggressive litigation posture by OMB defending the impoundments against Government Accountability Office and judicial challenges. The litigation is still developing; some impoundments have been enjoined, others have been allowed to proceed, and the constitutional question is moving toward the appellate-court system and potentially the Supreme Court.

## The Center for Renewing America operational footprint

CRA under Vought (2021-2024) was operationally distinctive among Trump-orbit think tanks. Its small staff size (under 30 at peak) was the smallest of the principal Project 2025-contributing organisations; its policy-document output volume was nonetheless among the highest. The model was: Vought-authored or Vought-supervised chapters and policy proposals, with CRA serving as the institutional vehicle rather than as a substantial research-staff operation in its own right.

The CRA funding model was correspondingly concentrated: a small number of major donors (the specific composition of which has been partially reconstructed from IRS filings but not fully publicly documented) supplied the bulk of operating revenue. The model produced disproportionate policy-influence-per-dollar relative to larger Heritage-style operations.

## Religious-right adjacency and Catholic Integralism

Vought's public-facing identity includes substantial religious-right positioning. His OMB tenure has been operationally aligned with [[Project 2025]]'s religious-right policy priorities; his public commentary draws on theological framing for policy positions in ways that mainstream OMB directors typically do not.

The Vought-Catholic-Integralism adjacency is operationally documented (Vought has spoken at events with [[Patrick Deneen]], has published in First Things magazine, has cited integralist-adjacent intellectual material) even though Vought is theologically evangelical-Protestant rather than Catholic. The vault treats Vought's evangelical and integralist-adjacent positioning as operationally convergent rather than competitive: the underlying policy substance (anti-abortion absolutism, traditional-family policy, scepticism of religious neutrality in public institutions) is substantively aligned across the denominational differences.
