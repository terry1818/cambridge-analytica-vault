# John Hagee

John Hagee (born 1940) is the pastor of Cornerstone Church (San Antonio, Texas) and the founder of [[Christians United for Israel]] (CUFI). Hagee is the principal Christian-Zionist religious-political figure of the past two decades and has been one of the most operationally consequential single Christian-Zionist organisers in US politics.

## Cornerstone Church operational base

Cornerstone Church is a non-denominational charismatic-evangelical megachurch with reported membership of approximately 22,000. The church operates as the principal operational base for Hagee's broader Christian-Zionist organisational programme, with substantial church-based programming on Israel-policy and end-times-eschatology themes.

## Theological positioning

Hagee's theological positioning combines:

- Dispensationalist eschatology (the dominant 20th-century US evangelical end-times-prophecy framework)
- Substantial pro-Israel theological alignment derived from dispensationalist reading of biblical prophecy
- Charismatic and Pentecostal-adjacent positioning on direct divine revelation
- Substantial overlap with [[New Apostolic Reformation]] intellectual and operational ecosystem

His 2008 book "In Defense of Israel" and his various subsequent Israel-policy books are the principal published statements of the theological-political positioning.

## CUFI founding 2006

Hagee's founding of [[Christians United for Israel]] in 2006 produced the largest single Christian-Zionist mass-organising vehicle in US history. CUFI has substantially shaped US Republican-aligned Israel-policy positioning at both the elite and mass-base level.

## Connection to broader vault network

Hagee is the principal individual node connecting Christian-Zionist mass organising to the broader Religious Right institutional infrastructure ([[Council for National Policy]], [[Heritage Foundation]] adjacencies) and to the Israel cluster ([[AIPAC]], [[Republican Jewish Coalition]] coordination).
