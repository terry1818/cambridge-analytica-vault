# Wilks brothers

Farris Wilks (born 1952) and Dan Wilks (born 1956) are Texas brothers whose fracking-industry wealth (sale of Frac Tech Services in 2011 produced approximately $3.5B family wealth) has made them principal contemporary religious-right and Tech-Right adjacent funders. The brothers are devout members of the [[Assembly of Yahweh]] (a small Christian denomination with Pentecostal-and-Sabbatarian theological positions); Farris Wilks serves as the denomination's pastor.

## Documented funding flows

Wilks brothers funding has flowed substantially to:

- [[Daily Wire]] (substantial early-and-continuing funding, including substantial 2020-onward expansion capital)
- [[PragerU]] (multi-year substantial giving)
- Ted Cruz 2016 presidential campaign (substantial direct-and-PAC giving, including the Mercer-coordinated [[Keep the Promise PAC]] structure)
- Various religious-right political-advocacy organisations
- [[Conservative Partnership Institute]] adjacent funding
- Anti-abortion litigation infrastructure
- Wisconsin and Texas state-level conservative organising

## Operational style

The Wilks brothers' funding pattern is distinctive within the broader conservative-movement donor architecture. The brothers operate principally outside the [[Council for National Policy]] coordination layer that the Mercer-DeVos-broader-CNP donor architecture operates within. The Wilks-aligned funding decisions are made independently, frequently in direct-relationship with specific recipient operators rather than through CNP-style coordination.

## Why this is load-bearing

The Wilks brothers are the principal post-2010 religious-right donor family whose operational scale rivals the historical Mercer-DeVos-Bradley-Coors axis. The Daily Wire and PragerU funding flows specifically have substantially shaped the contemporary conservative-media-and-children's-content infrastructure.
