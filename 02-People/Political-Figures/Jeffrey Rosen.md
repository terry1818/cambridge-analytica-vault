# Jeffrey Rosen

Born February 16, 1958, Brooklyn, NY. US Deputy Attorney General May 22, 2019 - December 23, 2020. **Acting US Attorney General** December 24, 2020 - January 20, 2021, following William Barr's resignation. Central figure in the DOJ's response to Trump pressure to falsely declare the 2020 election compromised. **Held the line at the January 3, 2021 Oval Office meeting** where Trump considered firing Rosen and installing [[Jeffrey Clark]].

## DOJ response to Trump pressure (Tier 1)

After Barr's resignation, Trump escalated pressure on Rosen, [[Richard Donoghue]] (Acting Deputy AG), and Steven Engel (head of Office of Legal Counsel) to:
- Issue a public statement that DOJ had found evidence of widespread election fraud (no evidence had been found)
- Send a letter to Georgia state officials urging them to convene a special session
- Take action to support Trump's election challenges

Rosen, Donoghue, and Engel:
- Refused to issue false statements about election integrity
- Refused to sign the Clark-drafted letter to Georgia
- Documented contacts in real-time notes (Donoghue's notes became key evidence)

## January 3, 2021 Oval Office meeting (Tier 1)

Trump met with Rosen, Donoghue, Clark, [[Pat Cipollone]], and Engel. Trump considered firing Rosen and installing Clark as Acting AG. The DOJ side (and White House counsel office) made clear they would resign en masse if Trump proceeded.

Rosen reportedly told Trump: "Sir, I'm not the kind of lawyer who would resign quietly." The mass resignation threat -- combined with Cipollone's assessment that the Clark plan was a "murder-suicide pact" -- caused Trump to back down.

## Senate Judiciary testimony (August 2021)

Rosen testified at length to the Senate Judiciary Committee about the post-2020 DOJ pressure campaign. Testimony was published as [[Source: Senate Judiciary DOJ Pressure Report 2021]].

## J6 Committee testimony

Testified to J6 Select Committee in closed-door deposition and at the June 23, 2022 public hearing.
