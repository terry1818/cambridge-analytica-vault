# Marat Gelman — Russian Political Technologist and Gallerist

## Summary

Marat Gelman is a Russian political consultant and contemporary art gallerist who occupied a distinctive position at the intersection of Kremlin political management and the cultural avant-garde. Born in Chișinău (then Moldavian SSR) on 24 December 1960, he moved to Moscow and built parallel careers: as a co-founder with [[Gleb Pavlovsky]] of the Foundation for Effective Politics (FEP) — the primary political technology consultancy of the 1990s–2000s Kremlin — and as the founder of one of Russia's first private contemporary art galleries, established in 1990. Pomerantsev's *Nothing Is True and Everything Is Possible* uses figures like Gelman to illustrate how the polittekhnolog class operated simultaneously in political and cultural registers, using art as a form of soft legitimation for regime-adjacent work. Gelman later became a critic of the Putin system and was eventually designated a foreign agent and placed on the Russian wanted list.

## Key Facts

- Co-founded the Foundation for Effective Politics with Gleb Pavlovsky, which advised the Kremlin on electoral strategy from the late 1990s through the early 2000s.
- Served as deputy director of Channel One from June 2002 to February 2004, placing him at the top of Russia's primary state television apparatus during a formative period.
- Established the Marat Gelman Gallery in 1990 — among the first private contemporary art galleries in post-Soviet Russia — which showed artists including Blue Noses, Avdey Ter-Oganyan, and the AES Group.
- Directed the Perm Museum of Contemporary Art 2009–2013, building a regional contemporary arts hub before the project was shut down amid political pressure.
- Member of Russia's Public Chamber (state-civil society consultative body) in the 2010–2012 convocation.
- Moved to Montenegro circa 2014 as cultural freedoms contracted under Putin.
- Added to Russian Ministry of Justice "foreign agent" list in December 2021; vandalism of his former gallery space in Moscow ("foreign agent" written in what appeared to be blood) followed in January 2022.
- Placed on Russian Interior Ministry wanted list in December 2022.

## Relationships

- **[[Gleb Pavlovsky]]**: Co-founder of Foundation for Effective Politics; the two were the central partnership of the Kremlin-adjacent polittekhnolog world in the 1990s.
- **[[Vladislav Surkov]]**: Worked within the same political management ecosystem; Surkov was the senior Kremlin-side figure while Gelman and Pavlovsky were external consultants.
- **[[Vladimir Putin]]**: Gelman's political work served the consolidation of Putin's power; he later became a critic.
- **[[Political technologists (polittekhnologi)]]**: Gelman is a canonical figure in this professional class.

## Public Statements

After his exile Gelman has spoken openly about the nature of political technology work: that the FEP produced techniques of electoral manipulation that were initially deployed for "modernizing" purposes but ultimately served authoritarian consolidation. He has been a vocal opponent of the Ukraine invasion.
