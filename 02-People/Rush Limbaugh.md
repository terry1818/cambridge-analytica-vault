## Summary

Rush Hudson Limbaugh III (January 12, 1951 – February 17, 2021) was the most-listened-to radio talk host in United States history and the architect of a format that transformed AM radio into the primary delivery channel of right-wing political messaging. His nationally syndicated program, *The Rush Limbaugh Show*, ran from 1988 until his death. Benkler, Faris, and Roberts in *Network Propaganda* treat Limbaugh as a foundational institution in the construction of the right-wing media ecosystem — predating Fox News and establishing the emotional, adversarial, entertainment-driven style that cable and internet outlets later replicated.

## Key Facts

- Born in Cape Girardeau, Missouri; dropped out of college after one year.
- First worked in radio in Kansas City in the early 1970s; spent years in local markets before national syndication.
- The nationally syndicated show launched August 1, 1988, on WABC in New York, distributed by Edward F. McLaughlin's EFM Media; by 1990 it was carried on over 600 stations.
- At peak (early 2000s), estimated audience of 20 million weekly listeners; Talkers Magazine estimated 15.5 million weekly cumulative listeners in December 2019.
- Forbes listed 2018 earnings at $84.5 million; consistently among the highest-paid figures in US radio.
- Hosted a syndicated TV program 1992–1996 (produced by Roger Ailes).
- Inducted into the National Radio Hall of Fame (1993) and the Missouri Hall of Fame (2012).
- Awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by Donald Trump during the State of the Union address, February 2020.
- Diagnosed with advanced lung cancer in February 2020; died February 17, 2021.

## Timeline

- **1951-01-12** — Born, Cape Girardeau, Missouri.
- **1988** — *The Rush Limbaugh Show* nationally syndicated; begins on WABC New York.
- **1990s** — Program carried on 600+ AM stations; becomes the defining voice of conservative talk radio.
- **1992–1996** — Hosts syndicated TV program; series produced by [[Roger Ailes]].
- **1994** — Republican Congress credited in part to Limbaugh's grassroots mobilization; members name him "Honorary Member of the 104th Congress."
- **2003** — Resigns from ESPN football analysis role after racially charged comments about Donovan McNabb.
- **2003** — Publicly discloses OxyContin addiction and enters treatment.
- **2009** — Statement that he hopes Barack Obama "fails" becomes major political flashpoint.
- **2020-02** — Reveals advanced lung cancer diagnosis; receives Presidential Medal of Freedom from Trump.
- **2021-02-17** — Dies at 70.

## Relationships

- [[Roger Ailes]] — Produced Limbaugh's TV show; both operated in the same conservative media ecosystem.
- [[Fox News]] — Parallel institution; Fox and Limbaugh's radio show functioned as mutually reinforcing pillars of the right-wing media ecosystem through the 1990s–2010s.
- [[Sean Hannity]] — Hannity cited Limbaugh as a formative influence; similar adversarial format.
- [[Steve Bannon]] — Bannon's *Breitbart* and later War Room occupy the same ideological media space Limbaugh pioneered.
- [[Edward Bernays]] — Intellectual precursor: Limbaugh applied emotional persuasion at mass scale in the media sphere.

## Public Statements

- "I hope he fails" (on Obama, January 2009) — crystallized the "loyal opposition" posture of right-wing media throughout the Obama years.
- Referred to Sandra Fluke as a "slut" and "prostitute" (2012) — triggered advertiser boycott, losing approximately 140 sponsors.
