## Summary

Virginia "Ginni" Thomas is one of the most politically active spouses of a sitting Supreme Court justice in American history, a profile that has generated sustained ethics controversy given the conflict-of-interest implications of her lobbying and activism while Clarence Thomas adjudicates cases touching her political allies and causes.

A career conservative operative, she worked for Republican Congressman Hal Daub, the US Chamber of Commerce, the Department of Labor, and Congressman Dick Armey before joining the [[Heritage Foundation]] in 2000. She founded Liberty Consulting, a lobbying and consulting firm. Anne Nelson's [[Source Anne Nelson Shadow Network 2019]] documents Thomas as a [[Council for National Policy]] figure embedded in the CNP-Heritage network.

Her most consequential documented activity relates to the January 6, 2021 Capitol attack and its antecedents. Text messages between Thomas and White House Chief of Staff Mark Meadows, obtained by the House Select Committee on January 6, showed her urging Meadows between November 2020 and January 2021 to pursue election-overturn strategies, describing the 2020 election results as "the greatest Heist of our History" and relaying conspiracy theories about ballot fraud. She voluntarily interviewed with the Committee in September 2022.

She also founded Groundswell — described as an informal coalition of conservative operatives — with reported support from Steve Bannon.

## Key Facts

- Born February 23, 1957 (Nebraska)
- Married Clarence Thomas 1987; he became Associate Justice of the Supreme Court 1991
- Career: aide to Rep. Hal Daub; US Chamber of Commerce; US Department of Labor; aide to Rep. Dick Armey
- Joined Heritage Foundation 2000
- Founded Liberty Consulting (lobbying/consulting firm)
- Founded Groundswell, an informal conservative strategy group, with reported Steve Bannon involvement
- Member of the [[Council for National Policy]]
- November 2020–January 2021: texted Chief of Staff Mark Meadows urging election overturn; texts obtained by January 6 Select Committee
- September 2022: voluntarily interviewed by House January 6 Select Committee
- Her activist group Crowdsourcers received nearly $600,000 in anonymous donations per Washington Post investigation (2023)
- Clarence Thomas's failure to recuse from January 6-related cases despite her documented involvement generated ethics complaints and Senate scrutiny

## Public Statements

To the House January 6 Committee (September 2022): Thomas stated she had "no plans" to attend January 6 events, disagreed with the Committee's characterization of her texts, and stood by her views that the 2020 election involved irregularities. (Per press pool accounts of the interview.)

From her texts to Meadows (quoted in press coverage): "This is the greatest Heist of our History" and requests to pursue alternative electoral strategies.
