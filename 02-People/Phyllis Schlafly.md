# Phyllis Schlafly

## Summary

Phyllis Stewart Schlafly (1924–2016) was an American attorney, author, and conservative activist whose organizing fundamentally reshaped the American right. Initially a Cold War anti-communist activist — her 1964 book *A Choice Not an Echo* helped propel Barry Goldwater's nomination — she became nationally prominent in the 1970s as the leader of the campaign against the [[Equal Rights Amendment]]. In 1972 she launched STOP ERA ("Stop Taking Our Privileges") and founded the [[Eagle Forum]], the organization she would direct until her death in 2016.

Schlafly matters to this vault because she pioneered the organizational template the modern religious right would use for decades. STOP ERA mobilized conservative — and especially churchgoing — women into a disciplined grassroots lobbying force, deploying tactics like delivering homemade bread and jam to state legislators. She was among the first activists to recast a constellation of conservative positions on abortion, gay rights, education, and the military as a single "family values" agenda, and to bind that agenda to the Republican Party. The coalition she built helped move evangelical and Catholic conservatives into a shared political identity, prefiguring the constituency later organized by figures such as [[James Dobson]] and [[Jerry Falwell]].

## Key facts

- **Role(s):** Attorney; author of more than twenty books; founder and lifelong president of the [[Eagle Forum]]; architect of the STOP ERA campaign.
- **Affiliations:** [[Eagle Forum]], STOP ERA, the Republican Party, the Daughters of the American Revolution.
- **Active period:** 1950s–2016 (early anti-communist activism through her last campaign).
- **Known for:** Defeating the [[Equal Rights Amendment]]; the "family values" framing; *A Choice Not an Echo* (1964).

## Relationships

- **[[Eagle Forum]]** — the organization she founded in 1972 and led until her death; her primary institutional vehicle.
- **Barry Goldwater** — 1964 presidential candidate whose nomination her book *A Choice Not an Echo* helped advance.
- **[[Donald Trump]]** — endorsed him in the 2016 Republican primary, one of her final political acts; the endorsement triggered a leadership split within Eagle Forum.
- **[[James Dobson]]** — contemporary and ally in the broader "family values" coalition she helped pioneer.
- **[[Jerry Falwell]]** — contemporary religious-right organizer; the Moral Majority drew on the constituency-building model STOP ERA demonstrated.
- **Betty Friedan / feminist organizations** — her principal political opponents in the ERA fight.

## Public statements

- STOP ERA framing: "Stop Taking Our Privileges" — argued the ERA would strip women of protections such as exemption from the military draft, "dependent wife" Social Security benefits, and maternal custody preference.
- On feminism: described feminist goals as "extremely destructive" and as having "poisoned the attitudes of many young women" — language quoted in Eagle Forum materials.
