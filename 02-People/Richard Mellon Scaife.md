## Summary

Richard Mellon Scaife (3 July 1932 – 4 July 2014) was the principal heir to the Mellon banking, oil, and aluminum fortune and the most important individual patron of the American conservative movement in the second half of the twentieth century. Jane Mayer estimated he directed approximately $620 million (unadjusted) toward influencing American public affairs. The Washington Post called him "the leading financial supporter of the movement that reshaped American politics in the last quarter of the 20th century." Operating almost entirely outside public view — he rarely gave interviews and almost never explained his motives — he funded institutions that collectively built the ideological and organizational infrastructure later leveraged by the Koch network, the [[Heritage Foundation]], and [[Project 2025]].

## Key Facts

- Inherited the core of the Mellon fortune when his mother died in 1965; assets were split between personal holdings and a cluster of private foundations.
- Controlled three main philanthropic vehicles: the **Sarah Scaife Foundation**, the **Carthage Foundation**, and the **Allegheny Foundation**.
- In 1974, alongside beer magnate Joseph Coors, provided the founding seed money for the [[Heritage Foundation]], which became the flagship of conservative policy infrastructure.
- Substantially funded: [[Heritage Foundation]], American Enterprise Institute, [[Cato Institute]], Judicial Watch, [[FreedomWorks]], and the American Legislative Exchange Council ([[ALEC]]).
- Owned and published the Pittsburgh Tribune-Review; used it to pursue personal vendettas, most notoriously funding "the Arkansas Project" in the 1990s — an investigation operation targeting Bill Clinton.
- Gave almost no public interviews; participants in funded institutions were not told the source of their grants.
- Evidence tier on funding totals: **Tier 1** (documented by foundation tax filings and investigative journalism).

## Relationships

- **[[Charles Koch]] / [[Koch Network]]** — parallel patron track; Scaife predated and partly inspired the Koch model of policy philanthropy, though the two networks operated largely independently.
- **[[Heritage Foundation]]** — co-founder and long-term board vice-chairman; the Heritage Resource Bank concept of distributing policy papers to lawmakers drew on his funding model.
- **[[Paul Weyrich]]** — ideological collaborator; Weyrich co-founded Heritage and benefited from Scaife money in multiple ventures.
- **[[DonorsTrust]]** — the anonymized pass-through model that later dominated conservative dark money drew on the precedent Scaife established of concealing donor identity.
- **[[Sarah Scaife Foundation]]** — primary vehicle; continued operating after his death under foundation governance.

## Public Statements

Scaife was notable for his near-total public silence on his philanthropy. No significant on-record statements on political strategy are documented. Evidence of intent derives from the patterns of his grants and the accounts of intermediaries.

## Open Questions

- The full scope of overseas or foreign-connected grants from Scaife foundations has never been comprehensively mapped.
- The degree to which Scaife coordinated directly with the Koch brothers versus operating a parallel track remains unclear from public records.
- Whether "Arkansas Project" funding included any intelligence-for-hire contractors of the type later used by SCL/CA-adjacent networks is unconfirmed (Tier-3 speculation).
