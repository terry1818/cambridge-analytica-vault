## Summary

Joel Kaplan is the most consequential Republican political operative to have embedded inside a major tech platform. A Harvard Law graduate, Marine Corps veteran, and former law clerk to Justice Antonin Scalia, Kaplan spent eight years in the George W. Bush administration — rising to Deputy Chief of Staff for Policy — before joining [[Facebook]] in 2011 as its VP of US Public Policy. He became VP of Global Public Policy in 2014 and was appointed President of Global Affairs in January 2025, replacing the departing [[Nick Clegg]]. For over a decade, Kaplan served as Facebook's internal conservative advocate, repeatedly intervening in content moderation, algorithmic decisions, and policy choices to protect right-wing publishers and political actors from platform enforcement.

## Key Facts

- Law clerk to Justice Antonin Scalia after Harvard Law (1998)
- Served as White House Deputy Chief of Staff for Policy under George W. Bush
- Trump transition team reportedly considered him for a cabinet role in 2016
- Publicly supported and attended Brett Kavanaugh's Supreme Court confirmation hearings in September 2018, sitting directly behind Kavanaugh — an act that caused significant internal controversy at Facebook
- Hosted a private party for Kavanaugh at his home following the confirmation
- Advocated internally for Breitbart News and The Daily Caller to receive favorable algorithmic treatment
- Reportedly warned Facebook leadership in 2016 that removing misinformation pages would "disproportionately affect conservatives," limiting the scope of [[Project P Facebook 2016]] enforcement
- Succeeded [[Nick Clegg]] as Meta's President of Global Affairs, effective early 2025, in a transition widely read as Meta's recalibration toward the incoming Trump administration
