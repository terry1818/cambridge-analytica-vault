## Summary

Ken Strasma is an American Democratic political consultant and data scientist who was instrumental in bringing sophisticated individual-level predictive modeling to presidential-scale campaigns. He founded Strategic Telemetry in 2003, served as national targeting director for John Kerry's 2004 campaign, and then built the targeting infrastructure credited with contributing significantly to Barack Obama's 2008 primary victory over Hillary Clinton and the general election win. [[Source - Issenberg 2012 - The Victory Lab]] profiles Strasma as one of the key figures who industrialised micro-level voter modeling on the Democratic side.

## Key Facts

- **Strategic Telemetry** (founded 2003): Strasma's firm specialized in individual-level predictive modeling — assigning each registered voter a probability score for vote intention, persuadability, and turnout, rather than targeting demographic aggregates.
- **Kerry 2004**: Served as national targeting director, one of the first uses of sophisticated individual-level modeling at presidential scale on the Democratic side, though the campaign's losses limited public visibility.
- **Obama 2008 primary**: Strategic Telemetry's models showed Obama's path through Iowa and other early states by identifying low-propensity voters who could be turned out in caucuses — a core factor in defeating the Clinton machine's traditional demographic-coalition strategy.
- **Obama 2008 general**: Continued as national targeting director through the general election, helping direct field resources at individual persuadable and low-turnout voters.
- **HaystaqDNA** (founded 2012): After the Obama campaigns, Strasma launched HaystaqDNA to apply political-style microtargeting to commercial clients — an early example of the bidirectional technology transfer between political and commercial data operations that later characterized the [[Cambridge Analytica]] model.

- **[[Analyst Institute]]** — shared epistemic community of Democratic data practitioners
