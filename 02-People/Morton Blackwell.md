# Morton Blackwell

## Summary

Morton C. Blackwell (born 1939) is a Louisiana-born, Virginia-based conservative activist, organizer, and institution-builder who has shaped the infrastructure of American conservatism over six decades. His two most significant institutional contributions are the **[[Leadership Institute]]** (founded 1979), which has trained more than 305,000 conservative political activists, and the **[[Council for National Policy]]** (co-founded 1981), the secretive umbrella body that coordinates the US hard right's major donors, religious leaders, and movement figures.

Anne Nelson's *Shadow Network* identifies Blackwell as one of the founding generation of the CNP — alongside [[Richard Viguerie]], [[Paul Weyrich]], Tim LaHaye, Howard Phillips, and Phyllis Schlafly — and as the movement's leading expert on Republican Party rules, a tool he has deployed to consolidate conservative control of the GOP's internal machinery.

Where Viguerie provided the financial infrastructure (direct mail) and Weyrich provided the think-tank architecture (Heritage, ALEC), Blackwell provided the human capital pipeline: a systematic, replicable method for identifying, training, and placing conservative activists in campaigns, media, government, and advocacy organizations. The Leadership Institute is his primary instrument.

## Key Facts

- Born 1939 in Louisiana; developed early interest in conservative politics as a student.
- Was **Barry Goldwater's youngest elected delegate** at the 1964 Republican National Convention in San Francisco — an entry point that defined his movement identity and gave him insider knowledge of convention rules.
- Was an Alternate Delegate for Ronald Reagan in 1968 and 1976; became a Reagan Delegate at the 1980 convention.
- Served as **Special Assistant to President Reagan** on the White House Staff, 1981–1984, handling political and liaison functions.
- Founded the **Leadership Institute** in 1979 in Arlington, Virginia, as a 501(c)(3) educational foundation. Mission: "increase the number and effectiveness of conservative activists" by teaching political technology — campaigns, fundraising, grassroots organizing, youth politics, and media.
- The Leadership Institute has grown to offer **55 distinct training programs** and has trained **more than 305,932 students** (Leadership Institute self-reported, as of most recent available data).
- The Institute's campus network has grown to more than **1,700 conservative campus groups and newspapers** — making it the dominant infrastructure for conservative student organizing in the US.
- Notable alumni of Leadership Institute training programs include: Karl Rove, Mike Pence (who attended a Blackwell seminar), and hundreds of Republican members of Congress and Senate staff.
- Co-founded the **Council for National Policy** in 1981.
- As of 2022, the CNP's published membership list still included Blackwell.
- Recognized as Heritage Foundation's "Titan of Conservatism" award recipient.
- Described by colleagues as the movement's foremost expert on **Republican National Committee rules**, which he has used at multiple conventions to shape delegate selection processes and platform outcomes.
- His campus outreach operation has been connected to the growth of organizations like **Moms for Liberty**, whose leaders have been documented as Leadership Institute-trained.

## Public Statements

- Blackwell's mantra, widely quoted from Leadership Institute materials: "The basis of politics is organization. The basis of organization is communication."
- On the Leadership Institute's mission: "to identify, train, recruit and place conservatives in politics, government, and media."
- On his political philosophy: describes himself as a Goldwater-Reagan conservative; committed to building durable institutions rather than electoral cycles.
