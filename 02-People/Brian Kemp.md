## Summary

Brian Kemp presents one of the starkest documented conflicts of interest in modern American election administration. From 2010 to 2018 he served as Georgia Secretary of State — the official responsible for managing voter rolls and overseeing elections — and in that final year simultaneously ran for Governor. During his tenure he implemented and defended [[Exact match voter verification]], purged 1.4 million voters from Georgia's rolls (including 668,000 in a single 2017 action), and placed 53,000 registration applications on hold weeks before the 2018 election. Approximately 70% of those 53,000 pending applications were from Black voters. He defeated Democrat Stacey Abrams in November 2018 by approximately 55,000 votes (1.4 percentage points), declining to recuse himself from administering the election he was competing in.

## Key facts

- Introduced "exact match" verification policy for voter registration in 2010; a version was suspended under 2016 litigation settlement, then revived by the Georgia legislature in 2017 with documented knowledge of its disparate racial impact.
- Between 2013 and 2018, purged approximately 1.4 million voters from Georgia's rolls; the 2017 purge alone removed 668,000.
- In October 2018 — weeks before the gubernatorial election — placed ~53,000 voter registrations in "pending" status under exact-match rules; ~70% of those applicants were Black.
- Political scientists Michael Bernhard and Daniel O'Neill characterized Kemp's 2018 actions as the worst voter suppression case of that election cycle.
- Kemp denied suppression intent, arguing all actions followed federal (NVRA) and state law; a USA Today fact check noted the actions could be characterized as routine under existing law, though critics disputed this framing.
- Won 2018 gubernatorial election over Stacey Abrams (who founded [[Fair Fight]] in response and filed legal challenges).
- Re-elected Governor in 2022, defeating Abrams again.
- Became a notable Republican target in 2021 when he refused Trump's pressure to overturn Georgia's 2020 election results.
