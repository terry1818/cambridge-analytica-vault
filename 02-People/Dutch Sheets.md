# Dutch Sheets

## Summary

Dutch Sheets is one of the most operationally significant figures in the [[New Apostolic Reformation]] (NAR), functioning as what Rolling Stone described as "first among equals" among C. Peter Wagner's apostles and Wagner's top lieutenant and protégé. He is the founder of Dutch Sheets Ministries, author of 23 books, and the creator of the GiveHim15 daily-prayer platform. Between 2013 and 2021, Sheets was the primary driver of the [[Appeal to Heaven flag]] as a Christian nationalist symbol and, according to religion scholar Matthew D. Taylor, "no Christian leader did more to mobilize Christians to be in DC on January 6th than Dutch Sheets."

## Key Facts

- Born 1954 in Middletown, Ohio; son of a Nazarene evangelist.
- Graduated Christ for the Nations Institute (Dallas) with a degree in Biblical Studies, 1978.
- Founded Dutch Sheets Ministries in 1997; founding pastor of Freedom Church, Colorado Springs (1991–2010).
- Member of C. Peter Wagner's Apostolic Council of Prophetic Elders alongside [[Cindy Jacobs]], Mike Bickle, [[Chuck Pierce]], and James Goll.
- In 2015 launched the GiveHim15 daily-prayer app and YouTube channel, which became the primary digital organizing infrastructure for post-election spiritual-warfare mobilization in 2020.
- In 2013, received an [[Appeal to Heaven flag]] at a graduation ceremony at Christ for the Nations and publicly declared it the prophetic symbol of a campaign to "restore America to the Christian nation God intended" — the act that triggered the flag's mass adoption in NAR circles.
- The Appeal to Heaven flag was visible as a constant backdrop on his GiveHim15 broadcasts during the 2020 post-election period.
- Taylor documents Sheets as having orchestrated a network of approximately 20 apostles and prophets who traveled to contested swing states in late 2020 to hold prayer-and-prophecy meetings in megachurches on behalf of the election-overturn effort.
- In late December 2020, Sheets and a team of NAR prophets and apostles held a reported two-hour meeting at the White House with unnamed officials.
- On January 6, 2021, while not physically at the Capitol breach, Sheets was reached by phone and prayed over the Capitol via speakerphone, engaging in what NAR theology calls "spiritual warfare" against territorial spirits. Taylor states that as violence broke out, NAR leaders on-site called Sheets for this remote prophetic intervention.
- Post-January 6, Sheets condemned the physical violence as demonic but did not retract his role in mobilizing the crowd; he has faced no prosecution.
