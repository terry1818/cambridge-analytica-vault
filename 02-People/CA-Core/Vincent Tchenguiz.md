# Vincent Tchenguiz

## Summary

British property tycoon and investor of Iranian-Jewish heritage; founder of **Consensus Business Group** and a long-running figure in UK property and financial-services investing. Reported among the **most substantial early UK backers of [[SCL Group]]** in its pre-CA defence-and-influence-contracting phase — see [[Source Knight New Yorker - Life Inside SCL]] and adjacent reporting. Tchenguiz's involvement with SCL is the **largest single investor relationship** publicly documented from the UK pre-Mercer backer cluster, alongside [[Jonathan Marland]], [[Roger Gabb]], and [[Lord Ivar Mountbatten]]. Tchenguiz has separately been a high-profile figure in UK financial and legal disputes — including the well-publicized **Serious Fraud Office (SFO) investigation that was later withdrawn**, with Tchenguiz winning damages for the wrongful investigation.

## Key facts

- **Nationality & origin:** British citizen; Iranian-born (Jewish-Iranian heritage; family fled Iran after the 1979 revolution)
- **Brother:** Robert Tchenguiz — fellow property tycoon; the brothers' businesses have been linked in some reporting
- **Business:** founder of **Consensus Business Group**; extensive UK and international property portfolio; financial-services interests
- **SCL relationship:** **major reported early backer** of [[SCL Group]] — multiple Cadwalladr-and-others investigative pieces have cited Tchenguiz as among the largest individual investors in SCL's pre-CA period #confidence/medium
- **SFO investigation history:** UK SFO investigated Tchenguiz in connection with the 2008 collapse of Icelandic bank Kaupthing; the investigation was withdrawn and Tchenguiz won substantial damages in subsequent litigation against the SFO — a separate matter from the SCL funding context but part of the public-record context

## Significance for the vault

Tchenguiz is the **largest single named investor** in the **UK pre-Mercer SCL backer circle**, which makes him a structurally important figure for understanding **how SCL was financially possible before Mercer capital arrived**:

- The **UK aristocratic / business / Conservative-establishment funding tier** is what allowed SCL to develop its defence-contracting methodology
- The methodology built in that defence-contracting phase is what made SCL credible to the Mercers and to CA's later commercial-political clients
- Tchenguiz's involvement specifically — given his investment scale relative to other named UK backers — is **load-bearing for the [[Hypothesis - Defense-to-elections pipeline]] account** of how SCL came to exist as a vehicle in the first place
