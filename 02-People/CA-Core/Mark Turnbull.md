# Mark Turnbull

## Summary

British political operative; **Managing Director, Political Global** at [[Cambridge Analytica]] during the firm's 2016-2018 peak. Featured prominently in the **[[Channel 4 Sting 2018]]** broadcast of 2018-03-19, where he discussed CA's electoral operations across **Mexico, Malaysia, Brazil, Australia, and China** — establishing the scale of CA's international footprint as a matter of public record. Two months after CA's May 2018 insolvency, Turnbull co-founded **[[Auspex International]]** (July 2018) with Egyptian-British operative Ahmad Al-Khatib, focused on Africa and Middle East political consulting. The Auspex launch is the cleanest single example of the [[Hypothesis - Successor entities preserve operational capability|CA-to-successor personnel continuity]] pattern.

## Notable statements (Channel 4 sting, 2018-03-19)

Per [[Source Channel 4 2018-03-19 Exposed]] (verified broadcast):

- Discussed CA's **operations in Mexico, Malaysia, Brazil, Australia, and China**
- Described the use of **subcontracting via local proxies** to handle operations in jurisdictions where CA's direct work might be politically sensitive
- Discussed the deployment of **honey traps, bribery stings, and "beautiful Ukrainian girls"** for entrapment operations alongside [[Alexander Nix]]
- Explained the **separation of campaign-facing entities** ("we already have a few websites") from the operational firm to obscure attribution

These statements — recorded on undercover video and broadcast — are the most fully attributed public account of CA's international operational methodology, distinct from the Facebook-data scandal that dominated US/UK coverage.
