# Rebekah Mercer

## Summary

American Republican donor and board member; daughter of [[Robert Mercer]]. Invested in [[Cambridge Analytica]] alongside her father and provided strategic political direction. After CA's collapse, became director of [[Emerdata Limited]] (the holding company widely considered CA's successor) in March 2018, alongside her sister Jennifer Mercer, [[Alexander Nix]], and [[Johnson Chun Shun Ko]]. Also a major funder of Breitbart News and conservative platform Parler. ([[Source Wikipedia - Cambridge Analytica]])

## Role and operational footprint

[[Rebekah Mercer]] is the operational lead of the Mercer political-influence apparatus. Where her father [[Robert Mercer]] supplied the capital, she ran the network: vetting recipients, sitting on the boards of the entities the family funded, and acting as the principal liaison between the Mercer family and [[Steve Bannon]]'s organisational sprawl.

She sat on the board of [[Cambridge Analytica]] (and of SCL US, its corporate predecessor) and chaired [[Make America Number 1 Super PAC]], the vehicle that funnelled Mercer money first into [[Ted Cruz Campaign]] and then into Trump's general election operation after Cruz withdrew. She was also a board member of [[Breitbart News]] (during the Bannon era), the [[Heritage Foundation]] (where Mercer money helped reshape the institution's direction), and the Government Accountability Institute (with Bannon and Peter Schweizer).

Her operational style is distinctive: low public profile, deep board penetration, conditional funding (donors retain operational input rather than writing arms-length checks). This pattern is significant for the broader vault because it is the template several later donor-funder relationships followed: the network states project, parts of the [[Federalist Society]] funding base, and the [[Council for National Policy]] donor architecture.

## The 2017-2018 Bannon break in detail

The break was triggered by Bannon's cooperation with Michael Wolff on "Fire and Fury" (published January 2018) and specifically by Bannon's quoted characterisations of Trump family members including Donald Trump Jr. The Mercer family's public statement distancing from Bannon (issued within days of the book's release) was operationally distinct from typical donor-recipient disputes: the Mercers had been Bannon's principal capital source for five years; the family had been the principal funder of the Bannon-led conservative-movement infrastructure across multiple organisations; the break was therefore a substantial restructuring of the conservative-movement funding map, not just a personal disagreement.

Operational consequences of the break: Rebekah Mercer's Breitbart board exit; the redirection of Mercer family donor flows away from Bannon-affiliated entities; the partial demobilisation of the Mercer-Bannon coordination that had organised much of the 2014-2017 conservative-movement strategy. The post-break Mercer funding map shifted toward Parler, the [[Government Accountability Institute]] (which continued under Schweizer without Bannon), and conservative-media infrastructure that did not have direct Bannon adjacency.

## Parler and the post-2018 media-infrastructure bet

Parler (the Twitter alternative that Rebekah Mercer invested in and held a board seat at) is the most operationally distinctive Mercer family post-CA bet. Mercer joined Parler's board in 2019; her involvement was publicly disclosed in November 2020 when she announced expanded operational role amid Parler's traffic surge following Twitter's post-election labeling of Trump tweets. The platform's effective collapse after the January 6, 2021 capitol attack (Amazon Web Services suspended Parler hosting; Apple and Google removed Parler from app stores) eliminated Mercer's principal post-CA media infrastructure bet.

The Parler episode is operationally instructive: the Mercer family attempted to build a permanent alternative-media infrastructure capable of providing distribution capacity outside the constraints of Twitter and Facebook content-moderation policy; the attempt failed when the infrastructure dependencies (cloud hosting, app stores) refused to host the platform after the January 6 events. The lesson the Mercer apparatus appears to have absorbed: alternative-media infrastructure requires alternative-infrastructure infrastructure, which is much harder to build.

## Mercer Family Foundation as a separate vehicle

The Mercer Family Foundation (501(c)(3)) is the philanthropic vehicle that handles a portion of family political-aligned giving. Foundation 990s (publicly available) document substantial annual disbursements to: [[Heritage Foundation]], the [[Government Accountability Institute]], various climate-skeptic policy organisations (Heartland Institute, CO2 Coalition), various judicial-pipeline organisations (Federalist Society-adjacent), various anti-abortion and religious-right organisations, and a long tail of smaller conservative-movement organisations.

The Foundation's 2018-2024 disbursement map (the post-Bannon-break period) shows substantial continuity with the pre-break disbursement pattern at the foundation-tier giving, even as the direct family-office giving shifted.
