# Alexander Nix

## Summary

British executive; CEO of [[Cambridge Analytica]] from its January 2013 founding until suspension on 2018-03-20 following the [[Channel 4 Sting 2018]]. **Old Etonian.** Son of banker Paul David Ashburner Nix (himself an SCL investor). Director of multiple SCL-related companies including [[Firecrest Technologies]] and [[Emerdata Limited]]. **Disqualified as a UK company director for 7 years effective 2020-10-05** by the UK Insolvency Service for "marketing potentially unethical services" and "lack of commercial probity."

## The Channel 4 sting and what it actually showed

[[Alexander Nix]] was the CEO of [[Cambridge Analytica]] (and previously of SCL Elections). The defining public moment of his career was the March 2018 [[Source Channel 4 2018-03-19 Cambridge Analytica dirty tricks]] sting, in which Channel 4 reporters posing as Sri Lankan political clients secured on-camera admissions from Nix and senior CA staff about a menu of services that included (in the reporters' framing) bribery sting operations against opposition candidates, hiring sex workers to compromise targets, and disinformation through proxy actors.

The vault treats the Channel 4 footage as Tier-1 evidence of what CA marketing was prepared to claim it could deliver. Whether CA actually deployed those services in past contracts is a separate evidential question; Nix's recorded sales-pitch claims are not in themselves operational evidence. But the sting produced the public-facing collapse trajectory: Nix was suspended by the CA board within days, the firm filed bankruptcy in May 2018, and the regulatory net (ICO, FTC) tightened immediately.

## Career arc

Nix joined SCL Group in 2003, became director of SCL Elections in 2006, and was the public face of the firm's pivot from defence-side behavioural-change work to commercial political consulting through the 2010s. He was central to the 2013-2014 founding of [[Cambridge Analytica]] as the SCL US vehicle and to the Mercer capitalisation that followed.

His public-pitch style (high-status English self-presentation, military and intelligence-adjacent language, name-drops of clients and contacts) was operationally central to CA's commercial success and to its eventual collapse. The same pitch register that closed deals on camera with prospective clients closed the firm when the camera was Channel 4's.

## Post-2018 status

Nix has not been criminally prosecuted in the US or the UK. He was subject to a UK disqualification proceeding (banned from acting as a UK company director for seven years, settled in 2020); the disqualification was the principal formal consequence. He has remained out of the public-facing political-consulting world since 2018, though he has given a number of media interviews and has been associated with various small-scale advisory ventures.

## SCL career trajectory in detail

**2003 (entry):** Nix joined SCL Group as director of elections from a background in international finance and family-office investment. The SCL Group at that time was principally a defence-and-behavioural-change consultancy under Nigel Oakes; the elections division was a small commercial offshoot.

**2006-2010 (SCL Elections build-out):** Nix took director of SCL Elections in 2006 and spent the late 2000s building out the firm's international elections-consulting practice. The deployments through this period included [[Nigeria 2007 2015 Elections]] (multiple cycles), [[India SCL elections 2003-2012]] (state-level work for various clients), [[Trinidad Tobago elections]] (a documented behavioural-change campaign that became a CA marketing case study), [[Kenya 2013 2017 Elections]] (Uhuru Kenyatta's campaigns), and a broader caseload across Africa, the Caribbean, and parts of South Asia.

The 2006-2010 period is also when SCL's behavioural-change methodology consolidated. The firm's pitch deck through this period emphasises targeting voter groups by psychometric and behavioural characteristics rather than demographic ones, even before the [[OCEAN Model]] was operationalised at scale.

**2013-2014 (CA founding):** Nix was the executive who led the formation of [[Cambridge Analytica]] as the SCL US vehicle, coordinated the Mercer capitalisation with Bannon's introduction, and took CEO of CA from inception. The CA brand was deliberately separate from SCL: Nix understood that the US political-consulting market would be more receptive to an academic-credibility-coded brand than to a defence-coded brand.

**2014-2016 (CA commercial operations):** Nix was the principal closer for CA's commercial pitches. His personal style (high-status English self-presentation, military-and-intelligence-adjacent vocabulary, name-drops of clients and contacts) was central to CA's commercial success. The 2014 Cruz primary contract was a Nix-closed deal; the 2016 Trump general-election engagement was substantially shaped by Nix's relationship-building with the Mercer-Bannon-Conway team.

**2017 (peak public profile):** Nix gave TED talks, attended Davos, spoke at industry conferences, and was profiled in major business publications. The public profile was operationally useful (it drew clients) but it was also what made him the obvious target for the [[Source Channel 4 2018-03-19 Cambridge Analytica dirty tricks]] sting that landed in March 2018.

## The Channel 4 sting in detail

Channel 4's undercover team posed as Sri Lankan political clients over multiple meetings in late 2017 and early 2018. The recorded conversations captured Nix and senior CA staff describing a menu of services that included bribery-sting operations against opposition candidates ("we'll have a, a guy go in, posing as someone, a wealthy developer or something," with Ukrainian women supplied), disinformation through proxy actors, and the use of former intelligence-service contacts.

The vault's analytical position on the sting: the recordings document what CA marketing was prepared to claim it could deliver to a prospective client. Whether CA actually deployed those services in past contracts is a separate evidentiary question that the Channel 4 footage does not directly answer. But the recordings produced the public-facing collapse trajectory: Nix was suspended by the CA board within days of broadcast (March 20, 2018), the firm filed bankruptcy on May 2, 2018, and the regulatory net (ICO, FTC, FBI, parliamentary committees in the UK and Canada) tightened immediately.

## Post-2018 status and the disqualification

The principal formal consequence for Nix was a seven-year UK disqualification from acting as a company director, settled in 2020 with the UK Insolvency Service following its post-CA-bankruptcy investigation. The disqualification was a civil rather than criminal proceeding; Nix did not face criminal prosecution in the UK, the US, or any other jurisdiction where CA had operated.

Nix has remained largely out of the public-facing political-consulting world since 2018. He has given a small number of media interviews (notably to the Guardian and the FT) characterising the Channel 4 sting as entrapment and the broader CA narrative as substantially overstated. The vault treats those self-characterisations as primary-source material on his own account while noting that the regulatory record reached different conclusions.

## Why the vault treats Nix as a top connector

Nix is the operational hinge between [[SCL Group]]'s defence-and-international roots and [[Cambridge Analytica]]'s commercial-political deployment. He is the single human node whose business-development decisions explain why CA happened at the scale and timing it did. Without Nix, the Mercer capital might have gone to a different vehicle; without Nix, the Trump campaign's adoption of CA might not have happened on the same timeline; without Nix, the Channel 4 sting that catalysed the 2018 reckoning would not have produced the same on-camera admissions.

He is also the principal commercial relationship-holder for SCL/CA's international elections caseload across Africa, the Caribbean, South Asia, and Latin America. The vault treats that caseload as a documented record of the methodology's operational scope before the 2016 cycle made the firm globally famous.
