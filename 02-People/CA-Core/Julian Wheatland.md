# Julian Wheatland

## Summary

British executive; long-serving **chairman of [[SCL Group]]** (CA's parent) and appointed **CEO of [[Cambridge Analytica]] from April 2018** following [[Alexander Nix]]'s suspension and [[Alexander Tayler]]'s brief acting role. Wheatland led CA through its final weeks — through the public scandal, the [[Source Channel 4 2018-03-19 Exposed|Channel 4 sting]] fallout, the [[2018 Wylie Disclosures]], and the May 2018 insolvency filing. He was simultaneously a **director of [[Emerdata Limited]]**, the corporate vehicle that "fully acquired" CA/SCL assets through the wind-down per [[Source Pasternack Witt Fast Company 2019-07-26]].

## Key facts

- **Role(s):** SCL Group chairman; CEO of Cambridge Analytica (April 2018 – May 2018 collapse)
- **Position at the handoff:** SCL chairman first, elevated to CA CEO after Nix suspended 2018-03-20 and Tayler stepped aside 2018-04
- **Successor-entity role:** director of [[Emerdata Limited]]
- **Public visibility:** lower than Nix or Turnbull; surfaced primarily in Companies House records, DCMS testimony, and successor-entity filings

## Significance — the structured handoff

Wheatland's promotion timing is part of the [[Hypothesis - Successor entities preserve operational capability|pre-planned succession]] picture:

- 2018-03-20 — [[Alexander Nix]] suspended after Channel 4 sting
- 2018-03-28 — [[Alexander Tayler]] (acting CEO) appointed director of [[Emerdata Limited]]
- ~2018-04 — Wheatland (SCL chairman) elevated to CA CEO
- 2018-05 — CA enters insolvency
- 2018-2019 — Emerdata, under directors including Wheatland and the Mercers, "fully acquires" CA/SCL assets through the bankruptcy

Wheatland's dual role — **wind-down CEO of CA, simultaneously director of the acquiring vehicle Emerdata** — is structurally unusual and consistent with the thesis that the May 2018 insolvency was an asset-shielding maneuver rather than a clean dissolution.
