# Geoffrey Pattie

## Summary

British politician and businessman; **founding chairman of [[SCL Group]]**. Conservative MP for Chertsey and Walton 1974-1997. **Defense minister under Margaret Thatcher** (Minister of State for Defence Procurement 1983-1984, then Minister of State for Industry and Information Technology). His SCL chairmanship is one of the most direct documented links between the British defense establishment and the firm that became CA.

Per [[Source Knight New Yorker - Life Inside SCL]] and [[Source Brown 2018-03-21 Times SCL founders]], Pattie was a key figure in giving SCL its early credibility with UK MoD and US DoD clients during the 1990s-2000s defense-contracting era. The combination of his Thatcher-era defense ministerial credentials with SCL's "behavioral conflict" methodology pitch gave the firm an unusual level of access to defense procurement.

## CA-relevance

Pattie embodies the **defense-establishment-to-CA pipeline** thesis ([[Hypothesis - Defense-to-elections pipeline]]). His ministerial credentials enabled SCL's early defense contracts, which produced the methodology that CA later commercialized for political clients.
