## Network position and operational role

[[Steve Bannon]] is the operational connector between five otherwise separate vault clusters: the [[Mercer]] funding apparatus, [[Cambridge Analytica]] / [[SCL Group]] (he was a vice president of CA), [[Breitbart News]] (executive chairman until 2018), the international populist-right network (Movement, Italy/Hungary/Brazil linkages), and the first Trump administration (chief strategist, January-August 2017). His departure from the White House did not end his political activity; it shifted it to media (War Room podcast, daily multi-hour broadcast) and to the international Movement project.

His ideological framing is explicit: he describes himself as an economic nationalist, a [[Catholic Integralism]]-adjacent traditionalist, and (in his own self-presentation) the architect of a multi-decade civilisational confrontation with progressive liberalism, "globalist" elites, and the Chinese Communist Party. The vault is not a venue for evaluating that worldview as politics; it is the venue for tracing what that worldview operationally generated in money flows, personnel pipelines, and infrastructure.

## Documented CA-era operational contribution

Bannon's specific contribution at CA (per Wylie and Kaiser testimony, corroborated by ICO records) was the political-strategy overlay on the data-targeting infrastructure: which themes to test, which audiences to prioritise (suppression of low-propensity Democratic voters, energisation of low-propensity GOP-leaning voters), and the editorial direction of the Breitbart content engine that CA's microtargeting then amplified. He sat at the intersection of the data side and the content side and operationalised both at once.

## Post-2018 international and movement work

After leaving the White House and (separately) departing Breitbart following the "Fire and Fury" episode, Bannon launched The Movement, a Brussels-based vehicle aimed at coordinating European populist-right parties. He has documented working relationships with [[Matteo Salvini]] (Italy), [[Marine Le Pen]] (France), [[Viktor Orban]] (Hungary), and [[Jair Bolsonaro]] (Brazil). The Movement underperformed its own organisational ambitions but established the international relationships that subsequent right-wing infrastructure has built on.

His [[War Room]] podcast became, by 2024-2025, one of the highest-reach voices in the MAGA media ecosystem and a documented coordination surface for Trump 2.0 personnel decisions and policy framing.

## Legal exposure and current status

Bannon was pardoned by Trump in January 2021 on the federal "We Build the Wall" charges. He was subsequently convicted on contempt of Congress charges related to refusal to comply with the January 6 Committee subpoena, served prison time, and was released in 2024. New York state charges related to "We Build the Wall" proceeded independently of the federal pardon and were pending at various points in 2024-2025.

## Detailed timeline of Bannon's CA-era operational role

**2011-2013 (pre-CA setup phase):** Bannon was running [[Breitbart News]] as executive chairman following Andrew Breitbart's March 2012 death. He met the Mercer family through Andrew Breitbart's introductions and through the [[Government Accountability Institute]] (GAI), which Bannon co-founded with Peter Schweizer and which the Mercers seed-funded. GAI's first major project was the "Clinton Cash" research that became Schweizer's 2015 book â€” an opposition-research operation explicitly designed to feed mainstream-media coverage cycles. The GAI model (long-form research that journalists could not easily ignore) is the prototype for Bannon's later CA-era strategy of feeding narrative ammunition into both partisan and mainstream channels simultaneously.

**2013-2014 (CA founding):** Bannon was instrumental in convincing Robert Mercer to capitalise the new SCL US entity that became [[Cambridge Analytica]]. The Mercer commitment (approximately $15 million over multiple tranches) is what made CA operationally distinct from SCL Group's earlier commercial work. Bannon took the title of vice president at CA and was the executive sponsor of CA's pivot from research-oriented behavioural-change communications to candidate-focused political operations. He also named the company: "Cambridge Analytica" was a deliberate signal â€” invoking academic credibility (the Cambridge name) and technocratic legitimacy (analytica) over the more militaristic SCL branding.

**2014-2015 (CA operations under Bannon's strategic direction):** Bannon's operational contribution at CA was the political-narrative layer that sat on top of the technical-data layer. Where [[Christopher Wylie]] and [[Aleksandr Kogan]] built the targeting infrastructure, and where [[Alexander Nix]] sold it, Bannon defined what it was being used for: which themes to test, which audiences to demobilise (low-propensity Democratic-leaning voters), which audiences to mobilise (low-propensity GOP-leaning voters, especially in the Rust Belt). The 2014 GOP primary cycle was the test laboratory; the 2016 Trump general election was the production deployment.

**2016 (Trump campaign):** Bannon joined the Trump campaign as chief executive in August 2016, succeeding Paul Manafort. He served through Election Day and into the transition. CA's operational role on the Trump campaign tightened during the Bannon-led period, with daily coordination between the CA team (under [[Brad Parscale]]'s digital operation) and the Bannon-led strategic team.

**January-August 2017 (White House):** Bannon was chief strategist and senior counselor to the president. The first six months produced the Muslim ban EO, the Paris Agreement withdrawal, and the early personnel decisions that locked in the Trump-era national security and immigration architecture. His White House departure in August 2017 was variously attributed to Jared Kushner conflict, Charlottesville fallout, and his on-record cooperation with Michael Wolff (which would surface in "Fire and Fury" in January 2018).

## Bannon's post-2018 reconstruction

After the Mercer break (over "Fire and Fury") and the Breitbart departure, Bannon rebuilt his operational base in three directions simultaneously. First, the international Movement project (Brussels-based, aimed at coordinating European populist-right parties â€” [[Matteo Salvini]] in Italy, [[Marine Le Pen]] in France, [[Viktor Orban]] in Hungary, [[Jair Bolsonaro]] in Brazil). Second, the War Room podcast (launched 2019, became one of the highest-reach voices in the MAGA media ecosystem during 2020-2024). Third, the "We Build the Wall" private border-wall fundraising operation that produced his August 2020 federal indictment.

The Movement under-performed its own organisational ambitions in Brussels but established the international relationships that subsequent right-wing infrastructure has built on. The War Room podcast became a documented coordination surface for Trump 2.0 personnel decisions, [[Project 2025]] policy framing, and [[Article V Convention]] state-by-state organising. The Mercer-aligned funder pool he lost in 2018 was partially replaced by smaller-dollar audience funding via War Room and by the broader MAGA donor expansion.

## Legal exposure and current status

Bannon was pardoned by Trump in January 2021 on the federal "We Build the Wall" charges. He was subsequently convicted on contempt of Congress charges related to refusal to comply with the January 6 Committee subpoena, served prison time, and was released in 2024. The New York state charges related to "We Build the Wall" proceeded independently of the federal pardon and were pending at various points in 2024-2025.

His 2024-2026 public posture has been explicit alignment with Trump 2.0, advocacy for aggressive Project 2025 implementation, and continued international Movement-style coordination on right-wing populism in Europe, Latin America, and increasingly Asia.
