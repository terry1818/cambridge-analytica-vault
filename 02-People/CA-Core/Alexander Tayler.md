# Alexander Tayler

## Summary

British data scientist; **Chief Data Officer (CDO) of [[Cambridge Analytica]]** during the firm's peak operational years and the 2018 scandal. Following [[Alexander Nix]]'s suspension on **2018-03-20** after the [[Channel 4 Sting 2018]], Tayler briefly served as **acting CEO of CA** before being replaced by [[Julian Wheatland]] in April 2018. His appointment as a director of **[[Emerdata Limited]] on 2018-03-28** — within days of Nix's suspension and weeks before CA's May insolvency — is a key piece of evidence cited by [[Hypothesis - Successor entities preserve operational capability]] that the post-CA successor structure was being established **before** the public collapse, not in response to it.

## Key facts

- **Role(s):** Chief Data Officer, Cambridge Analytica (2014-2018); briefly acting CEO of CA (~March 20 – April 2018)
- **Successor role:** director of [[Emerdata Limited]] from **2018-03-28** ([[Source Companies House 2018-03-16 Emerdata appointments]])
- **Background:** data science / quantitative methods
- **Profile:** less publicly visible than Nix; surfaced primarily through Companies House filings, CA staff lists, and successor-entity directorships

## Significance of the timing

The **2018-03-28 Emerdata appointment is significant**:
- Nix suspended: 2018-03-20
- Tayler appointed Emerdata director: 2018-03-28
- CA enters insolvency: ~2018-05-01

Per [[Source Pasternack Witt Fast Company 2019-07-26]], the Emerdata vehicle "fully acquired" SCL/CA assets through the bankruptcy process. Tayler's appointment to Emerdata **eight days after the public scandal broke** is one of the cleanest data points for the **pre-planned-succession** reading of CA's collapse (`#confidence/high` per [[Hypothesis - Successor entities preserve operational capability]]).
