# David Ellison

## Summary

**Larry Ellison's son** (born January 10, 1983). Founded **[[Skydance Media]]** in 2010 with funding from his father (initial investment ~$350M+). **Engineered the August 7, 2024 Skydance-Paramount merger** that created **[[Paramount Skydance Corporation]]** - now CEO of the combined entity. **The merger gave the Ellison family operational control of one of the largest US media empires** including:

- **CBS broadcast network + CBS News** (one of three legacy US broadcast networks)
- **Paramount Pictures** (one of the "Big Five" Hollywood studios)
- **Showtime + Paramount+** (premium + streaming)
- **MTV, Nickelodeon, Comedy Central, BET, TV Land, VH1, CMT, Pop, Logo TV** (cable portfolio)
- **Pluto TV** (free-ad-supported streaming, 80M+ MAU)
- **Smithsonian Channel, Pop TV, BET+ subscription**
- **CBS Sports + various regional sports networks**

**Subsequently acquired [[The Free Press]] (Bari Weiss) October 2025** for ~$150M; Weiss installed as Editor-in-Chief of CBS News. **Documented post-merger editorial shift** including: settlement of Trump's 60 Minutes lawsuit ($16M, July 2025), reorientation of CBS News leadership, multiple high-profile journalist departures, Weiss-led restructuring.

**Operationally significant** as one of the most consequential single ownership transitions in modern US media history; the Ellison family now controls **direct broadcast access to ~120M US households** via CBS owned-and-operated stations + affiliated stations + cable distribution + streaming.

## The Paramount merger (2024)

**Timeline:**
- **2022-2024:** Multi-year acquisition discussions
- **2024-07-08:** Skydance-Paramount merger formally announced
- **2024-08-04:** Shari Redstone (Paramount controlling shareholder) approved deal
- **2024-08-07:** Merger closed; Paramount Skydance Corporation formed
- **2024-08+:** David Ellison installed as CEO; Jeff Shell (former NBCUniversal CEO) President; Charles "Skip" Vinson II Chief Restructuring Officer

**Deal structure:** ~$8B transaction involving Skydance + RedBird Capital (Gerry Cardinale); RedBird put up additional capital. **Larry Ellison personally provided ~$6B+ in equity backing** making the family the controlling owner.

**Subsequent corporate actions:**
- Rebranded combined company as **Paramount Skydance Corporation** (officially mid-2025)
- Headcount reductions ~15-20% across legacy Paramount operations
- Executive reshuffles at CBS News + Showtime + Paramount Pictures
- **July 2025:** Settled Trump 60 Minutes lawsuit for $16M (widely criticized as politically motivated to enable FCC merger approval which Trump 2.0 FCC was reviewing)
- **October 2025:** Acquired The Free Press (Bari Weiss) for ~$150M
- **November 2025:** Bari Weiss installed as CBS News Editor-in-Chief
- **December 2025 onward:** Multiple high-profile journalist departures from CBS News + 60 Minutes; editorial reorientation documented

## Trump-administration regulatory environment

**Critical context:** The Skydance-Paramount merger required **FCC approval** for transfer of CBS broadcast licenses. **Trump 2.0 FCC** was the approving body. **Brendan Carr** (Trump-appointed FCC Chair) was vocal about merger conditions including news-editorial commitments. **The 60 Minutes settlement is widely understood** to have been a condition of FCC approval that the Ellisons negotiated separately with Trump.
