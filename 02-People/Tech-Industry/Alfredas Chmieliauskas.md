# Alfredas Chmieliauskas

## Summary

Lithuanian data scientist; engineer at [[Palantir Technologies]] who **reportedly worked with [[Cambridge Analytica]]** per testimony to the UK Parliaments [[DCMS Disinformation Inquiry]] (2018). The direct documented personnel bridge between Palantir and CA.

## DCMS testimony documentation

Per [[Source DCMS Final Report 2019]] and DCMS Committee hearing transcripts:

- [[Christopher Wylie]] told DCMS that Chmieliauskas was personally involved in CAs data infrastructure development
- Wylie said Chmieliauskas helped develop "production environment" for CAs psychographic targeting
- Wylie further alleged there had been **informal early discussions between Palantir and CA at company level**

## Palantirs response

Palantir as a company **denied formal partnership** with CA. The company acknowledged Chmieliauskas's involvement was "informal" -- not authorized by Palantir corporate. Palantir position was that Chmieliauskas had moonlighted with CA without company sanction. Critics noted that:

- Chmieliauskas was an active full-time Palantir employee at the time
- The "informal" framing is convenient for both Palantir and CA
- No internal disciplinary action against Chmieliauskas was publicly disclosed
- Chmieliauskas's continued Palantir employment after the disclosure raises questions about how informal it actually was

## Significance

Chmieliauskas is the **single documented personnel link** between Palantir and CA. The link matters because:

- Palantir is a major US defense / intelligence contractor (NSA, CIA, FBI, ICE clients)
- [[Peter Thiel]] (Palantir co-founder) was simultaneously on Facebooks board and a major Trump 2016 donor
- The Palantir methodology overlap with CAs work is structural, not just personnel
- The Palantir-CA-Facebook-Trump cluster represents a multi-vector conflict-of-interest that received less attention than it deserved during the CA scandal
