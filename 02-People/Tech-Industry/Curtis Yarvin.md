# Curtis Yarvin

## Summary

American political philosopher and software engineer. Founder of **Urbit** (decentralized computing project). Under the pseudonym **"Mencius Moldbug"** (2007-2014), wrote extensively on a political philosophy he called **"neoreactionary"** ("NRx") or part of the broader **"Dark Enlightenment"** movement.

**Key intellectual influence on [[Peter Thiel]], [[JD Vance]], and the Trump 2.0 administration ideology.** Yarvin advocates **dismantling democratic governance** in favor of a return to monarchical or corporate-CEO-style governance -- described in slogans like "**RAGE: Retire All Government Employees**" which the 2025 Trump 2.0 DOGE federal-employee firings have operationally implemented.

## The neoreactionary / NRx movement

Core arguments per Yarvin's writing:

1. **Democracy is incompetent governance** -- produces poor outcomes and is captured by what Yarvin calls "**the Cathedral**" (a coordinated mainstream-institutions network including universities, media, NGOs, civil service)
2. **The Cathedral is the real ruling class** -- democratically-elected officials are figureheads
3. **The solution is "monarchy" or "CEO-king" governance** -- concentrated executive power, with the existing democratic civil service dismantled
4. **The federal civil service must be eliminated** -- replaced by political appointees loyal to the executive
5. **The "Cathedral" institutions (universities, media) must be defunded or destroyed**

## Intellectual position and political influence

Curtis Yarvin (born 1973, also written as Mencius Moldbug for his early blog persona) is the principal intellectual architect of what he and his interlocutors variously call "neoreactionary" (NRx), "Dark Enlightenment," or "neocameralist" political theory. The substantive position: modern liberal democracy is structurally unable to produce competent governance because of distributed-decision-making pathologies; explicit hierarchical-corporate models of government (the "neocameralist" frame, modelled loosely on city-states or sovereign-debt-backed corporate governance) would produce better outcomes; the existing democratic system should be replaced rather than reformed.

Yarvin's writing on these themes ran from approximately 2007 through 2014 (the Mencius Moldbug Unqualified Reservations blog), then resumed under his own name from approximately 2020 onward (the Gray Mirror substack, podcast appearances including the Tucker Carlson show, and increasing engagement with the Tech-Right intellectual ecosystem). His specific 2020-2026 visibility has been operationally significant because the Trump 2.0 personnel pool (specifically [[JD Vance]], who has cited Yarvin directly, and the broader Vance-adjacent intellectual circle) has substantially absorbed Yarvin's frame into the operational vocabulary of contemporary Tech-Right politics.

## The Unqualified Reservations corpus and its principal claims

Yarvin's "Unqualified Reservations" blog (writing as Mencius Moldbug, 2007-2014) produced a substantial corpus of long-form essays that articulate the neoreactionary intellectual frame. The principal claims:

**The "Cathedral" thesis.** The contemporary US political-cultural environment is shaped by a distributed-but-coordinated system of academic-credentialing institutions, mainstream-press editorial direction, and federal-bureaucracy professional culture, which Yarvin calls "the Cathedral." The Cathedral is not formally hierarchical (there is no single decision-maker) but produces effectively coordinated outputs because of shared training, shared professional norms, and shared institutional incentives. The Cathedral's actual operational consequences are similar to those of an explicit hierarchical authority while maintaining the formal appearance of distributed pluralism.

**The "Brahmin" caste analysis.** Contemporary US political-cultural elite are functionally a distinct social caste, with characteristic credentials (Ivy League education, advanced degrees from elite institutions), characteristic professional roles (academic faculty, journalism, federal bureaucracy, nonprofit foundation leadership), and characteristic cultural-political alignments that operationally reproduce the caste across generations. Yarvin's framing is adapted from older sociological work on professional-class identity but applied with explicit polemical intent.

**The neocameralist alternative.** The proposed alternative governance form is "neocameralist" -- explicitly hierarchical-corporate governance, modelled loosely on sovereign-debt-backed city-state structures, in which the political authority is treated as a corporate entity owned by sovereign-debt-holders rather than as a representative-democratic entity accountable to citizens. The neocameralist frame is openly hostile to representative-democratic legitimacy as a governance principle.

## The "Dark Enlightenment" adjacencies

The broader neoreactionary intellectual ecosystem ("Dark Enlightenment," after Nick Land's 2012 essay) includes Land's own writing on accelerationism and post-Enlightenment political theory, Nick Bostrom-adjacent transhumanist thinking, parts of the [[Effective Accelerationism]] (e/acc) frame that Marc Andreessen has articulated, and various smaller intellectual nodes. The relationship between these adjacencies is operationally complex: shared opposition to mainstream-progressive cultural politics; substantial disagreement on what specifically should replace the current configuration.

The vault treats the Dark Enlightenment cluster as the principal intellectual-right counter-tradition to the broader mainstream liberal-progressive intellectual consensus.
