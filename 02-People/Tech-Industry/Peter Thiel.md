# Peter Thiel

## Summary

German-born American billionaire investor; **the single most important node in the Tech Right network**. Co-founded **PayPal** (1998) and **[[Palantir Technologies]]** (2003). First major outside investor in Facebook (2004, ~$500K for 10.2% stake); sat on Facebook board from 2005 until **May 2022**. Founded multiple investment vehicles including **[[Founders Fund]]** and **[[Mithril Capital]]**. **First Silicon Valley billionaire to publicly back Trump** in 2016. Patron of **[[JD Vance]]** -- funded Vances Senate campaign with ~$15M and recommended him as Trumps 2024 VP nominee; Vance became **Vice President of the United States** in January 2025.

## Detailed network map and operational footprint

**Founding ventures and exit capital:** Thiel co-founded PayPal in 1998 (sold to eBay 2002, generating the founder-pool wealth and personal relationships that became the [[PayPal Mafia]]), [[Palantir Technologies]] in 2003 (with In-Q-Tel as anchor investor), and [[Founders Fund]] in 2005 (VC vehicle through which most of his subsequent portfolio investments have flowed). He was the first outside investor in Facebook in 2004 ($500,000 for 10.2 percent equity), serving on the Meta board until 2022.

His political-vehicle structures include [[Mithril Capital]] (a separate VC fund focused on later-stage growth, co-founded with Ajay Royan), [[Narya Capital]] (the Cincinnati-based VC vehicle that JD Vance ran before entering politics, Thiel-anchored), and a network of nonprofit and policy-advocacy vehicles including the Thiel Foundation, the [[Hoover Institution]] (board), [[American Enterprise Institute]] (donor), and various smaller think-tank and fellowship structures.

**Documented portfolio overlaps that matter for the vault:** Founders Fund holdings include [[Palantir Technologies]], [[Anduril]] (Palmer Luckey defence-tech), Stripe, SpaceX, and a long list of frontier-tech firms. Mithril Capital portfolio overlaps with the political-influence question via specific investments that the [[Source Fortune 2025-05-21 Thiel Proteges Trump]] reporting catalogued.

## Thiel's intellectual positioning

Thiel's intellectual public-facing positioning ("competition is for losers," monopoly-defence as the right venture-capital posture, openly stated scepticism of democratic accountability for technology development) is documented in his book "Zero to One" (2014, with Blake Masters) and in a longer series of speeches and interviews. The 2016 "What I Believe" address at the Republican National Convention was the first major public alignment with the Trump movement; subsequent appearances and writings have deepened the [[Catholic Integralism]]-adjacent ([[Patrick Deneen]] reading), [[Network States]]-aligned ([[Balaji Srinivasan]] cross-pollination), and [[Effective Accelerationism]]-debating positions that he occupies in the broader intellectual-right landscape.

## Political donations and PAC activity

Thiel was a 2016 Trump campaign donor ($1.25M to the Trump Victory Committee plus smaller direct contributions) and a 2016 RNC speaker. He largely withdrew from direct political giving in 2018-2020, then returned aggressively in 2022 with the Vance Senate PAC commitment and the Masters Senate PAC commitment ($15M each, the two largest single-candidate PAC commitments in 2022 cycle outside the very top tier). His 2024 cycle giving was deliberately less visible than 2022 -- the public reporting suggests he largely stepped back from direct candidate giving while continuing portfolio-company government-relations work.
