# Patrick Deneen

## Summary

American political philosopher; **professor at the University of Notre Dame**. Author of **"Why Liberalism Failed"** (Yale University Press, 2018) â€” an influential critique of contemporary liberal democracy arguing that liberalism's own internal logic has produced its current crises. Author of *Regime Change: Toward a Postliberal Future* (2023). Among the most prominent figures in the **"post-liberal" conservative intellectual movement**, alongside Adrian Vermeule and others. **Cited as an intellectual influence by [[JD Vance]]**.

## Key arguments

- Modern liberalism is exhausting itself
- Atomized individualism and consumerism have eroded community
- Democratic procedures are insufficient without civic virtue
- Return to thicker community, religious tradition, and moderating institutions
- Critical of both progressive and libertarian variants of liberalism

## Connection to Vance / Tech Right

- JD Vance has publicly cited Deneen as influence
- Vance's Catholic conversion (2019) was partly shaped by post-liberal intellectual influences including Deneen
- Deneen's framework provides **conservative-intellectual justification** for departures from procedural-liberal norms
- Adjacent to but distinct from [[Curtis Yarvin]]'s more radical neoreactionary position

## Why he matters for this vault

Deneen represents the **respectable Catholic-intellectual face** of the broader anti-liberal-democratic intellectual movement that has converged around Trump 2.0. Where Yarvin provides the radical version (dismantle democracy), Deneen provides the more measured version (community/religious tradition over procedural liberalism). Both contribute to the **intellectual permission structure** for the Trump 2.0 administration's institutional restructuring.
