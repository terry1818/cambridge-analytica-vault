## Summary

Kate Starbird is a Professor in the Department of Human Centered Design & Engineering (HCDE) at the University of Washington and a co-founder of the [[Center for an Informed Public]] (CIP). She is internationally recognized for her research on how rumors, misinformation, and disinformation spread through online networks — particularly during crises, disasters, and elections. Her work sits at the intersection of crisis informatics, human-computer interaction (HCI), and computer-supported cooperative work (CSCW). Starbird and the CIP became subjects of congressional scrutiny during the [[Congressional campaign against disinformation research]] (2023–2024), targeted alongside the [[Stanford Internet Observatory]] for their role in the Election Integrity Partnership.

## Key facts

- Her research community is CSCW (Computer-Supported Cooperative Work and Social Computing); she holds nearly 90 peer-reviewed publications and numerous best paper awards in HCI and CSCW.
- Co-founded the [[Center for an Informed Public]] in 2019 with colleagues Ryan Calo, Chris Coward, Emma Spiro, and Jevin West, funded by a $5 million Knight Foundation grant and a $600,000 Hewlett Foundation award.
- CIP is a cross-campus initiative spanning the Information School, HCDE, School of Law, and Communication Leadership Program.
- Participated in the Election Integrity Partnership (EIP) — a cross-institutional collaboration monitoring election-related misinformation in the 2020 and 2022 elections — which became a focal point of Republican congressional criticism.
- Named an ACM Fellow in January 2026 for advancing research on misinformation and information ecosystems.
- Her work demonstrates how "participatory disinformation" — where ordinary users amplify false content believing it to be true — differs structurally from centrally-coordinated state campaigns.
- Has been described in press as a "rumor researcher" for her real-time tracking of misinformation during breaking news events.
- The Seattle Times reported in 2024 that she had been subject to harassment and pressure campaigns as a result of congressional targeting.
