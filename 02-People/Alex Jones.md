# Alex Jones

## Summary

Alexander Emerick Jones (born 1974) is an American radio host and conspiracy-media entrepreneur who built [[Infowars]] into the most commercially successful conspiracy-theory operation in the United States. Beginning on Austin, Texas public-access television and local radio in the mid-1990s, Jones developed a syndicated program built on the premise of a globalist plot — variously the "New World Order," a depopulation agenda, or government false-flag operations — against ordinary Americans. He monetized his audience principally through Infowars Life, a dietary-supplement and survival-goods store, making conspiracy content a customer-acquisition funnel for product sales.

Jones matters to this vault as a foundational figure in the modern disinformation ecosystem. He pioneered the fusion of conspiracy entertainment, audience monetization, and political mobilization that the post-2016 information environment industrialized. He claimed personal credit for helping elect [[Donald Trump]], hosted Trump on his program in December 2015, and served as a visible bridge between fringe conspiracy media and the Republican mainstream. His promotion of the false claim that the 2012 [[Sandy Hook]] Elementary School shooting was a staged "hoax" involving "crisis actors" produced sustained harassment of bereaved families and ultimately roughly $1.4–1.5 billion in defamation liability — one of the largest such judgments in U.S. history.

## Key facts

- **Role(s):** Radio/video broadcaster; founder and host of [[Infowars]]; owner of Free Speech Systems LLC.
- **Affiliations:** [[Infowars]], Free Speech Systems LLC, PrisonPlanet.com.
- **Active period:** ~1996–present.
- **Known for:** 9/11 "inside job" advocacy; "Pizzagate" amplification; Sandy Hook hoax claims; deplatforming in 2018; the Sandy Hook defamation judgments.

## Relationships

- **Co-conspirators / collaborators:** [[Roger Stone]] — longtime ally and frequent Infowars guest; [[Steve Bannon]] — adjacent figure in Trump-era right media.
- **Promoted / amplified:** [[Donald Trump]] — Jones gave Trump a friendly platform in 2015–2016 and claimed credit for his win.
- **Opponents / accusers:** Sandy Hook families (Neil Heslin, Scarlett Lewis, the Lafferty family and others) — plaintiffs in the defamation suits.
- **Platform context:** Banned by major platforms in 2018, a watershed "deplatforming" event in [[content moderation]] history.

## Public statements

- "It's as phony as a $3 bill ... the whole thing was fake." — Jones on the Sandy Hook shooting, 2014–2015 broadcasts (later the basis of the defamation suits). `#claim/false`
- "Your reputation is amazing. I will not let you down." — Donald Trump to Jones, Alex Jones Show, December 2, 2015.
- Jones later conceded under oath in 2022 that the Sandy Hook shooting was "100% real," after years of claiming otherwise.
