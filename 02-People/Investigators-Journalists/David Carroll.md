# David Carroll

## Summary

Associate professor of media design and director of the MFA Design and Technology graduate program at the **Parsons School of Art, Media and Technology at The New School**. **In January 2017 filed a Data Subject Access Request (DSAR)** with Cambridge Analytica seeking his personal data. The response he received was analyzed and found to contain unlawful elements per UK Data Protection Act. **In March 2018 filed a legal claim in UK High Court** against [[Cambridge Analytica]]. Central protagonist of **[[Source The Great Hack Netflix 2019|The Great Hack]]** alongside [[Brittany Kaiser]]. **Worked with [[Paul-Olivier Dehaye]]** (Swiss mathematician) on the data-rights legal strategy. In September 2020 Krishnan Guru-Murthy (Channel 4 News UK) finally presented Carroll with his complete personal data file as part of a US voter-dissuasion-tactics investigation.

## Why his case matters

Carroll's case **established the legal viability of using EU/UK GDPR-era data-rights law to surface psychographic-targeting evidence from political consultancies.** CA's failure to comply fully with the DSAR became part of the broader ICO investigation and DCMS hearings. Carroll's persistence demonstrated that **individual data-subject claims can be operational counter-weapons against political-consultancy data abuse.**
