## Role as the principal whistleblower

[[Christopher Wylie]] is the former [[SCL Group]] and [[Cambridge Analytica]] research director whose March 2018 disclosures to The Observer, the New York Times, and Channel 4 News produced the public CA reckoning. He worked at SCL Canada (which the vault treats as functionally [[AggregateIQ]]) and at SCL/CA in London during 2013-2014, was involved in the original [[Aleksandr Kogan]] / GSR / [[Facebook Graph API v1]] data-harvesting arrangement, and left in late 2014.

His decision to whistleblow developed over several years in cooperation with [[Carole Cadwalladr]]; the coordinated 2018 publication strategy (Observer, NYT, Channel 4 simultaneously) was designed to make the story too large for any single outlet to bury or for any single institution to absorb privately.

## Distinctive contribution to the evidentiary record

Wylie's value as a source is technical: he understood the data side (what was actually collected, how, from whom, and what was done with it) at a level no other contemporaneous source matched. His testimony documented the original CA pitch deck, the GSR contract, the data-flow diagrams, and the specific psychographic methodology in detail that made the rest of the journalistic reconstruction possible.

Where his testimony has been contested (by Nix, by some former CA staff, by some commentators), the points of contention are mostly about emphasis and framing rather than about underlying facts. The documentary evidence Wylie supplied (emails, contracts, internal slides) has not been substantively undermined.

## Author of "Mindf*ck"

Wylie's 2019 book "Mindf*ck: Cambridge Analytica and the Plot to Break America" is the most comprehensive single source for the inside-CA narrative. The vault treats specific factual claims in the book as Tier-2 (testimonial, not court-adjudicated) and uses it as a reference for relationship maps, organisational structure, and methodology that the regulatory record corroborates.

## Post-2018 activity

After the CA disclosures, Wylie worked as research director at H&M (the fashion retailer) on data-driven sustainability and trend forecasting, then moved into independent consulting and public speaking. He has continued to comment on subsequent information-warfare events (the [[Doppelganger]] Russian operation, the 2024 election cycle, the AI-era successor playbook) and remains a relevant voice in the broader debate, though not in the day-to-day reporting sense Cadwalladr is.

## Detailed pre-whistleblower career at SCL/CA

Wylie joined SCL Group in 2013 at age 23, recruited principally for his statistical-modelling background (he had done political-targeting work for the Canadian Liberal Party as a teenager and held a Wikipedia-listed academic-adjacent reputation for political-data sophistication beyond his years). His specific role at SCL was research director, with technical responsibility for what became the OCEAN-Facebook data-modelling pipeline.

The chronology of his involvement with the [[Aleksandr Kogan]] / GSR / [[Facebook Graph API v1]] data harvest is reconstructible from his subsequent testimony, the contracts the [[ICO Investigation]] obtained, and Kogan's parallel account. The contract between SCL/CA and GSR was signed in mid-2014 for the data-licensing arrangement. Wylie was the SCL/CA-side technical lead on the data integration through 2014.

He left SCL/CA in late 2014, citing a combination of ethical concerns about the deployment direction the firm was taking, dissatisfaction with management decisions around the Mercer capitalisation, and personal-and-professional reasons that he has elaborated in subsequent media. He moved to Eunoia Technologies (a short-lived political-consulting venture that he founded with two other SCL/CA alumni) and then to various other ventures before the 2017-2018 whistleblowing window.

## The 2017-2018 disclosure sequence

The disclosure was a multi-year coordinated build with [[Carole Cadwalladr]]. Cadwalladr first approached Wylie in 2017 through prior reporting connections. The two spent more than a year preparing the disclosure: assembling the documentary evidence (emails, contracts, slide decks, data-flow diagrams), coordinating with the [[ICO Investigation]] (Wylie became a formal whistleblower with UK regulatory protection), coordinating with the [[DCMS Disinformation Inquiry]] (Wylie testified at length), and coordinating with the publication partners (Observer, New York Times, and Channel 4 News for simultaneous publication).

The simultaneous publication strategy was deliberate: a single-outlet exclusive could be absorbed by Facebook's PR machine; a coordinated multi-outlet drop across the UK and US press could not. The strategy worked. The CA story dominated international news for weeks following the March 17-19, 2018 publication cycle and produced the regulatory and political reckoning that followed.

## Specific contribution to the evidentiary record

Wylie's contribution to the evidentiary record is technical and documentary in ways no other contemporaneous source matched. He supplied:

- The original SCL-GSR data-licensing contract (or the relevant excerpts).
- Internal CA pitch decks and slide materials that documented the firm's representations to prospective clients.
- Email correspondence between SCL/CA leadership and Mercer-side principals during the 2013-2014 capitalisation period.
- Data-flow diagrams that documented how Facebook data moved from the Kogan/GSR harvest into SCL/CA's modelling infrastructure.
- His own first-person account of meetings, decisions, and operational practices that the firm's leadership has not contested in core particulars.

Where his testimony has been challenged (by Nix in his post-2018 interviews, by some former CA staff, by some commentators), the points of contention are typically about emphasis, intent, and characterisation rather than about the underlying facts the documentary record supports.

## "Mindf*ck" (2019) and continuing commentary

Wylie's 2019 book is the most comprehensive single source for the inside-CA narrative from the data-side perspective. The vault treats specific factual claims in the book as Tier-2 (testimonial, not court-adjudicated) and uses it as a reference for relationship maps, organisational structure, and methodology that the regulatory record corroborates.

Post-book, Wylie spent a period working as research director at H&M on data-driven sustainability and trend forecasting (a deliberate pivot away from political work). He has since returned to independent consulting and public commentary, including continuing analysis of the [[Doppelganger]] Russian operation, the [[Generative Propaganda]] / AI-era successor playbook, and the 2024 election cycle's information environment. His commentary on AI-era influence operations carries weight because of his unique pre-2018 vantage point, even though the specific 2014-era CA methodology he documented has been superseded.

## Why the vault treats Wylie as a top connector

Wylie is the source-side node that made the entire CA reckoning possible. Without his disclosure, the public-record case against CA would have been substantially thinner; the [[ICO Investigation]] would have proceeded slower with fewer documents; the [[FTC v Facebook 2019]] settlement might have taken a different shape; and the broader public awareness of the data-side architecture of contemporary political operations would not have developed in the form it did.

He is also the principal living technical source on what SCL/CA's data operation actually was, at a level of detail that none of the regulatory documents or journalistic accounts can match without his input.
