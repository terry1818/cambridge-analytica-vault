# Brittany Kaiser

## Summary

American whistleblower and former Director of Business Development at [[Cambridge Analytica]]. Came forward in March 2018 contradicting CA's public denials about work for [[Leave.EU]]: "In my opinion, I was lying." Released the **[[HindsightFiles]]** on January 4, 2020 -- over 100,000 documents showing CA work in 68 countries. Author of [[Source Targeted (Kaiser 2019)|*Targeted*]] (2019). Founded the **Own Your Data Foundation** and coined **#OwnYourData**. Featured in [[Source The Great Hack (2019)|The Great Hack]] documentary. ([[Source Wikipedia - Cambridge Analytica]])

## Role at Cambridge Analytica

[[Brittany Kaiser]] joined SCL/CA in 2015 as a business development director and rose to be a public face of the firm, conducting client pitches and representing CA at high-profile speaking engagements (TED, World Economic Forum adjacent events). She left in early 2018 and emerged as a second-tier whistleblower behind [[Christopher Wylie]], cooperating with [[Carole Cadwalladr]]'s reporting and the [[DCMS Disinformation Inquiry]] in the UK.

Her cooperation produced two distinct evidentiary streams: documentary (internal emails, contracts, and pitch decks that corroborated or expanded the Wylie disclosures) and testimonial (sworn appearances before the DCMS committee and the [[ICO Investigation]]). She is the co-founder of the Own Your Data Foundation and authored "Targeted: My Inside Story of Cambridge Analytica and How Trump, Brexit and Facebook Broke Democracy" (2019).

## Distinctive contribution to the vault

Where Wylie was the data-side technical insider, Kaiser was the commercial-side relationship insider. Her testimony documents the client list (which campaigns, which donors, what was sold) more comprehensively than Wylie's. Her account of the [[Leave.EU]] / [[Arron Banks]] / Brexit thread, in particular, supplied evidence the ICO eventually relied on.

She also surfaced detail on CA's international election work in [[Nigeria 2007 2015 Elections]], [[Kenya 2013 2017 Elections]], and elsewhere that Wylie had less direct exposure to. Kaiser's narrative on the international work is preserved in the [[HindsightFiles Release 2020]] document leak.

## Caveats and source-criticism notes

Kaiser's account has been contested on several specific points by other former CA staff and by some of the journalists who interviewed her. The vault treats her testimony as Tier-2 evidence: corroborated by documents where the documents exist, treated as a lead-not-finding where they do not. Some commentary has argued that Kaiser's late-stage cooperation was partly motivated by personal reputational considerations after CA's collapse. That commentary is itself reported, but it does not invalidate the documentary record she produced.

## Detailed career inside SCL/CA

Kaiser joined SCL Group in February 2015 as business development director, after a career path that included Obama-campaign volunteer work (2008), human rights research at Amnesty International, and an MA in Human Rights at Birkbeck University of London. Her recruitment into SCL was facilitated by [[Alexander Nix]]'s relationship network; her function at the firm was principally commercial -- closing political-consulting deals on behalf of CA across North America, Africa, and the Caribbean.

Her client-facing role gave her operational visibility across the [[Cambridge Analytica]] caseload that the more technical staff did not have. Through 2015-2017 she pitched and helped close engagements with [[Leave.EU]] / [[Arron Banks]], with the [[Ted Cruz Campaign]] (in the early phase before the Mercer-side handoff to direct CA management), with the Nigerian APC presidential operation, with multiple US Senate and gubernatorial campaigns, and with commercial-political consulting clients in the Caribbean and parts of Africa.

The substantive commercial relationship between CA and Leave.EU (which became a regulatory question after 2018) was substantially handled through Kaiser. Her contemporaneous emails, internal memos, and presentation decks formed the documentary backbone of the [[ICO Investigation]]'s reconstruction of the CA-Leave.EU relationship.

## The whistleblowing arc

Kaiser left CA in January 2018, before the March 2018 public reckoning. Her exit was reportedly tied to internal disputes about post-Trump-election strategic direction, the failed Leave.EU contract conversion, and a personal reassessment about the work the firm was doing. Her public emergence as a whistleblower followed [[Christopher Wylie]]'s March 2018 disclosure by several weeks; her cooperation was coordinated with [[Carole Cadwalladr]] and with the [[DCMS Disinformation Inquiry]] committee.

Her testimony to DCMS (April 2018, June 2018, and follow-up sessions) was the most extended on-record account from any former CA principal. Where Wylie's testimony was technically detailed but operationally narrow (the data-and-modelling layer), Kaiser's testimony was commercially broad (the full client and pitch-and-deployment picture). The two were complementary and the DCMS final report drew on both extensively.

## The "Targeted" memoir (2019)

Kaiser's book "Targeted: My Inside Story of Cambridge Analytica and How Trump, Brexit and Facebook Broke Democracy" was published in October 2019 by HarperCollins. It is the second principal book-length insider account of CA after Wylie's "Mindf*ck"; the two cover overlapping ground from different vantage points. Specific Kaiser contributions: detailed account of the Leave.EU pitch-and-deployment relationship; the Nigeria 2015 deployment from the commercial-relationship side; the post-Election-Day 2016 internal dynamics at CA; the Mercer-Bannon-Nix executive triangle as experienced from the BD-side staff role.

The book has been challenged on several specific points by former colleagues; the vault treats specific factual claims as Tier-2 (corroborated by document where the documents exist; treated as lead-not-finding where they do not).

## The HindsightFiles document leak (2020)

Kaiser was the principal source for the [[HindsightFiles Release 2020]] document leak, in which approximately 100,000 internal CA documents were released publicly through a Twitter-led campaign. The documents principally addressed CA's international deployments (Nigeria, Kenya, Trinidad, Malaysia, Brazil, US internal documents, Mexico PRI) and provided substantial additional documentary evidence for vault entries on those countries.

The HindsightFiles release was operationally significant because it broke the post-2018 information embargo. Most CA internal documents had remained subject to regulatory and litigation confidentiality; the HindsightFiles release publicly disclosed documents that the public-record reconstruction would not otherwise have access to.

## Own Your Data Foundation and continuing work

Post-2019, Kaiser co-founded the Own Your Data Foundation, a non-profit focused on data-rights education and individual data-sovereignty advocacy. She has continued speaking and consulting on data-and-political-influence topics through 2024-2026, including commentary on the AI-era successor playbook, the [[Doppelganger]] Russian operation, and the [[Tech Right]] / [[DOGE]] federal data-aggregation programme.

Her current-day commentary is fragmented across many platforms (podcasts, individual articles, conference appearances) and is more difficult to track than Wylie's similarly fragmented output. The vault treats her continuing analysis as Tier-2 commentary informed by relevant insider experience.

## Why the vault treats Kaiser as a top connector

Kaiser is the second principal whistleblower-tier source for the CA story, and she is the principal source for the commercial-relationship-and-international-deployment side that Wylie's data-and-modelling-focused account did not cover in depth. Without Kaiser, the public-record picture of CA's commercial pitch process, client list, and international caseload would be substantially thinner.

She is also the principal living source on the post-Trump-election internal dynamics at CA (the brief period when CA was at peak commercial visibility before the March 2018 collapse), and her account of that period is largely uncontested in its operational outline.
