# Chris Jankowski

## Summary

Chris Jankowski is the Republican political operative who designed and executed [[REDMAP]], the Republican Redistricting Majority Project, while serving as executive director of the [[Republican State Leadership Committee]] in 2009–2010. His insight — that targeted spending on state legislative races immediately before the decennial Census redistricting round would yield structural Congressional advantages lasting a full decade — is widely regarded as one of the most consequential pieces of electoral strategy in modern American political history. David Daley's 2016 book *Ratf**ked* is built largely around interviews with Jankowski and presents him as REDMAP's principal architect.

## Key Facts

- Holds a J.D. from the University of South Carolina School of Law and a B.A. from the College of Charleston.
- Read a New York Times story in summer 2009 about Republican opportunities in 2010 governors' races and immediately recognized the redistricting dimension.
- Partnered with RSLC chairman [[Ed Gillespie]] to reorient the RSLC from a low-profile state fundraising vehicle into a $30-million precision operation.
- Directed $30 million in spending to 107 state legislative races in 16 states for the 2010 cycle.
- After 2010, oversaw the RSLC as president through the post-Census map-drawing phase.
- Was described by Rachel Maddow on MSNBC as a "genius" — a characterization Jankowski accepted without protest.
- Returned to Richmond, Virginia around 2014 to run a private political consulting firm.

## Timeline

- **2009:** Joins RSLC as executive director; develops REDMAP concept with Gillespie.
- **2010:** Directs REDMAP's $30 million spending; Republicans gain ~680 state legislative seats and control of 20 additional legislative chambers.
- **2011–2012:** Oversees RSLC through post-Census redistricting; Republican-controlled chambers draw favorable maps with [[Thomas Hofeller]] as primary mapmaker.
- **2012:** Republican candidates collectively receive ~1.4 million fewer Congressional votes than Democrats but win a 33-seat House majority — the clearest demonstration of REDMAP's structural effect.
- **2013–2014:** Serves as RSLC president.
- **2014:** Returns to private consulting in Richmond, Virginia.

## Relationships

- **[[Ed Gillespie]]:** RSLC chairman and Jankowski's senior partner in conceiving REDMAP. Gillespie provided donor credibility and national Republican network; Jankowski provided the operational intelligence and targeting.
- **[[Thomas Hofeller]]:** The mapmaker who executed the redistricting advantage REDMAP created. Hofeller and Jankowski/RSLC operated in sequence — RSLC won the legislatures; Hofeller drew the maps.
- **David Daley:** Author of *Ratf**ked*; Jankowski cooperated extensively with Daley's research.

## Public Statements

Jankowski granted extensive interviews to David Daley for *Ratf**ked* and described REDMAP's strategy candidly. He has also participated in public debates on redistricting. He has defended REDMAP as legitimate electoral strategy rather than manipulation, arguing Democrats had ample opportunity to pursue the same approach and declined to.
