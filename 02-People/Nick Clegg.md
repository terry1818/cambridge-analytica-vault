## Summary

Nick Clegg was one of the UK's most prominent centrist politicians before joining [[Facebook]] in October 2018 as Vice President of Global Affairs and Communications. His recruitment was widely understood as a strategic move by [[Mark Zuckerberg]] to acquire political credibility and regulatory navigation capacity in Europe, where GDPR enforcement and EU digital regulation were creating significant compliance pressure. Clegg had been knighted (2022) and retained extensive networks in Brussels and European capitals. He was promoted to President of Global Affairs in 2022. For six years, he was the primary external face of Meta's content-moderation and platform-governance communications — defending the company before parliamentary committees, managing the [[Facebook Oversight Board]], and articulating Meta's policies during the Trump account suspension controversy. In January 2025, Clegg announced his departure as Meta repositioned toward the incoming Trump administration, with [[Joel Kaplan]] named as his successor.

## Key Facts

- Cambridge and College of Europe educated; worked in European Commission before entering UK politics
- Led the Liberal Democrats from 2007 and oversaw the party's coalition with the Conservative Party under David Cameron (2010–2015)
- The coalition government's implementation of austerity policies devastated the Liberal Democrats electorally; Clegg lost his own Sheffield Hallam seat in the 2017 general election
- Recruited by Zuckerberg in 2018 at a reported salary of approximately $3–4 million per year (per various media estimates)
- Became the first VP-level hire at Facebook with a background as a head of government
- Managed Facebook's testimony and policy submissions to the EU, UK Parliament, and US Congress
- Oversaw the creation and launch of the [[Facebook Oversight Board]] in 2020
- Became President of Global Affairs in 2022 after Sheryl Sandberg's announcement of departure
- Knighted in 2022 for political and public service
- Departed Meta in early 2025; replacement by Kaplan was announced January 2, 2025, widely read as Meta's alignment with Trump's second administration

## Public Statements

Clegg gave numerous public speeches and op-eds defending Facebook's approach to content moderation, including a notable 2020 op-ed arguing against treating political speech the same as misinformation. He testified before various parliamentary bodies and defended the Oversight Board as a model of platform self-governance.
