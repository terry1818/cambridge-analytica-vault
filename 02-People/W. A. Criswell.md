# W. A. Criswell

## Summary

Wallie Amos Criswell (1909–2002) was an American Southern Baptist pastor who, for fifty years, led First Baptist Church of Dallas — during his tenure the largest church in the Southern Baptist Convention. Succeeding the famous moderate George W. Truett in 1944, Criswell grew the congregation from roughly 7,800 members to some 26,000, expanding it across five downtown Dallas city blocks. He was twice elected president of the [[Southern Baptist Convention]] (1968–1970) and authored numerous books, most influentially *Why I Preach That the Bible Is Literally True* (1969).

Criswell appears in this vault as the doctrinal patriarch of the [[Southern Baptist conservative resurgence]] — the organized takeover that, beginning at the 1979 SBC annual meeting, moved the nation's largest Protestant denomination decisively rightward on theology and, in time, politics. The two principal strategists of that takeover, [[Paige Patterson]] and [[Paul Pressler]], operated under Criswell's influence and patronage; Criswell himself "worked effectively behind the scenes but seldom in public view." His insistence on strict biblical inerrancy supplied the theological test that the resurgence used to purge moderates from seminaries and agencies. One recent biography summarized him as "Truett on the outside and [the militant fundamentalist J. Frank] Norris on the inside." His career is a key upstream node for the SBC's later alignment with [[Christian nationalism]] and the [[religious right]].

## Key facts

- **Role(s):** Senior pastor, First Baptist Church of Dallas (1944–1995); two-term SBC president (1968–1970); biblical expositor and author.
- **Affiliations:** [[First Baptist Church of Dallas]], [[Southern Baptist Convention]], Criswell College (which bears his name).
- **Active period:** 1944–1995 (Dallas pastorate); influence on the resurgence c. 1969–1990.
- **Known for:** Biblical-inerrancy preaching; mentoring the architects of the SBC conservative resurgence.

## Relationships

- **George W. Truett** — his celebrated predecessor at First Baptist Dallas, a denominational moderate; Criswell represented a sharp doctrinal turn from Truett.
- **[[Paige Patterson]]** — co-architect of the conservative resurgence; mentored within Criswell's orbit; later led Southern Baptist seminaries.
- **[[Paul Pressler]]** — Texas judge and co-strategist of the resurgence; allied with Patterson under Criswell's influence.
- **J. Frank Norris** — earlier Texas fundamentalist firebrand; a recent biography argues Criswell was temperamentally "Norris on the inside."
- **[[Billy Graham]]** — joined First Baptist Dallas as a member during Criswell's pastorate.
- **[[Southern Baptist Convention]]** — the denomination he led and reshaped doctrinally.

## Public statements

- *Why I Preach That the Bible Is Literally True* (1969): articulated the strict-inerrancy position that became the doctrinal litmus test of the conservative resurgence.
- 1956: delivered remarks opposing racial integration — a stance he later distanced himself from; the episode is cited as evidence of the entanglement of mid-century SBC conservatism with segregation. `#claim/disputed` (extent of later repudiation is debated)
