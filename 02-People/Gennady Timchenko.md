## Summary

Gennady Timchenko is a Russian-Finnish billionaire whose fortune was built through oil trading in the post-Soviet period. He co-founded [[Gunvor Group]] in 2000 (formalizing a partnership begun in 1997) with Swedish trader Torbjörn Törnqvist; the company became one of the world's largest commodity traders, handling a significant share of Russian oil exports, before Timchenko divested his stake the day before US sanctions were imposed in March 2014. US diplomatic cables published by WikiLeaks in 2010 called Gunvor "Putin's wallet." Timchenko denies any special relationship with the Kremlin beyond ordinary business dealings, but he has been a close associate of [[Vladimir Putin]] since the early 1990s.

## Key Facts

- Born 1952; studied at the Leningrad Mechanical Institute.
- In 1991, Putin — then head of the St. Petersburg Committee for External Relations — granted Timchenko an oil export license. This is the earliest documented official interaction between the two men.
- Co-founded Gunvor with Törnqvist in 1997/2000; the company's Geneva operations began in 2003.
- By 2007 Gunvor had a turnover of $43 billion; by 2010, $65 billion.
- US WikiLeaks cables (2010) alleged Putin had a hidden ownership stake in Gunvor. Neither claim has been proven in court; Gunvor denies it. Evidence tier: T3 (allegation in diplomatic communication, not adjudicated).
- Timchenko sold his 43.5% Gunvor stake to Törnqvist on 19 March 2014 — one day before his US sanctions designation. The timing is widely noted by investigators; the sale is a matter of public record.
- Founder of Volga Group, a private investment holding company with stakes in energy, transport, and infrastructure, including a major position in Novatek (Russia's second-largest gas producer).
- Also associated with Stroytransgaz and Transoil.

## Relationships

- **[[Vladimir Putin]]**: Close associate since early 1990s; nature and depth of personal or financial ties disputed.
- **[[Gunvor Group]]**: Co-founded and co-owned 2000–2014.
- **[[Ozero Cooperative]]**: Not a formal member; Belton and others place him in the adjacent Ozero-era network. Timchenko's relationship to the cooperative is contextual rather than documented membership.
- **Torbjörn Törnqvist**: Swedish co-founder and, after 2014, sole principal of Gunvor.
- **Novatek**: Major shareholder; Novatek is also associated with Leonid Mikhelson.

## Public Statements

Timchenko has consistently denied that his business success derives from political favoritism. In a 2014 interview following sanctions, he characterized the designations as politically motivated and denied any hidden stake by Putin in Gunvor.
