Lesson 7: The Legal Network - Leonard Leo and the Federalist Society

This is the single most important institutional story in the book. Read it twice if you need to.

In 1982, a group of conservative law students at Yale and the University of Chicago founded a club called the Federalist Society. Their idea was simple: train conservative lawyers, get them into clerkships and firms, and eventually put them on the bench.

For 40 years, this is exactly what happened. The Federalist Society became the most effective political pipeline in modern American history.

The man at the center of it is Leonard Leo.

Leo is a lawyer who has personally vetted and recommended every Republican-appointed Supreme Court nominee since 2005. He handed Trump the list that produced Gorsuch, Kavanaugh, and Barrett. Today, all six Republican-appointed Supreme Court justices are Federalist Society members. So are roughly two-thirds of federal appeals court judges.

But the Federalist Society is only part of Leo's operation. Around it, he has built a network of nonprofits, foundations, and "dark money" groups that fund:

  • Judicial confirmation campaigns (millions in TV ads when a justice is nominated).
  • State Attorney General races.
  • State Supreme Court elections.
  • Legal advocacy groups that file the lawsuits the justices then rule on.
  • University law school chairs.
  • Think tanks and policy shops.

The whole thing is one coordinated machine. The lawyers who file the lawsuit, the judges who hear it, the academics who write the supporting articles, and the donors who fund all of them - many of them know each other personally, attend the same conferences, and belong to the same network.

The 1.6 billion dollar donation. In 2021, an elderly Chicago manufacturer named Barre Seid gave 1.6 billion dollars - in a single transaction - to a new political group called the Marble Freedom Trust. The Marble Freedom Trust is controlled by Leonard Leo. That is the largest known political donation in American history. It was reported a year later by ProPublica and the New York Times.

If you do not understand how this works, the news will keep surprising you. If you do understand it, things make more sense. The same network is behind the abortion rulings, the gun rulings, the EPA rulings, the immunity ruling, and the next ten years of rulings.
