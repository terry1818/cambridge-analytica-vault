Lesson 10: Schedule F - Replacing the Civil Service

Most of the U.S. government is run by career employees - civil servants. They are scientists, lawyers, accountants, analysts, doctors, regulators. They are not political appointees. They keep their jobs when administrations change.

That has been the rule since 1883, when Congress passed the Pendleton Civil Service Act. Before Pendleton, every president fired thousands of workers and replaced them with loyalists - it was called the "spoils system." Pendleton ended that, because it was producing rampant corruption and incompetence.

Schedule F is a way to undo Pendleton without repealing it.

Trump signed Executive Order 13957 in October 2020, near the end of his first term. It created a new category of federal employee - "Schedule F" - and reclassified anyone in "policy-related" positions into it. Schedule F employees can be fired at will, with no civil service protection.

Biden rescinded Schedule F in January 2021.

Trump re-signed it on his first day back in office in January 2025. The Office of Personnel Management is now reclassifying tens of thousands of positions. Estimates of how many workers could be fired range from 50,000 (Project 2025's number) to over 200,000 (analysis by federal employee unions).

The targets are usually:

  • Scientists at the EPA, NOAA, FDA, CDC, USDA.
  • Lawyers at the Department of Justice.
  • Analysts at intelligence agencies.
  • Inspectors general (the internal watchdogs of every agency).

The people fired will be replaced with political loyalists - many already vetted through Heritage's "Presidential Personnel Database," a parallel list of pre-screened candidates Project 2025 spent two years assembling.

In Trump's first term, career civil servants were often the thing that slowed or stopped the most extreme orders. Schedule F is designed to remove that brake. That is the entire point of it.
