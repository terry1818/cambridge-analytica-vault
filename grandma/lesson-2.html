Lesson 2: The Big Picture - The Three Big Ideas

This whole book is built on three big ideas. If you remember nothing else, remember these three:

Idea One - The What. A small group of very wealthy people has spent the last 40 years quietly building the machinery to take control of America's courts, government, and Constitution - instead of trying to win elections the normal way.

Idea Two - The Why. They are doing this because their voters are a shrinking minority of the country. They know they cannot win the popular vote consistently. So they have built ways to win without it: the Electoral College, the Senate filibuster, gerrymandering, voter ID laws, and the courts.

Idea Three - Where It Leads. The thinkers and writers at the center of this movement have openly said they want to replace American constitutional democracy with something else. They have names for it: "post-liberal," "national conservatism," "the new right." They publish books about it. They give speeches about it. The current Vice President has cited their books by name.

That is the whole argument of this book, in three paragraphs.

The next eighteen lessons walk you through the evidence - who, how, and where. The very last group of lessons comes back to Idea Three in plain detail, because it is the strangest and hardest to believe.

This is not a conspiracy theory. The people involved have written books about what they want to do. They give speeches. They run organizations. They are doing it in public. They are just doing it slowly enough that most people do not notice.
