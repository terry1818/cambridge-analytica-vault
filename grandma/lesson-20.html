Lesson 20: What You Can Do

You will not personally stop any of this. But you can be one of the people who refuses to let it happen quietly. That matters more than you think.

1. Vote in every single election.

Not just presidential. Primaries. Runoffs. Midterms. School board. Judges. Especially state legislatures. Most of what is in this book was built at the state level, where 15 percent of voters show up. Your vote in a state legislative primary counts roughly 20 to 50 times more than your vote for president.

2. Know your state legislators by name.

They vote on Convention of States. They vote on voter ID laws. They draw your congressional districts. Look them up at openstates.org - type in your address. Save their phone numbers. Call them. State legislators almost never get calls. Five calls about one bill is a lot to them. Twenty calls is overwhelming.

3. Subscribe to a local newspaper.

Local journalism is dying, and that is not an accident - the same donor network has been funding the gutting of local news for 20 years. Local papers are the early-warning system for everything in this book. If your town still has a paper, subscribe. It is the most underrated political act available to you.

4. Talk to people in real life.

Not on Facebook. Not in comment sections. In person, on the phone, at dinner. Your grandchildren. Your neighbors. Your church. People you actually know change their minds - strangers on the internet do not.

5. Look up who your local officials are.

Your mayor, your county commissioner, your sheriff, your district attorney, your school board members. These offices have enormous power and tiny electorates. A school board race can be decided by 50 votes.

6. Watch state Supreme Court races.

In most states, state Supreme Court justices are elected. They decide gerrymandering cases, voting cases, and major state laws. The Leonard Leo network has been quietly investing tens of millions in these races. Most voters do not know who is running.

7. Do not panic, but do not tune out.

Both reactions help the people who want you to stop paying attention. The goal of much of what is in this book is to make you so tired, so scared, or so cynical that you give up. Do not give them that.

8. Share this guide.

This whole book exists to be passed around. Print it. Email it. Read it aloud to someone. Disagree with it. Tear it apart. The point is to start the conversation. Almost nobody you know has heard most of what is in here.

You made it. Thank you for reading. Now go call your grandchildren.
