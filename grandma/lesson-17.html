Lesson 17: The Epstein Network - Elite Protection

This lesson is shorter and uglier than the others. It is in this book because it shows how elite networks across politics and finance protect each other - which is part of why the rest of this book happens.

Jeffrey Epstein. A financier who, from at least the late 1990s, ran a sex trafficking operation that targeted underage girls. The girls were brought to his Manhattan townhouse, his Palm Beach mansion, his Caribbean island ("Little St. James"), and other properties.

The 2007-2008 non-prosecution agreement. Federal prosecutors in Florida (led by U.S. Attorney Alex Acosta) had Epstein on multiple federal sex trafficking charges. They cut a secret deal: Epstein pleaded guilty to two minor state charges of soliciting prostitution and served 13 months in a county jail, with work-release. The federal charges were dropped. The victims were not told. Acosta later became Donald Trump's Secretary of Labor.

The 2019 arrest. New federal charges were brought against Epstein in July 2019, largely because of investigative journalism by the Miami Herald (reporter Julie K. Brown). He was arrested. He died in his jail cell on August 10, 2019. The official cause was suicide. The two guards on duty fell asleep simultaneously. The security cameras outside his cell failed. Many people - including former cellmates - have publicly questioned the official story.

Ghislaine Maxwell. Epstein's longtime partner and procurer. Convicted in December 2021 of sex trafficking minors. Sentenced to 20 years.

The connections. Epstein's "little black book" and flight logs included names from across the elite: Trump, Bill Clinton, Prince Andrew, Bill Gates, Larry Summers, Leon Black, Glenn Dubin, Les Wexner, and many others. Not all of these people are accused of wrongdoing. But all of them socialized with a known sex trafficker for years, in some cases after his 2008 conviction.

Prince Andrew settled a civil suit out of court with Virginia Giuffre, one of Epstein's victims. Leon Black resigned from Apollo Global Management after disclosures about his Epstein payments.

Epstein matters not because his crimes are uniquely awful (they are awful but not historically unique), but because the protection he received - the secret 2008 deal, the unusual circumstances of his death, the slow walk of investigations - shows how elite networks across politics, finance, royalty, and tech protect each other. This is the operating environment in which the rest of this book happens.
