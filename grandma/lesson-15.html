Lesson 15: Russia 2016 - What's Proven in Court

The 2016 Russia story has been so politicized that many people have stopped tracking which parts are proven and which are speculation. This lesson is just the proven parts.

The IRA - Internet Research Agency. A Russian organization based in St. Petersburg. From 2014 onward, it ran fake social media accounts pretending to be American voters. It reached an estimated 126 million Americans on Facebook alone, plus tens of millions more on Twitter, Instagram, and YouTube. The accounts pretended to be Black Lives Matter activists, Texas secessionists, evangelical Christians, Muslim Americans - whatever was inflammatory in a given moment.

The hacks. In March 2016, Russian military intelligence (the GRU, Unit 26165) hacked the email accounts of Hillary Clinton's campaign chairman John Podesta. They also hacked the Democratic National Committee. They leaked the contents in waves through July to October 2016, mostly through WikiLeaks, timed to disrupt the Clinton campaign.

The criminal charges. Special Counsel Robert Mueller's investigation indicted:

  • 13 IRA employees and 3 Russian companies (February 2018).
  • 12 GRU officers for the email hacks (July 2018).
  • Several Americans for related crimes: Paul Manafort (Trump campaign chair, convicted of fraud and tax evasion), Roger Stone (convicted of obstruction), Michael Flynn (pleaded guilty to lying to FBI), George Papadopoulos (pleaded guilty to lying to FBI), Michael Cohen (pleaded guilty to multiple charges).

Most of the Russians were never extradited - Russia does not extradite citizens. But the indictments themselves are publicly filed federal documents you can read.

The Senate Intelligence Committee report. In 2020, the Senate Intelligence Committee - chaired by Republicans at the time - published its final report on Russian interference. It is five volumes long. It confirmed all of the above. It went further on some points than Mueller. It is the most authoritative account of Russian interference and was a bipartisan finding.

The remaining argument is whether the interference was decisive - whether it actually changed the result. Reasonable people disagree on that. But the interference itself happened. It is documented. It is in court records. It is in the Senate report.
