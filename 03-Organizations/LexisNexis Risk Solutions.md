# LexisNexis Risk Solutions

## Summary

LexisNexis Risk Solutions is the data-broker and analytics division of RELX PLC, the British information conglomerate. Distinct from the LexisNexis legal-research product most people know, Risk Solutions sells data and risk-scoring services to insurers, financial institutions, employers, and — most consequentially for this vault — government and law-enforcement agencies. Its flagship investigative platform, **Accurint**, assembles encyclopedic dossiers on individuals by drawing on more than 10,000 data sources, including credit headers, utility records, license-plate reader data, property records, and public records. LexisNexis Risk Solutions provides Accurint to U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement and Customs and Border Protection through the Law Enforcement Investigative Database Subscription (LEIDS) program, under contracts reported to be worth tens of millions of dollars. Alongside Thomson Reuters CLEAR, it is one of the two primary commercial data brokers powering ICE's surveillance and immigration-enforcement operations. LexisNexis Risk Solutions is a central illustration of the vault's thesis that government can simply *buy* a population-scale profiling capability rather than building one — or hiring a [[Cambridge Analytica]]-style firm to build one.

## Funding & money flows

LexisNexis Risk Solutions' government revenue is documented through federal procurement records. Reporting indicates an ICE contract worth roughly **$22 million** running to 2028, with combined ICE/CBP contracts (for Accurint access) reported as worth up to about **$38 million**. The LEIDS program is the contracting vehicle. Beyond federal contracts, the division sells to state and local law enforcement and to commercial clients (insurers, banks, landlords, employers). RELX, the UK-listed parent, derives substantial revenue from the Risk Solutions segment.

## Activities

**Accurint dossiers.** Accurint compiles individual-level profiles from 10,000+ sources. A single query can return addresses, relatives and associates, vehicles, utility connections, phone numbers, license-plate sightings, and more — an "encyclopedic dossier" assembled without judicial oversight and sold to government, law enforcement, and private entities.

**Immigration enforcement.** Through LEIDS, ICE and CBP use Accurint to locate and build cases against individuals. Reporting documented that ICE's Chicago Field Office alone ran more than 13,000 searches in the LexisNexis database between March and September 2021, generating roughly 1,800 reports for immigration enforcement.

**Risk and identity scoring.** For commercial clients, the division provides fraud detection, identity verification, and risk scores used in insurance underwriting, credit-adjacent decisions, and tenant/employment screening.

## Controversies / investigations

**ICE surveillance role.** The central controversy is LexisNexis Risk Solutions' role as infrastructure for immigration enforcement. *The Intercept*'s 2021 reporting established the ICE contract; transparency projects map it as a core "data broker for ICE."

**Lawsuit (2022).** Immigration advocates sued LexisNexis, alleging that Accurint facilitates unwarranted surveillance of immigrants and political activists and violates consent, compensation, and privacy laws (including Illinois data-privacy statutes). `#claim/disputed` (allegations; litigation).

**Academic critique (2025).** A peer-reviewed analysis in *Big Data & Society* argued that using commercial databases like LexisNexis in immigration enforcement is unjust — the databases were never designed for immigration purposes and contain unverified data that can produce wrongful targeting. This is a documented [[algorithmic accountability]] failure: consequential coercive decisions made on un-audited, error-prone commercial data.

**Local-government entanglement.** In 2025 the Cook County board's reapproval of a contract with the ICE-linked firm — even as raids swept Chicago — illustrated how deeply LexisNexis data is embedded across government levels and how hard it is to disentangle.
