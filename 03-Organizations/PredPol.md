## Summary

PredPol was a US-based software company that sold predictive-policing algorithms to law enforcement agencies, claiming to predict where crimes were likely to occur within 500-by-500-foot grid cells. Based on research by UCLA anthropologist Jeff Brantingham and mathematician George Mohler, the software analyzed historical crime data to generate daily patrol hot spots. The LAPD was an early adopter, deploying PredPol alongside the person-based [[Operation LASER]] as a companion location-targeting system. PredPol became the leading commercial vendor in the predictive-policing market before controversy, documented inaccuracies, and community activism drove most clients to terminate contracts. In March 2021 the company rebranded as Geolitica; it ceased operations on December 31, 2023, with key assets purchased by SoundThinking (formerly ShotSpotter).

## Identity

PredPol marketed itself as a purely location-based system that did not use individual demographic data — a claim intended to deflect racial-bias critiques. Critics argued this distinction was misleading because historical crime data reflects decades of racially biased enforcement patterns, so the algorithm reproduced and amplified that bias in its predictions.

## Leadership & key personnel

- **Jeff Brantingham**: UCLA professor of anthropology; co-founder; algorithm derived from his academic earthquake-prediction research adapted for crime.
- **George Mohler**: Mathematician; co-founder; later moved to Indiana University–Purdue University Indianapolis.

## Funding & money flows

Publicly traded clients paid per-jurisdiction licensing fees. The company received early backing from the Bureau of Justice Assistance (federal) through the Smart Policing Initiative, which funded LAPD's pilot deployment.

## Activities

- Supplied hot-spot prediction software to police departments in Los Angeles, Santa Cruz, Atlanta, New Orleans, and dozens of other US cities.
- Algorithm processed historical robbery, burglary, and car-theft data; did not (by design) incorporate individual identity data.
- Partnered with the LAPD during the Smart Policing Initiative, which bundled PredPol with [[Operation LASER]].

## Controversies / investigations

- **Accuracy**: No independent peer-reviewed study ever confirmed PredPol's efficacy claims. Smithsonian (2018) noted this gap. The Markup (October 2023) found <0.5% accuracy for Plainfield PD.
- **Racial bias**: Mic (2016) reported PredPol directed police disproportionately to minority neighborhoods. The feedback loop problem — policing hot spots generates more arrests there, which generates more data that confirms the hot spot — was identified by critics as structurally unfixable.
- **LAPD/LASER**: PredPol's location component was bundled with the person-targeting [[Operation LASER]]; the combined system was cancelled by LAPD in April 2019 after sustained community activism.
- **Santa Cruz ban**: In 2020 Santa Cruz became the first US city to legally prohibit predictive policing.
