# Moral Majority

## Summary

Religious-right political organization **founded 1979 by [[Jerry Falwell Sr.]]** with help from [[Paul Weyrich]] (who coined the name), [[Tim LaHaye]], Howard Phillips, and Ed McAteer. **Disbanded 1989** but its infrastructure flowed into the [[Christian Coalition]] (Pat Robertson / Ralph Reed). Played a key role in mobilizing conservative Christians as a political force during the Reagan era; credited with helping Reagan win 1980. Operations included voter registration, lobbying, fundraising. Opposed abortion, ERA, pornography, gay rights. **The first mass-mobilization political organization of the modern Christian right.**

## Identity

- **Founded:** 1979
- **Founder:** [[Jerry Falwell Sr.]]
- **Strategist:** [[Paul Weyrich]] (coined "Moral Majority"; later Heritage / ALEC / CNP co-founder)
- **Dissolved:** 1989
- **Successor:** Functionally, the [[Christian Coalition]] (1989+)

## Why it matters historically

The Moral Majority is the **first wave** of the modern Christian right's electoral mobilization. Before 1979, evangelical voters were not systematically organized as a partisan bloc; they tended to vote D in the South and split elsewhere. **The Moral Majority converted them into the now-reliable GOP base.**

## Methodology

- Direct mail at scale (Paul Weyrich's specialty)
- Pastor networks for voter mobilization
- Single-issue voter scorecards
- Coalition with secular New Right operatives
