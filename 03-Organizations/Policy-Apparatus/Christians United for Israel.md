# Christians United for Israel (CUFI)

Christians United for Israel (CUFI) is a 501(c)(4) Christian-Zionist mass-organising infrastructure founded 2006 by [[John Hagee]] (Cornerstone Church pastor in San Antonio, Texas). CUFI is the principal Christian-Zionist mass-organising vehicle in US politics, claiming membership of over 10 million across multiple denominational categories.

## Operational scope

CUFI operates through:

- Annual Washington Summit drawing tens of thousands of Christian-Zionist activists, members of Congress, and Israeli political leadership
- Year-round mass-organising programmes across approximately 4,000 affiliated US Protestant churches
- The CUFI Action Fund (affiliated 501(c)(4) lobbying vehicle)
- Substantial coordination with [[AIPAC]] and broader pro-Israel infrastructure on shared policy priorities
- Educational programming on US-Israel policy and theological background

The membership scale is substantially larger than [[AIPAC]] and reflects the broader US evangelical-Protestant population's substantial Christian-Zionist theological positioning.

## Theological substrate

CUFI's organisational base draws on dispensationalist Christian theology that holds:

- Restored Jewish sovereignty over biblical-era territory is theologically required as precursor to end-times prophecy fulfilment
- US support for Israeli state policy is theologically obligatory for Christian believers
- Opposition to Israeli state policy is theologically suspect

The theological substrate is operationally distinct from secular pro-Israel positioning and produces a substantially larger and more politically-active mass base than secular pro-Israel infrastructure alone could mobilize.

The theological substrate substantially overlaps with [[New Apostolic Reformation]] / [[Dominionism]] / dispensationalist tradition that the vault tracks within the broader Religious Right cluster.

## Connection to broader vault network

CUFI sits at the intersection of:

- Religious Right cluster (substantial NAR/dispensationalist theological substrate)
- Israel cluster (principal Christian-Zionist mass-organising vehicle)
- Patron Wing (substantial coordinated funding with [[AIPAC]], [[Republican Jewish Coalition]])
- International Right (international Christian-Zionist organisational expansion)

The cross-cluster reach is operationally distinctive: no other vault entity bridges as many clusters at the mass-organising level.
