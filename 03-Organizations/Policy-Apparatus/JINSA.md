# JINSA (Jewish Institute for National Security of America)

The Jewish Institute for National Security of America (JINSA) is a Washington-based 501(c)(3) think tank, founded 1976, focused on US national-security policy with particular attention to US-Israel security relationship. JINSA is one of the principal neoconservative-aligned defense-policy think tanks in US politics.

## Operational scope

JINSA operates principally through:

- Policy research and publication on US-Israel security topics
- The JINSA Generals and Admirals Programme (organising study tours of Israel for retired US senior military officers)
- The Gemunder Center for Defense and Strategy (the principal research vehicle within JINSA)
- Annual Henry M. Jackson Distinguished Service Award (named after Sen. Henry Scoop Jackson)
- Substantial coordination with [[AIPAC]], [[Republican Jewish Coalition]], and adjacent Israel-policy infrastructure

## Personnel pipeline

JINSA's personnel pipeline has substantially shaped Republican-administration defense-policy appointments across multiple administrations. The pattern: JINSA fellows and JINSA-affiliated retired military officers have featured substantially in Republican-administration DoD and intelligence-community appointments.

## Connection to broader vault network

JINSA sits at the intersection of the patron-wing Israel-policy donor architecture, the neoconservative foreign-policy intellectual tradition, and the Republican-administration defense-policy personnel pipeline. The neoconservative-foreign-policy tradition substantially intersects with the broader [[Federalist Society]] / [[Heritage Foundation]] / [[Project 2025]] policy architecture even though JINSA itself focuses on defense-policy questions.
