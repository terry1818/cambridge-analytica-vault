# Israeli-American Council (IAC)

The Israeli-American Council is a 501(c)(3) founded 2007 by [[Sheldon Adelson]] as the principal political-organising vehicle for Israeli-Americans in US political life. IAC operates as a non-partisan organisation but has substantially aligned with Republican Israel-policy positioning across multiple cycles.

## Operational scope

IAC operates through:

- Annual IAC National Conference drawing thousands of Israeli-American attendees, Republican presidential candidates, and Israeli government leadership
- Year-round programming across approximately 25 regional offices in Israeli-American population centres
- Educational and cultural programming for Israeli-American families
- Substantial coordination with [[AIPAC]], [[Republican Jewish Coalition]], and the broader pro-Israel infrastructure

The Israeli-American population in the US is approximately 500,000-700,000; IAC is the principal organisational vehicle that engages this population politically.

## Adelson family operational anchor

The Adelson family (Sheldon historically, Miriam continuing) has been the principal operational anchor for IAC. Adelson-family funding has been the principal financial substrate; Adelson-family operational direction has substantially shaped strategic positioning.

## Trump-administration alignment

IAC's substantial alignment with Trump-administration Israel-policy positioning across the first administration and Trump 2.0 administration has produced operational consequences. The principal pattern: Trump and Trump-administration figures featured prominently at IAC events; IAC programming reinforced Trump-administration Israel-policy positioning; the broader operational relationship was substantially closer than what a non-partisan 501(c)(3) would typically maintain.
