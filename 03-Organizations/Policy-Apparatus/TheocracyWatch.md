# TheocracyWatch

## Summary

Nonprofit research project founded in **2000 by Joan Bokaer** following the contested Bush-Gore election. Hosted by Cornell University's **Center for Religion, Ethics and Social Policy (CRESP)**. The project's central thesis: **the religious right has taken substantial working control of the Republican Party**, and its working through Republican-Party institutional infrastructure rather than as a discrete external actor. Bokaer's primary methodology was **studying congressional voting patterns** to gauge dominionist influence over time. The project published a comprehensive web-archive resource called **"Rise of the Religious Right in the Republican Party"** (theocracywatch.org) plus a YouTube playlist of educational videos on dominionism and theocratic ambitions.

**As of September 2025, the original TheocracyWatch.org website was reportedly removed/offline** (per Democracy High reporting). The YouTube playlist remains active. The archive itself is one of the most foundational citizen-research resources documenting the dominionist movement.

## Key documented findings

- **Dominionism is the theory** that Genesis's "dominion" mandate has become a political teaching that Christians must gain and hold power over secular institutions
- The modern dominionist movement started **1973 with R.J. Rushdoony**, Christian Reconstructionist theologian
- **Moral Majority** (1979-1989) launched the political phase
- **Christian Coalition** (1989-) replaced Moral Majority with more sophisticated grassroots organizing under Ralph Reed
- **Council for National Policy** (1981-) became the coordinating institution
- **Seven Mountains Mandate** (early 2010s) is the current charismatic-evangelical version
- Republican Party voting patterns increasingly align with dominionist priorities since 1980
