# The Federalist Society

## Summary

Conservative legal society founded **1982** at Yale, Harvard, and University of Chicago law schools by Steven Calabresi, David McIntosh, Lee Liberman Otis, and others. Now has **~70,000 members** across student chapters at virtually every US law school + lawyer chapters in every major city. **The personnel pipeline behind every Trump-era SCOTUS appointment** (Gorsuch, Kavanaugh, Barrett) plus hundreds of federal-judiciary appointments. **[[Leonard Leo]]'s primary institutional vehicle** - though Leo's broader operations now extend well beyond FedSoc.

**The judicial-capture taproot.** Where [[The Heritage Foundation]] authors policy and [[Council for National Policy]] coordinates strategy, **FedSoc supplies the lawyers and judges who lock it all in legally**.

## Identity

- **Founded:** 1982
- **HQ:** Washington DC
- **Type:** 501(c)(3)
- **Membership:** ~70,000 (lawyers + students)
- **Annual budget:** ~$30M+

## Structure

- **Student Division** - chapters at virtually every US law school
- **Lawyers Division** - city-based chapters in every major US city
- **Practice Groups** - subject-matter expert networks (admin law, criminal, etc.)
- **Conferences** - annual National Lawyers Convention; student symposia

## How FedSoc + Leonard Leo built the judicial pipeline

1. **Recruit at law schools** (Student Division identifies conservative talent early)
2. **Mentor through career** (clerkships, jobs, fellowships funneled to vetted personnel)
3. **Vetting list** (Leo's lists became Trump's actual judicial-nominee lists)
4. **Confirmation infrastructure** (Judicial Crisis Network / Concord Fund spent $100M+ confirming Kavanaugh)
5. **Post-confirmation network** (alumni stay connected via FedSoc continuing engagement)
