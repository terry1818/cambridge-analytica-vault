# DonorsTrust

## Summary

**Conservative dark-money donor-advised fund (DAF)** founded **1999 by Whitney Ball** (deceased 2015). **~$1.5B+ in assets** as of mid-2020s. **Dubbed "the dark-money ATM of the right" by Mother Jones / The Nation**. Routes anonymous individual + corporate + foundation donor money to right-wing 501(c)(3) and 501(c)(4) recipients. Operates with companion fund **[[Donors Capital Fund]]** (501(c)(3); founded 1999 by same network).

**DonorsTrust has been the single most consequential dark-money distribution channel** for the post-2000 conservative coalition. Distributed ~$1B+ to Koch-network entities, climate-denial orgs (Heartland Institute), election-denial orgs, Convention of States Action, and broader patron-class infrastructure 1999-2025.

## Operational model

DAF structure:
- Donors contribute to DonorsTrust (tax-deductible)
- Donors retain "advisory" role over distribution
- DonorsTrust distributes to chosen recipients
- **Recipients see "DonorsTrust" as donor, not original individual**
- Provides full donor anonymity

**This is the structural mechanism** by which patron-class donors fund the entire coalition infrastructure without public attribution.

## Counter-strategy implications

DonorsTrust is **structurally an anonymity mechanism**, not an ideological actor. Counter-strategy:

- **DAF reform legislation** (require minimum payout rates; restrict anonymity)
- **State-level DAF disclosure requirements**
- **501(c)(3) status review** (DonorsTrust's public-charity status arguably violated by overwhelming-political-advocacy distribution pattern)
- **Federal-grants restrictions** on DonorsTrust-recipient orgs
