# Constitutional Sheriffs and Peace Officers Association (CSPOA)

The Constitutional Sheriffs and Peace Officers Association (CSPOA) is a network founded by former Graham County, Arizona sheriff Richard Mack in 2011. CSPOA promotes the doctrine that county sheriffs are the highest constitutional authority within their county, with authority to refuse to enforce federal laws the sheriff deems unconstitutional.

## The constitutional doctrine

The CSPOA doctrine is operationally derived from older "posse comitatus" movement intellectual material adapted into a modern law-enforcement-focused framework. The principal claims: county sheriffs are elected directly by county voters and therefore hold democratic legitimacy that federal agencies do not; sheriffs have constitutional authority to refuse to cooperate with federal agencies on matters the sheriff judges unconstitutional; sheriffs are obligated to protect county residents from federal overreach including gun-control enforcement, immigration enforcement contrary to local preferences, and election-related federal involvement.

## Membership and operational scope

CSPOA claims hundreds of member sheriffs across the US. The actual membership count varies by source and metric (formal members vs ideologically-aligned sheriffs vs sheriffs who have attended CSPOA training). The operational consequence is that a substantial fraction of US county sheriffs are operationally sympathetic to CSPOA doctrine even when not formally affiliated.

## Election-administration crossover

Post-2020, CSPOA has expanded into election-administration positioning. CSPOA-aligned sheriffs have asserted authority to investigate election-administration in their counties, to inspect voting equipment, and to coordinate with [[True the Vote]] and adjacent organisations on local election-monitoring. The Sheriff Mark Lamb of Pinal County, Arizona was a prominent example through 2022-2024.

## Why this is load-bearing

CSPOA is the principal organisational vehicle for sheriff-sovereignty doctrine. The doctrine's operational application to election administration is one of the principal mechanisms through which the broader post-2020 election-skepticism programme has been translated into specific local law-enforcement actions.
