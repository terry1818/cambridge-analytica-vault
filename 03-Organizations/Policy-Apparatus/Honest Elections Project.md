# Honest Elections Project

The Honest Elections Project (HEP) is a 501(c)(4) launched in 2020, structured as a fiscally-sponsored project of the [[Concord Fund]] (formerly Judicial Crisis Network). HEP is the principal conservative-legal-infrastructure entity coordinating state-and-federal election-law litigation.

## Operational scope

HEP's litigation portfolio has included:

- Amicus briefs in election-administration cases across multiple jurisdictions
- Direct litigation challenging state-level voter-registration policies, drop-box operations, mail-in voting expansions, and election-official conduct
- Coordination with state-attorney-general offices on election-related litigation strategy
- Strategic-litigation support for the broader EIN coordination ecosystem

## Why this is part of the [[Leonard Leo]] infrastructure

HEP is operationally part of the Marble Freedom Trust / CRC Advisors / Concord Fund / The 85 Fund ecosystem that Leo controls. The fiscal-sponsorship arrangement with Concord Fund places HEP within the broader Leo-coordinated conservative-legal infrastructure that has substantially shaped post-2020 election-related litigation strategy.

## Detailed litigation portfolio and operational pattern

The Honest Elections Project's litigation portfolio has substantially expanded across the 2020-2026 period. Specific operational patterns:

**State-level voter-registration challenges.** HEP has been the principal coordinating vehicle for legal challenges to state-level voter-registration policies including automatic voter registration programmes, online voter registration access, and the broader voter-roll-maintenance regulatory framework.

**Drop-box and mail-in voting challenges.** Following the 2020-cycle expansion of mail-in and drop-box voting (substantially driven by COVID-era public-health concerns), HEP has coordinated litigation challenging the post-2020 retention of those expanded voting modalities. The litigation portfolio includes both substantive challenges (whether specific voting modalities comply with state constitutional or statutory requirements) and procedural challenges (whether the regulatory authority to authorise the modalities was properly exercised).

**Election-administration personnel challenges.** HEP has supported litigation challenging specific election-administration personnel conduct including secretary-of-state, county-election-board, and adjacent personnel decisions.

## Funding architecture and the Leo-coordinated ecosystem

HEP's fiscal-sponsorship arrangement with [[Concord Fund]] places the organisation within the broader [[Leonard Leo]] / [[Marble Freedom Trust]] / [[CRC Advisors]] conservative-legal infrastructure. The funding-architecture-and-strategic-coordination is operationally significant: HEP litigation is coordinated with the broader conservative-legal-infrastructure strategy, not pursued as independent litigation by an isolated organisation.
