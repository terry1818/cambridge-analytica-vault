# The 85 Fund

The 85 Fund is the **501(c)(3) charitable counterpart to the [[Concord Fund]]** in [[Leonard Leo]]'s dark-money network. Originally called the **Judicial Education Project (JEP)**, it was rebranded as The 85 Fund in **early 2020** as part of Leo's strategic re-architecture. The pairing — a 501(c)(3) "education" entity and a 501(c)(4) "advocacy" entity, sharing personnel and donors — is a textbook IRS-arbitrage structure. In 2022 alone, The 85 Fund **distributed $134 million** to right-wing causes.

## Structural function

Tax law lets a 501(c)(3) receive tax-deductible donations but limits its political advocacy; a 501(c)(4) can do unlimited political advocacy but cannot offer tax deductibility. Pairing them — with overlapping personnel and routine fund transfers — gives a donor:

- A **deductible donation** route (into the 85 Fund) for the donor's tax bill
- A **maximum-flexibility political-advocacy** route (via The 85 Fund's grants to Concord Fund, which then runs advocacy)
- **Anonymity** — neither entity discloses individual donors

The 85 Fund's 2022 disclosed transfers include very large flows to **[[DonorsTrust]]** (~$92M of the $134M distributed) — itself a dark-money behemoth that disburses ~$2B/year and was founded in 1999 specifically as a libertarian/conservative donor-advised fund. The 85 Fund → DonorsTrust transfer pattern launders attribution one additional layer deeper.

## Personnel overlap with Concord Fund

The personnel design is the most legally revealing feature:

| Person | Concord Fund role | The 85 Fund role |
|---|---|---|
| [[Carrie Severino]] | President (continuing as JCN brand) | Director and Secretary |
| Gary Marx | President | Director and Treasurer |
| [[Leonard Leo]] | De facto architect | De facto architect |

This level of personnel overlap is what makes Concord Fund and The 85 Fund **operationally a single entity** despite their separate legal status — a structure designed to maximize legal protection while minimizing administrative friction.

## Project 2025 connection

CREW (Citizens for Responsibility and Ethics in Washington) has tracked specific grants from The 85 Fund to organizations on the **[[Project 2025]] advisory board** including [[Heritage Foundation]], [[Center for Renewing America]], [[America First Policy Institute]], [[Foundation for Government Accountability]], and others. Senator Sheldon Whitehouse's "Scheme 18" speech summarized: *"your tax dollars have in effect subsidized the creation of a detailed plan for the radical right wing to assume command of the federal government."*

The mechanism: 501(c)(3) status means donors get a federal tax deduction. Those deductions reduce federal revenue. So the federal government is, in net effect, funding part of the cost of a political project to capture the federal government.

## Why this matters

The 85 Fund is the **invisible engine** of the Leo network. Concord Fund gets press coverage because of its explicit political advocacy; The 85 Fund operates with much less visibility because its activities (officially "education") are less newsworthy. But the dollars flowing through The 85 Fund are larger than Concord Fund's, and the 85 Fund's grants pattern is what reveals the network's *strategic* priorities (versus Concord Fund's *tactical* ad-buy priorities).

For mapping the actual money topology of the post-2022 Republican-aligned infrastructure, The 85 Fund's 990s are the single most important public document.

## 2025-2026 status

Unlike Concord Fund (dissolved December 2025), The 85 Fund as of mid-2026 **remains active**. No public restructuring of the 501(c)(3) side has been disclosed. This is consistent with the legal logic: 501(c)(3) status is harder to acquire and protect, so the network keeps the (c)(3) structure stable while shuffling the (c)(4) entities.
