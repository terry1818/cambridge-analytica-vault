# Hillsdale College

Hillsdale College is a private Christian liberal-arts college in Hillsdale, Michigan, founded in 1844. Under President Larry Arnn (since 2000), Hillsdale has substantially expanded its operational reach beyond traditional higher education into the K-12 curriculum infrastructure, the political-personnel-pipeline space, and the broader conservative-Christian institutional ecosystem.

## The 1776 Curriculum

Hillsdale launched the 1776 Curriculum in 2021 as a K-12 history-and-civics curriculum package marketed as a counter to "1619 Project"-style framing. The curriculum has been adopted by charter schools in multiple states; the [[Hillsdale K-12 Charter School Network]] has grown to dozens of affiliated schools by 2024-2026.

## Charter-school network

Hillsdale has been the founding-or-supporting institution for charter schools (Barney Charter School Initiative) across multiple states. The model: Hillsdale provides curriculum, teacher-training, and accreditation support; local charter operators provide the operational infrastructure. The network has grown to dozens of affiliated schools with continuing expansion through 2024-2026.

## Trump-era personnel pipeline

Hillsdale was substantially aligned with the first Trump administration and has continued in the Trump 2.0 administration. Arnn served on Trump's 1776 Commission; multiple Hillsdale-affiliated figures hold or have held Trump-administration appointments. The institutional-positioning is explicit conservative-Christian alignment.

## Federal-funding independence

Hillsdale's distinctive financial structure: the college refuses all federal funding (including indirect funding through federal student aid). This structural choice gives Hillsdale operational independence from Title IX, accessibility, anti-discrimination, and adjacent federal requirements that other higher-education institutions must comply with. The structural choice has been the operational basis for Hillsdale's explicitly conservative-Christian institutional identity.
