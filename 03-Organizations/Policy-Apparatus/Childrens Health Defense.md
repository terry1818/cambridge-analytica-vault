# Children's Health Defense

Children's Health Defense (CHD) is a 501(c)(3) founded by [[RFK Jr]] in 2007 (originally as the World Mercury Project, rebranded in 2018). CHD is the principal anti-vaccine advocacy and litigation organisation in the US and the operational infrastructure for the broader [[Make America Healthy Again]] (MAHA) movement.

## Operational scope

CHD operates through:

- Litigation programme challenging vaccine-related policy and product-liability questions
- Media operation including The Defender (online publication) and CHD.TV (streaming-video platform)
- Conference programming and movement-coordination work
- Grant-making to adjacent anti-vaccine and alternative-medicine organisations
- Direct advocacy in state-and-federal legislative venues

## Funding and operational scale

CHD's annual operating budget grew substantially through 2020-2024 as RFK Jr's public visibility increased. Annual revenue in 2023 was reportedly in the $20-30M range. Funder base includes substantial individual giving from anti-vaccine-aligned donors and smaller-scale grassroots contributions.

## Operational role in MAHA implementation

Following RFK Jr's appointment as HHS Secretary, CHD's institutional positioning relative to the federal government substantially changed. CHD now operates as a movement-coordination infrastructure that is operationally adjacent to (rather than oppositional to) the federal health-policy apparatus. The operational consequences for CHD's advocacy posture and policy-influence work have been continuing.
