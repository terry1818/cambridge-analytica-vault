# AIPAC (American Israel Public Affairs Committee)

AIPAC is the principal pro-Israel lobbying organisation in US politics, founded 1963 by Isaiah L. Kenen. AIPAC has historically operated as a bipartisan lobbying organisation, but the 2022-2024 expansion through the [[United Democracy Project]] super PAC has substantially shifted operational positioning toward Republican-aligned and centrist-Democrat-aligned candidates.

## Operational structure

AIPAC operates principally as a 501(c)(4) lobbying organisation that focuses on:

- Direct lobbying of Congress on Israel-related legislation
- Annual policy conference (AIPAC Policy Conference) drawing thousands of activists, members of Congress, and Israeli political leadership
- Member-recruitment and political-organising programmes across US Jewish communities and Christian-Zionist communities
- Educational programming on US-Israel policy questions

The membership claim is approximately 100,000+ members, with annual operating budget reportedly $100M+ across recent years.

## United Democracy Project (UDP)

UDP is AIPAC's super PAC, launched 2022. UDP has substantially reshaped Democratic-primary politics around Israel-policy positioning:

- 2022 cycle: UDP spent approximately $30M defeating progressive challengers in Democratic primaries
- 2024 cycle: UDP spent approximately $100M, with principal operational consequences:
  - Jamaal Bowman (NY-16) primary loss (UDP spent ~$14M against)
  - Cori Bush (MO-1) primary loss (UDP spent ~$8M against)
  - Multiple additional Democratic-primary defeats of anti-AIPAC-aligned candidates
- Substantial deterrent effect on remaining Democratic members of Congress regarding Israel-critical positioning

The UDP operational model is operationally analogous to [[Fairshake PAC]]'s crypto-industry capture of congressional crypto policy: single-issue super PAC deploys substantial primary-cycle spending to eliminate anti-position incumbents, producing systemic shift in remaining-member behaviour beyond the specific defeated candidates.

## Funding architecture

UDP funding has been substantially documented through FEC filings. Major funders include the [[Adelson]] family (post-Sheldon-death continuing giving via Miriam), pro-Israel mega-donors including Paul Singer-network donors, and substantial Pritzker-family-adjacent and adjacent Democratic-aligned pro-Israel donor giving.

## Post-October 7 operational expansion

The post-October 7, 2023 environment substantially expanded UDP's operational scope. The 2024-cycle spending pattern reflected post-Oct-7 prioritization of eliminating Democratic incumbents who had been publicly critical of Israeli military operations in Gaza.
