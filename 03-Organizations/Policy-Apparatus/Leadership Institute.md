# Leadership Institute (LI)

## Summary

Conservative training organization founded **1979 by Morton Blackwell** in Arlington VA. **Trains conservative activists, candidates, journalists, and student-organizers** in campaign techniques, communications, fundraising, and political operations. Claims **250,000+ alumni since 1979**. Operates **Campus Reform** (right-wing campus media), **Campus Leadership Program**, and dozens of specialized training tracks. **One of the three principal CNP-affiliated personnel-pipeline arms** alongside [[State Policy Network]] and [[Family Research Council]] (per Bill Moyers' *Shadow Network* coverage).

Morton Blackwell is a **longtime [[Council for National Policy|CNP]] member** and chair of CNP's Republican-Party-engagement committee. His Leadership Institute is functionally **CNP's training arm**.

- **Founded:** 1979

## Activities

- **Candidate training:** how to run for office; campaign management
- **Activist training:** lobbying, communications, fundraising
- **Campus Reform:** right-wing student journalism organization
- **Campus Leadership Program:** right-wing campus chapter network
- **Specialized tracks:** youth, women, minority outreach
- **Direct training of:** Mitch McConnell, Karl Rove, James O'Keefe, Ralph Reed, Mike Pence, many others

## How LI fits the architecture

- **Personnel pipeline** complement to [[Conservative Partnership Institute]] (which targets already-placed personnel) and [[Capitol Ministries]] (which targets cabinet officials)
- **Earlier in the funnel** than CPI - LI trains pre-political-career activists; CPI trains already-elected/appointed
- **Critical for [[Convention of States Action]] state-by-state operations** - many state-legislator allies trained at LI
- **Critical for [[New Apostolic Reformation]] political operations** - many NAR-aligned activists trained at LI
