# Christian Coalition

## Summary

**Founded 1989 by [[Pat Robertson]]** after his failed 1988 Republican presidential nomination bid. Operated by **[[Ralph Reed]] as executive director 1989-1997**, the "GOP wunderkind" who built it into the most sophisticated grassroots political organization in American religious-right history. Replaced the disbanded [[Moral Majority]] as the principal religious-right political organization. **Trained conservative Christians to run for office** and to manage other people's campaigns, with these trainees becoming the GOP infrastructure of the 1990s and 2000s in many states. Greatly diminished from peak influence after Reed left to start what eventually became [[Faith and Freedom Coalition]], but the personnel pipeline it built remains operational.

## What made it different from Moral Majority

- **Grassroots-organizing focus** rather than direct-mail-only
- **Voter scorecards distributed in churches** as election-eve pamphlets (the famous "Christian Coalition voter guides")
- **State-level GOP infrastructure capture** rather than just federal-issue lobbying
- **Trained operatives** rather than just mobilized voters

## Documented impact

- 1994 "Republican Revolution" House takeover heavily attributed to Christian Coalition voter mobilization
- 2000 Bush nomination secured with Christian Coalition support
- State Republican parties in many Southern + Midwestern states became substantially Christian-Coalition-controlled by the late 1990s
