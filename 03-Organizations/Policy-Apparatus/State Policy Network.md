# State Policy Network (SPN)

## Summary

**The conservative-coalition's state-level operational arm.** Founded 1992 in Arlington VA. Network of **68 affiliates + 99 associates** with member groups in **all 50 states** as of 2021. Functions as **the state-level analog to [[The Heritage Foundation]]** - while Heritage publishes federal-level policy, SPN affiliates publish state-level policy frameworks, model legislation, and astroturf organizing for conservative state legislatures. **SPN is the most-important coordinating institution for state-level conservative policy that most voters have never heard of.**

Heavily integrated with **[[Council for National Policy]]** strategy and **[[American Legislative Exchange Council|ALEC]]** model legislation. Same funder cluster (Koch network, DeVos, Bradley, DonorsTrust, Scaife). Provides on-the-ground state infrastructure for [[Convention of States Action]] state-by-state strategy, for [[Project 2025]] state-level implementation, and for Christian-nationalist-aligned state policy.

## SPN affiliate operational mechanics

State Policy Network's 65+ affiliate organisations operate under varying state-level brand names ([[Texas Public Policy Foundation]], Mackinac Center for Public Policy (Michigan), Goldwater Institute (Arizona), Pacific Research Institute (California), Manhattan Institute (federal-and-state hybrid), and many smaller state-specific think-tanks). The operational coordination across affiliates includes shared policy-research production, shared model-legislation drafting, shared personnel pipelines, and shared communications infrastructure.

The model-legislation pipeline is the operationally most consequential single SPN function. SPN affiliates produce policy proposals; the proposals are coordinated through SPN's central staff; the coordinated proposals are then promoted to state legislators across multiple states simultaneously, producing legislative outputs that look like state-by-state independent decisions but are in fact coordinated programmes. The pattern overlaps substantially with [[ALEC]] (American Legislative Exchange Council) model-legislation work, though SPN and ALEC operate as separate institutional vehicles.

## Funding and donor base

SPN's funder base spans the [[Koch Network]] (substantial and documented across multiple decades), the [[DeVos Family Network]], the [[Mercer Family Foundation]], the [[Bradley Foundation]], the [[Scaife Foundation]], and a broader base of mid-tier conservative-movement donors. The aggregate annual budget across all SPN affiliates is in the $100M+ range; the central SPN organisation operates on a substantially smaller budget that coordinates the larger affiliate network.
