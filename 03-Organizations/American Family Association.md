## Summary

The American Family Association (AFA) is a major evangelical activist organization headquartered in Tupelo, Mississippi, founded in 1977 by United Methodist minister Donald Wildmon as the National Federation for Decency. AFA is best known for organizing consumer boycotts against corporations, broadcasters, and media properties deemed hostile to "traditional family values," and for operating American Family Radio (AFR), a network of over 175 stations across more than 40 states. Anne Nelson's *Shadow Network* (2019) documents AFA's role as a key node in the Council for National Policy's media-and-mobilization infrastructure.

## Identity

Wildmon founded AFA after a December 1976 experience watching primetime television and concluding it was inappropriate for his family. His innovation was to treat religious conservatives as a consumer bloc capable of inflicting financial damage on corporations through organized boycotts — a tool he introduced to the broader Religious Right. Unlike politically oriented organizations focused on electoral or legislative strategy, Wildmon concentrated on media and cultural production as the primary battlefield.

AFA's radio network, AFR, provides sustained access to a geographically dispersed evangelical audience independent of commercial or public broadcast infrastructure. This capacity — to reach millions of listeners in rural and exurban markets with cost-effective, faith-framed political messaging — is precisely the infrastructure Anne Nelson identifies as central to the shadow network's electoral reach.

The AFA is designated a hate group by the Southern Poverty Law Center, primarily for its positions and rhetoric regarding LGBTQ people.

## Leadership & Key Personnel

Donald Wildmon (1938–2023) founded the organization and served as chairman until stepping down in March 2010. His son Tim Wildmon succeeded him as president. Bryan Fischer, formerly director of issues analysis, became one of the organization's most prominent and extreme voices on LGBTQ issues before his dismissal from formal spokesperson duties in 2015 (though he retained his radio program). Fischer's rhetoric became an embarrassment when the AFA served as a sponsor of a 2015 Republican National Committee event.

## Funding & Money Flows

AFA is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit. It is a member organization of the [[Council for National Policy]] and has received funding through the broader dark-money infrastructure documented by Nelson, including connections to DonorsTrust-adjacent networks. Its primary revenue source is listener donations to American Family Radio. Specific donor details are not publicly disclosed. Annual revenues are estimated in the tens of millions of dollars.

## Activities

- Organizing corporate boycotts (Disney, Ford, McDonald's, American Airlines, and many others)
- Operating American Family Radio (175+ stations in 40+ states)
- Publishing American Family News (OneNewsNow), an online news service
- Lobbying through AFA Action
- Distributing voter guides to evangelical churches during election cycles
- Campaigns against LGBTQ equality, reproductive rights, pornography, and perceived secularization
