# Republican National Committee

## Summary

The Republican National Committee (RNC) is the central governing body of the U.S. Republican Party, founded in 1856 — two years after the party's formation — to support the presidential campaign of John C. Frémont. It develops and promotes the party platform and brand, raises money, coordinates national campaign strategy, oversees the state Republican committees, organizes the Republican National Convention, and coordinates with the party's two legislative campaign arms (the National Republican Congressional Committee and National Republican Senatorial Committee). For this vault, the RNC matters on two specific axes: it is the institutional owner of the party's modern **voter-data operation** — the side of the political-data industry that parallels [[Catalist]] on the Democratic side — and, after the 2020 election, it became a central organizer of the party's **"election integrity" legal and poll-monitoring programs**, connecting it to the broader [[Election Denial Infrastructure]].

## Identity

- **Legal name(s):** Republican National Committee.
- **Other names / fronts:** "RNC."
- **Founded:** 1856; the 1856 Republican National Convention appointed the first committee, one member per state and territory.
- **Parent / owner:** none; the RNC is the party's national committee.
- **Registered jurisdiction:** United States (Washington, D.C.).

## Leadership & key personnel

- The RNC is led by a Chair elected by committee members; the chairmanship has turned over repeatedly across recent cycles, and the office — not any one holder — is what this vault tracks.
- Members are chosen by state delegations; since 1952 membership expanded to include state party chairs from states meeting partisan-performance criteria, reaching 145+ members by 1968.

## Funding & money flows

The RNC is a major political fundraising entity, raising and spending hundreds of millions of dollars per election cycle for presidential and coordinated campaign activity, reported through the Federal Election Commission. It funds field operations, advertising, data infrastructure, and legal activity.

## Activities

**Voter data.** After the Obama campaigns won wide praise for sophisticated data operations, the RNC built a dedicated data capability to collate information on Republican voters and supply it to campaigns and state parties. The committee promoted a "permanent ground game" branded "V365" and a national voter file it described as containing roughly 190 million active U.S. voters, accessed through a dashboard or "control panel." This is the Republican counterpart to the data infrastructure analyzed at [[Catalist]] and is connected to the wider Republican data ecosystem (including the party-aligned Data Trust and the Koch-network's i360).

**Election integrity programs.** Following the 2020 election, the RNC substantially expanded an "election integrity" effort — recruiting and training poll watchers and "poll observers," building a legal network, and pursuing election-related litigation. This program shares personnel, vocabulary, and aims with the [[Election Integrity Network]] and is part of the post-2020 [[Election Denial Infrastructure]] this vault tracks.

## Timeline

- `1856` — RNC founded to support the Frémont presidential campaign.
- `1952` — committee membership expanded to include qualifying state party chairs.
- `~2013-2014` — RNC builds a modern data operation in response to the Democratic data advantage; later promotes "V365" and a ~190M-voter file.
- `2020-2021` — after the disputed 2020 election, the RNC channels fundraising and legal activity around fraud claims (see [[Stop the Steal]]).
- `2022-2026` — RNC expands "election integrity" poll-watching and litigation programs in coordination with allied groups.

## Successor / related entities

- National Republican Congressional Committee, National Republican Senatorial Committee — the party's legislative campaign arms; coordinated with, not subsidiaries of, the RNC.
- [[Data Trust]] / [[i360]] — Republican-aligned voter-data infrastructure operating alongside the RNC's own file.
- [[Election Integrity Network]] — aligned post-2020 election-integrity coalition.

## Controversies / investigations

- [[Stop the Steal]] — the RNC's role in channeling money and legal effort around the 2020 fraud claims.
- The post-2020 "election integrity" poll-watching program has drawn criticism that it functions as voter intimidation and partisan capture of nominally neutral observation roles; the RNC frames it as lawful ballot-security work. `#claim/disputed`
