## Summary

The Berkman Klein Center for Internet & Society is a Harvard University research center studying how internet technologies shape and are shaped by society. It was co-founded in 1996 by Harvard Law School professors Charles Nesson and Jonathan Zittrain as the "Center on Law and Technology." In 1997, the Berkman family endowed the center and Lawrence Lessig joined as its first Berkman Professor. The center was elevated to a university-wide interfaculty initiative in 2008, and renamed to include "Klein" in 2016 following a $15 million gift from Michael R. Klein.

Within the research domains of this vault, the Berkman Klein Center is significant as the institutional home of the *Network Propaganda* research team (Yochai Benkler, Robert Faris, Hal Roberts), and as co-developer of [[Media Cloud]], the open-source media analysis platform that powered the *Network Propaganda* hyperlink dataset.

## Identity

The center operates across internet law, governance, network security, digital journalism, and political communication. Its fellows and affiliates have included many of the most prominent internet-law and digital-rights researchers of the past three decades. It is funded by a combination of university support, foundation grants, and individual gifts; its funders include the Knight Foundation, the MacArthur Foundation, and similar organizations. Unlike advocacy organizations, BKC produces academic research with peer-review norms, though critics from the right have occasionally characterized it as a liberal institution.

## Leadership & Key Personnel

- **Charles Nesson / Jonathan Zittrain** — co-founders, 1996.
- **Lawrence Lessig** — first Berkman Professor (1997); later moved to Harvard Law full-time; known for Creative Commons and internet-governance work.
- **Yochai Benkler** — Harvard Law professor and Berkman faculty associate; lead author of *Network Propaganda* (2018); earlier work includes *The Wealth of Networks* (2006).
- **Robert Faris** — research director; co-author of *Network Propaganda*.
- **Hal Roberts** — senior researcher; co-author of *Network Propaganda*; lead architect of the Media Cloud dataset methodology.

## Activities

- Produces academic research on internet law, governance, security, and political communication.
- Co-developed and hosted [[Media Cloud]]; now part of a broader consortium.
- Hosts fellows from government, law, journalism, and technology.
- Publishes the Internet Monitor report on global internet censorship and surveillance.
- Organized workshops and conferences that shaped internet governance debates.

## Timeline

- **1996** — Founded as Center on Law and Technology by Nesson and Zittrain at Harvard Law School.
- **1997** — Berkman family endowment; renamed Berkman Center; Lawrence Lessig joins as first Berkman Professor.
- **1998** — Renamed "Berkman Center for Internet & Society at Harvard Law School."
- **2008-05-15** — Elevated to Harvard-wide interfaculty initiative.
- **2009** — [[Media Cloud]] launched in partnership with MIT.
- **2016-07-05** — Renamed Berkman Klein Center following $15 million gift from Michael R. Klein.
- **2018** — *Network Propaganda* published; BKC research establishes asymmetric polarization finding.
