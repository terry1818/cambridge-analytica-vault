# GiveHim15

## Summary

GiveHim15 (styled "Give Him 15") is a daily prophetic devotional and mobilization platform created in 2015 by [[Dutch Sheets]], a prominent figure in the charismatic-prophetic movement adjacent to the [[New Apostolic Reformation]]. The concept asks believers to "give" fifteen minutes a day to prayer as a continual "appeal to heaven" for the nation; the name derives from a prophetic-worship refrain ("Will you give me fifteen?"). Delivered as a daily video, podcast, written devotional, mobile app, and YouTube channel, GiveHim15 functions as both a religious-devotional product and a political-mobilization instrument. It matters to this vault because after the 2020 presidential election Sheets used the platform as a **real-time mobilization tool** for promoting election-fraud claims, framing the contested result as a spiritual battle and rallying his audience to "decree" Trump's victory. Sheets is also the figure who, around 2013–2015, resurrected and reinterpreted the Revolutionary-era **"Appeal to Heaven" (Pine Tree) flag** as a Christian-nationalist symbol — a flag later flown at the January 6 Capitol attack and, controversially, displayed at the home of Supreme Court Justice Samuel Alito.

## Leadership & key personnel

- **[[Dutch Sheets]]** — founder and daily presenter. Sheets is an author and charismatic minister with more than 45 years in ministry; he is a leading voice in the prophetic-charismatic movement and a key figure in revitalizing the [[Appeal to Heaven flag]] as a contemporary Christian-nationalist symbol. He has been associated with NAR networks and with the post-2020 prophetic election-denial movement.
- GiveHim15 is built around Sheets personally; the platform's authority and reach derive from his prophetic standing.

## Funding & money flows

GiveHim15 operates under Dutch Sheets Ministries and is supported by donations, book sales (the "Give Him 15: An Appeal to Heaven Devotional" volumes), and the broader Sheets ministry apparatus. As a digital devotional platform it has low distribution cost and a wide audience. Detailed financials are not consolidated in reviewed public sources.

## Activities

GiveHim15's core activity is the **daily devotional**: a short video/podcast and written prayer published every day, combining biblical teaching with "prayers and decrees for our nation." The "decree" element is theologically and politically significant — in the charismatic tradition Sheets works within, believers are taught to "decree" outcomes into being, blurring the line between prayer and the assertion of a desired political result as a spiritual fact.

The platform's role in this vault centers on three things:

1. **2020 election-fraud mobilization.** Following the November 2020 election, Sheets used GiveHim15 as a real-time channel to discuss and amplify election-fraud claims, framing Joe Biden's win as illegitimate and as the work of demonic forces, and calling his audience to pray and "decree" a reversal. This made GiveHim15 part of the prophetic-charismatic engine of post-2020 election denial — the religious counterpart to the secular "Stop the Steal" effort, supplying a theological rationale (genuine prophecy cannot fail; therefore the result must be fraud) for rejecting the outcome.

2. **The Appeal to Heaven flag.** Around 2013–2015 Sheets resurrected the Revolutionary-era Pine Tree "An Appeal to Heaven" flag and reinterpreted it as a symbol of God's plan to "restore" the United States to a Christian foundation. Through GiveHim15 and his wider ministry he promoted the flag, which spread through NAR and Christian-nationalist networks. The flag flew at the January 6, 2021 Capitol attack alongside other right-wing banners, and in 2024 reporting revealed it had been displayed at a vacation home of Supreme Court Justice Samuel Alito — making Sheets's symbol a national controversy about the entanglement of Christian nationalism and the judiciary.

3. **Ongoing mobilization.** GiveHim15 continues as a daily platform that frames U.S. politics in spiritual-warfare terms, sustaining a mobilized prophetic-charismatic audience.

In the vault's analytical frame, GiveHim15 is **mobilization infrastructure disguised as devotion**: a low-cost, high-frequency channel that reaches a committed audience daily and that demonstrably functioned as a tool for election denial. Built as an "organization/platform" per the brief, it is best understood as the operational mouthpiece of Dutch Sheets Ministries.
