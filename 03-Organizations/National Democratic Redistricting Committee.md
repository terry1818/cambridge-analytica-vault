# National Democratic Redistricting Committee

## Summary

The National Democratic Redistricting Committee (NDRC) is a Democratic Party-aligned organization founded in 2016 by former U.S. Attorney General [[Eric Holder]] with the backing of former President [[Barack Obama]], and publicly launched on January 12, 2017. Its stated purpose is to end partisan gerrymandering and create fairer electoral maps. The NDRC was created as a direct response to the Republican REDMAP project — the GOP's successful post-2010 effort to win state legislatures and control the subsequent decade of map-drawing. Holder framed the NDRC as "the first-ever strategic hub for fair redistricting," pursuing a multi-pronged strategy of litigation, down-ballot political investment, and ballot initiatives for independent redistricting commissions. The organization operates through affiliated entities — including the National Redistricting Foundation (a 501(c)(3)) and action-fund arms — that allow it to combine charitable, advocacy, and political activity. In this vault, the NDRC is the principal organized counterweight to the structural minority-rule mechanism analyzed in [[Redistricting]].

## Identity

- **Legal name(s):** National Democratic Redistricting Committee; affiliated National Redistricting Foundation and action funds.
- **Other names / fronts:** commonly "NDRC."
- **Founded:** registered with the IRS August 15, 2016; publicly launched January 12, 2017.
- **Parent / owner:** none; aligned with the Democratic Party.
- **Registered jurisdiction:** United States (Washington, D.C.).

## Leadership & key personnel

- [[Eric Holder]] — Chairman; 82nd U.S. Attorney General; public face and chief strategist.
- [[Barack Obama]] — co-founder and prominent supporter; lent fundraising and political weight.
- [[Kelly Ward Burton]] — President; runs day-to-day operations.
- Early founding partners reportedly included Nancy Pelosi and Terry McAuliffe.

## Funding & money flows

The NDRC and its affiliates are funded by Democratic donors and aligned foundations; its multi-entity structure (a 501(c)(3) foundation alongside political arms) lets it accept charitable and political money for different lines of work. Precise donor breakdowns are partially disclosed through tax filings of the constituent entities.

## Activities

Holder articulated a three-part "going into the states" strategy: (1) **litigation** — challenging gerrymandered maps in state and federal courts; (2) **political investment** — funding down-ballot Democratic candidates so the party holds more seats at the table when maps are drawn; and (3) **ballot initiatives** — promoting independent redistricting commissions through direct democracy where state law allows. The NDRC backed or supported litigation in multiple states; after the 2020 census cycle, it claimed substantial credit for fairer congressional maps, with *The New York Times* describing the 2022 national map as "the fairest in 40 years." In 2025–2026, amid a wave of mid-decade Republican re-gerrymandering and a Supreme Court ruling threatening Voting Rights Act protections, the NDRC publicly committed to contesting the loss of majority-minority opportunity districts.

## Timeline

- `2010-2011` — Republican REDMAP project succeeds; GOP controls most post-census maps.
- `2016-08-15` — NDRC registers with the IRS.
- `2017-01-12` — NDRC publicly launches with Holder as chair and Obama's backing.
- `2019` — *Rucho v. Common Cause* closes federal courts to partisan-gerrymandering claims, pushing NDRC strategy toward state courts and initiatives.
- `2021-2022` — NDRC active across the post-2020-census redistricting cycle; claims credit for fairer maps.
- `2025-2026` — NDRC responds to mid-decade GOP re-gerrymandering and a SCOTUS Voting Rights Act ruling; warns of 12–19 at-risk Southern majority-minority seats.

## Successor / related entities

- National Redistricting Foundation — affiliated 501(c)(3) handling litigation and education work.
- [[All On The Line]] — affiliated grassroots-organizing campaign.

## Controversies / investigations

- [[Rucho v. Common Cause]] — the 2019 ruling that reshaped NDRC strategy by removing the federal partisan-gerrymandering remedy.
- Critics on the right characterize the NDRC as a partisan operation pursuing Democratic advantage under a "fair maps" label; the NDRC responds that its standard is partisan symmetry, not Democratic gain. `#claim/disputed`
