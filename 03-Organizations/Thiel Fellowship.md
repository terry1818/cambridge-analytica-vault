## Summary

The Thiel Fellowship, founded in 2011 by [[Peter Thiel]] and administered through the Thiel Foundation, pays selected young people under the age of 22 grants of up to $100,000 (raised to $250,000 for the class of 2026) to pursue entrepreneurial projects instead of attending or completing college. Originally called "20 Under 20," the program is explicitly ideological: Thiel has argued publicly that higher education is a credentialism racket and a bubble — an overpriced certification mechanism that serves institutional gatekeepers rather than genuine learning or value creation. The Fellowship is the operational expression of that thesis, and a talent-pipeline for the Thiel venture ecosystem.

Notable alumni include Figma CEO Dylan Field and Scale AI co-founder Lucy Guo. Total business value generated by fellows has been estimated at over $100 billion.

## Identity

The Fellowship selects approximately 20–30 fellows per year. Fellows receive the grant and access to the Thiel network (mentors, co-investors, introductions) for a two-year period. They are expected to work full-time on their projects during this period rather than attend university; a small minority finished their degrees before or after participating.

The program doubles as a demonstration of the Thiel thesis: that the most talented young people are better served building companies than sitting in classrooms. This frames the Fellowship as an ideological project against the university system as much as a venture development program.

## Leadership & key personnel

- **[[Peter Thiel]]**: founder; primary funder; the fellowship embodies his personal philosophy on education and credentialism.
- **Danielle Strachman**: program director in early years; managed fellow selection and support.
- **[[Blake Masters]]**: ran the Thiel Foundation as president, with oversight of Fellowship operations.

## Funding & money flows

Funded through the Thiel Foundation (a 501(c)(3) private foundation). Grant amounts: originally $100,000 per fellow; raised to $250,000 for the class of 2026. Total program cost per year: roughly $2–5M depending on cohort size.

## Activities

- Annual cohort selection (approximately 20–30 fellows).
- Two-year grant and mentorship period.
- Network of 200+ alumni across roughly a decade of cohorts.
- Networking events, community building within the Thiel ecosystem.

## Controversies / investigations

- Critics argue the program is elitist: it primarily benefits already-privileged young people who would succeed with or without the grant, and the "skip college" message is harmful for those without safety nets.
- The program's critics note that Thiel himself has degrees from Stanford (BA and JD); the "college is a trap" message is delivered by someone who benefited from elite credentialism.
- Inside Higher Ed (2012) noted that the fellowship did not technically require dropping out — some fellows had already graduated or enrolled after the program.

## Open questions

- Does the program demonstrably improve outcomes beyond what recipients would have achieved independently?
- Is the Fellowship a meaningful talent pipeline into the broader Thiel political network ([[Tech Right]]) or primarily a venture portfolio tool?
