# Agentura.ru — The Soldatov/Borogan Intelligence-Watchdog Project

## Summary

Agentura.ru is an independent Russian investigative project founded in September 2000 by journalists Andrei Soldatov and Irina Borogan to scrutinize Russia's intelligence and security services — principally the FSB, SVR, GRU, and their predecessor the KGB. It is the primary research platform behind the authors' two major books: *The New Nobility* (2010), on FSB expansion under Putin, and *The Red Web* (2015), the definitive account of Russian internet surveillance and the [[SORM]] system. Agentura.ru represents a rare example of sustained, open-source intelligence watchdog work inside Russia, operating for two decades before the full crackdown that followed the 2022 invasion of Ukraine.

## Identity

The site launched in the month after [[Vladimir Putin]] — himself a former FSB director — assumed the Russian presidency, a timing the founders have described as deliberate. Its stated mandate was to track the growing role of the FSB and siloviki (security-state personnel) in Russian political and economic life. For most of its existence Agentura.ru operated as a bilingual platform (Russian and English) publishing document analyses, organizational charts of security services, and investigative articles. It drew on open-source materials, court records, leaked documents, and interviews with current and former officers.

## Leadership & Key Personnel

**Andrei Soldatov** (born 1975) is a Moscow-based investigative journalist who has covered security services and terrorism since 1999. He was a columnist for Novaya Gazeta and contributed to major international outlets including The Guardian, the New York Times, and Foreign Affairs. On 6 June 2022, the Russian Ministry of Internal Affairs placed him on the federal wanted list. He has since operated from exile.

**Irina Borogan** is deputy editor and co-founder. She has co-authored all major Agentura.ru investigations and both books with Soldatov. She is also listed as a research fellow at the Center for European Policy Analysis (CEPA).

## Funding & Money Flows

Agentura.ru appears to have operated primarily on journalism revenue, book advances, and speaker fees. No major institutional funder has been publicly disclosed. Both founders have been fellows or contributors at CEPA (a Washington-based think tank), which has provided some institutional support post-2022. The Russian government's "foreign agent" apparatus has not (as of the research date) formally designated Agentura.ru, though Soldatov's wanted status functions as a de facto suppression mechanism.

## Activities

- **Database publication**: Maintains organizational databases of Russian security services, including personnel, structure, and unit histories.
- **SORM documentation**: Published the most detailed public account of [[SORM]] hardware mandates and FSB access protocols, including analysis of leaked technical specifications.
- **The New Nobility (2010)**: First major English-language account of FSB expansion under Putin; coined the phrase "new nobility" for the siloviki class.
- **The Red Web (2015)**: Traced Russian internet surveillance from Soviet origins through the internet era; won widespread critical recognition and was used in Congressional testimony on Russian surveillance.
- **Ukraine war reporting**: Soldatov has published extensively since 2022 on FSB operational failures in Ukraine and on internal Kremlin intelligence structures.

## Controversies / Investigations

No significant disputes about Agentura.ru's factual record have been documented by independent reviewers. The Russian security establishment has never publicly rebutted the site's core findings. Soldatov's wanted status is the primary form of official response.
