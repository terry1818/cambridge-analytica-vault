# National Christian Foundation

## Summary

The National Christian Foundation (NCF), legally the National Christian Charitable Foundation, Inc., is the largest Christian donor-advised-fund (DAF) provider in the United States and one of the largest DAF sponsors of any kind — by 2017 revenue it ranked fourth-largest among all U.S. donor-advised funds. Founded in 1982 in Georgia by financial advisers Larry Burkett, Ron Blue, and Terry Parker, NCF allows wealthy Christian donors to place assets into charitable accounts and direct grants to causes of their choice. Its significance to this vault is structural: NCF is the principal **financial conduit** of the modern religious right. It moves hundreds of millions of dollars a year — grants of roughly $960 million in a single recent year — and a substantial share flows to right-wing advocacy, anti-abortion, and anti-LGBTQ organizations. Crucially, the DAF structure allows donors to give **anonymously**: the grant appears as coming from "the National Christian Foundation," shielding the actual donor's name. NCF is therefore the patron-wing plumbing through which much of the theocratic and Christian-nationalist movement is quietly financed.

## Funding & money flows

NCF is itself a money-flow institution. Its model:

- **Inflow:** Wealthy Christian donors contribute cash, appreciated stock, business interests, real estate, and other assets into NCF "Giving Fund" accounts (donor-advised funds). Donors receive an immediate tax deduction.
- **Holding:** Assets sit in the donor-advised fund, invested, until the donor recommends grants.
- **Outflow:** NCF disburses grants to charities the donor designates. NCF reported grants of roughly **$960 million** in a single recent year and cumulative giving in the tens of billions over its history.

The key feature is **anonymity**: because the legal grantmaker of record is NCF, not the original donor, a wealthy family can fund a controversial organization without their name appearing on its donor rolls. Inside Philanthropy described NCF as "probably the single biggest source of money fueling the pro-life and anti-LGBT movements over the past 15 years." A 2019 Sludge investigation, drawing on tax filings, found that between 2015 and 2017 NCF distributed **$56.1 million** to 23 organizations that the Southern Poly Law Center had designated as hate groups — mostly anti-LGBTQ, some anti-immigrant or anti-Muslim. (NCF disputes the SPLC's "hate group" designations as politically motivated; the dollar flows themselves are documented from tax records.)

## Activities

NCF's core activity is operating donor-advised funds and related charitable-giving vehicles for Christian donors. Functionally, it serves three roles in the religious-right ecosystem:

1. **Aggregation and scale.** By pooling many donors, NCF can move very large sums and gives a unified, well-resourced financial backbone to a movement that would otherwise depend on scattered individual giving.

2. **Anonymity / reputational shielding.** The DAF structure lets donors fund litigation firms, advocacy groups, and Christian-nationalist organizations without public attribution — a meaningful asset for businesspeople wary of consumer or employee backlash.

3. **Conduit to the broader movement.** Candid's Foundation Funding for U.S. Democracy database recorded hundreds of NCF grants to public-policy organizations, advocacy groups, and think tanks. NCF money has reached religious-liberty litigation firms, anti-abortion organizations, and Christian-nationalist policy infrastructure — overlapping with the donor "patron wing" that this vault tracks in the constitutional-capture and theocratic clusters.

NCF's defenders emphasize that the bulk of its grantmaking also funds uncontroversial churches, missions, and humanitarian charities — which is true, and is part of what makes the structure effective: politically charged giving is embedded within a much larger stream of ordinary religious philanthropy.
