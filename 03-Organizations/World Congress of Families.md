## Summary

The World Congress of Families (WCF) is an international anti-LGBTQ and anti-abortion umbrella network co-founded in 1997 in Moscow by American academic Allan Carlson and Russian intellectuals Anatoly Antonov and Viktor Medkov. WCF defines the "natural family" as "the union of a man and a woman in a lifelong covenant of marriage" and coordinates a global network of organizations opposing LGBTQ rights and reproductive freedom. Katherine Stewart's *The Power Worshippers* (2020) identifies WCF as a key node in the international infrastructure of the Christian nationalist movement, documenting its role in exporting US Religious Right legal, political, and rhetorical frameworks to Europe, Africa, and Latin America.

The Human Rights Campaign has called WCF "one of the most influential groups in America promoting and coordinating the exportation of anti-LGBT bigotry, ideology, and legislation abroad." Twenty-eight American Christian right organizations associated with WCF spent at least $280 million in dark money promoting anti-LGBTQ and anti-abortion campaigns across five continents between 2007 and approximately 2019.

## Identity

WCF's founding in Russia is significant: the American-Russian co-founding created an early and structurally embedded channel between the US Religious Right and Russian conservative-nationalist actors — a channel that became politically significant in the 2010s as Russia promoted its own anti-LGBTQ legislation (the 2013 "propaganda law") and as WCF conferences were held in Moscow (2014) and other Russian-allied cities.

WCF operates primarily as a convening and networking organization, hosting biennial international conferences that bring together anti-LGBTQ activists, legislators, clergy, and funders from across the globe. The organization has been linked to anti-LGBTQ legislation in Uganda, Russia, Nigeria, and elsewhere.

## Leadership & Key Personnel

- **Allan Carlson**: Reagan appointee to the National Commission on Children; founded the Howard Center for Family, Religion and Society as the institutional home for WCF
- **Brian Brown**: Current president of the International Organization for the Family (IOF, the renamed Howard Center); also president of the National Organization for Marriage
- **Anatoly Antonov / Viktor Medkov**: Russian co-founders; academics at Moscow State University

## Funding & Money Flows

WCF is operated through the International Organization for the Family (IOF). Funding flows through the broader dark-money infrastructure of the US Christian right. The HRC report identifies 28 partner organizations that collectively spent $280M+ internationally. Specific WCF budget figures are not consistently publicly available. Key US donor networks connected to WCF partners include those associated with the [[Council for National Policy]].

## Activities

- Biennial international congresses (held in major cities across Europe, North America, Africa, Latin America)
- Networking anti-LGBTQ legislative and legal actors internationally
- Publishing "natural family" policy frameworks
- Providing a venue for coordination between US Christian right and Russian/Eastern European conservative actors
- Supporting anti-LGBTQ legislation in Uganda, Russia, Nigeria, and Georgia
