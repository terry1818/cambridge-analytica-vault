## Summary

The Tambov Gang (Tambovskaya Bratva) is a Russian organized crime group founded in Leningrad circa 1987–1988 by Vladimir Kumarin and associates from Tambov Oblast. Through the 1990s it became one of the most powerful criminal organizations in St. Petersburg, controlling protection rackets, the fuel trade, port operations, real estate, and liquor distribution. At the peak of his power, Kumarin was nicknamed the "night governor of St. Petersburg." Catherine Belton's *Putin's People* (2020) and investigative journalists including RFE/RL document the gang's alleged entanglement with the circle of [[Vladimir Putin]] during his years as St. Petersburg deputy mayor (1991–1996). These accounts are serious allegations documented in investigative journalism; they have not been adjudicated in a Western court. Kumarin was ultimately convicted in Russia in 2019 and sentenced to 24 years.

## Identity

The gang originated as a protection racket operation in the late Soviet period and evolved rapidly through the 1990s chaos into a semi-legitimate criminal-commercial enterprise. By the mid-1990s it had moved heavily into the fuel trade and port logistics. Its rivalry with the Malyshevskaya gang was the defining turf war of St. Petersburg criminal politics in the early 1990s. The gang distinguished itself by transitioning earlier than most Russian criminal groups from pure extortion into the ownership of businesses, real estate, and eventually political influence.

## Leadership & Key Personnel

- **Vladimir Kumarin (Vladimir Barsukov)**: Founder and leader; survived an assassination attempt in 1994 that cost him his right arm; known as the "night governor"; convicted 2019 on charges of organizing a criminal community, sentenced to 24 years.
- **Alexander Malyshev**: Leader of the rival Malyshevskaya gang; their conflict and periodic alliances shaped St. Petersburg criminal geography.

## Activities

- **1988–1993**: Protection rackets; narcotics (briefly); violent consolidation of criminal turf.
- **1993–2000**: Transition to fuel trade; control of the Petersburg Fuel Company (PTK) in partnership with state-connected figures; port logistics; real estate; liquor trade.
- **Private security companies**: Used as fronts for coercive activities and as a mechanism of control over legitimate businesses.

## Alleged Connections to Putin Circle

Investigative accounts allege the following, none of which has been adjudicated in a Western legal proceeding (evidence tier T2–T3):

- Security for the [[Ozero Cooperative]] was allegedly provided by a company run by Tambov-linked figures (Belton, *Putin's People*; European Security journal).
- In 1995–1996, the Petersburg Fuel Company was established with involvement of Putin's deputy mayor office and Kumarin's network, giving the gang a monopoly over city fuel distribution. This is the most-cited specific allegation.
- Putin's office is alleged to have facilitated foreign travel documents for known Tambov associates.
- Viktor Ivanov, a KGB/FSB officer and Putin associate, is documented in multiple sources as having ties to Tambov-linked figures.

All these are journalistic allegations corroborated by multiple sources but not proven in court proceedings targeting Putin.

## Controversies / Investigations

The gang's alleged intersection with the Putin circle is the core controversy for this vault. Key sources disagree on the depth of the relationship: some characterize it as opportunistic co-optation (Putin used the gang's muscle to secure city infrastructure deals), others as a more organic alliance. The honest evidence tier is T2 for the investigative accounts, with specific documented facts (PTK founding, Ozero security) and unproven broader claims.

## Open Questions
