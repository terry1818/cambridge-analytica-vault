# FBI

## Summary

The Federal Bureau of Investigation is the United States' principal domestic intelligence service and a federal criminal investigative body, operating as a component of the [[DOJ|U.S. Department of Justice]]. Created in 1908 and renamed the FBI in 1935, it holds dual law-enforcement and national-security mandates: it investigates federal crimes and is also the lead agency for domestic counterterrorism and counterintelligence. For this vault the FBI matters because it sits at the exact junction of the threads being traced — it is the agency legally permitted to investigate Americans, it has a documented century-long history of surveilling political dissent, and in the 2020s it has become a major buyer of commercial data-broker information and a heavy user of warrantless [[Section 702]] database queries. Its conduct is a recurring test case for whether national-security infrastructure can be turned against domestic political opponents.

## Identity

- **Legal name(s):** Federal Bureau of Investigation (since 1935); previously Bureau of Investigation (1908–1932) and U.S. Bureau of Investigation (1932–1935).
- **Other names / fronts:** none; "the Bureau" colloquially.
- **Founded:** July 26, 1908, by order of Attorney General Charles Bonaparte under Theodore Roosevelt.
- **Parent / owner:** [[DOJ|U.S. Department of Justice]]; the Director reports to the Attorney General and the Director of National Intelligence on intelligence matters.
- **Registered jurisdiction:** United States; headquartered in Washington, D.C.

## Leadership & key personnel

- **J. Edgar Hoover** — Director 1924–1972; built the Bureau into a near-autonomous institution and personally directed its political-surveillance programs.
- **Christopher Wray** — Director 2017–2025; oversaw the agency during the period of expanding data-broker procurement and the 2024 [[Section 702]] reauthorization fight.
- **Kash Patel** — Director from 2025; in a 2025–2026 Senate hearing he declined Senator [[Ron Wyden]]'s request to commit that the FBI would stop buying Americans' cell-phone location data, stating the Bureau buys commercially available information "consistent with the Constitution."

## Funding & money flows

The FBI is funded through annual congressional appropriations to the Department of Justice (its budget is roughly $11 billion in the mid-2020s). Relevant to this vault, the FBI has known contracts for tools that rely on cell-phone location information sourced from commercial [[data broker|data brokers]] — placing it, alongside the [[DHS]] and [[ICE]], inside the documented "data-broker loophole" pipeline.

## Activities

The FBI's surveillance-relevant activities fall into several documented categories. (1) **Political-surveillance history:** under Hoover the Bureau ran COINTELPRO (1956–1971), opening close to a million intelligence investigations on Americans and conducting infiltration, illegal wiretaps, burglaries, forged documents, and disinformation against civil-rights, anti-war, and labor groups; a 1976 Senate committee (the Church Committee) called it a "sophisticated vigilante program" aimed at the First Amendment. (2) **Section 702 backdoor searches:** the FBI routinely searches [[NSA]]-collected [[Section 702]] data for the communications of Americans without a warrant; documented targets have included Black Lives Matter protesters, journalists, members of Congress, and roughly 19,000 donors to a single congressional campaign. (3) **Data-broker purchases:** the Bureau buys commercially available data — including location data — that it would otherwise need legal process to obtain, exploiting the "data-broker loophole." (4) **Data integration:** FBI records feed cross-agency platforms; the [[Palantir]]-built ImmigrationOS system for [[ICE]] incorporates FBI data.

A 2026 disclosure cited in congressional debate reported that FBI warrantless queries touching political, religious, and media organizations rose sharply — from 227 in 2024 to 839 in 2025 — a pattern privacy advocates read as the warrantless-surveillance apparatus turning toward domestic political targets.

## Successor / related entities

- [[DOJ|U.S. Department of Justice]] — parent department.
- [[DHS]] — partner agency; overlapping domestic-security mandate and shared data-broker contractors.
- [[ICE]] — receives FBI data via cross-agency platforms including ImmigrationOS.
- [[NSA]] — source of the [[Section 702]] data the FBI queries.
- [[Palantir]] — contractor whose platforms integrate FBI records.

## Controversies / investigations

- **COINTELPRO** — the defining historical abuse; established that the FBI will, absent oversight, surveil and disrupt lawful political activity.
- **Section 702 backdoor searches** — ongoing; the central modern controversy over warrantless access to Americans' communications.
- **Data-broker procurement** — the FBI's refusal to forgo purchased location data is a live 2025–2026 dispute.
