# Affinity Partners

Private equity fund founded by [[Jared Kushner]] in 2021, six months after leaving the Trump White House.

## Capital base

-  from [[Saudi Public Investment Fund]] (June 2021)
-  from Qatar Investment Authority
-  from UAE Mubadala
- Additional commitments from Terrah Holdings and other Gulf-state-adjacent vehicles
- Total publicly reported initial commitments: ~, predominantly Gulf sovereign wealth

## PIF investment committee objection (Tier 1)

- Reported in [[New York Times]] (April 2022): PIF's screening committee unanimously recommended AGAINST the  investment in Kushner's fund, citing:
  - Inexperience (Kushner had no PE track record)
  - Above-market fees Affinity proposed (1.25% management, 20% carry)
  - "Public relations" risks
- The PIF board overrode the committee. [[MBS]] is presumed to have been the decision-maker

## Significance for vault

Affinity is the cleanest single documented case of post-presidency Gulf-state direct payment to a senior Trump-administration figure for non-conventional-investment reasons. The investment-committee override is the smoking-gun-quality evidence that the transaction was political rather than financial.

The structural counterpart is the [[LIV Golf - PGA Tour deal]] funding Trump properties directly.
