## Summary

Media Cloud is an open-source, open-data platform designed to enable quantitative analysis of online news media. It was developed beginning in 2009 as a joint project of the [[Berkman Klein Center]] for Internet & Society at Harvard University and the Center for Civic Media at MIT. The platform aggregates content from more than 50,000 news sources in over 20 languages, enabling researchers to track which topics receive coverage, how media outlets link to one another, and how stories spread across the media ecosystem.

Media Cloud served as the primary empirical infrastructure for *Network Propaganda* (2018) by Yochai Benkler, Robert Faris, and Hal Roberts. Their analysis of millions of news articles and social-media sharing patterns — the data foundation for the [[Asymmetric polarization]] finding — was built on Media Cloud's aggregation and hyperlinking datasets.

## Identity

Media Cloud is unusual in the research-infrastructure landscape because it is genuinely open-source and open-data: researchers outside Harvard and MIT can access its datasets and tools. This makes it a public-interest research utility rather than a proprietary platform. It has been used in studies of media coverage of climate change, immigration, and political campaigns in multiple countries.

As of approximately 2020, the project's administration was transferred to a consortium that includes the Media Ecosystems Analysis Group, the University of Massachusetts Amherst, and Northeastern University.

## Leadership & Key Personnel

Media Cloud was co-founded by Ethan Zuckerman (MIT Media Lab) and has been associated with multiple Berkman Klein Center researchers. For the *Network Propaganda* project, the key investigators were Benkler, Faris, and Roberts, all based at Berkman Klein.

## Funding & Money Flows

Funded by a combination of academic grants (including from the Knight Foundation, the MacArthur Foundation, and similar journalism and internet-freedom funders) and Harvard and MIT institutional support. Specific funder disclosure is limited in publicly available sources.

## Activities

- Aggregates and indexes news content from 50,000+ sources globally.
- Provides hyperlink analysis tools enabling mapping of how outlets cite one another.
- Tracks social media sharing of news content (Twitter, Facebook, etc.) historically.
- Supports academic research on media ecosystem structure, disinformation spread, and political communication.
- Powers the empirical findings in *Network Propaganda*, including the asymmetric polarization mapping.

## Timeline

- **2009-03** — Media Cloud launched as a joint Berkman/MIT project.
- **2017-03** — Berkman Klein hosts public introduction luncheon for Media Cloud researchers.
- **2018** — *Network Propaganda* publishes; Media Cloud's hyperlink analysis dataset is central to findings.
- **~2020** — Administration transitions to a consortium including UMass Amherst and Northeastern.
