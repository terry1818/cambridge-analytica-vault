# NSO Group

NSO Group is the principal Israeli commercial spyware vendor and producer of the Pegasus zero-click mobile-device exploitation toolkit. NSO is the most operationally consequential single commercial-spyware company globally and the prototypical [[Unit 8200]] commercial-veteran spinout.

## Pegasus capability

Pegasus is a zero-click exploit chain capable of fully compromising iOS and Android devices without user interaction. Once deployed, Pegasus provides full read access to messages (including end-to-end-encrypted messages decrypted on-device), calls, microphone, camera, location, photos, and stored documents. The capability is operationally distinct from typical commercial surveillance products because the zero-click delivery eliminates the user-action requirement that legitimate-use security tools depend on for defense.

## Documented deployments

Pegasus has been documented in deployment against:

- Jamal Khashoggi's inner circle preceding his October 2018 Saudi-state murder
- Princess Latifa al-Maktoum during her 2018 escape attempt from UAE
- [[Carole Cadwalladr]]-adjacent journalists during CA-era reporting
- Multiple heads of state including French President Macron's phone
- Catalan independence movement leadership
- Mexican journalists and activists at scale
- Spanish opposition politicians
- Hundreds of additional documented civil-society targets globally

The Citizen Lab (University of Toronto Munk School research centre) has been the principal academic-tier source on Pegasus deployments.

## US Commerce Department Entity List 2021

NSO Group was added to the US Commerce Department Entity List in November 2021, effectively restricting US commercial entities from doing business with NSO. The decision was operationally consequential: it cut NSO off from US capital markets and US technology supply chains, producing substantial financial pressure on the firm.

The 2021 Entity List action also added [[Candiru]] and one other Israeli vendor, and was the first time the US government had taken such action against commercial spyware vendors as a regulatory category.

## Trump 2.0 era regulatory positioning

The Trump 2.0 administration's posture toward NSO and the broader commercial spyware sector has been a continuing question. Reports through 2025-2026 have documented partial easing of the Entity List restrictions in some commercial categories, substantial NSO-adjacent commercial engagement with US PE firms, and the broader pattern of post-Trump 2.0 reduction in commercial-spyware regulatory pressure.

## Unit 8200 pipeline

NSO Group founders Shalev Hulio and Omri Lavie are Unit 8200 veterans (Israeli IDF SIGINT/cyber unit). The Unit 8200 talent pipeline is the principal source of NSO engineering talent and is the structural reason NSO can build offensive-cyber capability at scale. The pipeline pattern is documented across multiple Israeli commercial-spyware firms ([[Candiru]], [[Paragon Solutions]], [[Quadream]]).
