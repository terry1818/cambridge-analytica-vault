# Internet Research Agency (IRA)

## Summary

Russian state-linked organization based in Saint Petersburg, founded in 2013, that ran an **industrial-scale influence operation against the 2016 US presidential election** and continues operating into 2026. Funded by **[[Yevgeny Prigozhin]]** (later Wagner Group leader; killed August 2023 after the Wagner mutiny). Indicted by [[Mueller Investigation]] in February 2018 (United States v. Internet Research Agency).

## Mueller indictment findings

The February 16, 2018 Mueller indictment charged the IRA, two affiliated companies, and 13 Russian nationals with:

- Operating ~80+ employees in St. Petersburg "Translator" department devoted to US operations
- Building hundreds of fake American social media accounts impersonating US activists
- Spending **millions of dollars** on US election operations
- Reaching **126 million Facebook users** with IRA-generated content (per Facebook 2017 disclosure)
- Spending $100,000+ on Facebook ads (publicly known; likely understated)
- Organizing real-world rallies in US through fake online personas (some attendees attended unwittingly)
- Targeting both left and right voters with divisive content

## Relationship to CA

The IRA ran in **parallel** to CA during the 2016 election cycle. Mueller and Senate Intel Vol 5 both found:

- No documented direct coordination between IRA and CA
- Both were active in the same information environment
- IRA's persona network amplified Trump-favorable content; CA targeted real-user microtargeting; both aligned in net effect
- Some Russian-IP infrastructure overlap with [[Aleksandr Kogan]] / [[Cambridge Psychometrics Centre]] (per ICO finding)

## Post-2016 operations

- 2018-onwards: continued operations against US midterms, EU elections, Ukrainian war information operations
- 2022-onwards: heavily integrated with Wagner Group / Prigozhin's broader operations
- 2023-08: Prigozhin death after mutiny; IRA reportedly restructured
- 2024-2026: operations continue under new leadership; "Doppelganger" operation likely overlap
