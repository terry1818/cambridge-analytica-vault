# Cellebrite

Cellebrite is an Israeli digital-forensics company, founded 1999, headquartered in Petah Tikva. Cellebrite is the principal commercial supplier of mobile-device-extraction tools (UFED - Universal Forensic Extraction Device) to US law enforcement and to law-enforcement agencies globally.

## Operational scope

Cellebrite's UFED product line provides:

- Mobile-device forensic extraction across iOS, Android, and legacy device platforms
- Cloud-data extraction from major commercial cloud providers
- Encryption-bypass capabilities for various device-encryption schemes
- Forensic analysis software that processes the extracted data

The customer base is substantial: ICE, FBI, Secret Service, DEA, ATF, US Marshals, most major US state and local law-enforcement agencies, and equivalent agencies globally. ICE specifically has been a substantial Cellebrite customer with documented Cellebrite UFED deployment in immigration-enforcement operations.

## San Bernardino iPhone

Cellebrite was widely reported as the vendor that unlocked the San Bernardino shooter's iPhone in 2016 after Apple's refusal to assist the FBI. The reporting was never officially confirmed but the operational pattern aligns with Cellebrite's documented capability set.

## Trump 2.0 era operational expansion

The Trump 2.0 administration's substantial ICE-enforcement expansion has produced substantial Cellebrite contracting expansion. Documented ICE-Cellebrite procurement through 2025 includes substantial UFED licensing and forensic-extraction-service contracts across multiple ICE field offices.

## Civil-liberties and accountability concerns

Cellebrite UFED deployment has produced sustained civil-liberties concerns documented by [[ACLU]], [[Electronic Frontier Foundation]], [[Citizen Lab]], and adjacent civil-liberties organisations. The principal concerns: lack of judicial oversight on UFED deployment in most US jurisdictions; export to foreign law-enforcement agencies with substantial human-rights concerns; the broader privacy implications of cloud-data extraction that goes beyond device-resident data.

## Connection to broader vault network

Cellebrite is the principal Unit-8200-pipeline firm whose operational footprint sits in domestic US law-enforcement rather than in foreign-state targeting. The firm's US-law-enforcement-customer profile makes it operationally distinct from [[NSO Group]] (foreign-state spyware), [[Paragon Solutions]] (state-aligned spyware), or [[Candiru]] (state-spyware). Cellebrite's vault function is principally as the law-enforcement-side analog to the broader Israeli commercial-intelligence sector.
