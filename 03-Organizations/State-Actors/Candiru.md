# Candiru

Candiru (also known under successor brand names including Saito Tech, Grindavik Solutions, and Taveta) is an Israeli commercial spyware vendor founded 2014. Candiru is the principal commercial competitor to [[NSO Group]] in the offensive-cyber commercial market, with comparable capability profile and substantially overlapping customer base.

## Capability and operational profile

Candiru's principal product is the DevilsTongue spyware toolkit, capable of full device compromise on Windows, macOS, iOS, and Android. The capability is operationally similar to NSO Pegasus though with different exploit-chain composition.

Candiru's commercial-positioning has been deliberately less public-facing than NSO's, with substantial use of shell-company customer-facing branding (Saito Tech, Grindavik Solutions, etc.) to maintain operational separation between specific customer engagements.

## US Commerce Entity List 2021

Candiru was added to the US Commerce Entity List in November 2021 alongside [[NSO Group]] (the same Commerce Department action). The Entity List addition has produced operational consequences though Candiru's smaller commercial scale meant the impact was less publicly visible than for NSO.

## Documented deployments

[[Citizen Lab]] research has documented DevilsTongue deployment against:

- Saudi Arabia-targeted civil society figures
- Uzbekistan-targeted journalists and activists
- Spain-targeted independence movement figures
- Multiple additional documented civil-society targeting episodes

## Unit 8200 pipeline

Candiru was founded by Unit 8200 veterans including Eran Cahana. The founding-team profile is operationally typical of the broader Israeli commercial-spyware sector: high-aptitude Unit 8200 talent productizing offensive-cyber methodology.
