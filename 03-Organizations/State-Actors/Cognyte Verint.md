# Cognyte / Verint

Cognyte is an Israeli surveillance and intelligence-analytics software vendor that was spun out from Verint Systems in 2021. Verint had been a US-listed company with substantial Israeli operations; the 2021 spin-out separated the offensive-cyber-and-surveillance business (Cognyte) from the customer-experience-and-call-centre business (the remaining Verint).

## Commercial scope

Cognyte's product line includes:

- SIGINT-style mass-data collection and processing platforms
- Open-source intelligence (OSINT) collection and analysis tools
- Social-media monitoring and analytical platforms
- Communications-intercept processing platforms
- Cyber-threat intelligence platforms

The customer base is principally state intelligence services, military intelligence units, law-enforcement agencies, and substantial corporate-security customers globally. Customer composition includes substantial Middle Eastern, African, Southeast Asian, and European state-customer engagement.

## Verint historical Israeli operational base

Verint's pre-2021 history includes substantial Israeli operational base with documented [[Unit 8200]] talent-pipeline integration. The post-spin-out Cognyte retains substantially all of the Israeli operational infrastructure; the remaining Verint operates principally as a US-customer-experience software business.

## Documented controversies

Cognyte (and the predecessor Verint operations) have been documented in:

- Surveillance-platform deployment in multiple jurisdictions with substantial human-rights concerns
- Specific documented engagements with state customers under sanctions or restrictions
- The broader pattern of Israeli surveillance-technology export with limited Israeli-government oversight
