# AnyVision / Oosto

AnyVision (rebranded Oosto in 2021) is an Israeli facial-recognition technology firm founded 2015. The company is the principal Israeli facial-recognition vendor with substantial deployment in Israeli military operations and substantial global commercial-and-government customer base.

## West Bank deployment

AnyVision technology has been documented in deployment at West Bank checkpoints and as part of the broader Israeli surveillance architecture in Palestinian areas. The deployment was substantially reported in 2019-2020 (NBC News, Haaretz, adjacent reporting) and produced operational consequences for AnyVision's commercial customer relationships.

Microsoft was an early AnyVision investor and partner. Following the West Bank deployment reporting, Microsoft divested its AnyVision stake in 2020. The divestment was operationally significant as the first major US-tech-company explicit-exit from an Israeli surveillance vendor over Palestinian-rights concerns.

## Oosto rebrand and post-2021 positioning

The 2021 Oosto rebrand was part of broader operational repositioning following the Microsoft divestment and adjacent reputational pressure. The post-rebrand commercial positioning has emphasised access-control and security-application use cases rather than the broader surveillance positioning that produced the earlier controversy.

## Global customer base

Oosto's global customer base includes:

- Substantial commercial customers in retail security, transportation security, and access control
- Government customers in multiple jurisdictions including reported sales to US law enforcement at state and local levels
- Continuing Israeli military and security customer relationships

## Unit 8200 pipeline

AnyVision was founded by Eylon Etshtein and Neil Robertson with substantial Unit-8200-pipeline staff. The company's technical-team composition is operationally typical of the Israeli surveillance-technology sector.
