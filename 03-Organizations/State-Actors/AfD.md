# Alternative für Deutschland (AfD)

Alternative für Deutschland (AfD) is a German far-right political party founded in 2013, originally as a Eurosceptic conservative party and subsequently moved substantially further right. The party has grown across multiple federal-election cycles to become the second-largest party in the 2025 German federal election results.

## US Tech-Right alignment

[[Elon Musk]]'s late-2024 and early-2025 public endorsement of AfD was operationally consequential for both the party and the broader US-European right-wing-coordination landscape. Musk explicitly endorsed AfD as the party Germany should support, conducted a high-visibility live-stream interview with AfD leader Alice Weidel, and used X/Twitter platform amplification to substantially boost AfD's pre-election media presence.

The Musk-AfD alignment is operationally significant because it represents direct US Tech-Right intervention in European electoral politics on behalf of a party that mainstream-European-conservative parties have explicitly refused to coalition with. The Musk endorsement crossed political-norms boundaries that previous US-European right-wing coordination had stayed within.

## Internal structure and the Höcke wing

AfD's internal politics has been characterised by tension between the party's more-moderate "Bürgerlich" wing and the more-explicitly-radical Höcke wing (associated with Thuringia state leader Björn Höcke). The Höcke wing has substantially expanded in influence across recent years; the broader party operational direction has substantially moved toward the Höcke positioning.

The German Federal Office for the Protection of the Constitution has classified portions of AfD as right-extremist; the legal-and-political consequences of that classification have been a continuing thread.
