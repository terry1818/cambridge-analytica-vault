# Israel

The State of Israel appears across this vault primarily through the **Israeli private-intelligence-for-hire ecosystem** — staffed predominantly by **former Israeli military and intelligence operators** but operating commercially for paying clients. This is **structurally distinct** from formal Israeli state operations, but the personnel pipeline and methodological lineage from state intelligence services into commercial operators is documented and analytically significant.

This is the analytical subject of **[[Hypothesis - Israeli intel-for-hire is parallel to Russian state operations]]** — that the Israeli private-intel ecosystem operates at scale comparable to Russian or Chinese state operations but for paying clients rather than under state direction.

## Major operations attributed to Israeli-origin operators

- **[[Psy-Group]]** — Israeli ex-military commercial influence-operations firm; documented MOU with [[Cambridge Analytica]] December 2016; involved in the August 3, 2016 Trump Tower II meeting alongside [[Joel Zamel]]
- **[[Team Jorge]] / [[Tal Hanan]]** — commercial election-manipulation service exposed by the 2023 Forbidden Stories consortium; AIMS persona-network platform; claimed influence in 33+ elections globally
- **[[Black Cube]]** — Israeli ex-Mossad private-intelligence firm; commercial corporate-and-political work
- **[[Wikistrat]]** — Israeli-founded geopolitical analysis firm; military / corporate / political client base

## On the state-vs-private boundary

The Israeli case **breaks the clean state-vs-private distinction** in influence operations. Some patterns observed:

- **Personnel** flow continuously between Israeli state intelligence (IDF Unit 8200, Mossad, Shin Bet) and private commercial operators
- **State-strategic interest** in commercial firms' client lists may be informal but real
- **Regulatory environment** for Israeli private-intel operators is permissive relative to other jurisdictions
- **Formal disavowal** of state coordination with commercial operators is the public posture, but the personnel-and-methodology pipeline is documented
