# Newsmax

## Summary

Newsmax is an American conservative media company founded in 1998 by [[Christopher Ruddy]], built first as a website and conservative-news brand and later expanded into Newsmax TV, a cable network launched in 2014. It rose to prominence after the 2020 presidential election, when it positioned itself to the right of [[Fox News]] and broadcast numerous false conspiracy theories — advanced by [[Donald Trump]], his campaign, and Newsmax hosts — alleging fraud in the 2020 election. The strategy briefly drew an audience of Trump supporters who felt Fox had been insufficiently loyal in calling Arizona for Joe Biden. Newsmax's 2020 election coverage produced defamation suits from voting-technology companies [[Dominion Voting Systems]] and [[Smartmatic]]; the network settled the Smartmatic suit for $40 million in 2024, and after a Delaware judge ruled its Dominion coverage defamatory and false, settled the Dominion suit for $67 million in 2025. In March 2025 Newsmax went public on the New York Stock Exchange (ticker NMAX); its shares, priced at $10, closed the first day above $83. Newsmax matters to this vault as a clear case study in the commercial incentive structure of election-denial media: a smaller outlet captured audience share by being more willing than Fox to broadcast disinformation, then paid substantial legal costs for doing so.

## Funding & money flows

Newsmax was privately held from its 1998 founding until 2025. On June 10, 2024 the company announced plans for an initial public offering; the plan was confirmed in September 2024, and Newsmax debuted on the NYSE on March 31, 2025 under the ticker NMAX. IPO shares priced at $10 closed the first trading day at $83.51 — a single-day gain of over 700%, widely characterized in financial press as a speculative, sentiment-driven surge rather than a fundamentals-driven valuation. The IPO proceeds offset, in part, the costs of the Dominion and Smartmatic settlements.

## Activities

Newsmax operates a conservative cable news channel, website, radio operations, and email publications. Its programming combines news segments with opinion shows aimed at a Trump-aligned audience. Its defining activity for this vault was its post-2020 broadcast of election-fraud conspiracy content — including claims targeting [[Dominion Voting Systems]] and [[Smartmatic]] — which it sustained for roughly a month before beginning to recognize Joe Biden as the duly elected president and, in 2021, issuing an on-air retraction and apology regarding voter-fraud allegations.

## Controversies / investigations

- Ruddy publicly characterized the Dominion settlement as influenced by the judge's perceived political views — a contested framing disputed by the plaintiffs.
