# Google Cloud

## Summary

Google Cloud is the enterprise cloud-computing division of [[Google]] (and thus [[Alphabet Inc.]]). It sells compute, storage, data-analytics, and artificial-intelligence services — including the Gemini AI models — to corporate, government, and military customers. For this vault, Google Cloud matters as the institutional vehicle through which Google reversed its post–[[Project Maven]] retreat from defense work and re-entered the business of supplying core infrastructure to state security apparatuses. It is one of four authorized providers on the Pentagon's $9 billion Joint Warfighting Cloud Capability (JWCC) contract; it co-provides, with [[Amazon AWS]], the Israeli government's $1.2 billion [[Project Nimbus]] cloud — a deal that triggered sustained worker protest and that internal Google documents show executives feared could be linked to human-rights violations. Google Cloud is the point where a surveillance-capitalist data company becomes a defense contractor.

## Leadership & key personnel

- **Thomas Kurian** — CEO of Google Cloud since 2019 (recruited from Oracle); has driven the division's aggressive expansion into government and defense markets
- **Sundar Pichai** — CEO of parent Alphabet/Google; set the strategic posture that re-opened defense work

## Funding & money flows

Google Cloud is internally funded by Alphabet and is now profitable at scale. Its government revenue is a growing line:

- **JWCC:** part of a collective up-to-$9 billion Pentagon cloud vehicle (with [[Amazon AWS]], Microsoft, Oracle); task orders crossed $1 billion by September 2024.
- **[[Project Nimbus]]:** a $1.2 billion contract with the Israeli government, split with Amazon, signed 2021. A draft March 2024 contract showed Google billing the Israeli Ministry of Defense over $1 million for consulting to expand Cloud access to "multiple units."
- Internal Google projections (reported 2025) aimed to add roughly $6 billion in federal contract value between 2025 and 2027.

## Activities

**Government cloud.** By the start of 2025, all four JWCC providers — Google last among them — had reached Impact Level 6 (IL-6) authorization, permitting work with information classified Secret. Google had created a dedicated public-sector battle-network unit in 2022. It signed an AI collaboration agreement with the Department of Defense for unclassified uses by 2024.

**Project Nimbus.** Announced by Israel's Finance Ministry in April 2021, Nimbus provides cloud and AI/ML services to Israeli government agencies including the defense establishment. Reporting by The Intercept (2024–2025) showed the deal does **not** run under Google's standard terms of service, and that Google officials internally warned the services "could be used for, or linked to, the facilitation of human rights violations, including Israeli activity in the West Bank." Google has publicly maintained the contract is bound by standard terms — a claim the documents contradict. `#claim/disputed`

**AI principles reversal.** In early 2025 Google removed the AI-principles language barring AI use for weapons and surveillance, clearing the policy obstacle to deeper defense work.
