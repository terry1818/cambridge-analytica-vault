# X (formerly Twitter)

## Summary

X — the platform launched in 2006 as Twitter — is one of the most politically consequential social-media platforms in the world, and since 2022 a personally controlled instrument of [[Elon Musk]]. Musk bought Twitter for $44 billion in October 2022, took it private, merged it into a holding company renamed X Corp. in April 2023, and in March 2025 folded X into his AI company [[xAI]] in an all-stock deal valuing X at $33 billion. For this vault, X matters because it is the clearest case of a single individual acquiring direct, unmediated control over a global communications platform and then reshaping its rules to his own politics. After the acquisition Musk disbanded much of the content-moderation apparatus, reinstated previously banned accounts including [[Donald Trump]]'s, weakened protections against disinformation, and used the platform himself — as its most-followed account — to amplify [[tech right]] and pro-Trump messaging. X is both a subject of the information-warfare story (a contested arena) and an actor in it.

- **[[Elon Musk]]** — owner since October 2022; executive chairman and CTO; the platform's single most-followed and most-influential account
- **Jack Dorsey** — co-founder; CEO 2008–2010 and 2015–2021
- **Linda Yaccarino** — CEO 2023–2025, recruited to manage advertising relationships; departed as X was absorbed into xAI

## Funding & money flows

Twitter was a publicly traded company until Musk's 2022 buyout. The $44 billion acquisition was financed substantially with debt — roughly $13 billion in loans loaded onto the company — plus Musk's own equity and co-investors. Advertising revenue, historically the platform's core, fell sharply after the acquisition as advertisers reacted to moderation changes and Musk's public conduct. The March 2025 absorption into [[xAI]] valued X at $33 billion (well below the purchase price) and the combined company at ~$80 billion.

## Activities

**Social platform.** X hosts real-time public conversation for hundreds of millions of users — a primary venue for political discourse, breaking news, and, correspondingly, disinformation and coordinated influence operations.

**Content-moderation rollback.** After the acquisition Musk disbanded Twitter's trust-and-safety / content-moderation team, reinstated many banned accounts (including [[Donald Trump]]'s, suspended after January 6), and announced a "content moderation council" that was never actually formed. Researchers and watchdogs documented increases in hate speech and disinformation reach.

**Training data for xAI.** Since the 2025 merger, X's real-time content stream feeds the training and operation of [[xAI]]'s Grok chatbot, which is embedded directly in the platform.

**Personal megaphone.** Musk's own account is the most-followed on the platform; he uses it to drive [[tech right]] narratives, attack opponents, and shape political conversation — an unusual concentration of platform ownership and personal influence.

## Controversies / investigations

- **Content-moderation collapse:** Watchdog and academic research documented increased hate speech and disinformation reach after Musk dismantled trust-and-safety functions. `#claim/established`
- **Election integrity:** X's weakened moderation and Musk's personal amplification of partisan content during the 2024 U.S. cycle raised election-interference and platform-bias concerns.
- **EU Digital Services Act:** X has faced formal EU proceedings over disinformation and content-moderation obligations under the DSA.
- **Advertiser exodus and litigation:** X sued advertisers and a watchdog group; advertising revenue fell sharply post-acquisition.
