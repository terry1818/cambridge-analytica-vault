## Summary

Law and Justice (Prawo i Sprawiedliwość, PiS) is Poland's national-conservative party and the primary European case study for how a democratically elected party can systematically dismantle constitutional guardrails while maintaining electoral competition. Founded in 2001 by the twin brothers [[Jaroslaw Kaczynski]] and Lech Kaczyński, PiS governed Poland in 2005–2007 and decisively in 2015–2023. Its 2015 parliamentary majority was used to subordinate the Constitutional Tribunal, convert the public broadcaster TVP into a government mouthpiece, restrict abortion rights, introduce anti-LGBTQ "ideology-free" zones, and resist EU rule-of-law requirements — producing a prolonged funding dispute with Brussels. Anne Applebaum's [[Source - Applebaum 2020 - Twilight of Democracy]] treats PiS as the primary illustration of how intellectuals and former dissidents can become advocates for illiberal authoritarianism.

## Identity

PiS combines three ideological strands:

1. **Catholic conservatism**: close alignment with the Polish Catholic Church on abortion, LGBTQ rights, education, and historical memory.
2. **Nationalist statism**: economic nationalism, hostility to EU oversight, "Poland first" foreign policy.
3. **Populist anti-elitism**: attacks on liberal judges, independent media, and cosmopolitan intellectuals as an out-of-touch "establishment."

The party's name derives from Lech Kaczyński's anti-corruption campaign as Justice Minister (2000–2001), but in power PiS pursued a conservative cultural agenda at least as prominently as any anti-corruption mission.

## Leadership & Key Personnel

- **Jarosław Kaczyński** — co-founder, chairman since 2003; the party's unchallenged ideological authority; served as PM 2006–2007 but wielded power as de facto ruler behind PM Szydło and PM Morawiecki 2015–2023.
- **Lech Kaczyński** — co-founder; served as President of Poland 2005–2010; died in the Smolensk air disaster, April 10, 2010.
- **Beata Szydło** — PM 2015–2017; implemented initial judicial and media reforms.
- **Mateusz Morawiecki** — PM 2017–2023; managed EU relations during rule-of-law funding dispute.
- **Zbigniew Ziobro** — Justice Minister 2015–2023; lead architect of judicial subordination; led internal "United Right" coalition faction Solidarna Polska.

## Funding & Money Flows

- As a governing party, PiS directed state resources and state-owned enterprise advertising to friendly media; this informal subsidy mechanism was documented by journalists and EU monitors as a quasi-funding channel.
- Received standard public party financing under Polish electoral law.
- Significant EU cohesion funds were frozen by the European Commission from 2021 onward under the rule-of-law conditionality regulation.

## Activities

- **Judicial capture (2015–2023)**: Packed the Constitutional Tribunal with loyalists; created parallel disciplinary chambers in the Supreme Court to control judges; the European Court of Justice ruled multiple Polish judicial reforms incompatible with EU law.
- **Media control**: Converted public broadcaster TVP and Polish Radio into government propaganda vehicles; used regulatory and ownership tools to pressure private regional media; replaced editorial leadership at public outlets with party loyalists.
- **Cultural and social policy**: Effectively banned abortion (Constitutional Tribunal ruling, 2020, resulting in near-total ban); local governments declared "LGBTQ-ideology free zones" (most later revoked under EU and domestic pressure).
- **Social transfers**: "500+" child benefit programme (expanded to "800+") provided significant cash transfers, boosting PiS support among lower-income families and rural voters.

## Successor / Related Entities

- **Solidarna Polska** (Zbigniew Ziobro's faction) — internal coalition partner; split from PiS in 2023.
- **United Right** — broader PiS-led electoral coalition 2015–2023.
- **Fidesz** (Hungary) — closest ideological partner; regular coordination between Orbán and Kaczyński; both parties founding members of the ECR and later associated with Patriots for Europe grouping.

## Controversies / Investigations

- **Rule-of-law proceedings**: Multiple infringement procedures before the CJEU; Poland found in breach of EU law on judicial independence in numerous rulings 2018–2023.
- **Smolensk conspiracy promotion**: Kaczyński and PiS promoted the claim (disputed by multiple investigations) that the 2010 crash was a Russian assassination; fueled nationalist grievance with no credible evidence base. Evidence tier: **disputed/unverified.**
- **TVP propaganda**: EU, Reporters Without Borders, and Freedom House documented systematic conversion of public media to government propaganda.

## Open Questions

- Can the Tusk government reverse PiS-era institutional changes without itself violating constitutional norms (the "illiberal correction" dilemma)?
- How durable is PiS's electoral base without incumbency and state media advantages?
