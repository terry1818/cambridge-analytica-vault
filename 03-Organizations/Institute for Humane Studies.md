# Institute for Humane Studies

## Summary

The Institute for Humane Studies (IHS) is a nonprofit founded in 1961 by economist **F. A. "Baldy" Harper** — a former staff economist of the [[William Volker Fund]] — explicitly as a successor to his Volker Fund work. The IHS inherited the Volker Fund's staff, approach, and strategy: using private money to cultivate sympathetic academics and build long-run intellectual infrastructure for the libertarian movement. Originally based in Menlo Park, California, IHS moved in 1985 to George Mason University in Virginia, where it remains.

The IHS occupies a central place in this vault's "education-capture" and "patron-wing" threads because it is the **academic-pipeline node** of the [[Koch Network]]. [[Charles Koch]] has long been the dominant funder and a board chairman of IHS; Koch-related foundations gave IHS an estimated $34 million between 1986 and 2015 (per Greenpeace data), with the Charles Koch Foundation alone reportedly giving over $23 million since 2005. IHS describes its own work, in effect, as libertarian talent-scouting — identifying promising young scholars, funding their graduate work and research, running fellowship programs, and helping place them into academic careers. Critics, notably UnKoch My Campus, characterize this as a deliberate strategy to seed higher education with scholars aligned to Koch-network priorities — deregulation, limited government, constitutional constraints on majoritarian policy.

## Leadership & key personnel

- **F. A. "Baldy" Harper** — founder, 1961; an economist who had taught at Cornell and worked for the [[William Volker Fund]] before founding IHS as a successor body.
- **[[Charles Koch]]** — longtime board chairman and the institution's principal funder; the Koch relationship defines IHS's modern history.
- IHS leadership has consistently been drawn from the broader [[Koch Network]] of free-market academics and operatives.

## Funding & money flows

- **Source:** Overwhelmingly Koch-network philanthropy. Per Greenpeace USA data, IHS received an estimated **$34.2 million** in combined donations from Koch-related foundations between 1986 and 2015.
- The **Charles Koch Foundation** reportedly gave IHS over **$23 million** since 2005 (a period in which it also gave roughly $9.8 million to the [[Mercatus Center]]).
- IHS in turn disburses money outward as fellowships, scholarships, research grants, and program support to graduate students and faculty across many universities.
- This is the same fund-the-academy model pioneered by the [[William Volker Fund]] — IHS is the structural and personnel continuation of that mid-century model.

## Activities

IHS's core function is **building a libertarian academic pipeline**. Its activities include:

- **Talent identification:** IHS has been described — including in sympathetic accounts — as a libertarian "talent scout," finding promising young libertarians "intent on a leveraged scholarly or intellectual career path."
- **Fellowships and funding:** For more than two decades IHS ran the **Charles G. Koch Summer Fellow Program** and offers graduate scholarships, research grants, and dissertation support.
- **Career placement:** IHS supports scholars through the academic-job pipeline, helping place sympathetic faculty into universities — extending the movement's reach into mainstream academia.
- **Faculty and curriculum support:** Workshops, networking, and grants for free-market-oriented faculty.

The strategic logic is long-horizon: rather than win a single policy fight, IHS aims to produce, over decades, a cohort of credentialed scholars who normalize free-market and limited-government ideas inside the academy — the supply side of the "constitutional capture" intellectual project.

## Successor / related entities

- **[[William Volker Fund]]** — predecessor; IHS is its direct mission and personnel successor via F. A. Harper.
- **[[Mercatus Center]]** — fellow Koch-funded GMU-based institution; the policy-translation counterpart to IHS's academic-pipeline role.
- **[[Koch Network]]** — the broader donor ecosystem that funds and directs IHS.
- **George Mason University** — host institution, itself a major recipient of Koch funding.

## Controversies / investigations

- **UnKoch My Campus** and allied campaigns argue that Koch funding of IHS (and of GMU more broadly) is a strategy to "infiltrate" higher education by manufacturing a pipeline of scholars predisposed to Koch-network policy priorities. IHS and Koch entities frame the funding as ordinary support for scholarship and academic freedom. The funding totals are documented; the characterization of intent is contested. `#claim/disputed`
- Related controversies over Koch influence on faculty hiring at George Mason University (the Mercatus and law-school funding agreements) produced reporting and a partial release of donor agreements after student and faculty pressure.

## Open questions

- How measurable is the IHS pipeline's actual effect on the composition of academic economics, law, and political science faculties?
- What current donor agreements govern IHS, and do any include influence over hiring or curriculum (the central question UnKoch My Campus raised about GMU)?
- The continuity claim — Volker Fund → IHS → broader Koch academic funding — is documented at the personnel level (Harper) but the framing as one continuous "project" remains interpretive.
