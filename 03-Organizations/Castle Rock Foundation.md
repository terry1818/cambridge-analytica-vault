## Summary

Castle Rock Foundation was the ideological giving vehicle of the Coors brewing family, carved out of the broader Adolph Coors Foundation in 1993 to concentrate conservative and free-market grantmaking. Its creation coincided with the Adolph Coors Foundation narrowing its focus to conservation, education, and medical research. Castle Rock funded Heritage Foundation-aligned think tanks, religious-right organizations, and anti-regulatory advocacy groups for nearly two decades before merging back into its parent foundation in 2011. The Coors family's conservative philanthropy long predates Castle Rock: patriarch Joseph Coors provided seed money for the [[Heritage Foundation]] in 1973 and funded Paul Weyrich's Free Congress Foundation.

## Identity

Named after the Coors hometown of Castle Rock, Colorado, the foundation was established with an initial endowment of approximately **$36.6 million** transferred from the Adolph Coors Foundation. Its stated mission was to promote free enterprise, ensure a limited role for government, promote personal responsibility, and uphold traditional Judeo-Christian values. Coors family members sat on the Board of Trustees throughout its existence.

The foundation's existence gained public attention during Pete Coors' unsuccessful 2004 US Senate campaign, when critics noted the tension between the Coors Brewing Company's efforts to market to LGBTQ consumers and Castle Rock's grants to organizations opposing LGBTQ rights.

## Leadership & Key Personnel

The Coors family — particularly Joseph Coors Sr. before his death in 2003, and his sons Jeffrey and Peter — controlled board composition. The broader Coors political network, which included Joseph Coors Sr.'s foundational role in establishing the Heritage Foundation alongside Edwin Feulner and Paul Weyrich in 1973, made Castle Rock part of a multi-decade family commitment to conservative institution-building.

## Funding & Money Flows

Castle Rock grants flowed primarily to:
- **[[Heritage Foundation]]** and affiliated state-level policy organizations
- **Free Congress Foundation** (Paul Weyrich's organization, earlier seeded by Joseph Coors Sr.)
- Anti-regulatory and free-market advocacy organizations
- Organizations promoting traditional religious and family values
- Colorado-based conservative civic infrastructure

Exact grant totals are not publicly reported in consolidated form; the foundation operated as a private foundation with standard IRS Form 990 filings.

## Activities

Castle Rock functioned as a targeted ideological funder rather than a multi-purpose foundation. It was part of the broader network of conservative family foundations — alongside Olin, Bradley, Scaife, and later Koch-affiliated vehicles — that Jane Mayer's *Dark Money* identifies as the philanthropic infrastructure of the modern right. The family's pattern of combining corporate wealth with partisan foundation work became a template examined in [[Weaponizing philanthropy]] scholarship.

## Successor / Related Entities

Castle Rock's grantmaking was absorbed into the reconstituted **Adolph Coors Foundation**, which returned to broader philanthropic activities. The [[Heritage Foundation]] — to which the Coors family was a founding patron — remains the primary institutional legacy of Coors family conservative giving.

## Controversies / Investigations

The foundation's grants to organizations opposing LGBTQ rights created reputational tension with the Coors Brewing Company's parallel marketing to LGBTQ consumers. This contradiction was extensively documented in media coverage of Pete Coors' 2004 Senate run and in progressive boycott campaigns against Coors products.
