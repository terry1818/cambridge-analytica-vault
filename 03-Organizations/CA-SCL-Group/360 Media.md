# 360 Media

## Summary

**Kenyan media and communications company** that partnered with [[Cambridge Analytica]] on the **2013 and 2017 [[Uhuru Kenyatta]] presidential campaigns** in Kenya. Per [[Source Daily Nation 2018-03-20 Kenya|Daily Nation reporting]] following the 2018 CA scandal, 360 Media was founded by:

- **Simon Gicharu** — founder of **Mount Kenya University**
- **Tom Mshindi** — then-**Editor-in-Chief of Nation Media Group**

360 Media was the **local Kenyan operational partner** through which CA's methodology was deployed in support of Kenyatta's Jubilee Party campaigns. The partnership is documented in **[[Source HindsightFiles 2020]]** Kenya-material disclosures and in [[Source Wikipedia - Cambridge Analytica]].

## Key facts

- **Country:** Kenya
- **Founders:** Simon Gicharu (Mount Kenya University founder), Tom Mshindi (then-Editor-in-Chief of Nation Media Group)
- **CA partnership era:** 2013 (Kenyatta's first campaign) and 2017 (Kenyatta's re-election)
- **Documented role:** local-market operational partner for CA's targeting and content-production work
- **Content disclosed:** 360 Media developed online campaigns in 2017 portraying opposition leader [[Raila Odinga]] as "a blood-thirsty individual who is also sympathetic to Al-Shabaab" while portraying [[Uhuru Kenyatta]] as "tough on terrorism" (per [[Source Wikipedia - Cambridge Analytica]] and Daily Nation reporting)
- **The "death of a CA employee" claim:** a CA employee said in 2018 that his predecessor at CA had been **found dead in his hotel room in Kenya while working on Kenyatta's 2013 campaign** — this claim is documented but the specifics remain under-investigated. `#confidence/medium` on the death claim as reported.
