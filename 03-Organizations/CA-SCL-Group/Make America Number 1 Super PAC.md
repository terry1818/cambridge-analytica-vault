# Make America Number 1 Super PAC (MA1)

## Summary

US Super PAC formed in **2016** by the **Mercer family** initially to support [[Ted Cruz]]'s presidential candidacy, then re-purposed in **August 2016** to support [[Donald Trump]] after Cruz dropped out. **Paid [[Cambridge Analytica]] millions of dollars** for data and targeting services during the 2016 general election. The MA1-CA relationship raised significant **FEC campaign-coordination concerns** because the same firm (CA) was simultaneously working for the Trump campaign -- campaigns and Super PACs are legally prohibited from coordinating strategy.

## Operational role

- Funded by Mercer family
- Operated by David Bossie (later Trump Deputy Campaign Manager) and other Mercer-aligned figures
- Paid CA for data services and audience-targeting
- Produced attack content on [[Hillary Clinton]] using CA-derived audience data
- Reported approximately $15M-20M+ in 2016 spending

## The coordination question

Per [[Source Channel 4 2018-03-19 Exposed]] and subsequent reporting:

- [[Alexander Nix]] was captured on hidden camera describing how MA1-CA work could be made "**unattributable, untrackable**"
- The Trump campaign could "take the high road" and "stay clean" while MA1 + CA produced negative content
- This structure suggested **potential illegal coordination** between Trump campaign and MA1 PAC
- No formal FEC enforcement action resulted (FEC has been largely deadlocked for years)

## Channel 4 footage implications

The Channel 4 sting captured Nix describing the operational pattern:

- CA serves both campaign and Super PAC
- Negative content goes through Super PAC
- Direct campaign distances itself from negative work
- "Unattributable" framing makes coordination hard to prove

This is **structurally similar** to many other Super PAC arrangements in modern US politics but the Nix description and the CA dual-role made the MA1 case unusually documented.
