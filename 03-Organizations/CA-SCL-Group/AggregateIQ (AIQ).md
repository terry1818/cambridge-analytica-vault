# AggregateIQ (AIQ)

AIQ is a **Victoria, British Columbia-based digital advertising and political tech firm** that functioned as the **principal external vendor** to [[SCL Group]] / [[Cambridge Analytica]] from approximately 2013 through 2017. AIQ built the **[[Ripon]]** campaign software platform -- the technical substrate that underpinned both CA's 2016 [[Ted Cruz Campaign 2016]] operation and the [[Vote Leave]] (Brexit) digital campaign. It is the second-most operationally important node in the CA story after CA itself.

## Founding and origin

- **Founded:** 2013, Victoria, BC
- **Co-founders:** Zack Massingham (CEO) and Jeff Silvester (COO)
- **Origin story (contested):** [[Christopher Wylie]] has stated he helped establish AIQ while working at [[SCL Group]], specifically to handle SCL's digital advertising work. Massingham and Silvester have publicly denied that AIQ is a "Canadian SCL." However, AIQ confirmed that **~80% of its revenue between 2013 and mid-2015 came from SCL** -- a financial dependence that makes the "independent vendor" framing legally true but practically misleading.

## Role in the CA operation

### The Cruz / Ripon era (2014-2016)

For the **[[Ted Cruz Campaign 2016]]** (the [[Mercer family]]'s preferred 2016 candidate), CA contracted AIQ to build **[[Ripon]]** -- a customized campaign-management software platform integrating voter data, psychographic profiles, and digital ad-targeting workflows. Ripon was, in practice, **the operational interface** through which CA's psychographic models reached actual political campaigns.

### The Brexit / Vote Leave era (2015-2016)

When [[Ted Cruz]] dropped out of the GOP primary, CA pivoted (via Bannon) to the Trump campaign -- and AIQ pivoted with its Ripon infrastructure to the **UK Brexit referendum**, where it became the digital vendor to:

- **[[Vote Leave]]** (Dominic Cummings's official Leave campaign)
- **[[BeLeave]]** (Darren Grimes's youth-focused Leave campaign)
- **[[Veterans for Britain]]**
- **[[DUP Vote to Leave]]**

The **UK Electoral Commission** later determined that Vote Leave and BeLeave **illegally coordinated** their spending through AIQ -- see [[Connection Vote Leave AIQ BeLeave illegal coordination]] and [[Source UK Electoral Commission 2018-07 Vote Leave]]. Vote Leave alone paid **~40% of its total campaign budget to AIQ** -- an extraordinary concentration on a single foreign vendor.

## The 2018 exposure

When the [[Facebook-Cambridge Analytica Scandal]] broke in March 2018:

- **6 April 2018:** Facebook suspended AIQ from its platform, citing "concerns over possible affiliation with SCL Group"
- AIQ executives testified to the [[UK Parliament DCMS Committee]] but were widely seen as evasive about the SCL relationship
- **November 2019:** Canada's BC privacy commissioner and federal Privacy Commissioner jointly determined AIQ had **violated both BC and Canadian privacy laws** through its handling of Vote Leave supporter data
- AIQ paid no major fine but the regulatory finding was unambiguous

## Current state (2024-2026)

Unlike CA and SCL, **AIQ did not collapse**. The firm:

- Remains operational from Victoria
- Has rebranded as a more general-purpose digital ad agency
- Continues to pitch political clients in Canada, the US, and abroad -- though its high-profile UK/US political work has largely dried up post-scandal
- Has not been definitively absorbed into any successor entity (unlike [[Emerdata]] for CA itself), but the personnel network remains intact

## Why this matters

AIQ is the cleanest case study of how **operational continuity survives reputational collapse** in the political-tech industry. CA's brand is toxic; CA's parent SCL is gone; but the **firm that actually built and ran the campaign software** is still in business, still in Canada, still controlling the IP and the methodology. Any analysis of post-CA capability that focuses on Emerdata alone misses where the *technical* substrate of the operation actually lives.

## Open questions

- **What is the current state of Ripon's codebase?** Was it transferred to any successor entity, dissolved into AIQ's general-purpose ad-tech stack, or simply sunsetted?
- **Did AIQ retain copies of the GSR-derived Facebook profile data?** This was a major question during the 2018 ICO investigation; the publicly disclosed answer is "no," but verification was limited.
- **What political work has AIQ done in 2020-2026?** Less visible than its 2014-2018 era but unlikely to be zero.
- **Why no successor "AIQ 2.0"?** Compare to Emerdata, Auspex, Data Propria -- CA-side actors created multiple successor entities. AIQ stayed AIQ. What's different?
