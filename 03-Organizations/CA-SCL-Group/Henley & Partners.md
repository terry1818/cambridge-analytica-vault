## Citizenship-by-investment industry pioneer

Henley & Partners institutionalized the modern framing of citizenship as a **tradeable asset for high-net-worth individuals globally**. Programs operated include:

- Malta Individual Investor Programme (controversial; EU pressure forced modifications)
- St. Kitts & Nevis (the original modern CBI program, predating Henley but advised on)
- Cyprus (suspended 2020 amid corruption scandals)
- Multiple Caribbean states
- Various European Golden Visa programs
- Multiple African and Pacific Island programs

## Controversies

Critics argue CBI programs:
- Enable money laundering (programs typically have weak due diligence)
- Facilitate tax evasion for wealthy clients
- Provide escape routes for sanctioned individuals and Russian oligarchs (especially exposed 2022+)
- Undermine sovereignty (governments selling citizenship)
- 2022 â€” EU Commission criticism of Maltese CBI
- 2023 â€” Henley pushed into new African markets following Caribbean program tightening
