# Cambridge Analytica

## Summary

British political consulting firm founded in 2013 as a subsidiary of SCL Group, that came to prominence through the **Facebook–Cambridge Analytica data scandal**. CA combined data mining, brokerage, and strategic communication for electoral campaigns, claiming to use psychographic profiling (Big Five / OCEAN) on millions of voters. Hired by 2016 presidential campaigns of [[Ted Cruz]] and [[Donald Trump]], as well as [[Leave.EU]] during the 2016 [[Brexit Referendum 2016|Brexit referendum]]. Collapsed May 2018 following [[2018 Wylie Disclosures]] and [[Channel 4 Sting 2018]] revelations. ([[Source Wikipedia - Cambridge Analytica]])

## Methods (claimed)

Three-step approach per Wikipedia/Kosinski research:

1. **Train Big Five (OCEAN) model** on data from ~200,000 US participants recruited via Qualtrics (paid $4 to complete personality questionnaire + share Facebook profile)
2. **Infer personality profiles** of millions via Facebook friends-data API exploitation
3. **Targeted micro-messages** delivered via social media, email, direct canvassing — tone tailored to inferred personality

Other methods:
- The "Cruz Crew" mobile app — tracked physical movements and contacts
- Monthly ongoing surveys covering political and consumer preferences
- 32 personality styles attributed to every US adult
- Claimed 5,000 data points on 220+ million Americans (Dec 2015)
- See [[Psychographic Targeting]], [[OCEAN Model]], [[Microtargeting]]

Effectiveness disputed:
- [[Brendan Nyhan]] (Dartmouth): research shows altering voter choice is hard; mobilizing partisans is easier
- [[Eitan Hersh]] (Tufts): "Every claim about psychographics etc made by or about [CA] is BS"
- NYT March 2017: "Cambridge executives now concede that the company never used psychographics in the Trump campaign"
- [[Cruz campaign]] stopped using CA after psychographic models failed to identify likely supporters (after South Carolina primary, 2016-02-20)
