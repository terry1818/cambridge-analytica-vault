# Cato Institute

Libertarian think tank founded 1977 in San Francisco (later relocated to Washington DC) by Charles Koch, Ed Crane, and Murray Rothbard (Rothbard purged 1981). One of the principal institutional nodes in the **Koch-network architecture** mapped in the vault's [[Lens - 2024-2026 Power Map]] and [[Patron Wing MOC]]. Promotes free-market deregulation, cuts to social programs, drug-policy reform (one of the relatively-libertarian positions), and constitutional originalism.

Annual budget approximately $30M+. Approximately 100 staff scholars, fellows, and policy experts.

## Founding and Koch relationship

- Originally majority Koch-funded
- Charles Koch served on board for decades
- 2012: legal dispute over board composition between Koch and Crane resolved with Koch retaining substantial influence
- Current funding base broader but Koch-network influence persists

## Major policy areas

- Constitutional law / originalism (often through amicus briefs)
- Economic policy / deregulation
- Foreign policy / interventionism critique
- Social Security / Medicare reform
- Drug policy reform
- Immigration (mixed positions)
