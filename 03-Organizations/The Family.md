# The Family / The Fellowship Foundation

US religious-political organization founded 1935 by [[Abraham Vereide]] as the International Christian Leadership / Fellowship Foundation. Led by [[Doug Coe]] from 1969 until Coe's death 2017.

## Stated mission

- Cultivate relationships with political leaders globally on a theological foundation of Jesus-as-leader
- Organize the annual [[National Prayer Breakfast]] in Washington DC

## Documented practices

- Maintains the [[C Street Center]] residence in DC where multiple senators and representatives have lived at below-market rent
- Operates internationally with relationships extending to authoritarian leaders (Suharto in Indonesia, multiple African heads of state, post-Soviet leaders in Russia and Belarus)
- Practices what Jeff Sharlet (the primary journalistic chronicler) characterizes as "elite-focused Christianity" - influence concentrated at senior-political level rather than mass evangelism

## Significance

The Family is the longest-running operational religious-political organizing infrastructure in the contemporary US (1935+). The pattern - elite-focused, secretive, with substantial international footprint - distinguishes it from broader [[Religious right]] organizations and gives it specific influence on US foreign policy that exceeds its public profile.
