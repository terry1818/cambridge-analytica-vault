## Summary

4chan and 8chan are anonymous English-language imageboards that became the primary infrastructure for far-right online culture, conspiracy theory generation, and coordinated harassment from the early 2010s onward. Their combination of anonymity, minimal moderation, and rapid-cycle content production created an environment where extremist memes, disinformation narratives, and harassment strategies could be generated, tested, and deployed at scale. Donovan, Dreyfuss, and Friedberg's *Meme Wars* treats these boards — especially 4chan's /pol/ ("politically incorrect") board — as the organizational substrate from which [[Gamergate]], [[Pizzagate]], QAnon, and the wider alt-right emerged.

## Identity

**4chan** was created in October 2003 by Christopher "moot" Poole, then fifteen, as an English-language clone of the Japanese imageboard Futaba Channel. Its defining feature is that most posts are anonymous (no account required) and threads expire. The /b/ ("random") board was its original home; /pol/ ("politically incorrect"), created in 2011, became the far-right organizing board.

**8chan** was created in 2013 by Fredrick Brennan as an explicitly less-moderated alternative to 4chan. It attracted users banned from 4chan and became a home for more extreme content. Jim Watkins acquired operational control in 2016; Brennan publicly disavowed the platform in 2019. After being removed from Cloudflare following the El Paso mass shooting (2019), it relaunched as **8kun** with reduced visibility.

## Leadership & key personnel

- **Christopher Poole ("moot")**: Founded 4chan; sold it to Hiroyuki Nishimura in 2015; subsequently joined Google.
- **Hiroyuki Nishimura**: Acquired 4chan 2015; original founder of 2channel (Japanese precursor). Still operator as of 2026.
- **Fredrick Brennan**: Created 8chan; handed control to Jim Watkins 2016; became a vocal critic of the platform by 2019.
- **Jim Watkins**: Operates 8kun from the Philippines; gave a deposition in the January 6 House Select Committee investigation regarding QAnon.

## Funding & money flows

Both platforms have operated on minimal-transparency funding models (ad revenue, limited donations). Financial flows are not well documented. 8kun's infrastructure was significantly disrupted when Cloudflare and multiple CDN providers dropped it in 2019.

## Activities

- **Meme production and propagation**: /pol/ functioned as a high-velocity meme factory; content generated there was systematically spread to Twitter, Facebook, and Reddit.
- **Coordinated harassment**: [[Gamergate]] was organized via 4chan /b/ and /v/ boards; targets' personal information was doxed and circulated.
- **Conspiracy theory generation**: [[Pizzagate]] originated on 4chan and 8chan after WikiLeaks released Podesta emails. QAnon originated on 4chan /pol/ in October 2017 before migrating to 8chan.
- **Radicalization**: Academic literature documents the "alt-right pipeline" moving users from 4chan through progressively more extreme content.
- **Mass shooter manifestos**: The El Paso shooter (2019) and Christchurch shooter (2019) posted manifestos to 8chan before or during their attacks.

## Controversies / investigations

- Multiple mass shooters have posted manifestos to 8chan/8kun, prompting congressional scrutiny and platform infrastructure action.
- January 6 House Select Committee subpoenaed Jim Watkins regarding QAnon's 8chan origins.
- 4chan's /pol/ is documented by multiple academic studies as a generator of racist, antisemitic, and misogynist content that propagates to mainstream platforms.

## Open questions

- Degree to which /pol/ radicalization is engineered vs. emergent; whether platform owners bear legal liability for downstream harms.
- Financial sustainability of 8kun and its actual current user base size.
