## Summary

Praxis Society is a startup city-building company founded in 2021 by Dryden Brown and Charlie Callinan, positioning itself as an "internet-native nation" for "high-agency individuals." It proposes to build a new city of approximately 10,000 people in the Mediterranean (specific site undisclosed as of mid-2026), designed around libertarian principles, reduced regulatory barriers, and a curated membership model. Praxis has raised over $525 million in financing and attracted investors from the [[Peter Thiel]] network including [[Pronomos Capital]], [[Balaji Srinivasan]], and Joe Lonsdale (Palantir co-founder). It represents the second-generation iteration of the [[Plutocratic secession]] project, adapting [[Balaji Srinivasan]]'s "network state" framework to a physical construction project.

## Identity

Praxis describes its model as building the "Next America" — a new society rather than reforming existing ones. It recruits online community members first, then plans physical city construction. Architectural design was commissioned from Zaha Hadid Architects, integrating futuristic and classical aesthetics. The founding narrative draws explicitly on Thiel/Srinivasan-adjacent "build, don't fix" rhetoric.

Unlike [[Próspera]] (which operated inside an existing legal framework, the Honduran ZEDE), Praxis has not yet secured a sovereign or semi-sovereign jurisdiction. It has explored potential sites including in the Adriatic, Mediterranean, and — per founder Brown — even pitched Greenland. The project thus far remains in community-building and fundraising phases.

## Controversies / investigations

- Critics note that $525M raised with no land secured, no regulatory approval, and no host government agreement represents an extraordinary bet on jurisdictional arbitrage that may never materialize.
- The "high-agency individuals" selection criteria raise concerns about exclusionary design mirroring [[Hans-Hermann Hoppe]]'s covenant community model.
- The project's tech-utopian framing has been characterized as "tech dystopia as the Next America" by critics who note the absence of democratic accountability in the proposed governance model.

## Open questions

- Will Praxis secure a host jurisdiction and what legal framework will govern it?
- How does the failure of Próspera (ZEDE repeal, ICSID dispute) affect Praxis's prospects?
- What is the actual governance model — will residents have electoral accountability?
