# EagleAI

EagleAI is an automated voter-roll-challenge software platform developed by Georgia-based conservative activist John "Rick" Richards. The platform was launched in 2022-2023 and has been adopted by [[True the Vote]] and adjacent organisations as the operational engine for industrialised voter-roll-challenge campaigns.

## Operational mechanism

EagleAI cross-references voter-registration databases against multiple commercial and government data sources (Social Security death records, USPS address-change records, commercial address databases, voter rolls from other states) and generates lists of registrations the system flags as potentially invalid. Local activists then submit those flagged registrations to county election officials as formal voter-roll challenges; election officials are required by state law to investigate each challenge.

The operational consequence: a few activists with EagleAI access can generate volumes of challenges that overwhelm county election-administration capacity. Even when individual challenges are not sustained, the aggregate administrative burden produces operational consequences for election administration.

## Adoption pattern

Adoption has been concentrated in specific battleground states. Georgia, where Richards is based and where the platform first deployed at scale, has been the principal test environment. Arizona, North Carolina, Pennsylvania, and Wisconsin have seen subsequent adoption. Specific county-level adoption rates have been documented through journalistic investigation.

## Coordination with broader infrastructure

EagleAI integrates with [[True the Vote]] for legal-and-operational support, with [[Election Integrity Network]] for coordination across jurisdictions, and with state-level Republican Party infrastructure for activist recruitment. The Heritage Foundation's Election Integrity research has substantially aligned with the EagleAI operational programme.

## Why this is load-bearing

EagleAI is the technical-automation layer that industrialises what would otherwise be manual voter-roll-challenge work. The platform's operational consequence is to make systematic voter-roll-purge programmes feasible at the activist level rather than requiring election-official-level cooperation.
