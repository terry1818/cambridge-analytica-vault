# Sinclair Broadcast Group

## Summary

**The largest single-owner local-TV network in the US** - ~185 local TV stations across the country. **Smith family controlled** (David Smith CEO). **Famous for "must-run" segments** - centrally-produced conservative content that local affiliates are required to air, often during local-news broadcasts.

**2018 viral video** showed Sinclair-station news anchors across the US reciting identical "fake news" attack scripts in unison - exposing the centralized messaging operation. **The most underdocumented media-capture vector in US politics** because most viewers don't realize their "local TV news" is centrally programmed from a single conservative ownership.

**Operates the most consequential local-news infrastructure** - reaches voters who don't follow national news; substitutes for declining local newspapers; embeds in trusted local-affiliate brand identity.

## Identity

- **Founded:** 1986
- **CEO/Chairman:** David Smith
- **HQ:** Hunt Valley, MD
- **Stations:** ~185 local TV
- **Audience reach:** ~40% of US households

## Operating model

- Buys local TV stations across the US
- Affiliates with major networks (ABC, NBC, CBS, Fox)
- **Must-run segments** centrally produced; aired during local news
- **Boris Epshteyn** (former Trump White House) has been a Sinclair commentator
- **Editorial-policy memos** mandate specific framing of news stories

## Why it matters

- **Bypasses voter distrust of national media** - viewers trust local-news brand
- **Reaches non-news-consuming voters** - people who only watch local TV
- **Substitutes for collapsing local journalism** - Sinclair fills the gap left by newspaper closures
- **Centralized conservative-coalition messaging** - functional propaganda apparatus disguised as local news
- **Subsidizes Trump-aligned political content** during election cycles
