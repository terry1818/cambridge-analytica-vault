# Eldon Insurance

## Summary

UK commercial insurance business owned by **[[Arron Banks]]** and operated alongside his other UK insurance interests (the Banks insurance group including GoSkippy and adjacent brands). Eldon Insurance appears in this vault as the **commercial half of the Banks commercial-political infrastructure** — the entity that **shared customer data with [[Leave.EU]]** (Banks's Brexit political vehicle) in violation of UK data-protection law, as documented in [[Source ICO 2020-07-20 Leave.EU Eldon fine]].

## Key facts

- **Owner / principal:** [[Arron Banks]]
- **Operational role:** UK commercial insurance services
- **Banks insurance group context:** Eldon sits within Banks's broader UK insurance portfolio
- **ICO action 2020:** Eldon fined for **unlawful marketing — sharing customer data with [[Leave.EU]] political-marketing operations** without proper consent, and receiving political-supporter data from Leave.EU for commercial-marketing use
- **Combined fine** (Eldon + Leave.EU): approximately **£135,000** total per ICO action
- **Audit notice:** ICO required Eldon to undergo compliance audit alongside Leave.EU

## Significance for the vault

Eldon Insurance is the **structural example of the commercial-to-political data crossing** in the UK Brexit context:

- **Commercial customer data → political mobilization:** Eldon's insurance customer base was used as a Leave.EU recruitment / persuasion target
- **Political supporter data → commercial marketing:** Leave.EU's political-supporter data was used by Eldon for commercial mailings
- **Same physical operation:** Banks operated both entities from the same UK office infrastructure with overlapping personnel — [[Andrew Wigmore]] worked across both
- **Regulatory finding documented:** unlike many commercial-political data-crossings that remain inferential, this one has **explicit ICO regulatory documentation** ([[Source ICO 2020-07-20 Leave.EU Eldon fine]])

This is **distinct from but parallel to** the [[Hypothesis - Defense-to-elections pipeline]] — Eldon represents a **commercial-to-political** version of the same structural pattern, where capabilities and data flow across the boundary between commercial and political purposes.
