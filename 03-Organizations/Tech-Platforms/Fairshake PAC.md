# Fairshake PAC

Fairshake PAC is a super PAC launched in 2023 by Coinbase, Ripple Labs, and a16z (Andreessen Horowitz) as the principal crypto-industry political-spending vehicle. The PAC deployed over $200 million across the 2024 cycle, becoming one of the largest single-cycle independent-expenditure operations in US political history.

## 2024 cycle operational outputs

Fairshake's 2024 cycle operational outputs have substantially reshaped congressional crypto policy:

- The PAC eliminated multiple anti-crypto Democratic incumbents in primary contests, most prominently Katie Porter (California Senate primary) and Jamaal Bowman (New York House primary)
- The PAC supported pro-crypto candidates across both parties in selected races
- The PAC effectively communicated to all sitting members of Congress that anti-crypto positioning would draw substantial primary-cycle opposition spending

The downstream consequence: the post-2024 congressional crypto-policy landscape has substantially shifted toward crypto-industry preferences, with several pieces of crypto-favourable legislation moving through Congress that would have been operationally impossible before the Fairshake spending campaign.

## Funding structure

Fairshake's funding has been almost entirely from a small number of crypto-industry corporate donors:

- [[Coinbase]] (substantial multi-million donations)
- Ripple Labs (substantial multi-million donations)
- [[a16z (Andreessen Horowitz)]] and Andreessen-aligned crypto-fund vehicles
- Smaller contributions from various other crypto-industry corporate sources

The concentration of funding is operationally distinctive: most super PACs depend on individual large-donor contributions; Fairshake depends principally on corporate contributions from a small number of crypto-industry entities.

## Why this is load-bearing

Fairshake demonstrates the operational model of single-industry political-spending capture. The model: an industry with concentrated wealth pools a substantial fraction of that wealth into a single coordinated political-spending vehicle, then deploys that vehicle to eliminate anti-industry incumbents and to deter remaining members of Congress from anti-industry positioning. The operational model is replicable; the question is which other industries will replicate it.
