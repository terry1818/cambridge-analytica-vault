# Palantir

Palantir Technologies is the **single most consequential private-sector node** in the 2024–2026 power architecture of federal surveillance and database integration. Founded in 2003 by [[Peter Thiel]] and CEO [[Alex Karp]] (with [[Joe Lonsdale]] and others), Palantir has, since Trump's return to office in January 2025, expanded from a defense-and-intelligence contractor into **the de facto backbone of inter-agency data fusion** across the IRS, ICE, Social Security Administration, HHS, and DOD. This is the structural realization of [[Hypothesis - Federal database supersedes CA model]].

## Two product lines

- **Palantir Gotham** — the original product, designed for defense and intelligence customers. Used by CIA, DOD, NSA, and allied intelligence services since the mid-2000s.
- **Palantir Foundry** — the newer enterprise platform, designed for commercial and civilian-government customers. **Foundry is the product being deployed across the Trump-era federal database integration projects.**

The architectural difference matters: Gotham was always understood as a classified-domain tool with appropriate legal scaffolding. Foundry is being deployed in domains (taxation, immigration, social services, education) where the legal scaffolding for cross-agency data integration is either absent or actively contested.

## 2025-2026 federal contracts

Since Trump's January 2025 return:

- **>$113M total federal spending** to Palantir documented in publicly available federal procurement data through mid-2026
- **IRS:** Palantir paid **>$130M** to build what insiders describe as a "mega API" unifying tax-return data, Affordable Care Act enrollment data, bank statements, and FinCEN financial-crimes data into a single searchable hub. This is the project Senators Wyden and Ocasio-Cortez challenged in their **June 17, 2025 letter to Alex Karp**.
- **ICE:** **$30M contract** for **[[ImmigrationOS]]**, a real-time migrant tracking platform; prototype due September 25, 2025; contract runs through September 2027
- **DHS more broadly:** Multiple smaller contracts integrating Palantir Foundry across border surveillance, biometric collection, and deportation operations
- **HHS:** Active deployment; details limited
- **DOD:** Long-standing customer, continued and expanded
- **SSA:** Negotiations underway for Foundry adoption
- **Department of Education:** Negotiations underway for Foundry adoption

The **March 20, 2025 Trump EO Eliminating Information Silos** ([[Trump EO Eliminating Information Silos 2025-03-20]]) is the legal/policy hook for this expansion: it explicitly directs federal agencies to share data across silos, a directive Palantir Foundry is engineered to operationalize.

## The Cambridge Analytica connection

The structural link to the CA story:

- **CA's model** was: harvest private-platform data → train psychographic models → target voters
- **The 2025 Palantir model** is: integrate government-held data → train AI models → target citizens for enforcement
- Both rely on cross-source data integration that the affected individuals never consented to, and both rest on stretched or absent legal authority

CA's bottleneck was: it could only get the data it could scrape or buy. The 2025 federal-database model has no such bottleneck — the data already exists, in agency silos, under the executive's command.

This is the core thesis of [[Hypothesis - Federal database supersedes CA model]]: the CA-era playbook of psychographic targeting through stolen-data models has been **superseded** by a far more powerful playbook of integrated-database surveillance through legitimate (if contested) executive authority.

## Lawsuits and oversight

- **Wyden / AOC letter (June 17, 2025)** demanding answers on the IRS mega-database
- **Multiple Privacy Act lawsuits** filed in 2025-2026 challenging DOGE's data access patterns (see [[Source State of Surveillance 2026 DOGE Privacy Act Lawsuits]])
- **ACLU** has filed challenges to ImmigrationOS deployment
- Congressional Democrats have proposed legislation to block specific Palantir-built integration projects

Palantir's public response (May 30, 2025 blog post by the company) was to deny that it is building a "master database" — but the technical distinction is somewhat semantic: Foundry's value proposition *is* cross-source data integration, even if Palantir doesn't itself own the resulting data warehouse.

## Open questions

- **What is the actual data-flow architecture across IRS / ICE / SSA / DOD Foundry deployments?** Is data moving between agencies (illegal without specific authorization), or are agencies independently using Foundry's analytic surface on their own data (legal under the EO)?
- **What machine-learning models are being trained on the unified data?** Predictive deportation? Predictive tax-fraud audits? This is the substantive question that hasn't been fully answered in public reporting.
- **What happens to the database integrations if a 2028 administration tries to dismantle them?** Technical dependencies on a single vendor (Palantir) create switching costs that lock in the architecture across administrations.
- **What is the connection (if any) between Palantir's federal-database work and the [[Cambridge Analytica]] derived data assets held by [[Emerdata]]?** No evidence of direct transfer, but the methodological convergence is unmistakable.
