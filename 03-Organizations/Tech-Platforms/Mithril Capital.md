# Mithril Capital

## Summary

US mid-stage venture capital firm co-founded in **2012** by **[[Peter Thiel]]** and Ajay Royan. Named after the precious metal Mithril from Tolkien's *The Lord of the Rings*. Distinct from Thiel's earlier-stage [[Founders Fund]] -- Mithril focuses on mid-stage and growth investments. **[[JD Vance]] worked at Mithril (~2016-2017)** as a principal early in his post-Yale Law career.

## Mithril → Vance → Narya → Senate → VP pipeline

The Vance career trajectory through Mithril is the **clearest documented Thiel-to-government pipeline**:

1. **2016-2017:** JD Vance joins Mithril as principal (post-Yale Law)
2. **2017-2019:** Vance at Mithril, then briefly at Revolution LLC
3. **2019:** Vance founds [[Narya Capital]] with Thiel as anchor investor
4. **2022:** Thiel funds Vance Senate campaign with ~$15M via Protect Ohio Values PAC
5. **2024-07:** Trump selects Vance as VP nominee (with Thiel reportedly central in advocacy)
6. **2025-01:** Vance inaugurated as 50th US Vice President

Mithril is the **first node** in that pipeline -- where Thiel patronage began before any of it had public political dimension.

Mithril's portfolio has included various mid-stage growth companies; specific portfolio composition has been less publicly visible than Founders Fund.
