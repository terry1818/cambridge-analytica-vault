# Stargate Project

## Summary

Joint-venture **AI-infrastructure project** publicly announced at the White House on **2025-01-21** — Donald Trump's second day in office for his second term. The announcement framed the project as a **$500 billion** US investment in **data-center and AI-compute capacity** over four years, with **OpenAI**, **Oracle**, **SoftBank**, and **MGX (an Abu Dhabi sovereign-wealth-adjacent vehicle)** as the named initial partners.

Stargate is significant in this vault as a node in the **2024-2026 federal-AI-infrastructure architecture** discussed in [[Hypothesis - Federal database supersedes CA model]] and [[Lens - 2024-2026 Power Map]]. It represents the **AI-compute / data-center half** of the broader federal-database-and-targeting capability picture, complementary to [[Palantir Technologies|Palantir]]'s data-integration role.

## Key facts

- **Announcement:** 2025-01-21 at the White House
- **Stated investment:** **$500 billion** over four years (initial $100B reportedly committed)
- **Named partners (initial):**
  - **OpenAI** — anchor AI customer / model partner ([[Sam Altman]])
  - **Oracle** — infrastructure and cloud-platform partner ([[Larry Ellison]])
  - **SoftBank** — capital partner ([[Masayoshi Son]])
  - **MGX** — Abu Dhabi-adjacent sovereign-wealth investor
- **First site:** Abilene, Texas (Oracle data-center buildout)
- **Subsequent sites:** announced across multiple US states 2025-2026
- **Government role:** White-House-led announcement framing; specific federal contracts and regulatory accommodations are part of the project structure but specific commitments are partially public, partially opaque

## Significance for the vault

- **AI-compute infrastructure as state asset:** Stargate's framing as a quasi-federal infrastructure initiative — announced at the White House, with Trump personally invoking national-security framings — marks a shift in how AI compute is treated relative to private-sector commercial activity
- **Convergence of operators:** the named-partner roster combines US Big Tech (Oracle, OpenAI) with foreign sovereign-adjacent capital (MGX / Abu Dhabi) — a pattern that mirrors the [[Affinity Partners]] / Saudi PIF flows on the Trump-orbit-capital side
- **Companion to [[Palantir Technologies]] data role:** if Palantir is the data-integration layer of the 2024-2026 architecture, Stargate is the **compute layer** — the two together represent the operational stack
- **The non-CA model:** Stargate is **not** CA-style external political-vendor work — it is **state-aligned infrastructure investment**. This is exactly the shift [[Hypothesis - Federal database supersedes CA model]] describes — capability moves inside (or adjacent to) federal infrastructure rather than being commercialized through external election-services vendors

## Disputed framings

- **Investment scale:** the $500B headline figure includes future commitments that may or may not materialize; critics note that comparable infrastructure announcements have historically over-promised
- **Government role:** the project is **not** technically a federal contract — it is private-sector investment with administration-level political endorsement. The boundary between political-blessing and operational state-backing is intentionally ambiguous in the framing.
- **Elon Musk public criticism:** [[Elon Musk]] publicly criticized the Stargate funding announcement on X, claiming SoftBank did not actually have the committed capital. SoftBank and Altman pushed back. The dispute illustrates that **the AI-infrastructure / Trump-tech-right alliance is itself fractured** rather than monolithic.
