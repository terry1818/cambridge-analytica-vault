# Smartmatic

International voting technology company founded in 2000 in Boca Raton, FL by Antonio Mugica, Roger Pinate, and Alfredo Anzola. Provides electoral systems and biometric identification globally. **Subject of false election-fraud claims** by [[Donald Trump]]-aligned media and attorneys following the November 2020 election, alleging connections to Venezuela / Hugo Chavez. **Plaintiff in major defamation lawsuits** against Fox News (announced settlement January 2024), OAN, Newsmax, [[Sidney Powell]], [[Rudy Giuliani]], and others.

## Use in 2020 election (Tier 1)

Smartmatic technology was used in 2020 **only in Los Angeles County, California** -- not in any of the contested swing states where Trump challenged results. The false claims about Smartmatic's role in flipping votes across the country had no factual basis. Smartmatic publicly clarified its limited 2020 footprint multiple times in the post-election period.

## False claims (November 2020 - 2021)

Trump allies including Powell, Giuliani, Lou Dobbs, Maria Bartiromo, Jeanine Pirro, and others made false claims including:
- Smartmatic was created by Venezuelan dictator Hugo Chavez to rig elections
- Smartmatic was connected to [[Dominion Voting Systems]] (they are separate companies; brief historical licensing arrangement years earlier)
- Smartmatic software was used to flip Trump votes to Biden
- Smartmatic was controlled by George Soros / international leftist actors
- Various technical claims

None of these claims had factual basis.

## Defamation lawsuits

### Smartmatic v Fox News Corporation ($2.7B filed February 2021)

Filed in NY Supreme Court. Discovery proceeded through 2022-2023. Trial originally scheduled 2025.

**Settled January 17, 2024** for an undisclosed sum (reported to be substantial; press estimates ranged $500M-$700M+). Fox issued joint statement.

### Smartmatic v Newsmax ($400M)

Settled September 2024 for undisclosed sum.

Pending.

Various lawsuits pending or in advanced stages.
