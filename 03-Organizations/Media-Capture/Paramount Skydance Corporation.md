# Paramount Skydance Corporation

## Summary

**One of the largest US media companies.** Formed **August 7, 2024** via the Skydance Media + Paramount Global merger. **David Ellison CEO**; **Larry Ellison principal financial backer** (~$6B+ equity). **Operationally controls direct broadcast access to ~120M US households** via CBS owned-and-operated stations + affiliated stations + cable distribution + streaming.

**Operational portfolio (highly consequential ownership concentration):**

### Broadcast television
- **CBS** - one of three US legacy broadcast networks
- **CBS News** (60 Minutes, CBS Mornings, CBS Evening News, Face the Nation)
- **CBS-owned local stations** (15 owned-and-operated stations in major markets including NYC, LA, Chicago, SF, Boston, Philadelphia, Dallas, Detroit, Miami, etc.)
- **CBS Sports** (NFL on CBS, March Madness rights, golf, others)
- **CW Network** (partial ownership; Nexstar majority since 2022)

### Cable networks
- **Showtime** (premium)
- **MTV** (music + reality)
- **Nickelodeon** (children)
- **Comedy Central**
- **BET / BET+** (Black entertainment)
- **TV Land, VH1, CMT, Pop, Logo TV**
- **Smithsonian Channel**

### Film
- **Paramount Pictures** (one of the "Big Five" Hollywood studios; founded 1912)
- **Skydance Animation**
- **Miramax** (partial)

### Streaming
- **Paramount+** (~80M global subscribers as of late 2025)
- **Pluto TV** (free ad-supported; 80M+ MAU)

### Digital
- **The Free Press** (acquired October 2025; ~$150M)
- Various digital + sports properties

### Other
- **CBS Studios** (TV production)
- **Showtime/MTV Entertainment Studios**
- **Skydance Sports**
- **Various regional sports networks**

## Operational significance

**Direct broadcast access:** CBS reaches approximately **94% of US TV households** via its station network. **Combined with CBS News + 60 Minutes editorial influence + Paramount Pictures cultural-product output + cable + streaming reach**, the combined entity represents one of the most operationally consequential single media ownership concentrations in modern US history.

**Post-merger editorial repositioning:**
- **July 2025:** Settled Trump 60 Minutes lawsuit for $16M (widely understood as condition of FCC approval)
- **October 2025:** Acquired The Free Press (Bari Weiss)
- **November 2025:** Bari Weiss installed as CBS News Editor-in-Chief
- **December 2025+:** Multiple high-profile CBS News + 60 Minutes departures
- **Multiple programming + editorial changes** documented as ideologically rightward
