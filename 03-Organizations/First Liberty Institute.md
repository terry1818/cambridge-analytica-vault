# First Liberty Institute

## Summary

First Liberty Institute is a conservative Christian public-interest law firm based in Plano, Texas, and describes itself as the largest legal organization in the United States dedicated exclusively to protecting religious liberty. It was founded in 1997 by [[Kelly Shackelford]] as the **Liberty Legal Institute**, a division of the Free Market Foundation; it became "Liberty Institute" in 2009 and "First Liberty Institute" in 2016, and expanded to nationwide litigation in 2012. First Liberty matters to this vault because it is one of the principal litigation engines of the religious right. Through a sustained, well-resourced strategy of taking carefully selected cases to the U.S. Supreme Court, First Liberty has won a string of precedent-setting victories — *American Legion v. American Humanist Association*, *Kennedy v. Bremerton School District*, *Carson v. Makin*, and *Groff v. DeJoy* among them — that have substantially expanded the permissible role of religion in public schools, public spaces, and public funding. It is, in effect, a tool of constitutional change: it does not lobby Congress so much as litigate the meaning of the First Amendment toward a more accommodationist, religion-favoring settlement.

- **Subsidiaries:** None notable; operates a national litigation and a "First Liberty Live!" media presence
- **Registered jurisdiction:** United States; headquartered in Plano, Texas

## Leadership & key personnel

- **[[Kelly Shackelford]]** — founder, president, and CEO; a Baylor Law School graduate and constitutional litigator. Shackelford previously worked as an attorney for the Rutherford Institute and as president of the Free Market Foundation before launching the Liberty Legal Institute. He has argued before the U.S. Supreme Court, testified before Congress, and is a prominent figure in the conservative legal movement, with ties to the Federalist Society network.
- First Liberty operates with a staff of attorneys and a large affiliated "volunteer attorney" network.

## Funding & money flows

First Liberty is donor-funded, supported by conservative Christian individuals and foundations. As a high-profile religious-liberty litigation firm, it is the type of organization that receives funding routed through conservative donor vehicles, including donor-advised funds such as the [[National Christian Foundation]]. The firm provides legal representation without charging clients, relying entirely on tax-deductible donations. Detailed donor breakdowns are not consolidated in reviewed public sources; First Liberty, like peer firms, benefits from the anonymizing structure of DAF giving.

## Activities

First Liberty's core activity is **strategic religious-liberty litigation**. Its approach mirrors the broader conservative legal movement's playbook: identify sympathetic clients and clean fact patterns, litigate selected cases up the federal appellate ladder, and aim for Supreme Court rulings that move constitutional doctrine. Its docket spans public-school religious expression, religious displays and monuments on public land, religious accommodation in employment, public funding of religious schools, and military and chaplaincy religious-liberty cases.

The results are doctrinally significant. First Liberty represented the prevailing parties in:

- ***American Legion v. American Humanist Association*** (2019) — upheld a large cross-shaped war memorial on public land, easing Establishment Clause limits on religious displays.
- ***Kennedy v. Bremerton School District*** (2022) — held that a public high-school football coach had a constitutional right to pray on the field, a major shift in school-prayer doctrine and in the Court's Establishment Clause framework.
- ***Carson v. Makin*** (2022) — required a state tuition-assistance program to include religious schools, advancing public funding of religious education.
- ***Groff v. DeJoy*** (2023) — strengthened employees' right to religious accommodation in the workplace.

In the vault's analytical frame, First Liberty is **infrastructure for constitutional capture by litigation**: it works alongside organizations like Alliance Defending Freedom and the [[Thomas More Society]], and is reinforced by a federal judiciary increasingly staffed by judges from the conservative legal pipeline (the Federalist Society, institutions like [[Regent University]]). Its victories reshape the church-state line in a more accommodationist direction case by case — a slower but durable form of structural change than legislation.

## Successor / related entities

- **Alliance Defending Freedom (ADF)** — the larger peer religious-liberty / conservative-cause litigation firm; parallel and overlapping mission.
- **[[Thomas More Society]]** — conservative Catholic litigation firm operating in adjacent territory.
- **Free Market Foundation** — the Texas organization under which First Liberty was originally founded.

## Controversies / investigations

- Critics and church-state separation advocates argue First Liberty's victories erode the Establishment Clause and disadvantage religious minorities and the non-religious; the firm frames the same rulings as protecting free exercise for all.
- The *Kennedy v. Bremerton* decision was criticized for a contested account of the underlying facts (whether the coach's prayer was truly private).
