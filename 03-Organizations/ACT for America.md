## Summary

ACT for America is the largest anti-Muslim advocacy organization in the United States, founded in 2007 by [[Brigitte Gabriel]] as an expansion of her earlier American Congress for Truth (2002). The organization presents itself as focused narrowly on "radical Islamic terrorism" and Sharia law, but critics — including the Southern Poverty Law Center, the Center for American Progress, and the Council on American-Islamic Relations — have characterized it as a hate group that makes no meaningful distinction between violent extremism and Muslim religious practice generally. Susan Posner's *Unholy* (2020) addresses ACT for America as part of the broader infrastructure of Islamophobic and theocratic-wing organizing that intersects with the Republican Party and the religious right.

## Identity

- Founded as a 501(c)(4) advocacy organization; maintains a 501(c)(3) educational arm.
- Claims a membership of approximately 750,000–1 million (unverified externally); widely described as the largest anti-Muslim grassroots organization in the US.
- Primary activities: lobbying against domestic application of Sharia law; opposing mosques and Muslim immigration; "anti-terrorism" policy advocacy.
- SPLC designated ACT for America as a hate group in its extremist files.
- Organized the "March Against Sharia" events in 2017 across multiple US cities.
- Operates a network of local chapters.

## Leadership & key personnel

- **[[Brigitte Gabriel]]**: Lebanese-American founder and president; formerly Nour Semaan; background in Christian Maronite Lebanon; her personal history of displacement during the Lebanese Civil War is central to her public narrative.

## Funding & money flows

Funding sources are partially disclosed through IRS 990 filings. Gabriel's speaking fees and book sales generate personal income that overlaps with organizational revenue. ACT for America has received donations from major conservative donor networks. Specific major-donor information is not publicly confirmed at evidence tier.

## Activities

- Lobbying state legislatures against "foreign law" (de facto anti-Sharia) bills; such bills have passed in multiple states.
- Training law enforcement agencies in "counter-terrorism" — content characterized by critics as anti-Muslim bias training.
- Organizing mass events: "March Against Sharia" rallies (2017) drew both supporters and counter-protesters.
- Publishing reports alleging Muslim Brotherhood infiltration of US institutions.
- Advocating against Muslim candidates for office and against Islamic center construction.
- Meeting with Trump administration officials 2017–21; Gabriel and ACT for America had access to the Trump White House.

## Controversies / investigations

- SPLC and CAP hate-group designation; Gabriel contests this characterization.
- Training contracts with law enforcement: documented in multiple states; critics argue the training instills anti-Muslim bias.
- 2017 "March Against Sharia": events featured participants from neo-Nazi and white nationalist groups; ACT for America denied coordination but acknowledged shared attendance.
- Gabriel's statements: NYT reporter Laurie Goodstein wrote that Gabriel "presents a portrait of Islam so thoroughly bent on destruction and domination that it is unrecognizable to those who study or practice the religion."
