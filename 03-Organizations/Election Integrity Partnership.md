# Election Integrity Partnership

US academic-civil-society research partnership tracking election-related disinformation. Active for the 2020 and 2022 US election cycles. Curtailed in 2024 under political pressure.

## Founding partners

- [[Stanford Internet Observatory]]
- University of Washington Center for an Informed Public
- Graphika
- Atlantic Council Digital Forensic Research Lab (DFRLab)

## Methodology

- Tracked viral election-related claims in real time
- Catalogued disinformation tactics across platforms
- Produced post-election reports for public and platform consumption

## Political backlash 2023-2024

- Multiple lawsuits (Missouri v. Biden / Murthy v. Missouri, Stephen Miller's America First Legal cases)
- House Judiciary Subcommittee investigations chaired by [[Jim Jordan]]
- The "Twitter Files" framing constructed EIP as a censorship apparatus
- Stanford ended SIO's EIP-related work in 2024
- The partnership did not operate for the 2024 election cycle

## Significance

EIP became one of the most-targeted institutional examples in the broader [[Disinformation-research infrastructure defunding]] campaign. The political-system effect: the 2024 election cycle had substantially less institutional capacity for tracking and responding to disinformation than 2020 or 2022.
