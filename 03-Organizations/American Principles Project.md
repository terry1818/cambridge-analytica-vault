## Summary

The American Principles Project (APP) is a 501(c)(4) social conservative political advocacy organization founded in 2009 by Princeton professor [[Robert P. George]], political consultant Jeff Bell, and Francis P. Cannon. Originally named "American Principles in Action," APP was conceived as the activist counterpart to George's academic work — a vehicle for translating natural-law arguments into electoral and legislative strategy.

APP's distinctive contribution to the social-conservative ecosystem is its focus on *using* social issues — rather than economic conservatism — as the primary lever for turning out conservative voters and electing conservative candidates. It has been particularly active in opposing Common Core education standards, same-sex marriage, transgender-related policies, and any liberalization of abortion law. APP's current president, Terry Schilling, has made transgender policy in youth sports and schools a primary campaign focus.

## Identity

APP occupies the advocacy/electoral niche that the [[Witherspoon Institute]] cannot fill as a 501(c)(3). Together they form a two-track operation: Witherspoon produces the scholarship; APP operationalizes it in campaigns, lobbying, and electoral spending. This 501(c)(3)/501(c)(4) pairing is a standard model in the conservative infrastructure (cf. [[Heritage Foundation]] / Heritage Action; ADF Research Institute / [[Alliance Defending Freedom]]).

The organization's founding rationale was George's conviction that social conservatives were losing electoral battles because economic conservatives had captured the party's messaging — APP would restore the primacy of social issues. Under Schilling, the emphasis has shifted toward anti-transgender messaging as the primary organizing vehicle.

## Leadership & key personnel

- **[[Robert P. George]]** — co-founder; Princeton professor; provided intellectual framework and initial legitimacy.
- **Jeff Bell** — co-founder; Republican political consultant; author of *Populism and Elitism: Politics in the Age of Equality* (1992); died 2015.
- **Francis P. Cannon** — co-founder.
- **Terry Schilling** — current president; transformed APP into one of the most active anti-transgender-policy organizations in the conservative movement by the early 2020s.

## Activities

- Electoral independent expenditures and issue advertising against candidates deemed insufficiently conservative on social issues.
- Legislative lobbying at state and federal level on abortion, marriage, education standards, and transgender policy.
- Produced research and polling arguing that social issues, not economic issues, are the superior driver of conservative electoral turnout.
- Campaign against Common Core education standards (2012–2016) was an early high-profile project.
- Post-2016: shifted major focus to anti-transgender policy advocacy; Schilling has testified before state legislatures and congressional committees.
- Maintains policy arm (APP Foundation) that produces policy papers and analysis.

## Controversies / investigations

- APP's anti-transgender campaign has been characterized by critics (GLAAD, Southern Poverty Law Center) as a coordinated effort to use transgender youth as a political wedge issue regardless of harm to the affected population.
- George's founding role has prompted criticism of the distance between his academic natural-law framing and APP's more openly partisan electoral tactics.

## Open questions

- The degree to which APP coordinates with or receives funding from [[Leonard Leo]]'s dark money network (Marble Freedom Trust, 85 Fund) has not been confirmed from primary sources reviewed.
- APP's role in state-level anti-transgender legislation — specifically whether it is drafting model bills or primarily lobbying — warrants further investigation.
