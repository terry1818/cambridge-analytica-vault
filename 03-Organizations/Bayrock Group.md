## Summary

Bayrock Group LLC was a New York City real estate investment and development firm founded in 2001 by Tevfik Arif, a former Soviet official from Kazakhstan. Bayrock operated from offices inside Trump Tower and entered into a close partnership with the Trump Organization on luxury real estate projects between approximately 2003 and 2008, most prominently Trump SoHo (a hotel-condominium in Manhattan) and Trump International Hotel and Tower Fort Lauderdale. [[Felix Sater]], a convicted stock fraudster turned FBI/CIA cooperating witness, served as managing director. Craig Unger's *House of Trump, House of Putin* (2018), and subsequent investigative journalism, argue that Bayrock served as a conduit for post-Soviet capital — including money of uncertain or criminal provenance — into US real estate through the Trump brand.

## Identity

Bayrock presented itself as a standard real estate development and investment company specializing in luxury mixed-use projects. Its competitive advantage was access to capital from former Soviet states combined with the Trump brand's appeal to international buyers. Its collapse following the 2008 financial crisis, combined with the exposure of Sater's criminal record, ended active operations. A whistleblower lawsuit filed by former CFO Jody Kriss alleged a pattern of financial improprieties including tax fraud, money laundering, and improper payments; this litigation extended the company's legal footprint into the 2010s.

## Leadership & Key Personnel

- **Tevfik Arif**: Founder; former Soviet-era official in Kazakhstan who worked in the Ministry of Trade before emigrating; little is publicly known about his capital sources.
- **[[Felix Sater]]**: Managing director 2003–2008; drove Trump partnership; criminal background emerged publicly in 2007–2008.
- **Jody Kriss**: CFO; filed a whistleblower lawsuit in 2010 alleging financial crimes at Bayrock, providing the most detailed internal account.

## Funding & Money Flows

The origin of Bayrock's capital is a central open question. Unger and other investigators note:
- Bayrock's investors included individuals from Russia, Kazakhstan, and other former Soviet states.
- Arif's own capital sources were never publicly explained; his Soviet-era connections remain opaque.
- The Kriss lawsuit alleged improper transfers, undisclosed investors, and systematic tax fraud.
- One investor, Tamir Sapir (the co-developer on Trump SoHo via the Sapir Organization), was himself a Georgian immigrant with opaque Soviet-era business background.

No US court has found that Bayrock was used as a vehicle for money laundering from Russia; these remain allegations in civil litigation and investigative journalism (evidence tier: T2–T3).

## Activities

- Development of Trump SoHo hotel-condominium (2006–2010)
- Development of Trump International Hotel and Tower Fort Lauderdale (partnership with Trump, ultimately collapsed)
- Licensing negotiations with Trump Organization for additional projects in multiple countries
- Sater-led Trump Tower Moscow negotiations (2015–2016; after Bayrock's operational period but using the same network)

## Controversies / Investigations

- **Kriss whistleblower lawsuit**: Most detailed internal allegation of fraud; alleges Bayrock was used to launder money and evade taxes. Evidence tier: T2 (civil complaint; not adjudicated as of writing).
- **Mueller investigation**: Bayrock and Sater's activities were investigated as part of the broader Trump-Russia inquiry. The Mueller Report references Bayrock but no charges were filed against Bayrock or Arif.
- **Trump Organization connection**: Trump received an 18% equity stake in Trump SoHo in exchange for branding; the value of this stake and the propriety of the partnership were scrutinized.

## Open Questions

- What were the original sources of Bayrock's capital, and were any funds traceable to Russian criminal networks or state-adjacent entities?
- Was Arif's Soviet-era background relevant to his investor relationships?
- What became of the legal proceedings initiated by Kriss?
