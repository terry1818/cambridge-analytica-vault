## Summary

Definers Public Affairs was a Washington, D.C.-based corporate opposition-research firm founded in 2016 by Republican political operatives Matt Rhoades and Joe Pounder. The firm brought "political campaign type research and communication tactics" to corporate consulting. It became a major controversy node in late 2018 when the *New York Times* reported that [[Facebook]] had engaged Definers to conduct opposition research against the company's critics — including seeding stories to journalists that linked philanthropist [[George Soros]] to the anti-Facebook advocacy movement. The episode exposed how Facebook's leadership attempted to neutralize reputational threats through political dark-arts methods rather than substantive policy engagement.

## Identity

Definers was closely connected to America Rising LLC, a Republican PAC co-founded by Rhoades that specializes in opposition research for Republican candidates and conservative groups. The two organizations shared senior executives. Definers applied the same rapid-research-and-distribute model used in electoral campaigns to corporate clients — in this case, running negative research on technology industry rivals and civil-society critics of those clients.

## Leadership & Key Personnel

- **Matt Rhoades** — Co-founder. Managed Mitt Romney's 2012 presidential campaign; also founded America Rising.
- **Joe Pounder** — Co-founder. Former RNC research director.
- **Tim Miller** — Led the firm's Silicon Valley operation from September 2016. Former communications director for Jeb Bush's 2016 presidential campaign; later became a prominent anti-Trump Republican commentator.

## Funding & Money Flows

Facebook hired Definers in approximately October 2017, expanding the relationship during 2018 amid mounting scrutiny of the platform over Russian election interference and the [[Cambridge Analytica]] scandal. The financial terms of the Facebook contract were not publicly disclosed. Responsibility for the hiring was formally attributed by Facebook to outgoing head of communications Elliot Schrage; [[Sheryl Sandberg]] also played a documented role in directing Definers' work.

## Activities

Definers undertook the following documented activities on behalf of Facebook:
1. **Soros research**: In January 2018, after George Soros called Facebook a "menace to society" at Davos, Definers was tasked to "work on George Soros." The firm subsequently circulated research to journalists alleging that Soros was funding much of the organized opposition to Facebook, invoking well-documented antisemitic tropes about Soros as a hidden manipulator.
2. **Competitor targeting**: Definers produced research attacking Facebook rivals and framing them as equally problematic, attempting to deflect scrutiny by creating "whataboutism" narratives in the press.
3. **Civil society monitoring**: The firm tracked and built dossiers on organizations and individuals critical of Facebook's data practices and political influence.

## Successor / Related Entities

Definers' principals dispersed; Matt Rhoades' America Rising continued operations in the electoral space. Tim Miller went on to become a prominent Never-Trump commentator and author.

## Controversies / Investigations

The *New York Times* investigation (November 14, 2018) was the primary disclosure event. It revealed that Facebook's crisis-management strategy included deploying astroturfing tactics and weaponizing antisemitic narratives about Soros. The revelations intensified congressional scrutiny of Facebook. [[Joel Kaplan]] — Facebook's VP of Global Public Policy and senior Republican voice — was not identified as directly managing Definers, but operated in the same political-influence ecosystem within Facebook.

The episode is directly relevant to the vault's platform-responses thread: rather than reforming internal practices after 2016, Facebook's leadership invested in narrative suppression through a political mercenary firm.
