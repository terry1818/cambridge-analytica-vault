# Koch network

Political donor network organized by Charles and David Koch (Koch Industries). David Koch died 2019; Charles Koch continues as principal. Headquarters: Wichita, Kansas (Koch Industries) and Arlington, VA (political organizations).

## Component organizations

- Americans for Prosperity (AFP) - primary 501(c)(4) advocacy arm
- AFP Foundation - 501(c)(3) educational arm
- Stand Together (rebranded from Seminar Network) - donor coordination body
- LIBRE Initiative - Hispanic outreach
- Concerned Veterans for America
- Generation Opportunity

## Strategic positioning over time

- Originally libertarian-think-tank focused (1970s-2000s)
- Tea Party era (2009-2014): massive scale-up; helped fund / coordinate Tea Party organizing
- Trump-skeptical 2015-2024: Koch network refused to endorse Trump in 2016, 2020, and 2024 primaries
- 2024 general: backed Trump tactically but maintained organizational independence from Trump apparatus

## Documented spending

- Cumulative ~+ in election cycles since 2010
- Major funder of [[Cato Institute]], Mercatus Center, [[Federalist Society]], [[Heritage Foundation]] (selectively)
- Tax policy and regulatory rollback have been consistent priorities

## Relationship to other vault clusters

- Tech right overlap: [[Peter Thiel]] and Koch network share libertarian instincts but Koch network has been less Trump-aligned
- Religious right tension: Koch network is secular libertarian; periodic conflict with Religious Right priorities (immigration, drug policy)
- The post-2024 question: whether Koch network integrates into the dominant Trump coalition or maintains an independent track
