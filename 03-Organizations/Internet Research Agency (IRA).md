# Internet Research Agency (IRA)

Russian online influence operation based in St. Petersburg. Financed primarily by **[[Yevgeny Prigozhin]]** ("Putin's chef"; also founder of [[Wagner Group]]). Conducted **systematic social media disinformation campaign targeting the 2016 US presidential election** in coordination with broader Russian active measures. **Indicted as an entity by Mueller February 16, 2018** along with 13 Russian nationals.

## Operations targeting 2016 US election (Tier 1)

Per [[Source: Mueller Report 2019]] Volume I and Senate Intelligence Committee bipartisan reports:

### Scale
- Reached approximately 126 million Americans on Facebook
- 1.4 million tweets from IRA-controlled Twitter accounts
- 80,000+ Facebook posts
- Tens of thousands of paid Facebook ads
- YouTube content; Instagram content

### Themes
- Pro-Trump (substantially)
- Anti-Clinton
- Voter suppression messaging targeting African-American voters
- Race-baiting on both sides (left and right)
- Anti-establishment messaging
- Promotion of real and fabricated divisive content

### In-person operations
- IRA operatives traveled to US for in-person reconnaissance
- Organized actual (small) rallies in US through fake organizing accounts
- Hired US individuals (typically unaware they were dealing with Russians) for political activities

## February 16 2018 indictment

Mueller's grand jury indicted:
- Internet Research Agency LLC
- Two related entities (Concord Management and Consulting; Concord Catering)
- 13 Russian individuals including Yevgeny Prigozhin

Charged with:
- Conspiracy to defraud the United States
- Conspiracy to commit wire fraud and bank fraud
- Aggravated identity theft

The individuals were not arrested (in Russia); the entities did not appear at trial. Concord Management briefly engaged US counsel and contested charges before withdrawing.

## Subsequent operations (post-2016)

- Continued operations during 2018 midterms
- Continued operations during 2020 presidential election
- Operations expanded to other regions
