# International House of Prayer (IHOPKC)

## Summary

The International House of Prayer of Kansas City (IHOPKC) was a charismatic Christian ministry founded in 1999 by [[Mike Bickle]] and built around a continuous, "24/7" prayer-and-worship room that ran without interruption for more than two decades. Its theology centered on end-times "forerunner" expectation — the belief that an intercessory remnant would prepare the way for Christ's return — and it became a major hub of the broader prophetic-charismatic movement adjacent to the [[New Apostolic Reformation]]. At its height IHOPKC anchored a Kansas City campus that included a ministry university (IHOPU), the Forerunner Church, internships, and a globally distributed network of affiliated prayer rooms; it drew thousands of young adults into multi-year unpaid or low-paid internships. The ministry collapsed in 2024 after multiple women came forward with credible, corroborated accounts that Bickle had committed sexual abuse and misconduct over several decades — including allegations of grooming and abusing a minor in the 1980s, before IHOPKC's founding. The scandal makes IHOPKC a documented case study in how charismatic "prophetic authority" can be weaponized to enforce silence and protect a leader.

## Identity

- **Legal name(s):** International House of Prayer of Kansas City; commonly IHOPKC / IHOP-KC
- **Other names / fronts:** International House of Prayer (the variant title; this note carries it as an alias)
- **Founded:** May 1999, by Mike Bickle, Grandview / Kansas City, Missouri
- **Dissolved / restructured:** 2024 — ministry severed ties with Bickle, announced closure of its university, and Forerunner Church held a final service
- **Parent / owner:** None — independent ministry
- **Subsidiaries:** International House of Prayer University (IHOPU); Forerunner Church; numerous affiliated prayer rooms
- **Registered jurisdiction:** United States (Missouri)

## Leadership & key personnel

- **[[Mike Bickle]]** — founder and dominant figure; built the 24/7 prayer model and the forerunner theology; permanently severed from the ministry in December 2023 after an internal investigation confirmed "inappropriate behavior."
- **IHOPKC executive leadership** — a team that initially dismissed the 2023 allegations, then commissioned an investigation and ultimately announced the ministry's wind-down in 2024.

## Funding & money flows

IHOPKC was donor-funded, sustained heavily by individual donors and the families of interns and missionary staff. The Roys Report cited leaked internal recordings indicating the ministry was losing roughly **$500,000 per month** in the wake of the scandal because so much of its donor base was personally tied to Bickle — a figure that illustrates how dependent the organization was on its founder's personal following.

## Activities

IHOPKC's defining activity was the **24/7 prayer room** — continuous, unbroken intercession and worship sustained by rotating teams. Around that core it ran IHOPU, internship and "intern track" programs, conferences (notably the long-running "Onething" young-adult conference), and a missions-sending operation. Its forerunner theology framed participants as a divinely commissioned vanguard preparing for the end times; this gave the ministry an intense, high-commitment culture in which Bickle's prophetic pronouncements carried enormous weight.

That same culture is what the abuse accounts indict. Survivors and witnesses described a pattern in which Bickle's "prophetic authority" was used both to approach victims and to suppress dissent: a recurring theme reported by multiple women was Bickle telling young women that God had revealed his wife would soon die and that he would marry them; he insisted on secrecy and reportedly framed anyone who tried to expose or restrain him as working with the devil. Concerns raised with leadership over the years were dismissed. This is a documented mechanism of abuse-enabling, not a contested interpretation — it is corroborated by multiple independent survivor accounts and reporting.

IHOPKC's political relevance to this vault is indirect but real: it was a major node in the charismatic-prophetic ecosystem from which the [[New Apostolic Reformation]] and figures like [[Lance Wallnau]] and [[Dutch Sheets]] drew, and it shaped a generation of young charismatic leaders. Bickle himself was associated with the broader prophetic movement that supplied theological framing for pro-Trump mobilization, though IHOPKC as an institution kept a lower partisan profile than overtly political ministries.
