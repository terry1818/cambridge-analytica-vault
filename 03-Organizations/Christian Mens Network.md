## Summary

Christian Men's Network (CMN) was founded in 1977 by Edwin Louis Cole (1922–2002), who is credited as "the Father of the Christian Men's Movement." Cole built his ministry around the thesis that "manhood and Christlikeness are synonymous" and produced a body of writing — most notably *Maximized Manhood* (1982) — that sold over four million copies in forty-plus languages. Kristin Kobes Du Mez's *Jesus and John Wayne* (2020) identifies CMN as the institutional and ideological foundation on which later evangelical men's movements, including [[Promise Keepers]], were built. Cole's framework — Christian manhood as strength, courage, discipline, and headship — became the template for evangelical masculinity theology in the late 20th and early 21st centuries.

## Identity

Cole's ministry articulated a theology of masculine identity grounded in his reading of biblical manhood: men are called to be "real men" in the sense of physical and spiritual courage, sexual discipline, provider and protector roles, and spiritual headship of family. The ministry's *Maximized Manhood* concept framed these obligations not as burden but as the means by which men achieved their God-ordained potential.

Du Mez documents how Cole's framework was explicitly militaristic: Christianity was presented as a masculine, warrior faith, and the domestication or feminization of Christian men was treated as a theological and cultural crisis. This framing ran directly contrary to the gentle, nurturing masculinity of mainline Protestant and progressive evangelical traditions.

Cole founded CMN in 1977 and focused exclusively on the men's ministry after April 1984 when he resigned other positions. His ministry grew slowly in the 1980s and became influential in the early 1990s, coinciding with the rise of [[Promise Keepers]]. Bill McCartney, Promise Keepers' founder, acknowledged Cole's influence. CMN's *Majoring in Men* curriculum became a widely used discipleship tool in evangelical men's small groups.

## Leadership & Key Personnel

- **Edwin Louis Cole** (1922–2002): Founder; born Dallas, grew up in Los Angeles; raised in Angelus Temple (Aimee Semple McPherson's church); USCG veteran (WWII); met wife Nancy Corbett in the Coast Guard; died 2002
- Cole's son and organizational heirs continued CMN after his death

## Funding & Money Flows

CMN is a ministry nonprofit. Revenue derived primarily from book sales, speaking engagements, curriculum licensing, and donor support. No documented ties to major dark-money foundations in available sources.

## Controversies / Investigations

No documented major controversies comparable to [[Mars Hill Church]] collapse. CMN's theology has been critiqued by feminist theologians and egalitarian evangelicals as reinforcing patriarchal family structures. Cole's *Maximized Manhood* framing has been analyzed by scholars of gender and religion as part of the backlash against 1970s feminism within evangelical culture.
