# Eagle Forum

US conservative interest group founded 1972 by [[Phyllis Schlafly]]. Headquartered in Alton, Illinois.

## Founding mission

- Founded specifically to oppose the Equal Rights Amendment (ERA)
- Schlafly's STOP ERA campaign (Stop Taking Our Privileges) successfully blocked ERA ratification after Congress proposed it in 1972
- The campaign became the prototype for conservative movement organizing at the state-legislature level

## Long-term influence

- Eagle Forum chapters in most US states
- Annual conventions function as a movement-organizing forum
- Phyllis Schlafly's son [[Andy Schlafly]] continued operations after her death 2016
- Split 2016 between traditionalists (Phyllis Schlafly Eagles, allied with Trump) and reform faction (Eagle Forum Education and Legal Defense Fund)

## Significance for vault

Eagle Forum is the foundational state-legislature-organizing model that [[Convention of States Action]] explicitly draws from. Phyllis Schlafly's playbook (state-by-state legislator targeting, model legislation, persistent multi-cycle organizing) is the methodology that has produced COS Action's [[19 state applications]].
