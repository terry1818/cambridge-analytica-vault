## Summary

The Silver Legion of America, universally known as the "Silver Shirts," was an American fascist paramilitary founded by [[William Dudley Pelley]] on 30 January 1933 — the day Adolf Hitler was appointed Reich Chancellor — making the symbolism explicit. Members wore silver shirts with a scarlet "L" on the left breast (for "Liberation"), blue ties, and blue corduroy trousers, closely modelling the Nazi SA uniform aesthetic. At its peak in 1935 the organisation claimed approximately 15,000 members across chapters in at least 46 states. It called for a "Christian Commonwealth" combining fascism, antisemitism, and theocracy. Rachel Maddow's *Prequel* uses the Silver Legion as evidence that American fascism was a genuine mass movement with organisational infrastructure, not a foreign import grafted onto unwilling soil.

## Identity

The Silver Legion's ideology fused explicit American fascism with Christian Identity-adjacent Protestant mysticism drawn from Pelley's occultist beliefs. It combined:
- Antisemitism as a theological and political programme
- Explicit admiration for Hitler as a model leader
- Calls to exclude Jews and non-whites from a reconstituted American government
- Paramilitary organisation with uniforms, salutes, and hierarchical ranks

The "Christian Patriots" self-designation placed the movement within the tradition of Christian nationalism, a lineage that remains relevant to the vault's research arc on [[Christian nationalism]].

## Leadership & key personnel

- **William Dudley Pelley** (1890–1965): Founder and sole leader; see [[William Dudley Pelley]] for full profile.
- State and local commanders managed chapters; few achieved independent public prominence.

## Funding & money flows

- Membership dues and publications revenue
- Pelley ran a publishing enterprise (Galahad Press) that subsidised the political organisation
- No confirmed evidence of foreign (Nazi German) government funding, though this was alleged

## Activities

- Paramilitary drilling and marching in silver uniforms
- Publication of antisemitic pamphlets, newspapers, and books through Pelley's Galahad Press
- Establishment of a national network of chapters (reportedly 46 states)
- Pelley's 1936 presidential campaign on the Christian Party ticket — gained ballot access in Washington state only, receiving negligible votes
- Operation of "Headquarters" and a "Silver Ranger" youth auxiliary
- Active intelligence-gathering on perceived enemies (Jews, communists)

## Successor / related entities

- [[Christian Front]] — contemporary ideological peer; similar Catholic/Protestant antisemitic militia tradition

- No direct organisational successor; Pelley was imprisoned; the organisation collapsed completely

## Controversies / investigations

- McCormack-Dickstein Committee (1934) investigated foreign fascist activities in the US; examined the Silver Legion
- Dies Committee (HUAC precursor) monitored the organisation
- Pelley arrested in 1942 for sedition; convicted on 11 of 12 counts; sentenced to 15 years (released 1950)
- Murphy Ranch (Los Angeles) was a Silver Legion supply depot; raided by LAPD after Pearl Harbor
