# Susan B. Anthony Pro-Life America

## Summary

Susan B. Anthony Pro-Life America — known for most of its history as the **Susan B. Anthony List (SBA List)** — is an American 501(c)(4) anti-abortion advocacy organization founded in 1993. Its defining purpose is electoral: it works to elect anti-abortion candidates to federal and state office, with a particular early emphasis on anti-abortion women, and to defeat candidates who support abortion rights. The organization was founded by sociologist and psychologist Rachel MacNair explicitly as a counter to EMILY's List, the pro-abortion-rights group credited with helping elect a wave of women to Congress in the 1992 "Year of the Woman." The name invokes the 19th-century suffragist Susan B. Anthony, whom the organization claims as an opponent of abortion — a historically contested attribution.

The group matters to this vault as one of the most effective pieces of the religious right's electoral infrastructure. Under President **Marjorie Dannenfelser** — recruited by MacNair as an early leader and president since 2006 — SBA List built a large-scale voter-contact and candidate-funding operation, operating political action committees (the SBA Pro-Life America Candidate Fund and Women Speak Out PAC) and running door-knocking and canvassing programs in battleground states. It was deeply involved in mobilizing anti-abortion voters for [[Donald Trump]] in 2016 and after, and Dannenfelser served on Trump's pro-life coalition. In June 2022 — the month the Supreme Court overturned *Roe v. Wade* in *Dobbs* — the group rebranded as Susan B. Anthony Pro-Life America, a name deliberately mirroring NARAL Pro-Choice America.

## Leadership & key personnel

- **Marjorie Dannenfelser** — President since 2006; recruited by MacNair as an early leader; previously executive director; the organization's primary public face; author of *Life Is Winning*.
- **Rachel MacNair** — founder (1993); sociologist and psychologist; recruited Dannenfelser and Jane Abraham as the group's first experienced leaders.
- **Jane Abraham** — board chairwoman; served as president from 1997 to 2006, before Dannenfelser.

## Funding & money flows

As a 501(c)(4) with affiliated political action committees, SBA / Susan B. Anthony Pro-Life America raises money from individual donors and conservative foundations and channels it into candidate support, independent expenditures, and voter-contact field programs. It is part of the broader donor ecosystem of the anti-abortion and religious-right movement; precise current donor breakdowns are not fully public.

## Activities

- **Candidate support and opposition:** funding and endorsing anti-abortion candidates (primarily, historically, women) and working to defeat abortion-rights candidates, via its PACs.
- **Voter contact:** large-scale door-knocking and canvassing operations targeting anti-abortion and persuadable voters in swing states.
- **Coalition work:** organizing the anti-abortion bloc within Republican presidential campaigns; Dannenfelser chaired pro-life coalitions for Trump campaigns.
- **Policy advocacy:** lobbying for abortion restrictions at the federal and state level.

## Timeline

- `1993` — Susan B. Anthony List founded by Rachel MacNair as a counter to EMILY's List
- `1990s` — MacNair recruits Marjorie Dannenfelser and Jane Abraham as the first experienced leaders
- `1997` — Jane Abraham becomes president
- `2006` — Marjorie Dannenfelser becomes president
- `2014` — *Susan B. Anthony List v. Driehaus* decided by the U.S. Supreme Court (a First Amendment standing case arising from a 2010 SBA List billboard dispute)
- `2016` — SBA List runs major field operations for anti-abortion candidates; backs Donald Trump
- `2020` — Continues large-scale battleground voter-contact programs
- `2022-06` — *Dobbs v. Jackson* overturns *Roe v. Wade*; the group rebrands as Susan B. Anthony Pro-Life America
- `2024–2026` — Remains active in post-*Dobbs* state-level abortion-policy fights and federal elections

## Successor / related entities

- **SBA Pro-Life America Candidate Fund** — the organization's candidate-funding political action committee.
- **Women Speak Out PAC** — affiliated PAC running independent expenditures and field programs.
- **Charlotte Lozier Institute** — the movement's affiliated research arm (SBA's "education and research" counterpart).

## Controversies / investigations

- **The Susan B. Anthony attribution** — the organization's foundational claim that suffragist Susan B. Anthony opposed abortion is disputed by many historians, who argue the attribution rests on thin and contested evidence. `#claim/disputed`
- **Susan B. Anthony List v. Driehaus (2014)** — the group's billboard claims about a member of Congress led to a Supreme Court case on First Amendment standing; critics characterized the underlying claims as misleading.
- **Electoral-effectiveness debate** — like other large field operations, the measurable independent impact of SBA's voter-contact programs is debated among political scientists.
