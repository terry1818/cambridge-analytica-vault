# Wilks Family Foundation

## Summary

The Wilks Family Foundation is shorthand for the philanthropic and political-giving network of Farris Wilks and his younger brother Dan Wilks — Texas brothers who began as masons, founded the hydraulic-fracturing company FracTech (later Frac Tech Services) in 2002, and in 2011 sold their roughly 70% stake for a reported $3.2 billion. With that fortune the brothers became among the most prolific funders of the American religious right and hard-conservative politics. The "foundation" is in practice two principal vehicles: the **Thirteen Foundation**, controlled by Farris Wilks and his wife Jo Ann, and the **Heavenly Father's Foundation**, controlled by Dan Wilks and his wife Staci. Both were seeded with proceeds of the 2011 FracTech sale.

The Wilks brothers belong squarely in the vault's patron wing, but with a distinct theocratic emphasis. Farris Wilks is a pastor of the Assembly of Yahweh (7th Day), a small Christian church near Cisco, Texas, and the family's giving has fused free-market politics with conservative Christian and Christian-nationalist causes. Through the Thirteen Foundation, Farris Wilks reportedly gave around $110 million to right-wing and religious causes, including roughly $4.7 million in seed funding for Ben Shapiro's The Daily Wire; the brothers were also significant backers of PragerU. In direct political giving, the brothers and their wives made a record-breaking $15 million super-PAC donation to support [[Ted Cruz]]'s 2016 presidential campaign and were major backers of Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton. The Wilkses sit at the intersection of the vault's "patron-wing," "Christian nationalism," and "constitutional-capture" threads.

## Identity

- **Legal name(s):** Thirteen Foundation; Heavenly Father's Foundation; assorted Wilks family LLCs and donor entities.
- **Other names / fronts:** "Wilks Family Foundation" is a colloquial umbrella, not a single legal entity.
- **Founded:** Thirteen Foundation incorporated 2011, after the FracTech sale.
- **Parent / owner:** Controlled directly by the Wilks brothers and their wives.
- **Registered jurisdiction:** Texas (Eastland County area; Cisco, Texas).

## Leadership & key personnel

- **Farris Wilks** — co-founder; controls the Thirteen Foundation with wife Jo Ann; pastor of the Assembly of Yahweh (7th Day).
- **Dan Wilks** — co-founder; controls the Heavenly Father's Foundation with wife Staci.
- **Jo Ann Wilks** — co-controls the Thirteen Foundation.
- **Staci Wilks** — co-controls the Heavenly Father's Foundation.

## Funding & money flows

- **Source of wealth:** The 2011 sale of the brothers' ~70% stake in Frac Tech Services for a reported $3.2 billion.
- **Charitable giving:** Farris Wilks reportedly directed roughly $110 million through the Thirteen Foundation to right-wing and religious causes; Dan Wilks's Heavenly Father's Foundation gave on a comparable scale.
- **Media funding:** ~$4.7 million in seed funding for Ben Shapiro's The Daily Wire; significant support for PragerU.
- **Political donations:** Over ~$27 million given to state and federal political candidates over roughly fifteen years; a record $15 million 2016 super-PAC gift backing [[Ted Cruz]]; major backing of Ken Paxton.
- The two-foundation structure means giving is split and reported separately, complicating any single tally.

## Activities

The Wilks network funds three overlapping areas. **(1) Religious-right media and education** — The Daily Wire, PragerU, and other conservative-Christian content operations, plus support for anti-abortion and "biblical worldview" education and ministry. **(2) Hard-conservative electoral politics** — large super-PAC contributions to presidential and statewide candidates, and funding of insurgent challengers in Texas legislative primaries (notably against then–Texas House Speaker Joe Straus and his allies). **(3) Land acquisition** — the brothers became among the largest private landowners in the United States, with extensive ranch holdings in Texas, Montana, Idaho, and elsewhere, a fact frequently noted in reporting on their low public profile.

The Wilkses are notable for combining the patron-wing free-market politics of the [[Koch Network]] with an explicitly theocratic, charismatic-Christian orientation — making them a bridge node between the vault's "patron-wing" and "Christian nationalism" clusters.

## Successor / related entities

- **Frac Tech Services** — the source company; sold 2011.
- **The Daily Wire** — conservative media company seeded with Wilks money.
- **PragerU** — conservative "education" media operation supported by the Wilkses.
- **[[Club for Growth]]** — anti-tax PAC operating in the same hard-right electoral space.
- **Assembly of Yahweh (7th Day)** — Farris Wilks's church; the religious base of the family's theocratic giving.

## Controversies / investigations

- The Wilks brothers' frac-sand and ranching operations have drawn local controversy, including disputes over public road access across newly acquired ranch land in Idaho and Montana.
- Their political spending against the Texas House leadership made them a recurring subject of Texas campaign-finance reporting.
- As private-foundation and super-PAC giving, much of the network's flow is documented in tax filings and FEC records, but the split-entity structure and use of intermediary PACs limit transparency.

## Open questions

- The full, current scale of Wilks giving is hard to total because it runs through two separate foundations plus direct PAC contributions and LLCs.
- Farris Wilks's sharply reduced public profile after the early 2020s — and what it means for the network's future direction — is an open reporting question.
- How tightly the Wilks religious-right giving coordinates with the broader [[Koch Network]] free-market infrastructure, versus operating as a parallel theocratic-patron stream, is a matter of interpretation.
