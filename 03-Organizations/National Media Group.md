# National Media Group

## Summary

> **Identification note.** "National Media Group" is not a precise registered name and is genuinely ambiguous. This note builds the most defensible identification: **National Media Research, Planning & Placement, LLC** (NMRPP, often shortened to "National Media"), the leading Republican political media-buying and advertising-placement firm, based in Alexandria, Virginia. NMRPP is the entity most consistently referred to as "National Media" within US political-vendor and campaign-finance contexts. If a source uses "National Media Group" to mean a different entity, this identification should be revisited; see Open Questions. The original phrasing is preserved as an alias.

National Media Research, Planning & Placement is one of the largest and most influential Republican political advertising agencies in the United States. The firm specializes in media targeting, strategic planning, and ad-buy placement for Republican candidates, party committees, public-affairs campaigns, and issue advocacy. NMRPP placed media for the Bush–Cheney presidential campaigns in 2000 and 2004 — and is credited as the first political shop to use syndicated consumer research to guide political ad buys, an early form of the data-driven audience targeting that later became central to firms like [[Cambridge Analytica]]. Its 2012-cycle clients included the [[Republican National Committee]] and the National Republican Congressional Committee. NMRPP matters to this vault as a piece of the Republican political-vendor infrastructure: the unglamorous, professionalized layer that converts campaign strategy and donor money into placed advertising. It represents the "legitimate" commercial ancestor of the psychographic-targeting model — using consumer data to decide where political messages run — and helps locate where ordinary political-media practice ends and the [[Cambridge Analytica]]-style profiling project begins.

## Funding & money flows

NMRPP is a vendor: its money flows are the reverse of a donor-funded organization. It is *paid by* campaigns, party committees, super PACs, and advocacy clients to plan and place advertising. Because political media buys are large and reported in FEC filings, NMRPP's footprint is partially traceable through campaign-finance disclosures — payments to "National Media" or NMRPP appear across numerous Republican committee filings. This makes it a useful follow-the-money node for tracing where Republican advertising spend is concentrated.

## Activities

- **Media planning and buying:** Determining which television, radio, digital, and other channels a campaign's advertising should run on, and purchasing that placement.
- **Audience targeting / segmentation:** NMRPP was an early adopter of syndicated consumer research (e.g., Scarborough, later set-top-box and addressable-TV data) to match political messages to audiences — a precursor logic to psychographic and data-broker-driven targeting.
- **Public-affairs and issue advertising:** Beyond candidate campaigns, the firm places advertising for corporate-reputation and issue-advocacy clients.
