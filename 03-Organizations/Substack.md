# Substack

## Summary

Substack is a San Francisco-based subscription publishing platform founded in 2017 by Chris Best (a co-founder of Kik Messenger), Jairaj Sethi, and Hamish McKenzie (a former tech reporter). It lets writers publish newsletters and charge readers directly, with Substack taking a percentage of subscription revenue. The platform was inspired by Ben Thompson's independent subscription newsletter *Stratechery* and is built on a thesis that direct writer-to-reader relationships can replace advertising-funded and institution-mediated journalism. Substack matters to this vault not as a partisan outlet but as infrastructure: it became a central host for "heterodox," contrarian, and right-leaning independent media — a place where writers who had left or been pushed out of mainstream institutions could rebuild audiences and revenue. That same disintermediation made the platform a venue for figures and narratives at the edge of and beyond the [[MAGA media ecosystem]]. In November 2023 Substack faced sustained criticism — including an open letter signed by more than 100 of its own writers — for hosting publications by white nationalists, neo-Nazis, and antisemites; co-founder Hamish McKenzie publicly defended a near-absolutist content-moderation posture, arguing that censorship would worsen rather than reduce extremism. Substack is a useful node for analyzing how "free speech" platform design choices shape the distribution of [[disinformation]] and extremist [[propaganda]].

## Funding & money flows

Substack is a venture-backed company; Andreessen Horowitz led a significant 2021 funding round (reported at roughly $65 million), and the company has run additional rounds including a community/investor round. Substack's revenue model is a percentage cut (around 10%) of writers' subscription income, plus payment-processing fees. The platform's economics directly incentivize retaining high-earning writers — a structural fact relevant to its reluctance to remove controversial but popular publications.

## Activities

Substack provides hosting, payment processing, email distribution, and discovery tools for independent writers. Since 2019 it has added podcasts, discussion threads, a Notes social feed, and a mobile app, gradually shifting from a pure newsletter tool toward a social-media-adjacent platform. For this vault, Substack's significant activity is its role as a redistribution and monetization layer for independent political media — including journalists doing accountability reporting (the [[Premise Data]] note in this vault cites Jack Poulson's Substack) but also figures advancing contrarian, anti-establishment, and at times disinformation-adjacent narratives.

## Controversies / investigations

- **2023 extremist-content controversy:** An open letter from 100+ writers, the "Substackers Against Nazis" campaign, and reporting (notably by *The Atlantic*) documented Nazi and white-nationalist newsletters earning revenue on the platform. Substack removed a small number of publications but declined a broader policy change. `#claim/documented`
- Ongoing debate over whether Substack's near-absolutist moderation stance functionally subsidizes extremist [[propaganda]] by giving it payment infrastructure and discovery.
