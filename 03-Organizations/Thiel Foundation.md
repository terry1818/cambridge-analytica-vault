# Thiel Foundation

## Summary

The Thiel Foundation is the private philanthropic vehicle of [[Peter Thiel]], the billionaire co-founder of PayPal, early investor in Facebook, and co-founder of [[Palantir Technologies]]. Created in 2006, the foundation channels Thiel's wealth toward what he frames as breakthrough technologies and against what he sees as a stagnant, conformist institutional order — particularly higher education and the regulatory state. The foundation has historically run three signature programs: the **Thiel Fellowship** (originally "20 Under 20"), **Breakout Labs**, and **Imitatio**.

The Thiel Foundation is a key node in the vault's "tech-right" and "patron-wing" threads because it operationalizes a distinct version of the patron-class project: not the [[Koch Network]]'s decades-long capture of academic economics, but a more directly **anti-institutional** intervention. The Thiel Fellowship pays talented young people $100,000 to *drop out of college* — a deliberate symbolic and practical attack on the university as a credentialing institution. The foundation's funding of the [[Seasteading Institute]] reflects the same logic at the level of governance: building exit options from existing nation-states rather than reforming them. The foundation channels the libertarian "constitutional capture" impulse — escape democratic majorities, build alternative governance — into the idiom of Silicon Valley.

## Funding & money flows

The Thiel Foundation is funded entirely by Peter Thiel's personal fortune. Reported giving has included:

- **Thiel Fellowship:** $100,000 per fellow over two years, with roughly 20–25 fellows selected annually since 2011.
- **[[Seasteading Institute]]:** Thiel pledged $500,000 on April 15, 2008, to the newly formed institute; by 2011 he had reportedly given a total of about $1.25 million.
- **Anti-aging / life-extension research:** $3.5 million to the Methuselah Mouse Prize / Methuselah Foundation.
- **Machine Intelligence Research Institute (MIRI):** roughly $1.6 million toward AI-risk research.
- **Breakout Labs:** grants to early-stage, speculative scientific ventures considered too radical or long-horizon for conventional funding.

## Activities

**The Thiel Fellowship.** Launched in 2011 as "20 Under 20," the fellowship offers people under ~22–23 a $100,000 grant, mentorship, and a network in exchange for *not* attending or for leaving college, to instead build a startup, pursue research, or work on a social movement. The program is explicitly framed as a critique: that elite higher education is an expensive, conformist credentialing system that suppresses innovation. The fellowship has produced notable startup founders and has become the foundation's most visible and controversial program.

**Breakout Labs.** A grant-making arm funding early-stage science — biotech, materials, energy — too speculative for venture capital and too unconventional for traditional grant agencies.

**Imitatio.** A project devoted to the work of philosopher René Girard, whose "mimetic theory" of desire and rivalry profoundly shaped Thiel's own thinking. Imitatio funds scholarship and events around Girardian ideas.

**Ideological function.** Taken together, the foundation's programs express a coherent worldview: distrust of mass institutions (universities, the regulatory state, democratic governance), faith in a small number of exceptional individuals, and a search for "exit" — alternative spaces (seasteads, charter-city–style enclaves, life-extension, frontier tech) outside the reach of conventional politics. This is the vault's "tech-right" inflection of the older libertarian patron project.

## Controversies / investigations

- The Thiel Fellowship has been criticized by educators as encouraging talented young people to forgo education for speculative ventures; defenders argue it exposes the cost-inflation and conformism of elite universities. `#claim/disputed`
- Thiel's broader political activity — secretly funding the Hulk Hogan lawsuit that destroyed Gawker, and bankrolling political candidates including [[J.D. Vance]] — has made his philanthropy inseparable from his political project in critics' analysis.
- The seasteading and "exit"-focused philanthropy has been criticized as an anti-democratic fantasy of wealthy escape from shared governance.
