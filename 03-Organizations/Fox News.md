## Summary

Fox News Channel launched on October 7, 1996, to 17 million cable subscribers. It was created by media mogul [[Rupert Murdoch]] through News Corporation (now Fox Corporation) and built by founding CEO [[Roger Ailes]], a veteran Republican media consultant who had worked on the Nixon, Reagan, and George H.W. Bush campaigns. The channel was explicitly designed to appeal to a conservative audience that Murdoch and Ailes believed was underserved by existing broadcast news.

By the mid-2000s Fox News had become the highest-rated cable news network in the United States, a position it has held with few interruptions. Benkler, Faris, and Roberts' 2018 analysis in *Network Propaganda* identified Fox News as the anchor of the structurally distinct right-wing media ecosystem — the largest, most-watched node in a cluster of outlets that link primarily to each other and exhibit weaker adherence to mainstream fact-checking norms than the left-center media cluster.

## Identity

Fox News occupies a unique structural position: it is simultaneously a mass-market broadcast property with ratings exceeding CNN and MSNBC combined (in prime time, through much of the 2010s–2020s) and a movement-media outlet whose prime-time hosts routinely amplify narratives that originated in fringe outlets such as [[Breitbart]] and [[The Gateway Pundit]]. This dual role — mainstream distribution paired with partisan content — is what *Network Propaganda* identifies as making Fox uniquely powerful in shaping conservative information flows.

## Leadership & Key Personnel

Roger Ailes ran the network from 1996 until his resignation in July 2016 following sexual harassment allegations (later settled). His departure accelerated the network's drift toward Trump loyalism. [[Sean Hannity]] has been the prime-time anchor most closely identified with MAGA politics. Tucker Carlson hosted the top-rated prime-time slot from 2016 until his abrupt departure in April 2023 amid revelations in the Dominion Voting Systems defamation litigation ($787.5 million settlement, 2023).

## Funding & Money Flows

Fox News generates revenue primarily through cable carriage fees and advertising. It is a subsidiary of Fox Corporation, itself controlled by the Murdoch family through a dual-class share structure. Rupert Murdoch stepped down as Fox Corporation chairman in November 2023, succeeded by his son Lachlan Murdoch.

## Activities

Fox News functions as prime-time political programming complemented by a daytime news operation. The prime-time bloc — historically anchored by Bill O'Reilly (until 2017), Hannity, and Carlson — has been the key distribution channel for narratives that move from fringe right-wing outlets into mainstream conservative opinion. *Network Propaganda* documents how Fox amplified stories originating at [[Breitbart]] and then entering mainstream political debate.

## Timeline

- **1996-10-07** — Launches on cable to 17 million subscribers; [[Roger Ailes]] CEO.
- **2002** — Surpasses CNN in prime-time ratings.
- **2016** — Roger Ailes resigns after harassment allegations; Fox News prime-time hosts [[Sean Hannity]] and others embrace Trump candidacy.
- **2017** — Bill O'Reilly fired after harassment settlements totaling $45 million.
- **2020** — Fox News calls Arizona for Biden on election night; creates internal crisis with Trump.
- **2023-04** — Tucker Carlson fired; Dominion Voting Systems settles defamation suit for $787.5 million.
- **2023-11** — Rupert Murdoch retires; Lachlan Murdoch takes over.

## Successor / Related Entities

- [[Newsmax]] and [[One America News]] (OAN) occupy the fringe-right space to Fox's right.
- [[Tucker Carlson Network]] — Carlson's post-Fox independent streaming venture.

## Controversies / Investigations

- Dominion Voting Systems lawsuit (settled 2023, $787.5 million) revealed internal communications showing hosts privately doubted 2020 election fraud claims they amplified publicly.
- Smartmatic defamation suit (ongoing as of 2026).
- Multiple sexual harassment settlements, including Roger Ailes and Bill O'Reilly.
