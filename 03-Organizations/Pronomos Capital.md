## Summary

Pronomos Capital, founded in 2019 by Patri Friedman, is the first venture capital fund dedicated to early-stage investment in charter city projects. Its thesis is that new political jurisdictions with better rules and institutions create economic value that can be captured through land appreciation, tax revenues, or lease prices. [[Peter Thiel]], [[Marc Andreessen]], [[Balaji Srinivasan]], and early Bitcoin investor Roger Ver were among the initial investors. Pronomos represents the financialization of the [[Plutocratic secession]] and exit-sovereignty project — translating ideological commitment to zone-based governance into a conventional VC asset class.

## Identity

Pronomos characterizes charter cities as "early-stage countries" and applies standard venture logic: invest early in the land-rezoning phase, support institutional design, and exit as economic activity drives asset appreciation. The fund's portfolio includes [[Próspera]] on Roatán, Honduras, and other zone projects worldwide. The name "Pronomos" derives from the Greek for "lawful" or "according to law" — a deliberate signal that the project operates within, not against, existing international legal frameworks.

Patri Friedman is the grandson of economist Milton Friedman. He previously co-founded the Seasteading Institute in 2008 with seed funding from [[Peter Thiel]], an early iteration of the physical-exit project that sought to build floating sovereign platforms beyond national jurisdiction.

## Leadership & key personnel

- **Patri Friedman** — founder and general partner; Google engineer turned libertarian activist; prior: Seasteading Institute founder, Future Cities Development co-founder.
- **[[Peter Thiel]]** — anchor investor; provided ideological and financial validation for the fund at launch.
- **[[Marc Andreessen]]** — investor; ideological alignment via "techno-optimism" and Little Tech agenda.
- **[[Balaji Srinivasan]]** — investor; associated with the adjacent "network state" concept.

## Funding & money flows

At founding in 2019, Thiel and co-investors contributed approximately $9 million. Total reported fundraising reached at least $13.3 million. The fund operates as a standard LP/GP venture structure. Subsequent portfolio includes [[Próspera]] (Honduras), [[Praxis Society]] (as co-investor), and other zone projects.

## Activities

- Early-stage investment and institutional-design support for charter city projects.
- Active portfolio: Próspera ZEDE (Honduras), Praxis, and undisclosed zone projects.
- Networking within the charter-cities movement, including the Charter Cities Institute.
- Patri Friedman participates in conferences and podcasts as a public intellectual for the movement.

## Controversies / investigations

- Critics characterize the fund as backing "colonies of tech bros" — private enclaves for wealthy expatriates that extract value from host nations while avoiding accountability.
- The Próspera ICSID arbitration (claiming ~$11 billion from Honduras) demonstrated the legal enforcement mechanisms underpinning the investment thesis, raising concerns about investor-state mechanisms overriding democratic mandates.
