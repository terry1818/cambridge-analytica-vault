# One America News

## Summary

One America News (OAN), operated by Herring Networks, Inc., is a far-right American cable news channel founded by conservative businessman Robert Herring Sr. It was announced on March 14, 2013 and launched on July 4, 2013. Herring testified in court in 2019 that the network was created at the urging of executives of AT&T, whose subsidiary DirecTV has at points supplied up to roughly 90% of the network's revenue — a dependency that made OAN's later loss of carriage agreements an existential business threat. The network has described itself as one of the "greatest supporters" of [[Donald Trump]] and is known for promoting falsehoods and conspiracy theories. After the 2020 election, OAN became one of the most aggressive broadcast amplifiers of false claims that the election was stolen, asserting that [[Smartmatic]] software "switched votes" from Trump to Joe Biden and stretching the company's past ties to Venezuela into a narrative of foreign election meddling. OAN matters to this vault as the most extreme broadcast node of the [[MAGA media ecosystem]]: smaller and more fringe than [[Fox News]] or [[Newsmax]], it was correspondingly less restrained, and court filings in the resulting litigation suggested OAN executives may have crossed into facilitating, not merely reporting, election-overturn efforts.

## Funding & money flows

OAN's revenue model depended heavily on cable-carriage agreements rather than advertising. Robert Herring testified that DirecTV (an AT&T subsidiary) supplied up to ~90% of the network's revenue and that AT&T executives encouraged the network's creation. DirecTV declined to renew its OAN carriage contract in 2022, a decision OAN characterized as politically motivated and which sharply reduced its distribution and revenue. This carriage dependency is a key structural fact: it explains both OAN's incentive to court a loyal Trump-aligned audience and its vulnerability when distributors withdrew.

## Activities

OAN operates a conservative cable news channel and website, mixing news segments with strongly partisan opinion programming. Its defining activity for this vault was its post-2020 election coverage: OAN broadcast claims that [[Smartmatic]] and [[Dominion Voting Systems]] machines manipulated the vote count. Court filings in Smartmatic's defamation case revealed that, on January 8, 2021, OAN president Charles Herring emailed Sidney Powell a spreadsheet purporting to contain passwords of Smartmatic employees; Smartmatic's lawyers argued the exchange showed OAN executives "may have engaged in criminal activities." OAN was also among the outlets given expanded White House press access in the second Trump administration.
