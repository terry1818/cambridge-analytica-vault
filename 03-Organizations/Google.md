# Google

## Summary

Google LLC, founded in 1998 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin and reorganized under the holding company Alphabet Inc. in 2015, is the dominant global provider of internet search and digital advertising. It is the original and most consequential architect of what Shoshana Zuboff named "surveillance capitalism" — the business model in which the systematic, large-scale capture of human behavioral data is converted into predictive products sold to advertisers. Google's free consumer services (Search, Gmail, Maps, Chrome, Android, YouTube) function as data-collection instruments; the resulting behavioral surplus powers an ad-targeting machine that, in 2024, generated the bulk of Alphabet's roughly $350 billion in annual revenue. For this vault, Google matters because it built the template — and much of the literal infrastructure — for individual-level behavioral targeting at planetary scale, the capability [[Cambridge Analytica]] crudely imitated and that platforms now run internally. It is also, increasingly, a defense and intelligence contractor.

## Funding & money flows

Google is not externally funded — it is one of the most cash-generative companies in history. Its money flows run the other direction: it is a buyer. The 2007 acquisition of **DoubleClick** (~$3.1 billion), waved through by the FTC without conditions, gave Google the ad-serving "stack" that prosecutors later argued it used to monopolize advertising technology. Other acquisitions (Android 2005, YouTube 2006) extended the surveillance surface. Government contracting is a growing revenue line through [[Google Cloud]].

## Activities

What Google actually does, in this vault's frame:

**Behavioral data capture.** Google collects data across search queries, location history, email content, browsing (via Chrome and the ubiquitous Google Analytics / ad tags embedded on millions of third-party sites), and Android device telemetry. A November 2025 academic study (arXiv) on "Google's hidden empire" documented reach far beyond the company's visible products, much of it invisible to users.

**Ad targeting.** The captured data feeds an auction-based targeting system that lets advertisers — commercial and political — reach individuals by inferred interest, demographic, and behavior. This is the same fundamental capability political operatives sought; Google's first-party version requires no third-party data leak of the kind CA exploited.

**AI and defense.** After the 2018 employee revolt over [[Project Maven]] led Google to drop that Pentagon imagery-AI contract, the company largely re-entered defense work. In early 2025 Google quietly removed AI-principles language that had barred AI use for weapons and surveillance. See [[Google Cloud]] for JWCC and [[Project Nimbus]].

## Controversies / investigations

- **Antitrust:** Found an illegal search monopolist in 2024 (`#claim/established` by federal court); a separate adtech monopolization case targets the DoubleClick-built ad stack.
- **Privacy:** Three FTC consent agreements since 2011; ongoing class actions allege Google tracks users "no matter what safeguards" are in place.
- **[[Project Nimbus]]:** $1.2 billion cloud contract with the Israeli government, including the Ministry of Defense — see the dedicated note.
