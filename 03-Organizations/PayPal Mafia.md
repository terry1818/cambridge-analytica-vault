# PayPal Mafia

Informal label for the founding cohort of PayPal (1998-2002) who went on to outsized influence in Silicon Valley founding, investing, and political organizing 2002-present.

## Core members

- [[Peter Thiel]] - co-founder, CEO 1999-2002; founded Palantir, Clarium, Founders Fund
- [[Elon Musk]] - merged X.com into PayPal; founded SpaceX, Tesla, neuralink, xAI
- [[Reid Hoffman]] - founded LinkedIn; OpenAI board; politically opposite (major Democratic donor)
- [[Max Levchin]] - co-founder; founded Slide, Affirm
- [[David Sacks]] - COO; founded Yammer; Trump AI/Crypto Czar 2025
- [[Keith Rabois]] - executive; later Khosla, Founders Fund
- [[Roelof Botha]] - CFO; managing partner Sequoia Capital
- [[Jeremy Stoppelman]] - founded Yelp
- [[Russel Simmons]] (the Yelp co-founder, not the music exec)
- [[Premal Shah]] - founded Kiva

## Political pattern

The cohort largely organized rightward, with Reid Hoffman as the prominent exception. Thiel was the early ideological organizer (2009 "Education of a Libertarian"). The 2024 Trump endorsement cycle (Sacks, Musk, Rabois) consolidated a public alignment that had been building privately for over a decade.

## Why the label matters

The cohort's wealth combined with founder-network density gave them outsized leverage on both Silicon Valley culture and US politics. The "Mafia" label was journalist shorthand (Fortune 2007) for the unusual density of subsequent success - it is not an organized entity but a recognized social structure.
