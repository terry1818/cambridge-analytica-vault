## Summary

The Public Religion Research Institute (PRRI) is a Washington, D.C.-based nonprofit research and education organization founded in 2009 by [[Robert P. Jones]]. It conducts quantitative and qualitative public opinion research at the intersection of religion, culture, and politics in the United States, producing the most comprehensive ongoing tracking data on American religious demographics, Christian nationalism sentiment, and the political behavior of religious communities. PRRI is widely cited by journalists, policymakers, and academic researchers; its American Values Atlas and annual American Values Survey are standard reference points for understanding the religious landscape.

## Identity

PRRI describes itself as "nonpartisan" and publishes data that is used by commentators across the political spectrum. Its research consistently generates findings that challenge both progressive and conservative narratives — for example, documenting the persistence of religious affiliation where secular commentators expect decline, while also quantifying the scope of white Christian nationalist sentiment that some conservative religious voices minimize.

The organization sits at the boundary between academic research and public-facing policy analysis. Its work is methodologically rigorous by polling standards but is not peer-reviewed in the academic journal sense. Its surveys are large-sample national instruments, often with state-level breakdowns that academic surveys rarely afford.

## Leadership & key personnel

**[[Robert P. Jones]]** — founder and president. Jones holds a PhD in Religion from Emory University and an MDiv from Southwestern Baptist Theological Seminary. Before founding PRRI he was a consultant and senior research fellow at several D.C. think tanks and an assistant professor of religious studies at Missouri State University. He is the primary author of PRRI's interpretive reports and has published multiple books (*The End of White Christian America*, 2016; *White Too Long*, 2020; *The Hidden Roots of White Supremacy*, 2023).

**[[Jemar Tisby]]** — senior fellow (as of 2024). Historian of race and Christianity; author of *The Color of Compromise* (2019). His affiliation with PRRI reflects the organization's engagement with the intersection of race and religion.

## Funding & money flows

PRRI is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit. Funding sources include foundations and individual donors; the organization does not publish a complete donor list publicly. This is a standard limitation for nonprofit research institutes; it should be noted as a gap in independent verification of non-influence on research outputs. No documented evidence of funder-directed research outcomes has surfaced in public reporting.

## Activities

- **American Values Survey (annual):** Tracks political and religious values across a nationally representative sample; includes CN-adjacent questions used in conjunction with the [[Christian Nationalism Scale]].
- **American Values Atlas (launched 2014):** Interactive tool providing state-level religious, political, and demographic data for all 50 states.
- **Topical reports:** PRRI produces research on specific issues — immigration attitudes among religious groups, LGBTQ acceptance, racial justice views — that are widely cited in political analysis.
- **Qualitative research:** In-depth interview projects complement the quantitative surveys.

## Successor / related entities

PRRI is not a successor to any prior organization; it was created to fill what Jones described as a gap in nonpartisan religious public opinion research.

## Controversies / investigations

No major controversies documented. Some conservative commentators have argued PRRI's framing of questions on topics like Christian nationalism reflects progressive assumptions embedded in question design — this is a legitimate methodological critique that applies to all survey research on contested ideological labels. No documented evidence of fabrication or funder interference.
