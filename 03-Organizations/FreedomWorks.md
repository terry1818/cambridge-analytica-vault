# FreedomWorks

## Summary

FreedomWorks was a conservative nonprofit advocacy organization that emerged in 2004 from the split of Citizens for a Sound Economy (CSE) — itself founded by [[Charles Koch]] and David Koch in 1984. While the Koch brothers retained the grassroots-mobilization apparatus under the [[Americans for Prosperity]] banner, the remainder of CSE merged with Empower America (led by Jack Kemp and [[Dick Armey]]) to become FreedomWorks. Under Armey's chairmanship, FreedomWorks became the primary organizational infrastructure for the [[Tea Party movement]] after February 2009, coordinating rallies, providing logistical support, training activists, and connecting local Tea Party groups to national conservative networks. The *New York Times* credited FreedomWorks in 2010 as having "done more than any other organization to build the Tea Party movement." It dissolved in May 2024 after a prolonged internal power struggle and funding difficulties.

## Identity

FreedomWorks occupied a distinct niche in the patron-class ecosystem: it was the "grassroots" mobilization arm of the Koch-linked network, responsible for recruiting and activating volunteers rather than producing policy research (that was [[Americans for Prosperity]]'s function) or academic scholarship (that was [[Cato Institute]]'s). Its organizational lineage from CSE gave it direct Koch-network DNA even as the Koch brothers' direct financial involvement diminished after the 2004 split. The name, according to Armey, derived from his saying "freedom works" — connoting both ideological commitment and a claim about policy efficacy.

## Leadership & Key Personnel

- **[[Dick Armey]]**: Co-chairman (2003 at CSE, then FreedomWorks chairman 2004–2012); provided political credibility as former House Majority Leader
- **Matt Kibbe**: President and CEO (2004–2014); the operational manager who built FreedomWorks' Tea Party infrastructure
- **Jack Kemp**: Co-chairman (2004–2009); supply-side economics icon who lent credibility until his death
- **C. Boyden Gray**: Co-chairman; former White House Counsel under George H.W. Bush
- **Adam Brandon**: Succeeded Kibbe as president; led the organization through its final years

## Funding & Money Flows

FreedomWorks was a 501(c)(4) social welfare organization, meaning donor identities were not publicly disclosed. Early funding included Koch-network sources inherited from CSE. By the Tea Party era, FreedomWorks raised money through its own small-donor base energized by Tea Party activism, as well as from wealthy conservative donors. After the 2012 internal split — when Armey attempted a boardroom coup that resulted in his resignation and a $8 million consulting contract settlement — FreedomWorks faced ongoing funding instability.

## Activities

- Coordinating and providing logistical support for Tea Party rallies (2009–2011)
- Training grassroots activists in campaign techniques, candidate recruitment, and media messaging
- Operating a "FreedomWorks University" training program
- Candidate endorsements and electoral support for Tea Party-backed primary challengers
- Producing conservative policy content and media
- Lobbying against the Affordable Care Act, cap-and-trade legislation, and the 2008–2009 bailouts

## Successor / Related Entities

- **[[Americans for Prosperity]]**: Sister organization from the 2004 CSE split; remained Koch-network aligned and active
- **[[Tea Party movement]]**: The broader movement FreedomWorks helped construct; outlasted FreedomWorks itself as a decentralized political force
- Former FreedomWorks staff and networks have dispersed into various conservative organizations

## Controversies / Investigations

- The 2012 internal crisis, in which Armey attempted to install a new president and was accused by Kibbe of a "coup," involved allegations of financial irregularities and resulted in Armey receiving an $8 million consulting contract — widely reported as a buyout.
- FreedomWorks was accused by critics (notably Lee Fang at ThinkProgress) of being "astroturf" — a corporate-funded operation masquerading as spontaneous grassroots activism. The organization maintained it was a genuine grassroots mobilizer.
- FreedomWorks' relationship to the Koch brothers was more arm's-length than its CSE predecessor's, but the organizational lineage was well-documented.

## Open Questions
