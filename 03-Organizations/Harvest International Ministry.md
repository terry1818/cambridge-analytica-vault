# Harvest International Ministry

## Summary

Harvest International Ministry (HIM) is an apostolic network founded in 1996 by [[Ché Ahn]] and his wife Sue Ahn, headquartered in Pasadena, California. It began by linking about fourteen churches and, by the ministry's own accounting, has grown to connect more than 25,000 churches and ministries across over 60 countries. HIM is one of the principal organizational expressions of the [[New Apostolic Reformation]] (NAR) — the charismatic movement that replaces traditional denominational structure with governance by present-day "apostles" and "prophets." Ché Ahn, mentored by NAR theorist C. Peter Wagner, is one of the movement's recognized apostolic leaders, and HIM functions as his apostolic covering for affiliated churches and leaders. The network is committed to the [[Seven Mountains Mandate]] — the dominionist project of placing Christians in control of the major spheres of culture — and "revival and reformation of the nations." HIM matters to this vault because it is a structured, transnational vehicle for dominion theology, and because Ahn personally tied it to the post-2020 Stop the Steal effort.

## Leadership & key personnel

- **[[Ché Ahn]]** — founder and "apostolic leader" / president; Korean-American charismatic pastor; Fuller Theological Seminary M.Div. and D.Min.; mentored by [[C. Peter Wagner]], who introduced him to apostolic leadership and spiritual warfare.
- **Sue Ahn** — co-founder; co-leader of HIM and HRock.
- **HIM International Apostolic Team** — a body of regional and national apostolic leaders who extend HIM's governance across continents.

## Funding & money flows

HIM is donor- and member-supported, drawing on the giving of affiliated churches, conference attendance, and Ahn's personal ministry (books, media, the Ché Ahn Media platform). Affiliated churches typically pay into the network and receive apostolic "covering" and conference access. Detailed financials are not consolidated in reviewed public sources; HIM's strength is its membership reach rather than concentrated patronage.

## Activities

HIM's core function is **apostolic networking**: it provides charismatic churches and ministries with an alternative to denominational affiliation, organized under Ahn's apostolic authority. Through this structure HIM equips leaders, plants churches, runs conferences, and propagates a theology of "revival and reformation." That reformation language is dominionist: HIM explicitly embraces the [[Seven Mountains Mandate]], the NAR doctrine that Christians should take controlling influence over government, media, education, business, family, religion, and arts/entertainment.

Ché Ahn's public activity gives HIM a concrete political footprint. Ahn was an early Trump supporter (from 2016) and is a recognized NAR apostle. In 2020 he sued California Governor Gavin Newsom over COVID-19 restrictions on worship, holding in-person services at his church; the litigation reached the U.S. Supreme Court and resulted in a ruling favorable to the church (and a reported $1.35 million settlement). After the 2020 election, Ahn aligned with the **Stop the Steal** movement and spoke at a January 5, 2021, rally in Washington, D.C. — the eve of the Capitol attack. Reporting and academic analysis (e.g., Baptist News Global) identify NAR networks of the kind HIM exemplifies as a driver of the January 6 mobilization. In the 2026 cycle Ahn launched a campaign for Governor of California, framing the run as a divine mandate — extending HIM-associated dominion theology directly into electoral politics. HIM's significance, therefore, is as an institutional carrier: it provides the network through which dominionist theology and pro-Trump political energy are organized and distributed.
