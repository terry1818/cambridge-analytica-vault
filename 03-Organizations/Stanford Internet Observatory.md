# Stanford Internet Observatory

Research center at Stanford University. **Founded 2019** by RenÃ©e DiResta, Alex Stamos, and others. Mission: study **online abuse, trust and safety, information operations, and platform governance**. Produced major investigations of 2020 election disinformation, COVID-19 vaccine disinformation, child sexual abuse material on platforms, and influence operations. **Subject of substantial Republican congressional and legal pressure 2023-2024**; substantially curtailed by Stanford University in summer 2024.

## Major outputs (Tier 1)

### Election Integrity Partnership (2020-2022)

Collaboration between SIO, University of Washington's Center for an Informed Public, Graphika, and the Atlantic Council's Digital Forensic Research Lab. Tracked election-related disinformation 2020 and 2022. Produced comprehensive post-election reports.

### Virality Project (2021-2022)

Tracked COVID-19 vaccine misinformation across platforms.

### Twitter Files / Hunter Biden context

Following Musk's 2022 release of "Twitter Files," Republican congressional committees focused on SIO's communications with platforms about election-related content. Framed as government-academic-platform "censorship cartel."

### Investigations of CSAM / Pornhub / etc.

Substantial work on child sexual abuse material detection and platform responses.

## Pressure campaign (2023-2024)

### Jordan / Judiciary Committee investigation

[[Jim Jordan]]'s House Judiciary Committee subcommittee on "Weaponization of the Federal Government" investigated SIO researchers and produced reports framing the work as government-coordinated censorship.

### Litigation

[[Stephen Miller]] and Stephen Miller's America First Legal filed suit; multiple Republican state AGs joined Missouri v. Biden (later Murthy v. Missouri) which named SIO researchers indirectly.

### Murthy v. Missouri (June 2024 SCOTUS)

SCOTUS ruled plaintiffs lacked Article III standing; did not reach merits but the pressure campaign continued.

### Stanford response (June-September 2024)

- Director Alex Stamos departed November 2023
- RenÃ©e DiResta departed June 2024
- Substantial staff departures
- Stanford announced SIO would not pursue election integrity work in 2024 cycle
- Multiple SIO projects ended
