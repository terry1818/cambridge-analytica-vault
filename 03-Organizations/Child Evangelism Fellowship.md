## Summary

Child Evangelism Fellowship (CEF) is an international evangelical organization founded in 1937 that operates the Good News Club program — after-school Bible clubs held primarily in US public school buildings. CEF's access to public schools was dramatically expanded by the 2001 Supreme Court ruling in *Good News Club v. Milford Central School* (533 US 98), which held that excluding a religious club from public school facilities constituted viewpoint discrimination. Katherine Stewart's *The Power Worshippers* (2020) examines CEF as a key vector of the education-capture strategy of the Christian nationalist movement, placing children's evangelism in the physical space of public schools with official-seeming access.

Following the ruling, CEF and its legal partner [[Liberty Counsel]] filed over 200 lawsuits against schools seeking to exclude Good News Clubs, dramatically expanding program scale: from 16,805 participating children in 2001 to 139,221 by 2009 — a 728% increase.

## Identity

CEF's core activity is targeting children aged 4–14 for conversion and discipleship. The Good News Club curriculum presents evangelical Protestant doctrine — sin, salvation, biblical authority — in an after-school format that Stewart argues exploits the institutional context of the public school, where children may not clearly distinguish between school-sponsored and merely school-hosted activities.

Stewart's investigation (documented also in her 2012 book *The Good News Club*) found that CEF's presence generated documented incidents of faith-based social pressure on non-Christian children and their families. CEF disputed Stewart's characterizations.

The broader significance for the theocratic-wing project: CEF represents a ground-level infrastructure for embedding evangelical religious identity formation in the institutional space of secular public education — a parallel to the higher-education capture strategies pursued by organizations like [[Hillsdale College]] and curriculum organizations.

## Leadership & Key Personnel

CEF was founded in 1937 by Jesse Irvin Overholtzer, a minister who believed children were the most receptive mission field. Current leadership is not prominent in public political discourse. The organization's legal work has been conducted primarily through [[Liberty Counsel]].

## Funding & Money Flows
