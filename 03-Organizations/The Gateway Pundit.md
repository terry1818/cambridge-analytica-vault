## Summary

The Gateway Pundit is an American far-right news and opinion website founded in 2004 by Jim Hoft of St. Louis, Missouri. It has been rated by multiple credibility-monitoring organizations — including PolitiFact, Media Bias/Fact Check, and NewsGuard — as one of the least factually reliable major political websites in the United States. Benkler, Faris, and Roberts' *Network Propaganda* analysis identifies the site as a representative node in the fringe-right layer of the asymmetric right-wing media ecosystem: content originating at The Gateway Pundit and similar outlets frequently migrates up to higher-traffic conservative outlets including [[Fox News]] and [[Breitbart]], and then enters mainstream political conversation.

## Identity

The site publishes a high volume of short-form posts, combining genuine news aggregation with fabricated stories, conspiracy theories, and selective or decontextualized evidence. It has been a major vehicle for voter-fraud conspiracy theories, anti-immigration content, and pro-Trump political messaging. The site achieved particular prominence during and after the 2020 election cycle for amplifying unsupported stolen-election narratives.

## Leadership & Key Personnel

Jim Hoft describes himself as "the stupidest man on the internet" — a label applied sarcastically by critics but which Hoft has occasionally embraced ironically. His twin brother Joe Hoft joined as a regular contributor and co-editor. The site employs a small staff supplemented by contracted freelancers.

## Funding & Money Flows

The site's revenue model is primarily advertising-based. In September 2021, Google demonetized the site for publishing medical misinformation about COVID-19 vaccines. It has also hosted sponsored content. Jim Hoft announced in April 2024 that the parent company (TGP Communications LLC) had filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy, which Hoft attributed to multiple defamation lawsuits; the bankruptcy case was dismissed in July 2024 by a federal judge who found it had been filed in bad faith to avoid litigation.

## Activities

- Publishes 50–100+ posts per day covering US politics from a far-right perspective.
- Has published false or unverified stories on subjects including: the Seth Rich murder (Russia-related conspiracy theory); 2020 election fraud; COVID-19 vaccine safety; immigration crime statistics.
- Served as an early amplifier of content later picked up by [[Fox News]] and [[Sean Hannity]].
- Received White House press credentials during the Trump administration (2017–2021 and 2025–present).
- Media Matters for America documented in 2019 that the site was amplifying Russian state media narratives.

## Timeline

- **2004** — Founded by Jim Hoft as a personal political blog.
- **2010s** — Grows to millions of monthly visitors; becomes a major far-right news aggregator.
- **2017** — Receives White House press credential under Trump administration.
- **2020–2021** — Major vehicle for stolen-election claims; Twitter permanently suspends Jim Hoft's account (February 2021), reinstated December 2022 after Elon Musk acquisition.
- **2021-09** — Google demonetizes the site for COVID vaccine misinformation.
- **2024-04** — Files Chapter 11 bankruptcy; dismissed July 2024 as bad-faith filing.

## Controversies / Investigations

- Multiple defamation lawsuits related to false claims about Georgia election workers (Ruby Freeman and Shaye Moss) and other subjects.
- Identified by Media Matters as a conduit for Russian state propaganda narratives.
- Repeated publication of fabricated quotes attributed to public figures.
