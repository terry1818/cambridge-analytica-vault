# Stanford Constitutional Law Center

## Summary

The Constitutional Law Center at Stanford Law School (commonly referred to as the Stanford Constitutional Law Center) is a research institution within Stanford Law School, directed by Michael W. McConnell — the Richard and Frances Mallery Professor and a Senior Fellow at the Hoover Institution. The Center is relevant to this vault primarily because [[Peter Prindiville]], co-author of *The Constitution in Jeopardy* (2022), holds a Non-Resident Fellowship there, meaning that the book's research on Article V convention risks was produced in part within this institution's intellectual context. The Center also hosted a public event on the book in 2022. The Center itself occupies a notable ideological position: McConnell is a prominent conservative-leaning constitutional scholar (former George W. Bush appointee to the Tenth Circuit), making the Center's association with a book warning against right-wing Article V capture a significant marker of how broadly Article V convention risks are taken seriously across the ideological spectrum.

## Identity

The Center focuses on constitutional law scholarship, public events, and the training of law students and fellows in constitutional theory and practice. It does not have an explicit left-right ideological identity as an institution — McConnell is a conservative constitutional scholar who has produced influential work on church-state separation, free exercise, and separation of powers, but the Center is a scholarly rather than advocacy institution.

Michael McConnell's profile: He served as a Circuit Judge on the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Tenth Circuit from 2002 to 2009 (nominated by President George W. Bush, confirmed by a Democratic Senate by unanimous consent). He has published widely on constitutional law and theory. His 2020 book, *The President Who Would Not Be King: Executive Power Under the Constitution* (Princeton University Press), reflects his constitutional originalism. He is also a Senior Fellow at the Hoover Institution.

## Leadership & Key Personnel

- **Michael W. McConnell**: Faculty Director. Richard and Frances Mallery Professor of Law at Stanford. Former Tenth Circuit judge. Senior Fellow, Hoover Institution. Member of the Federalist Society (noted on fedsoc.org contributor pages — Tier-2). His association with Article V scholarship is indirect; his relevance to this vault is primarily as the institutional host for Prindiville's fellowship.
- **Peter Prindiville**: Non-Resident Fellow. Co-author of *The Constitution in Jeopardy*. His research on Article V — including the [[Article V aggregation problem]] and [[Article V delegate-selection vacuum]] — was conducted under this institutional affiliation.
