# The Federalist Society for Law and Public Policy Studies

US conservative and libertarian legal organization. Founded 1982 by Steven Calabresi, Lee Liberman Otis, and David McIntosh as a student organization at Yale and University of Chicago Law Schools.

## Core function

- Networking and credentialing organization for conservative and libertarian lawyers
- Hosts campus chapters, lawyer chapters, annual conventions
- Publishes legal scholarship through Engage and other journals

## Judicial pipeline

- [[Leonard Leo]] has chaired or co-chaired the organization through much of its peak influence period (2010s-2020s)
- The Trump 1st-term judicial appointments (3 Supreme Court justices, ~230 lower court judges) were screened through Federalist Society lists
- The Trump 2nd-term judicial appointments continue this pattern

## Recent split (Tier 2)

- 2022-2024: Internal tensions emerged between traditional originalist FedSoc members and the "national conservative" wing
- [[Adrian Vermeule]]'s "common good constitutionalism" critique challenges traditional originalism from the right
- Leonard Leo's role has diminished publicly but continues through external dark-money structures
