# Immigration Reform Law Institute

## Summary

The Immigration Reform Law Institute (IRLI) is a Washington, D.C.-based public-interest law firm founded in 1986 as the affiliated legal arm of the [[Federation for American Immigration Reform|FAIR]]. It describes itself as the only public-interest nonprofit law firm in the United States devoted exclusively to immigration-related litigation on the restrictionist side. In practice IRLI performs three functions for the immigration-restriction movement: it drafts model anti-immigration legislation for state and federal lawmakers, it defends those measures in court, and it litigates against cities and states that adopt sanctuary policies or pro-immigrant ordinances — frequently via amicus briefs.

IRLI matters to this vault because it is where the immigration-restriction movement's politics became durable *law*. Most consequentially, [[Kris Kobach]] — the former Kansas Secretary of State — worked as IRLI counsel and used that platform to design and litigate a generation of restrictionist measures: Arizona's SB 1070 "show me your papers" law, similar statutes in Alabama and other states, and challenges to DACA (including *Crane v. Napolitano* and the multistate effort to terminate DACA). IRLI is part of the [[John Tanton|Tanton network]] — the cluster of restrictionist organizations anchored by FAIR — and several of its lawyers, like its executive director [[Dale Wilcox]], moved into IRLI after first working at FAIR. Through both litigation and the placement of personnel into government, IRLI made the Tanton network's agenda operative inside immigration policy during the Trump administrations.

## Identity

- **Legal name(s):** Immigration Reform Law Institute; a 501(c)(3) public-interest law firm.
- **Other names:** IRLI.
- **Founded:** 1986.
- **Parent / owner:** [[FAIR]] (Federation for American Immigration Reform) — IRLI is FAIR's legal affiliate.
- **Registered jurisdiction:** United States; headquartered in Washington, D.C.

## Leadership & key personnel

- **Dale Wilcox** — Executive Director and General Counsel; like many IRLI lawyers, first worked at FAIR before moving to its legal arm.
- **[[Kris Kobach]]** — served as IRLI Of Counsel / senior counsel; architect of SB 1070-style state legislation and of major anti-DACA litigation; later Kansas Secretary of State and Kansas Attorney General; vice chair of the short-lived Presidential Advisory Commission on Election Integrity.
- **Michael Hethmon** — senior counsel; longtime drafter of restrictionist model legislation.

## Funding & money flows

IRLI is funded as a FAIR affiliate; its resources flow through the broader FAIR / Tanton-network funding base. Precise donor breakdowns are not fully public. As a 501(c)(3) it conducts litigation and legislative drafting rather than electoral activity.

## Activities

- **Model legislation:** drafting template anti-immigration bills (notably the SB 1070 family of state enforcement laws) for state legislators.
- **Defensive litigation:** representing or supporting jurisdictions that adopt restrictionist measures when those measures are challenged.
- **Offensive litigation:** suing sanctuary cities and states, and filing amicus briefs in immigration cases, including challenges to DACA and to executive immigration relief.
- **Personnel pipeline:** IRLI lawyers have moved into senior immigration-policy roles in the executive branch, especially under the Trump administrations.

## Timeline

- `1986` — IRLI founded as the legal arm of FAIR
- `2010` — Arizona enacts SB 1070; Kris Kobach, working with IRLI, is identified as a principal drafter of the model
- `2011–2012` — Similar IRLI-influenced enforcement laws enacted and litigated in Alabama, Georgia, and other states
- `2012–2015` — IRLI involved in litigation challenging DACA and DAPA, including *Crane v. Napolitano* and *United States v. Texas*
- `2017` — Kris Kobach (IRLI counsel) named vice chair of the Presidential Advisory Commission on Election Integrity
- `2017–2021` — Tanton-network personnel, including IRLI-linked lawyers, occupy immigration-policy roles in the first Trump administration
- `2021–2024` — IRLI continues litigation against Biden-era immigration measures
- `2025–2026` — IRLI active in litigation supporting the second Trump administration's immigration agenda

## Successor / related entities

- **[[FAIR]]** — parent organization; IRLI is its legal affiliate.
- **[[Center for Immigration Studies]]** — sister organization in the Tanton network supplying restrictionist research.
- **NumbersUSA** — Tanton-network grassroots lobbying group.

## Controversies / investigations

- **Tanton-network associations** — IRLI is part of the network anchored by FAIR, which the SPLC designates a hate group; critics extend that critique to IRLI. IRLI rejects the framing. `#claim/disputed`
- **Kobach's record** — many of the state laws IRLI helped draft (SB 1070 and its successors) were partially struck down by courts; critics argue IRLI's model legislation invited racial profiling. Supporters frame the work as legitimate enforcement advocacy.
- **Revolving door** — the movement of IRLI-linked lawyers into federal immigration-policy positions is documented by oversight groups as evidence of the Tanton network's reach into government.
