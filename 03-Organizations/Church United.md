## Summary

Church United is a California-based pastoral mobilization network founded in 2016 by Pastor Jim Domen. The organization recruits and deploys pastors as political actors, organizing them for direct engagement with elected officials, voter mobilization around "biblical values," and civic activity on culture-war issues including abortion and LGBTQ equality. Katherine Stewart profiles Church United in *The Power Worshippers* (2020) as an example of the professionalized infrastructure through which the Christian nationalist movement converts pastoral networks into electoral and legislative power.

By 2023, Church United claimed more than 2,500 pastors in its network, including approximately 500 Spanish-speaking pastors, with ambitions to expand nationally and internationally.

## Identity

Church United's strategy is explicitly political: pastors are treated as influencers over captive congregational audiences, and the organization's programming is designed to translate that influence into votes and legislative pressure on "biblical" (typically anti-abortion, anti-LGBTQ) issues. Signature activities include "Awakening Tours" sending pastors to Washington, DC and state capitols for direct lawmaker access.

The organization has a notable racial-diversity component — founder Jim Domen emphasizes multiracial outreach, including Spanish-language pastoral recruitment — which distinguishes it somewhat from older all-white Religious Right infrastructure while pursuing the same policy agenda. This multiracial framing has made it a vehicle for Republican efforts to peel Latino evangelical voters from Democratic coalitions.

Jim Domen is a former Southern California Assemblyman candidate (2014) and identifies as an "ex-gay" pastor; his personal narrative around sexuality is central to Church United's anti-LGBTQ organizing. He co-chairs TPUSA Faith, the Turning Point USA religious outreach arm co-founded with Charlie Kirk.

## Leadership & Key Personnel

- **Jim Domen** (founder): Ordained pastor, former Assembly candidate (CA-2014), ex-gay identity activist, co-chair of TPUSA Faith alongside Charlie Kirk
