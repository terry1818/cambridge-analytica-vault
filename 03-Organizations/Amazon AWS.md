# Amazon AWS

## Summary

Amazon Web Services (AWS) is the cloud-computing division of Amazon.com, founded in 2006 and long the world's largest cloud provider by revenue and market share. Beyond its commercial dominance, AWS is the most deeply embedded commercial supplier to the U.S. national-security state. In 2013 it won a roughly $600 million contract to build the CIA's "C2S" (Commercial Cloud Services) environment — a private, on-premises Amazon cloud serving all then-17/18 U.S. intelligence agencies. In 2021 the NSA awarded AWS a secret cloud contract, internally codenamed "WildandStormy," reported at up to $10 billion, to migrate the agency's signals-intelligence data troves to commercial cloud. For this vault, AWS is the clearest case of the surveillance-infrastructure fusion: the same company that runs much of the commercial internet (and hosts countless data brokers and ad-tech firms) also runs the classified compute of the U.S. intelligence community. It is also a major data broker–adjacent actor through Amazon's own advertising and retail-behavior data.

## Funding & money flows

AWS is internally funded by Amazon and is the company's profit engine. Government-security revenue is substantial and partly classified:

- **CIA C2S (2013):** ~$600 million to build and operate a private intelligence-community cloud. The contract was considered legally and politically risky at the time — it placed top-secret classified data on Amazon-operated hardware. AWS won it over IBM after a bid protest.
- **NSA "WildandStormy" (2021):** reported up to $10 billion to migrate NSA data to a commercial cloud; Microsoft protested, the GAO denied the protest, and the contract was re-awarded to AWS in April 2022.
- **C2E (2020):** the CIA's multibillion-dollar Commercial Cloud Enterprise vehicle, awarded to AWS, Microsoft, Google, Oracle, and IBM as competitors for task orders.
- **JWCC:** AWS is one of four Pentagon JWCC providers (with [[Google Cloud]], Microsoft, Oracle).

## Activities

**Intelligence-community cloud.** The CIA's C2S environment, built and operated by AWS inside government facilities, became a shared resource across the U.S. intelligence community — the CIA's on-premises Amazon cloud is now available to other agencies. It hosts compute, storage, and data-sharing for classified workloads.

**NSA migration.** As of late 2024, the NSA was reported to be three to four years into moving its capabilities, including signals intelligence, into the Amazon-developed cloud — work built on more than a decade of prior AWS–intelligence-community collaboration.

**Commercial backbone.** AWS hosts a large share of the commercial internet, including many data brokers, ad-tech firms, and surveillance-adjacent services — meaning AWS is structurally upstream of the data-broker pipeline this vault tracks (compare [[Premise Data]], which is San Francisco–based and serves the same federal market).

**[[Project Nimbus]].** AWS co-provides, with [[Google Cloud]], the Israeli government's $1.2 billion Nimbus cloud.

## Controversies / investigations

- **JEDI contract dispute (2019–2021):** Amazon lost the Pentagon's $10 billion JEDI cloud contract to Microsoft and litigated, alleging the award was tainted by President Trump's animus toward Bezos; the Pentagon ultimately cancelled JEDI and replaced it with the multi-vendor JWCC.
- **Secrecy of intelligence contracts:** The classified nature of the NSA and CIA work means the public, and most of Congress, cannot scrutinize the scope of data AWS hosts for the intelligence community.
- **Concentration risk:** Critics note the structural danger of routing the bulk of U.S. classified compute through a small number of commercial firms.
