## Summary

The Christian Front was an antisemitic paramilitary organisation founded in late 1938 in direct response to [[Father Charles Coughlin]]'s radio broadcasts calling for a "crusade against the anti-Christian forces of the Red Revolution." Based primarily among Irish- and German-American Catholics in New York City, the Front organised street-level harassment of Jews, paramilitary training, and — according to FBI evidence — plots to steal military weapons for an eventual overthrow of the US government. In January 1940, seventeen Brooklyn members were arrested on conspiracy charges; the resulting trial collapsed with acquittals and a hung jury, producing no convictions. Rachel Maddow's *Prequel* uses the Christian Front's trial outcome as a central example of how domestic fascism escaped accountability — a pattern she traces to the present.

## Identity

The Christian Front combined:
- Catholic ethnic populism (primarily Irish-American working class in New York)
- Coughlin's framework of anti-communism as a proxy for antisemitism
- Paramilitary organisation: drilling, weapons acquisition, and talk of violent overthrow
- Neighbourhood-level antisemitic intimidation in New York City

The "Christian" framing positioned antisemitism within a religious resistance narrative — Jews were identified as the architects of Bolshevism threatening Christian civilisation — a template that traces forward through later [[Christian nationalism]] strands.

## Leadership & key personnel

- **Father Charles Coughlin**: The explicit ideological inspiration; broadcast the call that founded the Front; later distanced himself after the 1940 arrests.
- **John F. Cassidy**: St. John's Law School graduate; identified as leader of the Brooklyn chapter.
- **Macklin Boettger, John Viebrock, William Bishop**: Among the 17 arrested; their trial resulted in a hung jury.
- The broader membership numbered "several thousand" (FBI estimate); mostly Irish-American Catholics in New York.

## Funding & money flows

- No established funding structure; appears to have been a grassroots network
- Weapons reportedly stolen or purchased from sympathetic National Guard sources (alleged by FBI; never proven in court)

## Activities

- Paramilitary drilling in basements and rooftops in Brooklyn
- Street harassment and antisemitic intimidation in New York City
- Publication of antisemitic literature and distribution of *Social Justice* (Coughlin's magazine)
- Alleged weapons stockpiling and planning for violent insurrection (per FBI charges)
- Chapters existed in Boston ("Nazies of Copley Square") and other cities, though the New York network was the most documented

## Successor / related entities

- [[German-American Bund]] — ideological peer; partly overlapping far-right milieu
- [[Silver Legion of America]] — contemporary parallel organisation
- The institutional collapse was complete; no direct organisational successor

## Controversies / investigations

- January 1940 FBI arrests: 17 men charged with conspiracy to overthrow the government, steal government property, and interfere with military recruiting.
- Trial outcome (June 1940): 13 of 17 acquitted outright; 4 mistrials (hung jury). The prosecution's failure to secure any conviction was a significant humiliation for the FBI and Justice Department.
- NYPD had sympathisers within its ranks who had participated in Front activities — documented in subsequent investigations; see Catholic Fascists in the NYPD (The Revealer).
- The failed prosecution contributed to caution about subsequent sedition cases — context for the later [[Great Sedition Trial 1944]].

## Open questions

- Whether any of the arrested members maintained connections to broader far-right networks in the post-war period.
- The degree of Coughlin's actual operational involvement vs. mere ideological inspiration — the trial evidence suggested more connection than Coughlin admitted.
