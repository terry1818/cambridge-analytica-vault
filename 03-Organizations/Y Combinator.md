## Summary

Y Combinator (YC) is the startup accelerator that, since its founding on March 11, 2005, has been the most consequential single institution shaping the consumer technology industry. It pioneered the batch-funding model: twice-yearly cohorts of early-stage startups receive seed investment ($125,000–$500,000 in recent cycles, for approximately 7% equity) in exchange for participation in an intensive three-month program culminating in Demo Day. As of 2026, YC has funded over 5,000 companies. Its relevance to this vault is structural: YC-funded companies — Reddit (host of /r/The_Donald), Airbnb, Stripe, Coinbase, and others — are significant nodes in the digital infrastructure, political finance, and platform ecosystem documented throughout the vault. Antonio García Martínez worked in the Silicon Valley world that YC shaped, and *Chaos Monkeys* is partly an account of his own brush with the YC ecosystem.

## Identity

Y Combinator was named after a concept in lambda calculus (a function that can compute other functions without referring to itself by name) — a characteristically Graham-esque technical-intellectual branding choice. Its founding philosophy held that investors should fund very early-stage companies, prefer founders over executives, and make many small bets rather than few large ones. This philosophy restructured Silicon Valley's early-stage capital formation and normalized a younger, more technical, more ideologically libertarian founder culture.

## Leadership & key personnel

- **Paul Graham**: Co-founder; essayist and Lisp programmer; set the intellectual and cultural tone of YC. His essays on startups, growth, and founder psychology shaped a generation of tech founders.
- **Jessica Livingston**: Co-founder; author of *Founders at Work* (2007); managed YC operations and culture.
- **Sam Altman**: President 2014–2019; subsequently CEO of OpenAI — a connection relevant to AI governance discussions in this vault.
- **Garry Tan**: Current president (2023–); previously a YC partner; known for political commentary on San Francisco politics.

## Funding & money flows

YC invests in companies in exchange for equity (typically ~7%). It raises no traditional VC fund from LPs in the conventional sense for its main accelerator; its returns come from equity stakes in portfolio companies. Its alumni network and brand generate substantial deal flow and secondary influence.

## Activities

- Twice-yearly funding batches (W and S cohorts, for winter and summer).
- Demo Day: a pivotal Silicon Valley event at which cohort companies pitch to assembled investors.
- YC Safe (Simple Agreement for Future Equity): YC standardized early-stage investment documentation; the YC Safe became widely adopted industry-wide.
- YC Research: a nonprofit arm that has funded projects including OpenAI (Altman), Basic Income research, and other applied research.

## Controversies / investigations

- Reddit (YC batch 2005): became a major platform for alt-right organizing (r/The_Donald, r/pizzagate), raising questions about YC's indirect role in political disinformation infrastructure.
- Coinbase and crypto: YC's heavy crypto portfolio (Coinbase, multiple DeFi projects) intersects with the crypto-political financing threads documented elsewhere in this vault.
- YC's libertarian cultural orientation has been noted by critics as shaping a founder culture that resists regulation — including the privacy and data-broker regulations most relevant to this vault.

## Open questions

- Whether YC's selection criteria and culture systematically selected for founders unlikely to prioritize privacy, social harm, or democratic accountability in platform design.
