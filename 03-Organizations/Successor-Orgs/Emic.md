# Emic

## Summary

UK firm founded in the wake of the [[SCL Group]] / [[Cambridge Analytica]] May 2018 collapse, staffed by **former SCL Defence personnel**. Per [[Source Briant OCCRP 2020-10-12|Emma Briant's OCCRP reporting in 2020]], Emic was identified as continuing the SCL-Defence side of the operational portfolio — government and defence-influence contracting work — under a new corporate brand. One of the less publicly visible nodes in the [[Hypothesis - Successor entities preserve operational capability|CA-successor network]].

## Identity

- **Founded:** 2018 (post-CA collapse)
- **Jurisdiction:** UK
- **Lineage:** [[SCL Group]] / SCL Defence side of the operation (distinct from the political-consulting [[Cambridge Analytica]] arm)
- **Operational focus:** UK government work; behavioral / strategic communications contracting #confidence/medium

## Why it matters

SCL Group had two operational tracks: **SCL Elections** (which became Cambridge Analytica) and **SCL Defence** (defence and government contracting). The CA scandal focused public and regulatory attention on the elections side. **Emic represents the defence-side continuity** — same methodology, same personnel, less public scrutiny.

This split-lineage is a load-bearing piece of [[Hypothesis - Defense-to-elections pipeline]]: defence-contractor information operations toolkits cross into commercial election services via personnel and IP. Emic is the reverse direction — election-era personnel returning to (or staying in) defence-contracting work after CA's collapse made the election side untenable.

## Comparative position

- **vs. [[Auspex International]]** — Auspex focuses Africa/Middle East political consulting; Emic focuses UK government contracting
