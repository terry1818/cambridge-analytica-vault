# Koch Industries

## Summary

**Second-largest privately-held company in the US.** Koch family-owned conglomerate. Founded 1940 by Fred Koch (later co-founder of John Birch Society). Now led by **[[Charles Koch]]** as chairman (with son Chase Koch as co-CEO since 2024). **~$125B+ annual revenue**. Oil refining (Flint Hills Resources), chemicals (Koch Chemical), paper/forestry (Georgia-Pacific), fertilizers, electronics, finance, glass, ranching. **The single most consequential private-company-as-political-actor in US history** measured by 50-year infrastructure investment in conservative political coalition.

**Koch network political spending: $400M+ per cycle** (2010s peak). Built infrastructure including [[Americans for Prosperity]], [[State Policy Network]] support, [[American Legislative Exchange Council|ALEC]] underwriting, Cato Institute, Mercatus Center, [[DonorsTrust]] / [[Donors Capital Fund]] DAFs, university-funding network (360+ programs at 300+ universities), climate-denial infrastructure ([[Heartland Institute]], [[CO2 Coalition]]).

## Why Koch Industries matters in the vault

Koch is **the central patron of 5-decade conservative infrastructure investment**. Where the [[Mercer Family Foundation]] funds specific operations (CA, Heritage trustee, 1789 Capital), Koch funds **the entire infrastructure substrate**:

- ALEC model legislation
- SPN state-think-tank network
- AFP grassroots
- Cato + Mercatus intellectual frame
- University-program pipeline

Without Koch, the post-1970s conservative coalition infrastructure does not exist at current scale.
