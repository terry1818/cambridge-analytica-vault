# Breitbart News

## Summary

American right-wing news outlet founded in **2007** by **Andrew Breitbart**. After Breitbart's sudden death in March 2012, **[[Steve Bannon]] became executive chairman** until departing in August 2016 to run the Trump campaign. **[[Robert Mercer]] and [[Rebekah Mercer]] became major funders/stakeholders** during the Bannon-led era. Breitbart functioned as the **narrative-distribution engine** for the Mercer-Bannon-CA-era political project -- running stories that CA-targeted ads could amplify and that the broader conservative media ecosystem would echo.

## CA-era operational role

During the 2014-2018 peak of the Mercer-Bannon political infrastructure, Breitbart played the **media distribution layer**:

1. **[[Government Accountability Institute]]** (Schweizer + Bannon) produced opposition research
2. **[[Glittering Steel]]** (Bannon) produced documentary content from GAI research
3. **Breitbart News** published, amplified, and editorialized on the GAI/Glittering Steel content
4. **[[Cambridge Analytica]]** targeted the resulting content to audiences identified via psychographic profiling
5. **[[Make America Number 1 Super PAC]]** funded political-ad versions

The full stack illustrates the **coordinated content + targeting + funding ecosystem** that the Mercer-Bannon project built.

## Trajectory

- **2007:** founded by Andrew Breitbart
- **2012-03:** Breitbart dies; Bannon becomes executive chair
- **2012-2016:** Mercer family acquires substantial stake; Bannon-led era
- **2016-08:** Bannon departs to run Trump campaign
- **2017-2018:** post-Bannon era; reduced editorial influence
- **2018+:** Mercer family began distancing from Breitbart and Bannon
- **2020+:** continued operating with reduced political-cultural influence
- **2024+:** still publishing but no longer the dominant right-wing media engine it was during Bannon era
