## Role in the conservative policy infrastructure

The [[Heritage Foundation]], founded in 1973 by Edwin Feulner and Paul Weyrich (with Joseph Coors funding), is the oldest and historically largest think tank in the American conservative policy infrastructure. The vault treats Heritage as a load-bearing node for two distinct phases: the Reagan-era policy blueprint phase (Mandate for Leadership 1980 directly shaped Reagan administration policy) and the present-day [[Project 2025]] phase (Heritage is the institutional sponsor of the 2025 successor blueprint).

Between those two anchor points, Heritage produced the staffed pipeline of policy entrepreneurs that filled Republican administrations, the policy research that supplied conservative legislators with off-the-shelf legislative drafts, and the institutional credibility that made conservative policy positions debatable in mainstream venues.

## The Roberts-era pivot

Under [[Kevin Roberts]] (president since 2021), Heritage repositioned from a traditional movement-conservative think tank toward an explicitly New Right organisation. The Roberts-era pivot is documented in [[National Review 2025-12 Heritage Implodes]] and elsewhere: substantial staff turnover, ideological realignment toward [[Catholic Integralism]]-adjacent and post-liberal positions, public confrontation with previously aligned organisations, and the operational leadership of Project 2025.

The pivot also brought Heritage into more direct alignment with the [[Council for National Policy]] / [[Federalist Society]] / [[Claremont Institute]] convergence that the vault tracks. The earlier Heritage (1990s-2010s) was a coalition partner with these organisations; the Roberts-era Heritage is more nearly a coordination hub.

## Funding and donor base

Heritage's funder base includes the historical Coors family interests, the [[Mercer]] family (via direct giving and via Make America Number 1 PAC-linked giving), the DeVos family ([[DeVos Family Network]]), the Koch network historically (though the Heritage-Koch relationship cooled in the post-2016 period), and the broader CNP-affiliated donor architecture. The [[ProPublica Heritage Nonprofit]] disclosures are a primary source for the 990-level financial picture.

## Founding context and the 1980 Mandate

Heritage was founded in 1973 by Edwin Feulner (longtime president), Paul Weyrich (also founder of [[Council for National Policy]] and the [[Free Congress Foundation]]), and Joseph Coors (Colorado brewing-family principal and seed funder). The founding occurred in the immediate aftermath of the 1972 Nixon landslide and the early 1973 Watergate disclosures, in a political environment where the existing conservative-movement infrastructure was perceived as inadequate to the policy-formulation task ahead.

The 1980 publication of "Mandate for Leadership: Policy Management in a Conservative Administration" was the foundational document of the modern Heritage operating model. The 1,093-page volume contained more than 2,000 specific policy recommendations across the executive branch. Reagan-administration uptake was reportedly high (Heritage's own count claimed roughly 60 percent of the recommendations had been implemented or initiated by the end of Reagan's first term). The model â€” a comprehensive pre-administration policy blueprint produced by an external think tank for an incoming Republican White House â€” is the direct template for the 2023 [[Project 2025]] "Mandate for Leadership: The Conservative Promise" document.

## The Roberts-era pivot (2021-present)

[[Kevin Roberts]] became president in October 2021, succeeding Kay Coles James. The Roberts-era pivot has been the most consequential transformation in Heritage's history since the original 1980 mandate document. The pivot has involved: substantial staff turnover (longtime Heritage staff exited or were removed; new staff brought in from [[America First Policy Institute]], [[Claremont Institute]], and the broader New Right institutional ecosystem); explicit alignment with the [[Catholic Integralism]]-adjacent and post-liberal intellectual tradition that Roberts personally identifies with; public confrontation with previously aligned organisations (most notably the [[Cato Institute]] over libertarian-vs-national-conservative tensions); and operational leadership of [[Project 2025]] from the late 2022 launch through the November 2024 election.

The [[National Review 2025-12 Heritage Implodes]] reporting documents the internal controversies that followed the Roberts pivot and the staff departures that resulted. The vault treats this reporting as primary-source material on the institutional tension between traditional movement-conservative Heritage and Roberts-era New Right Heritage.

## Affiliated structure

Heritage operates as a 501(c)(3) educational organisation. Its principal affiliated structure is [[Heritage Action]] (the 501(c)(4) advocacy and electoral arm, founded 2010), which conducts the campaign-cycle work that the (c)(3) status precludes. The Heritage Foundation Board overlaps substantially with [[Council for National Policy]] membership and with the broader CNP-aligned donor-class architecture.

Specific affiliated initiatives that matter for the vault: the Margaret Thatcher Center for Freedom (UK-and-Atlanticist policy work, with overlap into the [[Wikipedia - Affinity Partners]] and Gulf-policy ecosystem), the Asian Studies Center (China-policy work with overlap into the broader Tech Right China-hawkish frame), and the Davis Institute for National Security and Foreign Policy.

## Funding base

Heritage's funder base spans the historical Coors-family interests, the [[Mercer]] family (via direct giving and via Make America Number 1 PAC-adjacent giving), the [[DeVos Family Network]] (substantial documented giving across multiple vehicles), the [[Koch Network]] historically (though the Heritage-Koch relationship cooled in the post-2016 period over national-conservative vs libertarian tensions), and the broader CNP-affiliated donor architecture. The [[ProPublica Heritage Nonprofit]] disclosures provide the 990-level financial picture.

Annual budget through the 2020s has been in the $80-110M range (varying by cycle and specific initiative funding). Heritage Action's separate (c)(4) budget adds another $20-30M.

## Operational relationship to Project 2025

[[Project 2025]] is institutionally housed at Heritage, with Heritage providing the operational backbone, the project management, the personnel-database hosting, the training-programme infrastructure, and the publishing-and-distribution capacity for the 900-page mandate document. The Heritage Foundation president (Kevin Roberts) was the lead public face of the project from late 2022 through the November 2024 election.

The relationship is operationally significant because it means Project 2025 is not a discrete project with a defined start and end. It is the current operational mode of Heritage itself: the staffing, the policy production, the personnel pipeline, and the training programme will continue post-2024 in whatever institutional shape Heritage adopts for the 2028 cycle and beyond.

## Why the vault treats Heritage as a top connector

Heritage is the institutional convergence point of the religious-right donor class, the movement-conservative policy infrastructure, the [[Federalist Society]] judicial-pipeline ecosystem (via overlapping board membership and personnel), and the Trump 2.0 executive-branch personnel pipeline (via Project 2025). Removing Heritage from the network graph would substantially disconnect the Religious Right cluster from the policy-execution layer and the Tech Right convergence cluster from the institutional credibility layer.
