# PragerU

Prager University Foundation (PragerU) is a 501(c)(3) founded in 2009 by talk-radio host Dennis Prager. PragerU is not an actual university; it is a video-and-content publishing operation distributing short-form conservative-educational videos online. The operation has substantially expanded into K-12 education programming through the 2020-2026 period.

## PragerU Kids

PragerU Kids is the children's-educational content brand. The brand's curriculum and content has been adopted into K-12 public-school curricula in multiple Republican-controlled states:

- Florida (2023 adoption)
- Oklahoma (2023 adoption)
- Texas (2024 partial adoption)
- New Hampshire (2024 adoption)
- Iowa, Montana, and additional states with smaller-scale adoption

The state-level adoption pattern is operationally significant: PragerU Kids content is partisan-political-conservative content positioned as educational material. State adoption converts the partisan content into curricular requirement in public schools.

## Funding and operational scale

PragerU's annual operating budget is in the $50M+ range. Funders include the Wilks brothers (substantial multi-year giving), the Adelson family historically, the Mercer Family Foundation, and a broader base of conservative-movement donors.

## Why this is load-bearing

PragerU Kids represents the operational success of curriculum-capture-via-state-adoption. The model demonstrates that explicit partisan-conservative content can be inserted into public-school curricular requirements through coordinated state-legislature and state-board-of-education action. The model is replicable; the question is whether it expands further through 2026-2028.
