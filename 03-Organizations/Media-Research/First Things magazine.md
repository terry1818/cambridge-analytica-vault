# First Things magazine

First Things is a Catholic-and-conservative-Protestant intellectual magazine, founded in 1990 by Richard John Neuhaus. The magazine is published by the Institute on Religion and Public Life and has been one of the principal intellectual venues for what became contemporary [[Catholic Integralism]], post-liberal, and broader religious-right intellectual material.

## Editorial trajectory

First Things has operated under successive editorial leadership:

- Richard John Neuhaus (founder, editor through 2009)
- Joseph Bottum (editor 2009-2010)
- R.R. Reno (editor 2011-present)

The Reno-era First Things has substantially shifted toward the explicit post-liberal and integralist intellectual positions that became operationally consequential in the Trump-and-post-Trump period. The magazine has been a continuing venue for [[Patrick Deneen]], [[Adrian Vermeule]], Sohrab Ahmari, and adjacent intellectual figures.

## Operational influence

First Things' operational influence has been intellectual rather than directly political. The magazine articulates intellectual positions; those positions then circulate through the broader conservative-intellectual ecosystem and are operationalised by other vehicles (Project 2025 policy programming, Trump 2.0 personnel decisions, Catholic-and-evangelical institutional positioning).

The magazine's venue role is operationally distinctive: it provides intellectual-credibility infrastructure for positions that lack mainstream-academic legitimacy, enabling operators in adjacent venues to cite First Things publications as intellectual cover for political positions.

## Why this is load-bearing

First Things is the principal intellectual-venue infrastructure for the contemporary religious-right intellectual programme. Removing First Things from the network would force the intellectual-positioning work into less institutionally-credentialed venues with substantial loss of intellectual legitimacy.
