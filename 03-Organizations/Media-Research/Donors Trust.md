# Donors Trust

Donors Trust (and its affiliated Donors Capital Fund) is a Virginia-based donor-advised fund (DAF) that has emerged as the principal anonymizing pass-through for the conservative-movement donor architecture. Donors Trust was founded in 1999 by Whitney Ball and currently operates as the central DAF infrastructure for conservative-aligned giving.

## How the DAF anonymizing mechanism works

A donor contributes to a DAF (Donors Trust) and receives an immediate charitable tax deduction. The donor then "advises" Donors Trust on which 501(c)(3) charities should receive distributions from the contributed funds. The recipient charity sees the grant as coming from Donors Trust rather than from the original donor. The result: the original donor's identity is operationally invisible in the recipient's IRS 990 filings, in publicly-available foundation-grant databases, and in journalistic reconstruction of conservative-movement funding flows.

The mechanism is not specific to conservative giving â€” DAFs are used across the political spectrum. The operational distinction of Donors Trust is its near-exclusive focus on conservative-movement recipients, making it the principal infrastructure for conservative-movement dark-money flows.

## Documented funding flows

Substantial journalistic reconstruction (principally by Brendan Fischer at the Center for Media and Democracy, Andy Kroll at Mother Jones, and the broader investigative-journalism ecosystem) has documented Donors Trust grants to:

- [[Heritage Foundation]] and Heritage Action
- [[Federalist Society]]
- [[State Policy Network]] affiliates across the network
- [[True the Vote]] and adjacent election-administration-challenge organisations
- [[Project Veritas]]
- [[Alliance Defending Freedom]]
- Various climate-skepticism organisations
- Various anti-immigration organisations
- The broader conservative-movement institutional ecosystem

Annual grant volume has been in the $100M-$200M range across recent years.

## Why this is load-bearing

Donors Trust is the single most operationally consequential dark-money vehicle in the conservative-movement ecosystem. Removing Donors Trust from the network would force conservative-movement donor giving back into direct-recipient relationships that would expose original-donor identities, with substantial operational consequences for donor recruitment and retention.
