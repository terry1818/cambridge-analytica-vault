# Bradley Foundation

The Lynde and Harry Bradley Foundation is a Milwaukee-based private foundation, founded in 1942 by Lynde and Harry Bradley. Substantially expanded operational scope post-1985 following the sale of Allen-Bradley to Rockwell International, which produced the substantial endowment that has funded the foundation's contemporary operational role.

## Operational scale and reach

Bradley Foundation grant-making is in the $30-50M annual range. The funder base is operationally important to the broader conservative-movement ecosystem:

- [[Federalist Society]] (substantial multi-decade funding)
- [[Heritage Foundation]] (multi-decade funding)
- [[State Policy Network]] affiliates (substantial state-level funding)
- The Bradley Prizes (annual $250K prizes awarded to conservative intellectual and policy figures)
- The Bradley Impact Fund (the foundation's DAF affiliate, providing donor-advised-fund services to the broader conservative-movement donor base)
- Wisconsin-specific institutional funding (the Marquette University Law School polling operation, the broader Wisconsin Institute for Law and Liberty WILL, the Bradley-funded Wisconsin conservative ecosystem)

## Wisconsin-specific operational footprint

The Bradley Foundation's Milwaukee base has produced substantial Wisconsin-specific operational consequences. The Wisconsin Institute for Law and Liberty (WILL), the Wisconsin Policy Research Institute, the broader state-level conservative-movement infrastructure all draw substantially on Bradley funding. The Wisconsin conservative-political environment (state-supreme-court election dynamics, school-voucher programmes, election-administration litigation) is substantially Bradley-Foundation-shaped.

## Connection to broader vault network

Bradley Foundation board membership overlaps substantially with [[Council for National Policy]] membership; Bradley Foundation funder relationships overlap with [[DeVos Family Network]], [[Mercer]] family giving, and the broader CNP-aligned donor architecture. The 2016 [[Bradley Foundation document leak]] (when internal foundation strategy documents became public) produced substantial visibility into the foundation's operational strategy.
