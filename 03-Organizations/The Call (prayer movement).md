# TheCall (prayer movement)

## Summary

TheCall was a prayer-rally organization founded by [[Lou Engle]] around 1999, operating through 12-hour and 24-hour mass gatherings that combined fasting, worship, sermons, and charismatic prayer. Originally conceived as a co-ed youth version of Promise Keepers, TheCall became one of the [[New Apostolic Reformation]]'s most visible public faces and among the most effective mass-mobilization mechanisms on the American Christian right in the 2000s and 2010s. Its events were explicitly political in orientation — aimed at overturning *Roe v. Wade*, blocking LGBTQ rights, and electing conservative evangelical candidates — while maintaining the legal and rhetorical frame of religious practice. In October 2018, Engle formally dissolved the organization and pivoted to Lou Engle Ministries.

## Identity

TheCall positioned itself as a "solemn assembly" movement rooted in the biblical book of Joel — a gathering of the nation for corporate repentance, fasting, and prayer to avert divine judgment. This framing allowed it to maintain tax-advantaged religious status while operating as de facto political infrastructure. The events drew their emotional intensity from charismatic worship traditions: prolonged fasting, shofar-blowing, speaking in tongues, and intercession on behalf of specific political outcomes.

The organization's core theology held that:
1. America's legalization of abortion and toleration of homosexuality constituted national sin inviting divine judgment.
2. Sustained prayer and fasting by a righteous remnant could reverse these sins and avert judgment.
3. Appointing judges to overturn "Antichrist legislation" (Engle's term) was a primary spiritual mandate.

This made TheCall an operational node where NAR apostolic authority structures translated directly into voter mobilization and candidate endorsement activity.

## Leadership & Key Personnel

- **Lou Engle** — sole founder and primary platform figure; apostle in the [[New Apostolic Reformation]]; senior leader at the International House of Prayer (IHOP).
- **Paul and Cheryl Amabile** — continued *TheBriefing* email newsletter after Engle dissolved the formal organization in 2018; the prophetic-assignment and political-organizing newsletter continued as a successor vehicle.
- **Mike Bickle** — IHOP co-founder; provided institutional base and audience for TheCall events.
- Associated speakers/supporters: James Dobson, Tony Perkins, Mike Huckabee, Sam Brownback, Sarah Palin, Newt Gingrich, Michele Bachmann, Ted Cruz.

## Funding & Money Flows

Specific financial disclosures are not well documented in open sources reviewed. TheCall operated as a nonprofit religious organization. Its events generated revenue through offerings, merchandise, and allied ministry donations. The SPLC noted up to 20,000 youth were annually called to fasting and pledge-taking at TheCall events — a scale requiring substantial organizational infrastructure. Financial records have not been independently audited in publicly available sources; this is a gap.

## Activities

**Mass rallies:** TheCall's signature events drew crowds estimated from 20,000 to 500,000 depending on venue. The inaugural 2000 National Mall rally is the largest single event on record (300,000–500,000). Events typically ran 12 or 24 hours with fasting required of attendees.

**Political mobilization:** Direct endorsement of ballot measures (California Prop 8, 2008), candidate support (Rick Perry, 2011; Sarah Palin, 2008), and Supreme Court campaigns (overturning *Roe v. Wade*).

**Youth culture war:** Up to 20,000 youths annually were called to 40-day fasts and culture-war pledges against pornography, abortion, and homosexuality, per SPLC documentation.

**International expansion:** Events in South Korea (2002, 2005), Uganda (2010), and Singapore (2018 controversy). The Uganda rally endorsed anti-LGBTQ legislation that included provisions for life imprisonment and, in some versions, the death penalty.

**Subsidiary vehicles:** TheResponse (stadium revivals, 2011 Rick Perry event); RiseUp (women's political-prayer, 2017); Esther Fast (2017 prayer campaign for Trump presidency and Roe reversal).

## Successor / Related Entities

- **Lou Engle Ministries** — direct successor; Engle continues ministry operations.
- **TheBriefing newsletter** — continued by Paul and Cheryl Amabile as a prophetic-assignment and political-organizing vehicle.
- **International House of Prayer (IHOP)** — organizational infrastructure anchor; continues operating.
- [[ReAwaken America]] — successor-era NAR mass-rally format targeting similar audiences.

## Controversies / Investigations

- **Uganda 2010:** Engle's May 2010 rally in Kampala drew international condemnation for endorsing Uganda's Anti-Homosexuality Bill, which proposed life imprisonment and the death penalty for gay Ugandans with HIV. Engle issued a partial statement condemning the penalties but did not reject criminalization of homosexuality.
- **SPLC designation:** Listed Engle as an extremist figure; documented battle rhetoric and "bloodlust" language in smaller settings; documented Joel's Army affiliation.
- **Singapore 2018:** Inflammatory anti-Muslim remarks at a Cornerstone Community Church conference triggered a Singapore Ministry of Home Affairs investigation; organizers apologized and pledged not to invite Engle back.
- **GLAAD Accountability Project:** Engle listed for use of platform to spread misinformation harmful to LGBTQ people.

## Open Questions

- Full financial records and donor networks for TheCall have not been publicly disclosed.
- The degree to which TheBriefing newsletter functioned as a continuation of TheCall's political-organizing infrastructure post-2018 is not well documented.
- Whether TheCall's international chapters (particularly South Korea) continued operating independently after 2018 is unclear.
