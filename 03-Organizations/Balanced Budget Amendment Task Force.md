# Balanced Budget Amendment Task Force

## Summary

The Balanced Budget Amendment Task Force (BBATF, also operating as Balanced Budget Amendment, Inc. and branding itself as BBA4USA) is a 501(c)(4) conservative lobbying organization founded in 2010 to pursue a balanced-budget amendment to the U.S. Constitution via the Article V convention mechanism. Unlike [[Convention of States Action]], which pursues a broader multi-topic convention, the BBATF's strategy is single-issue: accumulate applications from 34 state legislatures calling for a convention limited to proposing a balanced-budget amendment. As of the mid-2020s the organization claimed applications from approximately 27–28 states, well short of the 34-state threshold but representing the largest single-topic application count in U.S. history. The organization is a key node in the [[Article V aggregation problem]] debate because its applications vary in wording across states, raising the question of whether Congress could or would aggregate them.

## Identity

The legal entity is "Balanced Budget Amendment, Inc.," a Section 501(c)(4) organization. It operates under multiple trade names and domain identities (BBATF, BBA4USA). The organization maintains a bi-weekly newsletter, the *Balanced Budget Amendment Campaign Update*, tracking state legislative progress. Its website is bba4usa.org.

The BBATF operates in a distinct lane from [[Convention of States Action]]: COS seeks a broad convention covering fiscal restraints, federal overreach limits, and term limits simultaneously, while BBATF insists on a single-subject application for the BBA alone. This single-subject discipline is both a strategic choice (to minimize the [[Runaway Convention]] criticism) and a source of tension with broader COS coalition-building.

## Activities

- **State legislative lobbying**: The core activity. BBATF deploys lobbyists and model legislation in state legislatures, targeting states where BBA resolutions have not yet passed.
- **Coalition coordination with ALEC**: The American Legislative Exchange Council has promoted BBA applications through model legislation and state network outreach.
- **Application tracking and advocacy**: The organization publishes regular state-by-state tallies and advocates for uniformity in application language to minimize [[Article V aggregation problem]] risk.
- **Counter-rescue operations**: When states consider rescinding earlier BBA applications, BBATF mobilizes opposition to rescission.

## Timeline

- **2010**: Founded as a 501(c)(4) in Florida.
- **2017**: Organization cited a "record year" of new BBA applications; ALEC recap noted progress toward 34 states.
- **Mid-2020s**: Approximately 27–28 states had filed applications; the organization remained well short of the 34-state threshold. Some older applications of uncertain wording raised aggregation disputes.

## Controversies / Investigations

Critics — including Feingold and Prindiville — point out that:
1. The BBATF's applications vary in wording across states, creating an aggregation problem Congress would need to adjudicate.
2. Some applications were filed in the 1970s–1990s under different political conditions, raising [[Dillon v Gloss 1921]] contemporaneous-consensus concerns.
3. A "balanced budget" convention, once convened, could not be guaranteed to stay within its stated topic — the [[Runaway Convention]] risk that BBATF's single-subject framing was designed to defuse but cannot legally eliminate.
4. Eagle Forum (founded by Phyllis Schlafly) and other conservative organizations have *opposed* BBATF's approach precisely on runaway-convention grounds, creating cross-ideological opposition.

## Open Questions

- How many of the approximately 28 existing BBA applications use identical operative language, and would Congress find them aggregable?
- Could a court compel Congress to aggregate applications and call a convention, or is that a non-justiciable political question post-[[Coleman v Miller 1939]]?
- If the Republican-controlled Congress after 2024 decided to declare 34 applications sufficient, what legal check exists?
