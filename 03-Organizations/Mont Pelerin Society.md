# Mont Pelerin Society

## Summary

The Mont Pelerin Society (MPS) is an invitation-only international society of economists, political philosophers, historians, and allied intellectuals committed to classical liberal principles: free markets, individual liberty, limited government, and the rule of law. Founded on April 10, 1947, at the base of Mont Pèlerin on Lake Geneva, Switzerland, by Friedrich Hayek, it brought together thirty-nine scholars who shared alarm at the post-war spread of Keynesian economics, social democracy, and central planning. Its founding members included Milton Friedman, Ludwig von Mises, Karl Popper, and George Stigler — a who's-who of what would later be called neoliberal thought.

Over the seven decades since its founding, the MPS has functioned as the apex intellectual network of the global free-market movement. Eight of its members have won the Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences. More concretely, MPS membership has served as a connective tissue linking academic economists, think-tank operatives, and major funders — including [[Charles Koch]], a long-standing member who built his policy infrastructure in deliberate consultation with MPS-affiliated scholars. [[James M. Buchanan]] served as MPS president from 1984 to 1986 and was a member for decades; his Center for Study of Public Choice and the [[Mercatus Center]] sit in the same institutional ecosystem as the [[Koch Network]]'s think-tank investments. The Atlas Network — a coordinating body for over 460 free-market think tanks in 96 countries — was founded by a MPS member (Antony Fisher) and has deep structural overlap with MPS membership.

## Identity

The MPS describes itself as a scholarly society promoting "the principles and practice of a free society." It holds biennial general meetings and regional meetings; membership is by invitation only and requires sponsorship by existing members. The society produces no official policy documents and takes no formal political positions — it functions as a deliberative forum and network rather than an advocacy organisation.

Despite its self-description as purely academic, critics including Philip Mirowski and Dieter Plehwe (*The Road from Mont Pèlerin*, 2009) have characterised the MPS as a "neoliberal thought collective" — an organisation whose real function is to elaborate, standardise, and propagate a coherent ideological program across national contexts through the affiliated think-tank network. This characterisation is the subject of ongoing scholarly debate.

## Leadership & key personnel

- **Friedrich Hayek** — founder; first president; author of *The Road to Serfdom* (1944) whose success partly motivated the founding.
- **Milton Friedman** — founding member; president 1970–72; Nobel 1976; the most publicly prominent MPS intellectual.
- **Ludwig von Mises** — founding member; Austrian school economist; mentor to Hayek.
- **Karl Popper** — founding member; philosopher of science; attended first meeting.
- **George Stigler** — founding member; developed regulatory capture theory; Nobel 1982.
- **[[James M. Buchanan]]** — member; president 1984–86; Nobel 1986; brought [[public choice theory]] into the MPS mainstream.
- **Ronald Coase** — member; Nobel 1991.
- **Gary Becker** — member; Nobel 1992.
- **Charles Koch** — member; industrialist; used MPS connections to build his policy network; co-founder of the [[Cato Institute]], major funder of [[Mercatus Center]], GMU, and dozens of MPS-affiliated think tanks.
- **Antony Fisher** — member; founded the Atlas Network (originally Atlas Economic Research Foundation, 1981) to replicate the Institute of Economic Affairs model globally.

## Funding & money flows

The MPS itself is funded by membership dues and donations; it is a relatively small organisation by budget. Its significance is not its own finances but the overlap between its membership and the broader free-market donor ecosystem. Documented connections to the [[Koch Network]] include:

- Charles Koch is a long-standing MPS member.
- At least 12 of the US think tanks represented in MPS membership have received funding from at least one of three Koch family foundations (Charles G. Koch Charitable Foundation, Claude R. Lambe Charitable Foundation, David H. Koch Charitable Foundation).
- The Cato Institute — co-founded by Charles Koch — is the largest US think tank in the MPS membership.
- The [[Mercatus Center]], the [[Heritage Foundation]], the [[Hoover Institution]], the American Enterprise Institute, the Reason Foundation, the Foundation for Economic Education, and the Heartland Institute are among the organisations with documented MPS-member affiliations and Koch funding overlap.

## Activities

- **Biennial general meetings** and regional meetings at which members present and discuss research; membership network sustains cross-national ideological coherence.
- **Intellectual incubation**: Ideas developed within MPS discussions have migrated into mainstream conservative and libertarian policy. Hayek's critique of central planning, Friedman's monetarism, Buchanan's public choice, Stigler's regulatory capture, and supply-side economics all circulated through MPS before wider adoption.
- **Think-tank seeding**: Multiple MPS members have founded or co-founded think tanks that form the global free-market infrastructure. In the US: Cato Institute (Koch/MPS), Heritage Foundation (Weyrich/Feulner — overlap with MPS affiliates), Reason Foundation, Fraser Institute (Canada).
- **Atlas Network**: Founded by MPS member Antony Fisher; now coordinates 460+ think tanks in 96 countries, many of which have MPS-affiliated leadership.

## Successor / related entities

- **[[Atlas Network]]** — global think-tank coordinating body; founded by MPS member; functions as operational arm of the free-market movement the MPS intellectually anchors.
- **[[Cato Institute]]** — co-founded by Koch; staffed partly from MPS-affiliated economists.
- **[[Mercatus Center]]** — GMU-based; grew from the Buchanan Center; Koch-funded; MPS-adjacent in personnel.
- **[[Heritage Foundation]]** — not an MPS spin-off but deep personnel overlap; used MPS intellectual outputs in policy documents including Project 2025.

## Controversies / investigations

- **"Neoliberal thought collective" characterisation**: Mirowski and Plehwe's academic work argues the MPS functions as an ideological coordination mechanism, not merely a scholarly forum. MPS disputes this.
- **Koch network overlap**: DeSmog and SourceWatch have documented the dense funding and membership overlap between MPS and the Koch donor ecosystem; the MPS has not publicly responded to these specific analyses.
- **Climate denial linkages**: Multiple MPS-affiliated think tanks (Heartland Institute, Competitive Enterprise Institute) have produced climate-denial research. The MPS itself takes no position on climate science.
- **MacLean's thesis**: [[Source - MacLean 2017 - Democracy in Chains]] places the MPS at the centre of a coordinated long-run project to structurally entrench market outcomes against democratic revision; this claim is contested by scholars who accept the factual connections but dispute the conspiratorial framing.
