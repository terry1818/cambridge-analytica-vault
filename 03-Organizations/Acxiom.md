# Acxiom

## Summary

Acxiom is one of the oldest and largest consumer data brokers in the world. Founded in 1969 in Conway, Arkansas as Demographics Inc. — later CCX Network, then Acxiom Corporation in 1988 — it pioneered the mass aggregation of personal data for direct marketing, building individual-level dossiers on hundreds of millions of consumers from public records, purchases, surveys, and online behavior. For decades Acxiom was the archetypal data broker, the company most often cited when journalists and regulators tried to explain the industry. After the [[Facebook-CA Data Scandal]] and the arrival of GDPR and the California Consumer Privacy Act, Acxiom restructured: in 2018 it sold its Marketing Services division to the advertising holding company Interpublic Group (IPG) for about $2.3 billion, and its parent company rebranded as [[LiveRamp]], repositioning around identity-resolution "data onboarding." Acxiom's trajectory matters to the vault as a case study: the firm most associated with pre-CA data brokerage did not vanish under regulatory pressure — it split, rebranded, and continued, demonstrating the industry's resilience.

## Funding & money flows

Acxiom was a publicly traded company for decades. The defining money flow is the 2018 transaction: IPG paid roughly **$2.3 billion** for the Acxiom Marketing Services division, while the remaining company continued as the publicly traded [[LiveRamp]]. Revenue historically came from licensing consumer data and audience segments to advertisers, retailers, financial firms, and other marketers; LiveRamp's revenue centers on identity-resolution and "data collaboration" services.

## Activities

**Mass consumer profiling.** Acxiom's core historical business was compiling and selling detailed consumer profiles — name, address, household composition, income estimates, purchase history, interests, life-stage and lifestyle segments. At its peak the company described data on hundreds of millions of consumers worldwide, sorted into marketing segments.

**Identity resolution.** Acxiom developed AbiliTec, a persistent-identifier system that links a person's many emails, phone numbers, names, and addresses to a single durable identity — the technical backbone of cross-channel marketing. [[LiveRamp]]'s IdentityLink extended this for the digital era, matching offline identities to online activity for ad targeting.

**Onboarding.** LiveRamp's acquisition (2014) gave Acxiom "data onboarding" — bridging a client's offline customer data to digital advertising platforms — which became the strategic core of the rebranded company.

## Controversies / investigations

**Industry archetype for privacy concern.** Acxiom was the data broker most frequently named in 2010s privacy reporting and in the FTC's 2014 "Data Brokers: A Call for Transparency and Accountability" report, which scrutinized the opacity of the industry.

**Cambridge Analytica context.** Acxiom-type consumer data was part of the commercial-data layer that political operations, including [[Cambridge Analytica]], used to enrich voter models. The CA scandal, GDPR, and CCPA together pressured the consumer-data industry — and Acxiom's 2018 restructuring is widely read as a direct response.

**LiveRamp litigation (2025).** A class-action lawsuit alleged LiveRamp Holdings created "Identity Profiles" of users' personal information and resold them to numerous third parties for behavioral tracking and targeted advertising without users' knowledge or consent. `#claim/disputed` (allegation; outcome pending).
