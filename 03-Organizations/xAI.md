# xAI

## Summary

xAI (legally X.AI Corp.) is the artificial-intelligence company founded by [[Elon Musk]] in March 2023, after Musk's break with [[OpenAI]] — the company he had co-founded and then left. xAI's flagship product is **Grok**, a large language model and chatbot integrated directly into the [[X (formerly Twitter)]] platform. In March 2025 xAI acquired X in an all-stock deal valuing X at $33 billion and the combined entity at roughly $80 billion, fusing a frontier AI lab with a major global social network and giving Grok privileged access to X's real-time content stream. For this vault, xAI matters on two axes. First, Musk has explicitly positioned Grok as an ideological corrective to what he calls "woke" AI — making xAI the leading vehicle for the [[tech right]]'s project of building AI models shaped to a political worldview. Second, xAI's Grok has been at the center of disinformation and harm controversies, including generating sexualized images of women and minors, and its Memphis "Colossus" supercomputer has triggered an environmental-justice fight.

## Funding & money flows

xAI is privately funded and has raised very large sums in equity and debt across 2024–2025. The March 2025 X acquisition was structured as an all-stock deal, valuing the combined company at roughly $80 billion ($33 billion for X, less ~$12 billion debt). xAI is part of the Musk corporate group alongside [[SpaceX]]; investors overlap across Musk's companies. The Memphis "Colossus" data-center buildout represents a multibillion-dollar capital commitment.

## Activities

**Grok.** xAI's chatbot, integrated into X, where it answers user prompts and is invoked in-feed. Musk has marketed Grok as less filtered and more "truth-seeking" than rivals — framing competitors' safety guardrails as ideological censorship.

**Vertical integration with X.** Owning [[X (formerly Twitter)]] gives xAI a continuous feed of real-time human conversation for training data and a built-in distribution channel of hundreds of millions of users. Although the two are technically separate businesses, their operations and technical teams have become closely intertwined.

**Colossus supercomputer.** xAI built its "Colossus" training supercomputer in Boxtown, a roughly 90% Black working-class neighborhood of Memphis, beginning in 2024, and has since expanded toward nearly 2 gigawatts of computing capacity by purchasing additional buildings.

## Controversies / investigations

- **Grok harmful-content scandal (2025):** Grok was reported to generate sexualized images of women and minors, raising child-safety and platform-liability concerns. `#claim/established` (reported)
- **Memphis environmental justice:** A TIME investigation tied the Colossus data center's gas turbines to pollution surges in a historically Black community; the turbines were reported to increase Memphis smog by 30–60% and emit nitrogen oxides and formaldehyde. The NAACP sued in April 2026 alleging Clean Air Act violations; xAI was reported to have run turbines without a permit. `#claim/disputed` (xAI contests characterizations)
- **Ideological AI:** Critics argue Musk's framing of Grok as "anti-woke" and "truth-seeking" amounts to building a model tuned to a political viewpoint — a disinformation and info-warfare concern given Grok's integration into X.
