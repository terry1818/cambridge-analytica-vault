## Summary

Gunvor Group is a global commodity trading company founded in 2000 (partnership begun 1997) by Russian-Finnish oil trader [[Gennady Timchenko]] and Swedish trader Torbjörn Törnqvist. Named after Törnqvist's mother's middle name, the company grew from a niche oil exporter using Baltic transit routes to one of the world's largest commodity trading firms by the late 2000s. Its rapid growth tracked the expansion of Russian state oil exports; WikiLeaks diplomatic cables from 2010 referred to it as "Putin's wallet" and alleged — without conclusive evidence — that [[Vladimir Putin]] held a hidden stake. Both Gunvor and Timchenko denied this. Following Russia's 2014 annexation of Crimea, Timchenko sold his 43.5% stake the day before he was sanctioned by the US Treasury. The company has since restructured toward full employee ownership and positioned itself as independent of Russian state interests.

## Identity

Gunvor's business is physical commodity trading — buying and selling crude oil, petroleum products, LNG, coal, and agricultural commodities. It does not extract resources; it traders and moves them. The company's early competitive advantage was access to export contracts from major Russian state-connected oil producers (Rosneft, Surgutneftegas, and others), which critics and investigators attributed to Timchenko's political connections. Gunvor disputes this characterization and attributes its success to logistics expertise and market knowledge.

## Leadership & Key Personnel

- **[[Gennady Timchenko]]**: Co-founder; held approximately 43.5% until selling to Törnqvist on 19 March 2014.
- **Torbjörn Törnqvist**: Swedish co-founder; previously worked at BP and Amoco; acquired 87% stake after Timchenko exit; currently chief executive.
- Post-2025: Company is 100% employee-owned after a management buyout on 1 December 2025.

## Funding & Money Flows

At its peak, Gunvor had an annual turnover of $65 billion (2010), handling over 100 million metric tons of oil and petroleum products. Its financial model is commodity trading margin — buying cargoes at one price and selling at another. Critics note that access to large Russian state producer volumes, which Gunvor obtained in disproportionate quantities relative to its market position, was the structural basis of its growth. Whether this access was commercially earned or politically granted remains disputed.

## Activities

- Physical commodity trading (oil, gas, LNG, coal, refined products)
- Charter and logistics of oil tankers
- Terminal and storage operations in multiple countries
- Has expanded into renewable energy trading in recent years

## Controversies / Investigations

- **WikiLeaks cables (2010)**: US State Department cables alleged Putin had an undisclosed ownership stake in Gunvor. Gunvor and Timchenko denied this categorically. Not adjudicated; evidence tier: T3 (diplomatic claim, unverified).
- **US SDN listing of Timchenko (2014)**: The designation cited his relationship with Putin and his Gunvor co-ownership. Gunvor itself was not designated, though its access to Russian export markets was disrupted.
- **Belgian/EU investigations**: Belgian prosecutors conducted investigations into Gunvor's African operations (Congo, Ivory Coast) for alleged corruption in the 2010s; outcome was no charges against the company at the top level.
