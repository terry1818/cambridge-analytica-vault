# US Agency for International Development (USAID)

US federal agency administering foreign aid. Created 1961 by President Kennedy. Approximately 10,000 staff and  annual budget pre-2025. Substantially dismantled by the 2nd Trump administration February-March 2025.

## Pre-2025 footprint

- Operations in 100+ countries
- Major programs: humanitarian aid, global health (PEPFAR), democracy and governance, development assistance
- Largest single funder of HIV/AIDS programming globally (PEPFAR)

## Dismantling (Tier 1)

- January 24, 2025: USAID website removed; staff locked out
- February 3, 2025: USAID administrator role merged into State Department
- February 7, 2025: Most overseas missions ordered closed
- March 2025: Approximately 6,000 USAID employees terminated; remaining functions absorbed by State Department
- The agency continues to exist on paper but operationally has been dissolved

## Effect on global programming

- PEPFAR programs disrupted - HIV treatment interruptions documented in multiple countries
- Famine response programs in Sudan, Yemen, Ethiopia disrupted
- Multiple academic estimates (Lancet, BMJ) project significant excess mortality from program suspensions

## Litigation

- AFGE v. State Department (USAID employee class action) pending
- Foreign government plaintiffs filed suit over breach-of-contract on aid commitments

USAID dismantling is the cleanest single executive-action precedent for the [[Project 2025]] / [[Schedule F]] administrative-state restructuring. An entire federal agency was operationally dissolved without congressional approval, by executive action, in approximately 60 days.
