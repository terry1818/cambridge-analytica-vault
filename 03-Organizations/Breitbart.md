# Breitbart

## Summary

Breitbart News is an American far-right news, opinion and commentary website founded in 2007 by conservative media entrepreneur [[Andrew Breitbart]], who envisioned it as a right-wing answer to *The Huffington Post*. After Breitbart's sudden death in March 2012, [[Steve Bannon]] became executive chairman and steered the outlet toward a more nationalist, anti-immigration and culture-war posture. From around 2011 the site was sustained by major financing from hedge-fund billionaire [[Robert Mercer]] and his daughter [[Rebekah Mercer]] — reporting indicates an initial Mercer endowment of roughly $10–11 million. Under Bannon, Breitbart became the most influential single node of the emerging pro-Trump media sphere; in July 2016 Bannon declared the site "the platform for the alt-right," and weeks later left Breitbart to chair [[Donald Trump]]'s presidential campaign. Breitbart's significance to this vault is structural: it sits at the intersection of donor-funded political infrastructure (the Mercer money), the operative class around Bannon, and the [[MAGA media ecosystem]] that normalized disinformation narratives. It demonstrates how a media outlet was used less as a journalism business than as a political weapon and recruitment funnel.

- [[Andrew Breitbart]] — Founder and public face, 2007–2012; died March 1, 2012
- Larry (Laurence) Solov — Co-founder and CEO; ran the business side after Breitbart's death
- [[Steve Bannon]] — Executive chairman, 2012–2018; the figure most responsible for Breitbart's nationalist / alt-right turn
- Alexander Marlow — Editor-in-chief; longtime Breitbart staffer
- [[Robert Mercer]] / [[Rebekah Mercer]] — Principal financial backers; Rebekah Mercer reportedly held influence over personnel and direction

## Funding & money flows

- Hedge-fund billionaire [[Robert Mercer]] endowed Breitbart with roughly $10–11 million beginning around 2011 (Tier-2 reporting).
- The same Mercer money simultaneously financed [[Cambridge Analytica]], the [[Government Accountability Institute]], and Bannon's political projects — making Breitbart one arm of an integrated, donor-funded political-influence operation.
- In January 2018 Rebekah Mercer publicly withdrew her support from Bannon, which preceded his exit from Breitbart.
- See: [[Mercer Network]] for the broader money map.

## Activities

Breitbart functioned as an aggressively partisan news and opinion site. Under Bannon it ran sustained campaigns against immigration, "globalism," establishment Republicans and mainstream media. The [[Government Accountability Institute]] — co-founded by Bannon — produced opposition research (notably Peter Schweizer's 2015 book *Clinton Cash*) that Breitbart amplified into the wider press. The site's comment sections and framing were repeatedly documented by researchers as conduits for alt-right and white-nationalist content; the BuzzFeed News leak of internal emails (2017) showed editors soliciting input from white-nationalist figures. Breitbart also pioneered the "weaponized narrative" model later studied as a feature of networked [[propaganda]] and [[disinformation]].

- Documented platforming of alt-right and white-nationalist content (BuzzFeed leak, 2017).
