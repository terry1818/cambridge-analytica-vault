# HRock Church

## Summary

HRock Church — founded in 1994 as Harvest Rock Church — is a charismatic megachurch in Pasadena, California, founded by [[Ché Ahn]] and his wife Sue Ahn. It began as a small multiethnic congregation and grew into a large, Spirit-filled church emphasizing supernatural experience, revival, and "societal reformation." HRock is the flagship and home congregation of [[Harvest International Ministry]] (HIM), the apostolic network Ahn launched in 1996, and it operates squarely within the [[New Apostolic Reformation]] tradition of present-day apostles, prophetic ministry, and [[Seven Mountains Mandate]] dominion theology. HRock matters to this vault for two reasons. First, it is the local institutional base from which Ahn — an early Trump-supporting NAR apostle who spoke on the eve of January 6 — operates. Second, HRock was the named plaintiff in **Harvest Rock Church v. Newsom**, a high-profile legal challenge to California's COVID-19 restrictions on indoor worship that reached the U.S. Supreme Court and resulted in a favorable outcome for the church.

## Funding & money flows

HRock is funded through congregational giving, tithes, conferences, and the broader Ahn ministry apparatus. Following the COVID-19 litigation, California reportedly paid roughly **$1.35 million** to settle the church-closure case — funds that flowed to the church/its litigation effort. Detailed congregational financials are not consolidated in reviewed public sources.

## Activities

As a congregation, HRock runs the standard megachurch program — worship services, small groups, conferences, schools of ministry, and internships — but with a pronounced charismatic and dominionist emphasis: supernatural experience, prophetic ministry, "revival," and equipping believers for "reformation" of the seven cultural "mountains." It is the visible, local embodiment of the theology that Ahn's [[Harvest International Ministry]] propagates globally.

HRock's most consequential activity in this vault's terms is **Harvest Rock Church v. Newsom**. During the COVID-19 pandemic, HRock defied California's restrictions on indoor worship gatherings and, with litigation support from religious-liberty attorneys, sued Governor Gavin Newsom. The case became a leading test of pandemic-era worship restrictions; after the Supreme Court signaled in related cases that California's treatment of houses of worship was constitutionally suspect, the dispute resolved in the church's favor, with the state reportedly paying about $1.35 million. The case is frequently cited in religious-liberty advocacy and is part of the same litigation wave advanced by firms such as [[First Liberty Institute]] and [[Thomas More Society]].

Beyond litigation, HRock is the platform from which Ahn conducts his politically engaged ministry. Ahn was an early evangelical backer of Donald Trump, aligned with the post-2020 Stop the Steal movement, and spoke at a January 5, 2021 rally in Washington, D.C. In the 2026 cycle, Ahn — still HRock's senior pastor — launched a campaign for Governor of California framed as a divine mandate. HRock thus functions as the congregational anchor for a leader who carries NAR dominion theology directly into electoral and legal politics.
