# Council for National Policy

US conservative umbrella coordination body founded 1981. Membership-by-invitation. Notable for its secrecy practices (members historically required to keep membership and meeting content private).

## Founding and structure

- Founded 1981 by [[Tim LaHaye]] (Left Behind co-author), [[T. Cullen Davis]], and others
- Functions as a quarterly coordination meeting for the right - religious, business, political - at the leadership level
- 501(c)(3) educational arm; CNP Action is the 501(c)(4) advocacy arm
- Membership has historically included senior figures from Heritage, Family Research Council, NRA, conservative donor families (Wilks, DeVos, Friess), Trump-era officials

## Operational role

- Coordinated significant elements of the [[Stop the Steal]] organizing - 2020-2021 CNP meetings included presentations on the Eastman strategy
- Internal documents leaked to journalists (notably Anne Nelson's "Shadow Network", 2019) document the coordination function

## What the public record establishes (Tier 1)

- Member lists from leaked 2014 and 2020 internal directories establish breadth of membership
- 990 filings establish the corporate structure
- Specific meeting content is generally not public; reporting relies on leaked documents and inside accounts

## What is NOT established

- That CNP is a "conspiracy" in the sense of a coordinated criminal enterprise - it is a coordination forum, not an operational entity
- That all members coordinate on all member organizations' activity
