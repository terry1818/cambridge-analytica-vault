## Summary

The Mercatus Center is the premier Koch-funded policy research center within the George Mason University complex, and one of Washington's most productive sources of free-market, deregulatory, and anti-regulatory policy analysis. Founded by Richard Fink — the principal architect of Koch Industries' political strategy — as the Center for the Study of Market Processes at Rutgers University, it migrated to George Mason following major Koch donations to GMU in the mid-1980s, and took its current name in 1999. The Koch family has contributed more than **$30 million** to George Mason University, the bulk of which flowed through or adjacent to Mercatus. Nancy MacLean's *Democracy in Chains* situates Mercatus as a key node in the Koch network's effort to institutionalize libertarian economic thought and shape federal regulatory policy. Mercatus scholars regularly testify before Congress, provide expert witnesses in regulatory proceedings, and have influenced deregulatory rulemakings across administrations.

## Identity

Taking its name from the Latin word for "market," Mercatus describes its mission as generating "knowledge and understanding of the institutions that affect the freedom to prosper." It is formally housed at George Mason University but is privately funded and operates with significant independence from the university. This hybrid status has generated controversy: a 2018 *Intercept* investigation documented that Mercatus presents as a university research center when soliciting academic credibility, but as a private organization when seeking to limit disclosure — a structural ambiguity that serves donor opacity.

Mercatus is distinct from but operationally complementary to the [[Center for Study of Public Choice]] (Buchanan's academic unit) and the Institute for Humane Studies (Koch-funded scholar-pipeline): Mercatus is the policy-facing, Washington-engaged arm; CSPC is the academic theory arm; IHS is the talent-pipeline arm.

## Leadership & Key Personnel

- **Richard Fink**: Mercatus founder; also co-founded [[Citizens for a Sound Economy]]; Charles Koch's closest political strategist for decades
- **Tyler Cowen**: economist and author; general director; brings mainstream academic credibility
- **Wendy Gramm**: board member; former CFTC chair under Reagan; wife of Senator Phil Gramm; her CFTC tenure involved key deregulatory rulings later linked to Enron
- **Benjamin Klutsey**: current director (as of 2026)

## Funding & Money Flows

The Koch family has contributed **more than $30 million** to George Mason University; significant portions benefited Mercatus directly. Since 2005, Charles Koch's foundation gave the Institute for Humane Studies $23.4 million and provided an additional $9.8 million to Mercatus. The Mercatus Center also receives revenue from conference fees, publication sales, and other private donations. Its annual budget is not publicly itemized on a standard 990 (as it is embedded within a university structure), enabling a degree of financial opacity.

## Activities

Mercatus's primary outputs are:
- **Policy papers and regulatory comments**: submitted to federal agencies during notice-and-comment rulemaking; frequently cited in reducing regulatory burden calculations
- **Congressional testimony**: Mercatus scholars appear regularly before congressional committees, especially on economic and regulatory matters
- **Academic publications and working papers**: bridge academic public choice theory and applied regulatory economics
- **"Freedom and Prosperity" state rankings**: published indices ranking states by economic freedom; used by state-level Koch-network affiliates to advocate for tax and spending reduction
- **Media and public education**: op-eds, podcasts (including Tyler Cowen's "Conversations with Tyler"), and media appearances

The center's regulatory comments are disproportionately cited in deregulatory actions. During the George W. Bush and Trump administrations, Mercatus scholars occupied advisory and staff positions in key regulatory agencies.

## Controversies / Investigations

- **Wendy Gramm / Enron**: Gramm's Mercatus board membership and her prior CFTC ruling exempting Enron's energy trading from oversight were subjects of congressional scrutiny following Enron's 2001 collapse.
- **Identity ambiguity**: The 2018 Intercept investigation documented that Mercatus claims university affiliation for credibility purposes while asserting independence when accountability is at issue.
- **Donor influence on research**: Critics, including MacLean and various academic watchdog organizations, argue that Koch funding creates structural incentives toward pre-determined policy conclusions. Mercatus disputes this.
