# Plain English Glossary

> [!info] If you keep running into a term you don't recognize, look here first. Definitions are deliberately simple. Each term links to its full vault note for depth.

## Vault-specific shorthand

**MOC (Map of Content)** -- An index page that organizes a topic. Like a table of contents but as a wikilinked note. See `01-MOCs/`.

**Tier 1 / Tier 2 / Tier 3** -- Evidence strength labels. Tier 1 = documented (court records, sworn testimony). Tier 2 = strong inference (mainstream investigative journalism). Tier 3 = contested or single-sourced. See [[Evidence Tiers]].

**Source: X** -- A reference to a primary document, book, or filing. Source notes live in `06-Sources/`.

**Master Theses MOC** -- The vault's top-level synthesis. The three biggest claims the vault makes, with falsification tests.

## Political infrastructure

**Cambridge Analytica (CA)** -- Data-targeting firm that profiled ~87 million Facebook users for the 2016 Trump campaign and Brexit Leave campaign. Shut down 2018. See [[Cambridge Analytica]].

**SCL Group** -- Cambridge Analytica's parent company. UK military-intelligence-contracting roots. See [[SCL Group]].

**Mercer family** -- Major US conservative donor family. Funded CA, Breitbart, and the broader Trump-Bannon orbit. See [[Mercer Family Foundation]].

**Project 2025** -- The Heritage Foundation's 922-page transition blueprint for a second Trump term. Includes the personnel database and Schedule F implementation guide. See [[Project 2025]].

**Schedule F** -- The mechanism for reclassifying tens of thousands of career federal employees as at-will political appointees. Allows mass firing of civil servants. See [[Schedule F]].

**Federalist Society** -- The membership organization that has effectively credentialed every Republican-administration Supreme Court and federal judicial nominee since 1982. See [[Federalist Society]].

**Heritage Foundation** -- The oldest and largest conservative think tank (founded 1973). Operates Project 2025. See [[Heritage Foundation]].

**Council for National Policy (CNP)** -- A secretive quarterly coordination forum for senior conservative figures since 1981. See [[Council for National Policy]].

## Constitutional and legal mechanisms

**Article V Convention** -- A constitutional procedure that allows two-thirds of state legislatures (34 states) to call a convention to propose constitutional amendments without Congress. Has never been used. See [[Article V Convention]].

**Convention of States (COS)** -- A specific movement organized to trigger an Article V Convention with three subjects: term limits, federal spending limits, federal power limits. Has ~19 of the 34 state applications needed. See [[Convention of States Action]].

**NPVIC (National Popular Vote Interstate Compact)** -- A counter-mechanism. States agree to award their electoral votes to the popular vote winner once states totaling 270 EVs have joined. Currently ~209 EVs joined. See [[National Popular Vote Interstate Compact]].

**Electoral Count Act / ECRA 2022** -- The 1887 federal law governing how Congress counts electoral votes. Substantially reformed in 2022. See [[Electoral Count Act]].

**Citizens United v. FEC (2010)** -- The Supreme Court case that allowed unlimited independent political spending by corporations. Created modern dark money. See [[Citizens United v. FEC]].

**Dobbs v. Jackson (2022)** -- The Supreme Court case that overturned Roe v. Wade. Biggest single payoff of the Federalist Society judicial pipeline. See [[Dobbs v. Jackson]].

**Trump v. United States (2024)** -- The Supreme Court case granting Presidents broad immunity from criminal prosecution for "official acts." Effectively ended the Jack Smith federal cases. See [[Trump v United States]].

## Religious right specific

**Christian Nationalism** -- The political ideology asserting the US is and should be a Christian nation. See [[Christian Nationalism]].

**New Apostolic Reformation (NAR)** -- A charismatic Christian movement that elevates contemporary "apostles" and "prophets." Major influence on Trump-evangelical alignment. See [[New Apostolic Reformation]].

**Seven Mountain Mandate** -- The theological framework calling for Christians to control seven "spheres" of cultural influence. See [[Seven Mountain Mandate]].

**Religious right** -- The political coalition of conservative US evangelicals, traditionalist Catholics, and Mormons that emerged in the 1970s-80s. See [[Religious right]].

**The Family / Fellowship Foundation** -- A secretive religious-political network operating since 1935. Hosts the National Prayer Breakfast. See [[The Family]].

## Tech right

**Tech right** -- The Silicon Valley founders and venture capitalists (Thiel, Musk, Andreessen, Sacks, Lonsdale, etc.) who have realigned rightward 2020-2024. See [[Tech right]].

**PayPal Mafia** -- The founding cohort of PayPal (Thiel, Musk, Sacks, Levchin, Hoffman, Rabois, etc.) who went on to outsized influence. See [[PayPal Mafia]].

**Neoreaction / Curtis Yarvin** -- The intellectual current arguing democracy should be replaced with CEO-style executive rule. Curtis Yarvin (pseudonym Mencius Moldbug) is central. See [[Curtis Yarvin]].

**Post-liberalism** -- The intellectual current (Deneen, Vermeule, Hazony, Vance) arguing the post-WWII liberal-constitutional order should be replaced. See [[Post-liberalism]].

**Palantir** -- Data-analytics firm co-founded by Peter Thiel. Major contractor for ICE, intelligence community, Pentagon. See [[Palantir]].

## Dark money and donor infrastructure

**Dark money** -- Political spending by 501(c)(4) organizations whose donors are not required to be publicly disclosed. See [[Dark money]].

**501(c)(4)** -- The IRS classification for "social welfare" organizations. Can engage in political activity without donor disclosure. The legal vehicle for most dark money. See [[Dark money]].

**Marble Freedom Trust** -- The `.6B` Leonard Leo-controlled dark-money vehicle funded by a single Barre Seid donation in 2021. Largest single dark-money transaction on record. See [[Marble Freedom Trust]].

**Leonard Leo** -- The Federalist Society co-chair who credentialed the Trump 1.0 Supreme Court nominees and operates the largest dark-money judicial-influence apparatus. See [[Leonard Leo]].

**Koch network** -- The political donor network organized by Charles and David Koch since the 1970s. See [[Koch network]].

**Ghislaine Maxwell** -- Epstein's long-time associate. Convicted 2021 of sex trafficking minors. See [[Ghislaine Maxwell]].

**Les Wexner** -- Founder of L Brands (Victoria's Secret). Documented financial relationship with Epstein. See [[Les Wexner]].

## Russia and information warfare

**Russian interference 2016** -- The documented coordinated operation by the Russian state to influence the 2016 US election. Established by Mueller, ICA January 2017, and bipartisan Senate Intel report. See [[Russian interference 2016]].

**IRA (Internet Research Agency)** -- The St. Petersburg-based troll farm funded by Yevgeny Prigozhin. Indicted by Mueller February 2018. See [[Internet Research Agency (IRA)]].

**GRU** -- Russian military intelligence. Units 26165 and 74455 conducted the 2016 DNC and Podesta email hacks. See [[GRU Unit 26165]] and [[GRU Unit 74455]].

**Active measures** -- Soviet (now Russian) intelligence term for false-flag operations, forged documents, planted news stories, agents of influence, front organizations. See [[Active measures]].

## Surveillance and data

**Surveillance state** -- The government infrastructure of mass observation of citizens (post-9/11 FBI, NSA, DHS programs). See [[Surveillance state]].

**Surveillance capitalism** -- The economic logic of platform companies that monetize behavioral data extracted from users (term from Shoshana Zuboff). See [[Surveillance capitalism]].

**Data broker** -- Companies that aggregate personal data on individuals and sell it to advertisers, political campaigns, law enforcement (Acxiom, Experian, LexisNexis). See [[Data broker]].

**Microtargeting** -- The political-marketing technique of delivering custom messages to individuals based on individual-level data. See [[Microtargeting]].

**Psychographics** -- Personality-trait profiling, in CA's case using the Big Five / OCEAN model overlaid on Facebook data. See [[Psychographics]].
