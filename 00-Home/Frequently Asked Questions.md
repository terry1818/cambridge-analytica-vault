# Frequently Asked Questions

> [!info] These are real questions newcomers ask. The answers are intended to be honest about both the strengths and limits of the vault.

## "Is this a conspiracy theory?"

No, but it is a fair question to ask.

The protection against conspiracy framing is **the evidence tier system**: every claim is labeled Tier 1, 2, or 3. Tier 1 is documented (court records, sworn testimony). Tier 3 is contested or single-sourced.

If a dramatic claim is only Tier 3, the vault tells you that. If you only see Tier 3 evidence for something, treat it as allegation, not fact.

The vault also explicitly tracks what the public record does NOT establish for major claims. For example, the [[Jeffrey Epstein]] note flags that "Mossad asset" claims are uncorroborated, even though the broader compromised-elites pattern is well-documented.

See [[Evidence Tiers]] for the full framework.

## "Why should I care?"

If you live in the US, the people and structures described here are actively shaping the laws, regulations, and judicial decisions that affect you. Understanding the structure is the first step to engaging with it.

If you are a journalist, researcher, or activist, the vault gives you a sourced starting point. You do not have to take any claim on faith -- click the source notes and verify yourself.

## "Why does this look like it's hating on Republicans / conservatives?"

The vault traces a specific set of organized political projects. Most of those projects happen to be on the political right -- not because the left has no problems, but because the structural-capture project the vault analyzes is right-coalition.

The vault treats specific people and claims on their merits. [[Liz Cheney]], [[Mitt Romney]], [[Adam Kinzinger]], [[Marc Short]], [[Greg Jacob]] are all conservative Republicans who get treated as institutional defenders of constitutional norms.

The vault also does not flatter the left. The [[Disinformation-research infrastructure defunding]] note credits documented academic research without claiming it was perfectly neutral. The [[Hunter Biden]] note is calibrated: documented business activity is documented; political framing is flagged separately.

## "Why should I trust the person who made this?"

You shouldn't, fully. You should trust the sources.

Every claim links to source material. If a court filing supports a claim, the vault links to it. If only a single journalist's reporting supports a claim, the vault says so and labels the tier accordingly.

The vault is maintained by a single citizen-researcher in Columbus, Ohio (not affiliated with any think tank, party, foundation, or government). PRs are welcome from anyone with sourced additions.

## "What can I actually do with this?"

Several things, depending on what you have time for:

**5 minutes:** Read [[Master Theses MOC]] and understand the basic structure. Tell one friend.

**An hour:** Pick one cluster (e.g. [[Convention of States Action]] or [[Project 2025]]) and read the connected notes. You will be substantially more informed than 99% of the public.

**A weekend:** Read the full [[Reading List - Weekend]]. You will have a research-grade understanding of contemporary US right-coalition organizing.

**Ongoing:** Contribute. Sourced corrections, new entity notes, primary-source additions are all welcome PRs. The vault credibility depends on community verification.

**Operationally:** The vault includes state-level defensive playbooks for Ohio, Pennsylvania, Iowa, Virginia, Wyoming (see `09-Threads/State-Playbooks/`) -- defensive frameworks for civic engagement.

## "How is this different from existing books on this?"

Most books on these topics are single-author syntheses (Anne Nelson "Shadow Network," Jane Mayer "Dark Money," Nancy MacLean "Democracy in Chains," etc.). They are excellent and the vault cites them extensively.

The vault is different because it is **a wikilinked graph rather than a linear narrative**. You can enter at any node and trace connections. The trade-off: the vault lacks the narrative coherence of a book, but it lets you explore the structure non-linearly.

## "What's the worst-case for getting this wrong?"

The vault makes calibration mistakes. Every researcher does. The evidence tier system is designed to make those mistakes easy to spot and correct.

If you find a claim that is mis-tiered (something rated Tier 1 that is actually Tier 3, or vice versa), please open a GitHub Issue or PR. That is how the project's quality is maintained.

The vault explicitly avoids claims about specific individuals' private conduct that are not supported by Tier 1 or Tier 2 evidence. If a name appears in the vault, there is documented public-record reason for it.

## "I have a connection or correction to add. How do I contribute?"

- Open a [GitHub Issue](https://github.com/terry1818/cambridge-analytica-vault/issues) for a missing entity, broken link, or disputed claim.
- Open a Pull Request for new sourced content. Use the templates in `_templates/` and follow [[Conventions]] for naming.
- For substantial new sections or MOCs, open an Issue first to discuss the scope.

PRs without sources, or that lower the evidence-tier discipline, will be closed.

## "Is this politically neutral?"

No. The vault has an analytic point of view: that the contemporary right-coalition is pursuing a coordinated structural-capture project that has serious consequences for US democratic institutions. That is an argument, not a neutral description.

The vault is calibrated, not neutral. Calibrated means: every claim is sourced and tiered, opposing positions are steelmanned where relevant, and individual figures on all sides are treated on their merits.

If you disagree with the analytic frame, the vault still provides you a sourced research base you can use against it. The sources are the same regardless of which conclusions you draw.
