## Subject

**John C. Eastman** — American conservative legal scholar, former dean of Chapman University Fowler School of Law (2007–2010), Federalist Society member, and the principal legal architect of the **December 2020 / January 2021 effort to overturn the results of the [[2020 US Presidential Election]]**.

## Key claims drawn from this source

1. **Eastman authored the "Eastman memos"** — a series of memoranda (2-page and 6-page versions) laying out steps that **[[Mike Pence]]**, as Vice President presiding over the joint session of Congress on January 6, 2021, could allegedly take to **delay or refuse to certify Joe Biden's electoral victory**. The memos argued Pence had unilateral authority to:
   - Reject electoral slates from contested states
   - Send disputed slates back to state legislatures
   - Declare Trump the winner via 12th Amendment contingent procedure

2. **The memos were legally baseless** — multiple legal scholars (including conservatives) and Eastman's former Chapman colleagues publicly rejected the memos' constitutional reasoning. Pence's own counsel Greg Jacob rejected the theory after extended argument with Eastman in the days before Jan 6.

3. **The fake electors scheme** — Eastman coordinated with Trump campaign attorneys on the scheme to assemble alternative slates of "Trump electors" in seven swing states won by Biden, intended for Pence to count instead of the certified Biden slates.

4. **January 6 speech** — Eastman spoke at Trump's Ellipse rally on January 6, 2021, repeating the rigged-election claims.

5. **Disciplinary and criminal consequences:**
   - **California State Bar:** Filed disciplinary charges January 2023. Judge Yvette Roland recommended disbarment March 2024. California Supreme Court ordered Eastman's name "stricken from the roll of attorneys" — i.e., he was **disbarred**.
   - **Georgia indictment:** Charged in the Fulton County RICO case (August 2023) alongside Trump and 17 other co-defendants. Eastman surrendered to Fulton County authorities in August 2023.
   - **Federal exposure:** Named in the Jack Smith federal indictment of Trump (though Smith's case was dismissed after Trump's November 2024 victory)
